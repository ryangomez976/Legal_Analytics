or observation instrument. For example, the teacher could chart frequencies of specified behaviors.

2.    Teacher maintained interactive journal. (A written journal in which a responder or responders, chosen by the
evaluatee, provide regular written feedback on the evaluatee entries). Maintaining an interactive journal or thoughts,
reactions, progress, etc. focusing on a particular student, class, or one’s teaching in general can be a valuable tool for
self-reflection and professional growth. The focus of the interactive journal entries should be decided in advance.

3.    Progress through self-studv material. This method involves using some existing programmed materials for
reviewing one’s teaching style or to investigate alternative teaching techniques and materials. The materials typically
would involve some general introduction and then a set of modules to work through which required some kind of
response from the teacher.

4.    Teacher maintained portfolios. Portfolios of lesson plans, instructional materials developed, student assessments
used, or any other category of teaching products can be kept in a file and reviewed by the teacher as a way of
constantly improving on what has been done or used. The purpose and criteria for including pieces in the portfolio can
be maintained primarily for the self-reflective value to the teacher, a peer review of the content could also lead to some
valuable insights.

5.    Coaching. May include, but is not limited to demonstration lessons; providing release time to visit other classrooms;
conference attendance, sample lesson plans; alternative strategies and techniques; support and encouragement; and
responding to reasonable requests from the evaluatee.

6.    Other methods as agreed to by the unit member and evaluator.
