AGREEMENT

The articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
("Agreement") by and between the Governing Board of the Oroville Elementary School District
("District") and the Oroville Elementary Teachers Association ("Association"), CTA/NEA.

ARTICLE I
RECOGNITION

The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of the following unit
positions:

All Classroom Teachers

Counselors

Librarians

Nurses

Psychologists

Speech & Language Pathologists
The unit excludes the following job classifications:

Superintendent

Associate Superintendent, Human Resources & Administration

Assistant Superintendent, Business Services

Director of Instruction

Director of Special Education

Principals

Assistant Principals

Substitute Teachers

New classifications added or positions added to classes shall be subject of negotiations between
the Association and the District to determine if they are to be included in the bargaining unit.
Disputed cases shall be submitted to PERB and shall not be subject to the grievance procedure.

A "unit member" shall mean any member of the bargaining unit as set forth in this article.

ARTICLE VI
GRIEVANCES

Section 1 - Definitions

A "grievance" is a formal written claim by a grievant that he/she has been affected by a violation,
misapplication or misinterpretation of the specific provisions of this Agreement. The object of
this article is to resolve grievances at the lowest level possible.

5

A "grievant" may be any member of the unit or the Association.

An "aggrieved person" is the person or persons making the claim.

A "day" is any day in which the District Administration Office is open for business.

Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time limits specified
at each level should be considered to be maximums and every effort should be made to expedite
the process. The time limits may, however, be extended by mutual agreement.

All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a grievance will be
filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in a personnel file of any of the participants.

Forms for filing grievances, serving notices, taking appeals, making reports and
recommendations, and other necessary documents will be prepared jointly by the Superintendent
and the Association and given appropriate distribution in order to facilitate operation of the
grievance procedure. The costs of preparing forms shall be borne by the District.

The "immediate administrator" is the site principal who has been designated to administer
grievances and who has immediate jurisdiction over the grievant.

The association may file a grievance at the informal level or Level I on behalf of an individual(s)
at the same site or at Level II, if so desired, if grievants are from more than one site or multiple
grievants at the same site.

Section 2 - Preliminary Informal Resolution

Any employee who believes he/she has a grievance shall present the evidence orally to the
immediate administrator within five (5) days after the employee knew, or reasonably should have
known, of the circumstances which form the basis for the alleged grievance. Failure to do so will
render the alleged grievance null and void. The Administrator shall hold discussions and attempt
to resolve the matter within three (3) days after the presentation of such evidence. It is the intent
of this informal meeting that at least one personal conference be held between the employee and
the immediate administrator.

Section 3 - Formal Level

LEVEL I

If the grievance has not been resolved at the preliminary informal level, the grievant must present
his/her grievance in writing on a form provided by the District to his/her immediate administrator
within ten (10) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the grievance.
Failure to do so will render the grievance null and void. A unit member serving in a year-round
school program during any of the months of June, July, and/or August shall present his or her
grievance in writing within a thirty (30) day period after the occurrence of the act or omission
giving rise to the grievance. This time-line may be extended by mutual written agreement. 6

6

The statement shall include the following:

1.    A concise statement of the grievance including reference to the specific Article
and Section of the Agreement deemed to be violated, misapplied or
misinterpreted.

2.    The issue to be resolved.

3.    The circumstances involved.

4.    The decision rendered by the informal conference.

5.    The specific remedy sought.

The administrator shall communicate his/her decision within ten (10) days after receiving the
grievance. Decisions will be in writing setting forth the decision and the reasons therefore and
will be transmitted promptly to all parties in interest and to the president of the Association. If
the administrator does not respond within the time limits, the grievant may appeal to the next
level. Time limits for appeal shall begin the day following receipt of written decision by the
parties in interest. Within the above time limits either party may request a personal conference.
A unit member may be accompanied by an Association representative at the personal conference.
The administrator will advise the grievant of this right at the time of the request.

LEVEL II

If not satisfied with the decision at Level I, the grievant may appeal the decision within ten (10)
days either by:

A. Submitting a statement on a form provided by the District to the Associate

Superintendent, Human Resources & Administration, for the purpose of seeking
resolution through the Interest-Based (CFIER) Process between the Teachers’
Association and District management, or

The statement shall include a copy of the original grievance which is to include
the following:

1.    A concise statement of the grievance including references to the
specific Article and Section of the Agreement deemed to be
violated, misapplied or misinterpreted.

2.    The issue to be resolved.

3.    The circumstances involved.

4.    The decision rendered at Level I.

5.    A clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal to Level II.

7

OR

B. Proceeding to Level III

Failure to do so will render the grievance null and void.

Through the Interest-Based Process a decision will be issued within a mutually agreed upon time
frame. Decisions will be in writing setting forth the decision and the reasons therefore and will
be transmitted promptly to all parties in interest and to the president of the Association. If the
grievant does not agree with either the decision reached through Interest-Based Process or the
time line as established by the CFIER group, the grievant may appeal to the next level. Time
limits for appeal shall begin the day following receipt of written decision by the parties in
interest.

LEVEL III

If not satisfied with the decision at Level II, or if the grievant does not elect to utilize Level II, the
grievant may appeal the decision by submitting a statement on a form provided by the District to
the Superintendent within ten (10) days. Failure to do so will render the grievance null and void.

This statement shall include a copy of the original grievance which is to include the following:

1.    A concise statement of the grievance including references to the specific Article
and Section of the Agreement deemed to be violated, misapplied or
misinterpreted.

2.    The issue to be resolved.

3.    The circumstances involved.

4.    The decision rendered at Level I.

5.    A clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal to Level II.

The Superintendent shall communicate his/her decision within ten (10) days after receiving the
grievance. Decisions will be in writing setting forth the decision and the reasons therefore and
will be transmitted promptly to all parties in interest and to the president of the Association. If
the administrator does not respond within the time limits, the grievant may appeal to the next
level. Time limits for appeal shall begin the day following receipt of written decision by the
parties in interest.

LEVEL IV

If not satisfied with the decision at Level III, the grievant may, within ten (10) days, submit a
written request for mediation of the grievance. In this event the Association shall, within five (5)
days, submit to the California State Mediation and Conciliation Service a written request for the

8

immediate services of a mediator.

1.    The function of the mediator shall be to assist the parties to achieve a mutually
satisfactory resolution of the grievance. At the outset of this process, the mediator
shall schedule a meeting at a mutually agreeable time for the purpose of resolving
the matter through mediation.

2.    If a satisfactory resolution of the grievance is achieved by means of this mediation
process, both parties to the grievance shall sign a written statement to that effect,
and thus waive the right of either party to any further appeal of the grievance.

3.    The District and the Association have agreed that Level III may be waived by
mutual agreement.

LEVEL V

If the grievant is not satisfied with the results of the Level IV mediation process, he/she may,
with the prior written approval of the Association, within twenty (20) days of the final meeting
with the mediator, request in writing that the grievance by submitted to binding arbitration. If
any question arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such question will be ruled upon by
the arbitrator only after he/she has had an opportunity to hear the merits of the grievance.

1.    The costs for services of the arbitrator, including per diem expenses, travel and
sustenance expenses, if any, shall be shared equally by the District and
Association. The District shall provide a hearing room in the District at no
expense to either party. If the arbitrator requests use of a reporter and/or
transcripts, the parties shall split the costs. If the District requests use of a
reporter and/or transcripts, the District shall assume the costs unless the parties
mutually agree to an equal split. If the Association requests use of a reporter
and/or transcripts, the Association shall assume the costs unless the parties
mutually agree to any equal split. Otherwise, each party shall assume the expense
of the presentation of its own case.

2.    The fact that the grievance has been considered by the parties in the preceding
steps the grievance shall not constitute a waiver of jurisdiction limitations upon
the arbitrator in this Agreement.

3.    The parties shall select a mutually agreeable arbitrator who must be willing to
hold the arbitration hearing in the District. Should they be unable to agree on an
arbitrator within five (5) working days of the Associations' submission of the
grievance to arbitration, an arbitrator shall be selected from a list of five (5)
arbitrators provided by the American Arbitration Association, by alternate striking
of names until one name remains. The party who strikes the first name shall be
determined by the Association striking first on all even numbered grievances and
the District striking first on all odd numbered grievances.

4.    The arbitrator's decision will be in writing and will set forth his/her findings of
fact, reasoning and conclusions on the issues submitted. The arbitrator will be

without power or authority to make any decision which requires the commission
of an act prohibited by law, which is violative of the terms of this Agreement, or
to change any of the Agreement. However, it is agreed that the arbitrator is
empowered to include in any award such financial reimbursement or remedy as is
requested in the party's submission statement.

5. The decision of the arbitrator will be submitted to the Superintendent and the
Association and will be binding upon the parties.

b.    The certificated salary schedule shall be increased by the State

Legislature not adopt a final State budget by July 1st of that school
year, the above computations and adjustment shall be made as soon
as possible following such adoption, and the salary schedule

c.    Formula for Establishing COLA for Each Year, Beginning with 1990-1991
School Year: The District and OETA will yearly agree as to the State statutory
COLA and actual State-funded COLA for each year. Both groups acknowledge
that the difference between the statutory COLA and State-funded COLA is the
amount deficited. It is understood that the statutory COLA, funded COLA, and
deficited amounts will fluctuate with the State economy and will be altered by the
State. It is also understood that as the economy improves, the deficited amounts
may be repaid to the District by the State.

The yearly COLA which is adjusted on the certificated salary schedules for unit
members will be determined by the actual funded COLA by the State, which
equals the statutory COLA multiplied by the State deficit factor. As COLAs are
applied to the certificated salary schedules and the percentage funded is adjusted 10

10

upward or downward later by the State, the salary schedule shall also be adjusted,
provided the total adjustments are greater than 0.25%. If the total adjustments
are less than 0.25%, then the adjustments will be held to the following adjustment
period. The adjustments will occur annually on June 1, to be paid to unit
members by June 30.

The Oroville Elementary School District shall maintain a ledger, with copies for
the Association, which sets forth the statutory COLA, the State-funded COLA
(the deficited COLA), and all funds sent to the District to repay the deficits.

Negotiated language and salary schedules for the years 1989-1990 through the
current year's schedules shall be included in the CFIER Budget Committee's
documentation.

d.    Should the State COLA approach to determining basic percentage
increases in State funding for public school districts be
legislatively altered or abandoned during the period of this
Agreement and be replaced by a different funding approach, then
the parties agree to meet and negotiate an agreement as to a
comparable replacement computation for the above formula. If no
agreement is reached, impasse may be declared and the matter
submitted to mediation through PERB.

retroactive to July 1, 1996. The new salary schedule includes application

to the comparable group of school districts' salary schedules.

2.    The District agrees to apply the current year COLA to the psychologists'
salary schedule and to the extra-duty pay stipends, retroactive to July 1,
1996.

3.    Both parties agree that if additional funding is received by the District as
additional deficit reduction funding or equalization funding for the 1996-
1997 School Year, the parties agree to further negotiate compensation. 4

4.    Both parties agree that salary schedule benchmarks need to be set for
comparison purposes with other comparable districts' salary schedules
before the conclusion of spring, 1997, negotiations.

f.    For the 1997-1998 School Year:

1. The District will implement a revised certificated salary schedule
retroactive to July 1,1997. The new salary schedule incorporates

adjustments which will allow the District to rank favorably at all four
benchmarks with the eleven comparable districts. The adjustment equates
to a 3.76% increase (current cost $291,354).

2.    The District will apply a 3.81% COLA ($299,466 for the current year) to
the restructured certificated salary schedules.

3.    A separate schedule for nurses, identical in structure to the previous
certificated salary schedule, will be effective as of July 1,1997.

5.    No other salary adjustments (such as stipends and extra duty pay) will be

negotiations.

6. Horizontal Movement

Course credit for salary placement and movement shall be given only for post-graduate,
upper division or graduate course work taken at four-year colleges, universities or

graduate schools which are accredited by a regional accrediting commission.

Semester hours (units) as defined by the particular accredited college or university will
be acceptable for placement on the salary schedule. Quarter hour units are converted
to semester units by multiplying the total of such quarter units by two-thirds.

Teachers requesting reclassification from one class to another must file such
request with the District office no later than June 15. All course work must be
completed by September 1st to apply for the current school year.

Supporting records or transcripts verifying post-graduate units of study that are to
apply toward such a reclassification must be filed with the District office not later
than September 15. If by September 15 the teacher is unable to submit supporting
records or transcripts verifying post-graduate units of study which are to apply
toward reclassification, official notice in the form of a grade card or letter from
the college or university shall be submitted. Such temporary verifications which
indicate satisfactory completion of the course(s) shall be sufficient evidence to
meet the above requirements. The teacher shall provide the official transcript or
affidavit documents to the District as soon as it becomes available, but no later
than December 30.

The burden of proof of training, experience, possession of credentials and other

18

subsequent reclassification. Any error in classification shall be corrected as soon

course work taken in the armed services, except as it was taken in conjunction
with an accredited college or university and can be verified through official
transcripts.

tours for credits must be assigned upper division or graduate status, assigned a course
title, and given unit value. Such foreign university or other institution must be listed in
the current edition of accredited institutions of higher learning or one of its regional
accrediting commissions.

CLASS SIZE

Section 1-Class Size Reduction

1.    The District is currently assigning 20 students to each class in grades kindergarten through
grade three and attempting to maintain a 20 to 1 average per class;

2.    Pursuant to current State regulations, the District may exceed 20 students per class, provided
the year-long average of students per a single class does not exceed 20.4 students;

3.    The District is currently assigning the same number of students to combination classes in
grades kindergarten through grade three as in straight classes;

4.    If the District stops receiving State funding for class size reductions in grades kindergarten
through grade three, or the funding is reduced by the State, or the State alters the formula
for calculating funding incentives for class size reductions or the class size expectations by
the State and/or Board of Trustees is altered, the Board of Trustees may change the current
standard for class size reduction at grades kindergarten through grade three.

Section 2-Class Size Without Class Size Reduction Program

1.    The District and the Association agree that maintenance of small classes is in the
best interest of the District, and is the continuing goal of both parties.

2.    The District agrees to maintain the objective of reaching a District-wide
teacher/pupil ratio of no greater than 1:28 in all regular classrooms in grades K-8.

3.    Maximum class size for regular classroom teachers shall not exceed thirty-three
(33) pupils per class in kindergarten classes where the morning and afternoon
teachers team teach; thirty (30) pupils in grades 1-3 and kindergarten classes
which do not participate in team teaching (thirty-one (31) with teacher option);
thirty-one (31) pupils in grades 4-6 (thirty-two (32) with teacher option).

•    33    students shall be the limit when the kindergarten teacher has a teacher

partner for the entire portion of the School Year when the class is in session;

•    32    students shall be the limit, unless the teacher volunteers to take an

additional student, when the kindergarten teacher has a teacher partner for at
least 66% of the School Year when the class is in session;

•    31    students shall be the limit, unless the teacher volunteers to take an

additional student, when the kindergarten teacher has a teacher partner for at

31

•    30 students shall be the limit, unless the teacher volunteers to take an

additional student, when the kindergarten teacher has no teacher partner.

the teacher will have a minimum of a three-hour instructional aide assigned to the
class on days when a teacher partner is not scheduled to teach, i.e. the teacher
partner is off track.

C.    For year-round schools where the kindergarten teacher does not track on and off
with students, the class size formula will be determined by the maximum number
of students the teacher teaches on any one day except on common days (when all

ARTICLE XXVI

PEER ASSISTANCE AND REVIEW PROGRAM

Purpose of Program

Pursuant to Education Code, Section 44500, et seq., the primary purpose of the Peer Assistance
and Review (PAR) program is to address the instructional needs of permanent classroom teachers
with unsatisfactory evaluations, as provided in Article XII. A secondary purpose is to provide
support to other certificated teachers through mentors, staff development and beginning teacher
assistance.

The Oroville City Elementary School District and the Oroville Elementary Teachers’ Association
are continuously striving to provide the highest quality of education for all students. In order for
all students to succeed in learning, teachers must succeed in teaching. Therefore, the parties
agree to cooperate in the design and implementation of programs to improve the quality of
instruction through expanded and improved professional development and peer assistance.
Teachers who are referred to or who volunteer for the program are viewed as valuable
professionals who deserve to have the best educational resources provided them in the interest of
improving performance to a successful standard.

All activities undertaken pursuant to the Peer Assistance and Review program are focused on
maintaining and improving classroom instruction in the Oroville City Elementary School
District. All such activities are independent of the evaluation function of administration as
outlined in Article XII.

Section 4 - Application Process

A unit member who believes he/she qualifies for catastrophic leave shall submit, in writing, a request
for catastrophic leave. The request shall be submitted to the Oroville Elementary Teachers’
Association Representative Council on a form provided by OETA. The request shall state the facts
which support the need for a catastrophic leave. The OETA Representative Council is solely
responsible for reviewing all requests for catastrophic leave and informing the unit member of their
decision.

When a catastrophic leave request has been approved by the Representative Council, the OETA
president will inform unit members at each site that a unit member has been incapacitated or is
dealing with a catastrophic illness or injury of an immediate family member. The OETA president
will inform all unit members that a “leave donation drive” has been initiated to solicit donated sick
leave days for the needy unit member.

Each donation drive will be a total of ten (10) working days in length. OETA agrees to provide the
District with a “Notice of Intent” to hold a donation drive.

Unit members may donate up to two (2) days of accrued sick leave to the unit member who has been
approved for the catastrophic leave. All donations shall be submitted, in writing, on a form supplied
by the Oroville Elementary Teachers’ Association.

Donations shall be numbered in priority order by the Oroville Elementary Teachers’ Association as
they are received by OETA. The drive shall be declared as “finished” when either: 1) the amount of
sick days requested by the unit member has been donated; 2) thirty (30) sick days have been donated
by unit members; or 3) the drive has lasted for ten (10) workdays.

Section 5 - Processing of Catastrophic Leave Donations

The Oroville Elementary Teachers’ Association Representative Council shall have the responsibility
of maintaining records of the receipt and review of all catastrophic leave requests, donations of sick
leave and the status of each catastrophic leave drive. It will be the responsibility of the OETA
chapter president to communicate in writing, through use of approved forms in Appendix K, the
Representative Council’s decisions concerning catastrophic leave requests, the success of donation
drives and the status of each catastrophic leave incident to the donors of sick leave, recipients of

71

catastrophic leave, the human resources department and District business office. Any use of
catastrophic leave by a recipient shall be reported by OETA directly to the business office on or
before the 15th of each month to avoid loss of pay by the recipient.

If all donated sick days are not utilized by the approved unit member for the purpose of catastrophic
leave, then the unused sick days will revert back to unit members who donated them, in a numerated
order, with the first donated days being utilized as catastrophic leave and the later donated days being
returned to the donors.

Section 6 - Agreement Concerning Grievances and Litigation

The Oroville Elementary Teachers’ Association agrees that it will not file on its behalf, or on behalf
of a unit member, a grievance, claim or lawsuit related to an attempt by a unit member to challenge
the OETA Representative Council’s decisions concerning catastrophic leave and/or the content of
this article, Article XXVII, “Catastrophic Leave.”

OETA agrees to reimburse the Oroville City Elementary School District, as well as its officers and
agents, for all legal fees and related legal costs incurred, after written notice to OETA concerning a
challenge, in defending against any court of administrative action challenging OETA’s decisions, and
the decisions of OETA officers, in implementing this article, Article XXVII, “Catastrophic Leave.”

OETA shall have the exclusive right to decide and determine whether such action shall be
compromised, resisted, defended and/or appealed.
