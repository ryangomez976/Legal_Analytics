Oak View Union Elementary School District

Certificated Contract Agreement

2014-2015

ARTICLE I
AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT is made and entered into this 19th day of September 2013, by and
between the OAK VIEW UNION SCHOOL DISTRICT (hereinafter referred to as the "District" or
"Trustees") and the OAK VIEW TEACHERS BARGAINING UNIT (OVTBU, hereinafter referred to
as the "Bargaining Unit".)

ARTICLE II
RECOGNITION

The Trustees hereby recognize the Bargaining Unit as the exclusive bargaining representative of
all regular, probationary and permanent teachers and speech therapist employed by the District, excluding
but not limited to summer school teachers, day to day substitute teachers, long-term substitute teachers,
home teachers, management, supervisory, confidential, consultant and emergency employees for the
purpose of negotiating and processing of grievances and further, pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-
3549, of the Government Code (hereinafter referred to as the "Act").

ARTICLE III

NONDISCRIMINATION

The Trustees shall not discriminate against any employee within the bargaining unit on the
basis of race, color, creed, age, national origin, political affiliation, domicile, marital status or physical
handicap.

ARTICLE IV

TEACHER'S WORK DAY/HOURS/YEAR

SECTION A: All bargaining unit employees shall be assigned appropriate starting and dismissal
times; however, the work day shall consist of seven and one-half (7 1/2) consecutive hours, including a
duty-free lunch period; and preparation time.

Presentation of, or attendance at, demonstration lessons; participation in employee training and
conferences with the superintendent/principal or his/her designee; conferences with other employees of
the bargaining unit or with school counselors, parents, and other interested parties; time required
before and after school; and any other traditional responsibilities expected of certificated personnel shall
be included over and above, if necessary, the seven and one/half (7 1/2) consecutive hours. Such
additional duties shall be performed as requested and to the extent determined by the
superintendent/principal or his/her designee. Bargaining unit employees shall remain on duty for such
work-day period, except to attend faculty and other meetings and other functions required by the District,

2

unless special permission to be excused is given otherwise by the superintendent/principal or his/her
designee.

SECTION B: All employees within the bargaining unit shall have a duty-free lunch period each
school day of not less than forty-five minutes, which may be reduced to 30 minutes at the discretion of
the superintendent/principal in case of inclement weather or other unusual or emergency
circumstances, and which shall be allowed as near the noon hour as is reasonably possible.

SECTION C: Bargaining unit employees shall not absent themselves from school during the school day,
unless approved by the superintendent/principal or his/her designee. The superintendent/principal or
his/her designee must know the immediate whereabouts of each employee of the bargaining unit during
the school day, if that employee is on duty.

SECTION D: Full time bargaining unit employees work year shall consist of one hundred eighty-
seven (187) days of instruction, in-service, and instructional-related days as directed by the
superintendent/principal of which there shall be included at least one hundred eighty (180) teaching
days. When school is not in session, but the date is listed on the school calendar as a work day, all
employees shall be required to be in attendance.

The superintendent/principal shall endeavor to make a flexible schedule for work days prior to
the first teaching day with the intent of accommodating bargaining unit employee's schedule.

ARTICLE VI

EMPLOYEE PROTECTION AND SAFETY

SECTION A: Classroom interruptions are detrimental to a good learning environment and to the
continuity of a well planned classroom operation. Therefore, bargaining unit employees and the
superintendent/principal, or his/her designee accept the joint responsibility of minimizing such
interruptions.

SECTION B: The employee's authority in his/her classroom is undermined when teachers discover that
they have little or no administrative backing in discipline. As a result, the school could suffer deterioration
in standards, morale, and a climate favorable for teaching and learning.

SECTION C: The Bargaining Unit understands and agrees that the supervision of students entails pupil
behavior control anywhere on the school campus or anywhere that the classroom exists including field
trips. All employees within the bargaining unitwill require safe and prudent student behavior at all times
and at times when the employee has been directed to the task of supervision by the
superintendent/principal or his/her designee. The district will endeavor to ensure that employee's health
and safety are not jeopardized as a result of such direction.

SECTION D: Each and every employee within the bargaining unit shall be available to confer with the
superintendent/principal or his/her designee to provide the necessary information concerning any pupil
who may lead the employee to believe that the student's attendance is inimical to the welfare of other
students, or acts in such a way that the employee believes good cause exists for such student's
exclusion and shall provide a written statement of the problem within twenty-four (24) hours to the
superintendent/principal or his/her designee unless extenuating circumstances prevent this written report.

appropriate action. As soon as possible, the employee shall request the pupil's parents or guardian to attend

ARTICLE VII

SAVINGS CLAUSE

SECTION A: If any provision of this Agreement is or should at anytime during the term of this
Agreement be held to be invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, then such provision shall not be
applicable or performed or enforced, except to the extent permitted by law. All other provisions of
this Agreement shall continue to be in full force and effect.

SECTION B: Either party signatory to this Agreement may request renegotiation of the declared
invalidated subject matter and any substitute action shall be subject to appropriate negotiations as soon as
the requirements of Section 3547 of the Act are met; however, in no event less than ten (10) days after
such requirements are met.

ARTICLE VIII
EVALUATION

SECTION A: The re-employment recommendation for all bargaining unit employees rests with the
superintendent/principal and must be submitted to the Board of Trustees before March 1 of each
year. For the purpose of evaluation, a bargaining unit employee may request and be granted a
reasonable number of days of consecutive or non-consecutive evaluation at a mutually agreed upon
time.

All evaluations should be based upon wide observation of the bargaining unit employee's
work, spaced over a sufficient period of time to allow for an adequate sampling of performance. If any

7

indication of dehciencies are observed a reasonable period of time shall be given to correct them. The
employee shall also be given an indication in writing of the specific deficiencies and the specific steps
necessary to remedy each documented deficiency covered by the evaluation. Any recorded
observations by the superintendent/principal will include statements of on-the-job visitations and
pertinent data from other sources as well. Signed and dated copies of such records shall be made in
duplicate with the first copy being given to the bargaining unit employee, the second copy being
retained by the superintendent/principal.

SECTION B: Frequency of Evaluation: All permanent certified employees of the bargaining unit shall be
evaluated formally and in writing at least once each year. Additional appraisals may be made based
upon visits, observations, and conduct related to job activities. In the event the superintendent/principal
feels that "improvement is needed" as noted on the rating device, he/she will state the specific
problems as he/she sees them and outline a cooperatively determined course of action leading to
improvement.

SECTION C: Evaluation Format: Each formal evaluation shall include a conference between the
employee and the superintendent/principal. This conference shall be based upon discussion of the Board
adopted evaluation form and a thorough analysis of the employee's attainment of his/her goals for the
year. The superintendent/principal shall complete the evaluation form in triplicate to be signed by the
employee and him/herself. One copy of the form shall be given to the employee, one copy shall be
filed in the employee's personnel file, and the third copy shall be retained by the superintendent/principal.
The signing of the evaluation form by the employee indicates neither acceptance of nor agreement with
the evaluation. The signature indicates the employee has read and discussed the evaluation with the
superintendent/principal. Bargaining unit employees shall have the right to write a reaction or response to
the evaluation which shall become a permanent part of the employee's personnel file.

SECTION D: Independent Evaluation: This section may be reopened by either party during the life
of this Agreement to establish evaluation procedures for bargaining unit employees by an independent
evaluator. If an employee feels he/she does not deserve an unsatisfactory evaluation, the employee
may request to be reevaluated by an independent observer and/or may file a grievance. See Article
XIII.

ARTICLE IX
LEAVES

SECTION A: General Terms Governing Leaves:

8

1. The unit member exercising a leave of absence shall notify the District designee as soon as

known but, short of an emergency or sudden illness, not later than 6:30 a.m. prior to the beginning
of the workday to allow the District to secure substitute services. The notification described herein
shall also include an estimate of the expected duration of the absence.

2.    Whenever possible the unit member shall notify the District Office one (1) hour prior to the
end of the unit member's normal instructional day as to whether or not his/her substitute shall be
retained for the following day. A unit member who has previously informed the District Office
of the duration of his/her absence shall not be required to call in on a daily basis.

3.    A unit member becoming aware of the need for absence due to surgery, maternity, or
other predictable or previously scheduled cause shall notify the superintendent/principal as
soon as possible. The unit member shall complete the District's pre-absence form as part of
this notification. The District may request a physician's statement for the cause of disability.

4.    Upon return to service, the unit member shall complete the District absence form and submit
it to the District Office.

5.    The unit member shall provide, additional verification of the use of these leave provisions if
the District has any further questions.

6.    A unit member who has experienced a disability absence requiring surgery, hospitalization, or
extended medical treatment shall be required to submit, prior to return to service, a medical
statement indicating an ability to return to the unit member position without restrictions or
detriment to his/her physical and emotional well-being.

7.    Unit members on paid leaves of absence (whether partial or full pay) shall continue to receive
District-paid premiums for fringe benefits.

8.    Unit members on unpaid leaves of absence may continue their fringe benefit overage by
prepaying any required premiums to the District.

9.    A unit member on paid leave of absence shall not engage in any form of compensated outside
employment which is additional to that in which he/she was engaged concurrently with his/her
service to the District.

SRCTION B: Sick Leave

1.    The purpose of sick leave utilization shall be for physical and mental disability absences which
are medically necessary and caused by illness, injury, maternity disability, restoration of health or
quarantine.

2.    Twelve month unit members shall be allowed twelve (12) days and ten month unit members

9

shall be allowed ten (10) days per year of leave of absence for the purpose of sick leave utilization.
A unit member covered by this agreement working less than full time shall be entitled to sick leave
in the same ratio that his/her employment bears to full-time employment.

3.    Any unused sick leave credit accumulated may be used by the unit member for sick leave
purposes without loss of compensation.

4.    Sick leave is to be cumulative (without limit) and transferable from one California school
district to another after one year of employment in the school district.

5.    The District shall provide each unit member with an accounting of the number of days of sick

leave he/she has accumulated.

SECTION C: Maternity Disability Leave

1.    Maternity leave shall be granted for physical disability which is caused or contributed to
by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, or recovery thereafter. This leave shall be charged to
sick leave.

2.    A unit employee becoming aware of the need for absence due to maternity shall submit a
statement from her attending physician as far in advance as possible giving the beginning date of
disability and the anticipated date of return.

SECTION D: Differential Pay

1.    Upon exhaustion of all accumulated sick leave, a unit member who continues to be absent
for an approved absence shall be paid the difference between his/her daily rate of pay and the
established substitute daily rate of pay. In no case shall the unit member receive less than fifty
percent (50%) of his/her daily rate of pay.

2.    A unit member must first utilize all accumulated sick leave credit, and the days of differential
pay shall not exceed 100 working days in any one (1) school year. The 100 work days begin with the
first day of absence following exhaustion of the regular sick leave (10 days) granted for that school
year.

SECTION E: Personal Necessity Leave

1.    Unit members may use up to seven (7) days of their accumulated sick leave each year for
personal necessity leave.

2.    Whenever possible unit members shall submit a request for approval for personal necessity
leave, normally not less than three (3) working days prior to the beginning date of the leave. It is
recognized that prior approval shall not apply in cases involving emergencies such as death or

10

3.    Personal necessity leave is limited to use for the following reasons:

a.    Death in a member's immediate family if leave beyond bereavement is
required.

b.    Accident involving a member's person or property or the person or property of
his/her immediate family.

c.    Appearance in court as a litigant in an action not covered under Article IX,
Section H.

d.    Family illness requiring the presence of the employee.

e.    Doctor's visits, when it is demonstrated ahead of time that such
appointments could not be made outside of school hours.

f.    Events involving school-aged children of the unit member in functions such as
graduation, or field trips, as long as they are happening during school hours and could
not be reasonably rescheduled by the unit member.

4.    Personal necessity leave shall not be used for items of personal convenience or those which
reasonably could be addressed during off-duty hours or weekends. Personal necessity leave shall
not be used for such reasons as: extension of school holidays or vacations; extension of personal
vacation; a convention related to a unit member's avocation; recreation.

The superintendent/principal may request proof of personal necessity for purposes of this section.

SECTION F: Compelling Personal Importance

1.    Unit members shall use no more than three (3) days of accumulated sick leave with no reason
being given for the absence.

2.    The three (3) days shall be charged to the seven (7) days of personal necessity leave.

3.    Unit members may not use leave under this section for the purposes stated in Article IX,
Section E, Part 4.

4.    A day of compelling personal importance may not be used on the first or last student
attendance days of a school year nor on the day before or after any holiday, vacation, or holiday
period.

SECTION G: Bereavement Leave

1. Paid bereavement leave shall be granted for the death of a member of the immediate family.

11

2.    A unit member shall be granted up to three (3) days for bereavement purposes. If travel beyond
250 miles is required, two (2) additional days shall be allowed.

3.    In the event of the death of a parent, spouse, and/or child of a unit member, five (5) days of
bereavement leave shall be granted.

4.    Bereavement leave is only applicable to unit members during the normal school year and does
not apply to summer school employees.

SECTION H: Judicial and Official Appearance Leave

1.    Judicial and official appearance leave shall be granted for purposes of regularly called jury duty,
as a litigant arising from causes of action concerning the performance of classroom or official
duties, or to respond to an official order from another governmental jurisdiction for reason not
brought about through the misconduct, by action, or inaction of the unit member.

2.    The unit member seeking judicial and official appearance leave shall present a copy of the
subpoena directing his/her appearance before taking the leave if it is possible to do so.

3.    Upon dismissal prior to the end of the unit member's workday, he/she shall report to his/her
work site, if it is possible to do so prior to one (1) hour before the regular student dismissal time.

4.    A unit member granted a leave of absence under these provisions shall be granted

his/her regular District compensation. The unit member shall reimburse the District any
judicial and official appearance fees received excluding mileage compensation. Such monies
shall be deposited in the General Fund.

SECTION I: Reinstated Sick Leave Credits

1. If a unit member severs all official connection with the California Public Schools System as a
certificated employee and is re-employed as a certificated employee at a later date, not to exceed
thirty-nine (39) months, all unused sick leave credits on file at the close of the prior employment
period shall be reinstated.

SECTION I: Long Term Leave

1.    Employees may be granted unpaid long-term leaves of absence permitted by law and specific
District policies not to exceed the balance of the school year in which it commenced.

2.    The District reserves the right to specify the conditions under which long-term leaves may be
granted. Long-term leaves may be granted for the following purposes: maternity; child rearing;
restoration of health; disabilities in the immediate family.

12

3.    The unit member granted a long-term leave shall inform the District within thirty (30) days
of the scheduled return date as to his/her intentions.

4.    Long term leave does not count as experience towards advancement on the salary schedule
unless the unit member works at least 75% of the work days during the school year.

SECTION K: Personal Leave

1.    Unit members may be granted personal leave by the District without pay for a period not
to exceed a maximum of one (1) year upon their request with the following limitations.

a.    There is a compelling and unchangeable condition of an important nature as
determined by the District.

b.    An absence of the unit member would not create undue hardship on the operation
of the school district as determined by the District on the recommendation of the
superintendent/principal.

c. The requested leave would involve a short-term condition that is not expected
to project into an excess of one (1) year.

2.    The above leave policy would include, but is not limited to, leave of absence for child care,
business opportunity, and professional improvement.

3.    A unit member seeking a personal leave of absence shall submit a request, including the reasons
and any supporting information related thereto, and the duration of the requested leave.

4.    Unit members shall request leave of absence under this policy for a period which will
commence at the beginning of a school semester and terminate not less than at the end of a
school semester in order to permit the employment of a temporary teacher to replace the unit
member on leave of absence.

5.    Unit members granted leave under this section shall receive priority consideration for
employment at the end of the leave period and shall only be eligible for employment if a suitable
vacancy exists.

SECTION L: Military Leave and Duty

1. Any unit member may enlist or be conscripted into the defense forces of the United States of
America for service or training shall be granted military leave. He/she shall be reinstated to his/her
position in the District upon written notice, supported by adequate proof that he/she is fully
capable of performing the duties of the position. Said application for reinstatement shall be made
within a period of ninety (90) days of discharge.

13

2.    Upon return from military service to district service, a unit member shall be entitled to all the
rights and privileges in, connection with, or rising out of the office of employment which

he/she would have enjoyed if he/she had not been absent there from; provided however such unit
member shall not be entitled to sick leave, vacation, or salary for the period during which
he/she was on leave.

3.    Any unit member who is a member of the reserve corps shall be entitled to a temporary
leave of absence for ordered military duty not to exceed one hundred eighty (180) calendar days.

4.    He/she shall have absolute right to be reinstated in his/her position at the end of his/her
period of active duty or an equivalent one if the position has been abolished.

5.    The salary or compensation of a unit member while engaged in ordered military duty shall for
thirty (30) days be one-tenth of his/her annual salary.

SECTION M: Industrial Accident and Illness Leave

1.    Industrial accident and illness leave shall be granted for injury or illness incurred within the

course and scope of a unit member's assigned duties.

2.    A unit member who has sustained a job-related injury shall report the injury to the District
within forty-eight (48) hours, whenever possible. The unit member shall report any illness to the
District within twenty-four (24) hours, whenever possible, of the knowledge that the illness is an
alleged industrial illness.

3.    All unit members are covered by state compensation insurance for injury or illness in the
line of duty.

a.    Allowable leave shall be for not more than sixty (60) days during which
school is required to be in session or when the unit member would otherwise
have been performing work for the District in any one (1) fiscal year for the
same accident or illness.

b.    Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

c.    Industrial accident or illness leave shall commence on the first day of
absence.

d.    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be reduced by one (1) day for each
day of authorized absence regardless of a temporary disability indemnity
award.

14

e.    When an industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the
unit member shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due for the same
injury or illness.

f.    During any industrial paid leave of absence, the unit member shall endorse to the
District the temporary disability indemnity checks received on the account of industrial
accident or illness. The District, in turn, shall issue the unit member's appropriate
salary warrant for payment of his/her salary and shall deduct normal retirement and
other authorized deductions.

5. A unit member shall be permitted to return to service after an industrial accident or illness only
upon the presentation of a release from the authorized workers' compensation physician certifying
the unit member's ability to return to his/her position classification without restrictions or detriment
to the member's physical and emotional well-being.

ARTICLE X

EMPLOYEE BENEFITS

SECTION A: The annual premium for employee benefits coverage is $11,000.00 per employee for the
2013-2014 school years. Any future or differential increases in premiums shall be at the employee's expense.

SECTION B: An employee on unpaid leave, who worked more than 90% of the school year but less than
100%, including use of paid leave, shall have all the insurances paid through the summer.

SECTION C: An employee on unpaid leave, who worked more than 75% but less than 90% of the school
year, including use of paid leave, shall pay their prorated share of the premium cost of the insurances
through the summer.

SECTION D: An employee on unpaid leave shall have the right to continue the District's insurance
coverage by paying the full cost of the premiums to the District.

SECTION E: Retirement Health Benefits

1. Eligibility: To be eligible for this program the employee must:

a.    Have completed fifteen (15) years of service in the Oak View Union
School District prior to retirement.

b.    Have reached the age of fifty-five (55) on or before September 1 of the first
school year in which he/she elects to retire.

c.    Not have passed his/her sixty-fourth (64th) birthday at the beginning of

15

retirement.

d.    Have reached Classes E, F, or G on the Salary Schedule.

e.    Arrange retirement to coincide with the end of a school year when possible and to
provide sufficient advance notice to the District to obtain an acceptable
replacement.

f.    Employees eligible for disability retirement are not eligible for this provision.

2.    Eligibility Period: The employee will be eligible to purchase district paid health
benefits during early retirement only between the ages of fifty-five (55) and his/her
sixty-fifth (65th) birthday. Benefits purchased from the District will terminate at the
end of the month in which the retiree becomes sixty-five (65).

3.    Benefits: Health benefits provided in this plan will include only the same coverage
as provided the active employees. A notice will be mailed by October 1st of each
year to retirees covered under the benefit program listing the current benefits.

time teachers shall be offered to part time teachers with the following conditions and restrictions: The

or other benefit to any part time teacher who elects to not take insurance.

1.    The part time teacher must be contracted for no less than 50% as of October 1 of the

ARTICLE XI

GRIEVANCEARBITRATION PROCEDURE

1.    A grievance is an allegation by an employee, individually, or employees, collectively, of the
bargaining unit that a violation, misinterpretation, misapplication of this Agreement or with
board policy that would be inconsistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

2.    A "grievant" refers to any employee within the bargaining unit covered by the terms of this
Agreement.

3.    "Working Days" are those days which the bargaining unit member is required to be on
duty.

SECTION A: Purpose

1.    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level, equitable
solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise affecting the welfare or working
conditions of the employee within the bargaining unit. These proceedings will be kept as informal
as possible at any level of the procedure.

2.    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any employee

within the bargaining unit having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with the
superintendent/ principal or his/her designee and to have the grievance adjusted without
intervention by the Bargaining Unit, provided that the adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms
of this Agreement.

3.    A grievant may, at his/her option, be represented at all stages of the grievance procedure by
himself/herself or by a representative of the Bargaining Unit.

4.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time limits
specified at each of the following levels should be considered to be maximums, and every effort
should be made to expedite the process. The time limits specified may, however, be extended
by mutual agreement; the time shall begin the day following the alleged event.

5.    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be processed through all the steps in
this grievance procedure by the end of the school year that the time limits set forth herein will be
reduced so that the procedure may be exhausted prior to the end of the school year or as soon

thereafter as is practicable.

17

1.    Level One: Within hfteen (15) working days after the alleged occurrence of the act or omission
giving rise to the grievance, the grievant must first discuss it with the superintendent/principal or in
his/her absence, with his/her designee either directly or through the Bargaining Unit's designated
representative, with the object of resolving the matter.

2.    Level Two:

a.    If the aggrieved is not satisfied with the disposition of his/her grievance at Level
One, or if no decision has been rendered within ten (10) working days after
presentation of the grievance, he/she may file the grievance in writing
simultaneously with the superintendent/principal and the President of the
Bargaining Unit, within seven (7) working days after the decision at Level One, or
fifteen (15) working days after the grievance was presented, whichever is sooner.

b.    The grievance shall be written and shall include:

-    The name of the aggrieved;

-    The date of the alleged violation;

-    The provision or provisions of this Agreement alleged to have been violated;

-    The circumstances of the grievance (a concise statement of the facts constituting
the alleged violation, including dates, names, and places, as appropriate);

-    The specific remediation sought by the grievant or grievants;

-    The signature of the grievant or grievants.

c. Within seven (7) working days after the receipt of the written grievance by the

superintendent/principal, he/she or his/her designee will meet with the aggrieved and a
representative of the Bargaining Unit in an effort to resolve it.

3.    Level Three:

a. If the aggrieved is not satisfied with the disposition of his/her grievance at Level One, or
if no decision has been rendered within ten (10) working days after he/she first met with
the superintendent/principal or, in his/her absence, with his/her designee, he/she shall within
five (5) working days after he/she first met with the superintendent/principal or his/her
designee, whichever is sooner, request in writing that the Bargaining Unit submit is/her
grievance to arbitration. If the Bargaining Unit determines that the grievance merits further

18

hearings it shall, by written notice to the superintendent/principal or his/her designee, within
fifteen (15) working days after receipt of the request from the grievant, submit the
grievance to arbitration.

b.    The parties shall attempt to select a mutually acceptable impartial arbitrator. If the parties
are unable to agree upon an arbitrator within ten (10) working days, a joint request for a
list of arbitrators shall be made to the State Conciliation Service, and the parties will then be
bound by the American arbitration Bargaining Unit rules in the selection of an impartial
arbitrator and the conduct of the arbitration.

c.    The parties shall attempt to mutually agree upon the issue or issues to be submitted to the
selected arbitrator. If agreement cannot be reached upon the submission statement, either
party may submit its own arbitrator's submission statement and the arbitrator shall then
determine the issue or issues by referring to the grievance and the answers thereto at each step.

d.    The selected arbitrator will confer with the representatives of the District and the
Bargaining Unit and will schedule hearings to be held promptly and will issue his/her
opinion not later than thirty (30) calendar days from the date of the close of the hearing, or,
if oral hearings have been waived, then from the date the final statement and proofs were
submitted to him/her. If any question arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such
question will first be ruled upon by the arbitrator. The arbitrator's opinion will be in
writing and will set forth his/her findings of face, reasoning and conclusions on the issue or
issues submitted. The arbitrator shall have no power to add to, or subtract from, disregard,
alter or modify any terms of this Agreement nor the power to change any practice,
policy, or rule of the District, nor to substitute his/her judgments for that of the District
Trustees as to the reasonableness of any such practice, policy, rule or action taken by the
Trustees.

e.    The costs for the services of the arbitrator, including per diem expenses, if and
his/her travel and subsistence expenses, and the cost of any hearing room will be borne
equally by the District and the Bargaining Unit. All other costs will be borne by the
party incurring them.

f.    Issues arising out of the exercise by the district's administration of its duties and
responsibilities as defined by statute, rules, and regulations of the State Board of Education,
policies of the District, or administrative procedures of the District, or by its rights and
powers, including the underlying right to exercise its discretion therein, except as modified by

19

the terms and conditions of this Agreement, shall not be subject to the Grievance/Arbitration
article of this Agreement.

SECTION C: Reprisals: No reprisals will be taken by the superintendent/principal or by any member or
representatives of the administration or the Trustees against any employee within the bargaining unit for
the exercising of his/her rights under this Article.

SECTION D: Sole and Exclusive Method: In consideration of the foregoing arrangement for adjustment
of grievances or setdement of the disputes, the parties of this Agreement accept this procedure as the sole
and exclusive method of seeking adjustment or redress prior to instituting any other proceedings. Nothing
contained herein shall deny to any employee within the bargaining unit his/her rights under State or
Federal constitutions and laws. No probationary teacher shall make use of the grievance procedure in any
way to appeal a discharge or a decision by the Trustees not to renew his/her contract. No tenured
teacher shall use the grievance procedure to dispute any action by the District which is applicable to the
state tenure laws. No teacher shall use the grievance procedure or administration if such decision is
applicable to a State or Federal regulatory commission or agency.

SECTION E: Advisory Arbitration: The Trustees shall have the sole power to render a final and
binding decision of a grievance. The opinion of the selected impartial arbitrator shall only be advisory
and, if upon review of the transcripts of the entire hearing records, briefs, exhibits, and arguments, the
Trustees determine that they as a body are unable to render a final determination on the record, they
may reopen the record for the taking of additional evidence.
