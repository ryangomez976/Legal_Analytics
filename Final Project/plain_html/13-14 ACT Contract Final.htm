A.    AGREEMENT

by and between the CARMEL UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT, hereinafter referred to as "District", and
the ASSOCIATION OF CARMEL TEACHERS/CTA/NEA, hereinafter referred to as "Association".

The term "Agreement" as used herein means the written agreement provided under Section
3540.1(h) of the Government Code.

ARTICLE II
SALARIES AND WAGES

A. SALARY

1.    Salaries and Wages (Refer to Appendix A)

2.    A unit member of the Carmel Unified School District will be placed on the regular teachers'
salary schedule at whatever place he/she is entitled because of education and experience under
the following provisions:

a.    Full credit will automatically be given for up to twenty-two (22) years of teacher
experience. Service credit is to keep increasing by one (1) year annually until we reach
the top step. Two (2) partial years of full-time teaching under contract may be counted
as a full year of experience if the total number of days taught exceeds 180.

b.    Each year of approved successful service to the District, as determined by
administrative review, shall move a teacher up one (1) step on the salary schedule.

c.    Improvements to the salary schedule shall be applied to stipends and extra hours
prospectively beginning July of the following year. There will be no retroactive pay
applied to stipends and extra hours worked.

d.    When any permanent certificated unit member resigns from the District and is re¬
employed after thirty-nine (39) months, prior service with the District shall be evaluated
in the same manner as that of a new teacher for the purposes of determining salary.

3.    Professional Growth: All courses/in-service training for which a teacher wishes to receive credit
must be approved in advance of the first class or activity. Teachers shall use the Request For
Approval For Credit form. (Refer to Appendix D for Pre-Approval Form). When a teacher has
qualified for placement in a higher salary class, the salary shall be increased above the former
salary placement to the appropriate class and step for that teacher's qualifications and
experience under the following conditions:

a. Transcripts relating to pre-approved coursework have been filed in the Human
Resources office prior to:

(1)    September 20th for the first semester of the year in which the higher salary
is to be granted;

(2)    February 20th for the second semester of the year in which the higher
salary is to be granted.

b.    For salary schedule purposes, courses and semester units shall be described as
follows:

(1)    Courses approved for credit towards movement on the salary schedule
shall be for the reason of engaging in continuous and purposeful
professional growth (CSTP 6.2).

(2)    Courses shall be approved when filed in advance in the office of the
Superintendent and:

(2.1)    Are required as a part of an advanced program of an accredited
university; or related to the teacher’s assignment.

(2.2)    Are required to complete full certification of the teacher; or,

(2.3) Are required as part of a full program approved by the
Superintendent; or

(2.4)    Are offered as part of a District in-service program; or

(2.5) Are agreed upon by the Superintendent and teacher as
professionally beneficial to the teacher.

c.    All matter of appeal relating to the number of units shall be referred to the grievance
process.

addition to the teacher's regular assignment shall be made not later than the tenth day of the
following month's payroll period in which the service was performed.

ARTICLE III
CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

A. UNIT MEMBER DUTIES

1. Unit members shall have the professional responsibility to arrive prior to the beginning of the
teacher's assignment and to stay a reasonable period of time after the end of the teacher's
assignment to fulfill adequately the responsibilities required of the teacher.

2.    Each unit member shall also be at school as required for purposes of conference with parents,
for faculty meetings, for supervision of non-classroom activities, and for the performance of
other professional duties.

All full-time unit members are expected to accept a full share of the normal duties for
supervision of non-classroom activities. School principals making such assignments shall limit
unit members to no more than forty (40) hours of service each school year, excluding such
activities as faculty, department, parent and curriculum meetings.

3.    Each classroom teacher shall have a thirty (30) consecutive minute duty-free lunch period
during the student lunch period. Bargaining unit members who are not classroom teachers
shall have a thirty (30) consecutive minute duty-free lunch period, at or about the middle of
their workday, to be agreed upon with the site administrator to meet student needs.

4.    Each part-time unit member shall be responsible for the supervision of non-classroom
activities as assigned by the school principal. The total hours assigned during any
school year shall not exceed a pro rata portion of the hours assigned to full-time unit
members. Part-time unit members shall attend department and faculty meetings at their
assigned school, unless excused by the principal.

5.    Teacher assigned to two (2) or more schools (full or part-time basis) will attend the faculty
meetings when requested by the principal. The unit members specified herein shall not
normally be expected to attend more than one faculty meeting per week except when
necessitated by traditional program activities.

6.    A full-time assignment at the secondary level (grades 6-12) shall consist of five (5) teaching
periods.

7.    Elementary classroom teachers (grades 1-5) shall have two and one-half (2.50) hours of
preparation time per week unless there are irresolvable scheduling problems at the building
level.

ARTICLE V
EARLY RETIREMENT

A. PLANA

1.    Purpose: The purpose of this early retirement plan is to assist those staff members whose

salaries are determined by a certificated salary schedule to retire before the age of sixty-five

(65).

2.    Eligibility:

a.    Have at least (30) years of credited service in California State Teacher’s Retirement
System or be between the 55 and 65 years of age.

b.    Be under contract or on an approved leave of absence with the District at least three (3)
consecutive years immediately prior to retirement.

c.    Have served the District for a total of ten (10) years or more.

d.    The period for the effective date of retirement shall be between the conclusion of the
current school year and the beginning of the next school year.

3.    Benefits:

a.    District-paid health, dental and vision insurance shall continue until the early
retiree reaches his/her sixty-sixth (66th) birthday.

b.    The District shall not eliminate or reduce benefits below what is provided active unit
members and as agreed upon through contract negotiations prior to age sixty-six (66)
for those staff members who have elected early retirement.

B. PLAN B

1.    Purpose: The purpose of this plan is to provide the opportunity for a full-time unit member to
reduce the work year from full-time to part-time duties and retain all retirement benefits in the
years prior to retirement.

2.    Eligibility:

a.    The unit member shall have reached the age of fifty-five (55) prior to the first day of the
semester in which part-time employment is requested.

b.    The unit member shall have been employed full time in a position requiring certification
for at least ten (10) years of which the immediately preceding five (5) years were full
time employment within the district.

c.    The unit member may not have had a break in service during the five (5) years
immediately preceding the reduction in workload. For purposes of this subdivision,
sabbaticals and other approved leaves of absence shall not constitute a break in
service.

d.

Prior to the reduction of a unit member’s workload under this section, the
employer, in conjunction with the administrative staff of the State Teachers’ Retirement
Plan and/or the Public Employees’ Retirement System, shall verify the member’s
eligibility for the reduced workload program.

3. Conditions

a.

The option of part-time employment shall be exercised at the request of the unit
member and subject to agreement by the District. The specific assignment shall be
mutually agreed upon by the unit member and the District. The part-time service will be
at least one-half (1/2) that of a full-time unit member.

b.

The agreement to reduce the unit member’s workload shall be in effect at the beginning
of the school year.

c.

The unit member will resign no later than five (5) years after exercising this option or at
age sixty-five (65), whichever occurs first.

d.

A unit member who elects this plan may return to full-time service, if he/she so requests,
by March 1 of the first year of enrollment in Plan B, with the approval of the School
Board. A unit member may not change from Plan B to any other retirement option
unless the unit member has returned to full-time service for one (1) school year.

e.

A unit member who retires, or otherwise separates from service, prior to the end of the
school year shall be in violation of this section and the unit member’s service credit for
that period shall be computed in accordance with actual service credit performed.

f.

The unit member must meet all eligibility conditions specified in EC 44922, and other
relevant sections, including but not limited to items 2a, 2b, and section 3.

4. Benefits:

a.

The unit member will be paid a salary which is the pro-rated share of the salary earned

ARTICLE VI
LEAVES

A. SICK LEAVE

1.    Each full-time certificated unit member shall be allowed sick leave as provided in the Education
Code, plus one additional sick leave day. Any days not used shall be accumulated by the unit
member for use, if necessary, during the succeeding years. All unit members are covered by
Workers’ Compensation Insurance for injury in line of duty.

2.    Unit members contracted for work days before and/or after the regular work year will receive
additional sick leave on a pro-rata basis in increments of one-half (1/2) sick leave for each ten
(10) working days. This provision excludes all summer school unit members.

3.    Upon reasonable cause, the District may require doctor’s certification (or proof of treatment
and the need therefore by the practice of any well-recognized church or denomination) before
allowing pay for absence due to accident or illness.

4.    Any certificated unit member who has unused sick leave for accident or illness accumulated in
another California school district at the time he/she is employed in the District and such
employment occurs within one (1) year of his/her previous employment, shall be credited in
this District with the accumulated day(s) upon verification of the day(s) by the former school
district.

5.    Catastrophic Leave Program (Refer to Appendix F)

The purpose of the Catastrophic Leave Program is to permit employees to donate to a
Catastrophic Leave Bank that will be used to support an employee when that employee, or
a member of his or her immediate family, suffers from a catastrophic illness or injury, and
the employee has exhausted all sick leave and other paid leave options.

Catastrophic illness or injury means an illness or injury that is expected to incapacitate the
employee for an extended period of time, hereby defined as not less than ten (10)
consecutive duty days, or that incapacitates a member of the employee’s immediate family
that requires the employee to take time off from work for an extended period of time [ten
(10) consecutive days] to care for that family member.

Participants who have exhausted regular sick leave, but still have differential leave
available to them, shall be eligible for the Catastrophic Leave Program credits. The District
shall pay the participant full pay, and the credits shall be charged at one-half (1/2) day of
sick leave for each day of absence.

For purposes of this program, immediate family means spouse, domestic partner, or child.
Requests to use catastrophic leave for a family member other than the employee’s
immediate family will be submitted for consideration to the Superintendent and Association
President. If the Superintendent and Association President mutually agree to either
approve or disapprove the request, the matter is settled. Whenever the Superintendent and
Association President disagree, the matter will be referred to the Board of Education for a
final decision.

a. Donations to the Catastrophic Leave Bank

The District shall establish a Catastrophic Leave Bank to which all unit members
may donate one (1) day of their earned and unused sick leave days. This donation
shall be irrevocable, and shall be accomplished by the unit member filing a
Catastrophic Leave Bank: Sick Leave Donation Form (see Appendix F) prior to
October 1 of the school year or within thirty (30) days of employment. This process
shall be administered by the Fluman Resources Office. This form shall clearly state
that sick leave days being donated are irrevocably given to the Catastrophic Leave
Bank and cannot be rescinded for any reason whatsoever. A donation to the
Catastrophic Leave Bank shall be a general donation and shall not be donated to a
specific employee for his or her exclusive use. Participation is voluntary, but
requires a donation to the Bank. Only donors will be permitted to withdraw from the
Bank. Unit members who elect not to join the Catastrophic Leave Bank upon first
becoming eligible must wait until the next open enrollment period and are not
eligible to withdraw from the Bank until they are enrolled.

The annual rate of donation by each participating unit member for each school year
shall be limited to one (1) day of sick leave. Part-time unit members may contribute
and use catastrophic leave on a pro-rata basis. Donations will be accepted during a
thirty (30) day period beginning September 1 of each school year. The donation
period will not occur in any year in which the Bank contains ninety (90) or more
days. For employees who did not donate during the initial enrollment period, the
Bank will reopen every three (3) years. Existing participants will not be asked to
donate unless the Bank has fallen below ninety (90) days. New employees will be
offered the opportunity to participate in the Catastrophic Leave Program regardless
of the number of days in the Bank. At any time that the Bank is in danger of not
being able to meet the demand, the Superintendent and Association President may
call for a special donation period. Participating unit members shall make a donation
each time a donation period is opened until such time that a unit member notifies
the Fluman Resources Office in writing of his or her desire to cancel participation.
The unit member shall not be eligible to draw from the bank as of the effective date
of the cancellation. Any participating unit member who does not have earned and

unused sick leave available when a donation period occurs shall be allowed to
continue in the program until he or she has earned additional sick leave. At that
point the donation will be automatically deducted from the unit member’s accrued
sick leave.

b. Withdrawals from the Bank

Catastrophic Leave Program participants who are in danger of exhausting their sick
leave may apply to withdraw from the Bank for catastrophic illness or injury (see
Catastrophic Leave Bank: Request Form; Appendix F). Unit members must use all
sick leave, but not differential leave, available to them before being eligible for a
withdrawal from the Bank. Participants who are approved and who have exhausted
all accrued sick leave are entitled to supplement their differential leave with
Catastrophic Leave charged at one-half day increments. Withdrawals from the
Catastrophic Leave Bank shall be granted in units of not more than thirty (30) days.
Unit members may submit requests for up to three (3) extensions or withdrawals as
their prior grants expire. A Unit member’s withdrawal from the Bank may not exceed
a maximum of one hundred and twenty (120) days for the same injury or illness.

Requests for withdrawals shall be submitted to the Superintendent. The
superintendent or designee may then grant the withdrawal, provided that the unit
member meets the eligibility criteria established in the Agreement. Unit members
who apply to withdraw or extend their withdrawal from the Catastrophic Leave Bank
will be required to submit a doctor’s statement indicating the nature of the illness or
injury and the probable length of absence from work.

Catastrophic Leave credits shall not be used for illness or disability which qualifies
the unit member for worker’s compensation or disability benefits. Unit members do
not accrue sick leave while using catastrophic leave credits.

If the Catastrophic Leave Bank does not have sufficient days to fund a withdrawal
request, the District is under no obligation to pay the participant any funds
whatsoever. If a request for withdrawal is denied because of insufficient days to
fund the request, the District shall notify the unit member, in writing, of the reason
for the denial.

Participants who are denied a withdrawal or whose withdrawal is not renewed, or
who are terminated from the Bank, may, within ten (10) days of denial, appeal in
writing to the Board of Education. The Board shall hold a hearing in closed session
within fifteen (15) workdays and issue a confidential written decision within fifteen
(15) workdays of the hearing. If the unit member’s incapacitation does not allow
participation in this appeal process, the unit member’s agent or member of the
immediate family may attend the hearing. Approval or denial of catastrophic leave
requests shall be final and not subject to grievance.

Personal Necessity

Up to seven (7) days of earned sick leave per year may be used for reasons of “Personal
Necessity” as defined below. Personal Necessity leave may be utilized for family or personal
emergencies, or special situations, when the member’s presence is urgently required, as
opposed to situations where the particular matter could be taken care of during non-school
hours. Such situations are those that could not be avoided by a prudent person through

planning. Something that would be easier to do, but not necessary to do during the normal
workday, would not be considered Personal Necessity. The member shall not be required to
explain the reason(s) for which said leave will be used on the Certificated Leave Request
Form. Verbal verification may be required from the unit member by his/her immediate
supervisor. The form will be signed by the member’s immediate supervisor and forwarded to
the superintendent or his/her designee. The term “Personal Necessity” shall include the
following situations:

a.    Serious illness or injury of a member of the unit member’s immediate family. For
purposes of this provision, immediate family means spouse, domestic partner, children,
parents, brothers, sisters, parents-in-law, sons and/or daughters-in-law, or
grandparents, grandchildren, or any relative living in the immediate household of the
employee.

b.    An accident involving the member’s personal property or that of his/her immediate
family.

c.    An appearance in court as a witness    or litigant.

d.    Child-rearing issues.

e.    Other situations where the member’s    presence is urgently required.

f.    Personally Compelling

Up to two (2) days per year of the seven (7) Personal Necessity days may be used for
other personal reasons deemed by the member as Personally Compelling.

1.    The member must request and receive written approval 2 weeks in
advance from his/her supervisor.

2.    Personally Compelling days may not be used for recreation, vacation, or
hobbies. Regarding hobbies, an exception may be allowed to enable a
certificated unit member to participate in a significant event that cannot
otherwise be accommodated during a school day. This is intended to be
an exceptional request and is not intended to allow repeated participation
in such events.

3.    Personally Compelling days may not be used for earning extra income.

4.    Lesson plans for the substitute teacher must be in place two (2) days
ahead of the leave.

g. Under circumstances of catastrophic illness or injury of a family member (as

defined in Article VI,A,5) unit members may request to access more than seven (7) days
of earned sick leave per year. Requests will be submitted for consideration to the
Superintendent and Association President. If the Superintendent and Association
President mutually agree to either approve or disapprove the request, the matter is
settled. Whenever the Superintendent and Association President disagree, the matter
will be referred to the Board of Education for a final decision.

B. EMERGENCY LEAVE

A certificated unit member may be granted by the principal or site administrator an emergency leave
of up to three (3) days each school year with full pay when serious illness or accident happens to a
member of the immediate family or necessary surgery for such a member is performed and the
surgeon requests the presence of the unit member.

Emergency leaves granted under this provision are not cumulative from year to year, and this leave is
not to be subtracted from sick leave. The District may require verification by the attending physician.

C. BEREAVEMENT LEAVE

1.    Bereavement leave, without loss of pay, shall be granted up to three (3) days, five (5) days if
over three hundred (300) miles, in the event of death of a member of an immediate family.
Members of the immediate family shall include: the mother, father, grandmother, grandfather,
or grandchild of the unit member or the unit member’s spouse, son, son-in-law, daughter,
daughter-in-law, brother, or sister; or any relative living in the unit member’s immediate
household. (Education Code 44985, 45194).

2.    Bereavement leave to attend the funeral of a close family friend or relative, not a member of
the immediate family, may be granted for the time necessary to attend the funeral; up to one-
half (1/2) day if the funeral is on the Monterey Peninsula and up to one (1) full day if the funeral
is out of the immediate area.

D. JURY DUTY

The following provision shall prevail in the event a certificated unit member of the District is called to

serve as a juror in a court during a period in which the unit member is on duty:

normal work day.

2.    The unit member shall endorse and present to the District any warrants which represent
payments for jury duty.

3.    If the unit member is released from jury duty for the day prior to the close of the workday, the

assigned by the principal.

4.    If a substitute teacher has been hired to replace the teacher who is released early

from jury duty, the substitute shall be retained for the complete day to maintain the classroom
or accomplish other tasks as assigned by the principal.

1.    When an unit member is absent from his/her duties on account of illness or accident for a

2.    The sick leave, including accumulated sick leave, and the five (5) month period shall run

consecutively.

3. A unit member shall not be provided more than 1 five (5) month period per illness or accident.

member may take the balance of the five (5) month period in a subsequent school year.

K.    PART-TIME EMPLOYMENT - JOB SHARING PLAN

1.    Job sharing is a plan whereby two (2) certificated teachers of the District share the full
teaching responsibilities for one (1) identifiable full-time classroom position.

2.    Participation in job sharing requires four (4) full teaching years within the District within
the last seven (7) years.

3.    Before the job sharing plan can be implemented there must be mutual agreement among the
two (2) teachers, the building principal, District Superintendent, and the Board of Education.

4.    Any participant approved for and assigned to a job sharing plan shall become a part-time
teacher in the District.

5.    Faculty meeting attendance is required unless excused by the principal.

6.    The participating unit member's benefits (medical, dental, and vision, and sick leave credit)
shall be pro-rated.

7.    Salaries shall be computed in accordance with Article II, Section B.

8.    District and unit member contributions to STRS will be pro-rated.

9.    Salaries of the participants will be paid on a basis that is proportional to full-time service.

10.    Revision or termination of a job sharing plan will not modify or affect any vested unit member
rights acquired prior to or during participation in the program, except that a teacher
withdrawing from a job sharing plan or displaced if the plan is terminated by the Board, may be
re-assigned to a regular full-time position, if available. Such teacher(s) shall have preference
over temporary teachers only.

11.    If one of the teachers in the job sharing plan resigns, retires, dies, or goes on leave or returns
to other full-time employment, the remaining teacher may have the option of recruiting a
partner to be approved by the Superintendent or taking the position full-time.

12.    All other advantages pertaining to unit members not expressly modified within this

Article shall be retained under the terms of this Agreement and shall be made available to unit
members participating in job sharing plans.

13.    The agreement or contract for one-half (1/2) time service shall be executed by the member
and the employer, in writing, prior to the period of reduced service, before March 1 for the
beginning of the school year, or before October 15 for the beginning of the second half of the
school year. A unit member currently on the job sharing plan shall notify the District prior to
March 1 if he/she wishes to continue in the job sharing program for the following year.

L. EXCHANGE TEACHING LEAVE

1.    An exchange leave is a leave granted to permit a teacher to serve as an exchange
teacher in a foreign country or in any state, territory, or possession of the United States.

2.    Exchange leave of absence may be granted to certificated unit members who meet the
following requirements.

a.    Unit members must have permanent status in this District on the effective date of leave.

b.    Unit members must have rendered full-time service in a certificated position for at least
one (1) year immediately preceding the leave.

c.    The unit member must be a superior teacher and an excellent representative of the
United States and Carmel Unified School District.

3.    Request for exchange leave shall be made in writing and be presented to the building principal
for his/her review and recommendation. The request shall be submitted to the District Office
by April 1 of the year prior to the year for which the leave is requested.

4.    In leaves other than those arranged through the Federal Government, the District teacher must
ascertain from the other district its willingness to participate in the exchange. When such a
district is found, the CUSD unit member shall notify the District Office and send the necessary
papers to the other district. These papers shall be returned to the District Office on or before
May 1. When all arrangements have been approved, the contract will be negotiated by the
District Office.

5.    Applicants from CUSD will be evaluated by the Association and referred to the superintendent
and Board of Education.

6.    Applicants from other districts will be evaluated and approved by the superintendent and Board
of Education based on reference forms and other appropriate means, including personal
interview if possible.

7.    An Exchange Agreement must be signed by the unit members and the districts concerned.

8.    The exchange is for one (1) year unless extended for one (1) additional year by unanimous
consent of the Boards of Education and the unit members concerned.

9.    Compensation, including fringe benefits, shall be arranged according to one of the following

plans:

a.    Plan A - The CUSD unit member is paid by the other district and will receive the rate of
pay on the other district salary schedule which is nearest to the rate received in the
CUSD, but not more than the other district unit member would have received.

b.    Plan B - Applies to exchanges outside of California. The CUSD unit member is paid by
the CUSD at his/her regular rate. This plan is limited to exchanges with states or
countries where the other teacher will receive an adequate salary for living in this
community.

c.    Plan C - Applies to exchanges outside of California. The CUSD unit member is paid by
the other district at a negotiated rate. Since this plan allows the outside unit member to
be paid by the CUSD and to be placed on the District salary schedule, there are wider
opportunities for working out exchange agreements.

10.    Credit for service on exchange leave counts toward advancement on the salary schedule as if
such service were given in the District, provided that the unit member serves a sufficient
number of days in the exchange position to have been paid at least seventy-five percent (75%)
of the salary agreed to in the contract signed with the outside district. A notarized affidavit
shall be filed certifying this service.

11.    Service on an exchange leave grants credit toward retirement. If retirement contributions are
not deducted from compensation, the unit member must personally arrange for payment of
required contributions.

12.    One (1) year on an exchange leave counts toward the seven (7) year service required for
sabbatical leave.

13.    The CUSD unit member and the exchange unit member shall be covered by the existing health
and accident program.

14.    At the completion of any exchange, the CUSD unit member shall return to duty in the District
and shall serve full-time for at least two (2) consecutive years before being eligible for another
exchange teaching assignment.

15.    Before returning to service in the District, the Board may require the CUSD unit member to
submit to a physical examination (at the Board's expense). If so requested, the unit member
shall present evidence provided by a licensed physician as to his/her health and physical
fitness.

16.    A teacher may request the Association to review his/her case if a leave request is denied. The
Association may represent the unit member in an appeal action if it deems the case merits
further negotiations.

N.    INDUSTRIAL ACCIDENT OR INDUSTRIAL ILLNESS LEAVE

Industrial accident or industrial illness leave is granted to an individual as a result of a job-related

accident or illness and is in addition to regularly accrued sick leave.

1.    a. Allowable industrial accident or industrial illness leave with pay shall not exceed sixty

(60) working days in any one (1) fiscal year for the same accident or illness.

b. When an industrial accident or industrial illness occurs at a time when the full sixty (60)
days will overlap into the next fiscal year, the unit member shall be entitled to only that
amount remaining at the end of the fiscal year in which the injury occurred, for the same
accident or illness.

2.    Allowable industrial accident or industrial illness leave shall not be accumulative from year to
year.

3.    Industrial accident or industrial illness leave shall commence on the first day of
absence.

4.    Payment for wages lost on any day, when added to an award granted the unit member under
Workers' Compensation laws of this State, shall not exceed the unit member's actual wage if
he/she were on the job.

5.    Industrial accident or industrial illness leave will be reduced by one (1) day for each day of
authorized absence, regardless of a compensation award made under Workers'
Compensation.

6. Entitlement to this leave will be based upon finding that the disability has been due to industrial
accident or industrial illness. In such case, regular sick leave will not be deducted for absence
due to the industrial accident or industrial illness until the full sixty (60) days, if granted, has
been exhausted.

The Association may be granted up to fifteen (15) days of leave each school year, to utilize for local,

notification to the superintendent by the Association President. The Association shall reimburse the
District for the cost of substitutes, if substitutes are required.

Members of the certificated unit who hold the offices of president, vice-president, or president-elect, in
state or national organizations related to education may have paid leave to utilize for state or national
conferences or for conducting business pertinent to affairs of the educational organization. These
representatives may be excused from school duties upon two (2) days advance notification to the
superintendent by the Association President. The length of the leave is to be determined
cooperatively by the superintendent and the unit member. Each such officer shall be limited to four

(4) days of leave per year for these purposes.

Q. RELEASE TIME FOR DISTRICT PURPOSE

Certificated unit members may be released from duty at no loss in pay to attend professional
meetings, appear in court in behalf of the District, or participate in other professional or educational
activities that will benefit the Carmel Unified School District.

All such paid leaves shall be approved by the Superintendent. Such release time shall be kept to a
minimum for each individual so that the instructional program of the District will be disrupted as little
as possible. Generally, such leaves will be limited to no more than three (3) days per year for each
unit member.

ARTICLE XI
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A.    PURPOSE

1.    This grievance procedure shall be used to process and resolve grievances arising under this
Agreement.

2.    The purpose of this procedure is to provide an orderly procedure for reviewing and resolving
grievances promptly.

B.    DEFINITIONS

1.    A "grievance" is an alleged violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of the express terms
of the Agreement, which directly and adversely affect the grievant. Actions to challenge or
change the terms of this Agreement shall not be considered a grievance. Matters for which a
specific method of review is provided by law or by the terms of this Agreement are not within
the scope of this procedure.

2.    A "grievant" is a member (or members) of the association.

3.    The "immediate supervisor" means the administrator who assigns, reviews and directs the
work of the grievant.

4.    A "day" is any day in which the District Office is open for business.

C.    TIME LIMITS

1. Each party involved in a grievance shall act quickly so that the grievance may be resolved
promptly. Every effort should be made to complete action within the time limits contained in

the grievance procedure, but, with the written consent of both parties, the time limitation for
any step may be extended.

2. In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be processed through all the steps
in this grievance procedure by the end of the school term and, if left unresolved until the
beginning of the following school year, could result in harm to an aggrieved person, the time
limits set forth herein shall be reduced so that the procedure shall be exhausted prior to the
end of the school term or as soon thereafter as is practicable.

D.    INFORMAL DISCUSSION

1. Initially, the grievant shall meet with his/her immediate supervisor and attempt to resolve the
grievance informally.

E.    LEVEL I

1.    If the informal discussion does not resolve the grievance to the satisfaction of the grievant, a
formal grievance may be initiated. A formal grievance may be initiated no later than ten (10)
days after the grievant knew of, or with the exercise of due diligence would have known of, the
event or circumstances occasioning the grievance.

2.    A formal grievance shall be initiated in writing, on forms prescribed by the District, and shall be
filed with the immediate supervisor/principal. (Refer to Appendix H)

3.    Within ten (10) days after the filing of the formal grievance, the immediate supervisor/principal
shall investigate the grievance and give his/her decision, in writing, to the grievant. The
grievant may file a copy of the grievance with the Association.

F.    LEVEL II

1.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision rendered at Level I, he/she may appeal the
decision within ten (10) days to the superintendent or his/her designee. The grievant may file
a copy of the appeal with the Association.

2.    The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance, the decision rendered at Level I, and
a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

3.    Within ten (10) days, the superintendent or his/her designee shall investigate the grievance
and give his/her decision, in writing, to the grievant.

G.    LEVEL III

1.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision rendered pursuant to Level II, the Association
may submit a request in writing to the superintendent for arbitration of the dispute.

2.    Said request shall be made within five (5) days.

3.    Upon receipt of the written request, the superintendent shall request the American Arbitration
Association to supply a panel of five (5) names. A copy of this request shall be sent to the
grievant and the Association. Within three (3) days of the receipt of the panel of five (5)
names, the superintendent and the grievant shall either mutually agree upon an arbitrator or
notify the American Arbitration Association to select an arbitrator in accordance with its rules.

4.    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator and a court reporter, if required by the arbitrator, shall
be shared equally. Any additional expenses shall be borne by the party incurring such
expense.

5.    The rules of the American Arbitration Association shall govern the arbitration with the exception
stated within this Agreement. The arbitrator shall have no authority to add, delete, or alter any
provisions of this Agreement, but shall limit his/her decision to the application and interpretation
of its provisions.

6.    Within ten (10) days of selection, provided an arbitrator is available, the arbitrator shall conduct
a hearing and submit his/her findings and award in writing to the Board of Education, the
grievant, and the Association. The award of the arbitrator shall be binding on the grievant, the
Association, and the District. The award of the arbitrator may, on petition of either the Board of
Education or the grievant and the Association, be reviewed by a court of competent jurisdiction
in the same manner as a decision made by a hearing officer under Chapter 5 (commencing with
Section 11500) of Part 1 of Division 3 of Title 2 of the Government Code. The court, on review,
shall determine whether there was sufficient evidence to support the arbitrator's findings and
award. The reviewing court may not exercise its independent judgment on the evidence.

ARTICLE XII
REASSIGNMENT AND TRANSFER

A.    For purpose of this provision:

1.    Reassignment is the change of a unit member's position within the same school or facility.

2.    Transfer is defined as the change of a unit member's position from one school/facility to
another school/facility.

3.    The term vacancy shall include a new position and an opening arising from resignation,
retirement, leave, transfer, release, or reassignment.

4.    School day shall be defined as a teacher duty day.

B.    Assignments/Reassignments

1.    Assignments/Reassignments shall not be made arbitrarily, capriciously or without good cause.

2.    A certificated unit member who agrees to teach a subject which is outside of the scope of
his/her credential or major or minor areas shall be provided with all reasonable support, which
might include release time. In such cases, the agreement shall be made as early as possible.
A copy of any text previously used, when available, and any course outline and other materials
that are in the possessions of the administration will be provided to the certificated unit
member. Evaluations of the certificated unit member should be intended to assist him/her
overcome any problems.

3.    Special consideration shall be given to certificated unit members who are assigned to more
than one school to minimize travel time and insure an adequate amount of time for lunch and
conference period (if applicable).

4.    The administration may reassign a certificated unit member within the same school or facility.

Certificated unit members shall have the right to request and receive, in writing, the reasons for

the reassignment.

5.    If it becomes necessary for the principal to reassign a certificated unit member, he/she shall

meet with the certificated unit member at least three (3) school days prior to the reassignment

unless the certificated unit member agrees to be reassigned in a shorter period of time.

C. Transfers

1.    General Provisions:

a.    Transfers shall not be made arbitrarily, capriciously or without good cause.

b.    Certificated unit members involved in transfers after the opening of school shall be
given written notice not less than three (3) school days prior to such transfer unless the
certificated unit member agrees to the transfer in a shorter period of time.

c.    The certificated unit member shall be relieved of all teaching duties on at least one (1)
of these days for the purpose of planning, observing, moving and orientation to the new
position. The principal shall make every attempt to provide additional materials,
equipment, and secretarial help to produce a good learning situation.

2.    Voluntary Transfers:

a.    Vacancies in teaching positions that arise during the school term shall be announced by
the District Office by posting a notice at each school. The notice of vacancy shall be
distributed by the District Office prior to the effective application date that shall be noted
clearly on each notice of vacancy and shall remain posted for no less than five (5)
school days. The school administrator shall be responsible for posting the vacancy
announcement in a conspicuous place in the school.

b.    This posting procedure shall be waived during the two (2) weeks prior to the opening of
school and the first twenty (20) school days of the school term; however, posting shall
occur at the District Office on the day of the vacancy and the following day.

c.    Applicants shall contact the District Office by telephone or in writing indicating their
interest in the vacancy prior to the stated application deadline.

d.    Before making a final decision, the school administration shall review the personnel file
of each applicant and shall provide an opportunity for an interview to the applicant. All
applicants shall be notified of the decision prior to announcing the successful candidate.

e.    Certificated unit members with specific interests in possible vacancies shall notify the
District Office of their interest in writing.

f.    Should a vacancy occur, the District Office shall make every reasonable effort to contact
the certificated unit members who have expressed an interest in a position. The effort
shall include mailing the vacancy notice to the interested unit member.

g.    If two (2) or more certificated unit members apply for the same vacancy, the principal
shall consider criteria such as the following, not necessarily in the order listed:

g.1. Student, school and district program needs which include, but are not limited to:
willingness and ability to teach a specific program, i.e., math, reading, remedial,
gifted, bilingual, etc., age, sex and ethnic balance of staff; willingness and ability
to work with an instructional aide; ability to teach in a specific classroom
management arrangement or to teach specific combinations.

g.2. Academic preparation.

g.3. Previous evaluations.

g.4. Appropriate certification.

g.5. Length of tenure in District.

h. Qualified certificated personnel within the District shall be given primary consideration,
including interview, for any opening prior to any interview of outside candidates. If not
selected for the vacated position, upon request, the unit member shall receive a written
statement of reasons for not being selected.

Involuntary Transfers

a.    An involuntary transfer is one that occurs due to reasons such as the following:

1.    Declining enrollment.

2.    Certificated personnel returning from a leave of absence or sabbatical leave
where no need exists at the home school.

3.    Special program needs such as willingness and ability to teach a specific
program, i.e., math, reading, cooperative teaching and specific student needs;
and

4.    Degree of compatibility with school program and personnel.

b.    The certificated unit member to be involuntarily transferred shall have a right to indicate
preferences and shall be given consideration prior to those applying for voluntary
transfers.

c.    Conditions for Involuntary Transfers:

1.    Notice of a possible involuntary transfer shall be given to the affected certificated
unit member not later than May 15th except where mutual agreement or good
cause exists, including but not limited to programming and scheduling changes
resulting from resignations, transfers, and retirements.

2.    An involuntary transfer shall take place only after a meeting or consultation (such
as written communication or telephone call) between the certificated unit member
and the principal has been held. At this meeting or consultation, the principal
shall inform the certificated unit member of his/her right to appeal to the
Superintendent or initiate the grievance procedure if the unit member determines
that the transfer policy has been violated.

3.    Upon request, a written statement of reasons for the involuntary transfer shall be
provided to the unit member.

4.    In all cases where two (2) or more teachers from one (1) school could fill the
need being met by the involuntary transfer, the determination of which unit
member is transferred shall be based upon the following, (not necessarily in the
order listed):

(1)    Length of tenure at the school

(2)    Evaluation

(3)    Academic preparation

(4)    Experience

(5)    Best interest of the District

D. RELOCATION PROCESS

The District recognizes that relocation from one classroom to another requires an extra time
commitment on the part of the teacher. Relocation occurs when a teacher is required to vacate a
workspace and re-establish that workspace in another location. Relocation may occur as a result of
a transfer, a reassignment, or the need to vacate a workspace. The District will make every attempt
to give timely notification of relocation. Once teachers are notified of their relocation, they will meet
with the site administrator to formulate a transition plan, including the compensation option stated
below. This plan will include a timeline, and the assistance and materials that the district will provide
for the move. Sufficient boxes for packing will be provided in a timely manner.

A. There are three options for compensation for relocation to be determined by the principal. Full
time teachers (1.0 FTE) will be entitled to:

For Elementary Level Teachers:

a.    Up to three (3) days of stipend at the hourly teacher rate; or

b.    Up to three (3) days of release time; or

c.    Or a combination of the above (whole days only)

For Secondary Level Teachers:

a.    Up to two (2) days of stipend at the hourly teacher rate; or

b.    Up to two (2) days of release time; or

c.    Or a combination of the above (whole days only)

d.    An allowance of up to one additional day may be made by the principal for classrooms
that require an extraordinary amount of packing

B.    Part-time teachers will receive the percentage of the selected option that corresponds to the
percentage of full time equivalency.

C.    Principals will select one of the above-listed compensation options at least two weeks prior to
the scheduled move date by noting it on the Pre-Move Designation form.

D.    If a teacher chooses not to pack, he/she must notify the District via the pre-Move Designation
form no later than two weeks before the scheduled move. In such cases, District personnel will
pack the classroom materials. Teachers who do not pack their own classroom materials will
not be eligible for stipends or release time related to the move.

E. Teachers must get their principal’s prior approval when scheduling their release day(s). When
deciding whether to approve a requested release day, the principal will communicate with the
Personnel Office to determine if there is adequate substitute availability.

ARTICLE VI - Leaves Section A5:

5. Catastrophic Leave Program

The purpose of the catastrophic leave program is to permit employees to donate to a sick leave bank that will he used to
support an employee when that employee, or a member of his or her immediate family, suffers from a catastrophic
illness or injury, and the employee has exhausted all sick leave and other paid leave options.

Catastrophic illness or injury means an illness or injury that is expected to incapacitate the employee for an extended
period of time, hereby defined as not less than ten (10) consecutive duty days, or that incapacitates a member of the
employee’s immediate family that requires the employee to take time off from work for an extended period of time [ten
(10) consecutive days] to care for that family member.

Participants who have exhausted regular sick leave, but still have differential leave available to them, shall be eligible
for the catastrophic leave program credits. The District shall pay the participant full pay, and the credits shall be
charged at one-half (1/2) day of sick leave for each day of absence.

For the purposes of this program, immediate family means spouse, domestic partner, or child. Requests to use
catastrophic leave for a family member other than the employee’s immediate family will be submitted for consideration
to the Superintendent and Association President If the Superintendent and Association President mutually agree to
either approve or disapprove the request, the matter is settled. Whenever the Superintendent and Association President

disagree, the matter will be referred to the Board of Education for a final decision.

a.    Donations to the Sick Leave Bank

The District shall establish a Sick Leave Bank to which all imit members may donate one (1) day of their
earned and unused sick leave days. This donation shall be irrevocable, and shall be accomplished by the unit
member filing a Sick Leave Donation Form (see Appendix F) prior to October 1 of the school year or within
thirty (30) days of employment. This process shall be administered by the Fluman Resources Office. This
form shall clearly state that sick leave days being donated are irrevocably given to the Sick Leave Bank and
cannot be rescinded for any reason whatsoever. A donation to the Sick Leave Bank shall be a general
donation and shall not be donated to a specific employee for his or her exclusive use.

b.    Withdrawals from the Bank

Catastrophic Leave Program participants who are in danger of exhausting their sick leave may apply to
withdraw from the Bank for catastrophic illness or injury (see Sick Leave Request Form; Appendix F). Unit
members must use all sick leave, but not differential leave, available to them before being eligible for a
withdrawal from the Bank. Participants who are approved and who have exhausted all accrued sick leave are
entitled to supplement their differential leave with Catastrophic Leave charged at one-half day increments.
Withdrawals from the Catastrophic Leave bank shall be granted in units of not more than thirty (30) days.

Unit members may submit requests for up to three (3) extensions or withdrawals as their prior grants expire.

A unit member’s withdrawal from the bank may not exceed a maximum of one hundred and twenty (120) days
for the same injury or illness.

Requests for withdrawals shall be submitted to the Superintendent. The Superintendent or designee may then
grant the withdrawal, provided that the imit member meets the eligibility criteria established in the Agreement.
Unit members who apply to withdraw or extend their withdrawal from the Catastrophic Leave Bank will be
required to submit a doctor’s statement indicating the nature of the illness or injury and the probable length of
absence from work.

workers’ compensation or disability benefits. Unit members do not accrue sick leave while, using catastrophic
leave credits.

If the Catastrophic I ,eave Bank does not have sufficient days to fund a withdrawal request, the District is under
no obligation to pay the participant any funds whatsoever. If a request for withdrawal is denied because of

CATASTROPHIC LEAVE BANK: SICK LEAVE DONATION FORM

The purpose of the catastrophic leave program is to permit employees to donate to a CATASTROPHIC
leave bank (CLB) that will be used to support an employee when that employee, or a member of his or
her immediate family, suffers from a catastrophic illness or injury, and the employee has exhausted all
sick leave and other paid leave options. The CLB, while supporting your colleagues, also acts like an
insurance policy for you, in case of unforeseen events.

Donated Sick leave days are irrevocably given to the Catastrophic Leave Bank and cannot be
rescinded for any reason whatsoever. A donation to the Catastrophic Leave Bank shall be a general
donation and shall not be donated to a specific employee for his or her exclusive use.

Only donors will be permitted to withdraw from the CLB. Unit members who elect not to join the
Catastrophic Leave Bank upon first becoming eligible must wait until the next open enrollment period
and are not eligible to withdraw from the Bank until they are enrolled.

Donations will be accepted during a thirty (30) day period beginning September 1 of each school year.
The donation period will not occur in any year in which the Bank contains ninety (90) or more days.
For employees who did not donate during the initial enrollment period, the Bank will reopen every
three (3) years. Existing participants will not be asked to donate unless the Bank has fallen below
ninety (90) days.

Participating unit members shall make a donation each time a donation period is opened until such time
that a unit member notifies the Human Resources Office in writing of his or her desire to cancel
participation. The unit member shall not be eligible to draw from the bank as of the effective date of
the cancellation.
