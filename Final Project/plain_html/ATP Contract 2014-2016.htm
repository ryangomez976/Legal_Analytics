PREAMBLE

The following Articles and provisions constitute a bilateral and binding agreement between
Placer Union High School District Board of Trustees (“District”) and the Associated
Teachers of Placer/CTA/NEA (“Association”), an employee organization. It is the purpose
of this Agreement to achieve and maintain harmonious relations between the parties. 1

1

ARTICLE II

RECOGNITION

A.    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of that unit of
employees certified by the Educational Employment Relations Board in Case No. 25,
dated September 12, 1977, which states the appropriate unit as follows:

A bargaining unit consisting of all certificated employees including
teachers, counselors, librarians, psychologists, nurses, social workers, and
speech and language pathologists; but excluding management,
confidential and supervisory employees, adult school teachers and
summer school teachers.

B.    For purposes of this Agreement, the term “teacher” means any certificated employee in
the bargaining unit defined above.

2

ARTICLE III

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

A.    1. One (1) bulletin board will be set up in the staff lounge or staff dining room of

each school to be used exclusively by the Association. Items placed on the bulletin
board shall be dated and include the name of the person responsible.

2.    Teachers may use the District mail for Association business. The District mail
shall not be used for the transmittal of material which would libel or defame any
person.

3.    The District shall not be responsible for and shall be held harmless for any libelous
or illegal Association materials posted on bulletin boards or sent through the
District mail.

B.    Teachers may use school facilities, except for administrative offices, for Association
business if they fill out the required forms and the use does not conflict with official
school business.

C.    The Association may use school copy machines, typewriters, audiovisual equipment,
computers, technology hardware and software, and related materials under the
following conditions:

1.    Actual cost of such use shall be paid to the District.

2.    Teachers shall have precedence over Association use.

D.    1. Teachers shall not be contacted by Association representatives during the teacher’s

student contact hours.

2.    Association representatives not employed by the District must register in the
Principal’s office to contact teachers on the school premises.

3.    No teacher shall participate in Association business on campus during the teacher’s
student contact hours, except where provided elsewhere in this contract (Released
Time).

E.    The Association agrees to supply the District with a copy of the annual written financial
report required in Section 3546.5 of the Government code at the same time the report
is available to teachers.

F.    ATP shall file with the Superintendent and Personnel Office, on or about June 1 of
each school year, the following information:

1.    Name, mailing address, and telephone number of ATP Officers.

2.    Name, mailing address, and telephone number of ATP negotiation team members,
designating the chairperson.

G.    Any correspondence related to negotiations or this Agreement shall be directed to the
Superintendent and Director of Personnel with a copy provided to the Districts
negotiations representatives.

ARTICLE IV

CONSULTATION RIGHTS

A.    The Association has the right to consult on tire definition of educational objectives, the
determination of the content of courses and curriculum, and the selection of textbooks
to the extent such matters are within the discretion of the District under the law. The
Association may request consultation rights regarding existing or implemented
educational programs, governance, and improvements. The Association may request
timelines which are mutually agreeable with the site and district for the implementation
and conclusion of these evaluations and consultations. The Association may request
consultation rights regarding existing or implemented educational programs,
governance, and improvements. The Association may request timelines which are
mutually agreeable with the site and district for the implementation and conclusion of
these evaluations and consultations.

B.    If the Association desires to exercise this right to consult on the above-mentioned areas,
it will do so by requesting a meeting with the Superintendent. The Superintendent or

ARTICLE V

DISTRICT RIGHTS

A.    1. It is understood and agreed that the District retains all its powers and authority to

direct, manage, and control to the full extent of the law.

2.    Included in, but not limited to, those duties and powers are the exclusive right to:
determine its organization; direct the work of its employees; determine the times
and hours of operation; determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided,
and the methods and means of providing them; establish its educational policies,
goals, and objectives; ensure the rights and education opportunities of students;
determine staffing patterns; determine the number and kinds of personnel
required; maintain the efficiency of District operations; determine the curriculum;
build, move, or modify facilities; establish budget procedures and determine
budgetary allocation; determine the methods of raising revenue; contract out work;
and take action on any matter in die event of an emergency.

3.    In addition, the Board retains the right to hire, classify, assign, reassign, transfer,
evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline employees.

B.    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties, and responsibilities by
the District, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations, and practices in furtherance of,
and the use of judgment and discretion in connection with, shall be limited only by the
specific and express terms of this Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific
and express terms are in conformance widi law.

C.    The District retains its right to amend, modify, or rescind policies and practices referred
to in this Agreement in cases of emergency defined as natural disasters, acts of God, or
civil disorders which may result in danger to students and/or employees. This
provision shall not apply to acts covered under the Concerted Activities Article.
Exercise of District powers under this paragraph shall not continue beyond the period
of the emergency. The determination of whether or not an emergency exists is within
the discretion of the Board.

ARTICLE VIII

WORK DAY

A.    The work day for teachers, except nurses, counselors, psychologists, social workers, and
speech and language pathologists, shall be a continuous seven hours and fifteen minutes
(7:15), including a duty free lunch (except in emergency situations). A full-time teacher
shall be assigned a regular preparation period. The preparation period shall be the
equivalent of a regular class period. Extra duty assignments may extend the defined
work day. A teacher who agrees to teach a section during his/her assigned prep period
will be compensated at .17 of his/her placement on the salary schedule. Upon mutual
agreement of a permanent teacher and administrator, the continuous seven-hour-and-
fifteen-minute (7:15) work day requirement may be waived so that a teacher may work a
split shift to teach a course which will be part of the teacher’s regular work assignment.

B.    The work day for Instructional Coaches shall be a professional work day, including a
duty free lunch, except in emergency situations.

C.    The work day for nurses, counselors, psychologists, social workers, and speech and
language pathologists, shall be a continuous eight hour (8:00) period of time including a
duty free lunch, except in emergency situations.

D.    The lunch period shall be a minimum of thirty (30) minutes but shall not be less than
the lunch period provided for the students at the school.

E.    Teachers shall spend the work day on campus, except for lunch, unless excused by the
Principal or designee.

F.    1. It is recognized that teachers’ duties and responsibilities continue beyond the work
day. Principals shall distribute these duties and responsibilities as equally as practical
among all teachers.

2. Examples of professional duties for which teachers may be responsible include,
but are not limited to, the following:

a.    Club or class advisor

b.    Student supervision

c.    Reasonable number of inservice sessions scheduled at reasonable times

d.    Reasonable number of faculty meetings, department, or area meetings

e.    Parent conference

f.    Open House or after school activity

g.    Reasonable number of Committee Meetings

G.    Principals will establish the beginning time of each teacher’s work day.

9

H.    The Principal or immediate supervisor shall have the authority to excuse teachers earlier
than the time states; however, such authority is at the discretion of the administration
and shall not serve as precedent for any like request. Each request for an early dismissal
shall be individually considered by the administrator.

I.    The work day of part-time teachers shall fall within the designated seven hour and
fifteen minutes (7:15) work day established at each site.

J.    The work day of part-time nurses, counselors, psychologists, social workers, and speech
and language pathologists shall fall within the designated eight hour (8:00) workday at
each site.

K.    The work day of alternative education teachers may be designed by the Principal or
director to reflect an equivalent number of student contact minutes as instructors in the
comprehensive high schools.

ARTICLE X

WORK YEAR

A.    Work years for teachers, instructional coaches, and librarians, who do not have
extended year assignments, shall be 180 teaching days, plus one (1) work day, and three
(3) inservice days for a total of 184 days of service. At the conclusion of their
Instructional Coach assignment, the teacher shall return to their original work site.

B.    1. Work years for counselors, nurses, psychologists, social workers, and speech and

language pathologists, shall be 180 service days, plus one (1) work day, and three
(3) inservice days, plus an additional fifteen (15) days for a total of 199 days of
service.

The supervisor or principal of the counselors, nurses, psychologists, social workers,
or speech and language pathologists shall meet with these employee(s) to mutually
calendar the workdays beyond 184.

2. The one (1) work day shall be the day after school ends. Three (3) inservice days
shall be scheduled by fire District, but shall be contiguous to the established work
calendar. These three (3) days are funded by a State Grant and employees will be
required to sign in and sign out as a condition of the Grant.

C.    A snow day is a scheduled work day on which school will not be held because of snow
or storm related transportation problems. Teachers arc not expected to appear for
work if snow days are declared by the Principal or designee at each individual school
site. Any teacher work days lost will be made up by tire rescheduling of additional
teacher work days by the District.

D.    Days or time served on extra duty assignments shall not be counted as part of the
teacher’s regular work year.

E.    The Association and the District shall meet prior to December 15th of each school year
to establish the calendar for the following school year. 12

ARTICLE XI

CLASS SIZE

A.    The individual department/areas shall establish recommendations for class sizes for the
various classes in their department/area at that school. These recommendations shall
be submitted in writing by tire department/area chairperson by April 1 of each school
year.

B.    The Principal or designee will schedule classes in the best interests of the entire school
program. Consideration shall be given, but not limited to, the following scheduling
criteria:

1.    Student demand

2.    District graduation requirements

3.    Departmental recommended class sizes

4.    Budgetary limitations

5.    Facility capacity

6.    Safety

7.    Educational requirements of the class.

C.    Safety is a prime consideration of both teachers and the administration. When a
maximum class size is established, teachers may not increase the number of students
beyond that figure without written permission from the principal.

D.    During the first three (3) weeks of each semester the Principal shall adjust the teacher
loads in as equitable a manner as practical.

E.    Following this three (3) week period of time a teacher may request readjustment of
his/her teaching loads, if in his/her judgment and in light of the above criteria, the sizes
of his/her classes are unreasonable. The request will be in writing and shall specify the
problem and the requested adjustment. The principal or designee shall review the
teacher’s request and within five (5) teaching days make any adjustments which he/she
feels are appropriate.

F.    If the teacher does not find the Principal or designee’s response satisfactory, he/she
may request a meeting with the Superintendent or designee to discuss the matter.
Within ten (10) teaching days the Superintendent or designee shall meet with the
teacher, review the facts surrounding the complaint, and make a disposition of the
complaint.

G.    If the teacher finds the Superintendent or designee’s disposition unacceptable, he/she
may request in writing that the complaint be appealed to the Board. The issue shall be
placed on the agenda of the next regular Board meeting and shall be considered by the
Board at that meeting. The Board shall make final disposition of the complaint, and
notify the principal and teacher of its decision.

H.    Release periods increase the overall class size ratio. These periods also provide benefit
to the entire school community. The District will consult with tire Association prior to
increasing beyond the current four (4) release periods at each site. 14

ARTICLE XIII

EVALUATION

A. California Standards for the Teaching Profession and Stull Act Evaluation Procedure to
Assess Employee Performance

1.    The District shall evaluate and assess employee competency as it reasonably relates
to:

a.    The California Standards for the Teaching Profession

(1)    Engaging and supporting all students in learning

(2)    Creating and maintaining effective environments for student learning

(3)    Understanding and organizing subject matter for student learning

(4)    Planning instruction and designing learning experiences for all students

(5)    Assessing student learning

(6)    Developing as a professional educator.

b.    The progress of pupils toward the standards of expected student achievement
at each grade level in each area of study.

c.    The employee’s adherence to curricular objectives.

d.    Additional evaluation and assessment guidelines or criteria related to an
employee’s extra duty assignments.

2.    The evaluation and assessment of employee performance pursuant to the
evaluation procedure shall not include the use of publisher’s norms established by
standardized tests.

3.    The evaluation procedure shall consist of the following:

a.    Self evaluation:    Teachers    shall    complete    a    written    self-evaluation on the

approved form prior to the summary evaluation. This self-evaluation shall
include measurable student achievement data that will be monitored by the
teacher and their evaluator and will be included in the summary evaluation.

b.    Formal Observation:    A    documented    observation    of    at least fifty minutes

unless mutually agreed otherwise. This may include a conference before and
after the observation.

c.    Informal Observation: An unannounced observation. 16

16

d.    Summary Evaluation: A formal written evaluation which reflects the self-
evaluation, the formal observation, and any informal observations.

e.    A committee of stakeholders (teachers and administrators) shall form a
committee and meet in SY 13-14 to restructure the current teacher evaluation
to include student achievement indicators. The PUHSD Board of Trustees
shall have final approval of the committee’s recommendation.

4.    Times of Evaluation

a.    Every probationary employee shall be observed formally at least twice and
receive a written evaluation once each year.

b.    Employees shall be evaluated on the following schedule: fourth (4,h), sixth
(6th), ninth (9th), twelfth (12th), seventeenth (17th) years, and every five years
thereafter. Evaluations shall be completed no later than 30 days before the
last school day of the year in which the evaluation takes place. Additional
evaluations may take place as deemed necessary by the Superintendent or
assigned evaluator in cases where the employee’s last evaluation was marked
“Needs Improvement” or “Unsatisfactory,” the employee is in a different
teaching assignment or teaching at a different school, the employee requests
an additional evaluation, or other circumstances reasonably indicate that a
specific employee should receive an additional evaluation.

c.    Assigned evaluators shall be administrators. Instructional Coaches are not
administrators and are not involved in the evaluation process. A teacher may
request an evaluator different than the assigned evaluator. A request for an
evaluator change will be honored only once per year unless mutually agreed
otherwise.

5.    Observations and Conferences

a.    The immediate supervisor, or other assigned administrator, shall normally
conduct observations and evaluations.

b.    Each formal observation will include:

(1)    The immediate supervisor, or other assigned administrator, shall normally
conduct observations and evaluations.

(2)    The formal classroom observation

(3)    A written observation report within five (5) work days

(4)    A post-observation conference

c.    A conference may be requested by tire evaluator or by the employee.

d.    A Summary Evaluation Conference shall be held after the last formal
evaluation including a review of:

(1)    The self-evaluation

(2)    Formal observation(s)

17

(3)    Informal observation(s)

(4)    California Standards for the Teaching Professional and Stull Act
requirements

e. A rebuttal to an observation report, a report of a conference, or the summary
evaluation may be made by the employee within ten (10) working days after
receive of said report.

6.    Unsatisfactory Evaluations

a.    Definition of Unsatisfactory Evaluation:

Persistent teaching practices or methods which

(1)    Fail to meet two or more of the standards with two or more deficiencies
in each standard, or significantly fails to meet any one standard; or

(2)    Fail to adhere to curricular objectives; or

(3)    Fail to help pupils progress toward the standards of expected student
achievement at each grade level in each area of study.

b.    Any employee who receives a “Needs Improvement” or “Unsatisfactory”
rating on an evaluation report, upon written request within ten (10) working
days, shall be entitled to one subsequent observation, conference, and written
evaluation. During the ten (10) working day period, the evaluation and
supporting documents shall not be placed in tire employee’s permanent file.
If written request is made for an additional evaluation and such additional
evaluation does not indicate a “Needs Improvement” or “Unsatisfactory,” the
first evaluation and supporting document shall not be placed in the
employee’s permanent file.

c.    When any permanent certificated employee has received an unsatisfactory
evaluation, the District shall annually evaluate the employee until the
employee achieves a satisfactory evaluation or is separated from the District.

d.    An unsatisfactory evaluation of an employee’s performance in the area of
teaching methods or instruction may include additional requirements that the
employee shall, as determined necessary by the District, participate in a
program designed to improve appropriate areas of the employee’s
performance and to further pupil achievement and the instructional objectives
of die District. This may include the assistance of an Instructional Coach. If
an employee is required to participate in such a program, the program shall
relate to the unsatisfactory rating and shall not require costs to the employee,
unless agreed to by the employee.

7.    Guidelines

a. The District shall adopt or change evaluation forms only after consultation
with the Association.

b.    Observation forms/notes shall not be kept or included in an employee’s file
following the summary evaluation.

c.    The District may conduct additional evaluations, observations, or assessments
when deemed necessary.

d.    The Evaluation of members of the unit, except for the alleged violation of
procedural matters, shall not be subject to the grievance procedure. The
content of observations and evaluations is specifically excluded from the
grievance procedure.

e.    The employee may request Association representation at any level of the
evaluation process. 19

ARTICLE XVI

TRANSFER PROCEDURE

A.    Transfer shall mean the change of a teacher’s work site from one (1) school campus to
another, or to another bargaining unit position requiring a different credential.

B.    Procedure for Teacher-Initiated Transfers:

1.    Teacher-initiated requests shall be submitted in writing in letter form to the
Assistant Superintendent of Human Resources with copies to the teacher’s present
Principal or supervisor, and to the Principal or supervisor of the work site to which
the transfer is requested.

2.    Requests shall be made prior to March 1, or within five (5) working days of the
notice of an opening. Notice of opening will be emailed to ATP members to their
PUHSD email accounts.

3.    A teacher requesting a transfer shall receive written acknowledgment of his/her
request from the District Office.

4.    If a teacher initiates a transfer request for a vacant position for which they are
properly credentialed, that request shall be given priority consideration by the
Principal or other administrator malting the selection. Priority considerations shall
mean:

a.    The District shall advertise openings inside and outside the District for a
minimum of ten (10) working days.

b.    Qualified teachers requesting a transfer shall be interviewed and shall be given
first choice of interview time before outside applicants are scheduled.

c.    If no teacher requesting a transfer within the District is selected, then the
Principal or administrator making the decision will notify the requesting
teacher prior to offering the position to someone from outside the District.

ARTICLE XIX

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A. Definitions:

1.    a. A “grievance” is a written allegation by one (1) or more teachers that there has

been a violation or misapplication of a provision of this Agreement.
Implementation of any of the provisions of this Agreement in an arbitrary
manner shall be interpreted as a violation and may be grieved.

b.    An “association grievance” is a written allegation by the Association that there
has been a violation of a provision of this Agreement which direcdy affects
the operation of the Association as the exclusive representative of the
bargaining unit.

c.    It is not the intent of this procedure to provide a means of changing or adding
District policies, District regulations, or provisions to this Agreement

d.    Actions to challenge or change the general policies of the District, as set forth
in the rules and regulations or administrative regulations and procedures, must
be undertaken under separate legal process.

e.    Other employer-employee relation matters for which specific method of
review is provided by law are not within the scope of this procedure.

2.    A “complaint” is an unwritten claim referred to in paragraph one (1).

3.    A “complainant” is a person, group of persons, the Association (for Association
grievance only), or its representatives presenting a complaint prior to the formal
steps of grievance.

4.    A “grievant” is the teacher(s) or the Association of its representative, making or
presenting the grievance.

5.    The “immediate supervisor” is the lowest level administrator having immediate
jurisdiction over the grievance and being designated to adjust grievances.

6.    A list of District management employees shall be supplied to die Association at the
beginning of the school year. A list of Association officers shall be supplied to the
District at the beginning of the school year.

7.    “Working day” means a day when die central office of the District is open for
business, except for the normal mid-winter vacation and spring vacation.

8.    For die purposes of time limits, each day that a grievant is on an approved leave
will toll the time limits by one (1) day, except that the time limits shall not be tolled
above Level 1 for the time when the teacher or responding administrator is on
illness leave or other unforeseen leave of short duration.

33

B. Level 1:    Informal    Resolution

1.    The purpose of this level in the procedure is to realize, at the lowest level, equitable
solution or clear resolution to problems which may arise.

2.    Within ten (10) working days after the complainant knew, or should have known
of the act or omission giving rise to the complaint, the complainant shall request in
writing a meeting with tire immediate supervisor. The request shall specify the date
of the request and an indication of die topic. The complainant and the immediate
supervisor shall meet informally widiin five (5) working days of die request.

3.    Neither the complainant nor the immediate supervisor shall be allowed other
persons at the meeting as representatives, counselor, or witnesses at this level.

4.    No records, notes, quotations or comments, or allusions to meanings expressed by
either party shall be allowed to be considered at any other level of grievance, or by
courts, if the grievance concerns a provision of the law. Only the dates and topic
of the informal meetings will be allowed as evidence.

5.    Discussion of the complaint at this level need not be related to specific provisions
allowing a grievance under the definition. The problem should be discussed freely.

6.    Failure to resolve the grievance at this level has no effect upon the grievability of
the complaint.

C. Level 2:    first    Step    to Formal Resolution

1.    If there is no resolution at Level 1 and one or both of the parties deem it
necessary, die grievant may file a written Request for Settlement of Grievance with
the immediate supervisor or his/her representative within fifteen (15) working days
after the grievant knew or should have known of the act or omission giving rise to
the grievance.-

2.    This time limit may be extended by written mutual agreement.

3.    The request shall be prepared in triplicate with one (1) copy for the immediate
supervisor, one (1) for the grievant, and one (1) for the Association.

4.    The Request for Settlement of Grievance shall include:

a.    A description of the specific grounds for the grievance including names, date,
and places.

b.    A specific settlement or remedy requested.

5.    The immediate supervisor or his/her representative shall meet with the grievant
within five (5) working days of receipt of die Request to discuss die grievance.

6.    Both parties will be allowed other persons as representatives, counsel, or witnesses
at this level or any other level of the formal grievance procedure.

34

7. The immediate supervisor or his/her representative shall submit a written
disposition of grievance to the grievant within five (5) working days of this
meeting.

D.    Level 3:    Formal    Appeal to Superintendent

1.    If there is no resolution at Level 2 and one or both of the parties deem it necessary,
the grievant may file a Grievance Appeal to the Superintendent within five (5)
working days of receipt of the immediate supervisor’s disposition at Level 2.

2.    The Grievance Appeal shall be prepared in triplicate with one (1) copy for the
Superintendent, one (1) for the grievant, and one (1) for the Association.

3.    The Superintendent or designee shall meet widi the grievant within five (5)
working days of the receipt of the Request in an effort to resolve the grievance.

4.    A grievant may request the superintendent in cases where a designee would
normally hear the appeal only with the stipulation that tire grievant thereby waives
the usual five (5) working days time limit for a conference. If a grievant chooses to
exercise this option, the request shall be made in writing with a statement that the
five (5) day limit has been waived. Then the Superintendent shall meet within
fifteen (15) working days of the receipt of the request.

5.    The Superintendent or designee shall make a written disposition of the grievance
within five (5) working days after such meeting by completing a Reply to
Grievance - Level 3. The Principal and the grievant shall be notified in writing of
said disposition.

E.    Level 4:    Submission    to    Grievance Panel

1.    If there is no resolution at Level 3 and one or both parties deem it necessary, die
grievant may file a Submission to Grievance Panel widiin five (5) working days of
receipt of the Superintendent’s Reply to Grievance.

2.    The submission shall be prepared in triplicate with one (1) copy for the
administration, one (1) copy for die Association, and one (1) copy for the grievant.

3.    The Grievance Panel shall consist of two (2) members appointed by the
administration and two (2) members appointed by the Association.

4.    Widiin ten (10) working days of the request, the Panel shall meet to hear further
testimony or evidence, if necessary, to discuss die grievance and to render a
decision.

5.    Within five (5) working days of die meeting, the Panel shall make a written
disposition of the grievance. The Panel shall make disposition by:

a. a majority vote deciding in favor of one (1) of the parties, or

35

b.    a majority vote to make an adjustment as a remedy, or

c.    no majority vote on disposition, which shall leave the grievant free to choose
to submit the matter to the next level of grievance, or

d.    by majority vote the Panel may direct the parties to reconsider the grievance at
a particular level of grievance. All future appeals will then follow the
procedures from that specific level.

6. If the grievant finds the Panel’s decision unsatisfactory, the grievant may submit
the grievance to the next level of grievance. In such case, the grievant must bear
all costs of future grievance as an individual and neither tire Association nor the
District shall assume a share of these costs.

F. Level 5:    Formal    Submission    to    Advisory    Arbitration

1.    Grievances not being resolved in prior levels and reaching Level 5, subject to
provisions of Level 4, may be requested to be submitted to advisory arbitration

2.    Issues arising out of the exercise by the Board and administration of its

3.    The parties shall select a mutually acceptable Advisory Arbitrator. Should they be

4.    Each party shall individually prepare its case for submission to tire Arbitrator prior
to the date set for the hearing or the date set by the Advisory Arbitrator for receipt

5.    The Association shall be notified in writing if the arbitrability of the grievance is
challenged. If such a challenge is made, the question of arbitrability will be ruled

6.    The Advisory Arbitrator’s decision will be in writing and will set forth findings of

year including the number of hours of each such activity'. The professional
growth advisor shall certify those completed activities which are appropriate and
