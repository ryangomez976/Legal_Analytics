ARTICLE I
AGREEMENT

The articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and
binding agreement ("Agreement") by and between the Governing Board of
the Alta Vista Elementary School District and the Alta Vista Teachers
Association/California Teachers Association/National Education Association
(AVTA/CTA/NEA).

This Agreement is entered into pursuant to the Educational Employment
Relations Act (“Act”), Chapter 10.7, 3540 through 3549 of the California
Government Code.”

This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2012 until
June 30, 2015, or until its amendment by an instrument in writing duly
executed by both parties.

ARTICLE II
RECOGNITION

The Board recognizes the AVTA/CTA/NEA as the exclusive representative of
all certificated employees, excluding management, confidential and
supervisory employees as defined in the Educational Employment Relations
Act for the purpose of meeting and negotiating.

Association with the placement of personnel on the respective salary
schedules.

negotiating team pursuant to the Education Employment Relations Act.

ARTICLE VIII
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

I.    Definitions:

1.    A "grievance" is a claim by the Association or by one or more
teachers that there has been a violation, misinterpretation or
misapplication of a provision of this Agreement.

2.    An "aggrieved person" is the person or persons, including the
Association or representatives thereof, making the claim(s).

3.    A "party in interest" is any person who might be required to take
action or against whom action might be taken in order to resolve the
claim.

II.    Purpose

1.    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible
administrative level, equitable solutions to the problems which may
arise from time to time affecting the welfare or working conditions of
teachers. Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as
informal and confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the
procedure.

2.    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any
teacher having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with any
appropriate member of the administration, and to have the grievance
adjusted without intervention by the Association, provided that the
adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement and
the Association has been given an opportunity to be present at such
adjustment and to state its views.

3.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as
possible, the time limits specified at each level should be considered to
be maximums and every effort should be made to expedite the
process. The time limits may, however, be extended by mutual
agreement.

4.    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be
processed through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end
of the school year and, if left unresolved until the beginning of the
following school year, could result in harm to the aggrieved person,
the time limits set forth herein will be reduced so that the procedure
may be exhausted prior to the end of the school year or as soon as is
practical.

III. Procedure

An aggrieved person will first discuss the possible grievance with the
appropriate principal or immediate supervisor within a reasonable amount
of time either directly or through the Association's designated Grievance
Representative with the objective of resolving the matter informally.

ARTICLE IX
PUBLIC CHARGES

Any citizen or parent complaint about a teacher shall be reported
immediately to the teacher in writing by the Alta Vista School District, and
shall include the specific complaint and the name of the complainant.

Should the involved teacher or the complainant believe that the allegations in
the complaint are sufficiently serious to warrant a meeting, the teacher shall
schedule a meeting with the complainant. The immediate supervisor shall be
present at said meeting. A written report of the meeting shall be filed with
the immediate supervisor.

If the matter is not resolved at the meeting to the satisfaction of the
complainant, he shall put his complaint into writing and submit the original
to the teacher with a copy to the Superintendent. The teacher shall be given
reasonable compensated release time for the purpose of responding to the
written complaint in writing, as well as, initialing and dating the written
response to such complaint, the original to the complainant, and a copy to the
Superintendent.

The written complaint and the attached response shall be placed in the
teacher's personnel file. If the teacher challenges the truth of the allegations
contained in the complaint, he/she may (lie a grievance on that basis, and a
finding to the effect that such allegations are untrue shall result in the
immediate destruction of the written complaint. The failure by the teacher
to file a grievance shall not be construed as an admission by the teacher or
the District that the allegations contained in the complaint are true.

The Board shall not dismiss or refuse to re-employ a teacher on the basis of
allegations in a citizen or parent complaint.

ARTICLE X
PERSONNEL FILES

1.    The Board shall not base any adverse action against a teacher upon materials
that are not contained in such teacher's personnel file. Moreover, the Board
shall not base any adverse action against a teacher upon materials which are
contained in such teacher's personnel file unless the materials had been
placed in the file at the time of the incident giving rise to such materials and
the teacher had been notified at such time that such materials were being
placed in the file.

2.    Unless otherwise agreed to by the involved teacher, a teacher's personnel file
shall not include ratings, reports, or records which 1) were obtained prior to
the employment of the teacher, 2) were prepared by identifiable examination
committee members, or 3) were obtained in connection with a promotional
examination.

3.    A teacher shall be provided any negative or derogatory material before it is
placed in his/her personnel file. He/she shall also be given an opportunity
during the school day and with compensated release time to initial and date
the material and to prepare a written response to such material. The written
response shall be attached to the material. If a teacher refuses to sign, even
though a signature or initial does not signify agreement, refusal will be
indicated on the material placed in the personnel file.

4.    Upon written authorization by the teacher, a representative of the
Association shall be permitted to examine and/or obtain copies of materials
in such teacher's personnel file.

5.    The person or persons who draft and/or place material in a teacher's
personnel file shall sign the material and signify the date on which such
material was drafted and placed in the file.

6.    The Board shall keep a log indicating the persons who have requested to
examine a personnel file as well as the dates such requests were made. Such
log shall be available for examination by the teacher, his/her Association
representative, or legal representative, if so authorized by the teacher.

7.    Access to personnel files shall be limited to the members of the District
administration on a need to know basis. Board members may request the
review of a teacher's file at a personnel session of the entire Board. The
contents of all personnel files shall be kept in the strictest confidence.

ARTICLE XI
HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

I. Work Day

1.    The teaching workday shall be scheduled between seven-forty (7:40)

A.M. and Three-twenty (3:20) P.M. The student day shall not begin
prior to 8:10 a.m. or go beyond 2:50 p.m. on a regular school day.
Kindergarten student day shall not go beyond 2:00 p.m. on a regular
school day. Except for one (1) day a week, teachers shall be given at
least thirty (30) minutes of individual preparation time. Teachers
may sign out early on days in which they are enrolled in education
classes, for medical purposes, or family leave with District approval.

2.    The District may schedule a maximum of two (2) staff meetings a
month that require teachers to remain for one (1) hour after the
conclusion of the teaching workday (i.e. 4:20 p.m.).

3.    Fridays and the day preceding a holiday, teachers may leave fifteen
(15) minutes after the last dismissal time for student?, providing that
teaching obligations, such as lesson plans, are met.

4.    The District and the Association recognizes that a teacher performs
many additional duties beyond the time spent in class including but
not limited to planning, selecting, and preparing materials for
instruction, evaluating work of pupils, conferring with parents,
keeping records, cooperating in parent, community and open house
activities, attending faculty, department, grade level meetings,
attending Back to School, Graduation and Open House, and serving
on committees providing advice or services to the District. The
parties further recognize that the demands of probationary teachers
are even more intense so discretion should be used in seeking their
voluntary participation on committees and other activities of a
voluntary nature.

5.    Whenever students in a class are assigned to other unit members
because their regular teacher is absent for a half day or more and no
substitute is available, the unit members instructing these students
shall each be compensated the proportion of the substitute pay equal
to the proportion of the students that are assigned from the class.

n. Adjunct Duties

1. The District and the Association also recognize that the teacher
voluntarily participates in adjunct duties beyond the workday
providing leadership in pupil organizations and activities.

2.    The District recognizes that a teacher's instructional responsibilities

ARTICLE XVIII
LEAVES

I.    Sick Leave

Each teacher who is employed five days per week shall be entitled to ten (10) days of
sick leave at the teacher’s regular pay per full school year of service with unlimited
accumulation of the unused amount from year to year. An employee may use sick
leave for illness or injury as provided by Education Code 44978 or for pregnancy
and childbirth as provided by Education Code 44965. Sick leave may also be used
to attend to the illness of a child, parent, or spouse, regardless of the number of days
that an employee may be absent.

1.    Unused sick leave shall be accumulated from year to year.

2.    Accumulation of credit for leave of absence (for illness or injury) need
not occur prior to taking such leave by the teacher, and such leave of
absence may be taken at any time during the school year.

3.    The District may require a physician's verification of illness if a
teacher has been on sick leave for more than three (3) consecutive
days.

4.    The District shall provide each teacher with a written statement of
his/her accrued sick leave total and his/her sick leave entitlement on
their monthly payroll statement.

5.    Each day of sick leave will be credited as 8 hours.

II.    Personal Business Days Leave

1.    A teacher shall be entitled to the use of up to ten (10) days annually
during which he or she may be absent from his or her regularly
assigned duties for the purpose of conducting personal business.

2.    A teacher wishing to utilize the leave under this provision shall be
required to notify his or her immediate supervisor at least seventy-two
(72) hours in advance of the time at which he or she intends to be
absent.

3.    The use of such leave time shall be charged against the credited 37

37

9/05/13

and/or accumulated sick leave in the employee's sick leave.

4.    Leave shall be denied only if such leave would cause a hardship to the
functioning of the school. Reasons for denying such leave will be
given orally at the time of denial to be followed in writing within one

(1) working day.

5.    Example of purposes and/or reasons for which business leave shall not
be granted are:

a.    Political activities or demonstrations

b.    Extending vacation or recreational periods

c.    Association activities

d.    Job hunting

III.    Unpaid Personal Leave

1.    A permanent teacher may request an unpaid personal leave of
absence, and such leave may be granted upon approval of the School
Board, including the reasons and any supporting information related
thereto and length of the requested leave.

2.    Request for personal leave in excess of fifteen (15) working days shall
normally be limited to the balance of the school semester or a full
school year. A teacher requesting such an extended leave of absence
shall submit the request in sufficient time for the Superintendent's
consideration and presentation to the Board of Education (Trustees).

IV.    Extended Illness Leave

If a teacher has utilized all of his/her accumulated sick leave and is still
absent from duty on account of illness or accident, he/she shall be paid as
follows:

1.    For a period not to exceed (5) months, he/she shall receive an amount
equal to the difference between his/her salary and the salary paid the
substitute, or, if no substitute is employed or if a full-time certificated
employee is employed, the teacher will receive the difference between
his/her salary and the minimum amount which would have been paid
to a substitute had one been employed.

2.    For the remainder of the period of his/her contract in excess of the
five (5) month period, the teacher shall receive an amount equal to ten
percent of the remainder of his/her contract salary.
