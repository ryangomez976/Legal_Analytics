SOUTHERN TRINITY JOINT UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT
AGREEMENT WITH THE SOUTHERN TRINITY TEACHERS ASSOCIATION

2014-15 SCHOOL YEAR

Article I: Recognition

1.1    The purpose of this agreement is to comply with Chapter 10.7

Government Code as it    requires and sets forth the

requirements for collective bargaining in California schools.

1.2 The district recognizes    the Southern Trinity Teachers
Association as the exclusive representative of the
Certificated staff excluding the following: administration,
confidential, and supervisory personnel, day-to-day
substitutes and long term substitutes.

Article II: Term

2.1    This agreement shall be effective as of July 1, 2014 to June
30, 2015.

2.2    The contract period for certificated staff at the Southern

Trinity Joint Unified School District shall be 185 days. This
includes 3 staff days before the start of the school year and
2 staff days at the end of the school year. The district
agrees, if possible,    that 2    of the 3 staff    days before    school

starts will be    set    aside    for teachers    to prepare    their

classrooms. Customarily, the 2 days at the end of the school
year are set    aside for    teachers to    complete student

cumulative records, end of the year paper work, etc.

2.3    The staff and Board will begin negotiations by April 1, 2015

with the goal    of    having    an acceptable contract    before

preliminary budget approval by June 1st, 2015.

Article IV: Leave

4.1 Sick Leave

Each full-time unit member shall be entitled to 10 paid sick
leave days per year. Sick leave days will be changed from
days to hours. One day equals 7 hours.

4.2    Bereavement Leave:

Each unit member is entitled to a leave of absence, not to
exceed three days, or five days if out-of-state travel is
required, on account of a death of any member of his
immediate family. No deduction shall be made from the salary
of such employee nor shall such leave be deducted from leave
granted by other sections of this code or provided by the
governing board of the district. Members of the immediate
family, as used in this section, means the mother, father,
grandmother, grandfather, or a grandchild of the employee or
of the spouse of the employee, and the spouse, son, son-in-
law, daughter, daughter-in-law , brother, or sister of the
employee, or any relative living in the immediate household
of the employee.

4.3    Personal Necessity Leave

Any days of leave of absence for illness or injury may be
used by the employee, at his or her election, in cases of
personal necessity. Ed Code 44981 allows up to seven (7) days
for personal leave per year. Of those seven days three (3)
days may be "No Tell Days". Three "No Tell" days will be
offered until such time the district is able to offer members
a COLA; at that time the agreement will return to two (2) No
Tell days. Advance notice is required.

The employee shall not be required to secure advance
permission for leave taken for any of the following reasons:

(a)    Death or serious illness of a member of his or her
immediate family.

(b) Accident, involving his or her person or property, or
the person or property of a member of his or her immediate
family.

(c)    Personal business that cannot be conducted during
regular duty hours.

Article VII: Preparation Periods

7.1    High School

Each full time unit member assigned to a six period day at
the high school shall have an average of 1 preparation period
each duty day. Part time employees shall have prep-time
proportional to their duty periods.

7.2    Elementary School

Each unit member assigned to the elementary schools that
are required to have students in class from the beginning of
the school day until 3:15 p.m. will be entitled to ten (10)
curriculum development days per year. If teachers in grades
six through eight are required to be on a rotating schedule
with the high school they will be entitled to one preparation
period per day.
