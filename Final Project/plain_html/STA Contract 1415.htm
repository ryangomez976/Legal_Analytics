ARTICLE 4: HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

The District and the Association recognize that a teacher is a professional person
and that the professional task of certificated employees involves considerably more
than class instruction. The District and the Association recognize the importance of
study and research to keep abreast of new knowledge and techniques of evaluation
of students' work, and such key activities as lesson planning and preparation,
record keeping, assisting and/or conferring with students, parents, staff, and
principal, and completing assigned duties.

The basic on campus work day for full-time employees shall be from thirty (30)
minutes prior to the start of student class time and is defined as 7.5 hours per day
with a thirty (30) minute duty free lunch period.

Each unit of classes, Kindergarten, Primary, Intermediate, and Upper Grades shall
recommend equitable schedules for non-classroom supervision. Such duty rosters,
when developed, shall be presented for the Superintendent's approval.

Employees shall be at school as required for purposes of: parent conferencing,
faculty meetings, inservice activities, and District called evening meetings not to
exceed two (2) per school year.

The work days for full-time unit members shall be one hundred eighty (180)
teaching days, three (3) professional development days, one (1) day of preparation,
orientation, and/or inservice the week prior to the beginning of the school year.
Newly employed teachers may be required to work one additional day of
preparation, orientation, and inservice prior to school. In addition, all unit members
shall complete year-end check out responsibilities assigned by the District.

ARTICLES: LEAVES

Sick Leave

5.1.1    Employees employed five (5) days per week shall be entitled to ten (10)
work days leave of absence for illness or injury with full pay for each school
year of service. Employees employed less than five (5) days per week shall
be entitled to a proportionate amount of Sick Leave.

5.1.2    Employees may accumulate unused Sick Leave without limitation.

5.1.3    Employees returning to work from Sick Leave from surgery or a serious
illness, upon the request of the District, must provide a doctor's release
certifying medical permission to return to work.

5.1.4    When employees' employment terminates and more Sick Leave has been
used than earned, the amount used but not earned shall be deducted from
the final payment.

Extended Sick Leave

When an employee is absent from duties on account of illness or accident whether
or not the absence arises out of or in the course of employment of the employees
for a period of five (5) school months or less, the amount deducted from the salary
the employee for any month in which the absence occurs shall not exceed the sum
actually paid any substitute employee employed to fill the position during the
absence; or if no substitute employee was employed, the amount which would have
been paid to the substitute had he/she been employed. Every effort by the District
shall be made to secure a substitute teacher.

10

Personal Necessity Leave

5.3.1    Employees may use accumulated Sick Leave for Personal Necessity
Leave the following purposes:

A.    Death of a member of the immediate family when additional leave is
required beyond that provided under Bereavement Leave.

B.    Accident involving the person or property of the employee or person
or property of a member of the immediate family.

C.    Appearance in any court as a litigant.

D.    Serious circumstances which, in the opinion of the Superintendent
cannot reasonably be disregarded and which requires the
immediate and personal attention of the employee during assigned
hours of service.

5.3.2    According to the situation, all requests for Personal Necessity Leave
shall be in writing and should be presented to the Superintendent as far
in advance as possible and if known, shall be presented at least five (5)
days prior to the date of the leave. Such request shall state the nature of
the personal necessity and the time predicted to be absent. The
employees shall have plans available for the substitute teacher.

5.3.3    Employees shall not be required to secure advance permission for leave
taken for: (1) death or serious illness of a member of his/her immediate
family; (2) accident involving his/her person or property, or the person or
property of a member of his/her immediate family. Employees utilizing
this option must notify the District Office.

Personal Leave

Bargaining unit members shall be allowed up to three (3) days per year for

personal leave. During such leave, the cost of the substitute shall be deducted

from the bargaining unit member's salary or the bargaining unit member may

request that the leave be subtracted from his/her sick leave.

A.    Personal leave may be extended beyond the three (3) total days at the
sole discretion of the Superintendent. Such extended leave shall be
charged to sick leave. Each request shall be considered on its own
merits, and the denial and approval of the leave shall not set a precedent
for other requests.

B.    Three (3) additional days may be taken without compensation.

C.    A bargaining unit member shall notify his/her principal and the
Superintendent at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance of taking such
leave unless an emergency makes such advance notification impossible.

D.    Personal leave shall not be used to extend school holidays without written
permission from the Superintendent.

An employee who receives a subpoena for appearance in court or who is required

to serve on a jury shall receive full pay for such absence from duty, provided that

1)    An employee must not be a litigant in the court action

2)    An employee must present to the payroll clerk an actual notice to appear for
jury duty

3)    An employee must submit to the business office any check or warrant
received in payment for such court appearance or jury duty, exclusive of
mileage, lodging, meals, parking, etc., reimbursement.

ARTICLE 6: ASSIGNMENT AND REASSIGNMENT

6.1 General Provisions

6.1.1    The Superintendent may assign and reassign employees within the
District, limited only by the specific and express procedures contained in
this Article.

6.1.2    Only alleged procedural violations of this Article may be processed
through the grievance procedure of this Agreement.

6.1.3    In making any assignments and reassignments, consideration shall be
given to such factors as the employee's experience, credentials,
preferences, major and minor fields of study, length of District service,
established duties and requirements of vacant positions.

6.1.4    The Superintendent shall include staff members in the interviewing
process when assignments and voluntary reassignments are being
considered that will affect team teaching, cluster grouping, or close
department situations, and other similar situations that require a close
working relationship between personnel involved, except in emergency
situations such as a resignation at the beginning of the school year.

6.1.5    Voluntary reassignments that are made during the school year resulting
in different grade level assignments are to be worked out with the full
knowledge of the staff members to be affected. The administrator shall
involve staff members to be affected to make sure that their points of
view are heard and given consideration.

21

6.2 Definition:

A "vacancy" is a position within this representation unit occurring in any school
when the current certificated school staff has been assigned to fill all available
positions, and the District determines that an additional or replacement employee is
required.

6.3    Voluntary Reassignments

6.3.1    During the regular school session, the District shall post in each school a
list of vacancies. This list shall contain the closing date and procedures
for submitting a request for reassignment. No regular assignment to fill
such a vacancy may be made until after this closing date.

6.3.2    To be considered for vacancies that occur during the summer recess
period, employees shall file with the District Office a District form, which
indicates a desire to transfer to a particular assignment.

6.3.3    If a request for reassignment is not granted, the employee may schedule
a meeting with the Superintendent to discuss the reasons why the
request was not granted. Upon request of the employee, the reasons
will be given in writing.

6.4    Involuntary Reassignment

6.4.1 An employee who does not request a reassignment to a different grade
may not be reassigned until given the opportunity (a written
communication or telephone call if the employee is not available) for a
meeting with the Superintendent to discuss the reasons for the
reassignment. Upon request of the employee, the reasons shall be

22

given in writing. The employee shall also be given an opportunity to be
considered for other vacancies which are available at the time of the
impending reassignment for which the employee is qualified.

6.4.2    All such assignments shall normally be completed by the end of the first
week of June. In the event of an occurrence after this date, such as a
death, resignation, leave of absence, change of unexpected enrollment,
or other unforeseen circumstances, assignments may be made or
changed by the District.

6.4.3    Prior to an involuntary reassignment, teachers who will be directly
affected (such as team teaching or cluster groupings) shall be informed
of such reassignments by the Superintendent and given an opportunity
for input.

ARTICLE 7: SAFETY CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

The District shall confer and provide legal assistance and/or counsel consistent with
provisions of the California Education Code for any employee assaulted by a
student or a parent while in the lawful discharge of his/her duties. An assault shall
be defined as in the Penal Code "... as an unlawful attempt, coupled with a present
ability to commit a violent injury on the person of another.”

Any case of assault should be promptly reported to the employee's immediate
supervisor, who shall immediately report the incident to the appropriate law
enforcement agency. Such employee then shall submit a complete written report of
the incident to the supervisor. The employee shall be entitled to reasonable
release time necessary to complete such report.

When absence arises out of or from such assault or injury, illness insurance and
appropriate leave provisions of this Agreement shall apply. Under this article, the
allowable leave shall be extended from not more than sixty (60) days, to not more
than one hundred twenty (120) days.

Employees shall report to the immediate supervisor any condition, which he/she
believes to endanger his/her health and safety. Such report shall be in writing or
shall be presented verbally, followed as soon as possible by a written report.
According to the situation, the supervisor shall, as soon as practicable, see that
action is taken to investigate and remedy the reported condition and shall so inform
the employee within five (5) working days.

ARTICLE 9: EMPLOYEE EVALUATION PROCEDURES

Evaluation Procedures

The District retains responsibility for the evaluation and assessment of performance
of each unit member, subject only to the following procedural requirements:

9.1.1    The Principal shall evaluate, in writing, all certificated employees:

A.    Probationary Employees: at least twice each school year, no later
than the end of the 12th week of school and March 1.

B.    Permanent Employees: at least once every two (2) school years no
later than March 1 of the year of which evaluation takes place.

9.1.2    District shall evaluate and assess certificated employee competency as it
reasonably relates to:

(1)    the progress of students toward the established standards;

(2)    the performance of those current non-instructional duties and
responsibilities, including supervisory and advisory duties;

(3)    the establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning
environment within the scope of the employee's responsibilities;

(4)    the instructional techniques and strategies used by the unit
member;

(5)    the unit member's adherence to curriculum objectives; and

(6)    for non-instructional unit member, the job responsibilities
associated with the job description.

ARTICLE 13: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

Definitions

13.1.1    A "grievance" is a formal written allegation by a Grievant that he/she has
been adversely affected by a violation, misapplication, or
misinterpretation of the specific provisions of the Agreement.

13.1.2    A "Grievant" shall be an employee(s) of the District covered by the terms
of this Agreement of the Association.

13.1.3    A "day" is any day which is designated as a day of work for the grievant.

General Procedures

13.2.1    A decision rendered at any step in these procedures becomes final
unless appealed within the time limits specified.

13.2.2    Time limits given in these procedures may be modified by written
agreement of the parties involved.

13.2.3    If the same complaint or substantially the same complaint is made by
more than one employee against one party, only one employee on behalf
of himself/herself and the other complaints may process the grievance.
Names of all aggrieved parties shall appear on all documents related to
the processing of the grievance. This provision may be waived by all
parties concerned.

13.2.4    Any employee may present grievances in accordance with this Article
prior to Level 2 without the intervention of the Association. The District
shall not agree to the final resolution of such grievance until the
Association has been provided a copy of the proposed solution and has

38

been given an opportunity (within ten (10) working days) to file a
response.

13.2.5    The Grievant may be represented by the Association at all steps of the
Grievance Procedures.

13.2.6    All documents resulting from the processing of a grievance shall be kept
in a separate grievance file and shall not be kept in an employee's
personnel file.

13.2.7    In the event a grievance is filed at such a time that it cannot be
processed through all the steps in this procedure by the end of the
school year, and if left unresolved until the beginning of the following
school year, could result in harm in an aggrieved person or the District.
The time limits set forth herein will be reduced so that the procedure may
be exhausted prior to the end of the school year or as soon as is
practicable.

13.3 Informal Level

Before filing a formal written grievance, the Grievant shall attempt to resolve it by an
informal conference with the Principal or designee within twenty (20) days of when
the Grievant knew or reasonably should have known of the occurrence of the act of
omission giving rise to the grievance.

39

13.4

Formal Levels

13.4.1    Formal Level 1

A.    Within ten (10) days of the decision at the Informal Level, the
Grievant must present the grievance in writing to the Principal or
designee.

B.    The Grievant must provide a clear, concise statement of the
alleged grievance citing the specific section of this Agreement
upon which the grievance is based, the circumstances involved,
the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the specific
remedy sought. Within the time limit indicated in the next
paragraph, either party may request a conference.

C.    The Principal or designee shall communicate the decision to the
Grievant in writing within ten (10) days after receiving the written
grievance. If the Principal or designee does not respond within
the time limits, the Grievant may appeal to the next level.

13.4.2    Formal Level 2: Mediation

A. In the event the Grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level
1, he/she may, within five (5) days, request in writing that the
Association submit the matter to mediation. Within five (5) days of
the request of the Grievant the Association may notify the
Superintendent in writing of a request for the services of a State
Mediator. The District shall then contact the California State

40

Mediation and Conciliation Service within five (5) days and
request that a mediator be provided.

B.    At the onset of mediation, the mediator shall be presented with
the positions of both parties and shall attempt to mediate the
disagreement.

C.    The parties shall attempt to reach agreement through mediation
within fifteen (15) days. If mutual agreement by the parties is
reached, that agreement shall be reduced to writing and signed as
final and binding.

13.4.3 Level 3: Arbitration

A.    In the event that no agreement is reached at Level 2, the
Grievant, within ten (10) days may request in writing that the
Association submit the grievance to arbitration.

B.    Only the Association by written notice to the Superintendent or
designee within twenty (20) days of the above request of the
Grievant may submit the grievance to arbitration.

C.    The Association and the District shall by mutual agreement select
an arbitrator. If no agreement can be reached within five (5) days
of the above request of the Association, the parties shall request
the California State Conciliation Service to supply a list of five (5)
names of persons experienced in hearing grievances in California
public schools. Each party shall alternately strike a name until

41

only one name remains. The order of striking shall be determined
by lot.

D.    In each dispute, the arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear
evidence and render a decision on the issue(s) submitted. If the
parties cannot agree upon submission agreement, the arbitrator
shall determine the issue(s) by discussion with the parties and by
referring to the written grievance and the answers thereto at each
step.

E.    If any question arises regarding the arbitrate of a grievance, the
arbitrator shall make a determination on this issue prior to hearing
the merits of the grievance, unless he/she determines otherwise.

F.    After the hearing and after both parties have been given an
opportunity to make arguments, the arbitrator shall submit his/her
findings and award to both parties. The findings and award of the
arbitrator shall be final and binding, subject to established right of
judicial review.

G.    The arbitrator shall have no power to add to, subtract from, or
modify the terms of the agreement, nor shall the arbitrator be
empowered to render a decision on issue(s) not before the
arbitrator. The authority of the arbitrator to award back pay shall
be limited to the first of the fiscal year in which the grievance was
filed. The arbitrator shall also be without power or authority to

42

make any decision, which requires the commission of any act
prohibited by law.

The fees and expenses of the arbitrator shall be borne equally by
the District and Association. Concerning transcripts, the cost shall
be borne equally by the parties if the transcript is requested by
both parties or the arbitrator. If the transcript is requested by only
one party, that party shall incur the expense. All other expenses
shall be borne by the party incurring them.
