1.1    This is an Agreement made and entered into by and between the Trinity
Alps Unified School District ("District") and the Trinity Alps Unified Teachers
Association/ CTA/NEA ("Association").

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to section(s) 3540-3549 of the
California Government Code (The Educational Employment Relations Act "EERA”).

1

2.1    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of
all full time and part time certificated employees of the District, excluding preschool,
substitute and hourly (adult education, independent study and home and hospital)
teachers and management, confidential, and supervisory employees, as defined in
Chapter 10.7, Section 3540-3549 of the California Government Code for the purposes of
meeting and negotiating.

2.2    Certificated employees serving in excess of 50% of time in a management
position shall be considered management and not members of the bargaining unit. 2

ARTICLE III: TERM

3.1    This Agreement shall be effective July 1,2012 and shall continue in full
force and effect through June 30, 2015.

3.2    This completes negotiations on all the topics within the scope of
negotiation for the 2012-2013 year. All topics not included herein are deemed
withdrawn. Neither party is required to negotiate further on any topic within the scope of
negotiation for the 2012-2013 school year except by mutual written agreement.

3.3    Either party may reopen negotiations on Article VIII: Compensation
(including wages, salary schedule provisions, extra duty, insurance, travel expense
reimbursements and retiree insurance) and any two (2) subjects or articles within the
scope of negotiation by giving written notice to the other party no later than March 15, of
each year (2013 & 2014).

3.4    No later than March 15th of 2015, the Association and District shall submit
their initial proposals to each other for a successor Agreement. The District shall give
proper public notice of such proposals at the first regular Board meeting following
submission of the proposals. 3

ARTICLE IV: ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

4.1    The Association has a right, in accordance with applicable law, to access
at reasonable times, areas in which bargaining unit members work, when such access
does not interfere with duty assignments: the right to use institutional bulletin boards,
and the right to use institutional facilities at times which do not interfere with the
educational program of the District.

4.2    Subject to applicable Board policies, the Association has the right to use
District facilities when not otherwise in use for the purpose of meetings concerned with
the exercise of rights guaranteed by the Educational Employment Relations Act.

4.3    The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities or matters
of Association concern on Association bulletin boards, at least one of which shall be
provided in each school site in areas frequented by bargaining unit members.

4.4    The Association retains all of its rights as set forth in the Educational
Employment Relations Act.

4.5    The Association may use school mail boxes and other means of
communication as long as information distributed therein contains the date of
distribution and the identification of the sender and the organization, together with an
authorization provided by an Association officer that such material is an official
Association publication The Association may also use District e-mail with no expectation
of privacy.

4.6    At the time of mailing to the Board of Trustees, the District shall provide
the Association President two (2) copies of the Board meeting agenda packet, including
all non-confidential attachments.

4.7    As soon as practicable after ratification of an Agreement by both parties,
the District shall post a copy on the District’s web site and provide five (5) copies to the
Association. A copy shall also be available at each school site office.

4.8    The District shall provide a current copy of the bargaining unit seniority list
to the Association before the first day of pupil attendance for the school year and an
updated list no later than February 1.

4.9    No later than October 1 of each school year, the District shall provide the
Association a list of current names, addresses and telephone numbers (except those

4

that are unlisted) of bargaining unit members. The District shall also provide the
Association any changes in this list that occur during the school year.

ARTICLE VI: HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

6.1    The 2013-2014 work year for bargaining unit members shall be 183 days.
The 183 days will be made up of 180 instructional pupil attendance days and three (3)
teacher work days. An orientation day for unit members new to the District shall be
required, thus totaling 184 work days for them. Said additional day of service for unit
members new to the District shall be paid at the employee’s daily rate.

6.2    The District will meet and negotiate with the Association concerning
matters within the lawful scope of representation of the school calendar for the
subsequent school year and changes in the existing school calendar.

6.3    Unit members shall report for duty thirty (30) minutes before the beginning
of the first class at the work site and remain for at least fifteen (15) minutes after the
work site’s last class is dismissed. The work day for full-time unit members shall be a
minimum of seven (7) hours, exclusive of the duty free lunch period. Except as provided
in Section 6.7, on minimum student days, unit members shall use the remainder of the
work day for faculty meetings, in-service, IEP team meetings, meeting with parents
and/or staff members, and/or planning and preparation for their assignments.

6.4    In the event it is necessary for a unit member to leave his/her work place
during assigned duty times, the unit member shall notify the Principal or designee.

6.5    Unit members may be assigned professional responsibilities outside of the
hours specified in Section 6.3 to participate in faculty meetings, parent conferences,
open house, back-to-school night and graduation. Except in the case of regularly
scheduled meetings or an emergency, written notice of faculty meetings shall precede
the meeting by a minimum of 24 hours. A meeting agenda shall be distributed prior to
the meeting. No faculty meeting shall exceed ninety (90) minutes unless mutually
agreed to by the site Principal and staff.

6.6    If a unit member is absent from duty, he/she may do so for any reason
specified under Article VII "Leaves."

6.7    Minimum Day

If a minimum day is scheduled on the day before a holiday, unit members
may leave school fifteen (15) minutes after the work site’s last class is dismissed. This
provision also applies on days that a unit member is required to return for an after

7

school assignment such as open house or back-to-school night.

6.8    Meal Period

Unit members shall be provided a thirty (30) minute duty free lunch period.

6.9    On work days of non student attendance, starting times and ending times
may be modified by the District up to thirty (30) minutes as long as the total length of the
work day is not extended beyond that which is provided in Section 6.3.

6.10    Unit members may be assigned to travel between District campuses and
will be given adequate time to drive the trip safely. Actual miles driven between sites
shall be reimbursed at the current rate recognized by the Internal Revenue Service,
pursuant to District procedures. Such driving time is not part of the duty free lunch but
is part of the regular assigned work day.

6.11    Preparation Time

6.11.1    Unit members who teach in a departmentalized program shall be
assigned a preparation period within the school day. The preparation period shall be the
same length as the regular class period for that school day.

6.11.2    Grades 4-8 unit members shall have 225 minutes during a five day
work week for preparation and planning, unless assigned to cover a class.

6.11.3    The District may assign unit members to substitute during his/her
preparation period if no other feasible option is available. Unit members who sub during
their preparation period shall be eligible for compensatory time credit on a one for one
equivalency. A unit member who is unable to take his/her compensatory time during a
school year shall be permitted to carry over such time, not to exceed three (3) days for
use during the next school year. Any hours or days not used or carried over shall be
compensated at the rate of $35.00 per hour. K-6 or self-contained program unit
members who provide peer coverage shall be granted equivalent minutes for the
purpose of earning compensatory time. Carry-over provisions shall be equivalent to the
7-12 provisions. Any hours or days not carried over shall be compensated at the rate of
$35.00 per hour.

6.11.4    Unit members assigned to teach 7th periods in lieu of having a prep
period will be paid 1/7 of their daily rate. This requires Board approval.

6.11.5    Preparation periods are intended for such professional activities as

8

planning of instructional programs and meeting with students, parents, faculty members
and administrative staff.

6.12 Other Adjunct Duties

6.12.1    All student club sponsorships and paid extra-service assignments
shall be considered voluntary. However, once accepted, the unit member must complete
the assignment. In the case of class advisors, the unit member must serve through the
graduation of the class. In addition, if no volunteers are available, the District may
assign unit members as a class advisor. In no event will an assigned class advisor be
required to service more than two (2) consecutive years.

6.12.2    As soon as practicable after the start of the school year, the
Principal and faculty of each school site shall meet and discuss the student activities to
be offered during the year. The District shall then provide an opportunity for all staff
members at a school to sign up for supervision of the scheduled student activities
occurring during the school year. The Administration shall post by September 15 the
assignments for the certificated unit members at the school site. Such assignments shall
be made by the District in order to equalize, as much as possible, the amount of time
assigned unit members during the school year at each school site. However, it is
recognized that such factors as illness, staff changes and modifications of activity
schedules may cause variations in assigned time among unit members.

6.12.3    Assignments established after September 15 shall be contingent
upon the securing of volunteers.

ARTICLE VII: LEAVES

7.1    General Rules Regarding Leaves of Absence

If a unit member needs to be absent from duty, notice must be given on
the District’s leave of absence form. (See Appendix A.) Whenever possible, unit
members shall notify the school office or other District designated party prior to the day
of absence. It is the responsibility of the unit member to see that the class roll book and
lesson plans are made available at the work site

7.2    Sick Leave

7.2.1    Unit members employed five (5) days per week shall be entitled to
ten (10) work days leave of absence for illness or injury, with full pay during each
school year. Unit members employed fewer than five (5) days per week shall be
entitled to a proportionate amount of Sick Leave. Sick Leave is prorated for part time
unit members. Unit members who are employed under contract to serve beyond the
regular school year shall be entitled to one (1) additional day of Sick Leave for each
twenty (20) days of such service.

7.2.2    Unit members may accumulate unused Sick Leave without

limitation.

7.2.3    Unit members are entitled to use Sick Leave in two (2) hour

increments.

7.2.4    Unit members returning to work from Sick Leave following surgery
or hospitalization, upon the request of the District, must provide a written medical
doctor’s release certifying the reasons for such absence and medical permission to
return to work and any work related restrictions.

7.2.5    The District may require a medical doctor’s certification of illness or
injury as the condition for the payment of Sick Leave when a unit member is absent
more than five (5) consecutive work days or if there is a pattern of absence in
conjunction with the weekend or holiday. Such certification shall be provided promptly
by the unit member.

7.2.6    When a unit member's employment terminates and more Sick
Leave has been used than earned, the amount used, but not earned, shall be deducted
from the final pay warrant.

10

7.3 Extended Illness

Unit members are entitled to receive differential compensation, under
Education Code section 44977, for a period of up to five (5) school months. Differential
compensation shall be the difference between the employee's daily salary (annual
salary divided by the number of teacher work days) and the salary actually paid a
substitute employee.

7.4    Pregnancy Disability Leave

A unit member may utilize accumulated Sick Leave for the purpose of a
disability related to pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and the recovery there from. The
length of such leave, including the date on which the leave shall commence and the
date on which the unit member shall resume duties, shall be determined by the unit
member's physician; provided that such verification demonstrates that such leave is for
pregnancy related disability. Such pregnancy disability leave with pay shall be granted
and administered in the same manner as Sick Leave.

7.5    Industrial Accident and Illness Leave (Workers’ Compensation Leave)

7.5.1    Unit members shall be eligible to apply for a leave of absence
because of industrial accident or illness arising out of a claim which the District’s
insurance carrier determines to be valid and which is recognized as compensable after
adjudication under the workers’ compensation statues. Upon submission of a claim, the
unit member shall, as soon as possible, perform all appropriate actions necessary to file
for Workers’ Compensation, disability, income protection benefits, or the like.

7.5.2    Allowable leaves shall be for not more than one period of sixty (60)
work days in any one fiscal year for the same accident and shall commence with the first
day of absence.

7.5.3    Leave of absence under this provision shall not be accumulated
from year to year. When the industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next
fiscal year, the unit member shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due
him/her for the same illness or injury.

7.5.4    Unit members shall be paid such portion of the salary due them for
any month in which absence occurs as, when added to the temporary disability
indemnity under the California Labor Code, will result in payment to them of not more

11

than their full salaries.

7.5.5    Leave of absence applied for under this provision shall be reduced
by one day for each day of authorized absence, regardless of a temporary disability
indemnity award to the unit member.

7.5.6    Industrial illness and accident leave is to be used in lieu of Sick
Leave. If a unit member is receiving a temporary disability indemnity, the unit member
shall be entitled to utilize only so much of his/her sick leave which, when added to
his/her temporary disability indemnity, will result in a payment to him/her of no more
than a full day's wages or salary. The District, in turn, shall issue the appropriate salary
warrants for payment of salary and shall deduct normal retirement and other authorized
deductions.

7.5.7    Unless travel outside of California is authorized by the District in
writing prior to the commencement of said travel, unit members receiving benefits under
this Article during a period of illness or injury shall remain in the State of California. This
provision shall not apply in cases of necessary medical care of the employee.

7.5.8    The District may require, from time to time, a written statement from
the unit member’s physician verifying a unit member’s absence under this leave and
his/her ability to return to work, or verifying the continued illness or inability to work due
to an illness or accident.

7.5.9    The unit member shall provide a written statement from the unit
member's physician verifying the cause of the unit member's absence under this leave
and specifying a date for reevaluation or for return to work. The unit member shall
provide a written statement from the physician after the reevaluation specifying the date
for return to work. If the date to return to work is to be extended or shortened, the unit
member's physician shall provide a written statement specifying the reasons for the
extension or shortening of the date for return to work. The District may require a written
statement from the unit member's physician certifying medical permission to return to
service at the end of this leave, specifying any work related restrictions.

7.5.10    Leave of absence under this provision shall be reduced by one (1)
day for each day of authorized absence, regardless of a temporary disability indemnity
award or other compensation from any other source paid to the unit member.

7.6 Personal Necessity Leave

7.6.1 Unit members may use up to 7 days of Sick Leave for the following

purposes:

7.6.1.1    Death of a member of the immediate family when additional
leave is required beyond that provided under Bereavement Leave. Member of the
"immediate family" is defined as the parent (including step parent), grand parent or a
grandchild of the unit member or of the spouse of the unit member, and the spouse, son,
son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law, brother, or sister of the unit member, any relative
living in the immediate household of the unit member or any person for which the unit
member is serving in loco parentis. “Member of the immediate family” includes the step
child and step sibling of the unit member.

7.6.1.2    Accident involving the person or property of the unit
member, or the person or property of a member of the immediate family.

7.6.1.3    Appearance in any court or before any administrative
tribunal as a litigant, party or witness under subpoena or any order made with
jurisdiction.

7.6.1.4    Serious illness of a member of the immediate family.

7.6.1.5    Birth or adoption of the unit member's child.

7.6.1.6    Imminent danger to the home of the employee which
reasonably requires the attention of the employee during duty hours.

7.6.2    Unit members shall not be required to secure advance
permission for leave taken for any of the following reasons: death or serious illness of a
member of the immediate family or accident involving the unit member's person or
property or the person or property of the immediate family. The unit member must notify
the immediate supervisor as far in advance of the absence as practical.

7.6.3    A unit member must secure advance permission for all
absences not specified in the prior paragraph and shall normally notify the immediate
supervisor no less than one (1) working day before taking this leave. Leave to appear in
any court or before any administrative tribunal as a litigant, party or witness under
subpoena or any order made with jurisdiction requires prior notice of five (5) working
days.

13

7.6.4    Unit members shall complete the District's absence form (See
Appendix A) which shall verify that the unit member's use of leave was for personal
necessity as defined above.

7.6.5    Personnel Necessity Leave shall be taken in a minimum
increment of two (2) hours.

7.6.6    Up to three (3) of the above seven (7) days of Personal
Necessity Leave may be used in the case of Personal Compelling Importance. Such
leave is defined as matters which the unit member cannot reasonably be expected to
disregard and which require the employee’s personal presence during duty hours. Such
leave may not be used for recreation, the extension of a holiday or weekend, seeking or
engaging in other employment or concerted activity. The provisions of the first sentence
of Section 7.6.4 apply to this leave. Such leave is also contingent upon the District
securing a qualified substitute. However, prior approval will not subsequently be
withdrawn.

7.7 Personal Business Leave

7.7.1    An employee may use up to two (2) days of leave per school year
for personal business. The employee shall reimburse the District for the cost of a
substitute, whether or not one was used. There shall be no deduction from the
employee’s Sick Leave.

7.7.2    Such leave is defined as personal matters which require the
employee’s personal presence during duty hours. Such leave may not be used for
recreation, the extension of a holiday or weekend, seeking or engaging in other
employment or concerted activity. The provisions of the first sentence of Section 7.6.4
apply to this leave. Such leave is also contingent upon the District securing a qualified
substitute. However, prior approval will not be subsequently withdrawn.

7.8 Bereavement Leave

7.8.1    Unit members shall be entitled to a maximum of three (3) days
leave of absence without loss of salary for the death of any member of the unit
member's immediate family.

7.8.2    If travel out of state or more than two hundred fifty (250) miles one
way is required, a unit member shall be entitled to a maximum of two (2) additional days

14

paid Bereavement Leave.

7.8.3 Member of the "immediate family" is defined as the parent
(including step parent), grand parent or a grandchild of the unit member or of the spouse
of the unit member, and the spouse, son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law, brother,
or sister of the unit member, any relative living in the immediate household of the unit
member or any person for which the unit member is serving in loco parentis. “Member of

7.9    Family Care and Medical Leave

Unit members may be eligible for unpaid leave under the Family Medical Leave
Act and/or the California Family Rights Act; days for unpaid leave run consecutively.

7.10    Trial Jury Duty Leave.

A unit member is entitled to a leave to appear for trial jury duty. A unit member
shall receive the regular pay, less any amount received for trial jury duty, exclusive of
allowed travel expense and meal expenses. A unit member shall endorse over in favor
of the District all compensation received for trial jury duty except travel and meal
expenses. In the event a unit member is released from trial jury duty and is able to
return to duty (including reasonable travel time from the court to the work site) prior to
the last half of the work day, the unit member shall return to work. A unit member is not
entitled to this leave for service on a grand jury.

7.11    Military Leave

Unit members are eligible for military leave pursuant to applicable Federal and
State law.

7.12    Other Leave Without Pay

7.12.1    Unpaid leave for up to one school year may be approved at the
discretion of the District for reasons not specified under other leave
provisions in this Agreement. Unpaid leaves may be granted for such
reasons as study, health and family care. Unpaid leaves shall not be
granted to employees for the purpose of accepting other employment.

7.12.2    Leaves of up to twenty (20) working days may be approved by the
Superintendent or designee.

7.12.3    Leaves of greater than twenty (20) working days may be approved

15

by the Board of Trustees.

7.12.4    Unit members on an unpaid leave of greater than one calendar
month may participate at his/her own expense in the District group
insurance plans during the period of the leave, subject to the approval of
the insurance carriers and administrators. Premiums for such coverage
shall be paid to the District on a monthly basis in advance, pursuant to
District policies.

7.12.5    The District shall provide the unit member written terms and
conditions of unpaid leaves of greater than twenty (20) working days. This
written statement shall include any requirement for the unit member to
notify the District about his/her intent to return to the District after the
expiration of the leave.

7.12.6    It is agreed that a unit member who is absent from work other
than those days authorized by State law or this Agreement is taking
unauthorized leave.

7.13 Association Leave

The Association is entitled to released time pursuant to Education Code

ARTICLE VIII: COMPENSATION

8.1    Wages

8.1.1    Effective July 1, 2012 unit members covered by this Agreement
shall be paid the salaries shown on the salary schedule, Appendix B.

8.1.2    Unit members who are employed less than full-time shall be paid
prorated wages, based on a 7 hour work day.

8.1.3    Unit members covered under a Vocational or CTE credential will be
placed on the schedule contained in Appendix C.

8.2    Initial Step Placement

Unit members may be given credit on a year for year basis at the time of
initial placement on the salary schedule for previous teaching experience which
occurred after receipt of a valid preliminary teaching credential. A year of previous
teaching credit will only be awarded if the teacher actually worked 75% of a school year.
Previous credit will only be awarded if the previous teaching was within the authority of
the credential after receipt of the credential. No credit will be given for previous teaching
occurring prior to the unit member being credentialed, teaching which occurred outside
the scope of the credential, or teaching services rendered which are not ordinarily
expected of a teacher with that credential. Initial placement may be on any step of the
salary schedule. All experience shall be in a school accredited by a recognized
accrediting organization and verified in writing according to District procedures. Written
verification of prior teaching experience must be received by the District within thirty (30)
calendar days after the first day of employment or thirty (30) calendar days after the unit
member signs a contract of employment with the District, whichever occurs first.

8.3    Unit Conversion

Quarter units are converted to semester units by multiplying the quarter
units by two-thirds.

8.4    Unit Verification

On or before March 1 of each school year, unit members shall inform the
District of the number of units they expect to complete by September 1 of the next
school year. Unit members shall submit to the District official transcripts of satisfactory
completion of units necessary to change columns on the salary schedule. In the event

17

official transcripts are not available, an official grade card may be submitted. An official
transcript shall be submitted to the District when issued. If such written proof is
submitted by September 15, the column change on the salary schedule will be effective
that school year, i.e. it will be effective as of July 1, three months previous. If such
written proof is not submitted by September 15, the column change on the salary
schedule will be effective the following July 1, i.e. the commencement of the next school
year. Satisfactory completion means a grade of "C" or better in a graded course or pass
in a pass/fail course. All units shall be obtained from a college or university accredited
by a recognized accrediting organization.

Unit members hired after the commencement of the school year shall submit
official transcripts within thirty (30) calendar days of their first day of work or thirty (30)
calendar days of the date their employment agreement is signed, whichever occurs first.

8.5    Awarding of Unit Credit

All units must receive written approval on the unit advancement form prior
to the commencement of the class for which the unit member seeks credit. Unit credit
will be awarded for successful completion of units that contribute to the professional
growth of the unit member. Unit credit will not be awarded if the classes are held during
the pupil attendance day

8.6    Salary Step Movement

Movement on the salary schedule will only occur when an employee has
accrued one (1) year of credit (at least 75% of a school year). Salary schedule
movement shall be based on a seven (7) hour work day constituting a full time day. Unit
members who work less than a full time day will receive credit on the salary schedule as
follows:

8.6.1    Unit members working 5 1/4 per hours or more per day will receive
the same credit as full time employees.

8.6.2    Unit members working 3 1/2 hours per day but less than 5 1/4
hours per day will receive 1/2 year credit for each year of service.

8.6.3    Unit members working less than 3 1/2 hours per day will receive
one-quarter year of credit for each year of service.

8.7    Extra Duty

8.7.1    Unit members who volunteer for extra-duty assignment shall be
obligated to complete that assignment for the entire year or season unless prevented
from doing so because of illness, injury or approved leaves of absence.

8.7.2    Unit members who volunteer for paid extra-curricular or extra-duty
assignments shall continue to serve in that assignment from year to year unless the unit
members give the District notice of their intent not to continue in said assignment for the
next school year. Such notice shall be delivered on or before May 1. Such notice shall
be in writing and addressed to the Superintendent. A unit member who does not provide
such notice shall be deemed to have volunteered for another one year period and will fill
that assignment unless the District gives notice of non-renewal prior to July 1.

8.7.3    Payment of stipends shall be made upon completion of the
assignment (on a semester basis in the case of year long activities) and submission to
the District office of a stipend request form or time sheet.

8.7.4    The compensation for extra duty is set forth in Appendix D attached

hereto.

8.8    Insurance

8. 8.1 The maximum monthly insurance premium contribution for unit

ARTICLE IX: EVALUATION

9.1 Evaluation Procedures

9.1.1    District retains sole responsibility for the evaluation and assessment of
performance of each unit member, subject to the following procedural requirements.
Accordingly, no grievance arising under this Article shall challenge the content of an
evaluation; any grievances shall be limited to a claim that the following procedures have
been violated.

9.1.2    The District management shall evaluate all members of the representation
as follows:

9.1.2.1    Every probationary unit member shall be evaluated by the
administration in writing at least twice each school year; no later than December
1 for the first evaluation and March 1 for the second evaluation.

9.1.2.2    Every permanent unit member shall be evaluated by the
administration in writing every other year. The evaluation shall be completed in
writing on or before May 1, of a contract year (See Evaluation Forms in Appendix
G).

9.1.2.3    The “Prime Evaluator” shall be the unit member’s immediate
supervisor. A designate Principal shall not serve as an evaluator.

9.1.3    The prime evaluator shall, within the first two (2) months of school, hold
one or more staff meetings with employees to be evaluated during the year to review
specific evaluation procedures and the time-lines.

9.1.4    Each unit member to be evaluated shall meet with the prime evaluator
prior to November 1 to discuss performance objectives in the four areas listed below.
The unit member and evaluator will mutually agree on two areas of emphasis from the
following four areas: (It is recognized that the unit member is responsible for all areas
listed).

9.1.4.1    The progress of pupils towards the state adopted academic
content standards as measured by state adopted criterion
referenced assessments.

9.1.4.2    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the
employee.

22

9.1.4.3    The employee’s adherence to curricular objectives.

9.1.4.4    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning
environment, within the scope of the employee’s responsibilities.

9.1.5    By no later than the first workday in December, the prime evaluator and
the evaluatee shall attempt to mutually agree upon the written goals and objectives in
Section 9.1.4. If the prime evaluator and the evaluatee cannot agree upon the goals
and objectives by the above date, the disputed areas shall be submitted to the
Superintendent who shall confer with both evaluatee and prime evaluator. The
Superintendent shall make the final determination. The evaluatee may note for the
record and attach to the final determination his/her written disagreement with the
disputed objective(s).

9.1.6    When requested by the prime evaluator the evaluatee modifications or
additions to the performance objectives can be made by mutual agreement. In the
event the prime evaluator and the employee cannot come to an agreement concerning
said modification or additions, the review procedure of Section 9.1.5 above shall be
utilized.

9.1.7    The prime evaluator shall make no less than two (2) observations no less
than 30 days prior to the final evaluation summary, which shall include
recommendations, if necessary, as to areas of improvement in the performance of the
evaluatee. Each observation shall be conducted openly and with the knowledge of the
evaluatee. The prime evaluator shall prepare a written summary of each observation,
and a signed copy shall be given to the evaluatee. The prime evaluator shall hold a
conference with the evaluatee concerning each observation within three (3) working
days of the observation.

9.1.8    The evaluator shall prepare a final written evaluation no later than May 1st
and a copy shall be given to the employee prior to the end of the school year. The
evaluator shall also hold a conference with the evaluatee concerning the final written
evaluation. In the event the evaluatee is not performing his/her duties in a satisfactory
manner according to the standards prescribed by the District, the prime evaluator shall
notify the evaluatee in writing of such unsatisfactory performance. The prime evaluator
shall thereafter confer with the evaluatee and make specific recommendations as to the

23

areas of improvement in the evaluatee’s performance and endeavor to assist in
improving such performance. The prime evaluator shall also hold a conference with the
evaluatee concerning the final evaluation summary.

9.1.9    Should the evaluatee be performing less than satisfactorily in any area, the
prime evaluator shall:

9.1.9.1    Hold a conference with the evaluatee at least twenty (20) school
days prior to March 1st and notify him/her of the area(s) in need
of improvement, with specific written recommendations to the
evaluatee for improvement.

9.1.9.2    Hold follow-up conference(s) and observation(s), one of which
shall last the entire class period or encompass forty-five (45)
minutes.

9.1.9.3    Evaluate unsatisfactory performance once a year regardless of
employment status.

9.1.10    Within fifteen (15) school days following receipt of the evaluation
summary, the evaluatee shall have the right to initiate a written reaction or response to
the evaluation, and such response shall become a permanent attachment to the
evaluatee’s personnel file. In addition, the evaluatee shall have the right to meet with
the prime evaluator and discuss the evaluation. Any circumstances which give rise to
evaluation after the deadline shall be included in the evaluatee’s files for use in the next
final evaluation summary.

9.1.11    The District retains sole responsibility for the evaluation and assessment
of performance of each unit member subject only to the above procedural requirements.

9.2 Evaluation of Paid Extra Service Assignments

9.2.1    Unit members performing extra paid Service Assignments shall be
evaluated at least once during the performance of their duties.

9.2.2    This evaluation shall include the requirements specified in the job
description of the assignment.

9.2.3    Each evaluation shall include observations of the unit member in the
performance of his/her duties.

9.2.4    Written evaluations shall consist of a narrative statement and shall be

24

given to the unit members within thirty (30) calendar days of the end of the assignment.

9.2.5    The evaluator shall meet with the unit member to discuss concerns about
his/her performance in the assignment.

9.2.6    Prior to July 1, the Superintendent may provide the unit member written
notification that he/she will not be assigned to this position for the following year.

9.2.7    If the unit member does not wish to continue in this assignment for the
following year, he/she must so notify the Superintendent in writing, through the site
Principal, on or before May 1.

9.2.8    If the notification of Section 9.2.6 or 9.2.7 is not provided, the unit member

ARTICLE X: COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

10.1    General Provisions

10.1.1    The appropriate processing of complaints concerning unit members
shall be from the complainant to the unit member to the unit member’s Principal to the
Superintendent to the Governing Board.

10.1.2    This procedure does not apply to complaints filed under the
District’s Uniform Complaint Procedure.

10.1.3    Upon request of the unit member, he/she may be represented by
the Association at any level of the complaint procedure.

10.2    Informal Complaints

10.2.1    Parent/guardians are encouraged to attempt to resolve concerns or
complaints personally with the bargaining unit member.

10.2.2    If the Superintendent or the unit member’s Principal receives an
oral complaint, they may exercise their discretion whether or not to direct the
complainant to meet with the unit member to discuss the complaint.

10.3    Written Complaints

10.3.1    Level I: Upon receipt of a written complaint by the Superintendent
or the unit member’s Principal, a copy of the complaint shall be given to the unit member
within three (3) business days. The Superintendent or site Principal shall request the
complainant to meet with the unit member if they have not already met. Efforts shall be
made to achieve early resolution of the complaint. The complainant may decline to
meet with the unit member. The unit member may decline to meet with the complainant.
If a meeting is held, the unit member’s Principal or designee shall facilitate the meeting
in an attempt to resolve the complaint.

10.3.2    Level II: If the complaint is not resolved at Level I, or if the
complainant or the unit member refuses to meet at Level I, the Superintendent shall
attempt to meet with the parties and resolve the complaint.

10.3.3    Level III:

10.3.3.1 If the complaint is not resolved at Level II, if the
complainant or unit member(s) desires to have the complaint heard by the Governing
Board, he/she shall so advise the Superintendent in writing within five (5) business days

26

and the Superintendent shall provide a copy of the written complaint to the Board of
Trustees.

10.3.3.2    The unit member shall be given notification of the time,
date, and place of any Governing Board meeting at which the Governing Board will hear
the complaint at a date scheduled by the Board.

10.3.3.3    The bargaining unit member may choose a closed or open
session of the Board for consideration of the matter.

10.3.3.4    The Board shall make a decision regarding the matter

which shall be final.

10.3.4    If a unit member challenges the truth of the allegations contained in the
complaint, he/she may file a rebuttal on that basis. The failure of a unit member to file a
rebuttal shall not be construed as an admission that the allegations in the complaint are
true.

10.3.5    Written complaints shall not be placed in the employee’s personnel file
prior to the conclusion of an investigation. If the complaint is withdrawn, resolved in
favor of the employee, or determined to be false it shall not be placed in the employee’s
file.

ARTICLE XI: GRIEVANCE

11.1    Right to Informal Discussion

Nothing within this procedure shall be construed to limit the right of a unit
member to informally discuss any employment problem with any district administrator.

11.2    Definitions

11.2.1    "Grievance" is an allegation that there has been a violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of the specific provisions of this Agreement. Actions
to challenge or change the policies, regulations or other matters outside this Agreement
are not within the scope of this procedure.

11.2.2    "Day" is a day in which the central administrative office of the
District is open for business.

11.2.3    “Grievant” is one or more unit members or the Association. Any
grievance filed by the Association shall list the names of the unit members which have
been allegedly affected by the violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of the
specific provisions of this Agreement.

11.3    Levels of Procedure

11.3.1    Informal: A grievant will first discuss the grievance with the site
Principal or designee.

11.3.2    Level I: Within twenty (20) days of the alleged violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of the specific provisions of this Agreement by the
District which allegedly give rise to the grievance, the grievant must present such
grievance in writing on the appropriate form (Appendix H.) to the site Principal or
designee. The grievance shall consist of; (1) a clear and concise statement of the
problem: (2) the specific provision of the Agreement involved; (3) the specific remedy
sought, and; (4) the names of the unit members allegedly affected by the alleged
violation or misapplication of the specific provisions of this Agreement. The site
Principal shall communicate a written decision within five (5) days of receipt of the
grievance.

11.3.3    Level II: If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I,
or if the site Principal does not respond within the timelines as required by Level I, then
within five (5) days after receipt of the Level I response or the expiration of the time

28

within which the site Principal must respond, the grievant may then appeal the decision,
or lack thereof, on the appropriate form to the Superintendent. Upon receipt of an
appeal from the grievant to the Superintendent, the Superintendent shall review the
grievance and communicate a written decision within five (5) days of receipt of the
grievance. Within the stated time limits, either party may request and be granted a
personal conference concerning the grievance.

11.3.4    Level III: Mediation: If the grievance is not adjusted at Level II, the
Association may submit a written request to the Superintendent or designee for
mediation within ten (10) days of the Level II response. Upon receipt of the request for
mediation by the Superintendent or designee, the District and Association shall jointly
submit a request for the services of a mediator from the California State Mediation and
Conciliation Service or a mediator selected by mutual agreement of the District and
Association. During the pendency of mediation the time lines for further processing of
the grievance shall be stayed.

11.3.5    Level IV: Arbitration

11.3.5.1    If the grievance is not resolved at Level III, the Association
may provide a written request to the Superintendent that the grievance be submitted to
arbitration.

11.3.5.2    Within ten (10) days of the Association’s written request,
the parties shall attempt to agree on an arbitrator to hear the grievance. If no agreement
is reached within said ten (10) day period, the parties shall jointly request the California
State Mediation and Conciliation Service to supply a list of seven (7) names of persons
experienced in hearing grievances in California public schools. In the absence of a
mutual agreement of an arbitrator from this list, each party shall alternately strike a
name until only one name remains. The order of the striking shall be determined by lot.

11.3.5.3    In each dispute, the arbitrator shall, as soon as possible,
hear evidence and render a written decision on the issue(s) submitted. If the parties
cannot agree upon a submission agreement, the arbitrator shall determine the issue(s)
by referring to the written grievance and the answers thereto at each step. If any
question arises to the arbitrabilty of the grievance, the arbitrator shall rule on this issue
prior to hearing the merits, unless he/she rules otherwise.

29

11.3.5.4    After the hearing and after both parties have been given
an opportunity to make written arguments, the arbitrator shall submit findings and an
award to both parties.

11.3.5.5    The arbitrator shall have no power to add to, subtract
from, or modify the terms of this Agreement, nor shall the arbitrator be empowered to
render a decision contrary to law, or on issues(s) not before the arbitrator, nor on facts
not supported by evidence. The power of the arbitrator to award back pay shall be
limited to the first of the fiscal year in which the grievance was filed.

11.3.5.6    Any costs of a hearing room and the fees and expenses of
the arbitrator shall be borne equally by both parties. Concerning transcripts, the cost
shall be borne equally by the parties if the transcript is requested by the arbitrator or
both parties. If the transcript is requested by only one (1) party, that party shall incur the
expense. All other expenses shall be borne by the party incurring them.

11.3.5.7    The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding,
subject to appropriate judicial review, unless appealed to the next level.

11.3.6 Level V - Appeal to the Board

11.3.6.1    If either party is not satisfied with the decision at Level IV,
it may file a written appeal with the Board of Trustees within ten (10) days.

11.3.6.2    The Board shall hear the matter and render a written
decision as soon as practicable. The decision of the Board shall be final and binding.

11.4 General Provisions

11.4.1    A decision rendered at any step during the grievance procedure
becomes final unless appealed within the time limit specified. The time limits specified
at each level should be considered to be maximums. The time limits may, however, be
extended by mutual written agreement.

11.4.2    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be
processed through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end of the school
year, and could, if left unresolved until the beginning of the following school year, result
in harm to the grievant, the time limits set forth herein may be reduced by mutual written
agreement so that the procedure may be exhausted as close to the end of the school
year as possible.

30

11.4.3    Time limits for appeal provided in each level shall begin the day
following receipt of the written decision.

11.4.4    All documents, communications and records dealing with the
processing of a grievance shall be filed in a separate grievance file.

11.4.5    The District and the grievant may, at any step in the grievance
procedure, be represented during the proceeding.

11.4.6    All necessary forms for filing and processing grievances shall be
developed jointly by the District and Association.

11.4.7    Unit members may file grievances, and have such grievances
adjusted without the intervention of the Association, so long as the adjustment is not
inconsistent with the terms of the Agreement. The District shall not agree to a resolution
of the grievance until the Association has received a copy of the grievance and the
proposed resolution and has been given the opportunity to file a response.

12.1    General Provisions

12.1.1    In accordance with Education Code Section 35035, the Superintendent

12.1.2    A transfer/reassignment may be voluntary (initiated by a unit member)

before reassignments and/or transfers of current unit members.

12.2.2    “Reassignment”- A change in grade or department assignment within a
given school.

12.2.3    “Transfer”- The movement of a unit member from one school to
another within the District. Weaverville Elementary School, Trinity High School, Alps
View High School and Trinity River Community Day Schools (Elementary and High
School) are considered independent school sites.

A unit member may submit a request for transfer to the District office at any time,
whether or not a vacancy exists. Such requests shall remain on file only until August 31

submit a request for transfer in response to the posting of a vacancy pursuant to the
procedures of this Article.

12.3.2    Notification of Disposition of Transfer Requests

The District shall notify the unit member of the disposition of his/her request for

transfer. Upon request of the unit member within fifteen (15) business days, the

32

District shall provide in writing the reasons for not being selected for the vacancy.

12.4    Involuntary Transfer

involuntary transfer, but shall not be required to transfer any volunteer.

12.4.2    A unit member who does not request a transfer may not be transferred

not available) for a meeting with the Superintendent to discuss the reasons for the
transfer. Upon request of the unit member within twenty (20) business days, the reasons
for the transfer shall be given in writing.

12.4.3    The unit member shall also be given the opportunity to be considered
for other vacancies which are available at the time of the impending transfer.

ARTICLE XIII: CLASS SIZE

13.1    With the exception of Physical Education and Music, no individual class
may exceed 32 students per period and the total number of pupils per teacher shall not
exceed 170 students per teacher teaching 6 periods. Should a teacher be assigned to
teach a 7th period in lieu of having a prep period the student contact shall not exceed
198 students. However, these limits may be exceeded with the consent of the individual
teacher.

The Administrator will not assign students above this number to a class without
consulting with the instructor privately before the assignment. Each additional student
will require a separate conference.

13.1.1 Exceptions to this are Physical Education and Music classes,
which are not to exceed 45 students per period, keeping the total number of students
per day at 170 for teachers teaching 6 periods.

13.2    All class counts shall conform to the provisions of this Article by the end of
the third week of the semester. The need for flexibility during the opening days of
school is recognized. Therefore, the Principal will be allowed a three week period of
grace to balance class loads. The Principal will make every effort to consult the
instructor when the desirable limit is reached, during or after pre-registration. Any
students enrolled after the desirable limit is reached will be informed that their
enrollment is contingent upon the Principal being able to adjust the class enrollment to
the desired number.

Classes participating in the Class Size Reduction Program shall have the
maximum class size allowed by the legislation.

The average class size for self-contained classes, exclusive of those classes
participating in the Class Size Reduction Program shall be 1:30.

The class size average shall be determined by dividing the student enrollment by
the number of regular class room teachers.

If class size of any individual teacher in a self-contained classroom exceeds 1:30
the Superintendent or designee will meet with the affected teacher(s) to provide
alternatives. No individual self-contained class may exceed 32 students.

If the size of a combination class exceeds 1:27 the Superintendent or designee
will meet with the affected teacher(s) to provide alternatives. No individual self-

36

contained combination class may exceed 30 students.
