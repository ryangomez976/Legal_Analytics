B.    Professional Growth - The District believes that all members of the Association shall participate in a Professional Growth
Program. Implementation of one unit semester credit for fifteen (15) hours of activity shall apply only to plans approved
after July 1, 2000.

The District further believes that professional growth need not only be university or college work but activities related to
Peer Assistance Review (PAR) participation, which contribute to the on-going professional growth of the teaching staff
The completion of each Professional Growth Plan shall be the basis for all column advancement on the Certificated Salary
Schedule. The District shall recognize National Board Certification equal to 15 units of Professional Growth.

1. WHAT IS CONTAINED IN A PROFESSIONAL GROWTH PLAN? - Each bargaining unit member shall develop a
Professional Growth Plan, which is approved by the Principal or Superintendent. The plan shall be reviewed and updated
each year. Each plan shall be in effect until the bargaining unit member completes the equivalent of 15 semester units.
(See Exhibit B)

Each plan contains the following sections:

a.    Professional Goals - Goals are to be based upon the professional growth needs of the individual teacher, and the
instructional and curricular priorities of the School District.

b.    Professional Growth Activities -With the assistance of the Principal or Superintendent, the bargaining unit member is
to select activities that will enhance his/her job performance and/or effectiveness as a teacher in the School District.
Activities selected shall be designed to assist the member in accomplishing her or his professional growth goals.

(1)    The Areas of Professional Growth - All activities must clearly fit into one or more of the following areas:

(a)    A subject or subjects the member teaches.

(b)    A field of specialization in which the member serves, (i.e., remedial services, computer science)

(c)    Concepts, principles and methods of effective teaching, curriculum development and program evaluation.

(d)    Concepts and principles of physical, intellectual, social and emotional development of students.

(e)    Concepts and principles of human communication, learning, motivation and individualized instruction.

(f)    Languages and cultural backgrounds of students who attend the School District.

(g)    Service to the District selected from a list of District needs developed annually by the Superintendent’s
Curriculum and Instruction Council (CIC).

(2)    Standards to Follow in Carrying Out the Professional Growth Plan

(a)    Activities shall be of a high quality.

(b)    Activities are consistent with the bargaining unit member's professional growth goals as approved by the
Principal or Superintendent and relate directly to the classroom.

ARTICLE m - HEALTH AND WELFARE BENEFITS

A.    Health Plan

1.    District Benefits Advisory Committee - The District shall establish and maintain a Benefits Advisory Committee for the
purpose of annually evaluating existing plan carrier, and level of benefits. Changes in carrier and level of benefits shall be
negotiated annually. The committee will meet by February 15 of each year. The Association may appoint one
representative for each school site to this committee.

2.    Premium - The District’s contribution toward the medical, dental and vision coverage for each bargaining unit member
and dependents shall be the established CAP. Any increase in the CAP amount shall be mutually agreed upon by the
parties. If the cost of an employee’s health and welfare benefit package is less than the total amount contributed by the
District, the District shall pay the difference to the employee each month. This amount shall be subject to all federal and
state taxes.

Both parties agree to meet and confer if/when the Benefit Committee recommends a change to a new insurance
administrator and /or carrier.

3.    COBRA - The provisions required by the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1985 (COBRA) are
applicable to any members of the bargaining unit who resign, are laid off, have a reduction of hours or who are terminated
for any reason other than gross misconduct. (Refer to Exhibit C for further explanation.)

B.    Tuberculosis Examinations - The District shall pay for tuberculosis examinations as required by law.

C.    Mandatory Benefits - State Teachers' Retirement System deductions and premiums for Workmen's Compensation shall be

made by the District pursuant of existing law.

ARTICLE IV - EMPLOYMENT AND WORKING CONDITIONS

A.    Length of Teaching Dav - The District and Association recognize that the unique nature of the teaching profession does not
lend itself solely to a duty day of a rigidly established length. Unit members hold a professional obligation to fulfill their
required duties.

The district may establish a beginning and ending time for teachers to be on site, however, it must not exceed a seven-hour
professional day including a 30-minute duty free lunch and an approximate, contiguous 45-minute teacher preparation period.

B.    Duty-Free Lunch - Full-time bargaining unit members shall be afforded one duty-free lunch period of thirty (30) minutes
during each regular school day.

C.    Yard Supervision - Bargaining unit members shall not be given a yard duty assignment without a written plan which is
mutually agreeable with both the Association and the District.

D.    Release Time - Release time for teachers during the school day shall require the approval of the building principal.

E.    Work Year - A work year of 181 days is hereby established for the length of this contract, including 180 teaching days and one
teacher work day, which will be kept free of mandatory district/site meetings unless such meetings have prior VTA Board
approval.

Any additional Inservice days will be paid at the individual teacher’s per diem as negotiated on the salary schedule. If current
law changes, this item would be re-negotiated. A member shall be allowed one absence from an Inservice day. In order to be
compensated, the unit member shall be given an alternative assignment and due date approved by the site administrator. Staff
members will work with the Superintendent's Curriculum and Instruction Council (C and I) to develop Inservice schedules.

Inservice make-up should be in a subject related to the Inservice and documentation provided to the site administrator.
Inservice make-up must be completed prior to the end of the school year.

F.    Calendar - The Association acknowledges that the Board of Trustees is legally responsible for the adoption of the school
calendar. The District acknowledges that the school calendar affects the conditions of employment. The District and the
Association shall agree to form a committee composed of unit members, administrators, classified staff and Board members
for the development of the yearly calendar. The “draft” calendar will be approved by the VTA membership and then
submitted to the District for final approval.

G Staff Meetings - Mandatory district or site staff meetings shall not exceed two (2) per month. Additional meetings would
require approval of the VTA Governing Board. Every attempt shall be made to have meetings not exceed 90 minutes in length. 1

C.    Absence Due to Bereavement

Any bargaining unit members shall be allowed three working days of absence at full salary compensation in the event of
the death of a wife, husband, parent, parent-in-law, sister, brother, son, daughter, grandparent, grandchild, guardian, ward or
any person living in the immediate household of the bargaining unit member.

The allowed three days are not deducted from other leave allowances. The days are over and above the normally allotted
sick leave days. If the bargaining unit member must travel over 200 miles, one way, five days are allowed for said absence.

D.    Absence Due to Personal Necessitv/Personal Business- A full-time bargaining unit member may use no more than eight (8)
days of accumulated sick leave in any school year for the purposes of personal necessity/business. The limit of personal
necessity/business leave for part-time bargaining unit members shall be based upon a pro-ration of eight (8) days. This leave
shall not be taken as part of a concerted work stoppage or slowdown.

1. Annually, a unit member will provide advance written notification to the site administrator if the unit member plans to use
in excess of three (3) contiguous days.

Prior written notification shall not be required for the following situations:

Serious illness of a member of his/her immediate family.

Accident involving his/her person or property or the person or property of his/her immediate family.

However, within twenty-four (24) hours after the return from such leave, the bargaining unit member may be required to
provide the building principal with a written statement specifying the particular purpose for which the leave was taken.

E.    Absence Due to Jury Duty and Litigation Involvement - Any bargaining unit member who is subpoenaed to appear in
litigation, not instituted by himself, shall be allowed full salary for each day up to a maximum of five (5) days of such required
attendance. Full deduction shall be made from his/her salary beyond the aforesaid five (5) days. The amount of pay the
bargaining unit member is to receive is the difference between his/her regular salary and any amount he/she earns as
jury/witness compensation. IRS determined mileage amounts should not be considered compensation.

Any bargaining unit member who has been regularly called to jury duty shall not suffer loss of pay as a result of such

service.

ARTICLE XI - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A.    Definitions

1.    A "grievance" is a formal written allegation by a grievant that a violation or misapplication of the specific provisions of
this Agreement has adversely affected the grievant.

2.    A "grievant" may be any member of the bargaining unit or the Vallecito Teachers Association (VTA).

3.    A "day" for the purposes of this Article is any day in which the District Office is open for business.

4.    The "immediate supervisor" is the building Principal or manager having immediate jurisdiction over the grievant and who
has been designated to adjust grievances.

B.    Guidelines Applicable to all Grievances

1.    Unless otherwise mutually agreed, the employee has the right to one representative of his/her choice.

2.    For the protection of the grievant and the District, all grievance proceedings will be confidential.

3.    All documents, communications, and records dealing with grievance processing will be placed in a District grievance file.
No such material will be placed in an employee's personnel file.

4.    No reprisals shall be taken against any participant in a grievance procedure by reasons of such participation.

5.    Time limits specified in these procedures may be reduced or extended in any specific instance by written mutual
agreement between parties. If specified or adjusted limits expire, the grievant may proceed to the next step.

6.    Any grievance not appealed to the next step of the procedure within prescribed time limits shall be considered settled on
the basis of the answer given in the preceding step. The failure to pursue the grievance will not set a precedence, which
affects the filing of grievances on the same or similar circumstances.

7.    An employee who wishes to have a grievance heard under this procedure must initiate informal action within thirty (30)
business days of the time he/she had knowledge of the act or omission giving rise to the grievance.

8.    An investigation or other handling or processing of any grievance shall be conducted so as to result in minimal
interference with, or interruption of the instructional program and related work activities of the District.

9.    If one or more persons share a common grievance and all aspects of the grievance are common, then these same persons
may join together and invoke the grievance procedures together after filing separate grievances, but before any action is
taken on such separate grievances.

medical conditions, you are eligible to take a pregnancy disability leave (PDL). If you are affected by pregnancy or a related
medical condition, you are also eligible to transfer to a less strenuous or hazardous position or to less strenuous or hazardous
duties, if this transfer is medically advisable. You are also eligible to receive reasonable accommodation for conditions related to
pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions if you request it with the advice of your health care provider.

■    The PDL is for any period(s) of actual disability caused by your pregnancy, childbirth or related medical conditions up
to four months (or 88 work days for a full-time employee) per pregnancy.

■    The PDL does not need to be taken in one continuous period of time but can be taken on an as-needed basis.

■    Time off needed for prenatal care, severe morning sickness, doctor-ordered bed rest, childbirth, and recovery from
childbirth would all be covered by your PDL.

■    Generally, we are required to treat your pregnancy disability the same as we treat other disabilities of similarly situated
employees. This affects whether your leave will be paid or unpaid.

■    You may be required to obtain a certification from your health care provider of your pregnancy disabihty or the medical
advisability for a transfer or reasonable accommodation.

accommodation; and,

3)    a statement that, due to the disabihty, you are unable to work at all or to perform any one or more of the essential
functions of your position without undue risk to yourself, the successful completion of your pregnancy or to other
persons or a statement that, due to your pregnancy, the transfer or reasonable accommodation is medically advisable.

Education Code 44984

Governing boards of school districts shall provide by rules and regulations for industrial accident and illness leaves of
absence for all certificated employees. The governing board of any district which is created or whose boundaries or
status is changed by an action to organize or reorganize districts completed after the effective date of this section shall
provide by rules and regulations for such leaves of absence on or before the date on which the organization or
reorganization of the district becomes effective for all purposes as provided in Section 4064. Such rules or regulations
shall include the following provisions:

a.    Allowable leave shall be for not less than 60 days during which the schools of the district are required to be in session
or when the employee would otherwise have been performing work for the district in any one fiscal year for the same
accident;

b.    Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year;

c.    Industrial accident or illness leave shall commence on the first day of absence;

d.    When a certificated employee is absent from his duties on account of an industrial accident or illness, he shall be paid
such portion of the salary due him for any month in which the absence occurs as, when added to his temporary
disability indemnity under Division 4 or Division 4.5 of the Labor Code, will result in a payment to him of not more
than his full salary;

The phrase "full salary" as utilized in this subdivision shall be computed so that it shall not be less than the employee's
"average weekly earnings" as that phrase is utilized in Section 4453 of the Labor Code. For purposes of this section,
however, the maximum and minimum average weekly earnings set forth in Section 4453 of the Labor Code shall
otherwise not be deemed applicable.

e.    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be reduced by one day for each day of authorized absence regardless of a
temporary disability indemnity award;

f.    When an industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the employee shall be entitled to only
the amount of unused leave due him for the same illness or injury.

Upon termination of the industrial accident or illness leave, the employee shall be entitled to the benefits provided in
Sections 44977,44978 and 44983, and for the purposes of each of these sections, his absence shall be deemed to have
commenced on the date of termination of the industrial accident or illness leave, provided that if the employee
continues to receive temporary disability indemnity, he may elect to take as much of his accumulated sick leave which,
when added to his temporary disability indemnity, will result in a payment to him of not more than his full salary.

The governing board may, by rule or regulation, provide for such additional leave of absence for industrial accident or
illness as it deems appropriate.

During any paid leave of absence, the employee may endorse to the district the temporary disability indemnity checks
received on account of his industrial accident or illness. The district, in turn, shall issue the employee appropriate salary
warrants for payment of the employee's salary and shall deduct normal retirement, other authorized contributions, and
the temporary disability indemnity, if any, actually paid to and retained by the employee for periods covered by such
salary warrants.

Any employee receiving benefits as a result of this section shall, during periods of injury or illness, remain within the
State of California unless the governing board authorizes travel outside the state.

In the absence of rules and regulations adopted by the governing board pursuant to this section an employee shall be
entitled to industrial accident or illness leave as provided in this section but without limitation as to the number of days
of such leave.

REDUCED WORKLOAD PROGRAM

The CalSTRS Reduced Workload Program allows eligible members to reduce their workload from full-time to part-time
duties (a minimum of 50 percent of full time) for up to ten years, normally the last ten years before retirement.

You will receive full-time service credit, while working less than full time. At the time of retirement, your allowance will
be calculated as though you continued to work full time.

You must be age 55 or older, have been employed full time for a minimum of 10 years performing creditable service and
have been employed full-time performing creditable service five consecutive years immediately prior to entering the
program.

Both member and employer contributions are paid on the full-time eamable salary, rather than the actual part-time salary
earned while participating in the program. Therefore, the employer will be required to pay a higher contribution rate for a
member to participate.

The consequences of failing to work at least 50 percent as required by the program can be significant. This occurs if the
member does not earn creditable compensation for at least 50 percent of full-time in any one school year. Service credit
for the year will be calculated on actual time worked.

For example, a member who was employed 50 percent of full time had her pay docked because she became ill and ran
out of sick leave. Consequently, she failed to perform creditable service for at least 50 percent of full time that year.
Therefore, for that year she did not earn the service credit of one year but rather received credit only for the actual time
worked.

Retiring before the end of the school year can also impact program participants in the Reduced Workload Program.

After discussing the Reduced Workload Program with your employer and reviewing personal qualifications, you must
enter into a contractual agreement with your employer to participate.
