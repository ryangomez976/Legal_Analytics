Article I Agreement

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement ("Agreement") by and between the Board of Trustees of the Mountain
Elementary School District ("District") and the Mountain Teachers Association/MTA
("Association"), an employee organization.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code ("Act").

1.3    This Agreement shall remain in full force and in effect from July 1, 2014 until October
1, 2015. The parties shall reopen yearly on salaries and benefits and each side may
choose two additional articles. Any further reopeners shall be by mutual agreement
only.

1.4    Individual contracts between the Board and individual unit members shall be subject to
and consistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

Article 2 Recognition

2.1    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all certificated
employees of the District, including the classroom teachers, the Independent Study
teacher, and the resource specialist, and excluding substitute certificated employees and
the District Administrator.

2.2    No other group or organization or representative thereof shall be recognized or
permitted to engage on behalf of any unit member included in the bargaining unit in any
activities concerning meeting and negotiating. However, this shall not preclude the right
of an individual unit member to present grievances on his/her own behalf.

Article 4 Negotiations Procedure

4.1    No later than the first regularly scheduled Board meeting in February of the calendar
year in which this Agreement expires, the Association shall submit its initial proposals
for a successor Agreement to the Board of Trustees.

4.2    The parties shall meet and negotiate in good faith on negotiable items on a successor
agreement beginning no later than five (5) days after the first regularly scheduled Board
meeting in March of the calendar year in which this Agreement expires. Any tentative
Agreement reached between the parties shall be reduced to writing and signed by them.

4.3    Within thirty (30) days of ratification of the Agreement by both parties herein, the
Board shall have sufficient copies prepared and delivered to the Association for
distribution to each unit member in the District.

4.4    Reopeners shall follow the procedural format of sections 4.1, 4.2, and 4.3.

4.5    The Parties shall respond in writing to contract language proposals no later than twenty-
one (21) calendar days after receipt.

Article 5 Maintenance/Application of Standards

5.1    The specific terms of this Agreement shall supersede any rules, regulations or practices
of the Board which are or may in the future be contrary to or inconsistent with its terms.

5.2    The provisions of this Agreement shall not be interpreted or applied in a manner which
is arbitrary, capricious or discriminatory. Rules which are designed to implement this
Agreement shall be uniform in application and effect.

Article 6 Effect of Agreement

6.1    Effect of Agreement

It is understood and agreed that the specific provisions contained in this Agreement
shall prevail over District practices and procedures to the extent they are inconsistent.

6.2    Savings Provision

If any provisions of this Agreement are held to be contrary to law by the highest court
of the State or United States, such provisions will not be deemed valid and subsisting
except to the extent permitted by law, but all other provisions will continue in full force
and effect.

6.3    Completion of Meeting and Negotiating

The terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement represent the full and complete
understanding between the parties hereto. The terms and conditions may be altered,
changed, added to, deleted from, or modified only through the voluntary, mutual
consent of the parties in a written amendment. During the term of this Agreement, the
Association expressly waives and relinquishes the right to meet and negotiate, and
agrees that the District shall not be obligated to meet and negotiate with respect to any
other subject or matter whether referred to or covered in this Agreement or not, even
though such subject or matters may not have been within the knowledge or
contemplation of either or both the District or the Association at the time they met and
negotiated on and executed this Agreement, and even though such subjects or matters
were proposed and later withdrawn.

Article 7 Statutory Changes

Any change in benefits included in this Agreement which are brought about by the
amendment or repeal of statutory guarantees now provided in California or federal law
shall obligate the parties within ten (10) days of such amendment or repeal to negotiate.

Article 8 Complaint Procedure

Parent/Guardian or Pupil complaints regarding any unit member, other than allegations
of unlawful discrimination covered by Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations,
sections 4600-4671, shall be resolved in the following manner:

8.1    Level I: If the nature of the complaint is such that it can be discussed and resolved at the
employee level, the complainant is urged to discuss this with the person involved.

8.2    Level II: Lacking resolution at Level I, or if the complaint cannot be appropriately
discussed at Level I, it may be brought to the level of the District Administrator. If the
complaint is not presented in written form, the Administrator shall provide a Complaint
Form to the complainant during the initial discussion with the Administrator so that the
complainant may reduce the complaint to writing. Following the initial discussion, the
District Administrator shall conduct an investigation into all matters concerning the
complaint. During this phase, all parties concerned with the matter shall be heard. The
District Administrator shall endeavor to resolve the complaint within ten (10) working
days of its presentation at Level II. The District Administrator shall issue a decision
which shall be final unless the complainant, the unit member, or the District
Administrator requests a hearing before the Board of Trustees.

8.3    Level III: The Board of Trustees shall hear all complaints brought before that body.

This hearing shall be in closed session unless both the unit member and the complainant
request that it be heard in open session. The District Administrator will prepare a
written report concerning the complaint. The report shall contain, but not be limited to,
the following:

1.    The name of each employee involved.

2.    A brief, but specific, summary of the nature of the complaint and the
facts surrounding it, sufficient to inform the Board of Trustees and the
employee(s) as to the precise nature of the complaint itself.

3.    A true copy of the signed original of the complaint itself.

4.    A summary of the action taken by the District Administrator in
connection with the complaint, with the District Administrator's specific
finding that disposition of the case at the District Administrator's level
has not been possible, and the reasons therefore.

Article 9 Grievance Procedure

9.1 Definitions

9.1.1 A "grievance" Is an allegation that a grievant has been directly and adversely
affected by a violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of the specific
provisions of this Agreement.

4

9.1.2    A "grievant" is any unit member covered by the terms of this Agreement
except when there is an alleged violation of Association rights as provided in
Article 4, Negotiations Procedure, and Article 6, Effect of Agreement, in
which case the Association may be the grievant.

9.1.3    A "day" is any day that the District Office is open.

9.2    Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise in the
implementation of this Agreement which affect the welfare or working conditions of
teachers. Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal and
confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the procedure.

9.3    Procedure

9.3.1    Informal Level

Prior to initiating a formal written grievance, the potential grievant shall
meet with his/her immediate supervisor and attempt to resolve the potential
grievance informally. If the informal procedure does not resolve the
difficulty, the grievant may proceed to Level I.

9.3.2    Level I

9.3.2.1    Within ten (10) days after the grievant knew or reasonably should
have known of the alleged violation of the Agreement the
grievant shall present a grievance in writing using the grievance
form. The District Administrator shall meet with the aggrieved
party and/or designated Association representative, if any, within
ten (10) days of receipt of the grievance. The District
Administrator shall provide a written disposition the grievance,
including the reasons therefore, within ten (10) days of such
meeting.

9.3.2.2    If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of the
grievance, the grievance may be appealed to Level II.

9.3.2.3    Failure by a grievant to appeal a decision within the specified
time limits shall be deemed an acceptance of the decision.

5

9.3.3

Level II

9.3.3.1    The grievant shall notify the District Administrator in writing
within three (3) days of receipt of the Level I decision of the
decision to proceed to Level II. Within thirty (30) days of receipt
of notification, the District Administrator shall schedule a hearing
before the Board of Trustees and shall notify the grievant in
writing of the time and place.

9.3.3.2    The grievant and his/her representative, if any, shall be given an
opportunity at the hearing to present witnesses and documentary
evidence and to make arguments in his/her behalf.

9.3.3.3    The Board shall render a written decision within thirty (30) days
of the hearing, giving reasons for its decision. The decision of
the Board shall be final.

9.4 Miscellaneous Provisions

9.4.1    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by the District and/or the Association
against anyone by reason of participation in the grievance procedure or
support of any participant thereto.

9.4.2    All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a
grievance shall be filed separately from the personnel files of the
participants.

9.4.3    During the pendency of any proceeding and until a determination has been
reached, all proceedings shall be private and any preliminary disposition will
not be made known to the non-school public without the written agreement
of the parties.

9.4.4    The specified time limits in this procedure may be extended by mutual
agreement in writing between the District and the grievant.

9.4.5    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be processed
through all the levels of the procedure by the last working day of the school
year, the time limits set forth herein will be reduced by mutual agreement so
that the procedure may be completed prior to the end of the school year, or
as soon as practicable.

9.4.6    A unit member may be represented at all stages by legal counsel, by a
representative or representatives of the Association, or by any other
individual who does not represent an employee organization, as defined in
Government Code section 3540.1, subdivision (d), other than the
Association.

9.4.7

The grievant, representative, and any necessary witness shall be granted

9.4.8

release time to attend any hearing required by this grievance process.

When the grievant is not represented by the Association, the Association
shall be informed of any pending decision on a written grievance and shall
have the right to present in writing its views on the grievance prior to final
decision.

9.4.9

Grievances of a similar or like nature may be joined as a single grievance by
the Association.

Article 10 Organizational Security

10.1 Dues Deduction

10.1.1

The right of payroll deduction for payment of organizational dues shall be
accorded to the Association. Association members who currently have
authorization cards on file for the above purposes need not be resolicited.
Association dues and fees, upon formal written request from the association
to the District, shall be increased or decreased without resolicitation and
authorization from unit members.

10.1.2

Pursuant to authorization by the unit member, the District shall deduct one-
tenth (1/10) of the Association dues and fees from the regular salary check
each month. Deductions for unit members who sign such authorization after
commencement of the school year shall be appropriately prorated to
complete the payment by the end of the school year.

10.1.3

With respect to all sums deducted by the District pursuant to authorization of
the unit member, for membership dues, the District agrees promptly to remit
such monies to the Association alone, with an alphabetical list of unit
members for whom such deductions have been made and any changes that
may have occurred since the previous list.

10.2 Maintenance of Membership

10.2.1

The Association and the District agree that any unit member who is a
member of the Association at the time this Agreement becomes effective or
who enrolls during the term of the Agreement shall maintain such
membership from year to year unless revoked in writing between July 1 and
July 31 of the year in which this Agreement erminates. The District will
guarantee said maintenance of membership to the Association by enforcing
payment of dues by members required under the terms set forth above and
provisions of the Education Code and Government Code section 3540.1 (i)
(1).

10.3 Fair Share

10.3.1    Any unit member who is a member of the Mountain Teachers' Association
CTA/NEA, or who has applied for membership, may sign and deliver to the
district an assignment authorizing deduction of unified membership dues,
initiation fees and general assessments in the Association. Pursuant to such
authorization, the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues from

pay to the MTA a fee in an amount equal to unified membership dues and
general assessments, payable to the MTA in one lump-sum payment. In the

MTA shall notify the District in writing. The District shall then immediately
begin automatic payroll deduction as provided in Education Code Section
45061 and in the same manner as set forth in Article 10, section 1. There
shall be no charge to the MTA for such mandatory deductions. Payment of
such fee shall be a condition of continued employment.

employee organizations shall not be required to join or financially support
Mountain Teachers' Association, CTA/NEA as a condition of employment;
except that such unit member shall pay, in lieu of a service fee, sums equal
to such service fee to either a non-religious, non-labor organization or
charitable fund exempt from taxation under section 501 (c)(3) of Title 26 of
the Internal Revenue Code.

10.3.4    Proof of payment and a written statement of objection along with verifiable
evidence of membership in a religious body whose traditional tenets or
teachings object to joining or financially supporting employee organizations,
pursuant to section 10.3.3 above, shall be made on an annual basis to the
District as a condition of continued exemption from the provisions of
sections 10.3.1 and 10.3 .2 above. Proof of payment shall be in the form of
receipts and/or canceled checks indicating the amount paid, date of payment,
and to whom payment in lieu of the service fee has been made. Such proof
shall be presented on or before October 31 of each school year. The MTA
shall have the right of inspection in order to review said proof of payment.

Article 11 Association Rights

11.1    Mail Facilities

The Association shall have the right to use unit member mailboxes for communications
to unit members without prior examination of such communications by the employer.
All communications from the Association shall be so identified.

11.2    Bulletin Boards

The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities and matters of
Association concern on an Association bulletin board, which shall be provided in an
area frequented by unit members.

11.3    Access to Worksite

Authorized representatives of the Association shall have the right to transact official
Association business on school property and utilize District facilities at all reasonable

The Board, upon request by the Association, agrees to furnish to the Association, within
five (5) days, or as soon as is practicable, all available public information concerning
the financial resources and professional staffing of the District. Such information shall
include, but not be limited to: Annual financial reports and audits, register of
certificated personnel, tentative budgetary requirements and allocations, agendas and

minutes of all Board meetings and all attachments thereto at the time of distribution to
the Board, census and membership data, names, addresses and phone numbers of all
consenting unit members, salaries paid thereto, educational background, and other
information that may be used in negotiations and processing grievances.

Article 13 Procedures for Evaluation

13.1 Evaluations will be conducted according to the following procedures:

13.1.1    All probationary teachers shall be evaluated annually. Permanent teachers
shall be evaluated at least every two years. Probationary unit members who
have received satisfactory (exceeds or meets ratings) evaluations for three

(3) consecutive years shall be evaluated at least every two years.
Probationary teachers who do not receive satisfactory evaluations for three

(3) consecutive years shall return to the annual evaluation cycle. If a unit
member is scheduled to be evaluated during a particular school year, but is
granted a leave of absence for one (1) semester or longer, such evaluation
shall take place during the first year of return to duty.. This evaluation cycle
and related timelines apply to all unit members eligible to participate in the
Professional Growth option outlined in Section 13.1.11

13.1.2    By October 1, the evaluator will notify each unit member if s/he is subject to
the formal evaluation process and provide unit members who are to be
evaluated with a copy of the evaluation procedures and general criteria on
which the evaluation is to be based.

13.1.3    The unit member being evaluated and the evaluator shall meet no later than
November 1, to discuss:

13.1.3.1    Goals, objectives and standards to be achieved during the
evaluation period;

13.1.3.2    A schedule of the first formal observation and post conference.
The summative evaluation conference will be completed no later
than May 1.

10

13.1.4    In the event of a disagreement over the goals, objectives, standards and/or
evaluation schedule, the unit member and the evaluator shall make a good
faith effort to resolve the differences between them. If the differences are
not resolved, the evaluation shall proceed according to the goals, objectives,
standards and schedule proposed by the evaluator, but the evaluates shall
have the right to identify the differences which remain and attach a statement
regarding those differences to the final evaluation document.

13.1.5    The basis for evaluation shall be:

13.1.5.1    Board-adopted criteria

13.1.5.2    The standards for evaluation required by Education Code section
44662, which include: a) the progress of pupils toward the Board-
established standards of expected pupil achievement at each level
in each area of study; b) the instructional techniques and
strategies used by the unit member; c) the unit member's
adherence to curricular objectives; and d) the establishment and
maintenance of a suitable learning environment, within the scope
of the unit member's responsibilities.

13.1.5.3    The individual goals and obj ectives set for the unit members at
the beginning of the school year.

13.1.5.4    Any school-wide obj ectives set for the staff. The unit member
shall be provided with a copy of these specific criteria by
November 1, of the year in which s/he is to be evaluated.

13.1.6    The District Administrator shall observe all certificated instruction on a
continuing basis.

13.1.6.1 Formal observations: At least two written observations of at least
one-half (1/2) hour each, preceded by at least two (2) days'
advance notice, shall be part of every formal evaluation. A
conference between the evaluator and unit member shall be held
within three (3) school days following a formal observation, and
any commendations, deficiencies, and suggestions will be noted
in writing. Written records of formal observations shall include
references where appropriate, to the criteria, standards, goals and
objectives relevant to the specific observation. A record of all
formal observations shall be attached to the final evaluation
summary. The specific evaluation system to be utilized shall be
determined by the District Administrator. (BP 4415). These tools
shall be consistent with the criteria outlined in 13.1.5. 11

11

13.1.6.2 Additional observations shall not be subject to a minimum length
and may be performed with or without advance notice. Such
observations may, but need not be, followed by a conference
and/or written comments.

13.1.7    If the observations and/or evaluation disclose any areas in which
improvement is needed, the evaluator shall offer positive assistance aimed at
achieving the needed improvement. The unit member shall take action to
correct any cited deficiencies, based upon the evaluator's specific
recommendations for improvement and his/her assistance in implementing
such recommendations, which may include one or more of the following:

13.1.7.1    Specific recommendations for improvement;

13.1.7.2    Direct assistance to implement such recommendations;

13.1.7.3    Provision of additional resources to be utilized to assist with
improvement;

13.1.7.4    Techniques to measure improvement;

13.1.7.5    Time schedule to monitor progress.

13.1.8    The evaluation of unit members shall not include or be based upon
standardized achievement test results or achievement of objectives stated in
Individual Educational Programs (IEPS) of special education students.

13.1.9    During the course of the evaluation period, circumstances may change which
require modification of the original objectives and standards. The unit
member or the District Administrator may initiate a change of these
objectives and standards in the manner prescribed in sections 13.1.3 and

13.1.4 above. A signed copy of the summative evaluation will be given to
the unit member within 5 days after the summative evaluation conference.

13.1.10    A unit member shall not be evaluated on matters not directly observed by the
District Administrator unless the District Administrator has notified and
discussed them with the unit member. All matters subject to evaluation will
be correlated to the California Standards for the Teaching Profession
(CSTPs).

13.1.11 A professional growth plan option will be available to eligible unit members.

Probationary or permanent unit members who have received satisfactory
(exceeds or meets ratings) evaluations for three (3) consecutive years may
elect to participate in this alternative to formal evaluation. Formal
observations are not required for those participating in the professional
growth plan option. Professional growth plan components are outlined in
13.1.12, 13.1.13, 13.1.14 and 13.1.15.

13.1.12    Professional Growth Plan Obj ectives

a.    To offer an incentive for a unit member to accept responsibility for
and assume leadership in expanding his/her professional growth

b.    To offer opportunities for teachers to integrate innovative techniques
and strategies into their professional practice

13.1.13    Unit members requesting a professional growth plan cycle in lieu of formal
evaluation shall submit a written proposal including a description of project
focus no later than October 15. Professional growth plans shall include a
clear goal statement, description of student learning outcomes, relevant
timelines, and how the project will enhance teacher effectiveness in
instructional and/or assessment practices. In determining project focus,
teachers shall consider district strategic goals and current research. Unit
members will be notified by November 1 regarding project approval by the
District Administrator. If revisions should be required prior to approval, these
will be provided by the District Administrator. Absent mutual agreement on
required revisions, the cycle will revert back to formal evaluation.

13.1.14    District Administrator role:

a.    Encourage qualified staff to participate

b.    Continue to informally observe teachers and offer feedback.

c.    Provide input, discussion, guidance, and appropriate resources

13.1.15    A signed copy of the final evaluation of the professional growth plan
outcomes will be submitted for the personnel file by the unit member to the
District Administrator by May 15. Written comments from the District
Administrator will be attached to the plan.

13.2.2    Materials in the personnel file of a unit member, except as noted below, shall

the unit member's file or accompany the unit member in his/her review of
the file. Material which may be excluded from inspection shall be limited to

13.2.2.1    Were obtained prior to the employment of the unit member
involved.

13.2.2.2    Were prepared by identifiable examination committee members.

13.2.2.3 Were obtained in connection with a promotional exam. Unit
members shall have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of

13.2.3    Information of a derogatory nature, except material excluded in accordance

Article 14 Teaching Hours/Adjunct Duties

14.1    A unit member shall report for duty thirty minutes prior to the beginning of the regular
student school day and will remain after the last dismissal time until professional
responsibilities have been completed.

14.2    Unit members shall meet with the District Administrator at the beginning of each school
year in order to devise a schedule which includes bathroom breaks for unit members as
needed during morning recess.

14.3    Participation in non-instructional assignments shall be limited to thirty (30) hours per
semester. The Site Council assignment shall rotate among employees to ensure
fairness. Participation on all other District committees shall be voluntary.

14.4    The District Administrator may assign additional instructional duties to the kindergarten
teacher beyond the kindergarten instructional day if the kindergarten teacher's
instructional day is shorter than the primary instructional day.

14.5    Unit members shall provide instruction for 180 days per year. In addition, unit
members shall render service for one day prior to the first day of student instruction.
There shall be one student-free day immediately following the conclusion of the student
instructional year.

Article 15 Safety

15.1    Unit members shall not be required to work under unsafe or hazardous conditions.

15.2    All unit members shall report unsafe working conditions to the District Administrator
immediately upon the identification of said condition. The District shall take
reasonable steps necessary to correct such condition.

15.3    Unit members shall immediately report cases of student assault suffered by them in

15.4    At least once each school year the District Administrator shall meet with

concerns.

Article 16 Leaves

16.1 Illness/Injury Leave

16.1.1    Each unit member shall be entitled to ten (10) days leave of absence for
illness or injury during the school year, available to the unit member from
the first work day of each year. Any days not used by the unit member shall
be accumulated and carried over to the succeeding year. Part-time
employees shall earn sick leave credit on a pro-rated basis consistent with
their percentage assignment.

16.1.2    When the unit member's absence exceeds the earned sick leave, the unit
member shall be paid the difference between the unit member's regular
salary and the amount actually paid a substitute employee employed to fill
the unit member's position during his/her absence, or, if no substitute was
employed, the amount which would have been paid had a substitute been
employed. This salary rate shall commence when the sick leave ends and
shall continue up to a period of five (5) school months.

16.1.3    The District may request verification from a doctor or other recognized
practitioner after the unit member has been absent five (5) consecutive days.

16.1.4    The District shall provide each unit member with an accounting of the
number of days of illness/injury leave s/he has accumulated, plus the number
of days to which the unit member is entitled for the current school year. An
accounting of such days shall be provided to unit members by November 1,
of each school year.

15

16.1.5    A unit member shall have the right to utilize illness/injury leave provided for
in this Article and the benefits provided for by Section 44977 of the
Education Code for absences necessitated by pregnancy, miscarriage,
childbirth and recovery therefrom.

16.1.6    The use of sick leave for pregnancy disability shall be treated the same as
any other disability for which sick leave is granted.

16.2    Parental Leave

An unpaid leave of absence may be granted to a unit member for the purpose of raising
his/her biological or adopted child. Such leave shall normally be for no more than
twelve (12) months duration. Additional leave may be granted pursuant to 16.10,
below.

16.3    Personal Necessity Leave

16.3.1 Personal Necessity Leave shall be limited to circumstances that necessitate
immediate attention, and cannot be taken care of after work hours or on
weekends. A maximum of ten (10) days of accumulated sick leave may be
used in any school year for Personal Necessity Leave. Personal Necessity
Leave shall not be used for vacation, recreation, or personal convenience, or
for extension of weekends or holidays.

16.3.2 Notice of intent to use this leave shall be given to the District Administrator
as far in advance as possible. Advance notice shall not be required for leave
taken for any of the following reasons:

16.3.2.1

Death or serious illness of a member of the unit member's
immediate family;

16.3.2.2

Accident, involving the person or property of the unit member or
a member of his/her immediate family;

16.3.2.3

Illness of a family member which requires the unit member's
presence because the nature of the illness makes advance
alternative planning impossible.

16.3.3 Personal Leave - Four (4) of ten (10) sick leave days shall be deemed as
Personal Leave days and may be used at unit members' discretion with the
exception of extension of weekends or holidays. Sick leave records will be

maintained in the district office and available for unit members review
upon request.

16.4 Family Care Leave

Family Care Leave shall be granted pursuant to the requirements of the California
Family Care Leave Act (Gov. Code, 12945.2,) and implementing regulations.

16.5.1

A unit member shall be granted leave of absence for five (5) days for the

16.5.2

Immediate family means mother, stepmother, father, stepfather, sibling,

grandchild of the unit member or of the unit member's spouse, and the

16.7.1

Allowable leave shall be for the number of days of temporary disability but

Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

16.7.4    The leave under these rules and regulations shall commence on the first day

16.7.5    When a unit member is absent from his/her duties on account of an industrial
accident or illness, s/he shall be paid such portion of the salary due him/her
for any month in which the absence occurs as, when added to his/her
temporary disability indemnity under Division 4 or Division 4.5 of the Labor
Code, will result in a payment to him/her of not more than his/her full salary.

16.7.6    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be reduced by one day for each day

deduct normal retirement, other authorized contributions, and the temporary
disability indemnity, if any, actually paid to and retained by the employee
for periods covered by such salary warrants.

16.7.10    Any unit member receiving benefits as a result of this Section shall, during

Article 17 Health and Welfare Benefits

17.1a The District shall provide eligible unit members a health and welfare allowance of
up to $868.96 per month to be used for health, dental and vision insurance for each
unit member and their eligible dependents. This allowance cap will increase three
percent (3%) per year.

The total district benefit contribution for the 2014-15 school year (October 1, 2014-
September 30, 2015) will be $10.427.51 per employee, which is a total of $868.96
monthly per employee.

17.1b Unit members shall choose either the PPO, HMO High, HMO Middle, or HMO
Low plan offered by the current SISC health care provider.

17.1.2    Dental coverage shall be Delta Dental Plan of California. Unit members may
select one of the "Non-Voluntary Dependents" Plans. Unit members must
adhere to the requirements of the plan.

17.1.3    Vision care shall be provided by the Vision Service Plan.

17.1.4    The health, dental, and vision plan must be those offered in the County
Schools subject to the requirements of the carrier.

17.1.5    Unit members eligible for this coverage are those working 50% or greater.
Unit members working part-time on September 1, 1996 will retain eligibility
for coverage, regardless of the percentage worked, as long as they are
employed by the District.

17.1.6 Unit members not eligible for this coverage shall have the option to receive
District insurance coverage upon reimbursement to the District, subject to
the requirements of the carrier.

17.2    Retirees and eligible dependents shall have the option to continue to receive District

insurance coverage upon reimbursement to the District.

17.3    Duration of Benefits

17.3.1    Unit members who work a complete school year shall have fringe benefits
under the District's fringe benefit program effective through the last day of
August. Unit members who are employed subsequent to the first day of the
school year shall have their fringe benefits effective from the first day of
active employment, subject to the requirements of the carrier.

17.3.2    Should a unit member's employment terminate following the last day of the
school year and before the commencement of the ensuing school year, such
unit member shall be entitled to continue District paid coverage under all
insurance plans until August 31, of the ensuing school year.

17.3.3    Unit members on Board approved unpaid leaves of absence shall have the
option to continue to receive District insurance coverage for the period of the
leaves upon reimbursement to the District, subject to the requirements of the
carrier.

accepted, providing the private school was state accredited and the unit member in

program for salary schedule placement and advancement purposes,
consistent with 18.4, below.

year of service, except those whose placement is at the maximum step for their class.
For purposes of this section, a year shall be defined as at least seventy-five percent of

assigned to such restricted programs.

Article 21 Independent Study Teacher

21.1    The Independent Study Teacher will be a certificated employee.

21.2    The Independent Study Teacher will be initially compensated on the basis of the
percentage of the annual salary specified on the District's salary schedule for that
teacher which reflects the ratio between the District Independent Study enrollment on
the first day of school and the average class enrollment in the non-independent study
classes of the District on the same day up to 100%. Such annual salary shall be adjusted
up to 100% on the basis of Independent Study average daily attendance as a percentage
of the average class daily attendance at the first principal report of apportionment and
again at the second principal report of apportionment.

21.3    The Independent Study Teacher will have the same responsibilities as a regular
classroom teacher, (i.e., attend faculty meetings, participate in SBCP day in-services,
serve on District and Board committees, etc.).

21.4    The Independent Study Teacher will accrue seniority based on the same percentage
used for base salary calculations.
