3549.3, of the Government Code.

exclusive groups of certificated employees: certificated employees of the
Plumas Unified School District, and certificated employees of the Plumas
County Superintendent of Schools.

1.03    The Agreement shall continue in full force and effect until altered by negotiation as
provided in Section 3, Negotiation Procedures.

SECTION 3 - NEGOTIATION PROCEDURES

3.01    At the first regularly scheduled meeting in February, of the Governing Board of the
Plumas Unified School District and the Plumas County Board of Education, of any

Board.

them.

3.03    Negotiations shall take place at mutually agreeable times and places.

3.04    The Association shall designate the president, or his/her designee, and five members who
shall each receive a reasonable number of hours per week without loss of compensation
to attend negotiations and impasse proceedings.

SECTION 5 - SAFETY CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

5.01    The Board shall provide safe and healthful working conditions for all unit members and
students.

5.02    A. It shall be the responsibility of the Board to establish and publish a safety plan for

the district.

connection with their employment to their principal or other immediate superior who
shall immediately report the incident to the superintendent and the Sheriffs Office.

5.05    Saturday School Safety and Class Location: Each school site will determine the location
of this program by a committee. There will be a ten-minute unsupervised break mid¬
morning for the teachers and the students. The teacher will have a telephone available in
the room and an administrator/designee on call. The teacher will have the right to
suspend a student from the program.

SECTION 6 - CLASS SIZE

6.01    Class Size Staffing: The District will strive to maintain a 30:1 staffing ratio in grades 4-

12. As long as the State continues to fund the class size reduction program for grades K-
3, the District may implement that program. Economic factors including level of State
support, liabilities, and revenue uncertainties will be considered in establishing staffing
ratio. Unit members such as special education teachers, psychologists, and other support
personnel will be excluded when computing the staffing ratio.

6.02    School Site Class Size Committees:    A    school    site    committee    of no less than three

teachers and the principal or designee shall be convened at any school site which exceeds
the staffing ratio established in 6.01. The committee shall review staffing and class loads
to insure fairness and appropriate placement. The committee shall operate on a
consensus basis to resolve problems dealing with class loads and assignments. At school
sites with fewer than three teachers, all teachers shall serve on the committee. The
committee shall consider the recommendations of other staff in making any decisions.

SECTION 7 - HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

7.01 A. The work day for full-time unit members in the Plumas Unified School District
shall begin 30 minutes before the time at which regular classes of the school are
scheduled to begin as a matter of usual practice. It shall close not earlier than 30
minutes after the conclusion of all regularly scheduled classes in the afternoon for
that school on that day.

The bell schedule for the comprehensive high school in each attendance area will be
the measure for determining the work day for Independent Study Staff,
Continuation Staff, Itinerant Staff, and Home School Staff.

If a specially scheduled class extends beyond the regular school day, the work day
shall be extended only for the teachers of the extended class (i.e. a kindergarten
which begins before or goes beyond the rest of the school, or an "X" or "A" period
drama or music class).

B.    When unit members have other school-related responsibilities on a given day which
require their attendance during hours beyond the school day, they may leave
directly following their last regular school period assignment, after prior
notification and clearance with their immediate supervisor.

C.    During the periods of time before classes begin and after classes are concluded,
when unit members are required to be at school, unit members shall make
themselves available for supervision of students, conferences with students, with
parents, with administrators and supervisors, or with student groups which might be
part of the extra curricular assignments, and unless their duties require their
presence elsewhere, they shall remain at their duty stations, e.g. classroom,
counseling office.

D.    Unit members shall be afforded a duty-free lunch period of no less than 30
consecutive minutes each day. Should the scheduled elementary school lunch
period be longer than 30 minutes, the unit member at the elementary school shall
have the entire period duty free.

E.    The principal may, whenever possible, seek a teacher volunteer to provide noon
duty supervision in exchange for time for time. Time for time in this instance is
defined as an authorization from the principal to leave school after student
dismissal without staying the contractual 30 minutes.

8.02    Definitions.

A.    A grievance is an alleged violation, misapplication, or misinterpretation of specific
provisions of this contract.

B.    The "grievant" is any unit member, including the Association or representative
thereof, filing the grievance.

C.    A "day" is any day in which the central administrative office of the Plumas Unified
School District is open for business. All communication for the purpose of this
section between the grievant or his/her representative and the administration shall
be between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 4:30 p.m.

D.    The "immediate supervisor" at the school level is the principal; at other than the
school level, the administrator designated by the superintendent.

8.03    Any grievant may present a grievance relating to specific sections of the contract to
his/her employer and have such grievance(s) addressed and resolved consistent with
terms of this Agreement. There shall be no resolution of the grievance until the
Association has received a copy of the grievance and the proposed resolution.

8.04    The grievant has the right to have a representative present at any step of the grievance
procedure. The grievant, however, must be present at each step of the grievance
procedure.

8.05    The purpose of the procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to any grievance(s). Both parties agree that these proceedings will be
kept confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the procedure.

8.06    The filing of the grievance shall not be construed as reflecting unfavorably upon a unit
member's or immediate supervisor's good standing, performance, loyalty, or desirability
to the Association or Board. Grievant, grievant representatives, and all other persons
involved in the presentation of a grievance shall be free from restraint, interference,
coercion, discrimination, or reprisal.

described in this section will be conducted independently at both sites. Should the
overall evaluation rating on the two evaluations vary by more than one descriptor,

disparity. A PCTA representative may attend this meeting at the unit member’s
request. A district administrator may attend this meeting at an evaluator’s request.

as defined in their job descriptions. Upon request, unit members will be provided a

20.01    It is the policy of the board to educate students in a democratic tradition; to foster a
recognition of individual freedom and social responsibility and to inspire meaningful
awareness of, and respect for, the Constitution and the Bill of Rights. The Governing
Board believes that the existence and progress of a democratic society demands that there
be freedom in the teaching of the curriculum, as approved by the Board, and freedom of
learning in the schools. It is recognized that democratic values can best be transmitted in
an atmosphere which is fee from censorship* and in which academic freedom for the
teacher and student is encouraged. The Board accepts as one of its major responsibilities
the protection of these freedoms for students, teachers, and others directly concerned with
the school program.

* "Censorship" defined - The denial of right of freedom of press, right of freedom of
speech and of all those rights and privileges which are held under a free
government. Blacks Law Dictionary

20.02    The Governing Board has the responsibility to ensure that the school fulfills its
obligations to society at the local, state, and national levels. The obligation on behalf of
academic freedom requires that the Board cooperate with educational personnel and with
the community in a judicious and open-minded manner in making policies and decisions
for the preservation of that freedom.

20.03    It is mutually recognized that freedom carries with it responsibility; academic freedom
also carries with it academic responsibility. Discussion and analysis of controversial
issues should be conducted within the framework of the fundamental values of the
community as a whole as they are expressed in the educational philosophy and objectives
of the Board.

20.04    Within the frame of reference set forth in Section 20.03, and as it pertains to the course to
which a teacher is assigned, academic freedom in the schools is defined as:

A.    The right of teachers to a free expression of conscience as private citizens with the
responsibility of a professional presentation of balanced views relating to
controversial issues within the context of the course being taught. It is understood
that teachers shall not use the classroom as a vehicle to promote personal beliefs.

B.    The responsibility to use materials which are relevant to the levels of ability and
maturity of the students and consistent with the adopted District course of study
and reading lists.

C.    The responsibility of teachers to allow students to hold divergent ideas as long as
the expression of dissent is done within the guidelines of debate and discussion
which are generally acceptable in a normal classroom environment.
