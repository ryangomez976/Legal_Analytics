This is an Agreement made and entered into by and between the Luther Burbank
School (hereinafter referred to as “District”) and the Luther Burbank Education
Association; LBEA/CTA/NEA (hereinafter referred to as “Association”).

RECOGNITION

The District confirms its recognition of the Association as the exclusive representative
for that unit of employees recognized by the District, per its Resolution dated May 11,
1976. The representation unit consists of all certificated teachers who work in the
Luther Burbank School; and does not include management, confidential, supervisory,
intermittent part time (defined as not regularly scheduled), substitute, or non teaching
personnel.

Neither the District nor the Association shall unlawfully discriminate against any unit
member on the basis of race, color, creed, age, sex, national origin, membership or non
membership in or participation in or refusal to participate in the activities of an employee
organization.

ARTICLE 5
LEAVE AND REASSIGNMENT POLICIES

5.1 Personal Illness and Injury Leave

5.1.1    Full time unit members shall be entitled to ten (10) days leave with full pay
each school year for purposes of personal illness or injury. Part time unit
members who work less than five (5) days per week shall be entitled to
that proportion of ten (10) days as the number of days employed per week
bears to five (5).

5.1.2    If a unit member does not utilize the full amount of leave as authorized in

5.1.1 above in any school year, the amount not utilized shall be
accumulated from year to year.

5.1.3    Upon request by District Management, a unit member shall be required to
present a medical doctor’s certificate verifying that the absence was due
to personal injury or illness. When such a verification is required by the
District, the doctor will be selected by the District and the fee for such an
examination will be paid for by the District unless the doctor concludes
that the absence was not due to personal illness or injury. Further, if the
doctor concludes that the absence was not due to personal illness or
injury, or that the illness was not sufficiently severe to warrant absence
from work, then the Superintendent, after notice to the unit member, may
refuse to grant such leave. The doctor will make all necessary inquiries in
order to be fully informed as to the nature and severity of the illness or
injury and report such finding to the Superintendent.

5.1.4    If a unit member is absent for illness or injury for a period of time in excess
of ten (10) consecutive days, the unit member shall remain on sick leave
and shall not return to work until he/she submits his/her medical doctor’s
authorization to return to work.

5.1.5    Whenever possible, a unit member must contact the District as soon as
the need to be absent is known but in no event less than two (2) hours
prior to the start of the work day to permit the District time to secure
substitute service. Failure to provide adequate notice shall be grounds for
denial of leave with pay or other disciplinary action.

5.1.6    A unit member who is absent for one half (1/2) day or less, may, in the
discretion of the Superintendent, have deducted one half (1/2) day from
the accumulated leave; and if the absence exceeds more than one half
(1/2) day in a work day, a full one (1) day may, in the discretion of the
Superintendent, be deducted from accumulated leave. For purposes of
this Article, one half (1/2) day is defined to mean three and one half (3 1/2)

7:50-11:30 a.m. and 11:30 a.m.-3:00 p.m.

member fails to notify the District of the unit member’s intent to return to

5.2 Personal Necessity Leave

5.2.1    Leave which is credited under 5.1.1 of this Article may be used, at the unit
member’s election, for purposes of personal necessity.

5.2.2    For purposes of this provision, personal necessity shall be limited to:

a.    Death or serious illness of a member of the unit member’s
immediate family;

b.    An accident which is unforeseen involving the unit member’s
person or family;

c.    Religious holidays (provided written notice is given one (1) week in
advance of the day to be taken);

d.    Three (3) days for unspecified personal use provided written notice
is given (which may not be used the day before or the day after a
holiday or a weekend).

e.    Other personal necessities which are allowed at the discretion of
the Superintendent or designee.

5.2.3    Before the utilization of personal necessity leave, a unit member must
obtain prior written approval from the Superintendent or designee except
for cases a. and b. in Section 5.2.2. Should the circumstances outlined in

a. and b. arise, the employee shall make every effort to comply with
District procedures to enable the District to secure a substitute.

5.2.4    Under circumstances where prior approval was not obtained, a unit
member shall verify in writing that the leave was used only for purposes as
set forth above. A unit member will be subject to appropriate discipline if
the leave was used for purposes other than stipulated.

5.3 Bereavement Leave

5.3.1    A unit member shall be entitled to five (5) days of absence, without loss of
salary, on account of the death of any member of his immediate family. A
unit member shall be entitled to an additional two (2) days of paid
bereavement leave if travel of 500 or more miles is required to attend the
funeral services.

5.3.2    For the purposes of this Agreement, immediate family is defined as the
unit member’s spouse, mother, mother-in-law, step-mother, father, father-
in-law, step-father, brother, brother-in-law, step-brother, sister, sister-in-
law, step-sister, son, son-in-law, step-son, daughter, daughter-in-law,
step-daughter, the grandmother, grandfather, or grandchild of either the
unit member or the unit member’s spouse, or any relative living in the
immediate household of the unit member.

5.3.3    A unit member shall notify the District as soon as possible and also state
the expected duration of the absence to enable the District to secure a
substitute.

5.3.4    Upon return from the leave, the District shall require that a unit member
verify in writing that bereavement leave was used only for the purpose set
forth in 5.3.1. Verification forms shall be given to the unit member by the
District.

5.4 Leave for Pregnancy Disability

5.4.1    Unit members are entitled to leave without pay if disabled by pregnancy,
childbirth or a related medical condition under the same terms and
conditions as govern other leaves for disability. Unit members are entitled
to use accumulated sick leave as set forth in 5.1 during the pregnancy
disability leave and extended sick leave as provided by Education Code
Section 44977. A unit member is disabled by pregnancy if in the opinion of
her own doctor or other licensed health care practitioner she is unable
because of pregnancy, childbirth or a related medical condition to perform
the essential duties of her job or to perform these duties without undue
risk to herself or other persons. The length of such leave, including the
date on which such leave shall commence and the date on which duties
are to be resumed, shall be determined by the unit member and the unit
member’s physician.

5.4.2    The unit member on leave for pregnancy disability will be returned to her
original job or a substantially similar job.

5.5 Judicial Leave

5.5.1    Unit members will be provided leave for regularly called jury duty for
minimum jury duty service called under law. The unit member shall submit

5.6    Industrial Accident or Illness Leave

5.6.1    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be allowed to a maximum of sixty
(60) days during which the school is required to be in session or when the
employee would otherwise have been performing work for the District in
any one (1) fiscal year for the same accident. The other provisions for
such leaves provided for in the California Education Code are
incorporated herein, but are expressly excluded from the Grievance
Procedure, Article 9.

5.6.2    Employees who sustain an industrial accident or illness shall report such
to the District, on forms provided by the District, within two (2) workdays of
such accident or illness.

5.7    Sabbatical Leave

After the completion of seven (7) consecutive years of full time employment, unit
members may, upon the approval of the Board of Trustees, be allowed not more
than one (1) year sabbatical leave, subject to the following conditions:

5.7.1    The requested leave shall be for the purpose of permitting the unit
member to study, travel, or work in individual projects which will benefit
the school and pupils of the District;

5.7.2    An application for such leave shall be presented to the Superintendent at
least ninety (90) days before the proposed leave is to commence and shall
specify the length of sabbatical requested;

5.7.3    If more than one (1) unit member requests sabbatical leave for the same
period of time, or for overlapping periods of time, the unit member with the
longest seniority in the District will be granted precedence;

5.7.4    The leave shall be without salary, but with a continuation of fringe benefits.

5.8 Other Leaves Without Pay

5.8.1    Upon recommendation of the Superintendent and approval by the Board
of Trustees, leave without compensation, increment, seniority, or tenure
credit, but with continuation of benefits, may be granted. The employee
may elect non continuation of benefits.

5.8.2    The applications for and granting of such leave of absence shall be in

member has requested from the Superintendent an extension of the leave
of absence in writing at least 90 days before the termination of such leave.

America, or the State of California, during any period of local or national
emergency declared by the President or Governor or during any war in
which the United States of America is engaged, shall be entitled to absent
himself/herself from his duties with the District.

After honorably leaving such service or being placed on inactive duty , the
unit member shall be entitled as provided by state and federal law to
return to the position held at the time military service was entered upon, at
the salary such unit member would have been entitled to had military
service not intervened.

5.9 Reassignment

The District shall make preliminary assignments within the school for the ensuing
school year as soon as administratively practicable. Such assignment shall
consist of subject matter and/or grade level(s) responsibilities. Nothing in the
preliminary assignment shall be construed as limiting the right of the District to
make any changes in such assignments. The unit members affected by any such
change in preliminary assignment shall be given notice before the change is
effectuated.

Reassignment shall not be punitive or disciplinary in nature.

5.10 Notice of Intent to Resign

The parties agree that Unit Members will notify the District in writing no later than
June 1st of any school year that they intend to not return to employment for the
succeeding school year.

ARTICLE 6
SAFETY CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

The District shall endeavor to provide and maintain a safe place of employment. Unit
members shall not be required to work under unsafe or hazardous conditions or to
perform tasks which endanger their health and/or safety.

All unit members shall endeavor, in the course of performing the duties associated with
their employment, to be alert to unsafe conditions, equipment, or practices, and to

designee.

ARTICLE 7
CLASS SIZE

7.1    Whenever administratively feasible 20 to 1 ratio, and class size reduction
whenever the District’s financial resources allow, and whenever the facilities are
available, the maximum class size shall be twenty-seven (27) students (ADA).

7.2    In addition, the above listed class size is subject to modification for purposes
such as, but not limited to, low enrollment classes, large group or experimental
instruction, team teaching limitations because of distribution of pupils by
attendance areas, changes in enrollment, or other valid reasons.

The Superintendent or designee has the responsibility and obligation to
permanently assign students to classrooms.

7.3    When a class enrollment reaches 33 the administration will discuss the situation
with the teacher and reach a mutual agreement on a solution. Examples of
possible solutions, additional aide time, long term substituting, reorganizing
classes. When a class size reaches 35 students the District will make every
effort to arrange for out-of-district students to return to their home district.

ARTICLE 9
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

9.1 A grievance is an allegation by a member of the bargaining unit that there has
been a violation of a specific provision of this Agreement which has adversely
affected that member or an allegation by the Association that there has been a
violation of a specific provision of this agreement which has adversely affected a
unit member. Actions to challenge or change the policies of the District as set
forth in the rules and regulations or administrative regulations and procedures
are not recognizable under the grievance procedure. Matters for which a specific
method of review is provided by law, by the rules and regulations of the Board of
Trustees, or by the Administrative regulations and procedures of this District are
not within the scope of this procedure.

9.1.1    Level One - Before filing a written grievance, the grievant shall attempt to
resolve it by an informal conference with the Principal.

9.1.2    Level Two - If the grievant has complied with the requirement of Level
One and is not satisfied with the decision at that level, he/she must, within
five (5) working days after the grievant is aware or should have been
aware of the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the grievance,
present such grievance in writing to the Principal.

This statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
circumstances involved, the section of the Agreement alleged to have
been violated, the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the
specific remedy sought. Within five (5) working days, unless otherwise
mutually agreed, after receipt of the written grievance by the Principal, the
Principal will meet with the grievant upon request.

The Principal shall communicate a decision to the employee in writing
within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance unless otherwise
mutually agreed. If no such written decision is given, the grievance will be
deemed denied.

9.1.3    Level Three - In the event that grievant and association is not satisfied
with the decision or decisions at Level Two, he/she may appeal the
decision in writing within five (5) working days to the Superintendent.

Such an appeal must include a copy of the original written grievance, all
other written documents pertaining to the grievance, and a clear, concise
statement of the reasons for the appeal and the facts the grievant wants
the Superintendent to consider.

The Superintendent shall communicate a decision to the employee in
writing within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance unless otherwise
mutually agreed. If no such written decision is given, the grievance will be
deemed denied.

9.1.4    Level Four - In the event that grievant and association is not satisfied with
the decision or decisions at Level Three, he/she may appeal the decision
in writing within five (5) working days to the Board of Trustees.

Such an appeal must include a copy of the original written grievance, all
other written documents pertaining to the grievance, and a clear, concise
statement of the reasons for the appeal and the facts the grievant wants
the Board to consider.

The Board shall schedule, within fifteen (15) days after receipt of an
appeal, a special meeting to consider the grievance. If, upon review, the
Board desires further information on the grievance, it may request such
further information be supplied orally at its meeting or request that the
parties submit such information in writing to be considered by the Board at
a meeting no later than fifteen (15) days after the meeting at which such a
request was made. The Board may deliberate its decision either in an
open or executive session, but any decision reached must appear in the
official minutes of the Board meeting. The Board shall provide the parties
written notice of its decision within five (5) days.

The total time consumed in this Level Four shall not exceed forty (40)
days, unless time is extended by mutual agreement of the grievant and
the District.

9.1.5    Level Five - The decision of the Board on the grievance shall be final,
unless within five (5) working days of the notice of the Board’s decision,

APPENDIX C
EVALUATION PROCEDURE

A.    Standard of Performance

1.    In an evaluation year, the unit member to be evaluated shall, in
cooperation with the Principal, develop performance goals, based on the
California Standards for the Teaching Profession. These performance
goals are to be used in the evaluation of that unit member. If agreement
cannot be attained, the Principal shall set the performance goals based on
the standards.

2.    These performance goals shall be reduced to written form, and shall
consist of statements which describe the degree to which the unit member
will contribute to the attainment of program objectives and/or instructional
objectives.

3.    The program objectives and/or instructional objectives themselves shall be
developed by the unit member from those program objectives as set forth
in the current School Plan for Consolidated Programs. In developing
performance goals, unit members shall take into account the individual
differences between her/his students and base her/his performance goals
on the current course of study for Luther Burbank School District.

4.    The development of applicable performance goals shall take place at the
beginning of the school year.

5.    The performance goals may be adjusted during the school year if
changing conditions in a unit member’s job situation occurs.

6.    The unit member and the Principal shall confer at the mid point of the
school year regarding progress assessment and adjustments.

7.    The unit member and the Principal shall confer at the end of the school
year for final evaluation of the unit member’s performance.
