ARTICLE m
NEGOTIATIONS PROCEDURES

a.    A “day” is any day in which the District Office is open for business*

b.    A “working day” is a day which is a contract day for unit members.

A tentative agreement is a written agreement, initialed and dated, that is reached
by the District and Association on an article or item during negotiations and
subject to ratification by the members of the Association and approval by the
Governing Board.

Ratification is the act of approving the tentative agreement(s) as written. The
Association ratifies while the Governing Board approves.

The Association and the District shall meet to share and discuss their initial
proposals no later than the week before the October regular Board meeting in the school
year that the contract expires or is reopened in the school year the contract expires or is
reopened.

Negotiations shall take place at mutually agreeable times and places. Every
reasonable effort will be made to complete the negotiation process by April 15* If
the process is not complete by April 15, the District and Association may mutually
agree to extend the date, drop reopeners, move reopeners to the following
negotiation cycle, or either party may declare an impasse.

Negotiation meetings shall be held within fourteen (14) days from receipt of a
written request from either party.

The District and Association will state which negotiations process each party
intends to use (i.e., collaborative bargaining, traditional bargaining, etc.) at the
beginning of each negotiation cycle.

Within twenty (20) days of ratification of the Agreement by both parties, the
Superintendent or designee and the head negotiator or designee will certify the
final agreement as accurate. Within twenty (20) working days after certification
by both parties, the District will have copies prepared and delivered to all unit
members plus two (2) for use by the Association. The District will provide copies
of the Agreement to any new bargaining unit members within the first twenty (20)
working days after they are hired.

Definitions

A.    A "grievance" shall mean a claim by a unit member and/or association that
there has been a misinterpretation, misapplication, or violation of this
Agreement, which is filed by a grievant affected by the alleged violation,
interpretation, or application.

B.    A “unit member” is any person represented under Article I of this
Agreement.

C.    An "aggrieved person" is the unit member and/or association making the
claim.

D.    A "day" is any day in which the central administrative office of the District
is open for business.

E.    The “immediate supervisor” is the site administrator who has been
designated to adjust grievances and who has immediate jurisdiction over
the grievant.

Purpose

A.    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible
administrative level, equitable solutions to problems arising from
allegations of unfair or inequitable treatment under this Agreement. Both
parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal and
confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the procedure.

B.    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any unit
member having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with any
appropriate member of the Administration, and to have the grievance
adjusted without intervention by the Association, provided that the
adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement

C.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible,
the time limits specified at each level should be made to expedite the
process. The time limits may however, be extended by mutual agreement.

Level one must be initiated within fifteen (15) days from the occurrence of the act

or omission which gave rise to the grievance, or within fifteen (15) days from the

date the grievant knew or should reasonably have known of the act or omission.

A.    Level One: An aggrieved person will first present his/her grievance to
his/her immediate supervisor orally. The immediate supervisor shall orally
respond to the grievant within three (3) days.

B.    Level Two: If the grievant is not satisfied with the response at Level One,
and wishes to continue the grievance, the grievant must within five (5)
days of the oral response reduce the grievance to writing and file same
with his/her immediate supervisor. The grievance shall contain the name
of the grievant, the date of occurrence, the specific article and section of
the Agreement alleged to have been violated, a description of the
grievance, the specific remedy requested, and shall be signed and dated by
the grievant. The grievant's immediate supervisor shall respond within five
(5) days in writing to the grievant.

C.    Level Three: If the aggrieved person is not satisfied with the written
disposition of the grievance at Level Two, and wishes to continue the
grievance, he/she must file the grievance in writing with the Board within
ten (10) days after the written decision at Level Two. Within ten (10) days
after receipt of the written grievance by the Board, the Board or its
designee will ineet the aggrieved person and a representative of the
Association in an effort to resolve the grievance. The Board or its designee
shall then within ten (10) days of that meeting issue a written response of
the disposition of the grievance.

D.    Level Four: If the aggrieved person is not satisfied with the disposition of
his/her grievance at Level Three, and wishes to continue the grievance, a
mutually agreed upon mediator shall be selected within ten (10) days of
the disposition. Within ten (10) days of such selection, a formal meeting
will take place with the Board, and the mediator. At that meeting the
aggrieved person and a representative of the Association will present the
issues and facts pertaining to the grievance. Within ten (10) days of that
meeting, the mediator will submit to the Board and the aggrieved person
his/her decision with regard to settlement of the grievance. This decision
shall be final unless the Board or the aggrieved person requests submission
to Level Five.

E.    Level Five: If the aggrieved person, or Board, is not satisfied with the
disposition of the grievance at Level Four, and wishes to continue the
grievance, within ten (10) days after a decision by the mediator, either the

2) Alternative Certificated Evaluation Program

a.    Statement of Purpose

The Alternative Certificated Evaluation Program is designed to
stimulate and further motivate experienced, tenured unit members
to continue their professional development and growth. The
program is offered as an alternative to the contract’s existing
evaluation process. The goal of the alternative evaluation program
is to provide a process where unit members may enhance their
professional development and thereby most directly impact student
instruction, success and achievement.

b.    Eligibility

(1)    Participation is entirely voluntary, with participating
teachers committing to a good faith effort to complete the
process and meet all procedural expectations.

(2)    Participating teachers must be permanent, tenured teachers,
having begun his/her 10th year of service with the school
district, scheduled for a formal evaluation this school year,
having received a satisfactory or better performance rating
on the previous evaluations during the last five years.

c. Application

Alternative Program Evaluator and submits the application

year,

understandable behavioral terms.

(1.2)    Description of the process the unit member will use to learn
the new skill or enhance their performance.

d.    the last week of April to review P-2 ADA and next year’s preliminary budget.

If this portion of the formula generates a further increase of more than .5%, the percentage

.5%, the percentage will be added to the current year’s salary schedule and will be retroactive
to July 1st of the current year with the amount due from the prior year paid as an off-the-
schedule bonus. If this portion of the formula generates a decrease, the percentage will be
subtracted from the current year's salary percentage increase generated by items B through H.
The total of Items A through I shall not be less than zero.

Certificated Personnel    AR 4131.7(a)

CLASS ADVANCEMENT

Class advancement is included as a part of the salary policy of the District to encourage
certificated employees to increase their competence through additional training. This
encouragement is provided through compensation on the salary schedule. The process is
designed to:

1.    Ensure that advanced training is taken which will be of value to the certificated
employee in his/her assignment

2.    Provide for compensation for a variety of types of professional growth

3.    Ensure careful monitoring of each certificated employee’s professional growth

To obtain class advancement on the salary schedule, a certificated employee must apply in
writing by March 20 for advancement for the next fiscal year. The application shall be on
forms provided by the District.

An application for class advancement which includes courses to be accomplished subsequent
to the application shall be evaluated but salary for the fallowing year shall be set without
class advancement. If the application has been approved subject to the completion of a
specified number of units, the contract shall be rewritten at the new schedule position, upon
proof of completion of such courses.

Course Approval Guidelines

1.    Courses must be of value to the certificated employee in his/her assignment. The
decision as to whether a course is of value rests solely with the employee’s
supervisor. If an employee disagrees with his/her supervisor’s decision, he/she may
appeal that decision to the Superintendent. The Superintendent’s decision will be
final.

2.    Courses must have the prior approval of the employee’s supervisor. Request for
course approval must be submitted on the District Intent-to-Take form. Prior
approval means before the date the course actually commences. Failure to obtain
prior approval will automatically disallow any District credit for the course work.

Course Work Guidelines

Six (6) units per semester during the school year from September to June is the maximum

which may be approved. If they meet these requirements, then:

1. Lower division courses may be approved. These courses must contribute to the
professional competence of the certificated employee.

ARTICLE VI
TEACHING HOURS

1.    The length of the teaching day, relief periods and the time required before and
after school, but not including the lunch period, shall not exceed six and one half
(6 1/2) hours,

2.    A, Every IC - 6 unit member shall be present at school a minimum of thirty
(30) minutes before the beginning of his/her students' regular school day, and is to
remain at school a minimum of fifteen (15) minutes after the close of his/her
students' regular school day, except to attend faculty or other in-service training
meetings.

B.    Every unit member in a position at the Piner-Olivet Charter School shall
be present at school a minimum of twenty (20) minutes before the beginning of
his/her students’ regular school day, and is to remain at school a minimum of
fifteen (15) minutes after the close of his/her students’ regular school day, except
to attend faculty or other in-service training meetings.

C,    Every unit member in a position at Northwest Prep at Piner-Olivet shall be
present at school a minimum of ten (10) minutes before the beginning of his/her
students’ regular school day, and may leave at the end of his/her students’ regular
school day, except to attend faculty or other in-service training meetings.

3.    In addition, unit members may occasionally be required to be present on the
school grounds for an extended period as directed by the administration. Extended
means a continuous period of time immediately adjacent to the regular work day.
Such required extended work days shall be limited to seven and one half (7 1/2)
hours, not including the lunch period. Kindergarten teachers will be required to
have the same number of working hours as primary teachers.

4.    Every unit member shall be entitled to one (1) thirty (30) consecutive minute
duty-free, uninterrupted lunch period, with passing time before and after the thirty
(30) minutes, and a total of twenty (20) minutes of relief time each day.

5.    The administration may call no more than one (1) site level faculty meeting per
calendar month. Meetings will begin no later than 15 minutes after student
dismissal. The district administration may call no more than six (6) additional
meetings per school year. These meetings will start 30 minutes after student
dismissal. Starting times for District Special Education meetings will be
consensually determined by the attendees. Unit members are obligated to attend
regular district and site level faculty meetings if they take place on a duty day.
Attendance beyond one (1) hour is voluntary at all of these meetings. Additional

meetings may be called by the administration. Unit members’ attendance at these
additional meetings is voluntary. The administration shall provide attendance
options for unit members who have classes or district duties during these
meetings. Unit members who work a partial day, five (5) days per week shall
attend a percentage of site and district faculty meetings equivalent to their FTE
(rounded to the nearest whole number). The specific meetings attended will be

create a process for the exchange of information and input. Attendance of district

of report card preparation, Back to School Night or Open House. The District will
make an effort to avoid progress report weeks as well.

6.    Unit members are obligated to attend all district staff development days
which are held during the school year and during regular school hours if they take

optional. Credit for class advancement will be given for the optional after-school
workshop/inservice hours. Attendance at all after school inservice meetings shall

credit. Unit members shall be involved in selection of topics for inservice training.

October 31.

7.c. Whenever possible, IEP’s shall be scheduled on Fridays.

IEP’s may also be used to pay unit members or substitutes for compensatory time
for unit members who are required to attend IEP or SST meetings after the unit
member’s regular work day. The pay shall be the District’s extra duty hourly pay
(currently $35.00 per hour). To be paid for such time or to be granted
compensatory time off for such time, unit members must submit a District
timesheet to the District Office detailing the extra time the unit member attended
the IEP meeting(s). Compensatory time may be accrued for up to one year.

Combination Classes K-6

The parties met on April 10, 2014 and agreed that every effort would be made to avoid combination
classes. In the event that combination classes are unavoidable, the parties agreed to the following:

1. Class size:

(a)    In a combination class, every effort will be made not to exceed a class size of 3 less
than the established grade level average.

(b)    Every effort will be made to create an appropriate combination class, while
maintaining a reasonable balance throughout the grade level.

(c)    The Administrative Regulation 6600 “rule of four” does not apply to combination
classes.

2.    Assignment of teachers to combination classes:

(a)    When there is a need for a combination class, the site administrator will meet with the
. affected grade level staff and ask for volunteers.

(b)    • When there is a need to staff a combination class and there is no volunteer in die

affected grade level, transfer(s) will occur according to the applicable provisions of
Article 10, Transfers.

(c)    Every effort will he made to avoid assignment of a year 1 BTSA teacher or a PAS
referred teacher to a combination class,

(d)    While considering the needs of students and die District, efforts will be made to rotate
assignment to combination class(es).

3.    Combination class teachers shall be eligible for the following:

(a)    Training/staff development to prepare for two grade levels, including release time to

visit other combination classes.

(b)    Full sets of instructional materials for both classes.

(c)    Additional money for materials (double die “red folder” money).

In an effort to allow the combination class teachers to teach only one grade level in reading
and madi, flexible groupings of cross-grade level students for reading and madi is encouraged.

MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING
2014-2015
POCS Teaching Hours

The parties met and agreed to the following:

1)    POCS teachers recognize that research shows teacher collaboration has a significant
effect on positive student achievement. Collaboration is an integral component of the
POCS program established by the POCS charter. In order to provide the time necessary
for teacher collaboration, instructional time is adjusted in the following ways:

a)    Student instructional time is reduced by one and half hours one day per week.

b)    To make up the instructional time, the school morning time is extended by 20
minutes each student day.

2)    Wednesday collaboration time is used in the following ways:

a)    A minimum of two meetings a month focused directly on student learning: once a
month Department meetings and once a month Team Teacher meetings.

b)    Whole staff collaboration and planning with teacher input and Faculty meeting
concerns.

3)    Wednesday collaboration meetings will begin at 1:25 and finish by 2:50 p.m. or before.
The agenda will be distributed at least 24 hours before the meeting. Teachers wanting to
place specific items on the agenda will submit them in the manner agreed upon by the
staff.

4)    On shortened student days, teacher work hours will end at die usual time.

5)    During report card/progress report time, Open House, Back to School Night, Orientation
Night, etc., administration may allow for modification of the content of Wednesday
collaboration meetings.

6)    POCS staff meets weekly on Wednesdays. Monthly Faculty meetings will be conducted
within the Wednesday meeting time. POCS will find a way to cover District meeting
concerns within the Wednesday time frame and will not hold a separate District faculty
meeting.

To support die implementation of Common core, POCS may need to use District Faculty
Meeting hours during the 2014-2015 school year. If the District concerns can be covered
within the regular 6 hours of meeting/collaboration time per month, die 6 hours of
District Faculty Meeting time will not be required.

a) A Joint Committee of the POCS principal and two or more POCS teachers will
meet to plan the use of up to 6 District Faculty Meeting hours after the
District/Association Joint Committee meets in May/June, 2014 in order to align
die POCS plan with the District plan. The POCS meeting will occur on or before
the last POEA contract day.

b)    The May/June meeting will be up to one full day, as necessary. Teacher
Committee members will receive either release time or if a non-contract day,
his/her pro-rated daily rate.

c)    Items to be included in discussions are clear goals for trainings, curriculum
selection, benchmark assessments and other committee work for the following
year. The Committee may also flexibly schedule the use of up to six hours of
DFM time to support the goals, (e.g. six 1-hour mtgs, three 2-hour mtgs, or other
combinations equaling six hours or less)

d)    The subjects of the Professional Development days (if any) may also be discussed
by the Committee.

e)    The Committee will plan for notification to all staff of the meeting subjects and
schedule and for provision of appropriate reminders of each meeting at least one
week in advance.

f)    In scheduling the DFM hours, the Committee will not schedule meetings during
weeks in which Back-to-School Night, Open House, testing, report card and/or
conference day occur.
