1. Agreement

1.    The articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding Agreement
("Agreement") by and between the Board of Education of Martinez Unified School District
("Board") and the Martinez Education Association/California Teachers Association/National
Education Association ("Association"), an employee organization.

2.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code.

3.    Duration of Agreement: Three (3) years, July 1, 2012 through and including June 30, 2015.

4.    This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from the date of ratification. The term of
this Agreement shall be from July 1,2012, through June 30, 2015.

5.    If either the Association or the District chooses to reopen the Agreement for the 2013-2014 or
2014-2015 school year, bargaining proposals will be submitted to the Board no later than the
second Board meeting in May. if ratification for the prior two years occurs after April 1, both
proposals will be submitted to the Board within two (2) months following ratification of the prior
Agreement by both parties.

6.    Reopeners are limited to the following items:

a.    2013-2014: Salaries (Article 11), Employee Benefit, (Article 12), Workday (Article 15),
and one additional article selected by each party.

b.    2014-2015: Salaries (Article 11), Employee Benefit, (Article 12), and one additional
article selected by each party.

2. Recognition

The Board confirms its recognition of the Martinez Education Association/California Teachers
Association/National Education Association as the exclusive representative for that unit of
employees recognized by the District per its Resolution No. 93-57 dated May 11, 1993.

"...all certificated employees, including permanent, probationary, and temporary employees with
a contract of a specific duration. Those excluded are casual employees (including, but not
limited to, substitutes and adult school teachers), management, (including, but not limited to, the
Superintendent; Assistant Superintendents; Assistant Superintendent of Personnel; Director,
Student Services; Director, Curriculum and Instruction; Principal; Vice-Principal; Assistant
Principal), confidential and supervisory employees."

4. Nondiscrimination

The District shall not discriminate against any employee regarding the provisions of this
Agreement on the basis of race, color, creed, age, sex, national origin, political affiliation, life
style, marital status, sexual orientation, disability, membership in an employee organization, or

5. Negotiation Procedures

If either the Association or the District chooses to reopen the contract, bargaining
proposals will be submitted to the Board no later than the second Board meeting in May.
If ratification for the prior year occurs after April 1, both proposals will be submitted to the
Board within two (2) months. No later than the third week of September of each year,
the Board and the exclusive representative shall meet and negotiate in good faith on a
successor Agreement. Reopeners are limited to the following items:

Salaries, Article 11

Employee Benefits, Article 12

One unspecified article from each party

If mutually agreed, an additional common interest relative to IBB may be explored

Each party to the proposed Agreement stipulates that it has the full authority to negotiate
for its constituency. After tentative agreement has been reached on the total Agreement
by the employee and employer representatives, they jointly agree to submit and support
the proposed Agreement in all deliberations with their appropriate groups. Agreements
reached between the parties shall be reduced to writing and signed by them. Tentative
agreement may be achieved on each item in the proposal, but no tentative agreement
will become final until the entire Master Agreement is approved.

Either party may utilize the services of outside resource people. As a courtesy, the other
party will be notified twenty-four (24) hours prior to the meeting when resource people
shall be present.

The Board and the Association may discharge their respective duties by means of
authorized officers, individual representatives or committees.

a Negotiations shall take place at mutually agreeable times and places provided the
meetings shall be held within ten (10) contract days from receipt of a written
request.

b.    The agenda for the meetings shall be established by mutual consent of the parties.

c.    Only by mutual agreement will a change in time and place of a previously scheduled
negotiation session occur.

The Association and Board shall each designate not more than six (6) representatives
for purposes of official meet and negotiate sessions to develop an Agreement, and no
official meetings between the parties shall be held unless at least two (2) representatives
of each group are present. Any such Association representatives that are District
employees shall each receive approximately forty (40) hours of released time to be used
without loss of compensation for purposes of official meet and negotiate sessions with
the Board's representatives. Released time will be matched on an hour-for-hour basis
with Association contributed time, No more than two (2) consecutive days of released
time, as described herein, may be used without the mutual agreement of both parties;
and no released time shall be granted to employees during the last week of any school
quarter or semester without the mutual consent of the parties. No portion of this
released time allocation shall be used in less than one-half (14) day increments.
Employees utilizing this released time allocation shall follow normal District procedures

6. Association Rights

1 The representatives of the Association shall have the right to make use of school

equipment, buildings and facilities for Association business at all reasonable hours when
not in service.

2.    The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities and matters of
Association concern on designated bulletin boards, at least one of which shall be
provided in each school building in areas frequented by employees. The Association
may use employee mailboxes for communications to employees.

3.    The Association is allowed to use school facilities before or after regularly scheduled
school hours in compliance with District procedures. The Association has the right to
transact official business on school property provided that this shall not interfere with or
interrupt normal school operations as determined by the Site Administrator.

4.    Names, addresses and telephone numbers of all District employees shall be published
in an electronic District directory which shall be available without cost to the Association
no later than October 15 of each school year.

5.    Whenever negotiations go past 9 PM, the District shall provide one (1) day of released
time on the day following for members of the Association's bargaining team. Section 5 is
limited to a maximum of three (3) occurrences during any single contract year.

6.    The Association President or President’s designee shall be allowed to transact
Association business. The Association will reimburse the District for all compensation
and benefits paid to the President/designee on account of such leave for Association
business in accordance with Ed Code section 44987. Reimbursement shall not be
required when district invites the President/designee(s) to attend meetings or events that
require release time.

7    The Association shall have the right to present to the Board its views on District policy
making in areas relating to or affecting the teaching profession, or in which the
Association has unique experience and expertise.

8    The district shall notify a unit member and/or the association president of the unit
member’s right to association representation in a meeting with management which may
lead to disciplinary action. The meeting shall not be unreasonably delayed based on the
lack of availability of a representative.

7. Board Rights

1 It is understood and agreed that the Board retains all of its powers and authority to
direct, manage and control to the full extent of the law. Included in, but not limited to,
those duties and powers are the exclusive rights to: determine its organizations; direct
the work of its employees; determine the times and hours of operation; determine the
kinds and levels of services to be provided and the methods and means of providing
them; establish its educational policies, goals and objectives; ensure the rights and
educational opportunities of students; determine staffing patterns; determine the number
and kinds of personnel required; maintain the efficiency of District operations; determine
the curriculum; build, move or modify facilities; establish budget procedures and
determine budgetary allocation; determine the methods of raising revenue; contract out
work; take action on any matter in the event of an emergency. In addition, the Board
retains the right to hire, classify, assign, evaluate, promote and terminate employees.
The Board's right to discipline shall be in accordance with the Education Code.

2. The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the
Board, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof
and the use of judgment and discretion in connection therewith shall be limited only by
the specific and express terms of this Agreement and then only to the extent such
specific and express terms are in conformance with law.

other labor organizations to engage in such activity,

2 The Association and the District recognize the duty and obligation of its representatives
to comply with the provisions of this Agreement and to make every effort toward inducing
all employees to do so. In the event of a strike, work stoppage, or slowdown by
employees who are represented by the Association, the Association agrees in good faith
to take all reasonable steps to cause those employees to cease such action,

3.    It is agreed and understood that any employee violating this article may be subject to
disciplinary action.

4.    It is agreed and understood that in the event the Association violates this article, the
District may withdraw any rights, privileges or services provided for in this Agreement.

17. Evaluation of Employees

Purpose of Evaluation

1.    To offer personalized guidance and support to certificated staff for professional growth
and continuous improvement.

2.    To improve the delivery of instruction/services to maximize and support student learning.

The District accepts as a fundamental premise for a successful evaluation program the

necessity for mutual respect and confidence to exist between the Evaluator and those

evaluated. It is understood and agreed by the parties that the principal objective of the

evaluation process is to maintain and improve the quality of education in the District.

Evaluation Procedure #1

1. Employees to be evaluated in a given year shall be informed in writing by their

Immediate Supervisor. A meeting shall be held with these employees within the first
three (3) weeks after the opening of school to discuss and distribute the evaluation
procedure and forms.

2 Site employees shall be evaluated by the Principal or the Principal's administrative

designee. Employees who serve more than one (1) school site shall be evaluated by the
designated Administrator. Master Plan personnel who serve at a single site shall be
evaluated by the site Principal. Employees of Indian Education, Bilingual Education and
other District-wide special programs shall be evaluated by District or Site Administrators.

3.    The employee being evaluated and the Evaluator shall meet no later than October 15 to
discuss:

a.    Focus standards to be targeted during the evaluation period.

b.    A schedule of observations, conferences and final evaluation date.

4 Each employee shall develop in collaboration with site administrator a minimum of three

(3) focus standards. The focus standards will be chosen from the California Standards
for the Teaching Profession (CSTP) (See Appendix G-T); one (1) selected by the
Evaluatee, one (1) selected by the Evaluator and a third which shall be mutually agreed
upon. In developing these focus standards, personnel should consider District-wide
goals and their current job assignment. Where prior evaluations have indicated a need
for improvement, the employee shall include focus standards designed to address
identified concerns.

5.    Focus standards for non-instructional personnel shall be related to job responsibilities.

All focus standards will be entered on the appropriate form and signed by the Evaluator
and the evaluatee.

6.    After the first observation which must occur before winter break, if the Supervisor or the
Evaluator feels there is serious concern about an area of the employee's performance,
the Immediate Supervisor or Evaluator may substitute or add one (1) additional focus
standard to be addressed [not to exceed a total of four (4)]. The focus standard,
evidence of progress, and steps for improvement shall be written on or attached to the
evaluation form. The Evaluator(s), if other than the Immediate Supervisor, shall be
notified of this additional focus standard.

7.    The Evaluator is responsible for seeing that the evaluative process is initiated. The
Evaluator and employee are mutually responsible for seeing that the evaluative process
is carried out. The Evaluator shall maintain an up-to-date status report on the progress
of evaluation.

Frequency of Evaluations

1.    Evaluation and assessment of performance of each employee shall be made on a
continuing basis. Probationary and temporary employees shall be evaluated every year
for the first two (2) consecutive years of employment in the District and at least every
other year thereafter. Employees with permanent status shall be evaluated at least
every other year except as indicated in #4 below (5-Year Evaluation Cycle).

2.    Employees employed after November 1 or reassigned after October 1 will meet with
their Supervisor and set focus standards, evidence of progress, and check point dates
as appropriate to the evaluatee's job description and the time remaining in the school
year.

3.    At least two (2) formal observations shall take place prior to the final evaluation report.
Such observations shall be arranged between the Evaluator and the employee. The
Evaluator shall base his/her evaluation of an employee on information, Including but not
limited to, that collected through direct observation and assessment of and conference
with the employee. Any information obtained by means other than by direct observation
by the Evaluator that is to be placed in the evaluation report, shall be identified as to the
event, person(s) observing event, Evaluator's source of information, time, place and date
of occurrence. In the event the information is not documented as here required, it shall
not be placed in the evaluation report. These observations shall be written by the
Evaluator, discussed and signed by both parties at a conference.

serving in a position that requires this designation under state and/or federal law; (4)
had an immediately preceding evaluation meeting or exceeding standards (proficient or
above); and (5) the unit member and evaluator agree in writing to the five (5) year
evaluation cycle. The unit member or evaluator may withdraw from the agreement no
later than October 1 of any school year in which case the unit member shall be

observations at any time. (See “5-Year Evaluation Cycle Agreement, attached as

Personnel Files

1.    The Board shall not base any adverse action against an employee upon materials which
are not contained in such employee's personnel file.

2.    Materials in personnel files of employees, which may serve as a basis for affecting the
status of their employment, are to be made available for the inspection of the person
involved. Every employee shall have the right to inspect these materials upon request,
provided that the request is made at a time when such person is not actually required to
render services to the District. Such material is not to include ratings, reports, or records
which: (1) were obtained prior to the employment of the person involved, (2) were
prepared by identifiable examination committee members, or (3) were obtained in
connection with a promotional examination. All requested information from the
employee's file shall be either picked up in person by the employee or mailed to the
employee's home at the employee's direction.

3.    Information of a derogatory nature, except material mentioned in Section 2 above, shall
not be entered or filed unless and until the employee is given notice and an opportunity
to review and comment thereon. An employee shall have the right to enter and have
attached to any such derogatory statement his own comments thereon. Such review
shall take place during normal business hours, and the employee shall be released from
duty for this purpose without salary reduction.

4 Upon written authorization by the employee, a representative of the Association shall be
permitted to examine and/or obtain copies of materials at cost in such employee's
personnel file.

5. The person or persons who draft and/or place material in an employee's personnel file
shall sign the material and signify the date on which such material was drafted.

6 Access to personnel files shall be limited to the members of the Administration on a
need-to-know basis. The Board may request the review of an employee’s file at a
personnel session of the Board. The contents of all personnel files shall be kept in the
strictest confidence.

7.    If there is derogatory material in the employee's personnel file about which the employee
is concerned, the employee may request a review of the material by the Superintendent;
and the Superintendent will determine its disposition. In most cases, derogatory material
will not be removed in less than four (4) years from the date of the material. If the
Superintendent determines that the derogatory material not be removed from the
employee's file, a statement giving the reasons for this action will be provided to the

Grievance Procedure

Definitions

1.    A "grievance" is a written claim by one (1) or more employees, or by the Association
when so authorized in writing by the employee(s), that there has been a violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.    A "grievant" is a person or persons and/or the Association or representative thereof, as
indicated above, making the claim.

3.    A "party in interest" is any person who might be required to take action or against whom
action might be taken in order to resolve the claim.

4.    "Day" is any day the District Office is open

General Provisions

1.    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to a grievance.

2.    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any employee prior to
initiating the formal grievance process to discuss the matter informally with the
Immediate Supervisor or any appropriate member of the Administration to whom it
applies and to have the grievance adjusted without intervention by the Association,
provided that the adjustment is consistent with the terms of this Agreement.

3.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time limits
specified at each level should be considered to be maximums; and every effort should
be made to expedite the process. The time limits may, however, be modified by mutual
agreement.

4    In the event a grievance is filed at such a time that it cannot be processed through all the
steps in this grievance procedure by the end of the school year and if left unresolved
could result in harm to the grievant, the time limits set forth herein will be reduced by
mutual agreement so that the procedure may be exhausted prior to the end of the school
year or as soon as is practicable.

5    A grievant shall attend all meetings scheduled to hear his/her grievance when physically
able to do so.

6    The Association, when authorized in writing by an affected employee or employees, may
initiate a grievance which affects one or more employees in a single building or
employees in more than one building at Level II.

7.    If a grievance arises from action or inaction on the part of a member of the
Administration at the level above the Principal or Immediate Supervisor, the grievant
shall submit such grievance in writing to the Assistant Superintendent of Personnel and
the Association directly and the processing of such grievance will be commenced at
Level II.

8.    Decisions rendered at Levels II and III of the grievance procedure will be in writing
setting forth the decision and the reasons, and will be transmitted promptly to all parties
in interest and to the President of the Association.

19-1

9 When it is required that an employee of the District be absent from his/her duties

pursuant to these procedures, he/she shall not suffer any loss of pay. Authorization for
such absence must be obtained from the Assistant Superintendent of Personnel.

10.    All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a grievance
will be filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in the personnel file of any of
the participants.

11.    The grievance form is in the Appendix of this contract.

12 During these proceedings and until a final determination has been reached, all
proceedings shall be confidential.

13. A decision rendered at any step in these procedures becomes final unless appealed
within the time limit specified, providing the grievant is physically able to do so. If a
decision is not given within the time limit, an appeal may be taken directly to the next
level. The grievant shall continue to discharge assigned duties and comply with the
direction of the administration while the grievance is being resolved as long as
compliance does not endanger the health, safety or property of the person.

Procedure

Level 1

1.    Within ten (10) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to a potential
grievance or after such time as the grievant became aware of the omission not to
exceed one (1) year, a grievant will discuss the grievance with the appropriate principal
or appropriate Administrator, either individually or with the Association's designated
grievance representative, with the objective of resolving the matter informally.

2.    The Principal or appropriate Administrator has five (5) days within which to respond to
the employee with a proposed resolution.

3.    If the grievant has received no response within eight (8) days or the grievant is
dissatisfied with the response, the grievant then has ten (10) days to file a written
grievance with the Principal or appropriate Administrator and the President of the
Association.

Level II

1.    The Principal or appropriate Administrator has five (5) days within which to respond in
writing to the grievant with a proposed solution.

2.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of the grievance or receives no
response, he/she may appeal the grievance to the Assistant Superintendent of
Personnel in writing within five (5) days of the disposition at Level II.

Level III

1.    Prior to the development of a proposed solution, the Superintendent will meet with the
grievant to discuss the grievance.

2.    The Superintendent or designee has ten (10) days within which to respond in writing to
the grievant with a proposed solution.

3. If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level III, he/she may, within five (5)
days from the receipt of the decision, submit a request in writing to the Superintendent
for binding arbitration of the dispute.

19-2

Level IV

1.    The grievant and the Superintendent shall attempt to agree upon an arbitrator. If no
agreement can be reached, they shall request the American Arbitration Association to
supply a panel of five (5) names of persons experienced in hearing grievances in public
schools. Each party shall alternately strike a name until only one (1) name remains.

The remaining panel member shall be the Arbitrator. The order of the striking shall be
determined by lot.

2.    The fees and expenses of the Arbitrator and the hearing shall be borne equally by the
District and the grievant. All other expenses shall be borne by the party incurring them

3.    The Arbitrator shall as soon as possible hear evidence and render a decision on the
issue or issues submitted to him/her. If the parties cannot agree upon a submission
agreement, the Arbitrator shall determine the issues by referring to the written grievance
and the answers thereto at each step.

4 The Arbitrator will have no power to add, subtract or modify the terms of this Agreement
or the written policies, rules, regulations and procedures of the District.

5.    Issues arising out of the exercise by the Board and Administration of its responsibilities
under Article 7 of this Agreement, including the facts underlying its exercise of such
discretion, shall not be subject to this procedure.

6.    After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to make written
arguments, the Arbitrator shall submit in writing to all parties his/her findings and
decision. The decision shall be final unless appealed within thirty (30) days to Superior
Court by either party to this Agreement for judicial review.

Right of Employees to Representation

1.    No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the Superintendent or by any member or
representative of the Administration or the Board against any grievant, any party in
interest, any member of the Association or any other participant in the grievance
procedure by reason of such participation.

2.    An employee may be represented at all stages of the grievance procedure by
himself/herself or, at his/her option, by a representative selected by the Association. If
an employee is not represented by the Association or its representative, the District shall
not agree to a resolution of the grievance until the Association has received a copy of
the grievance and the proposed resolution and has been given the opportunity to file a
response.

22i. SICK LEAVE -PAID

1. Purpose

The purpose of sick leave utilization shall be for physical and mental disability absences
which are medically necessary and caused by illness, injury, maternity, disability or
quarantine.

2.    Eligibility

An employee working full time for a full-contract year shall be annually entitled to ten
(10) days of leave of absence for the purpose of sick leave utilization. An employee
working less than full time shall be entitled to sick leave in the same ratio that their
employment bears to full-time employment.

3.    Procedure

An employee exercising this leave of absence provision shall notify the District of his/her
need to be absent from service as soon as known. It is expected that the employee will
notify the District Office in sufficient time to secure substitute services. The notification
described herein shall also include an estimate of the expected duration of the absence.

4.    Requirements

An employee becoming aware of the need for absence due to surgery, maternity, or
other predictable or previously scheduled cause shall submit a statement from the
attending physician as far in advance of the initial disability date as possible. The
physician's statement shall include the beginning date of disability, the cause of the
disability, if appropriate, and the anticipated date of the return to active service. The
District may require, with the approval of the Association President, and if circumstances
justify, medical verification for absences of less than five (5) days.

A written statement verifying absence. Such a statement shall be on forms
prescribed by the District.

b.    For a period of over five (5) but less than thirty (30) days

c.    For a period of thirty (30) days or over

On or before the tenth of each month, a doctor's written statement shall be filed
with the Superintendent.

d.    The District shall provide each employee with a written statement of: (1) his/her
accrued sick leave total, and (2) his/her sick leave entitlement for the school year
by the last work day in August of each year during the contract period.

5.    Compensation

a.    After the first ten (10) days of sick leave, an employee who continues to be
absent for purposes of this policy shall be eligible to receive differential pay
(difference between employee's per diem amount and the cost of the substitute)
for a period not to exceed five (5) continuous school months. Any unused sick
leave credit may be used by the employee for sick leave purposes without loss of
compensation. In order to qualify for differential pay, an employee shall first
utilize all accumulated sick leave credit and in no event shall days of differential
pay when combined with days of sick leave utilization exceed one hundred ten

(110) days in any school year. Only one (1) increment of differential pay shall be
allowed for any single and continuous absence that extends into the next school
year.

b.    A new differential pay period may be initiated for a different illness or accident
within the school year.

a. Immediately upon return to active service, the employee shall be provided a

District absence form for completion and submission to the Immediate
Supervisor.

b.    The employee shall provide, upon District request, additional verification of the
use of these leave provisions.

c.    An employee who has experienced a disability absence requiring surgery,
hospitalization, or extended medical treatment shall be required to submit, prior
to return to active duty, a medical statement indicating an ability to return to
his/her position classification without restrictions or detriment to the employee's
physical and emotional well-being.

by such notification failure, a substitute is secured.

23. Catastrophic Leave

1.    The intent of this Article is to provide support for any bargaining unit member or
employee of the District when that employee or a member of his or her family suffers
from a catastrophic illness or injury.

2.    Catastrophic illness or injury means an illness or injury that is expected to incapacitate
an employee or a member of the employee’s family for an extended period of time and
which requires the employee to take extended time off work after exhaustion of all
accumulated leave.

3.    Under this agreement, all bargaining unit member employees of the Martinez Unified
School District may become members of the Catastrophic Leave Bank by voluntarily
contributing, on a one time basis, one (1) sick leave day for the establishment and
operation of the bank. Only contributors will be permitted to withdraw from the bank. All
donations of eligible leave credits to the bank are irrevocable.

4.    The annual enrollment period is during the month of February. Employees who elect to
join the Catastrophic Leave Bank upon first becoming eligible, have a waiting period of
ninety (90) days after joining before they can withdraw from the bank. Eligible
employees must notify the Personnel Office, using the appropriate form, of their desire to
participate in the bank. New employees with more than ten (10) days of transferable
sick leave days from another district may donate one (1) day of sick leave within the first
thirty (30) calendar days of employment if they wish to participate. The maximum anyone
can contribute to the bank is two (2) sick leave days.

5.    The cumulative number of days that any one person can be granted from the bank shall
be in units of no more than thirty (30) days. Employees may submit requests for
extensions of withdrawals as their prior grants expire. An employee's withdrawal from
the bank may not exceed a period of twelve (12) consecutive months. Members of the
bank may request to withdraw from the bank after all available leave has been
exhausted and ten (10) days of differential pay have been received. A member who
withdraws credits from the bank will be paid at his/her regular daily rate of pay.
Catastrophic Leave will not be granted for periods of disability when monies are being
paid to the unit member under Worker’s Compensation.

6.    A Catastrophic Leave Bank Committee will be established to review and either approve
or deny all requests. The Committee will consist of one (1) administrator and one (1)
member of the Martinez Education Association.

7.    Catastrophic Leave applicants must make application through the Catastrophic Leave
Bank Committee by completing the appropriate form. Employees applying to withdraw
credits or extend Catastrophic Leave will be required to submit a doctor’s statement
indicating the nature of the illness or injury and the probable length of absence from
work. Members of the committee shall keep information regarding the nature of the
illness confidential. If an employee has drawn thirty (30) Catastrophic Leave Bank days
and requests an extension, the committee may require a medical review by a physician
of the committee’s choice at the employee's expense. When the Catastrophic Leave
has ended, employees shall donate one (1) day to the bank when it becomes available
to the employee.

8.    By October 15 of each school year, the District shall notify the committee of the
following:

23-1

a.    The total number of accumulated days in the bank on June 30 of the previous
year.

b.    The number of days contributed by employees for the current year.

c.    The names of participating employees.

d.    The total number of days available in the bank.

9.    Once membership in the bank is established, membership continues from year-to-year
unless the member notifies the Personnel Office, in writing, of their intent to withdraw
from the bank. Such withdrawal from the bank must occur during the February
enrollment period or when additional days are solicited to replenish the bank and will not
result in reinstatement of the time contributed to the bank.

10.    All unused days contributed to the bank will be carried over from year-to-year. When the
total number of days in the bank is reduced to thirty (30) days or less, the committee will
inform the Catastrophic Leave Bank membership that a special assessment of one (1)
sick leave day per member will be made to replenish the bank.

11.    A statement of participation in the bank will be included in the annual sick leave
accounting update provided by the district to each employee.

12.    If the Catastrophic Leave Bank is terminated for any reason, the days remaining in the
bank shall be returned to current members of the bank proportionately.

Peer Assistance and Review (PAR Program)

Assembly Bill IX, the California Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) program for teachers is
intended to provide an opportunity to strengthen the teaching profession. The purpose of this
legislation is to improve the classroom performance of teachers by providing them with ongoing
professional assistance. The improvement of teacher performance is the primary impetus of
this legislation. In the event that PAR funding may be used for other purposes, the District
reserves the right to determine whether or not to continue the program.

1.    Joint Panel Composition and Selection

a.    The Peer Assistance and Review Program (PAR) will be administered by a Joint
Panel.

b.    The Joint Panel shall consist of three (3) members, one (1) of whom will be an
Administrator selected by the District, and two (2) of whom shall be classroom
teachers and selected by the Association. Teacher Representatives selected to
the Joint Panel shall serve for a three (3) year term. The teacher
vacancies/terms shall be staggered to maintain the continuity of the panel
composition. All MEA unit members are eligible for a position on the Joint Panel
including current Joint Panel members, should they choose to run again.

c.    The Joint Panel Chairperson thereafter rotates on an annual basis between an
Association member and a District Administrator.

2.    Joint Panel Procedures

a.    The Joint Panel shall establish its own meeting schedule and meet no less than
four (4) times per school year if there are participating teachers, and two (2)
times per year if there are no participating teachers.

b.    A majority of the members of the Joint Panel must be present for the panel to
meet.

c.    Joint Panel meetings shall take place after the regular teachers’ workday.

d.    Association members of the Joint Panel will receive a stipend of one hundred
dollars ($100) per meeting, and will serve in this capacity for a three (3) year
term. All MEA members are eligible for a position on the Joint Panel including
current Joint Panel members, should they choose to run again.

e The Joint Panel shall select by consensus a Program Coordinator/Consulting
Teacher.

f. The Joint Panel shall be assisted by a full-time Program Coordinator/Consulting
Teacher, who will 1) supervise the Support Providers, 2) provide necessary
training, and 3) provide other program support as the Joint Panel may require;
and

g The District shall designate a PAR secretary to provide secretarial and clerical
support to the Joint Panel and the Program Coordinator/Consulting Teacher.

Discipline Less Than Dismissal

1.    Just Cause

The District may discipline a unit member only for just cause. Discipline shall include
warnings, reprimands, or suspensions without pay for less than fifteen (15) working
days. Discipline shall not include dismissal or suspensions for more than 15 working
days. Nothing in this Article shall apply to procedures set forth in the Education Code
including but not limited to Education Code sections 44938, 44939, and/or the non¬
reelection of temporary or probationary teachers.

2.    Progressive Discipline

a.    The following progressive discipline procedures will be applied except where the
serious nature of the offense may require the District to directly impose a written
warning, written reprimand, or suspension without pay.

b Verbal Counseling/Warning

The employer shall first issue a verbal counseling/warning before imposing
further discipline. Verbal counseling/warning may result in a post-conference
summary memorandum. Post-conference summary memorandum will not be
placed in the unit member's personnel file but may be attached to a Letter of
Warning and/or Letter of Reprimand and included in the personnel file.

c.    Written Warning

Subject to 2. a. above, Written Warnings will not be used unless the unit member
has been verbally warned about similar actions in the past four (4) years. A
Written Warning will not be placed in the unit member’s personnel file but may be

d.    Written Reprimand

Subject to 2. a. above, written reprimands will not be used unless the unit
member has been verbally warned about similar actions in the past four (4)
years. The unit member will be provided with a copy of the reprimand and a copy
may be placed in the unit member's personnel file. The unit member may attach
a response pursuant    to Article 18 Personnel Files.

e.    Suspension Without    Pay

Subject to 2. a. above, suspension will not be used unless the unit member has
received a written reprimand about similar actions in the past four (4) years. No
unit member will be suspended more than fifteen (15) working days during a
school year. In all instances, however, the length of a suspension will relate to
the severity of the action.

3. Notice

Notice of suspension will be made in writing and served in person or by certified mail
upon the unit member by the superintendent or designee. A copy of the notice shall be
concurrently provided to the Association president. The notice of the suspension will
contain:

a    A statement of the specific acts or omissions upon which the action is based;

b.    A statement of the cause(s) for which action is recommended;

c.    Where applicable, the Education Code section, policy, rule, regulation, or
directive violated;
