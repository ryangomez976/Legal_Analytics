ARTICLE 1
AGREEMENT

1.1 The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and
binding Agreement ("Agreement") by and between the Oak Run
Elementary School District ("District") and the Oak Run Teachers
Association/CTA/N EA ("Association").

ARTICLE 2

RECOGNITION

2.1 The District confirms its recognition of the Association as the exclusive
representative of all District certificated employees employed under
contract, excluding substitutes, temporary, management, confidential,
and supervisory employees.

ARTICLE 3

DAYS OF WORK AND HOURS AT SCHOOL

3.1    Workdays

Unit members shall render regular service to the District on (183) days per
school year i.e. (180) instructional days and (3) non-instructional days. The
District shall calendar two of the three non-instructional workdays before the
first pupil attendance day or with approval of the unit, schedule one of these
days can during the school year. The District shall calendar one non-
instructional workday after the last pupil attendance day of the school year.
The District may schedule meetings or other District related activities on such
three non-instructional workdays.

3.2    Work Hours

Unit members shall report for duty 30 minutes before the beginning of the first
class and remain on duty for at least 30 minutes after school after the last
class in session is dismissed for a total of 6.5 hours of their 8-hour day or 40
hour workweek. Unit members will use their professional discretion as to when
and where they will complete the remaining 1.5 hours of their 8 hour workday.

During the non-instructional workdays and Minimum days unit members shall
render service during the same hours as rendered on pupil attendance days.
The District may schedule meetings or other District related activities on such
three non-instructional workdays.

In the event it is necessary for unit members to leave the work place
during the above assigned duty times, unit members shall notify the
Superintendent or designee in advance for approval when leaving. Unit
members shall be provided a 30-minute duty free lunch, but if they leave
campus they must notify the office in case of a student or school
emergency.

3.3 Collaboration /Meetings

Unit members may be required to stay an additional 60 minutes past their
normal duty day twice per month for meetings regarding general school
business, student achievement, collaboration, and/or professional development
All efforts will be made to use Minimum Days to accomplish these objectives.

ARTICLE 4

LAYOFFS

4.1    Release Time

Unit members who are laid off shall be provided up to three (3) days of paid
leave for any necessary interview for future employment. Upon request, the
member shall submit reasonable verification of the potential employer and
the fact, time and location of the interview.

4.2    Health Benefits

A unit member who is laid off shall be entitled, along with his or her
dependents, to continue enrollment in any health and welfare plan offered by
the District to unit members as described in Article 8.2.1. The District shall
pay the necessary premiums through October 31 unless the member obtains
employment elsewhere where the employee is eligible for such insurance
benefits. Thereafter, the unit member may, if he or she chooses, and if
permissible and acceptable under the rules and regulations of the insurance
carrier, continue to pay the necessary premiums on a monthly basis for no
more than a total of three (3) years if acceptable to the insurance carrier ol
permissible under law.

4.3 Recall Rights

4.3.1    Laid off unit members shall have first priority for filling any vacancies
which occur for up to 24 months following the effective date of his or
her layoff.

4.3.2    If any vacancy or open position, including, but not limited to a
temporary or substitute position, occurs, the District shall offer the
position to the most senior laid off unit member with a credential
allowing him or her to perform the service required by the open or
vacant position. Temporary or long-term substitute positions will be at
the negotiated pay for such positions for regular temporary or long¬
term substitutes. Such offer shall be by registered letter to the laid off
unit member’s current mailing address on file with the District.

4.3.3    Laid off unit members shall have the right to reject any job offered
without waiver of any recall rights.

4

4.3.4 If P-2 ADA is 76 or below, the Association and each of the certificate
employees grants to the District the unilateral and unconditional right
to layoff the most junior teacher without compliance with any
applicable provisions of law, including, but not limited to, the
Education Code or Government Code, such as Education Code
sections 44949 or 44955 or Government Code sections 11500, et seq.
Such layoff of the most junior teacher will be accomplished solely and
only by a letter from the District to said teacher to be delivered on or
before May 15. The Association and all certificated employees waive
any and all rights to hearing, notice, cause or otherwise, and agree
that cause exists within the meaning of the applicable Education Code
and Government Code statutes to reduce the certificated teaching
staff by one full-time equivalent, certified teaching position if the ADA
at P-2 is 76 or less. If the ADA at P-2 is over 76, then the District
must proceed to layoff the most junior teacher, should it choose to do
so, under the applicable provisions of law.

4.4 Layoffs Negotiated

This completes the negotiation on the topic of layoffs. No further negotiation is
required.

ARTICLE 5
LEAVES OF ABSENCE

5.1    Rules for General Absence

5.1.1 Unit members shall not be absent from duty without notifying and receiving

permission in advance from the Superintendent or designee, except during lunch
when unit member must notify the office they are leaving in case they are needed
in an emergency.

5.1.2    If a unit member needs to be absent from duty, notice must be given to the District on
the form attached hereto as Appendix A. Whenever possible, the District office shall
be notified prior to the day of absence. Except in the event of emergency, it is the
responsibility of the unit member to see that the class roll book, lesson plans and other
pertinent material necessary to continue the program are made available at the work
site.

5.1.3    The District may require a unit member to undergo a "fit for duty" examination at any
time. These costs thereof shall be paid by the District.

5.2    Sick Leave (Paid Sick Leave Deducted from Sick Leave Bankl

5.2.1    Unit members employed five (5) days per week shall be entitled to ten (10) work days'
leave of absence for illness or injury, with full pay during each school year. Unit
Members employed fewer than five (5) days per week shall be entitled to a
proportionate amount of sick leave. Sick leave is prorated for part time unit members.

5.2.2    Unit members may accumulate unused sick leave without limitation.

5.2.3    Unit members returning to work from sick leave after an illness requiring absence for
three (3) consecutive work days, or a contagious illness, or after three (3) absences in
any school year in conjunction with a weekend or holiday, upon the request of the
District, may be required to provide a written physician’s release certifying the reasons
for such absence and medical permission to return to work.

5.2.4    When a unit member's employment terminates and more sick leave has been used than

earned, the amount used, but not earned, shall be deducted from the next pay warrant.

5.3    Extended illness (When Sick Leave Bank is Exhausted)

Unit members are entitled to receive differential compensation, under Education Code
section 44977, for a period of up to five (5) school months. Differential compensation
shall be the difference between the employee's daily salary (annual salary divided by
the number of teacher duty days) and the District established daily rate for substitute or
temporary teachers.

6

5.4 Pregnancy Disability Leave

A unit member may utilize accumulated Sick Leave for the purpose of a disability
related to pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and the recovery therefrom. The length
of such leave, including the date on which the leave shall commence and the date on
which the unit member shall resume duties, shall be determined by the unit member's
physician; provided that such verification demonstrates that such leave is for pregnancy
related disability. Such pregnancy disability leave with pay shall be granted and
administered in the same manner as Sick Leave.

5.5 Industrial Accident and Illness Leave (Workers' Compensation Leave)

5.5.1    Unit members shall be eligible to apply for a leave of absence because of industrial
accident or illness arising out of a claim that the District's insurance carrier determines to
be valid and which is recognized as compensable after adjudication under the workers'
compensation statues. Upon submission of a claim, the unit member shall, as soon as
possible, perform all appropriate actions necessary to file for Workers' Compensation,
disability, income protection benefits, or the like.

5.5.2    Allowable leaves shall be for not more than one period of sixty (60) workdays in any
one fiscal year for the same accident and shall commence with the first day of absence.

5.5.3    Leave of absence under this provision shall not be accumulated from year to year. When
the industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the unit
member shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due him/her for the same
illness or injury.

5.5.4    Unit members shall be paid such portion of the salary due them for any month in which
absence occurs as, when added to the temporary disability indemnity under the
California Labor Code, will result in payment to them of not more than their full salaries.

5.5.5    Leave of absence applied for under this provision shall be reduced by one day for
each day of authorized absence, regardless of a temporary disability indemnity award to
the unit member.

5.5.6    Industrial illness and accident leave is to be used in lieu of sick leave. Ifa unit member is
receiving a temporary disability indemnity, the unit member shall be entitled to utilize only
so much of his/her sick leave and vacation leave which, when added to his/her temporary
disability indemnity, will result in a payment to him/her of no more than a full day's wages
or salary. The District, in turn, shall issue the appropriate salary warrants for payment of
salary and shall deduct normal retirement and other authorized deductions.

5.5.7    Unless travel outside of California is authorized by the District in writing prior to the
commencement of said travel, unit members receiving benefits under the policy during a
period of illness or injury shall remain in the State of California.

7

5.5.8    The District may require, from time to time, a written statement from the unit
member's physician verifying a unit member's absence under this leave and his/her
ability to return to work, or verifying the continued illness or inability to work due to
an accident, he unit member shall provide a written statement from the unit
member's physician verifying the cause of the unit member's absence under this
leave and specifying a date for reevaluation or for return to work. The unit member
shall provide a written statement from the physician after the reevaluation specifying
the date for return to work. If the date to return to work is to be extended or
shortened, the unit member's physician shall provide a written statement specifying
the reasons for the extension or shortening of the date for return to work. The
District may require a written statement from the unit member's physician or a
physician of the District's choice certifying the medical necessity of continued leave
of absence.

5.5.9    Leave of absence under this provision shall be reduced by one (1) day for each day
of authorized absence, regardless of a temporary disability indemnity award or other
compensation from any other source paid to the unit member.

5.6 Personal Necessity Leave (Paid Leave Deducted from Sick Leave Bank)

5.6.1    Unit members may use up to 7 days of Sick Leave for the following purposes:

5.6.1.1    Death of a member of the immediate family when additional leave is
required beyond that provided under Bereavement Leave. (No advance
notice required)

5.6.1.2    Serious illness of a member of the immediate family. (No advance notice required)

5.6.1.3    Accident involving the person or property of the unit member, or the person
or property of the unit member’s immediate family. (No advance notice
required)

5.6.1.4    Imminent danger to the home of the employee which reasonably requires
the attention of the employee during duty hours. (No advance notice
required).

5.6.1.5    Birth or adoption of the unit member's child. (Advance notice required)

5.6.1.6    Appearance in any court or before any administrative tribunal as a litigant, party or
witness under subpoena or any order made with jurisdiction (Advance notice
required).

5.6.2    "Member of the immediate family" shall be as defined in the Bereavement Leave
provision of this Agreement.

5.6.3 Unit members shall not be required to secure advance permission for leave taken for

property of the immediate family or imminent danger to home. The unit member must

5.6.4    A unit member must secure advance permission for all absences not specified in the

5.6.5    Unit members shall complete the District's absence form, which shall verify that the

5.6.6    Personnel necessary leave shall be taken in minimum increments of 1 day and not

Article 6

EVALUATIONS

6.1 Evaluation Procedures

6.1.1    The District retains responsibility for the evaluation and assessment of performance of
each unit member, subject only to the following procedural requirements. Accordingly, no
grievance arising under this Article shall challenge the content of the evaluation. Any
grievance shall be limited to a claim that the following procedures have been violated. Any
request to review the procedures of an evaluation must be made, in writing to the
Superintendent, within fifteen (15) calendar days after the unit member’s receipt of the
evaluation in issue. If such request is timely made in writing then the Board will schedule
a meeting to review the procedures in the evaluation and if the Board makes a
determination that the procedures have not been followed then the Board shall take
appropriate action to remedy the procedural error.

6.1.2    Frequency:

6.1.2.1    Probationary certificated unit members shall be evaluated at least twice each
school year: First evaluation shall be by December 15; and the second
evaluation shall be by March 15.

6.1.2.2    Permanent certificated unit members shall be evaluated at least once every two

(2) school years by May 15.

6.1.3 Criteria: Certificated unit members shall be evaluated on the following criteria:

6.1.3.1 The progress of pupils toward the standards established in accordance with
Education Code §44662.

6.1.3.2    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the unit member.

6.1.3.3    The unit member’s adherence to curricular objectives.

6.1.3.4    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment, within
the scope of the unit member's responsibilities.

6.1.3.6.1    Completion of required records and report such as roll sheets, grade
reports, cumulative records, daily attendance.

6.1.3.6.2    Participation in faculty meetings, grade level meetings, Open House,
Back-To-School Night, and curriculum and other staff development
programs.

6.1.3.6.3    Communication with parents, staff members, and community
members.

6.1.3.6.4    Showing respect and courtesy in relationships with others.

6.1.3.6.5    Plan or coordinate the work of instructional aides, volunteers and
other paraprofessionals as required.

6.1.3.6.6    Supervision of students at all time during the assigned workday.

6.1.3.6.7    Complies with applicable laws, policies and regulations.

6.2.2.2.2    Peer Observation/Coaching: All communication regarding the actual
observation/coaching, including the results shall be exclusively
between the peers and a summary shared with the supervisor.

6.3 Observations

6.3.1    Formal observations shall be based in part upon a minimum of sixty (60) minutes of
observation which shall include at least one observation of a minimum of thirty (30)
continuous minutes or one (1) class period, whichever is longer. All formal observations
shall be followed up with a post observation conference.

6.3.2    Informal observations need not be scheduled in advance and need not have a post
observation conference.

6.4 Post- Evaluation Conference

6.4.1    Probationary: The first evaluation for first and second year probationary employees shall
be completed by December 15. A second evaluation for first and second year probationary
employees shall be held by March 15.

6.4.2    Permanent: The final performance evaluation of unit members shall be in writing and a
copy shall be given to the unit member when completed, which shall not be later than May

15th.

6.4.3    The unit members shall have the right to comment upon the final written evaluation and
have these comments attached to the evaluation within his/her personnel file.

6.5 Training and Assistance for Unit Members

6.5.1    Both the District and the Association recognize that a unit member’s first priority is
student learning. All teachers, temporary, probationary, or permanent are eligible for
assistance.

6.5.2    Prior to developing an Individual Assistance Plan, the supervising administrator and
the teacher shall meet and discuss the reasons why the Individual Assistance Plan is
being developed.

6.5.3    For any unit member whose first (or second) evaluation is below District standards, an
Individual Assistance Plan shall be provided by the District in writing to the unit member and
the Association. This plan shall include:

a.    Criteria for improvement

b.    Duties not being performed in satisfactory manner

c.    Specific assistance and recommendations for improvement.

d.    Specific programs and/or person from whom assistance can be
obtained. (The unit member will have the option to choose his/her person
for assistance.)

6.5.4    Monthly job improvement meetings shall be held between the supervising administrator
and the unit member for the purpose of monitoring the unit member’s progress. An
Association representative may attend these meetings.

6.5.5    A unit member who has an Individual Assistance Plan may request a different
evaluator mutually agreed upon by the unit member and district administration.

6.5.6    Teachers receiving unsatisfactory evaluations shall not be eligible for a step increase on
the salary schedule for the following year and thereafter until a satisfactory evaluation is
achieved, but the unit member will continue to receive column credits for units. If an
employee receives a satisfactory evaluation in any of the following years, they will
receive step credit and be placed on the salary schedule step that they would have been
on if they had received a satisfactory evaluation. Step adjustment will be made at the
beginning of the following contract year in which the satisfactory evaluation was
received. There will be no retroactive salary adjustments for previous years.

6.5.7    No negative performance evaluation shall be based solely upon any written or verbal
material of a derogatory nature received by the evaluator from a citizen or a parent unless
the employee has first been given an opportunity to discuss the matter with the evaluator.
Within ten (10) calendar days of notice, the employee shall have the right to have written
comments attached to such derogatory material prior to its being place in the personnel file.

ARTICLE 11
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

11.1.1 A grievance is a complaint by an employee, group of employees, or
the Association of an alleged violation, or misapplication of the
expressed written terms and conditions of this Agreement.

11.2    Purpose

11.2.1    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible
administrative level, equitable solutions to the problems that may from
time to time arise affecting the welfare or working conditions of
teachers. Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as
informal and confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the
procedure.

11.2.2    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible,
the time limits specified at each level should be considered to be
maximums and every reasonable effort should be made to expedite
the process. The time limits may, however, be extended by mutual
consent.

11.2.3    In the event a grievance is filed at such a time that it cannot be
processed through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end
of the school year, and if left unresolved until the beginning of the
following school year, could result in harm to an aggrieved person, the
time limits set forth herein will be reduced so that the procedure may be
exhausted prior to the end of the school year or as soon as is
practicable.

11.3    Procedure

11.3.1    Processing - After trying to resolve a grievance at an informal level, a
formal grievance shall be initiated in writing, on an approved form, and
shall be filed with the immediate supervisor, on the form
contained in Appendix "D.”

11.3.2    A grievance filed which is not in accordance with the above
requirement may be returned with a written statement to the grievant
for proper completion.

11.3.3    The District shall provide the Association with a copy of the
grievance, the decisions rendered up to & including Formal Level 2.

20

11.3.4 In processing grievances, the parties will make a good faith effort to

avoid interruption of classroom activities and District operations and
any unnecessary involvement of students in all phases of a grievance
procedure.

11.4 Steps

11-4.1 Informal, Level 1

The grievant should first discuss the grievance with the immediate
supervisor with the objective of resolving the matter informally. This
should occur within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding winter break of
the alleged grievable occurrence or action and/or the grievant's
knowledge of the act, events, or omissions.

11.4.2    Formal. Level 2

If the grievant is not satisfied with the informal level disposition of
the grievance, he/she may communicate the grievance in writing to
the immediate supervisor within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding
winter break of the informal meeting. The grievance shall consist of

(I) a clear and concise statement of the problem; (2) the specific
provision of the Agreement involved; and (3} the specific remedy
sought. The immediate supervisor shall make a written decision with
a copy sent to the grievant within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding
winter break of receipt of the written grievance.

11.4.3    Formal, Level 3

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision of the immediate
supervisor, he/she may appeal to the Superintendent in writing
within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding winter break of receipt of
the decision. The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance,
the decision rendered and a statement of the reasons of the appeal.
The Superintendent shall make a written decision with a copy sent to
the grievant within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding winter break of
receipt of the written grievance. If immediate supervisor is the
Superintendent then move forward to step 11.4.4.

11.4.4    Formal. Level 4 (Mediation) or Formai Level 5 (Board of Trustees

11.4.4.1 Mediation - If the grievant and/or the Association is not

satisfied with disposition of the grievance, or if no disposition
has occurred pursuant to the provisions of Level Three, the
grievant may request Mediation or Board Hearing (11.4.5).

21

11.4.4.2    For mediation, the Association shall request that a
conciliator/mediator from the California State
Mediation/Conciliation Service, from any other mutually
agreeable recognized dispute resolution center, be
assigned to assist the parties in the resolution of the
grievance.

11.4.4.3    The mediator, within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding
winter break of the request shall meet with the grievant, the
Association and the District for the purpose of resolving the
grievance.

11.4.4.4    If an agreement is reached, the agreement shall be
reduced to writing and shall be signed by the grievant, the
Association and the District. This agreement shall not be
precedential and shall constitute a settlement of the
grievance.

11.4.4.5    In the event that the grievant, the Association and the
Superintendent or her/his designee have not resolved the
grievance with the assistance of the conciliator/mediator
within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding winter break
from the first meeting held by the conciliator/mediator,
the Association may terminate Level Four and the
grievance may proceed to Level Five.

11.4.5 Formal, Level Five (Board Appeal)

11.4.5.1    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision of the
Superintendent in Level 3 he may request Level 4 Mediation
or advance immediately to Level 5 and appeal to the Board in
writing within fifteen (15) calendar days excluding winter break
of receipt of the decision. The appeal shall include a copy of
the original grievance, the decision rendered at Level 2 & 3
and a statement of the reasons of the appeal. Appeals to the
Board shall be heard by the Board within fifteen (15)
calendar days excluding winter break of their receipt of the
appeal.

11.4.5.2    After the hearing before the Board of Trustees, the Board of
Trustees shall render a decision on the grievance and send
its decision to the Superintendent and the grievant.

11.4.5.3 The Board alone has the power to render a final and binding
determination of the grievance, subject to judicial review
which must be filed no later than 60 calendar days after the
date the decision of the Board of Trustees is mailed or
delivered to the grievant. The governing board may reopen
the record for the taking of additional evidence.

11.5 Rights of Teachers to Representation

11.5.3    No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the Superintendent or designee or
by any member representative of the administration or the Board
against any aggrieved person, any party in interest, any member of the
Association, or any other participant in the grievance procedure by
reason of such participation.

11.5.4    A grievant may be represented at all stages of the grievance procedure
by himself/herself, or, at his/her option, by a representative selected by
the Association. If a grievant is not represented by the Association or
its representative, the Association shall have the right to be present
and to state its views at all stages of the grievance procedure.

11.5.5 When it is necessary for a representative designated by the Association
to attend a grievance meeting or hearing during the day, s/he will, upon
notice to the Superintendent or designee by the President of the
Association, be released without loss of pay in order to permit participating
in the foregoing activities. Any unit member who is requested to
appear in such investigations, meetings, or hearing as a witness will be
accorded the same right.

11.6 General Provisions

11.6.3    The grievant shall be present at all stages of the proceeding of the
grievance, unless illness, accident or death in the immediate
family occurs.

11.6.4    A decision rendered at any step during the grievance procedure
becomes final unless appealed within the time limit specified. The time
limits specified at each level should be considered to be maximums.
The time limits may, however, be extended by mutual written
agreement. Time is of the essence to all provisions.

11.6.5    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be
processed through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end

23

of the school year, and could, if left unresolved until the beginning of
the following school year, result in harm to the grievant, the time limits
set forth herein may be reduced so that the procedure may be
exhausted as close to the end of the school year as possible. The
grievant shall process the grievance during the summer months if so
agreed upon by the grievant and the District.

11.6.6    Time limits for appeal provided in each level shall begin the day
following receipt of the written decision.

11.6.7    All documents, communications and records dealing with the
processing of a grievance shall be filed in a separate grievance file.

11.6.8    If more than one employee makes the same complaint against the
District, or substantially the same complaint, only one employee, on
behalf of himself/herself and the other complainants, may process the
grievance or complaint through the grievance procedure. The names
and signatures of all aggrieved parties shall appear on all documents
related to the processing of the grievance.

11.6.9    The District or the grievant may, at any step in the formal leave, have
one other person serve as a representative and to be present during
the proceeding.
