The Articles and provisions contained within this
Agreement constitute an Agreement ("Agreement") by and
between the    Val    Verde Unified School District

("District") and the Val Verde Teachers Association
("Association").

Term of Agreement

This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from
July 1 2012 to June 30,    2015.    Negotiations    will    take

place during 2013-2014 and 2014-2015 and for 2013-2014
each party will be able to renegotiate one additional
article per side.

Interest Based Bargaining

This Agreement is the product of an "interest based"
bargaining process. Interest based bargaining is based
on the following assumptions:    bargaining    enhances    the

relationship between parties; both parties can win in
bargaining and they can help each other to win; open and
honest discussion expands the areas of mutual interest
and expands the options available to parties in
negotiation; and, while power exists in any bargaining
relationship, mutually developed standards move decision¬
making away from reliance on power and toward satisfying
the interests of both parties. The Val Verde Unified 1

ARTICLE THREE
NEGOTIATION PROCEDURE

Reopener and Successor Agreement Negotiations
Each party shall be responsible for providing written
notice and a proposal to the other party of its desire to
negotiate reopeners or a successor agreement and the
nature of its proposed changes to this Agreement (see
section 1.2). Each party shall also be responsible for
ensuring the public notice provisions of the Act are
fulfilled.

Explanation of Negotiate in Good Faith

"Negotiate in    good    faith" means    the duty to meet and

negotiate, as provided by Section 3540.1(h) of the Act.
Consultants

Either party    may    utilize    the    services of outside

consultants.

Authorized Parties

Either party    may    discharge    their respective duties

required by this    Agreement    by    means of authorized

officers, individuals, representatives or committees.

Time and Place

Negotiations (requested by either party) shall take place
at mutually agreeable times and places.

Release Time for Representatives

25

The Association shall receive an aggregate total of two
hundred (200) hours each year of release time for its
representatives to meet and negotiate with the District's
representatives concerning reopener and successor
Agreement negotiations. Unless agreed to otherwise, any
additional time beyond the aggregate total of two hundred
(200) hours shall be before or after classroom duty
hours.

Signing of Agreement

Any agreement reached between the parties shall be
reduced to writing and signed by the designated
representatives of both parties.

Press Release

There will be no press release other than a joint press

release

during the course

of

negotiations.

unless an

impasse

has been declared

by

either party.

When an

agreement has been reached there shall be a joint press
release.

Completion of Negotiations

During the term of this Agreement, the District and WTA
expressly waive and relinquish the right to meet and
negotiate, except as set forth in Sections 1.2 and 3.1
above. The parties further agree that neither the
District nor WTA shall be obligated to meet and negotiate with
respect to any subject or matter, whether referred to or not in
this Agreement, even though such subject or matter may not have
been within the knowledge or contemplation of the parties at 6

subject or matter was proposed and later withdrawn.

4.1 Hours - General

Unit members are expected to remain at the school site to
work during the hours indicated below and shall also
remain on site beyond such hours as needed to perform
additional instructional and professional duties as
indicated below.

(a)    The length of the regular workday for unit members
shall be seven (7) hours and thirty (30) minutes,
inclusive of a thirty (30) minute lunch period. (See

4.3 regarding thirty (30) minute duty-free lunch
period for teaching employees.)

(b)    Unit members who are assigned to regular classroom
teaching shall not be required to report to work
more than thirty (30) minutes prior to the beginning
of the normal student instructional day.

ARTICLE FIFTEEN

LAY-OFFS

Procedures

In the event that the District's Board of Education
determines that it has become necessary by reason of any
of the conditions set forth in Education Code sections
44955 or 44955.5 to decrease the number of certificated
employees, said lay-off shall be conducted according to
the applicable provisions of the Education Code. For
purposes of a lay-off, a unit member's seniority shall be
based on the date upon which the unit member first
rendered paid service in a probationary position for the
District and shall be determined according to the unit
member's placement on the District's certificated
bargaining unit seniority list. The District, however,
may deviate from terminating a unit member in order of
seniority for any of the reasons set forth in Education
Code section 44955(d).

Notice to the Association

At least fifteen (15) calendar days before mailing out
notices of lay-off, the District shall send via U.S.
certified mail, return receipt requested, to WTA a list
of the positions to be eliminated and the reasons

therefore.

Notice to the Unit Member

No later than ten (10) calendar days after the District
mails notices of lay-off to affected unit member, the
District shall send to each affected unit member via U.S.
certified mail a list of unit members it intends to lay¬
off containing their seniority dates and numbers. At the
same time, the District shall deliver a copy of such list
to the Association.

Priority

Except as otherwise provided by statute, no permanent
certificated unit member may be laid-off while any
probationary certificated unit member is retained to
render a service which said permanent certificated unit
member is certificated and competent to render.

Development of the Certificated Seniority List
On May 1,    1992, representatives of the District and WTA

mutually participated in the development of a
Certificated Seniority List to determine the order of
seniority for all unit members. The order of seniority
for unit members who first rendered paid service on the
same date was determined by a random selection process
agreed to and conducted by the parties. The certificated
seniority list shall be revised annually to include new
unit members and delete unit members who have been
separated from District employment. During any revision,
a random selection process agreed to and conducted by the

78

parties shall be utilized to determine the order of
seniority for new employees who first rendered paid
service on the same date. The certificated seniority
list shall also be revised prior to the implementation of
any lay-off.

TO ESTABLISH A SICK LEAVE DONATION PROGRAM

The District and the WTA have established a Sick Leave Donation Program to
which WTA Bargaining Unit Members may irrevocably donate their accrued sick
leave on behalf of other members suffering from catastrophic illness or injury.

1.    The intent of this MOU is for employees in the certificated bargaining
unit to be able to assist fellow bargaining unit employees with
catastrophic illness or injury, who have exhausted their full-time sick
leave.

2.    A catastrophic illness or injury means an illness or injury that is expected
to incapacitate the employee for an extended period of time. Examples
include, but are not limited to, life threatening injury or illness; cancer;
heart surgery; or stroke.

3.    Employees may donate up to a maximum of 2 days of their accrued sick
leave per fiscal year. All sick leave donations are made in full days, not
partial days or hours. All donations are irrevocable. Donations will be
made between July 1 and October 31 of each school year. Employees
returning from extended leave which included the enrollment period and
new hires will be permitted to contribute within 30-calendar days of
beginning work. In addition and from time to time as the committee
deems necessary the committee may seek additional calls for donations.
Donation and withdrawal forms will be available from the WTA.

4.    Participation in the sick leave donation program is voluntary, but requires
a minimum donation of 1 day per school year. Beginning July, 2006
only current contributors will be permitted to request a withdrawal.
Participation in the voluntary sick leave sharing program is not subject to
the Grievance Procedure in die Bargaining Agreement between the
District and the WTA or District policy.

5.    Requests to receive sick leave shall be approved by the 3 member
Catastrophic Dlness/Injury Committee consisting of 2 members
designated by the WTA President and 1 member designated by the
District.

1

6. Sick leave may be approved by the Catastrophic Illness/Injury

Committee for donation to an employee if all of the following

requirements are met:

6.1    The employee is suffering from a catastrophic illness or injury
requests that sick leave be donated and provides verification of
catastrophic injury or illness as required by the Committee.
Applications for withdrawal or extensions of withdrawal will be
accompanied by a doctor’s statement indicating the nature of the
illness or injury and the probable length of absence.

6.2    The Committee determines that the employee is unable to work
due to the employee’s catastrophic illness or injury for an
extended period of time.

6.3    The Committee determines that the employee’s catastrophic
illness or injury will cause the employee to exhaust all fully paid
sick leave, thereby necessitating the donation.

6.4    Withdrawals from the Catastrophic Leave Bank shall be granted
in units of no more than 30 duty days. Requests for extensions of
withdrawals may be submitted to the committee as prior grants
expire. If an individual is incapacitated, applications may be
submitted to the committee by the participant’s agent or family
member.

6.5    The maximum amount of time that donated leave credits may be
used per employee is in conjunction with the up to 100 work days
provided under Section 11.8 of the VVTA Agreement. Thus,
should the catastrophic illness or injury continue for any
employee beyond the 100 work days, additional days may not be
approved by the Committee for that employee.

6.6    An example of the sick leave credit approved by the Committee
would be as follows: Assume that individuals have donated 50
days of sick leave to the sick leave pool. Assume that two
individuals have been approved by the Committee for receiving
sick days. Assume that each individual has accrued 20 days of
unused sick leave. Per Section 11.8 of the VVTA Agreement,
each individual would be entitled to up to 100 work days of the
lesser of either 50% of salary or the cost of a substitute. It is
agreed that each day of donated sick leave will equate to one-half
of a day for an employee on extended illness leave, so that the
assumed 50 days will equate to 100 half days. Thus, each

2

employee would be eligible for 100 half days of pay when
combined with his/her entitlement under Section 11.8 will result
in the employee receiving full pay for up to 100 work days. Thus,
there is not a strict conversion of days into dollars, but rather,
each donated day equals two half days, so that the employee may
continue to receive full pay as a result of receiving the extended
illness leave and the donated half day.

6.7    From time to time, the Committee may solicit donations of sick
leave from the bargaining unit due to actual or anticipated need.
The Committee may state the name of the employee and may,
with the employee’s permission, state the general nature of the
catastrophic illness or injury.

6.8    Employees working on a part-time basis or in the Pre-School
Head Start Program will have a pro-rated amount as determined
by the Committee regarding days to be donated or to be requested.

7.    The District, through its Business Division, will track the number of days
donated and utilized and provide such information as needed to the
Committee.

8.    This is a pilot program that will become effective immediately and will
be in place through June 30, 2007. In the Spring of 2007, the negotiating
teams for the District will review this program. Should the program not
be continued, any days not utilized will be returned to the donors on a
pro-rata basis, as determined by the Committee.

maximum of 60 minutes after the start of the teacher workday to notify the principal of
the need for a substitute. Should notice be given after the 60 minute grace period, the
teachers receiving students shall not qualify for compensation.

records.
