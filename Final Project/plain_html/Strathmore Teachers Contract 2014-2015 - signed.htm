ARTICLE VIII:

PERSONNEL FILES

8.1    The district shall maintain the official personnel files. Every employee shall have the
right to inspect materials in their personnel file upon request, normally during non-
instructional time.

8.2    Materials in personnel files of employees is not to include ratings, reports, or records
which were obtained prior to the employment of the person involved.

8.3    Derogatory material shall not be placed in the teacher's personnel file unless the person
filing the material has signed, and dated same and the teacher has had at least five (5)
working days to read and respond to the material. The teacher shall acknowledge having
read such material in advance by affixing his/her signature or the signature of a witness if
the teacher refuses to sign on the actual copy to be filed. Such signature does not indicate
agreement with content. A copy of such material shall be made available to the teacher
upon written request at no cost. He/she shall also be given an opportunity during the
school day to initial and date the material and to prepare a written response to such
material. The written response shall be attached to the materials.

8.4    Written authorization by the employee shall be required prior to any review of the
personnel file by an agent of the employee.

8.5    At the employee's request, negative or derogatory material in a teacher's personnel file
shall be sealed after remaining in the file for a period of three (3) years. After notifying
the employee, the Superintendent or Board of Trustees may direct that the files be
opened.

8.6    No material contained in the official personnel files shall be stored on computer disks.

8.7    The person or persons who draft non-routine material for placement in a unit member's
file shall sign said material and signify the date on which such material was placed in the
file.

8.7    Access to personnel files shall be limited to members of the District Management Team
and Board of Education on a need to know basis.

8.8    The contents of all official teacher persomiel files shall be kept in strictest confidence.

8.9    No disciplinary action against a teacher shall be based upon unsubstantiated hearsay.

ARTICLE XVII:    GRIEVANCE    PROCEDURE

17.1    Introduction

17.1.1    A    "grievance"    is    a    claim by the Association or by one or more teachers

that there has been a violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of a
provision of this Agreement, or a violation, misapplication, or
misinterpretation of any law. Board policy,or regulation.

17.1.2    The "grievant" is the unit member, unit members, or the Association
making the claim.

17.1.3    A    "party    of    interest"    is any person who might be required to take action or

against whom action might be taken in order to resolve the grievance.

17.1.4    A    "day"    is    any    duty    day in which the grievant is required by contract to

render service.

17.2    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise affecting the
welfare or working conditions of unit members and the Association. Both parties agree
that these proceedings will be kept as informal and confidential as may be appropriate at
any level of the procedure.

17.2.1    An    aggrieved    person    shall normally seek a solution to his/her problem

through at least one informal discussion with his/her immediate supervisor
prior to initiating a formalized grievance proceeding as hereinafter
described. Adjustment of a grievance through such an informal discussion
may be accomplished without the intervention of the Association.
However, the aggrieved person may, at his/her discretion, be represented
by the Association in any such informal discussion.

2014-2015

17.3

17.2.2    Since    it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible,

the time limits specified at each level should be considered to be
maximums and every effort should be made to expedite the process. The
time limits may, however, be extended by mutual agreement of grievant
and District representative.

17.2.3    In the event a grievance is filed at such a time that it cannot be processed
through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end of the school
year, and, if left unresolved until the beginning of the following school
year could result in harm to an aggrieved person, the time limits set forth
herein, may be, by mutual consent, reduced so that the procedure may be
exhausted prior to the end of the school year or as soon as is practicable.

Formal Procedure

17.3.1    Level    One:    Immediate    Supervisor

17.3.1.1    A grievance shall be presented in writing to the immediate
supervisor using the grievance form with a copy
simultaneously provided to the Association. The
immediate supervisor shall meet with the grievant and/or
designated Association representative within five (5) days
of receipt of the grievance. The immediate supervisor shall
provide a written disposition of the grievance, including the
