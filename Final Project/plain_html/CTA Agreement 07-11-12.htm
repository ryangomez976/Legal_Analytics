ARTICLE I.

AGREEMENT

1.1    This is an Agreement made and entered into by and between the Scott Valley Unified
School District ("District") and the Scott Valley Teachers Association ("Association").

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to section(s) 3540-3549 of the California
Government Code ("The Rhodda Act").

1.3    There are no other agreements between the parties. This is the first agreement between
fee-parties- All previous collective bargaining agreements between the Association and
any component District of the new District have expired and the terms thereof are
superseded and replaced in all respects by this agreement.

ARTICLE III.

RECOGNITION

The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative for that unit of employees
consisting of all certificated employees excluding preschool teachers, all management,
supervisory, confidential, temporary, or substitute certificated employees, and all certificated
employees performing administrative services.

This District and the Association recognize the right of employees to form, join and participate in
lawful activities of employee organizations and the equal alternative right of employees to refuse
to form, join and participate in employee organization activities.

ARTICLE IV.

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

4.1    All Association activities held on campus will be conducted by unit members and
Association officials outside unit members' assigned work hours, excluding the lunch
period, and in such a way which will not interfere with the duties of unit members, the
duties of classified employees or the operation of the District. Association
representatives who are not employed by the District shall follow District procedures
prior to visiting any District property or school site.

4.2    Subject to applicable board policies, the Association has the right to use District facilities,
when not otherwise in use, for the purpose of meetings concerned with the exercise of
rights guaranteed by the Educational Employment Relations Act subject to reasonable
regulation.

4.3    The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities on matters of Association
concern on Association bulletin boards in the staff rooms.

4.4    The Association may use school mail boxes for communications that are approved by the
Executive Board of the Association. At the time of distribution the Association shall
provide a copy of such material to the Superintendent or designee.

4.5    The Association retains all of its rights as set forth in the Educational Employment
Relations Act.

ARTICLE VI.

HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

6.1    Work Days

Unit members shall annually render services to the District on one hundred eighty (180)
pupil attendance days plus two (2) work days as scheduled by the District and three (3)
professional development days as scheduled by the District, for a total of one hundred
eighty five (185) work days. The District will consult with the Association before
establishing the school calendar for the subsequent school year or making changes in the
existing school calendar.

6.2    Work Hours

The workday for full-time unit members shall be seven (7) hours. Unit members shall
work on campus or as otherwise assigned.

6.2.1    Unit members shall have a thirty (30) minute duty-free lunch period, which is not
part of the seven (7) hour workday.

6.2.2    Unit members may leave right after students on approved minimum days or on
Fridays unless otherwise directed. Unit members may not leave early on
minimum or shortened days scheduled for the purposes of “Staff
Collaboration.”

6.3    If a unit member is absent from duty or desires to leave school within the duty hours set
forth above, he/she may do so for any reason specified under Article VII "Leaves."

6.4    Unit members shall perform, as assigned by the District, adjunct professional
responsibilities outside of the hours set forth above, including but not limited to, faculty
meetings, parent conferences, open house, back-to-school night, graduation and up to

6.5    Unit members employed as counselors may be assigned by the District to counseling
duties outside of their one hundred eighty-five (185) work days, and in such event, shall
be given three (3) work days minimum advance written notice and shall be paid their
regular daily rate of pay for such service.

6.6    Unit members that are assigned to sites will travel no more than once a day between
District campuses and will be given adequate time to drive the trip safely. Such driving
time is not part of the duty free lunch but is part of the regular assigned work day.

6.7    On work days of non-student attendance, starting times and ending times may be
modified by the District so long as the total length of the work day is not extended
beyond that which is provided in Section 6.2.

6.8    Professional Development Days

There will be three (3) additional days for Professional Development thus the work year
will increase to one hundred eighty-five (185) days. Three (3) additional Professional
Development Days are added at full pay to the salary schedule. Two (2) of these days are
to be added to the school calendar, and the third day is to be arranged individually by the
teachers with pre-approval from the site administrator. These teacher arranged days shall
be on days that are otherwise non-paid and shall be between July 1st and the district
arranged Professional Development day in the spring. The District will arrange a
Professional Development day in the spring that will fulfill the requirements for those
members who have not completed their individually arranged day of Professional
Development before that time. This district arranged Professional Development day will
be on a Saturday or some other non-paid day, and attendance shall be mandatory for

6.9    Preparation Time

The District agrees to continue the existing practice of scheduling preparation periods

school year.

Employees may be assigned to substitute during their preparation period. Such
assignments will be made so that the amount assigned any unit member in the school
does not exceed the amount assigned other unit members in a given year. However, it is
recognized that a reasonable variation in the amount of such assignments between unit
members may exist.

ARTICLE VII.

LEAVES

7.1 General Rules Regarding Leaves of Absence

7.1.1    Unit members shall not be absent from duty without notifying and receiving
advanced permission from the Superintendent or designee, except during lunch
when only notification is required for absence.

7.1.2    If a unit member needs to be absent from duty, notice must be given on the
District’s leave of absence form. Whenever possible, unit members shall notify
the school office prior to the day of absence. Except in the event of an
emergency, it is the responsibility of the unit member to see that the class roll
book or attendance sheet(s), lesson plans and other pertinent material, necessary
to continue the program, are made available at the work site.

7.1.3    The District retains its right to require a unit member to participate and cooperate
in any fit for work examination at any time conducted by a medical doctor or
other medical care professional selected by the District. The cost of such
examination shall be paid by the District to the extent not paid by the unit
member’s insurance carrier.

7.1.4    Definition, “Immediate Family”

“Immediate Family” is defined as the spouse, and the son, son-in-law, daughter,
daughter-in-law, brother, sister, mother, father, grandmother, grandfather, or
grandchild of the employee or spouse or any relative living in the immediate
household of the employee.

7.2 Sick Leave

7.2.1    Accrual

For a school year of service, every unit member employed five (5) days a week
shall be entitled to ten (10) days leave of absence for illness or injury. A unit
member employed for less than five (5) school days a week shall be entitled to
that proportion of ten (10) days leave of absence for illness or injury as the
number of days they are employed per week bears to five (5).

7.2.2    Returning to Work

Unit members returning to work from sick leave after an absence of five (5)
consecutive work days or more, or a contagious illness, upon the request of the
District, must provide a written medical doctor’s release certifying the reasons for
such absence and medical permission to return to work.

7.2.3    Rate of Pay

Pay for any day of such absence shall be the same as the pay which would have
been received had the employee served during the day.

7.2.4    Leave Not Accrued

Credit for leave of absence need not be accrued prior to taking such leave and
may be taken at any time during the school year.

7.2.5    Accumulation

If such employee does not take the full amount of sick leave allowed in any
school year, the amount not taken shall be accumulated from year to year.

7.3 Extended Illness

Unit members are entitled to receive differential compensation, under Education Code

section 44977, for a period of up to five (5) school months. Differential compensation
shall be the difference between the employee's daily salary (annual salary divided by the
number of teacher work days) and the District established daily rate for substitute or
temporary teachers.

7.4 Maternity Leave/Child Birth or Adoption Leave

7.4.1    The District shall provide for leave of absence from duty for any unit member
who is required to be absent from duties because of pregnancy, miscarriage,
childbirth, and recovery thereof, pursuant to BP/AR 4161.8 (FMLA).

7.4.2    Length of Leave

The length of leave of absence, including the date on which the leave commences,
and the date on which the employee shall resume duties, shall be determined by
the employee and the physician.

7.4.3    Leave with Pay for Disabilities

Leave with pay shall be granted only when it is necessary to do so in order that
leaves of absence for disabilities caused or contributed to by pregnancy,
miscarriage, or childbirth, be treated the same as leaves for injury, illness or
disability.

7.4.3.1 Temporary Disabilities

Disabilities caused or contributed to by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth
and recovery there from are, for all job related purposes, temporary
disabilities and shall be treated as such under any health or temporary
disability insurance or sick leave plan available in connection with
employment by the District.

7.4.4 Birth and/or Adoption

A unit member shall be granted two (2) days of paid absence leave for the birth or
adoption of each of their children. One day shall be granted for the day of birth or
adoption, and the second day shall be granted on a subsequent day following the
birth or adoption. The second day must be taken within five (5) days of the birth
or adoption.

7 5 Industrial Accident and Illness Leave (Workers’ Compensation Leave!

7.5.1    A unit member shall be eligible to apply for a leave of absence because of
industrial accident or illness. Upon submission of a claim, the unit member shall,
immediately, perform all appropriate actions necessary to file for Workers’
Compensation, disability, income protection benefits, or the like.

7.5.2    Allowable leaves shall be for not more than one period of sixty (60) work days in
any one fiscal year for the same accident and shall commence with the first day of
absence.

7.5.3    Leave of absence under this provision shall not be accumulated from year to year.
When the industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the
unit member shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due him/her for
the same illness or injury.

7.5.4    Unit members shall be paid such portion of the salary due them for any month in
which absence occurs as, when added to the temporary disability indemnity under
the California Labor Code, will result in payment to them of not more than their
full salaries.

7.7 Discretionary Leave

7.7.1 Unit members may use up to four (4) days of personal necessity leave as

discretionary leave with pay, without specifying a reason for such leave, upon the
following conditions:

(a)    The unit member provides at least three (3) working days advance written
notice on the District’s leave of absence form unless there is an emergency. Such
notice shall be given to the Superintendent or designee. The Superintendent or
designee has the discretion to grant or deny such leave in the event of a school
emergency.

(b)    Such discretionary leave may not be used during the first two weeks or last
two weeks of the pupil attendance year or during any Professional Development
days without the advance permission of the Superintendent. Under such
extenuating circumstances, it is agreed that the granting of such leave by the
Superintendent is neither a precedent nor past practice.

(c)    An acceptable substitute teacher is available.

(d)    Discretionary leave may only be used as full day and half day absences.

(e)    Use of discretionary days is limited to two (2) three (3) consecutive days at
any one time.

Covered Leave

Leave for one (1) hour or less may be granted within the work day, without loss of pay,

particulars on this topic.

7.15 Unauthorized Leave

A new employee with ten (10) years previous experience would begin service on Step 11.

9.1 Evaluation Procedures

9.1.1    The District retains the responsibility for evaluation and assessment of

(NCLB), and whose previous evaluation rated the employee as meeting or

minutes or one class period. The unit member shall have the right to identify any

decline to meet with the complainant. If a meeting is held, then the unit

ARTICLE XX.

SHARED CONTRACTS

20.1    The primary criteria for approving shared contracts shall be successfully meeting the
educational needs of students and ensuring the other provisions of BP 4113 are met.

20.2    Requests for shared contracts shall be made through the site administrator to the
Superintendent by February 1 of the school year preceding the school year in which the
partial assignment is desired.

20.3    Unit members desiring a shared contract shall apply for a partial leave of absence for
the portion of the contract they wish to vacate.

20.4    Request for partial contracts shall be approved by the site administrator, superintendent,
and the Board and will be conditioned upon filling the remainder of the position if a full-
time equivalent position is needed by the District. The proposed division of the contract
must be approved by the site administrator who shall then make his/her recommendation
to the Superintendent. The employee shall be notified by May 15 if the shared contract is
approved.

20.5    Unit members who were full-time prior to a shared contract will maintain their seniority
at the end of the shared contract.
