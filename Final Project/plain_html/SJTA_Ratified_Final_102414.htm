ARTICLE I: AGREEMENT AND TERMS

1.1    The articles and provisions contained herein constitute an Agreement by and between the
Governing Board of the San Jacinto Unified School District (hereinafter referred to as "District")
and the SJTA-CTA-NEA (hereinafter referred to as "Association"), an employee organization.

1.2    During the term of this Agreement, the Association and the District are committed to the Interest
Based Bargaining process. This is an ongoing problem-solving process responsive to changing
needs and conditions. In the spirit of maintaining a positive working relationship through this
process, the Association and District will meet regularly to resolve differences in a fair manner
without disruption of District's operations nor diminution of unit members' rights.

1.3    Term of the Agreement

This will replace Article 1.3 in the Certificated Collective Bargaining Agreement. The term of this
agreement is from July 1, 2012 - June 30, 2013.

ARTICLE II: RECOGNITION

2.1    The District confirms its recognition of the Association as the exclusive representative for that unit
of employees recognized by the District per Resolution 75-3 dated May 4, 1976, and modified May
18, 1976."

2.2    The District recognizes the Association as the sole and exclusive agent for all certificated
employees employed by the District; as per District resolution 75-3 dated May 4, 1976 and
modified May 18, 1976; in accordance with the inclusions and exclusions described below:

The bargaining unit includes all certificated employees including classroom teachers, intern
teachers, school nurses, school psychologists, speech and language pathologists, special
education/resources teachers, counselors, teachers on special assignment, music teachers, and
children’s center permit teachers. Certificated employees excluded from the unit include ROP,
ROTC, summer school. Adult Education, guest (substitute) teachers, supervisory employees,
confidential employees, employees designated by the Board as Management, including but not
limited to. Superintendent, Assistant Superintendent, Directors, Coordinators, Curriculum,
Assistants, Principals, and Assistant Principals.

ARTICLE III: DISTRICT RIGHTS

3.1 It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority to direct, manage,
and control to the full extent of applicable laws. Included in, but not limited to, those duties and
powers are the exclusive right to: determine its organization; direct the work of its employees;
determine the times and hours of operation; determine the kinds and levels of services to be
provided, and the methods and means of providing them; establish its educational policies, goals,
and objectives; ensure the rights and educational opportunities of students; determine the number
and kinds of personnel required; maintain the efficiency of District operations; determine the
curriculum; build, move, or modify facilities; establish budget procedures and determine budgetary
allocation; determine the methods of raising revenue; contract out work; and take action on any
matter in the event of an emergency. In addition, the Board retains the right to hire, classify, assign,
evaluate, promote, terminate and discipline employees.

3

3.2    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties, and responsibilities by the District,
the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof, and the use of
judgment and discretion in connection therewith, shall be limited only by the specific and express
terms of this Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific and express terms are in
conformance with law.

3.3    The District shall, when practicable, consult with the Association prior to the declaration of an
emergency and shall seek consensus on the need for suspension of the relevant provisions of the
Agreement for the duration of the emergency. When such a meeting is not possible prior to the
declaration of an emergency, the District shall consult with the Association as soon as possible.

3.4    In the event an emergency has been determined by the Board, the Association shall have the right to
negotiate remedies for any adverse affects upon the Association/unit member(s) caused by
suspension of relevant provisions of the Agreement. The request to negotiate must be submitted in
writing to the Superintendent within 30 days after the emergency has ceased.

ARTICLE IV: ASSOCIATION SECURITY

4.1    Any employee who is a member of the Association may sign and deliver to the District an
assignment authorizing deduction of membership dues in the Association. Pursuant to such
authorization, the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues from the regular salary check
of the teacher each month for ten (10) months and remit the same to the Association.

4.2    Any dispute as to dues deductions under this Article shall be between the individual unit member
and the Association. The Association shall defend and hold the District harmless against any and
all claims by individual unit members, including all legal fees and other expenses arising out of
dues deductions under this Article.

4.3    Any employee who chooses to join the Association will be considered a member for the term of the
Agreement and will continue to pay dues during the term of the Agreement.

6.1 Salary Schedules and Minimum Teacher Salary

6.1.1    During school year 2007-2008 the District will implement a restructured salary schedule.
The restructured schedule will be implemented effective with the mid-year point of each
unit member’s contract year. For a 184 day employee the mid-year point is 92 workdays.

year of service to the District.

6.1.3    Adjust salary and health benefits for full-time eligible Preschool/Head Start employees per
applicable funded COLA (as specified in 6.9).

6.1.4    It is understood by the parties that a minimum salary amount shall not obligate the District
to increase the salary schedule proportionately.

6.1.5    The District and the Association mutually agree to negotiate, in accordance with the law,
any one-time monies received from the State that are designated as “bonuses” for
certificated employees, as a result of Academic Performance Index improvements at
individual schools.

6.1.6    If a preparation period is worked as a regularly scheduled class, as approved in writing by
the Superintendent or designee, the teacher will receive l/6th of their regular contract pay
calculated on a per diem basis and adjusted prorata if for less than a contract year.

6.1.7    Middle school and high school unit members may be assigned by the administration to

substitute whenever a colleague is absent and a substitute is not available. The site
administrator shall first request volunteers.    In the absence of volunteers, the site

administrator shall assign unit members to substitute on a rotational basis. The pay per
period will be at the summer school rate. The minimum increment of pay will be one-half
(1/2) hour.

6.1.8    At the elementary schools, if a teacher is absent from campus for the whole day and a
substitute teacher is not provided by the District, up to three unit members who receive the
equal divided class shall receive proportionate (i.e., 100, 14, 14) compensation given to off¬
track unit member District substitute teachers, or a prorated amount if the substitute service
is for one-half a day. The compensation shall be divided equally among the teachers
receiving said students.

6.1.9    Effective July 1, 2007, the summer school/extra duty hourly rate is set at $42.66 per hour.

6.1.10    Credit toward initial salary placement on the salary schedule begins with the Bachelor’s
Degree. Only units earned after the receipt of the Bachelor’s Degree shall be used in the
initial salary placement. Graduate course work credits taken during attainment of a
Bachelor’s Degree earned by unit members hired after July 1, 2006, as part of a
comprehensive, accredited credentialing program shall also be considered in initial salary
placement. The Personnel Department will validate that the graduate course credits are
appropriate for salary placement prior to initial salary placement. Credits earned must be
directly related to the unit member’s assignment, credential (s), advanced education degree
or educational professional growth.

6

6.1.11 Effective August 15, 2008, all new non-credentialed, emergency credentialed, and
internship certificated unit members, including waivers, provision intern permits, short term
staffing permits, and limited assignments, will be placed on column 1, step 1 of the
appropriate certificated salary schedule (teacher, psychologist, counselor, nurse or speech
therapist). These unit members will not move forward on step or column of the salary
schedule until they receive a valid preliminary or clear credential. All current interns and
non-credentialed unit members, as described above, employed by the District as of July 1,
2008, shall be y-rated at their present column and step. Y-rating means that the unit
member will not progress on the salary schedule until the member meets specified
requirements. The specified requirement is the receipt of his or her preliminary or clear
credential specific to the area in which he or she teaches. Once the unit member has
received a preliminary or clear credential, they will be eligible to advance to the appropriate
column of the salary schedule, in compliance with the deadlines set forth in the SJTA
Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article 6.8. The unit member is also eligible to advance
a maximum of one step on the salary schedule in July following the member's receipt of a
preliminary or clear credential, regardless of the number of years the unit member was y-
rated or frozen on the salary schedule.

lunch provided the normal on-campus day shall not exceed 7 hrs. and 20 minutes. The normal
work day for other unit members shall be 7 hrs. excluding a duty-free lunch. Unit members'
contractual obligations shall be within the normal work day except as specified in 7.3 and 7.4.

ARTICLE XII: LEAVE PROVISIONS

The District and the Association agree that as professionals, leave time shall not be used for purposes of the
extension of a holiday or a vacation period, for matters which can reasonably be taken care of outside the
work hours, or for recreational activities.

12.1 Personal Illness and Injury Leave

12.1.1 Full-time unit members shall be entitled to ten (10) days leave with full pay at the
beginning of each school year for purposes of personal illness or injury'. Unit members
who work less than full time shall be entitled to that portion of the ten (10) days leave as
the number of hours per week of scheduled duty related to the number of hours for a full¬
time unit member in a comparable position. Full-time unit members shall be entitled to 1.0
day of paid leave for each calendar month of full-time employment.

12.1.1.1 Full-time unit members shall be entitled to use, in any school year, 7 days of
accrued and available sick leave to attend to an illness of their child, parent or
spouse. This entitlement shall be prorated for part-time unit members.

18

12.1.2    After all earned leave as set forth in 12.1.1 above is exhausted, additional non-accumulated
leave shall be available for a period not to exceed five (5) school months, provided that the
provisions of 12.1.4 below are met. The amount deducted for leave purposes from the unit
member's salary shall be the amount actually paid a substitute employee employed to fill
the position during the leave, or, if no substitute is employed, the amount which would
have been paid to a substitute.

12.1.3    If a unit member does not utilize the full amount of leave as authorized in 12.1.1 above in
any school year, the amount not utilized shall be accumulated from year to year.

12.1.4    Upon request by District management, a unit member shall be required to present a medical
doctor's certificate verifying the personal illness or injury. The District may require, at
District expense, a certified medical specialist to visit the unit member and make all
necessary inquiries in order to be fully informed as to the nature and severity of the illness
or injury, and to report such findings to the Superintendent or designee. If the report
concludes that the absence is not due to personal illness or injury, or that the illness is not
sufficiently severe to warrant continued absence, then the Superintendent or designee, after
notice to the unit member, may refuse to grant such leave. If requested by the District
management, a unit member shall not return to work until he submits a medical doctor's
authorization to return to work. This paragraph shall be invoked only in those instances
where a District representative has reasonable cause to believe that an abuse of the leave
provisions has occurred.

12.1.5    A unit member must notify the District’s automated substitute calling system as soon as the
absence is known, but, except in cases of emergency, not later than 5:45 A M. for unit
members working at the secondary sites, or 6:15 A M. for unit members working at
elementary sites or other district locations, prior to the start of the work day to permit the
employer time to secure substitute service. In cases of advanced scheduled medical
appointments, the unit member shall notify the automated substitute calling system at least
three days prior to the appointment.

12.1.6    A unit member who is absent for one-half day or less shall have deducted one-half day
from the accumulated leave; and if the absence exceeds more than one-half day, a full day
shall be deducted from accumulated leave. Exception: the employee may be released from
on-site duties after the instructional day at the administrator's discretion for emergency
illness or a medical appointment without loss of sick leave.

12.1.7    A unit member shall not be allowed to return to work and shall be charged an additional
sick day if the unit member fails to notify the District of his/her intent to return to work at
least one (1) hour prior to the close of the preceding work day if such failure results in a
substitute being secured.

12.1.8    Each unit member shall be notified of the accumulated leave by no later than October 15 of
each school year.

12.2 Personal Necessity Leave

12.2.1 Leave which is credited under 12.1.1 of this Article may be used, at the unit member's
election, for purposes of personal necessity, provided that use of such personal necessity
leave does not exceed seven (7) days in any school year.

12.2.2    For purposes of this provision, personal necessity shall be limited to: (a) death or serious
illness of a member of the unit member's immediate family, or (b) an accident which is
unforeseen involving the unit member's immediate family.

12.2.3    Whenever possible, the unit member will give advance notification to his/her immediate
supervisor and shall make every effort to comply with District procedures to enable the
District to secure a substitute.

12.2.4    Under all circumstances, a unit member shall verify in writing on the appropriate form that
the personal necessity leave was used only for purposes as set forth in 12.2.2 above.

12.3    Bereavement Leave

12.3.1    Every unit member shall be entitled to five (5) days of paid leave of absence, or seven (7)
days if travel of more than 300 miles one way is involved, in the event of the death of a
member of his/her immediate family. This leave shall not be deducted from sick leave.

12.3.2    Bereavement Leave shall be used before Personal Necessity Leave days are used for
purposes allowed in this article.

12.3.3    Members of the unit member's immediate family are defined as mother, mother-in-law,
stepmother, father, father-in-law, stepfather, spouse, son, stepson, son-in-law, daughter,
stepdaughter, daughter-in-law, brother, sister, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, grandmother,
grandfather, or grandchild of the unit member, or any relative living in the immediate
household of the unit member.

12.3.4    The Superintendent may grant bereavement leave in situations involving other than the
immediate family, if an unstipulated family relationship is established.

12.4    Leave for Pregnancy Disability

12.4.1    Unit members are entitled to use sick leave as set forth in Article 12.1.1 for disabilities
resulting from pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and recovery therefrom on the same
terms and conditions governing leaves of absence for other illness or medical disability.
Such leave shall not be used for child care, child rearing, or preparation for childbearing,
but shall be limited to those disabilities as set forth above. The length of such disability
leave, including the date on which the leave shall commence and the date on which the
duties are to be resumed, shall be determined by the unit member and the unit member's
physician. However, the District may require a verification of the extent of disability
through a physical examination of the employee by a physician appointed by the District at
District expense.

12.4.2    Unit members are entitled to leave without pay for disabilities because of pregnancy,
miscarriage, childbirth, or recovery therefrom when sick leave as set forth in Articles 12.1.1
and 12.1.2 have been exhausted. The date on which the employee shall resume duties shall
be determined by the unit member on leave and the unit member's physician. However, the
District may require a verification of the extent of disability through a physical examination
of the employee by a physician appointed by the District and at District expense. If a unit
member returns to work full time within the same school year, effort will be made to return
the unit member to their previous assignment.

12.5    Leave without Pay for Childbearing Preparation and Child Rearing

20

12.5.1    Leave without pay may be granted to a unit member for preparation for childbearing and
for child rearing.

12.5.2    The unit member shall request such leave as soon as practicable, but under no
circumstances less than forty (40) work days prior to the date on which the leave is to

12.5.4    The duration of such leave shall consist of no more than twelve (12) consecutive months

12.5.5    The unit member is not entitled to the use of an accrued sick leave or other paid leave while

12.5.6    There shall not be a diminution of employment status for childbearing or child rearing

12.5.7    If a unit member is on leave for childbearing or child rearing, and in the event of a

qualified, the District will assign the unit member to a position as soon as possible.

12.6 Industrial Accident Leave

12.6.1    Unit members will be entitled to industrial accident leave according to the provisions in
Education Code Section 44984 for personal injury which has qualified for worker's
compensation under the provisions of the current contracted worker's compensation carrier.

12.6.2    Such leave shall not exceed sixty (60) days during which the schools of the District are
required to be in session or when the employee would otherwise have been performing
work for the District in any one fiscal year for the same industrial accident.

12.6.3    The District reserves the right to have the unit member examined at District expense by a
physician designated by the District to assist in determining the length of time during which
the unit member will be temporarily unable to perform assigned duties and the degree to
which a disability is attributable to the injury involved.

12.6.4 For any days of absence from duty as a result of the same industrial accident, the unit
member shall endorse to the District any wage loss benefit check from the current
contracted worker's compensation carrier which would make the total compensation from
both sources exceed 100 percent of the amount the unit member would have received as
salary had there been no industrial accident or illness.

12.6.5 If the unit member fails to endorse to the District any wage loss disability, indemnity check
received on account of the industrial accident or illness as provided above, the District shall
deduct from the unit member's salary warrant the amount of such disability indemnity
actually paid to and retained by the unit member.

12.7    Judicial Duty

12.7.1    Unit members shall be provided leave with pay for required jury duty. The employee shall
provide his/her immediate supervisor with official notification of jury duty at least ten (10)
days prior to the reporting date. If the unit member receives notification less than ten (10)
days prior to the reporting date, he/she shall notify his/her immediate supervisor the day
following receipt of notice. The District reserves the right to request the unit member to
seek a delay in such duty to a time most convenient for the District.

12.7.2    The unit member shall endorse all compensation received for jury duty accrued on a regular
workday over to the District in return for full pay from the District. Mileage compensation
provided by the court may be retained by the unit member.

12.8    Military Leave of Absence

12.8.1    Every unit member employed by the District in a probationary or permanent position who
is required to enter the active military service of the United States or the State of California,
including active service for training, shall be entitled to and granted a Military Leave of
Absence in accordance with Education Code 44800.

12.8.2 Unit members who have not been employed by the District for at least one year
immediately prior to the day in which the absence begins shall not be entitled to a Military
Leave.

12.8.3    Unit members on military leave shall receive compensation for the first 30 calendar days of
such an absence.

12.8.4    Unit members exercising a military leave shall only be allowed to be paid for one 30-day
calendar period during any one fiscal year.

12.9    Unspecified Leave(s) Without Pay

12.9.4    This year of leave would not constitute one year of service in terms of movement on the

12.9.5    Neither salary nor fringe benefit contributions will be made by the District to the unit

12.9.6    Upon return from this leave, the District shall return the unit member to an assignment for

12.10    Compelling Personal Importance Leave

12.10.1    A leave of absence for personal matters of concern to employees that are characterized by

shall make his/her request, on the appropriate district form, at least three days prior to the

is not possible. In such cases, the unit member shall give as much advance notification as
possible or provide justification upon return.

12.10.2    Compelling Personal Importance Leave shall include: (a) attendance at the funeral of a

advisor in matters not involving the District, or others such as IRS official, court obligation,
etc., when office hours or obligations preclude use of after school hours; (d) transaction of
responsibilities on behalf of incapacitated relative when employee is the only person

•    protection of personal property due to a natural disaster

•    accident

•    environmental conditions making travel to and from work impossible or unsafe

be charged to accumulated personal illness and injury leave defined in Article 12.1.1. The
Superintendent or designee may limit the number of employees on Professional Discretion
Leave on any given day.

instructional and classroom management skills, to expand curricular knowledge, and to encourage
personal and professional growth. This process shall incorporate the requirements of the California
Education Code, which includes the development of annual goals and objectives. The goals and
objectives will be developed with input from the unit member and the administrator. Individual
goals and objectives may be established by the administrator at the pre conference

ARTICLE XVII: DISCIPLINE

17.1    The purpose of the Employee Discipline Article is to establish a corrective process of professional

improvement for unit members. The intent is to have a process that is fair, consistent, and
progressive in application. Unit members shall be disciplined for just cause only.

17.2    General Provisions

17.2.1    Administrators are encouraged to address unit member infractions through informal means,
prior to beginning the formal steps in the discipline process.

17.2.2    Disciplinary action in the form of dismissal shall be in accordance with the appropriate
provisions of the Education Code.

17.2.3    Probationary unit members are excluded from the Grievance Procedures as it applies to this
article.

17.2.4    The unit member will be notified in writing on the appropriate district form by the
administrator that a disciplinary conference will be held. Twenty-four hour notification
will be given unless circumstances dictate more immediate action as determined by the
administrator.

17.2.5    The unit member has the right to secure Association representation at any step of the
discipline process.

17.2.6    A unit member who begins a discipline conference without Association representation may
interrupt the conference at any time to secure representation. The administrator will
reschedule the conference. The administrator will reschedule the conference and issue
written notice as specified in 17.2.4.

17.2.7    For the purposes of this article a school year is defined as July 1 through June 30.

17.3    Steps for Discipline

17.3.1 The following progressive discipline procedures shall be applied, except where the nature
of the offense may require an administrator to skip steps and go directly to a written
summary, a written warning, a written reprimand, or a suspension without pay. When a
step(s) is skipped the conditions of the previous steps shall not apply. Incidents which may
require the skipping of steps include but are not limited to the following:

•    Endangering the safety of others

•    Immoral/unprofessional conduct

•    Illegal acts

•    Behavior which disrupts the normal work of schools

•    Insubordination

When a step(s) is skipped, a written rationale justifying this action will be included in the
documentation. Prior to being placed on suspension without pay, a unit member may be
placed on paid administrative leave pending the outcome of an investigation by the District.

33

17.3.2    A discipline conference will include a verbal discussion of the following elements:

•    identification of the step of the discipline conference

•    facts of the problem

•    mle broken or infraction

•    impact on students, program or district

•    suggestions for improvement

•    knowledge of consequences if infractions continue

17.3.3    Steps in the progressive discipline process shall include:

Step 1. Counseling Conference/Verbal With Written Summary. During a verbal counseling
conference, the unit member shall be informed of the infraction and the expected performance
level. A written summary of the conference will be provided to the unit member within five days of
the conference. The unit member may respond in writing to the administrator. The unit member
may request to the Personnel Office a review of the facts of the incident by the Superintendent or
designee. This request will be in writing. The summary and the response will be attached. The
post-conference summary shall not be placed in the unit member's personnel file. This step is not
subject to the Grievance Procedures. After two step one conferences, should a third infraction
occur within the same school year, movement to step 2 is mandated.

Step 2. Conference/Written Warning. During this conference the unit member will be given a
copy of the written warning. Written summary documentation from step one may be attached. The
unit member shall sign the warning to acknowledge receipt. A copy will be placed in the unit
member's personnel file in accordance with Education Code 44031. This step is subject to the
Grievance Procedures. As a part of this step a unit member may be placed on an Individualized
Assistance Plan which will be placed in the personnel file. After a step two conference, should
another infraction occur within ninety duty days or the end of the school year, whichever is longer,
movement to step 3 is mandated.

Step 3. Conference/Written Reprimand. During this conference the unit member will be given a
copy of the written reprimand. Documentation from prior steps may be attached. The unit
member shall sign the reprimand to acknowledge receipt. A copy will be placed in the unit
member's personnel file in accordance with Education Code 44031. This step is subject to the
Grievance Procedures. As a part of this step a unit member shall be placed on an Individualized
Assistance Plan, which will be placed in the personnel file. After a step three conference, should
another infraction occur within one year from the date of the conference, movement to step 4 may
result.

Step 4. Conference/Suspension Without Pay. Suspension without pay shall not be used unless the
unit member has received a written reprimand about a similar infraction(s) within the last twelve
months. No unit member shall be suspended without pay for more than six working days per
infraction or more than fifteen working days for all infractions during any school year. Notice of
suspension shall be made in writing and served in person or by certified mail upon the unit member
by the superintendent or designee. A copy of the suspension notice will be placed in the unit
member's personnel file in accordance with Education Code 44031. This step is subject to the
Grievance Procedures.

ARTICLE XVIII: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

18.1    The purpose of this process is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level, swift and
equitable resolutions of grievances and the maintenance of a positive labor-management
relationship. The Association and District agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal and
confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the process.

18.2    Definitions

18.2.1    A "grievance" is a written allegation by a grievant that there has been a violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of a specific provision of this Agreement.

18.2.2    A "grievant" may be the Association or a unit member covered by the specific provisions of
this Agreement. The grievant will be identified on the Dispute Resolution Form.

18.2.3    A "day" is any day which the central administration office of the District is open for
business.

18.2.4    The "immediate supervisor" is the lowest level administrator having jurisdiction over the
disputed issue.

18.2.5    "Computation of time" shall begin on the "day" following the act, omission or practice
giving rise to the grievance.

18.3    General Provisions

18.3.1    The grievant and immediate supervisor must be present at all levels of the processing of a
grievance. The grievant may be represented by the Association at all levels of the
grievance resolution process. The District may be represented by any person or agent
designated by them to act on their behalf.

18.3.2    A grievance shall not be valid for consideration unless it is submitted in writing, on the
prescribed form, to the immediate supervisor setting forth the facts and specific

35

provision(s) of the Agreement allegedly violated and the particular relief sought. The
written grievance must be submitted within thirty (30) days from the date of the most recent
act, omission or practice giving rise to the grievance. Failure to file any grievance within
such time period shall be deemed a waiver thereof.

18.3.3    A decision rendered at any level in these procedures becomes final, unless appealed within
the time period specified.

18.3.4    Failure by the District or its representative(s) to render a decision within the time periods
specified herein shall constitute a denial of the grievance and the grievant may appeal to the
next level.

18.3.5    Time periods given in these procedures may be modified by written agreement between the
Association and the District.

18.3.6    If the same problem is identified by more than one unit member, the Association or one (1)
unit member on behalf of himself/herself and the other members may process the problem
through the grievance process. Names of unit members known to be affected shall appear
on all documents related to the processing of the grievance. This provision may be waived
by written agreement between the Association and the District.

18.3.7    Any unit member may present grievances in accordance with this Article without
intervention of the Association. However, the proposed resolution of the grievance shall
not be final until the Association has been provided a copy of such resolution and been
given ten (10) days to either accept it or appeal it to the next level.

18.3.8    A unit member's grievance shall in no way interfere with the right of the District to proceed
in carrying out its management responsibilities. Unit members shall carry out all
management directives pending the final resolution of a grievance.

18.3.9    All documents resulting from the processing of a grievance shall be kept in a separate
grievance resolution file and shall not be kept in a unit member's personnel file. Facts and
information disclosed through the grievance procedure may also be part of other documents
related to the unit member's personnel file.

18.3.10    At any time after Level III, the Superintendent or designee may attempt to resolve the
grievance by meeting with the grievant and the Association representative.

18.3.11    When it is necessary for a representative designated by the Association to attend a
grievance meeting or hearing during the day, she/he shall be provided reasonable release
time without loss of pay in order to permit participation in the foregoing activity.

18.4 Process

18.4.1    Level I. Problem Solving. Prior to filing a grievance, the potential grievant will schedule
a meeting with the immediate supervisor to identify the problem and seek resolution. In the
event the problem is not resolved at Level I, a grievance may be submitted on the
prescribed form at Level II within the thirty (30) day time limitation provided in paragraph
17.3.2.

18.4.2    Level II. Immediate Supervisor. After receipt of the completed grievance resolution
form, the immediate supervisor shall have ten (10) days in which to render a written
decision regarding the grievance. In the event the grievance is not resolved at Level II, the

18.4.3    Level III. Superintendent. Within ten (10) days after receipt of the appeal, the
Superintendent or designee shall render a decision in writing to the grievant. In the event
the grievance is not resolved at Level III, the decision may be appealed to Level IV
Grievance Mediation provided such appeal is submitted on the prescribed form to the
Superintendent or designee within ten (10) days after receipt of the decision at Level III.

18.4.4    Level IV. Grievance Mediation.

18.4.4.1 Within ten (10) days after receipt of the appeal, the District must request that a
mediator be assigned by the California State Conciliation Service to assist in
facilitating resolution of the grievance.

18.4.4.2    If an agreement is reached, the agreement shall be reduced to writing and shall be
signed by the grievant, the Association and the District. This agreement shall be
non precedential and shall constitute a settlement of the grievance.

18.4.4.3    If the grievance is not resolved at Level IV, the grievance may proceed to
arbitration. The grievant must submit this request on the prescribed form to the
Superintendent or designee within ten (10) days after the conclusion of the
grievance mediation.

18.4.5 Level V. Arbitration.

18.4.5.1    Within ten (10) days after receipt of the appeal, the District and Association shall
attempt to agree upon an arbitrator. If no agreement is reached, the parties shall
request a list of arbitrators from the California State Mediation and Conciliation
Service. The selection of the arbitrator shall be made by alternately striking
names from such list until one name remains. The party who strikes the first
name shall be determined by lot.

18.4.5.2    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be borne equally
by the District and the Association. All other expenses shall be borne by the
party incurring them.

18.4.5.3    The arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear evidence and render a decision on
the issue or issues submitted. If the parties cannot agree upon a submission
agreement, the arbitrator shall determine the issues by referring to the written
grievance and the answers thereto at each step.

18.4.5.4    The arbitrator will have no power to add to, subtract from or modify the terms of
this Agreement or the written policies, rules, regulations and procedures of the
District.

18.4.5.5    The arbitrator may hear and determine only one grievance at a time unless both
parties mutually agree to consolidate similar grievances.

18.4.5.6    If any question(s) arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such question(s)
shall be first decided by the arbitrator in a separate hearing before consideration
of the merits of the grievance.

37

18.4.5.7 After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to make written
arguments, the arbitrator shall submit in writing to all parties his findings and
recommendations which shall be final and binding.

18.5    The exercise of management rights and prerogatives by the Board under Article III, which are not
limited by the express terms of this Agreement, shall not be subject to the grievance resolution
process, provided, however, that any action by the Board which suspends, modifies, or amends this
Agreement, after it has declared an emergency pursuant to Article III, shall be grievable under this
procedure to determine whether such action is clearly unreasonable, unwarranted and an abuse of
the Board's discretion in relation to the emergency circumstances. A grievance challenging the
Board's actions under this paragraph shall be filed in writing with the Superintendent and shall be
commenced at Level III under paragraph 17.4.3.

18.6    Election of Remedies

18.6.1    This Article is not cumulative and shall be the exclusive procedure and remedy for the
determination of any claim, the subject matter of which constitutes a grievance. Any unit
member who brings an action on any such claim, or on whose behalf such an action is
brought, in a court of law or equity, state or federal, or before any administrative tribunal,
shall immediately thereupon waive any and all rights under this Article.

ARTICLEXIX: JOBSHARING

19.1    Job sharing is defined as two (2) permanent employees in satisfactory status sharing one (1)
full-time teaching assignment. Job sharing shall be by mutual consent of the District and
the teachers and shall not result in any additional cost to the District.

19.2    A written plan for job sharing shall be presented to the District for approval on or before
April 15 prior to the year in which job sharing is requested. Unit members shall be
informed of the status of their proposal by May 15. The plan must be in the best interest of
the students, parents, site administrators, and District. The plan shall include all pertinent
information concerning the assignment (i.e. indicated duration of the assignment,
description of the subject/grade level, location, salary, and benefits). The plan shall include
provisions for attendance at regularly scheduled meetings called by the site administrator,
parent conferences, and staff development in-services.

19.3    Participants shall be placed appropriately on the salary schedule and receive a prorated
portion of the individual yearly salary based on their percent of participation.

19.4    Fringe Benefits

19.4.1    Each shared contract carries only one set of employee benefits.

19.4.2    Unit members working in job sharing positions shall receive prorated amounts of
the maximum employer contribution for health and welfare benefits.

19.4.3    If one participant elects to waive health benefits the remaining participant shall
receive 100% of the applicable District contribution.

19.5    Unit members shall receive a proportionate share of leave benefits. Contributions to

S.T.R.S. shall be based on the percentage of participation in the job share.

38

19.6    Teachers sharing jobs will receive salary step advancement only when they have
accumulated 184 full-time equivalent work days.

19.7    If one participant leaves the arrangement, the remaining participant shall move to full-time
employment unless another job sharer is approved by the District and the unit member.

19.8    Participating unit members shall be returned to full-time employment at the beginning of
the subsequent school year provided they have informed the District of their desire to do so
by March 15 of the current school year.

19.9    Participants involved in the job share must jointly attend all pre-service, Back-to-School,
and Open House events. All obligations regarding duty schedules and District committees
must be met.

19.10    Participants involved in the job share may agree to substitute for each other to promote
program continuity.

19.11    Approval for participation in a shared teaching contract must be obtained on a yearly basis.
Approval must be granted by the site administrator and the Superintendent (or designee).
