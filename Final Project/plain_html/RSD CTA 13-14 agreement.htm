PREAMBLE

This is an Agreement made and entered into this first day of July, 2013, by and between
the Redding Elementary School District ("District") and the Redding Teachers' Association,
CTA/NEA ("Association").

6

ARTICLE 1 - RECOGNITION

The Board recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all certificated
employees designated in the unit as recognized by the Redding Elementary School District
Board of Trustees; excluding those Board-designated management, confidential, and
supervisory employees as defined by the Educational Employment Relations Act.

7

ARTICLE 2 - DISTRICT RIGHTS

2.1    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its power and authority to
direct, manage, and control to the full extent of the law.

2.2    The exercise of the District powers, rights, authority, duties, responsibilities, and the
adoption of policies shall be limited by the specific and express terms of this
Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific and express terms are in
conformance with law.

2.3    This article shall be subject to the grievance procedure for the sole purpose of
determining whether the District acted within the discretionary powers described in
this article. 8

8

ARTICLE 3 - ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

3.1 Use of Bulletin Board and Mail Service

3.1.1    The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities and
matters of Association concern on District bulletin boards. One such
bulletin board shall be provided in each school building in an area that is
frequented by teachers. The Association may use the intradistrict
communication services for communicating with teachers, consistent
with District policies.

3.1.2    The Association President, or designee, may after the student day and
during the teacher day, meet members of the unit at their school site.
The designee shall check with the local site administrator to avoid
conflicts with the administrator's prior planned activities.

3.1.3    The District shall provide the Association with the names of teachers
appointed to District-wide committees.

3.1.4    RTA will select and provide two members to sit on the MAA Advisory
Committee.

3.2 Association Leave

3.2.1    The Association officers and official Association representatives shall be
granted a maximum of twelve (12) paid leave days annually for the
conduct of the Association's affairs.

3.2.2    The Association President shall notify the Superintendent two (2) school
days prior to utilizing such leave. In the event of unforeseen
circumstances, the Association President and the Superintendent may
agree to waive this requirement.

3.3 Dues Deduction and Representation Fee

3.3.1 Dues Deductions

Any teacher who is a member of the Association, or who has applied for
membership, may sign and deliver to the District an assignment authorizing
deduction of unified membership dues, initiation fees and general
assessments in the Association. Pursuant to such authorization, the District
shall deduct one-tenth (1/1 Oth) of such dues from the regular salary check of
the bargaining unit member each month for ten (10) months. Deductions for
bargaining unit members who sign such authorization after the
commencement of the school year shall be appropriately prorated to
complete payments by the end of the school year 9

9

3.3.2 Employee Rights

3.3.2.1    The District and the Association recognize the right of
employees to form, join and participate in lawful activities of
employee organizations and the equal, alternative right of
employees to refuse to form, join and participate in employee
organizations as specified by the Educational Employment
Relations Act, Government Code Section 3540, et seq.
Neither party shall exert pressure upon nor discriminate
against an employee in the exercise of these alternative rights.

3.3.2.2    Accordingly, membership in the Association shall not be
compulsory. A bargaining unit member has the right to
choose, either: to become a member of the Association; or, to
pay to the Association a fee for representation services, or to
refrain from either of the above courses of action upon the
groups set forth in Section 3.3.6 below.

3.3.3 Bargaining Unit Members' Obligations to Exclusive Representative

A bargaining unit member who does not fall within one of the exempted
categories as set forth in Section 3.3.6 below, and who has not voluntarily
made application for membership in the Association within the thirtieth (30th)
day following the date upon which said employee has commenced duties in
the District as a bargaining unit member, must as a condition of employment
in the District pay annually to the Association a representation fee, in
exchange for representation services necessarily performed by the
Association in conformance with its legally imposed duty of fair
representation on behalf of said bargaining unit member who is not a
member of the Association.

In the event that a bargaining unit member shall not pay such fee directly to
the Association, the District shall begin automatic payroll deduction as
provided in Education Code Section 45061 and in the same manner as set
forth in Paragraph 3.3.1 of this Article. There shall be no charge to the
Association for such mandatory agency fee deductions.

Prior to beginning such payroll deduction, the Association will certify to the
District in writing that the employee whose pay is to be affected by the
deduction has: (1) refused to join the Association; (2) has refused to tender
the amount of the agency fee as defined herein; and (3) does not qualify for
an exemption under 3.3.6. 10

10

3.3.4 Definition of Representation Fee

The representation fee to be collected from non-Association bargaining unit
members shall be the amount authorized by Section 3540.1 (l)(2) of the
Government Code.

Any dispute as to the amount of the representation fee shall be resolved
pursuant to the provisions of Section 3.3.7

3.3.4.1    Exceptions

3.3.4.1.1    Full-time, non-exempt bargaining unit members
hired during the school year shall pay a prorated
representation fee. Such pro rata share shall be
based upon the number of days of actual
employment for a school year as compared with
the number of days available for full-time
employment in the school year.

3.3.4.1.2    Part-time, non-exempt bargaining unit members
shall pay a prorated representation fee on the
basis of the said bargaining unit member's annual
salary as compared with the same annual salary
for a comparable full-time bargaining unit member.

3.3.4.1.3    Bargaining unit members on leave without pay,
and bargaining unit members who are on laid-off
status shall be exempt from these provisions
herein; except that the election as to membership
or payment of a fee as set forth in Section 3.3.3
must be exercised within the first ten (10) days
upon return to paid status.

3.3.5    Annual Verification of Representation Fee By Association

At least thirty (30) days prior to any requested enforcement of a
representation fee from any bargaining unit member pursuant to Section

3.3.3, the Association shall submit a written certification to the District
verifying that the total amount of its representation fee conforms to this
Article.

The parties agree that such certification is a condition precedent to
enforcement of the fee by the District as a condition of employment.

3.3.6    Bargaining Unit Members Exempted From Obligation to Pay

11

Any bargaining unit member may be exempted from payment of any fees to
the Association if that person is a member of a religious body whose
traditional tenets or teaching include objections to joining or financially
supporting an "employee organization" as defined in Section 3540.1(d) of the
Government Code.

Such exempt bargaining unit member shall, as an alternative to payment of a
representation fee to the Association, pay an amount equivalent to such
representation fee to:

a.    The    United Way; or

b.    The    American Cancer Society; or

c.    Any    other non-religious, non-labor charitable fund mutually agreed

upon by the District and the Association.

The District, upon written request from the Association, may require such
exempt bargaining unit member to submit a written affidavit to the
Association verifying the existence and nature of the allowable objection to
payment of a representation fee and, in addition, may require such exempt
bargaining unit member to submit proof of payment of an amount equivalent
to such representation fee to one of the alternative funds or organizations
listed above.

Such payments shall be made on or before October 1 of each school year
for returning bargaining unit members or no more than thirty (30) days after
commencing duties for any newly hired bargaining unit member.

3.3.7 Procedure for Bargaining Unit Members Who Contest the Amount of Fee

The parties agree that, in order to provide a uniform definition of the amount
of the representation fee, any such disputes involving the amount of such fee
shall first be deferred to the Public Employment Relations Board for
determination, provided that the parties have first complied with the other
provisions of this Section.

The Association will verify in writing to the District that all of the conditions of
Section 3.3.3 have been met prior to the District's initiation of the fee
deductions set forth in Section 3.3.3. Thereafter the District will notify the
effected bargaining unit member in writing that such deductions will
commence. A copy of the Association's written verification will be attached
to the District's notice. Thereafter, the District will begin the deductions.

If, at any time, the Public Employment Relations Board determines that some
or all of the fees deducted shall be held in escrow pending a determination of
the correct amount of the fee, the District will deposit such amount in an 12

12

escrow account. The monies held in escrow shall be released to the
appropriate party upon the rendering of a final decision by the Public
Employment Relations Board.

3.3.8 Hold Harmless and Indemnity Provision

The Association as defined in this Agreement shall hold the District
harmless, and shall fully and promptly reimburse the District for any fees,
costs, charges or penalties incurred in responding to or defending against
any claims, disputes, challenges, which are actually brought against the
District or any of its agents, in connection with the administration or
enforcement of any Section in this Agreement pertaining to representation
fee. Such reimbursement shall include, but not be limited to, court costs,
litigation expenses, and attorneys' fees incurred by the District.

Upon notice that the District is going to seek indemnification or to be held
harmless under this provision, the Association shall have the right to meet
with the District regarding the reasonableness and merit of any ciaim,
demand, suit or action for which the District seeks indemnification, and shall
attempt to agree whether any such action listed in the paragraph above shall
be compromised, resisted, defended, tried, or appealed.

In determining whether or not such action shall be compromised, resisted,
defended, tried or appealed, the District will defer to the Association's
interests if the District does not have a distinct and separate legal interest in
the matter in dispute.

The District shall not be entitled to be reimbursed for any costs for which the
Association was not properly notified and provided the opportunity to discuss
as set forth herein; nor will the District be entitled to any reimbursement
when the District's efforts in defending against such action would be
duplicative, or when the District does not have a separate and distinct
interest to defend, or when the District is defending any activity which is
arguably subject to criminal liability on the part of any District administrator. 13

ARTICLE 4 - HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

4.1    Professional Day

4.1.1    The orderly conduct of school business requires certain set hours of on¬
site responsibilities; therefore, the teacher day is set at seven and one-
half (714) hours which includes a thirty (30) minute duty-free lunch period.
This seven and one-half (7/4) hours is flexible, at bargaining unit member
discretion, around a seven (7) hour period of time established by the
Board of Trustees for each school site. Site administrators may set the
beginning times on days of building faculty meetings.

4.1.2    In addition to the teacher day noted above, all bargaining unit members
are to participate as a professional obligation in Open House, Back-To-
School Night, Report Card Conferences, Student Study Teams and
lEP’s, Graduation and Parent Orientations.

4.1.3    Bargaining unit members may be called upon to perform other duties
outside the teacher day. The site administrator may require up to eight
(8) hours annually per bargaining unit member for additional
assignments. These hours are non-cumulative and shall not be assigned
in a discriminatory manner.

4.1.4    The building principal may at his/her discretion permit bargaining unit
members to depart from their school site early.

4.1.5    Bargaining unit members who complete their assigned responsibilities in
Kindergarten Roundup outside the contract year will receive one (1) day
paid leave.

4.1.6    Bargaining unit members shall not be assigned to noon duty as
supervisors of students on the playground or in the cafeteria. Bargaining
unit members shall continue the practice of escorting students to the
cafeteria or lunchroom facility.

4.2    Environmental School

Bargaining unit members assigned to the Environmental School will have the option
to be paid as follows:

•    $100 per day of attendance at Environmental School, or

•    one comp day per day of attendance at Environmental school or

•    a combination of the above. 14

14

Because Environmental School is a 5th grade activity, it is assumed that 5th grade
teachers will attend this activity, however, mitigating circumstances for non-
attendance will be resolved by the principal and teacher. (Revised 7/13/99)

4.3    Preparation Period

Bargaining unit members teaching at the sixth through eighth grade levels in
departmentalized programs shall have a daily preparation period.

4.4    Work Year

Effective July 1,2014, a bargaining unit member’s work year shall be a total of one
hundred eighty-four (184) days of which there shall be one hundred eighty (180)
teaching days, one (1) day of preparation and three (3) staff development days..

A district calendar committee which includes two members from the Association
(appointed by the Association President) will review and discuss a draft calendar to
be presented to the association and the district prior to February 1st of each year.
The Association will provide input to the District regarding the draft calendar not
later than March 1 st.

Following board approval, a calendar for each year of this contract shall be attached
to this Agreement as Exhibit A and shall include five (5) minimum days in November
for conferencing plus one (1) minimum day on the last day of school, all to be built
into each year's calendar, along with Wednesday collaboration meeting days
excluding the first and last week of school. The principal will solicit input from the
staff in developing the content of the meeting. The snow day(s) will be determined
in conjunction with calendar negotiations.

ARTICLE 5 - ASSIGNMENTS, REASSIGNMENTS, TRANSFERS

5.1    Assignments and Change of Assignments

5.1.1    For purposes of this Article, "assignment" is the designation of a specific
position or responsibilities within a school or department or work location.
Assignment includes the initial placement of a newly employed bargaining
unit member in a specific school or work location as well as the change of a
bargaining unit member's position or responsibilities within the same school
or department or work location. "Assignment" does not include any extra¬
curricular duty.

5.1.2    Principals shall assign bargaining unit members. In making assignments,
principals shall consider bargaining unit member’s training, experience,
major and minor fields of study, competencies, credentials, and advanced
degrees related to the assignment. A bargaining unit member may request
the reasons for an assignment or change of assignment.

5.1.3    Once each year, a bargaining unit member will submit a District
"Employment Projection Survey." To the extent possible, the principals shall
consider a bargaining unit member's preference in making assignments.

5.1.4    During the first week of May the principal shall indicate the available
teaching positions and shall endeavor to fill the position from the existing
staff in the school.

5.1.5    A tentative assignment list shall be published by the District no later than
June 1 of the school year preceding the effective date.

5.2    Reassignments Due to Layoff

5.2.1    For purposes of this Article, "reassignment" shall be defined as a change to
a different program and/or a movement from one work site to another as a
result of layoff pursuant to Education Code Section 44955, and which may
be necessary to assure the retention of certificated bargaining unit members
with seniority greater than those laid off. This definition specifically excludes
any transfer or change in program assignment which does not result from
the District's obligation to reassign pursuant to Section 44955 of the
Education Code.

5.2.2    Any bargaining unit member scheduled to be reassigned due to layoff shall
be entitled to a written notice of such reassignment within ten (10) calendar
days of the effective date of such reassignment and, in addition, upon
request, will be entitled to a conference with the principal and the
Superintendent regarding the reassignment, at which time the bargaining 16

16

unit member will be provided with the reasons for the reassignment and with
the opportunity to discuss those reasons.

5.3 Bargaining Unit Member-Initiated (Voluntary) Transfers

5.3.1    For purposes of this Article, a "transfer" shall consist of a change in work
location of a Bargaining unit member from one school or work site to
another school or work site within the District. Such a transfer does not
encompass the process of assignment to a specific position and
responsibilities within the school, or department, or work location. A
bargaining unit member assigned to more than one work site shall be
considered as being transferred only when moved from one District-wide
program to another program. Voluntary transfers for the next school year
will be considered from January 31 until the first day teachers report to work.

5.3.2    A "vacancy" is a new position, an opening arising from a resignation,
retirement, or, termination, any position to which a bargaining unit member
is not assigned or which is not committed for purposes of leaves,
unresolved involuntary transfers or layoffs.

5.3.3    Vacancies that become available after the first day teachers report to work
of each school year will be filled by the District for that school year. Such
vacancies shall be posted and filled according to Sections 5.3.4-5.3.7 for
the next school year, (revised 7/1/97)

5.3.4    Notices of vacancies shall be posted no later than the first week in May of
the school year preceding the effective date of the transfer and shall remain
posted for at least five (5) working days in the District administration office
and the staff room at each site. Such notices shall be posted as soon as
the District determines that a vacancy exists and shall include the position
description and location, grade level or subject matter assignment,
credential and other special requirements. A copy of the vacancy notice
shall be forwarded to the Association President. During summer vacation,
vacancy notices will be mailed to designated Association officers and to
bargaining unit members who have indicated to the District, in writing, an
interest in vacancies.

5.3.5    A request for transfer will be sent to the Personnel Office. A conference
shall be held at the request of the bargaining unit member with the
Personnel Office in order to discuss the request.

5.3.6    Requests for voluntary transfer for each vacancy shall be processed as
follows:

5.3.6.1 The Personnel Office shall determine whether each bargaining
unit member requesting the transfer holds the necessary
credential and endorsements/authorizations.

(Revised 3/12/96)

In accordance with this Article and the procedures set forth in the District's

In accordance with this Article and the procedures set forth in the District's
Evaluation Handbook (which procedures are hereby incorporated by
reference) every permanent certificated bargaining unit member shall be
evaluated by an administrator in writing two times during the first two years of
temporary and probationary employment. Year three through ten
evaluations may be on a bi-annual basis. After ten years, evaluations may
be every three years. During the last year of employment, employees who
indicate that they are going to retire at the end of that school year will not be
evaluated during that school year. Upon request, the Association shall be
provided with a list of bargaining unit members being formally evaluated
during the current year. (Revised 7/1/05)

8.9.1    Sabbatical leave is an investment in the bargaining unit member's future
value to the District more than a reward for past services. It may be
granted by the Board for a period not to exceed one (1) year for travel or
study and must be followed by two (2) years of service to the District.

8.9.2    Sabbatical leave must be preceded by at least seven (7) consecutive
years of teaching service, all of which have been served as a regular full¬
time bargaining unit member in the Redding Elementary School District.
Seventy-five percent (75%) of the teaching days in each year count as a
full year of teaching service.

8.9.3    The number of bargaining unit members absent on sabbatical leave at
any one time shall not exceed two percent (2%) of the total number of
bargaining unit members. The number eligible will be rounded to the next
highest whole number.

8.9.4    Sabbatical leave will be granted for no more than one (1) year, but a
leave of absence without pay for one (1) year may follow or precede the
sabbatical leave. Sabbatical leaves may be granted for a semester
during each of two (2) school years with the approval of the
Superintendent.

8.9.5    Compensation while on sabbatical leave shall be sixty percent (60%) of
the annual salary of bargaining unit member in accordance with the
provisions of the salary schedule in effect during the period of the leave.
Eligible bargaining unit members shall continue to receive paid health
and welfare benefits while on sabbatical leave. 34

equivalent to twice the period of the sabbatical leave following the

ARTICLE 17 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

171 Definitions

17.1.1    A "grievance" is a claim by one or more bargaining unit members or the
Association that there has been a misinterpretation, misapplication, or
violation of a provision of this Agreement.

17.1.2    A "day" is any day the bargaining unit member is required by this
Agreement to be on duty.

17.1.3    The "immediate supervisor" is the lowest level administrator who has
been designated to address and attempt to resolve grievances and who
has immediate jurisdiction over the grievant.

17.1.4    A "grievant" is the person or persons, including the Association, making
the claim.

17.2    Purpose

The purpose of the procedure is to attempt to secure equitable solutions to
grievances at the lowest possible level. The grievant and immediate supervisor
should attempt to resolve the grievance at the informal level.

17.3    General Provisions

17.3.1    All documents dealing with the processing of a grievance shall be filed
separately from the personnel files of the participants.

17.3.2    Forms for filing grievances, serving notices, making appeals, making
reports and recommendations, and other necessary documents shall be
prepared jointly by the District and the Association and given appropriate
distribution by the Association so as to facilitate operation of the
grievance procedure.

17.3.3    Failure of the grievant to adhere to the time deadlines shall mean that the
grievant is satisfied with the previous decision and waives the right to
further appeal. The grievant and/or the Association and the District may
extend any time deadline by mutual agreement. Absence of either the
grievant or the immediate supervisor due to illness or emergency
constitutes a basis for extension of time lines and the parties may agree
to extend timelines.

17.3.4    An effort will be made to schedule meetings for the processing of
grievances at times which will not interfere with the required work day of 61

61

the participants. If any grievance meeting or hearing is scheduled during
the school day, any employee required by either party to participate as a
witness or grievant in such meeting or hearing shall be released from
regular duties without loss of pay for a reasonable amount of time.

17.3.5    Up to the arbitration level, either party to the grievance may be
represented at any step of the grievance procedure by an individual of
that party's choice.

17.3.6    This Agreement shall not be applied in an arbitrary, discriminatory or
capricious manner.

17.3.7    Any bargaining unit member may at any time present grievances to the
District and have such grievances adjusted without the intervention of the
Association, as long as the adjustment is reached prior to arbitration and
the adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement. The
District shall not agree to a resolution of any grievance until the
Association has received a copy of the grievance and the proposed
resolution and has been given the opportunity to file a response. Upon
request of the grievant, the grievant may be represented at any stage of
the grievance procedure by a representative of the Association.

17.4 Procedure

Grievances will be processed in accordance with the following procedures:

17.4.1 Informal Meeting

17.4.1.1 Any grievant shall attempt to present the grievance orally to the
immediate supervisor within fifteen (15) days after the grievant
knew, or reasonably should have known, of the circumstances
which form the basis for the grievance. The immediate supervisor
shall meet with the bargaining unit member and attempt to resolve
the matter within ten (10) days after the presentation of the
grievance.

17 4.2 Level I - Formal Written Grievance

17.4.2.1 If the grievance is not settled during the informal conference or
one is not held, the grievant shall present the grievance in writing
on the appropriate form to the immediate supervisor within ten
(10) days after the oral decision by the immediate supervisor if
one is held. In any event, within ten (10) days of the informal level
time lines, the written grievance shall include: 62

62

a.    A description of the specific grounds of the grievance,
including names, dates, and places necessary for a complete
understanding of the grievance;

b.    A listing of the provisions of this Agreement which are alleged
to have been violated;

c.    A listing of the reasons why the immediate supervisor's
proposed resolution of the problem is unacceptable;

d.    A listing of specific actions requested of the District which will
remedy the grievance.

17.4.2.2    The immediate supervisor shall communicate the decision to the
grievant in writing within ten (10) days after receiving the
grievance. If the immediate supervisor does not respond within
the time limits the grievance shall be deemed denied and the
grievant may appeal to the next level.

17.4.2.3    Within the above time limits either party may request a personal
conference.

17.4.3    Level II - Greivance Appeal

17.4.3.1    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I, the
grievant may, within ten (10) days of the receipt of the decision at
Level I, appeal the decision to the Superintendent on the agreed
form. This statement shall include a copy of the original grievance
and appeal along with a statement of the reasons for the appeal.

17.4.3.2    The Superintendent shall communicate the decision to the
grievant within ten (10) days. If the Superintendent does not
respond within the time limits provided, the grievance shall be
deemed denied and the grievant may appeal to the next Level.

17.4.4    Level III - Mediation

If a grievance is not resolved at Level II, either the Association or the
District shall refer the grievance to non-binding mediation within 10 days.

The Association and the District may mutually agree to use the interest
based process and may mutually agree to a facilitator. If either party
does not wish to use the interest process, that party shall request
appointment of a mediator by the California State Mediation and
Conciliation Office. 63

63

The mediator or facilitator shall assist the parties in the resolution of the
grievance. Referral to Level IV shall not occur until the
mediator/facilitator has released the parties from the mediation process.

17 4.5 Level IV - Binding Arbitration

17.4.5.1    If the grievant is not satisfied with the proposed resolution at Level
III, the grievant may, within ten (10) days of the receipt of the
decision, submit a request in writing to the Association for
arbitration of the dispute. Within twenty (20) days of the grievant's
receipt of the proposed resolution at Level III, the Association shall
inform the District of its intent as to whether or not the grievance
will be submitted to arbitration. The Association and the District
shall attempt to agree upon an arbitrator within five (5) days. If no
agreement can be reached, the parties shall request that the State
Conciliation Service supply a panel of five names of persons
experienced in hearing grievances in public schools. Each party
shall alternately strike a name until only one name remains. The
remaining panel member shall be the arbitrator. The order of the
striking shall be determined by lot. Nothing shall prevent the
parties by mutual agreement from requesting an additional list of
prospective arbitrators.

17.4.5.2    The arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear evidence and
render a decision on the issue or issues submitted to him/her
including the arbitrability of the grievance. If the parties cannot
agree upon a submission agreement, the arbitrator shall
determine the issues.

17 4.5.3 The District and the Association agree that the jurisdiction and
authority of the arbitrator so selected and the opinions the
arbitrator expresses will be confined to the interpretation of the
specific provision or provisions of this Agreement at issue between
the parties. The arbitrator shall have no authority to add to,
subtract from, amend, or modify any provisions of this Agreement.
The arbitrator shall be without power or authority to make any
decision that requires the District or the Association to do an act
prohibited by law.

17.4.5.4 After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to
make written arguments, the arbitrator shall submit, in writing to all
parties, his/her findings and award. If the parties mutually agree
to expedited arbitration the written brief shall be waived.

17 4.5.5 The award of the arbitrator shall be final and binding. 64
