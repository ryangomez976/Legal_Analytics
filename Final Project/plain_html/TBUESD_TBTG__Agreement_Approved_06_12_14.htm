ARTICLE I:    AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
(Agreement) by and between the Governing Board of the Terra Bella Union School
District (Board) and the Terra Bella Teacher’s Group (Association).

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code, Act.

1.3    This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2014 through June 30,
2017 with the following provisions:

1.3.1    Article XVIII (Employee Benefits) and Article XXII (Salaries) shall be opened for
negotiation each fiscal year the Agreement is in effect.

1.3.2    Items pertaining to Year-Round Education (YRE) shall be subject to negotiations
at anytime during the duration of this Agreement.

1.3.3    During each fiscal year, each party to this Agreement shall have the option of
opening up to five (5) Articles for re-negotiation.

1.4    The parties agree that it is to their mutual benefit to encourage the resolution of
differences through the processes provided by this Agreement. Therefore, it is agreed
that the Association and the District will support this Agreement for its term, and will not
appear before any public body or bodies to seek change in the existing agreement of the
District and the Association.

1.5    This Agreement shall supersede any rules, regulations, or practices of the District which
are, or may be in the future, contrary to or inconsistent with its terms.

1.6    The provisions of the Agreement shall not be interpreted or applied in a manner which is
arbitrary, capricious, or discriminatory. Rules which are designed to implement this
Agreement shall be uniform in application and effect.

ARTICLE II:    RECOGNITION

2.1    The Board recognizes Terra Bella Teacher’s Group (T.B.T.G.) as the exclusive
representative of all certificated employees of the Board, excluding the Superintendent,
Project Director, day-to-day substitutes, Principal and Assistant Principals for the
purposes of meeting and negotiating.

2.2    For the purposes of this agreement the terms Terra Bella Teacher’s Group, T.B.T.G.,
Bargaining Unit, Unit, or Association shall be considered synonymous.

ARTICLE III:    DEFINITIONS

3.1    “Teacher” refers to any employee who is included in the Association as defined in Article
II and therefore covered by the terms and provisions of this Agreement. Terms such as
unit member(s), bargaining unit member(s), association member(s), member(s) of the
association, orteacher(s) shall be considered synonymous.

3.2    “Negotiate in good faith” means a serious and honest effort on the part of each party to
reach agreement, including the duty on the part of each party to provide the other with all
information, records, data, worksheets and budgetary materials which are available to the
public and are also relevant to the negotiations of negotiable items, and also the duty to
meet and negotiate as provided by Section 3543.7 of the Government Code.

3.3    “Days” mean school days during which students are required to be in attendance and/or
District receives A.D.A.

3.4    “School day” means the amount of time in a regularly scheduled student attendance day
during which students are required to be in school, unless otherwise provided for in this
Agreement.

3.5    “Immediate family” shall include:

3.5.1    the spouse, mother, father, grandmother, grandfather, or grandchild of the
employee;

3.5.2    the mother, father, grandmother, grandfather, or grandchild of the employee’s
spouse;

3.5.3    the employee’s or employee’s spouse’s son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-
law, brother, sister, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, step son, step daughter, aunt, uncle
nieces, nephews, foster child; and/or

3.5.4    any person living in the immediate household of the employee.

3.6    “Daily Rate of Pay” means the teacher’s annual salary as determined by the Certificated
Salary Schedule and divided by the number of days specified in that teacher’s contract.

3.7    The terms “District” and “Board” as utilized in this Agreement shall be deemed to be
synonymous, and shall, as appropriate, include the District as an entity, the Board of
Trustees, the Superintendent, and all other members of District Management.

3.8    “Duty Day” means days when teachers are required to be working regardless of whether
or not students are required to be in school.

3.9    “Hourly Rate of Pay” is the daily rate of pay divided by 7 14 hours for traditional and 7 %
hours for YRE.

3.10    “Seniority” shall be defined as the unit member’s date of hire by the District, calculating
only consecutive years of service until the present, with the following provisions:

3.10.1    Unit members with the same date of hire shall have their seniority date
determined by lot.

3.10.2 The lottery shall be conducted in the presence of at least two (2) association
representatives. Once the lottery is used to determine the unit member’s
seniority, that seniority position shall remain in effect for the unit member while
employed in the district.

3.10.3    A unit member on an approved leave of absence shall continue to maintain
seniority number while on such leave.

3.10.4    A unit member’s seniority shall accrue during layoff.

3.10.5    When all other factors are for all intents equal, seniority shall be the determining
factor in granting all transfers, assignments and reassignments, unless otherwise
specified in this contract.

3.11    A “minimum day” is defined as any day in which students are in attendance for the
minimum number of minutes required by the State Board of Education.

3.12    “YRE” refers to Year Round Education.

3.13    In reference to 29.2, “Required Attendance Meetings” are those meetings that go beyond
the time outlined in Article 29.1 in the Bargaining Unit Agreement.

3.14    A Master Teacher shall be any unit member who has been assigned a teacher trainee
and who is responsible for evaluation of that trainee.

3.15    An Emergency is an unexpected situation that requires prompt action.

3.16.1 Full-Time-Equivalent (FTE) for the purposes of this agreement shall be the same as one
(1) full-time teaching assignment described in Article 25 Section 1 and Article 29 Section
1 of this agreement.

3.17 For the purposes of this agreement, Date-of-Hire shall be the most recent date that the
employee was hired.

ARTICLE IV:    NON-DISCRIMINATION

4.1    The Board shall conform with all current laws regarding discrimination toward any unit
member.

4.2    Teacher application forms and oral interview procedures shall not refer to membership in
or preferences for employee organizations.

ARTICLE V:    NEGOTIATION    PROCEDURES

5.1    Not later than April 1, of the calendar year in which this Agreement expires, the Board
and Association shall meet and negotiate in good faith on negotiable items.

5.2    Either party may utilize the services of outside consultants to assist in the negotiations.

5.3    The Board and the Association may discharge their respective duties by means of
authorized officers, individuals, representatives, consultants, or committees.

5.4    Negotiations shall take place at mutually agreeable times and places provided that
meetings shall be held within five (5) working days from receipt of a written request by
either party unless extenuating circumstances exist to which both parties have agreed.

5.5    The Board shall furnish the Association with copies of all county and State-required
reports as soon as they are transmitted to the County or State. In addition the Board
shall furnish copies of all budgetary and other public information it produces that are
necessary for the Association to fulfill its role as the exclusive bargaining representative.

5.6    The Board shall furnish the Association with the placement of personnel on the
certificated salary schedule upon request.

5.7    The Association shall designate not more than six (6) representatives who shall be
entitled to the use of a reasonable amount of time during which said persons shall be
released from their normal duties in the District without loss of compensation to meet
and/or negotiate. The Association’s representatives may be allowed up to three (3)
release days annually for Association business or negotiation preparations.

ARTICLE VI:    EMPLOYEE    RIGHTS

6.1 The parties mutually recognize that rights of all employees covered herein to join and
participate in the lawful activities of the Association or other employee organizations of
their own choosing. No employee shall be interfered with, intimidated, restrained,
coerced or discriminated against because of the exercise of these rights. The District
agrees that it will not persuade or dissuade nor attempt to persuade or dissuade an
employee in regards to joining or participating in any activities of an employee
organization.

ARTICLE VII: ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

7.1    Representatives of the Association shall have the right to utilize District equipment and
facilities for conducting meetings with District employees.

7.2    The District shall provide a bulletin board in an area frequented by teachers at each work
site in the District for use by the Association for purposes of communicating with
bargaining unit members.

7.3    Existing District mailbox facilities may be utilized by the Association for communication
with members or with other employees of the District.

7.4    Names, addresses and telephone numbers of all District teachers shall be provided to the
Association upon request. Unit members who do not wish to have this information made
available should notify the District in writing.

7.5    The District will provide the Association with the complete Board Agenda, minutes, and
all other public documents to be considered by the Board in its’ meetings at the time they
are made available to the Board.

7.6    The Association President and/or designee shall be provided release time not to exceed
a total of five (5) days per year at no loss of compensation or benefits to conduct
Association business including meetings, workshops or conferences. The District shall
not be responsible for any expenses incurred with such conference attendance.

7.7    The time immediately following the regular duty day on the first Wednesday and the third
Tuesday of each month shall be reserved exclusively for association business. No
District meetings shall be scheduled during this time.

ARTICLE VIII: DISTRICT’S RIGHTS

8.1 The District retains, solely and exclusively, all rights, power, and authority exercised or
enjoyed prior to execution of this Agreement, and/or as mandated or recommended by
law, except as such rights, powers and authority are explicitly abridged, modified or
otherwise addressed in this Agreement.

ARTICLE IX:    MAINTENANCE    OF    BENEFITS

9.1 The Board shall not reduce or eliminate any teacher benefit within the scope of
representation as enumerated in 10.7 of the Government Code, Section 3543.2, without
mutual consent of the Board and Association.

ARTICLE X:    ACADEMIC    FREEDOM

10.1    It is recognized that teachers must be free to think and express ideas, free to select and
employ materials and methods of instruction, free from undue pressures of authority, and
free to act within their own professional group. Teachers shall use such freedom
judiciously and prudently to the end that it promotes the free exercise of intellectual
discussion and student learning.

10.2    The District and State Framework will serve as a curricular guide for academic
instruction. The Board recognizes that some deviation from the approved course of study
is necessary in the free exchange of ideas in the classroom. However, the Board
specifies for the guidance of the Superintendent and the staff that discussions in the
classrooms shall:

10.2.1    Be related to the instructional goals of the course of study and level of maturity of
the student.

10.2.2    Encourage fair presentation and open-mindedness.

10.2.3    Be conducted in a spirit of scholarly inquiry.

10.2.4    Be initiated by curricular design or by the students themselves.

10.2.5    Draw upon information and insights from the widest feasible range of resources.

ARTICLE XI:    GRIEVANCE    PROCEDURE

11.1    DEFINITIONS

11.1.1    A “grievance” shall mean an alleged violation, misapplication or misinterpretation of

provisions of this Agreement.

11.1.2    An “aggrieved person(s)” is a unit member including the Association or
representative thereof making the claim.

11.1.3    A “party in interest” is any person who might be required to take action or against
whom action might be taken in order to resolve the claim.

11.1.4    “Day(s)” for grievance resolution purposes shall mean any day on which the
central administrative office of the District is open for business.

11.2    PURPOSE

11.2.1    The purpose of this procedure is to secure at the lowest possible administrative
level equitable solutions to the problems, which may from time to time arise
affecting the welfare or working conditions of teachers. Both parties agree that
these proceedings will be kept as informal and confidential as may be
appropriate at any level of the procedure.

11.2.2    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the
time limit(s) specified at each level shall be considered to be maximum(s) and
every effort should be made to expedite the process. The time limits may be
extended by written agreement.

11.3    INFORMAL PROCEDURE

11.3.1    An aggrieved person shall seek a solution(s) to his/her problem(s) through at
least one (1) informal discussion with his/her immediate supervisor prior to
initiating a formalized grievance through the procedure hereinafter described.
Adjustment of a grievance through such an informal discussion may be
accomplished without the intervention of the Association. However, the
aggrieved person may at his/her discretion be represented by the Association at
any level of this procedure.

11.3.2    Such discussion shall take place within thirty (30) days from the time the
aggrieved person learned of the event or matters, which gave rise to the
grievance.

11.4    FORMAL PROCEDURE

11.4.1    LEVEL 1. An aggrieved person will first present his/her grievance in writing
to the appropriate principal or immediate supervisor either directly or through the
Association’s designated grievance representative within ten (10) days of the
informal discussion with his/her immediate supervisor. This grievance statement
shall be clear and concise, setting forth the circumstances involved, the decisions
rendered at the informal level and specific remedy sought. The principal or
immediate supervisor will communicate his/her written decision on the matter to
the aggrieved person within ten (10) days after grievance statement.

11.4.2    LEVEL 2. If the aggrieved person is not satisfied with the disposition of the
grievance at Level 1., or if no written decision has been rendered within the
indicated ten (10) day period, he/she may forward the grievance in writing to the

Superintendent. The written appeal statement shall include a copy of the original
grievance, the decision rendered at the Levell., and a clear, concise statement
of the reasons for the appeal. This must be done within ten (10) days after the
Levell., decision has been received or the ten (10) day period for such a
decision has expired.

11.4.2.1    Within    ten    (10)    days    after    he    has received the written grievance,

the Superintendent or his designee will meet with the aggrieved
person. Within ten (10) days after such a meeting, the
Superintendent or his designee shall communicate a written
decision on the matter to the aggrieved person.

11.4.3    LEVEL 3. If the aggrieved party is not satisfied with the disposition of
his/her grievance at Level 2., or if no written decision has been rendered by the
Superintendent and his/her designee within the indicated ten (10) day period,
he/she may forward the grievance in writing to the Board of Trustees. The
written appeal statement shall include a copy of the original grievance, the
decisions rendered at Level 1. and 2., and a clear, concise statement of the
reasons for the appeal. This may be done at any time not to exceed twenty (20)
days after the Level 2. decision has been received, or the ten (10) day period for
such a decision has expired.

11.4.3.1    Within    twenty    (20)    days    after it has received the written

grievance, the Board of Trustees will meet with the aggrieved
person. Within ten (10) days after such a meeting, the Board of
Trustee’s or its designee shall communicate a written decision of
the matter to the aggrieved person.

11.4.4    LEVEL 4. If the aggrieved party is not satisfied with the disposition of
his/her grievance at Level 3., or if no written decision has been rendered by the
Board of Trustees within the indicated ten (10) day period, he/she may request in
writing that the Association submit the grievance to arbitration. This may be
done at any time not to exceed twenty (20) days after the Level 3. decision has
been received, or the ten (10) day period for such a decision has expired.

11.4.4.1    The    Association    shall    retain full and complete authority to

determine whether or not a grievance shall be forwarded for
arbitration. In the event that the grievance is to receive further
consideration the Association, by written notice to the
Superintendent within ten (10) days after receipt of the request
from the aggrieved person, shall submit the grievance to
arbitration.

11.4.4.2    The    Association    and    the    District shall attempt to agree upon an

arbitrator. If no agreement can be reached, the parties shall
request the California State Conciliation Service, or, if they are
unavailable, the American Arbitration Association, to supply a
panel of five names of persons experienced in hearing
grievances in public schools. Each party shall alternately strike a
name until only one name remains. The remaining panel
member shall be the arbitrator. The order of striking shall be
determined by lot. The parties and arbitrator will then be bound
by the rules of the arbitration service used.

11.4.4.3    The    fees    and    expenses    of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be

borne by the District.

11.4.4.4    The arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear evidence and
render a decision on the issue or issues that were submitted to
him/her.

11.4.4.5    If    the    parties    cannot    agree    upon    a    summary    of    the    issues, the

arbitrator shall determine the issues by referring to the written
grievance and the answers thereto at each level. In disputed
cases regarding whether or not a grievance claim is within the
scope of these proceedings, the arbitrator shall rule on whether
the issue is within the scope of arbitration.

11.4.4.6    The arbitrator will be without power or authority to add to,
subtract from, or modify the terms of the Agreement. The
arbitrator will be without power or authority to make any decision
which requires the commission of an act prohibited by law or
which is a violation of the terms of this Agreement. However, it
is agreed that the arbitrator is empowered to include in any
award such financial reimbursement or other remedies as he/she
judges to be proper.

11.4.4.7    After    a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to

make written arguments, the arbitrator shall, within thirty (30)
calendar days, submit to all parties his/her written findings of
fact, reasoning and conclusions on the issue(s) submitted. Upon
receipt of the arbitrator’s recommendation for resolution, the
Board shall within ten (10) days notify the grievant in writing of
the Board’s acceptance or rejection of the arbitrator’s
recommendation of the grievance. Should no decision to the
contrary be rendered to the grievant within the specified time, the
decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding upon all
parties.

11.5 MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

11.5.1    The filing of a grievance shall not reflect unfavorably on the grievant.

11.5.2    If an aggrieved person chooses not to be represented by the Association, the
District shall provide the Association with a copy of the proposed grievance
resolution and the Association shall be given an opportunity to respond.

11.5.3    The aggrieved person and appropriate administrator shall have the right to
include in a grievance meeting such witness(es) as each deems necessary to
develop facts pertinent to the grievance. Names and witnesses to be utilized
shall be made available twenty-four (24) hours in advance of the meeting.

11.5.4    All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a
grievance will be filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in
personnel files of any of the participants. Documents utilized in this grievance
procedure which emanate from a personnel file shall be returned to such file
without indication they were utilized in the procedure.

ARTICLE XII: COMPLAINTS OR CHARGES AGAINST TEACHERS

12.1    In order to promote fair and constructive communication, the following procedures shall
govern the resolution of complaints. Every effort should be made to resolve a complaint
at the earliest possible stage.

12.2    Complaints concerning school personnel should be made directly by the complainant to
the person against whom the complaint is lodged. Parents / guardians are encouraged to
attempt to orally resolve concerns with the staff member personally. Either party may
request the presence of a principal or supervisor during this attempt to orally resolve the
complaint.

12.2.1    The unit member may request the TBTG President or designee be present
during such meeting(s).

12.3    If the complaint is not resolved at this level, the complainant may submit the complaint in
writing to the school principal or immediate supervisor. When necessary, district
administration shall assist in the preparation of the written complaint so as to meet the
requirement of this regulation. The administrative staff shall inform the complainant that
such assistance is available if he / she is unable to prepare the written complaint without
help.

12.3.1    A written complaint must include the name of each employee involved and a brief
but specific summary of the complaint and the facts surrounding it. It must also
include a specific description of a prior attempt to discuss the complaint with the
employee involved and the failure to resolve the matter.

12.4    The principal or immediate supervisor is responsible for investigating complaints and will
attempt to resolve the complaint to the satisfaction of the person(s) involved. If the
complaint is resolved, the principal will so advise all concerned parties, including the
Superintendent or designee.

12.5    If the complaint remains unresolved after review by the principal or the immediate
supervisor, the principal shall refer the written complaint, together with a report and
analysis of the situation, to the Superintendent or designee.

12.6    Should the Superintendent or designee decide in favor of the complainant, the employee
has the right to prepare a written rebuttal which shall be attached to the complaint and
placed in his / her personnel file.

12.7    Should the Superintendent or designee decide in favor of the employee and determine
that the complainant’s allegations are untrue or unfounded and the complaint is not
forwarded to the Board, the employee shall have the right to have the written complaint
and all associated documents destroyed. The District shall not take any adverse action
against an employee for an unverified allegation.

12.8    The complainant, the employee, or the Superintendent may ask to address the
Governing Board regarding the complaint.

12.8.1    All parties to a complaint, including the school administration, may be asked to
attend a Board meeting or part of such meeting for the purpose of presenting all
available evidence and allowing every opportunity for explaining and clarifying
the issue. Each party may be accompanied by a representative who may assist
in presenting evidence or clarifying the issue.

12.8.2    Complaints concerning an employee shall be addressed in a closed session of
the Board unless the employee requests that the issue be addressed in open
session.

12.9    The decision of the Board following the hearing shall be final.

ARTICLE XIII: LEAVES

13.1    SICK LEAVE

Every teacher shall be entitled to ten (10) days of sick leave per school year.

13.1.1    At the beginning of each school year every teacher shall receive a sick leave
allotment credit, equal to his sick leave entitlement for the school year. A teacher
may use his / her credited sick leave any time during the school year.

13.1.2    Unused sick leave shall accrue from school year to school year.

13.1.3    The Board shall provide each teacher with a written statement of 1) his/her
accrued sick leave total, 2) his/her sick leave entitlement for the school year, and
3) the number of NQ (see article 13.7.1) days available for that year. Such
statement shall be provided by August 1.

13.1.4    The Board shall not require a physician’s verification of illness until a teacher has
been on sick leave for five (5) or more consecutive days.

13.2    EXTENDED ILLNESS LEAVE

13.2.1    Any teacher who must be absent from his/her assignment because of illness or
accident shall be entitled to all accumulated sick leave in his/ her account. After
the accumulated sick leave has been used, a sum, which is equal to the amount
actually paid to his/her substitute, shall be deducted from his/her normal monthly
salary.

13.2.2    Teachers on extended illness leave shall continue to be provided with the full
range of regular employee benefits as provided in this contract.

13.2.3    Time spent on extended illness leave shall be accepted by the District for
purposes of allowing a teacher’s advancement on the salary schedule, and for
the achievement of tenure, provided he/she has served fifty (50) percent or more
of that school year.

13.2.4    Persons utilizing these extended illness leave provisions may return to a
substantially equivalent position to the one previously held upon notification to
their immediate supervisors of their intent to return.

13.2.5    Persons who return to active employment after having used the extended illness
leave provisions of this contract shall be fully entitled to subsequent use of these
provisions.

13.3    MATERNITY LEAVE

13.3.1    In conformance with California Education Code Section 44977 and 44978, any
teacher who is pregnant shall be entitled to the use of her accumulated sick
leave for maternity purposes.

13.3.2    Teachers as described above shall have the option of either using or not using
her accumulated sick leave during the period of maternity leave.

13.3.3    The length of the maternity leave period, including the date on which it shall
commence and the date on which it shall end, shall be determined by the
employee’s physician.

13.3.4    Upon request, a male employee, may use accumulated sick leave for the
purpose of his spouse’s childbearing / rearing.

13.3.5 Guidelines described in Appendix D shall be followed to ensure that the quality of
the instructional program is not diminished.

13.4 PARENTAL LEAVE OF ABSENCE

A parental leave of absence with differential pay (a sum, which is equal to the amount
actually paid to his/her substitute, shall be deducted from his/her normal monthly salary)
may be granted to a teacher for a time period mutually agreed to by the teacher and the
District for the purpose of childbearing/childrearing. Should a teacher request such
leave, they will be required to prepare general lesson plans and will be responsible for
the grading of students for the duration of the leave.

13.4.1    A teacher who is pregnant shall be entitled upon request to a parental leave of
absence which may begin at any time between the commencement of her
pregnancy and one (1) year after a child is born. Said teacher shall notify the
Superintendent in writing of her desire to take such leave, and, except in case of
emergency, shall give such notice at least thirty (30) days prior to the date on
which her leave is to begin. She shall include with such notice either a
physician’s statement certifying her pregnancy or a copy of the birth certificate of
her child, whichever is applicable. A teacher who is pregnant may continue in
active employment throughout her pregnancy as long as she is able.

13.4.2    Any teacher may be entitled, upon request, up to a one (1) year parental leave of
absence to begin within two (2) years after the birth of his / her child, or within
one (1) year after receiving custody of any child, or prior to receiving such
custody, if necessary, in order to fulfill the requirements for adoption.

13.4.3    A maximum one (1) year extension of regular parental leave of absence may be
secured provided that request for such extension is received by the District
Superintendent no later than forty-five (45) working days prior to the expiration of
the original leave.

13.4.4    A maximum of two (2) consecutive years absence per teacher will be allowed for
maternity and parental leave purposes.

13.4.5    At the end of the agreed parental leave of absence, the employee shall be
assigned to a position substantially equivalent to the one previously held. If an
employee wishes to return to active employment prior to the agreed terms of the
leave, this maybe done by mutual agreement of the employee and District.

13.4.6    If a teacher on parental leave notifies the District of his / her desire to return to
active employment after he / she has been on such leave for more than one (1)
year, said teacher shall be assigned to the first available vacant position for
which he / she is qualified. If more than one (1) teacher has given notice of his /
her desire to return to active employment from a District approved leave, the
teacher who gave notice at the earliest date shall be assigned to the position in
question. In any case, the teacher shall be assigned to an appropriate regular
teaching position at the beginning of the next school year after he / she indicates

ARTICLE XV: TRANSFERS/JOB SHARING/YEAR-ROUND EDUCATION (YRE)

15.1    A transfer is any District action which results in the movement of a teacher from the
position he / she held immediately preceding such action.

15.1.1    Transfers may be teacher initiated (voluntary) or District initiated (involuntary).

15.1.2    YRE ASSIGNMENTS Unit members shall choose their grade/subject level and
track assignment by seniority with the District with the exception that a member may not
displace a grade level position held by another member. Unit members must have the
proper credential to teach the assignment they have chosen.

15.1.3    The District shall assign the children of the YRE unit members to the same track
that the unit member is teaching, if the unit member so desires.

15.1.4    JOB SHARING Job Sharing shall refer to two (2) unit members on regular
contracts sharing one (1) teaching assignment.

15.1.4.1    Two unit members may share an assignment for a period to be
determined by the District and the unit members.

15.1.4.2    Applications    for    a    job    sharing    assignment    for    the    following    school

year shall be filed with the District not later than March 1.

15.1.4.3    Applications    may    not    be    denied except for just cause. The

Board shall approve or deny the request and notify the
applicants, in writing, of its decision by May 1. If the application
is denied, the applicant shall be notified, in writing, of the
reasons for denial.

15.1.4.4    Notwithstanding    other    provisions    of    this    agreement,    job    sharing

unit members’ wages, benefits, and paid leaves shall be prorated
relative to the actual time worked, including preparation times. In
no event shall the total amount of health and welfare benefits for
the job sharers exceed the amount the District would have paid if
the position had not been shared.

15.1.4.5    Each job-sharing member shall receive salary schedule
increment.

15.1.4.6    Upon request of the two unit members by March 1, and
concurrence by the District, a job-sharing assignment may be
renewed. In the event the unit members fail to notify the District
of their desire to continue the Job-sharing assignment, or in the
event the District does not approve the continuance of the
assignment, the unit members shall be returned to full-time
assignments.

15.1.4.7    If a unit member on a regular contract is in a job-sharing
assignment and elects to return to full-time teaching, the unit
member will be assigned to the first available full-time teaching
position for which he / she is certificated. This shall be
considered a voluntary transfer, and the provisions of that
section of this agreement shall apply.

15.2    POSTING OF VACANCIES/VOLUNTARY TRANSFERS

15.2.1 The administration shall, within a reasonable time after becoming aware of a

vacancy in any certificated staff position, post a notice of said vacancy on an

openly accessible bulletin board in the District office and at each work site where
unit members are currently on duty. Said notices shall remain posted for not less
than ten (10) working days prior to the closing date for applications.

15.2.2    Certificated positions may be posted concurrently outside of the District. First
consideration for any position will be given to bargaining unit members who have
the credentials and experience to fill the position.

15.2.2.1    Openings    that    occur    within ten (10) days of the first teacher

workday will be announced immediately to all bargaining unit
members. On the first teacher workday, the District will convene
a meeting for the purpose of filling any and all certificated
openings within the District. Temporary/substitute employees
will fill positions that come open after the first workday.
Bargaining unit members will have the opportunity to request
transfers for these positions by the following March 15th.

15.2.3    Vacancies that occur during the summer shall be posted at the District office
YRE schools, and summer schools. The announcements of vacancies shall be
sent to employees who are on leave, vacation, and who have requested in
writing such notice, and to employees who have applied for a comparable
position (i.e., school, grade and/or subject matter within the twelve (12) months
preceding the posting date.

15.2.4 A request for a transfer shall be maintained in an active file for twelve (12)
months following receipt of the request. For that twelve month period, the
administration shall consider the request in connection with subsequent posted
vacancies for positions comparable (i.e., school, grade and/or subject matter) to
those listed in the request for transfer or reassignment.

15.2.5    Unit members who desire to transfer shall file a transfer application with the
superintendent, personnel office. Transfers shall be addressed to specific
vacancies. All transfer applications shall be considered.

15.2.6    The following criteria shall be used in consideration of transfer requests:

15.2.6.1    Legal requirements of the District.

15.2.6.2    Individual training, experience, credentials.

15.2.6.3    Skills in areas which are secondary to the regular assignment.

15.2.6.4    Balance of staff relative to training, experience and background.

15.2.6.5    Educational    needs    of    the school or department to which the unit

member is transferred.

15.2.6.6    If the above factors are for all intents equal between two (2) or
more candidates for a position, the candidate with the greatest
seniority in the District shall be appointed to fill the vacancy.

15.2.6.7    Vacancies shall normally be filled within ten (10) working days

after the closing date for application. Each applicant shall be
informed in writing as to the disposition of his/her application
relative to the position under consideration. Upon request the
applicant will receive written explanation for denial.

15.2.7 Unit members who have voluntarily changed assignment during the school year
and would be subject to loss of annual work days shall be provided an
opportunity to substitute teach sufficient days to fulfill the annual contract
obligation and thereby receive a full year of service for retirement purposes. If a
change of tracks results in a longer school year, the unit member shall be paid
per diem for all days worked over the normal contract year as stipulated for YRE.

15.3 INVOLUNTARY TRANSFERS/REASSIGNMENTS

15.3.1    Involuntary transfers shall not be initiated by the District except for the good and
sufficient reason based upon the educational needs of the District.

15.3.2    A vacancy shall not normally be filled by an involuntary transfer if there is an
acceptable qualified volunteer available.

15.3.3    Selection of employees for involuntary transfer shall be based upon possession
(by the transferee) of the credential to perform the needed service; legally
required affirmative action needs of the District and possession of special training
which is in particular demand for the position in question. If the above factors are
for all intents equal between two (2) or more employees being considered for
transfer into a position, the person with the least seniority in the District shall be
appointed to fill the vacancy.

15.3.4    A meeting between the teacher and his/her immediate supervisor (principal) will
be held before a new assignment is made. At the written request of the
employee who is to be transferred, written reasons for the impending transfer
shall be given.

15.3.5    An employee who is to be involuntarily transferred shall receive written notice of
said transfer sixty (60) days prior to the first teaching day of the new assignment,
except in case of emergency.

15.3.6    Any transfer that requires the acquisition of an additional credential/certificate
must be voluntary.

15.3.7    No unit member shall be involuntarily transferred more than once in any two
consecutive school years, unless the District has experienced a sharp decline, or
increase, in enrollment the preceding year.

15.3.8    In case of emergency, any teacher involuntarily transferred shall be granted five
(5) days of paid service without teaching duties to prepare for the new
assignment.

15.3.9    If the involuntary reassignment requires a change of rooms, the site administrator
shall arrange appropriate custodial assistance for the move.

15.3.10    Unit members involuntarily transferred from one track to another shall be paid in
full according to the original anticipated annual wage. If less days are served
due to the move, the unit member shall substitute at their daily rate to obtain full
retirement service credit for the year to the extent possible. If the involuntary
transfer results in more days of service, the teacher shall be paid per diem for
those additional days.

ARTICLE XVI: CLASS SIZE

16.1    LIMITATIONS

The Board agrees to class sizes of twenty-eight (28) for first grade, twenty-nine (29) for
kindergarten, second and third grades, thirty (30) for grades four, five, six, seven, and
eight in self-contained settings.

The Board agrees to an average class load of 30 in departmentalized and team-teaching
classes in the Mathematics, English/Language Arts, Social Science and Science
departments. Average class loads for departmentalized subjects shall be calculated by
dividing the students assigned to a teacher in the above subjects by the assigned
teaching periods. Example: 150 students / 5 periods = 30 class load average.

(The District will provide for the physical care of mainstreamed severely handicapped
students should they have a physical disability requiring such additional assistance).

16.2    The District shall make every effort to maintain equality of class size in classes at any
given grade level or in any subject area on a district-wide basis. Every effort shall be
made to maintain combination classes at or below the class sizes stated in article 16.1.

16.3    Traditionally large classes such as Band, Chorus, or Physical Education shall not be
subject to the above class size limits.

16.4    If average class size is exceeded by one (1) for a period of twenty (20) days, the District
may elect to transfer students, pay the affected teacher $6.00 per pupil per day in self-
contained classes or $1.00 per pupil per period in departmentalized classes, or
reorganize a class or classes.

16.5    Payments for class size overage shall be calculated and made a soon as practicable. All
overage claims should be processed through the district’s standard timecard process.

ARTICLE XIX: CERTIFICATED EMPLOYEE EVALUATION

19.1    It is understood and agreed by the parties that the object of the evaluation process is to
maintain or improve the quality of education. To this end, agents of the District who are
fulfilling their responsibilities as “evaluators” under the provisions of this article shall
render criticism in a constructive fashion and offer positive reinforcement to teachers
being evaluated.

19.2    EVALUATION PROCEDURE

19.2.1    Every probationary certificated employee shall be evaluated by the administration
in writing at least twice each school year. The first such evaluation shall be
completed no later than December 1, and the second shall be completed no later
than April 15.

19.2.2    Every permanent certificated employee shall be evaluated by the administration
in writing every other year, no later than thirty calendar days prior to the end of
the school year, or in the case of those unit members in YRE, 30 days prior to
the end of the last trimester of the school year for their track.

19.2.3    No later than the end of the seventh school week of the year, the evaluator and
the certificated employee shall meet and mutually agree to instructional goals
and objectives for the school year, as well as other criteria upon which
evaluations of the employee are to be based.

19.2.4    During the course of the evaluation period, circumstances may arise which
require modification of the evaluation criteria. Such modifications shall be
accomplished by mutual written agreement between the evaluator and the
evaluatee.

19.2.5    The formal written evaluation of a teacher shall be based upon a sufficient
number of classroom observations of sufficient length to enable the evaluator to
form a valid opinion of the teacher’s work. The writing of the evaluation shall be
preceded by an evaluation conference in which the evaluator and the certificated
employee shall review the observation(s) and what is to be incorporated into that
evaluation report.

19.2.6    Extended observation(s) for the purpose of formal evaluation shall be arranged
by the evaluator and the evaluatee by mutual agreement in advance of the
observation(s). Short-term observations may be carried out on an unannounced
basis.

19.2.7    Any certificated employee who receives a negative evaluation shall, upon
request, be entitled to a subsequent observation, conference and written
evaluation, as prescribed above. Such entitlement shall continue after each
written evaluation until the problems cited in the evaluation are rectified, or until
the initiation of termination procedures as specified in the California Education
Code Section 44932-44950.

19.2.8    The certificated employee’s evaluator shall take affirmative action to assist the
teacher in the correction of any cited deficiencies. Such action shall include
specific recommendations. If agreeable to all parties such affirmative action shall
include release time for the certificated employee to visit and observe the classes
of teachers in the evaluatee’s field which are considered exemplary by the
evaluator.

19.2.9    If subsequent remedial action eliminates the negative evaluation and/or the
identified deficiencies, the evaluator shall place appropriate comments in the
evaluatee’s personnel file.

19.2.10    Unit members shall not be required to participate in the observation and/or
evaluation of other unit members nor shall they be required to formally assess
their own performance except by mutual agreement between the evaluator and
the evaluatee.

19.2.11    An evaluator shall base a formal evaluation of a certificated employee on
information which is collected through direct observation of such employee.

19.2.12    The evaluatee may respond, in writing, at any point during the evaluation
process. Any written response will become part of the formal written evaluation
report.

ARTICLE XXXIII: PEER ASSISTANCE AND REVIEW

33.0    PROGRAM COMPONENTS

33.1    Beginning July 1, 2000 there shall be a Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program,
hereafter referred to as “PAR Program”, for all teachers. The PAR Program provides a
mechanism whereby exemplary teachers assist other teachers in the areas of subject
matter knowledge, teaching methods, and teaching strategies. The Program shall have
four components:

33.1.1    Permanent    Teacher    Peer    Assistance    Program:    This    component    shall

provide peer assistance to permanent teachers who receive an
“unsatisfactory” evaluation in the area of teaching methods or instruction.

33.1.1.1    Teachers receiving an “unsatisfactory” evaluation in the area
of teaching methodology and/or instruction will be referred to
the PAR Program by the principal or designated evaluator
for assistance under this Program.

33.1.2    Voluntary    Teacher Peer Assistance Program: This component shall

provide assistance to teachers who volunteer for the Program.

33.1.2.1    Teachers desiring assistance in improving their teaching
practice may apply to the Program for such assistance on a
confidential basis. The PAR Panel shall have the authority to
accept or reject such referrals.

33.1.2.2    If a teacher is accepted into the PAR Program as a
volunteer, documentation will not be placed into the
personnel file only so long as participation continues on a
voluntary basis.

33.1.3    Beginning    Teacher    Coaching    Program:    This    component

shall provide peer assistance to teachers participating in Pre-Intern,
Intern, and Beginning Teacher and Support (BTSA) programs, as well as
first and second year teachers. Coaches will be determined by the
number of eligible participants. Participation in the Program shall not
create nor expand any rights to retention or permanency as set forth in
Education Code Section 44929.21 pertaining to probationary employees.

33.1.4    Ongoing    Staff    Development:    This will include in-services,

conferences, curriculum selection and development. The PAR Panel will
have input in selecting and implementing Staff Development for the
District.

33.2    The PAR program shall be governed by the PAR Panel. The PAR Panel shall be
composed of no more than five (5) members. The PAR panel membership shall
consist of one (1) administrator selected by the Superintendent and four (4)
certificated teachers chosen by the bargaining unit. A quorum for PAR Panel
meetings shall be two-thirds of the membership. Decisions shall be made by
consensus when possible. Should a vote be required, action must be taken on an
affirmative vote of the majority.

33.3    PAR Panel members will be selected to serve terms of 3 years with the following
exception. Initially, half of the PAR panel members shall serve two-year terms and
half of the PAR Panel members shall serve three-year terms. The two-year terms
shall be determined by lot. After this start-up period, each panel member shall serve
a three-year term.

33.3.1

The PAR Panel Selection Committee shall use the following
qualifications as guidance in selection of PAR Panel candidates.
Qualifications for the teacher members of the Panel should include:

♦    A Clear Teaching Credential;

♦    Five-years of recent classroom teaching;

♦    Demonstrated exemplary teaching ability as provided by Education
Code Section 44500 and reflected in the applicant’s performance
evaluation;

♦    Effective leadership skills.

33.4

If a member of the PAR Panel leaves the PAR Panel prior to the
completion of his or her term, the vacant position shall be filled for the
remainder of the term in the same manner by which the departed
member was originally chosen or designated.

33.11 Permanent Teacher Due Process Rights

33.11.1    The    permanent teacher shall be entitled to review all reports generated by the

peer coach prior to their submission to the Panel and to have affixed thereto his
or her comments. To effectuate this right, the peer coach shall provide the
permanent teacher being reviewed with copies of such reports at least five (5)
working days prior to any such meeting.

33.11.2    The    permanent teacher shall have a right to be accompanied by the Association

representative in any meetings of the Panel to which they are called, and shall be
given a reasonable opportunity to present his or her point of view concerning any
report being made.

33.11.3    The permanent teacher shall have the right to timely progress reports.

33.11.4    The permanent teacher shall have the right to present reasons why a specific
peer coach should be replaced and another coach substituted and to have those
reasons considered.

33.11.5    A teacher shall not have access to the grievance process to challenge the
content of reports, or decisions by the Panel, but may file responses, which shall
become part of the official record of the intervention.

33.11.6    This PAR Program in no manner diminishes the legal rights of bargaining unit
members.

The Terra Bella Teacher’s Group and the District agree to Year Round Schools effective

The purpose of this article is to bring together issues that are of specific concern to unit
members assigned to a multi-track Year Round Education (YRE) program. Except as
specifically noted herein, all provisions of this agreement shall apply for teachers in the
YRE program.

increasing the number of year round schools. The language in this agreement will apply

District will include the Association on any study committees formed to convert or start
year round schools in the District.

35.3.1    Unit member’s participation in year round school shall be, whenever possible,
voluntary.

administrator will mutually agree upon a schedule.

comes back on-tract, he/she returns to his/her original classroom, and the Roving
unit member moves again.

APPENDIX C

Guidelines for Leave of Absence
(Differential Pay)

It is the intent of these guidelines to ensure that the instructional program of a
class with a teacher on leave of absence (with deferential pay) will continue as if
the teacher were actually there. Communication between all parties (teacher,
substitute, administration) to this agreement is essential if the process is to be
successful.

Duration of Leave of Absence

The leave of absence request should include a clear beginning and ending date.
For example: The leave of absence shall begin (Insert Date) and will continue
through (Insert Date).

Note: The narrative should clearly state the days the teacher will work (if any)
and the days the substitute will work.

Communication

•    There should be regular (at least once per week) physical contact between
the teacher and substitute. The teacher and substitute should communicate
by telephone as necessary to meet the needs of the students.

•    There should be regular contact between the teacher and the site
administration. The teacher and administration should communicate by
telephone as necessary to meet the needs of the students.

•    The teacher is responsible for notifying in a timely manner the administration
of any situations that might alter his/her ability to fulfill obligations relative to
this leave of absence.

Lesson Planning and Scope of Curriculum

•    The teacher shall be responsible for weekly lesson planning. Further, it is
expected that the teacher provide the substitute with a general
outline/timeline of the curricula to be presented during the duration of the
leave.

•    The lesson plans should be as nearly the same as if the teacher were
conducting the class.

Grading and Record Maintenance

• The teacher shall be responsible for grading student work.

1.    The teacher should be sufficiently knowledgeable about each student
to accurately inform a parent of his/her child's progress at any time.

2.    The teacher is responsible for keeping an accurate grade book.

award grades, make promotion retention recommendations, etc.

MENTOR TEACHER AND INSTRUCTIONAL IMPROVEMENT PROGRAMS

Note: This appendix was previously Article XXXIII and has been placed here for reference.

The Peer Assistance and Review Program (PAR) has replaced the Mentor Teacher

Program.

D.1 PURPOSE

D.1.1 It is mutually recognized by the District and the Association that the primary purpose of
the Mentor Teacher Program is to encourage retention of exemplary teachers and to
provide assistance and guidance to new and experienced teachers. This is to be
accomplished by selecting mentor teachers and designating part of their time for staff
development with teacher trainees, new and experienced teachers, and for work on
curriculum development.

D.1.2 In return, these exemplary teachers will receive an annual stipend determined by
the State or a pro-rata amount thereof if the program is not fully funded by the
State. Mentors are selected on the basis of exemplary teaching.

D.2    FUNDING

D.2.1 No portion of this program shall be paid from general fund moneys. It shall be
implemented only to the extent that special funding from the State is provided.
This stipulation shall apply to the mentor stipend, release time costs,
administrative costs, and all other costs created by the mentor program. If the
funding is decreased at any time during the term of this Agreement, the program
will be decreased proportionately.

D.3    SELECTION (NOMINATION) COMMITTEE

D.3.1 The establishment of the selection committee shall conform with the rules and
regulations establishing guidelines for implementation of the California Mentor
Teacher Program as approved by the State Board of Education.

D.3.2 The selection committees will be composed of six members, four of them being
unit members, representing grade levels K-2, 3-5, 6-8, and special education
including Special Ed., music, industrial arts, etc. The selection committees shall
include, only certificated employees of the Terra Bella Union School District. The
following grade levels may recommend candidates for the Mentor Teacher
Program:

D.3.2.1 Kindergarten through grade two.

D.3.2.2 Grades three through five.

D.3.2.3 Grades six through eight.

D.3.2.4 Special Services staff.

D.3.3 The Terra Bella Teachers Group shall have the responsibility of conducting an
election with probationary and permanent unit members for the purpose of
nominating classroom teacher members to the selection committee.

D.3.4 The term of office for unit members on the selection committee shall be
determined by the Association but shall be less than one (1) year.

D.4 APPLICATION OF INDIVIDUAL TEACHERS FOR MENTOR TEACHER DESIGNATION

Any full-time classroom teacher who meets the following qualifications is eligible to seek

classification as a mentor teacher.

D.4.1 Holds a valid California teacher credential.

D.4.2 Has achieved permanent status.

D.4.3 Has taught five (5) years out of the last seven (7) years in the classroom.

D.4.4 An application shall include written consent for release of personnel information,
relating to teaching experience and performance, to selection committee
members.

D.4.5 An application shall include a written explanation of the contributions the
applicant feels he/she can make in the following areas: assisting new and
experienced teachers, curriculum development activities, or other areas.

D.4.6 Selection committee members are eligible to make application for mentor teacher
status.

D.5 REVIEW OF APPLICATIONS AND NOMINATIONS OF COMMITTEES

D.5.1 Before nominating any candidate, the selection committee may review the
applicant’s personnel information relating to his or her teaching experience and
performance.

D.5.2 The selection committee may nominate for mentor teacher classification any
applicant who meets the qualifications of Article D.4 provided the committee
determines upon review of all the information before it that the applicant has
demonstrated exemplary teaching ability including, but not limited to, effective
communication skills, subject matter knowledge, and mastery of a range of
teaching strategies necessary to meet the needs of pupils in different contexts.

D.5.3 Members of the selection committee, with prior written consent of the candidate,
may make classroom observations of the candidate, at mutually agreed upon
times, as one part of the selection process. Committee members, upon the
request of the committee, shall be given release time to participate in such
observations. Classroom observations shall be limited to no more than forty-five
(45) minutes.

D.5.4 Each nominee shall have received the vote of at least four members of the
selection committee.

D.6 REVIEW OF NOMINEES AND DESIGNATION OF MENTOR TEACHERS

D.6.1 Prior to designation of any nominee as mentor teacher, the District
Superintendent may gather such further information as deemed necessary to
evaluate the nominee.

D.6.2 The Board of Trustees may consider the appointment of any nominee to be a
mentor teacher in the same manner that they may consider the employment of
other employees.

D.6.3 The Board of Trustees shall designate as mentor teachers only teachers, who
have been nominated by the selection committee and may designate all, part, or
none of the quota of candidates authorized for funding by the State.

D.7    RENOMINATION

D.7.1 During his or her year of service as a mentor teacher, a teacher may apply for re¬
nomination.

D.8    DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF MENTOR TEACHERS

D.8.1 The duties and responsibilities of each mentor teacher shall be determined on an
individual basis, and may include:

D.8.1.1    Providing    assistance    and guidance to teacher trainees or to

temporary, probationary or permanent teachers.

D.8.1.2    Providing    staff    development    for    teachers.

D.8.1.3    Providing    special    curriculum research and services.

D.8.2 The time and manner in which each mentor teacher shall render service in the
program shall be determined on an individual basis, subject to the following:

D.8.2.1    Mentor    services    shall    be provided during regular school hours

unless other times are mutually agreed upon.

D.8.2.2    Each mentor shall spend on the average not less than    sixty

percent (60%) of his/her time in the direct instruction of pupils.

D.8.3 The mentor teacher shall have no authority over any other teacher by virtue of
his/her position as mentor, nor shall he/she participate in the evaluation of
teachers.

D.8.4    Mentor teachers shall be responsible to their regular school principal and/or

superintendent, unless otherwise assigned by the superintendent.

D.8.4.1    The    superintendent may delegate his supervisory responsibility

for mentor teachers to the Mentor Teacher Committee.

D.8.5 Mentor teachers shall not perform any administrative duties.

D.9 GENERAL PROVISIONS

D.9.1 A Mentor teacher shall not be exempt from any usual duty assignment required
of any other teacher.

D.9.2 Arrangements for release time for mentor teachers and/or for selection
committee members shall not be such as to increase class size, teacher-pupil
ratio, nor extra-duty assignments for other teachers.

D.9.3 A mentor teacher may resign his/her position as mentor at any time provided
he/she gives written notice ten (10) working days prior to leaving the position.
However, the mentor teacher shall not be entitled to any unearned stipend.

TERRA BELLA UNION SCHOOL DISTRICT’S
CODE OF CONDUCT

The Terra Bella Teacher’s Group and the Terra Bella Union School District have a shared belief that a
character framework that is based on strong values and good character will make a difference in the lives of
our teachers and especially our students.

A person who is trustworthy; treats people with respect; is responsible; is fair; is caring; is a good citizen and
a person of character.

Citizenship:

Do: play by the rules; obey laws; do your share; respect authority; stay informed;
vote; protect your neighbors; pay your taxes; be charitable; help your community
by volunteering service; protect the environment; conserve natural resources.

Trustworthiness:

Do: tell the truth; be sincere.

Don’t: betray a trust, deceive, mislead, cheat, or steal; don’t be devious or tricky.

Integrity

Do: stand up for your beliefs; be your best; walk your talk; show commitment,
courage, and self-discipline.

Don’t: do anything you think is wrong.

Promise-Keeping

Do: keep your word and honor your commitments; pay your debts and return
what you borrow.

Loyalty

Do: stand by, support, and protect your family, friends, and country.

Respect for Others:

Do: judge all people on their merits; be courteous and polite, tolerant,
appreciative and accepting of individual differences; respect the right of
individuals to make decisions about their own lives.

Don’t: abuse, demean, or mistreat anyone, don’t use, manipulate, exploit or take
advantage of others.

Responsibility:

Do: think before you act; consider the consequences on all people affected; think
for the long-term; be reliable; be accountable; accept responsibility for the
consequences of your choices; set a good example for those who look up to you.
Don’t: make excuses, blame others for your mistakes or take credit for others’
achievements.

Excellence

Do: your best and keep trying; be diligent and industrious.
Don’t: quit or give up easily.

Self-Restraint

Do: exercise self-restraint and be disciplined.

Fairness:

Do: treat all people fair, be open-minded; listen to others; try to understand what
they are saying and feeling, make decisions which affect others only after
appropriate considerations.

Don’t: take unfair advantage of others’ mistakes or take more than your fair
share.

Caring:

Do: show your care about others through kindness, caring, sharing and
compassion, live by the Golden Rule and help others.

Don’t: be selfish, mean, cruel or insensitive to others’ feelings.
