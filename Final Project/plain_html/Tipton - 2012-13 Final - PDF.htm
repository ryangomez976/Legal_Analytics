ARTICLE 3:_EMPLOYEE RIGHTS

The parties mutually recognize the rights of all employees covered hereby to join and participate
in the activities of the Association, or to have the Association represent them in their employee
relations with the District, or to refuse to join or participate in the activities of the Association. It
is further agreed that nothing herein shall prohibit such employees from representing themselves
individually, or appearing in their own behalf, in their employment relations with the District.

No employees shall be interfered with, intimidated, restrained, coerced, or discriminated against
because of the exercise of these rights.

ARTICLE 5:_GRIEVANCE    PROCEDURE

5.1    Definitions

5.1.1    A    "grievance"    is    an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or

misapplication of the express terms of this Agreement which
directly and adversely affects the grievant. Actions to challenge or
change the terms of this Agreement shall not be considered a
grievance. Matters for which a specific method of review is
provided by law or by the terms of this Agreement are not within
the scope of this procedure.

5.1.2    A    "grievant"    is    a    member or members of the representation unit

Tipton Elementary School District

4

2012-2013

covered by this Agreement who files a grievance.

5.1.3    A    "day"    is    any    day in which the District Office is open.

5.2    Step    1— Immediate Supervisor: The grievance shall be presented orally or in

writing to the Superintendent or his/her designee within ten (10) days of the
occurrence giving rise to the grievance. The Superintendent or designee shall
reply orally to an oral grievance, or in writing to a written grievance, within ten
(10) days thereafter.

5.3    Step    2— Mediation:

5.3.1    If    the    grievant    and/or the Association is not satisfied with the

disposition of the grievance, or if no disposition has occurred
pursuant to the provisions of Level One, the grievance shall be
referred to mediation.

5.3.2    The    Association    shall    request    that    a conciliator/mediator from the

California State Mediation/Conciliation Service, or from any other
mutually agreeable recognized dispute resolution center, be
assigned to assist the parties in the resolution of the grievance.

5.3.3    The    mediator    shall meet with the grievant, the Association and the

District for the purpose of resolving the grievance.

5.3.4    If    an    agreement    is reached, the agreement shall be reduced to

writing and shall be signed by the grievant, the Association and the
District. This agreement shall be nonprecedential and shall
constitute a settlement of the grievance.

5.3.5    In    the    event    that    the    grievant,    the    Association    and    the

Superintendent or his/her designee have not resolved the grievance
with the assistance of the conciliator/mediator within ten (10) days
from the first meeting held by the conciliator/mediator, the
Association may terminate Level Two and the grievance may
proceed to Level Three.

5.4    Step    Three - Board of Education: If the grievant is not satisfied with the reply in

Step 2, the grievant shall have ten (10) days after receipt thereof to submit a
written request to the Board of Trustees for a hearing. If the Board and
Association determine that the grievance is within the scope of matters subject to
this Grievance Procedure, it shall schedule within the next two (2) regularly-
scheduled meetings a hearing upon the grievance. Such hearing may be

Tipton Elementary School District

5

2012-2013

conducted by the Board as a whole, or with a quorum. The Board, after hearing
the evidence, shall issue a decision no later than its next regularly-scheduled
meeting, which shall be final and binding upon the parties.

5.5    Each    of    the    formal requirements and time limitations stated herein for the

processing of grievances shall be strictly adhered to; provided, however, that any
such time limits may be extended by the express written agreements of the parties.
If the District's authorized representative fails to answer a grievance within the
time limit specified in any step of the grievance procedure, the grievant shall have
the right to appeal the grievance immediately to the next step of the grievance
procedure. If the grievant fails to appeal the grievance to the next step of the
grievance procedure, the grievance shall be deemed waived and terminated.

5.6    All    preparation,    filing,    investigation,    processing,    and hearing of grievances shall

be conducted during the non-working time of all involved employees, and no
release time shall be provided.

5.7    All    legal    fees and costs will be shared equally by the Association and the School

District.

Sick Leave: Every full-time teacher in the District shall be entitled to ten (10)

in the immediate household of the employee.

permitted to utilize up to a maximum of two days of accumulated
and/or earned sick leave in any school year in the case of

Confidential Leave days may turn in a time sheet by the last day of school at the

ARTICLE 10:_REASSIGNMENTS

10.1    A reassignment means a change in the grade level(s) that a teacher has taught
immediately prior to the change.

10.2    Reassignments may be teacher-initiated (voluntary) or District initiated
(involuntary).

10.3    Voluntary Reassignments

10.3.1    Teachers    may initiate their own reassignments from one (1)

teaching assignment to another by submitting a written request for
such reassignment to the District Superintendent.

10.3.2    Requests    for reassignment may be submitted, in writing, at any

time during the District's regular office hours.

10.3.3    Current    employees    who    apply for existing vacancies shall not be

denied placement in such positions for reasons that are arbitrary or
capricious.

10.3.4    The    District    shall    post    notice of all known certificated vacancies

when they come to the District's attention. Such notice shall,
except in emergency circumstances, be posted on the District office
bulletin board for at least five (5) days prior to a closing date for
applications which shall be specified on the posted notice.

Tipton Elementary School District

11

2012-2013

10.3.5    A reassignment request may be submitted in response to a

particular opening or simply for purposes of receiving
consideration for vacancies as they occur.

10.4    Involuntary    Reassignments

10.4.1    Involuntary    reassignments    shall    not    be made for reasons which are

arbitrary or capricious.

10.4.2    Teachers    shall    be notified of their tentative assignments for the

coming school year by the last day of school of the current school
year.

10.4.3    Upon request, a meeting between the teacher and the Principal will
be held before a new assignment is made, at which time the teacher
will be notified as to the reasons for the proposed reassignment.

10.4.4    An involuntary reassignment shall not result in the loss of
compensation, seniority, or any fringe benefit to a teacher.

10.4.5    Any employee who is involuntarily transferred in any school year
will not be considered for an involuntary transfer during the next
two (2) years. Exceptions may be considered based on a
significant change in student population in any particular grade
level.

ARTICLE 11:_CERTIFICATED    EMPLOYEE    EVALUATIONS

11.1    Evaluation    of certificated employees shall be made on a continuous basis. At

least two summary evaluations shall be prepared each year for probationary
personnel. At least one summery evaluation shall be prepared every other year for
permanent personnel. In addition, the employee may request and receive
additional summary evaluations when unsatisfactory ratings have been received.

11.2    The    first    summary evaluation of probationary employees must be completed no

later than December first (1), and the second no later than May first (1).

11.3    The    summary    evaluation    of permanent personnel shall be completed no later than

30 days prior to the last day of school.

11.4    The    summary    evaluation    of permanent and probationary employees shall consist

of at least two formal thirty (30) minute observations, as well as, walk through
observations during the year.

Tipton Elementary School District

2012-2013

11.5    One of the classroom observations shall be made known to the employee at least
two (2) days prior to the occurrence and shall be followed by a conference with
written feedback within seven (7) days of the observation.

11.6    The evaluatee shall have the right to initiate a written reaction or response to
his/her observation reports and evaluations. Such written statements shall be
dated and shall become a permanent attachment to the teacher's file.

11.7    The certificated employee and the evaluator shall take positive action to correct
any cited deficiencies. Such actions shall include specific recommendations.

11.8    Probationary employees shall be kept informed of their status throughout the
probationary period.

11.9    No evaluations shall be based on information recorded by a mechanical or an
electronic device in the classroom or transmitted from the classroom without the
knowledge and consent of the teacher.

11.10    Any employee receiving unsatisfactory ratings on a summary evaluation will be
evaluated again the following school year.

A. Rules for Initial Placement and Advancement on Salary Schedule

1.    Classification Requirements

Class I    -    Bachelors Degree plus

24 semester units

Class II - Bachelors Degree plus 36 semester units
Class HI    -    Bachelors Degree plus    48 semester units

Class IV    -    Bachelors Degree plus    60 semester units

Class V    -    Bachelors Degree plus    72 semester units

2.    Newly employed teachers shall be given credit on a year-for-year basis at the time
of initial placement on the salary schedule for previous teaching experience.
Teaching experience for salary schedule placement purposes shall include all
experience in positions requiring certification qualifications. No more than nine

(9) years of credit for experience from outside the District will be granted, thus
maximum experience placement will be on Step 10. Each year following the
initial year of employment in Tipton Elementary School District, the new
employee will advance a double step until the teacher’s years of experience and
the Salary Schedule Steps coincide.

3.    Teachers shall be placed on the appropriate class of the Salary Schedule in
accordance with the degrees and advanced preparation they have completed.

4.    No credit will be given for upper division units taken before the Baccalaureate
Degree is granted, except when such units have been accrued during the semester
of graduation.

5.    Upon initial employment, salary payments may be delayed until all verifications
are completed and on file.

6.    Lower division courses earned after employment may be accepted as units beyond
the degree for purposes of advancing on the District salary schedule when the
attainment of such units can be demonstrated as having the potential for
improving the employee's capability to meet the needs of his/her particular
assignments, providing prior approval is received.

7.    Except when specifically approved by the Board of Trustees, teachers shall not be
permitted to advance more than one column per year on the District salary
schedule.

8.    Advancement on the salary schedule shall be at the rate of one step for each year
of teaching experience. If a teacher is employed for at least seventy-five percent
(75%) of the school year, he/she shall be given salary schedule advancement.

9.    Teachers contemplating a change of classification because of additional work
during the summer should file, in the Superintendent's office, notice of intention
to do such work by June 1, and must submit a certification of completion by
September 10 in order to qualify for the new classification, at which time the
teacher's individual employment will be adjusted and reissued to comply with the
previously planned completion of said work.

10.    A teacher must receive a grade of "C" or better, "Pass", or "Credit" in any college
course while attempting to qualify for a higher classification, or that course shall
not be counted for classification credit.

11.    Unit Conversion - Quarter units are converted to semester units by multiplying the
quarter units by two-thirds (2/3).

12.    The District shall provide each teacher, by May 1 of each school year, a statement
of the number of units that the District has on file for him/her.

13.    In-service and travel experience may be utilized to earn credit for advancement on
the salary schedule provided that the following criteria are met:

a.    No more than two (2) such units shall be earned for such application in
any four (4) year period.

b.    A minimum of fifteen (15) hours of direct involvement in the in-service or
travel activity shall be required in order to qualify for a single unit for
application towards schedule advancement.

work for advancement on the salary schedule must be completed in
duplicate and returned to the Superintendent prior to beginning any such
proposed project. In the case of summer travel applications, they must be
submitted prior to May 15 if credit for summer travel experience is
contemplated. The Governing Board shall have sole discretion in granting
such credit.
