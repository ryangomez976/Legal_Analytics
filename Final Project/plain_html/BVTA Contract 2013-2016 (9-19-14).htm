ARTICLE I: AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a binding agreement ("Agreement") by
and between the Governing Board of the Bennett Valley Union School District ("Board") and the
Bennett Valley Teachers Association/CTA/NEA ("Association"), an employee organization.

1.2    This agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the Governing
Code ("Act").

1.3    This agreement shall remain in full force and effect upon ratification by the Board and the
Association until June 30, 2016. Salary and benefits will be reopened annually. The interest
based bargaining team will meet outside of regularly scheduled bargaining sessions by mutual
agreement.

1.4    Bankruptcy Prevention Plan - Financial solvency is important to all of us in the district. It is a
goal of both the Board and the Association that the district budget meet State and County
guidelines for reserve designated for economic uncertainty. Should the district's reserve
designated for economic uncertainty be in danger of not meeting these guidelines, the Interest
Based Bargaining Team will reconvene to brainstorm and recommend a course of action.

ARTICLE II: RECOGNITION

2.1 The Board recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all certificated
employees of the Board (permanent, probationary and temporary employees) for the
purposes of meeting and negotiating. Home teachers, summer school teachers, day-to-day
substitute teachers, substitute teachers relieving a teacher on paid leave, day care teachers,
supervisory employees, confidential employees, consultant employees, management employees,
and emergency employees are excluded.

ARTICLE III: GRIEVANCES

3.1    Purpose: A grievance procedure is a necessary, useful, and constructive means by which to
clarify language in the Contract. Whenever possible, all grievances will be resolved informally
or at the earliest possible stage of this grievance procedure. Both parties recognize that the
procedure must be available without any fear of retribution because of its use.

3.2    Definitions: A "grievance" is a dispute regarding an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or
misapplication of the specific provisions of this Agreement, which is filed by a unit member who
is directly affected by the alleged violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the collective
bargaining agreement.

A "grievant" is a member directly affected by an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or
misapplication of this Agreement, or is the Association in matters concerning Recognition (1),
Association Rights (2), Grievance Procedure (3), or those that involve an individual, or a group
or class of teachers. Any article of the contract can be grieved subject to this grievance
procedure except as noted in Article VI Leaves and Absences, 6.1: Leaves “With
Compensation” is not subject to the Grievance Procedure.

A "day" is any day that the district administrative office of Bennett Valley Union School District
is open for business except for winter and spring recesses.

The "immediate supervisor" is the lowest level administrator having immediate supervision over
the grievant. The Superintendent will have immediate jurisdiction over the grievances by the
Association.

3.3    Preliminary Level

3.3.1 Before filing a grievance and within thirty (30) days from the occurrence of the act or
omission which gave rise to the grievance, or within thirty (30) days from the date the
grievant knew or should reasonably have known of the act or omission, the employee and
immediate supervisor shall attempt to resolve the problem by an informal conference. If
agreeable to both parties, the superintendent may participate in the initial informal
conference, and/or an additional conference with either party at the informal level. At the
informal conference the employee shall inform his/her immediate supervisor of his/her
intent to file a grievance.

3. 3.2 Before filing an Association grievance, the Association shall attempt to resolve the

problem by an informal conference with the Superintendent within fifteen (15) days of
the alleged violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of this Agreement, or within
fifteen (15) days of when knowledge of such problem was possible.

3.3.3 Summary of Preliminary Level Grievance - Form shall be completed (See Appendix 1).

3.4 Formal Level (See Grievance Forms - Appendix 1)

3.4.1    Level I: Within thirty (30) days from the occurrence of the act or omission which gave
rise to the grievance, or within thirty (30) days from the date the grievant knew or should
reasonably have known of the act or omission, the grievant must present his/her
grievance in writing on the appropriate form to his/her immediate supervisor. The
grievance will be responded to by the immediate supervisor within five (5) days.

3.4.2    Level IF If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision of Level I, he/she may within
five (5) days appeal the decision on the appropriate form to the Superintendent or his/her
designee.

This statement shall include a copy of the original grievance and appeal, the decisions
rendered and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

The Superintendent or his/her designee shall communicate his/her decision to the
grievant on the grievance form within five (5) days. If the Superintendent or his/her
designee does not respond within the time limits provided, the grievant may appeal to the
next level.

3.4.3    Level III: In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the decision reached in Level II
the grievant may appeal the decision in writing on the appropriate form within five (5)
days to the Board of Trustees. At its next regular meeting, the Board will review the
decisions at Levels I and II and may allow the parties to make brief statements in its
discretion.

3.4.3.1    The Board shall communicate its decision to the grievant within ten (10)
days. The Superintendent will implement the Board's decision at the
earliest possible time.

3.4.4    Level IV: If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level III, the grievant may
within five (5) days of receipt of the decision submit a request in writing to the
Association for arbitration of the dispute. Within ten (10) days of the grievant's receipt of
the decision at Level III, the Association shall inform the District of its intent as to
whether or not the grievance will be arbitrated. The Association and the District shall
attempt to agree upon an arbitrator. If no agreement can be reached, they shall request
that the State Conciliation Service supply a panel of seven (7) names of persons
experienced in hearing grievances in public schools. Each party shall alternately strike a
name until only one (1) name remains. The remaining panel member shall be the
arbitrator. The order of the striking shall be determined by lot

3.4.4.1    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be borne by
the party who is ruled against. Any other expenses shall be borne by the
party incurring them.

3.4.4.2    The arbitrator shall as soon as possible hear evidence and render a
decision on the issue or issues submitted to him/her. If the parties cannot
agree upon a submission agreement the arbitrator shall determine the
issues by referring to the written grievance and the answers thereto at each
step.

3.4.4.3    The arbitrator will have no power to add to, subtract from, or modify the

terms of this Agreement or the written policies, rules, regulations, and
procedures of the District.

3.4.4.4    After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to make
written arguments the arbitrator shall submit in writing to all parties
his/her findings and award.

3.4.4.5    The    District    and    the    Association    agree    that    the    jurisdiction and authority

of the arbitrator so selected and the opinions the arbitrator expresses will
be confined exclusively to the interpretation of the expressed provision or
provisions of this agreement at issue between the parties. The arbitrator
shall have no authority to add to, subtract from, alter, amend, or modify
any provisions of this Agreement, or impose any limitations or obligations
not specifically provided for under the terms of this Agreement. The
arbitrator shall be without power or authority to make any decision that
requires the District or the administration to do an act prohibited by law.

3.4.4.6    By submitting the grievance to arbitration the grievant expressly waives

any right to statutory remedies or to the exercise of any legal process other
than as provided by this grievance/arbitration procedure. The processing
of a grievance beyond Level III shall constitute an express election on the
part of the grievant that the grievance/arbitration procedure is the chosen
forum for resolving the issues contained in the grievance, and that the
grievant shall not resort to any other forum or procedure for resolution or
review of the issues.

3.4.4.7    The    award    of    the    arbitrator    in grievances shall be binding on all parties

covered by this agreement.

distributed to certificated employees, administrators, and trustees.

4.5.5    The interest-based bargaining team agrees that "collaboration" means supporting each
member of the team.

4.5.6    Those resource people necessary to the process (budget, legal, facilitation) will not be
considered members of the Interest-based bargaining team.

4.5.7    The ideas of each individual shall have value in the process of decision-making.
Discussions can concern all aspects of the mutual interest-based bargaining tasks.

4.5.8    Contract issues may go to preliminary subgroups. Subgroups may solicit input on the

The term "immediate family" for the purposed of using sick leave to care for them is defined as mother,
father, sister, brother, husband, wife, child, registered domestic partners, grandmother, grandfather, son-
in-law, daughter-in-law, grandchild, mother-in-law, father-in-law, stepchild, or any person living in the

6.1 With Compensation: The following paid leave benefits are reprinted for informational purposes

6.1.1    Sick Leave: Education Code Section 44978. Every certificated employee employed five
days a week by a school district shall be entitled to ten (10) days' leave of absence for
illness or injury and such additional days in addition thereto as the governing board may
allow for illness or injury, exclusive of all days he is not required to render service to the
district, with full pay for a school year of service. A certificated employee employed for
less than five (5) school days a week shall be entitled, for a school year of service, to that
proportion of ten (10) days leave of absence for illness or injury as the number of days he
is employed per week bears to five and is entitled to such additional days in addition
thereto as the governing board may allow for illness or injury to certificated employees
employed for less than five (5) school days per week. Pay for any day of such absence
shall be the same as the pay that would have been received had the employee served
during the day. Credit for leave of absence need not be accrued prior to taking such leave
by the employee and such leave of absence may be taken at any time during the school
year. If such employee does not take the full amount of leave allowed in any school year
under this section the amount not taken shall be accumulated from year to year with such
additional days as the governing board may allow.

6.1.2    Extended Illness Leave: If a teacher has utilized all of his/her accumulated sick leave and
is still absent from his/her duties on account of illness or accident for a period of five (5)
school months, or less, then the amount of salary deducted in any month shall not exceed
the sum which was actually paid or would have been paid a substitute during his/her
period of absence. The five (5) months or less period during which the above deductions
occur shall not begin until the teacher has exhausted all available sick leave, including all
accumulated sick leave and any other available paid leave. If the teacher continues to be
absent beyond the additional five (5) school month period of differential pay, the
employee shall be placed on a reemployment list for 24 months if probationary and 39
months if permanent. As such, the sick leave, including accumulated sick leave, and the
five (5) month period shall run consecutively. A teacher applying for leave under this
Section shall notify the District in writing, specifying the commencement date desired
and reasons for the leave. District shall make every reasonable effort to secure the
services of a substitute employee.

11.2.8.1    Notification: The evaluator will inform the teacher of any areas that need

written copy of the evaluator’s concerns and be offered an opportunity for

teacher, and teacher shall complete the Improvement Plan form at a
conference. The Improvement plan shall identify each area of need.

11.2.8.3    Evaluator’s Support and Monitoring: During the improvement process,
the evaluator shall note the concerns in writing, meet with the teacher to
discuss the concerns, discuss forms of assistance and provide written
feedback, using the Monitoring of Improvement Plan form, regarding the
progress of the teacher toward attainment of the goals enumerated in the
improvement plan.

11.2.8.4    Peer Mentor/Consulting Teacher: A mentor teacher shall be assigned to a

evaluation process for the teacher on an improvement plan. The mentor
teacher shall be paid $50 per hour to assist the teacher on the improvement
plan up to 10 hours.

11.2.8.5    Observation: The evaluator shall conduct a minimum of three classroom
observations and post observation conferences with the teacher during
each school year the teacher is on an improvement plan.

11.2.8.6    Concluding the Improvement Plan Process: Following at least 3
observations and post-observation conferences, the evaluator shall
continue to provide assistance to the teacher until he/she concludes that

productive. A copy of the evaluator’s report shall be submitted to and
discussed with the teacher to receive his/her input and signature before it
is placed in the teacher’s personnel file. The signature of the teacher
signifies only receipt of the copy of the report. The teacher shall have
twenty (20) days to respond to the report in writing and to have the
response attached to the final report.

Santa Rosa, California

Bennett Valley Union ESD
Administrative Regulation 5114

Our mission in class placement is to best meet the needs of each individual child. It is our intent that each child
will belong to a well-balanced, teachable group. Class placement will help to ensure optimum growth for each
child intellectually, emotionally, and socially. In order to accomplish this, information will be collected on each
child. Multiple measures of academic progress including student performance on district assessments, teacher
designed assessments, standardized test scores, and special needs are sources of information considered in
balancing classes

Parent/Guardian Input

Parents/Guardians are partners in the educational process and will be offered the opportunity to provide input that
will be considered as part of the placement process. The child’s teacher knows the child in the academic setting
and will be present at placement meetings to help ensure a positive class assignment for the following year.
Keeping in mind that it is difficult, if not impossible, to honor all requests and maintain well-balanced classes,
parents/guardians who wish to offer additional information or specific attributes of their child that the teacher may
not know, may complete a form available in the school offices. For those parents/guardians who wish to request
particular teachers, three or more teacher’s names must be circled (without ranking) on the fall line up of teachers
list on the back of the form. This will help the placement team understand the parents/guardians concerns and
requests. Again, it is difficult to honor all requests for specific teachers and maintain well-balanced classes.
Providing several options increases the likelihood that we can create well-balanced classes for all children.
Without at least three teachers names circled, placement teams will be unable to consider the request.
Parents/Guardians who would like to request that their child not be considered for a combination class or job
share when one is scheduled to exist for the next grade should so indicate on the form.

Parents/Guardians must place completed forms in the school office in the appropriate grade level box between the
dates designated annually. No forms will be accepted after the designated date. The principal and the placement
teams (teachers) will consider only the forms filled out correctly and received by the designated date when
placing students.

Page - 77

In order to be fair, offering the same opportunity to all parents/guardians, other than the aforementioned letters, no
conversations, e-mail, voice mail, or personal letters regarding the placement of students with administrators or
teachers will be considered. Since they must be used on appeal, the Superintendent and Governing Board will not
be involved in the placement of students and will not influence placement decisions except as noted in the "appeal
process" below.

Teacher Input

The classroom teacher has a wealth of information on each of his/her students and is a critical part of the
placement process. Teachers will complete a card on each child in their class indicating such attributes as the
child's academic performance (whether the child is achieving below, at, or above grade level on district standards,
whether the child presents significant behavioral issues), any special needs the child may have, learning style,
program participation, and any comments that would assist in placing the child (such as positive and unfortunate
student combinations, etc.) Special education teachers collaborate with the site administrator to request specific
placements prior to class placement meetings. The cards will be utilized by the principal and placement teams for
making class placements. Receiving teachers may make placement requests based on familiarity with the family,
experience with the child in various school activities, etc.

The Process

1.    Teachers will complete placement cards for each student by the first Friday in May. Teachers may add

additional information (such as DIBELS scores) once cards are returned to the teams for placement.

2.    Teachers may complete teacher request forms during the parent request window. Forms are due by 4pm on

the day after Open House.

3.    The school principal will collect the placement cards, the parent/guardian request forms, and teacher

request forms.

4.    The principal and special needs programs specialists will meet before school dismisses to make special

education and EL clusters of no more than 3 students per class.

5.    Before the class placement meetings, principals will make administrative placements for students with
significant behavioral/emotional needs.

6.    Teams will meet before the end of the school year to create preliminary class lists following the Class

Placement Directions (included in the appendix). They will receive the lists with the Special Ed., EL
clusters and administrative placements. They will receive the placement cards coded with parent and
teacher requests to complete placements for the remaining students. (Other arrangements may be made
for Kindergarten placements.)

7.    Sending teams will meet to review preliminary class lists before school starts. Any changes due to students

leaving or entering the district over the summer will be made as needed. Assignment of special needs
students must be equitable across the grade level.

No Guarantees

Due to possible changes of grade level, new teachers hired during the summer, enrollment fluctuations, the
impossibility of fdling all requests, and our desire for the best placement for all children, we cannot guarantee that
parent/guardian requests will be honored.

Posting

Since the lists will not be finalized until the teachers return, the lists will normally be posted at 4:00 pm in the
school offices the Friday (if school begins on a Monday or Tuesday) or the Monday (if school begins on a
Wednesday) before school begins. Once the lists are posted, the lists and the assigned class placements will be
considered final.

New students

Students who are registered before the day the lists are posted will be placed on class lists. Students who register
on or after the first day of school will not be able to be placed in a class until the day after their registration is
complete so that an accurate count and balance of the students in each class can be made prior to making the

Page - 78

placement for a new student. Teachers will be verbally notified by noon of the day before a student is to start.

Placement of all students will be based upon maintaining balanced classes. Balance issues that will be considered
in the placement of all students include the total number of students in each class, the number of boys and girls,
special program participation (EL, Special Education, Gifted and Talented Education, Title 1), the number of
children achieving below, at, and above grade level on district standards, the number of children who present
significant behavioral issues, and students with any other special needs. The placement of new students will made
by the principal after consultation with affected teachers (e.g. the teacher(s) at the grade level with the fewest
students, a teacher where the class composition may result in a better, more balanced class placement for the
child, etc.)

Requests for Changes of Placement

Requests for changes of placement are not encouraged. Placement changes are always a disruption for the class,
teacher, and student involved. A request for a change of placement is considered as carefully as the initial
placement to make sure it is truly in the best interest of all students. The policy requires that parents/guardians go
through specific steps and complete certain forms. The timing of these steps is to support parents/guardians and
students in giving the assigned class a fair chance and to get acquainted with the assigned teacher.

Requesting a change out of the assigned class does not guarantee a particular replacement teacher, nor does it save
a "spot" in a class in anticipation of a possible change. If the principal decides to make the change and remove
the child from the assigned class, the principal will use the same criteria to determine the new class placement (to
maintain the equitable class balance) as in the original placement process.

The protocol for requesting a change of placement is to be followed in this order:

1.    The parent/guardian shall approach the class placement with an open mind, shall give the child a chance in
the class for at least 2 weeks, and shall communicate any concerns with the assigned teacher.

2.    The parent/guardian shall attend the entire Back to School Night presentation in your child’s classroom.

3.    The parent/guardian must meet, in person, with the teacher and principal to discuss unresolved concerns
and reasons for continuing to want a change of placement.

4.    The principal shall have 5 school days to render a final decision on the placement.

5.    Requests for a second change of placement during the same school year will not be considered.

Appeal Process

If dissatisfied with the principal’s decision, parents/guardians may appeal the principal’s decision to the
Superintendent, and, if necessary, the Superintendent’s decision to the Board. The Superintendent shall have up
to 10 school days from the appeal meeting to finalize a decision. If a Board appeal is necessary, the appeal shall
be heard and acted upon at the next regularly scheduled board meeting. Because they are a part of the appeal
process, the Superintendent and the Board should not be involved unless and until an appeal is made.

Once adopted, the Board of Trustees agrees to consult with the official representative of the teachers (BVTA
President) before making any changes to this policy.

Maternity Protocol: Certificated

Maternity leaves are strictly governed by law and contract. The board will approve maternity leave per
the terms of the BVTA contract (starting on page 20).

Maternity leave is a provision that guarantees an employee a job following leave. It is an unpaid leave
except under certain provisions as described below.

Under the law, pregnancy is not classified as an illness for which sick leave can be taken. (Please see
page 20 sections f and g). If one is sick because of the pregnancy (on bed rest, etc) then a doctor's note
verifying that is used to allow the use of sick leave for the doctor specified length of the illness (or bed
rest).

On page 21 of the contract, there is a section regarding combining maternity and sick leave—maternity
leave is unpaid (page 20 l.f) Sick leave can be used with maternity if the doctor specifies that one is sick
as a condition of the pregnancy (provisions a and b on the top of page 21)

Sick leave can be used for recovery from childbirth when the doctor so indicates in a signed note (with
specified recovery dates) AND when the employee will be returning to work following the recovery.
Doctors routinely grant up to 4 weeks before and up to 6 weeks following birth as needed for this. So
with a doctor's note, sick leave can be used during this time.

Other than that, one can combine a few things to get paid for some of the days:

•Personal leave (out of sick leave) for up to 5 days per year for any purpose. (Page 17 1.1.4)
•Days of convenience (Page 17 1.1.5) or wellness days (page 16 1.1.1.2) accrued
• 1.1.1.2 allows one to use wellness days (up to 2).

The family medical leave act allows one to get 12 weeks of unpaid leave AND 12 weeks of benefits
while out, at district expense, (page 23 3.3). For the rest of the time, the employee may purchase district
benefits at the district cost.

Page 17 in the contract 1.1.3 Personal Necessity: One can take a total of 10 days (out of sick leave) to
care for the newborn baby, allowable under provision 1—illness of an immediate family member.
Although newborns are not technically ill, they are as vulnerable as a seriously ill person for the first 10
days of life.

Family medical leave act gives 12 weeks of health benefits.

Shared Teaching Assignments

The Governing Board realizes that for personal reasons, a certificated teacher sometimes may prefer to
share the responsibility, salary and benefits of a full-time teaching position with one of his/her fellow
teachers. When those sharing a position have compatible work patterns and similar goals, this situation
can benefit teachers and students alike. By accommodating requests for shared teaching assignments, the
district is more likely to retain teachers of outstanding capabilities.

Sharing contracts is available and may be approved by application process as a benefit to employees,
when it also benefits students. Some of the increased costs incurred from a shared teaching situation
may be in statutory benefits, processing payroll, processing absences, conducting evaluations, and in
certain circumstances, compensation for a teacher to come in on a day when he/she does not normally
work. To the extent possible, the district should not incur additional costs due to two employees wishing
to job share. District policy and contract language encourage job share partners to substitute for each
other whenever possible to minimize academic disruption to students. Subbing is compensated at the
current "sub rate" or by the partners trading days. Each job share partner will receive a prorated share of
salary, sick leave, personal leave days, and personal necessity days, based on percentage of full-time
equivalent (FTE). Job share partners working at least .5 FTE will receive a prorated share of "wellness"
days and days of convenience. To avoid a compromise in the consistency of instruction to students, job
share applications must be considered carefully. Following approval by the principal and
Superintendent, job share agreements must be approved by the Board of Trustees.

There shall be no more than the equivalent of 1 job share per grade level at each school site (4 at
Yulupa; 3 at Strawberry) with a maximum of 2 in any one grade level.
