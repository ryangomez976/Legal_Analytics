1.1    The terms of this Agreement constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
between the Governing Board of the Sonoma Valley Unified School District
and the Valley of the Moon Teachers Association, an affiliate of CTA and NEA,
an employee organization.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-
3549 of the Government Code.

1.3    This Agreement may be changed by mutual consent between the Board and
the VMTA as a result of subsequent negotiation.

1.4    Within thirty (30) days of ratification by the Board and Association, the
District, at its own expense, shall deliver to each bargaining unit member a
copy of the agreement as an e-mail link. In addition, the District shall
deliver to the Association President thirty (30) printed copies for Association
use, and shall make available to any unit member a printed copy on request.

1.5    The District shall maintain a digital copy of the Agreement on the District's
website. This copy shall be updated upon ratification of negotiated changes
during the term of the agreement.

1.6    By October 1st of each year of the Agreement, the District shall deliver to
each bargaining unit member an annually revised copy of the Agreement as
an e-mail link and written notification. In addition, the District shall deliver
to the Association President thirty (30) printed copies for Association use.

ARTICLE 2 - RECOGNITION

2.1    The Sonoma Valley Unified School District Governing Board (hereinafter
referred to as the "Board") recognizes the Valley of the Moon Teachers
Association CTA/NEA (hereinafter referred to as "VMTA") as the sole and
exclusive bargaining agent for certified employees as defined in Chapter
10.7, Sections 3540 through 3549.3 of Division 4, Title I of the Government
Code.

2.2    The certificated employees bargaining unit shall consist of all certificated
employee positions excluding the positions of substitute teacher,
home/hospital teacher, adult education teachers, certificated charter school
employees, summer school teachers, and certificated confidential,
supervisory, and management positions as listed by the Board of Trustees.

2.3    The certificated exclusive bargaining agent recognizes the Board as the duly
elected representative of the people and negotiates, for the purposes of the
Rodda Act, only with the officially authorized representatives designated by
the Board.

2.4    The District shall notify the Association upon the establishment of any new
certificated positions by the District. Should the parties agree that the
position falls within the bargaining unit, the parties shall meet to negotiate in
a timely manner negotiable issues applicable to the new position. Such
negotiations shall not delay the District's decision to post and or fill the
position. Disputes regarding bargaining unit jurisdiction shall be referred to
PERB.

ARTICLE 4 - DISTRICT RIGHTS

4.1    All District rights and functions, including its authority to direct, manage, and
control the operation of the District, shall remain with the District except as
limited by the specific and express provisions of this Agreement.

4.2    In the event of emergency. District shall have the right to rescind any portion
of this Agreement directly related to the nature of the emergency.
"Emergency" as used in this Article is limited to natural catastrophic
situations that would prevent the normal functioning of the school district
pursuant to this Agreement.

4.3    When a department or school is over-staffed, the District may reassign a unit
member to other duties for which they are certified or to perform curriculum,
within department, or in-school substitute duties, or other mutually agreed
upon duties. In such circumstances, the District shall seek volunteers prior to
using Article 13.5.

ARTICLE 5 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

5.1 Definitions

5.1.1

A "grievant" is any unit member directly affected by an alleged
violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of this agreement;
or is the Association, if the Association names a specific unit
member who is directly affected by an alleged violation of this
agreement, and that person agrees to the pursuit of the
grievance.

5.1.2

If more than one (1) unit member has the same grievance in
issue and fact, the grievances shall be consolidated for purposes
of hearing and decision, provided the rights of the parties are
not thereby prejudiced.

5.1.3

A "day" is a day on which the District Office of the School
District is open for business. In the event of a grievance during
teacher vacation, suspension of the grievance timeline shall be
given if requested by either the District or the affected unit
member prior to the lapse of any stipulated timeline.

5.2 Informal Level

5.2.1

Before filing a formal grievance, the grievant shall attempt to
resolve it by an informal conference with his/her immediate
supervisor or with the Superintendent if 5.4.7 applies. This
informal conference shall take place within ten (10) days after
the grievant knew or should have known of the act or omission
giving rise to the grievance.

5.3 Formal Level

5.3.1

Level I. In the event the qrievance is not resolved at the
informal conference or the grievant is not satisfied with the
resolution, the grievant must present his/her grievance in
writing on the appropriate form (See Appendix D) within ten
(10) days after the informal conference.

5.3.2

This statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the
grievance, of the specific section of the Agreement violated,
misinterpreted, or misapplied, the circumstances involved, the
decision rendered at the informal conference, and the specific
remedy sought.

6

5.3.3    The    immediate    supervisor shall communicate his/her decision in

writing within ten (10) days after receipt of the grievance.
Failure by a grievant to appeal the decision within the specified
time limits shall be deemed an acceptance of the decision.

5.3.4    Level    II.    In    the    event    the    grievant    is    not    satisfied with the

decision at Level I, the grievant may appeal the decision to the
Superintendent or his/her designee within ten (10) days after
receiving the decision from Level I.

5.3.5    This    statement    shall    include    a    copy    of    the    original    grievance and

appeal, the decision rendered and a clear, concise statement of
the reasons for the appeal. The Superintendent, or his/her
designee, shall communicate his/her decision, in writing, within
ten (10) days to the grievant after receiving the appeal.

5.3.6    Level    III:    Mediation

5.3.6.1    If the grievant and/or the Association is not
satisfied with the disposition of the grievance, or if
no disposition has occurred pursuant to the
provisions of Level II, the grievance shall be
referred to grievance mediation within ten (10)
days after receiving the decision from Level II.

5.3.6.2    The    Association shall    request that    a

conciliator/mediator from the California State
Mediation/Conciliation Service be assigned to assist
the parties in the resolution of the grievance. Such
request shall be made in writing within seven (7)
days of the referral in 5.3.6.1 with a copy provided
to the District. If the parties mutually agree on an
alternative mediation service, prior agreement
must be made on costs.

5.3.6.3    The mediator, within twenty (20) days of the
request shall meet with the grievant, the
Association and the District for the purpose of
resolving the grievance.

5.3.6.4    If an agreement is reached, the agreement shall be
reduced to writing and shall be signed by the
grievant, the Association and the District. This
agreement shall be non-precedential and shall
constitute a settlement of the grievance.

7

5.3.6.5

In the event that the grievance is not resolved with
the assistance of the conciliator/mediator within
ten (10) days from the first meeting held by the
conciliator/mediator, the Association or the District
may terminate Level III and the grievant may
proceed to Level IV.

5.3.7    Level    IV: Binding Arbitration. The Association may submit the

grievance to final and binding arbitration if either:

5.3.7.1    The grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of
the grievance at Level III, or

5.3.7.2    No written decision has been rendered within ten
(10) days after the Superintendent/designee
received the appeal.

In either case, such submission by the Association must be
made within fifteen (15) days after receipt of the decision, in
writing, of the Superintendent or his/her designee. That
demand shall identify each aspect of the Superintendent's or
designee's decision with which the Association disagrees.

5.3.8    The    parties shall select a mutually acceptable arbitrator. Should

they be unable to agree on an arbitrator within ten (10) days of
submission of the grievance to arbitration, submission of the
grievance shall be made to the California State Conciliation
Service with a request that a list of arbitrators be submitted.

5.3.9    Each    party shall alternately eliminate names of said list until one

(1) remains and that person shall be appointed as the
arbitrator. A toss of the coin shall determine the first party to
eliminate the first name. The arbitrator shall have no power to
add or delete from the terms and conditions of the Agreement.
Technical rules of evidence shall apply at the hearing. The cost
of the arbitrator shall be shared equally by the parties. The cost
of any reporter shall be borne by the party making the request
or shared equally by both parties if mutually requested. A
decision shall be rendered within thirty (30) days of the hearing.

6.1.1

Kinderqarten: Unit members shall be on site for 370 minutes
per day, exclusive of a lunch period. Of the 370 minutes, 200
shall be used for direct instructional class time. The schedule
for the remaining on site time shall be determined by the
affected unit members and their immediate supervisor.

6.1.2

the affected unit members and their immediate supervisor.

average of no more than 281 minutes shall be used for direct

immediate supervisor.

Grades 4-5/ Unit members shall be on site for 370 minutes per

immediate supervisor.

Grades 6-8 Middle School: Unit members shall be on site for

immediate supervisor.

Grades 9-12: Unit members shall be on site for 370 minutes
per day, exclusive of a lunch period. In a consecutive two (2)

10.1    Unit members who feel that they are being required to work under unsafe
conditions or to perform tasks which endanger their health and safety shall
report such conditions to the site administrator. The site administrator shall
take whatever action is appropriate within his/her authority to correct the
unsafe or unhealthy condition. If the site administrator is unable to correct
the situation, the problem shall be referred to the Superintendent and Board
who shall report back as to the disposition of the matter to the unit member.

10.1.1 The District and the Association recognize that a unit member
and/or the Association may report allegations of health and/or
safety concerns to appropriate public agencies. The District will
correct deficiencies cited by the agency for which the District is
responsible. Unit members are responsible for cooperating with
and adhering to applicable safety regulations.

shall be made at a time which will not interfere with the students'
instructional day.

arrange for a conference to be held as soon as possible between
himself/herself, the teacher and parent (when appropriate), and appropriate
specialist, to discuss the problem and to decide upon proper steps for its
resolution.

connection with their employment to their principal or immediate supervisor
and to the police. Such notification shall immediately be forwarded to the
Superintendent.

discipline as provided in the Education Code, Board policy, and teacher
handbook.

students.

10.7    Field Trips: A member of the unit shall obtain written authorization from the
District to take students on a field trip and to transport such student in the
member's personal automobile. Authorization shall mean that the trip is a
school sponsored activity and the District liability insurance is applicable.

10.8    The provisions of this Article also pertain to teaching related duties.

10.9    The employer shall reimburse unit members the full cost of replacing or

ARTICLE 12 - LEAVES

12.1 Sick Leave

12.1.1

Bargaining unit members who are employed full time shall be
entitled to ten (10) days of paid sick leave annually,
commencing on the first day of employment. Unused sick leave
shall be cumulative from year to year.

In addition to all sick leave entitlement that a unit member may
accumulate within the District, he/she shall also be entitled to
all unused sick leave which may have been accumulated while
employed in a position requiring certification qualifications in
another school district.

12.1.2

Bargaining unit members who work more or less than full time
shall be entitled to sick leave in the same ratio that their work
bears to full time employment.

12.1.3

The amount of sick leave accrued shall be given in writing to
each unit member at the beginning of the school year.

12.1.4

The District shall provide a process for unit members to report
and track absences.

12.1.5

If a unit member fails to give notice of his or her intention to
return and the substitute appears for the day's work as a result
of failure to receive such notice, the substitute shall receive a
full day's substitute pay and this amount shall be deducted from
the unit member's salary for that month.

12.1.6

A sick leave day once commenced may not be reinstated as a
working day unless previously approved by the principal or
immediate supervisor. If the illness extends beyond two weeks,
the unit member shall notify his/her supervisor of the expected
date of return. The unit member shall notify the supervisor at
the earliest possible time of any change in that date.

12.1.7

No payment for such leave shall be made until the
miscellaneous leave report is submitted by the unit member and
signed by the principal or immediate supervisor.

12.1.8

A unit member whose absence under this section exceeds five
(5) calendar days shall provide, upon request, a statement from
a licensed physician or practitioner stating the reason for the
absence. Additionally, the District may require verification of an
absence of less than five (5) calendar days if the District has
reason to believe that the absence may not have been used for

39

proper illness/accident leave purposes.

12.1.9

When requested by the District, a unit member shall undergo a
physical or mental examination by a doctor jointly selected by
the unit member and the Superintendent, and the cost of such
examination shall be borne by the District. In the event that the
Superintendent and unit member are unable to agree upon a
doctor, a doctor will be selected by the President of the Sonoma
County Medical Association, and both parties shall be bound by
that decision. The unit member shall authorize the doctor to
release the results of the examination to the District. The
provisions of this section shall only be applied in the event of
compelling circumstances.

12.1.10

In the event of an emergency, and no substitute can be
employed to replace a unit member absent for accident or
illness, with the approval of the site principal, the absent unit
member's classes may be taught by unit members already in
full-time employ of the School District. Those unit members
shall be compensated for extra classes taught within their
normal teaching day. The compensation shall be by
compensatory time off equal to the additional classroom
assignment or by sick leave equal to the additional classroom
assignment at the option of the unit member.

The principal shall be responsible for seeing that such time is
credited. The unit member and principal shall arrange for the
compensatory time to be taken and a substitute may be hired
where necessary for such compensation time.

12.2 Extended Disability

12.2.1

For a period not to exceed five (5) months, commencing with
the eleventh (11th) day of absence, the unit member shall
receive not less than the difference between his/her pay and the
rate paid the individual who substitutes in the unit member's
position. The deduction for the substitute pay shall not exceed
One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) per day.

12.2.2

This leave requires monthly certification by the unit member's
physician, on a form provided by the District, that the employee
is physically or mentally disabled and unable to perform his/her
job.

12.2.3

If the illness extends beyond two (2) weeks, the unit member
shall notify his/her supervisor of the expected date of return.
The unit member shall notify the supervisor at the earliest
possible time of any change in that date.

26.3.2    Any one or more of the causes specified in Education Code

by the governing board without notice of hearing except as may be required

ARTICLE 27 - COMPLAINTS

27.1    The purpose of this Article is to provide the means to handle complaints in
such a manner that the specific problem is resolved as quickly as possible
and further, to reduce the possibility that the same problem will recur. It is
understood that this Article does not apply to concerns, complaints and/or
changes involving allegations of sexual harassment, child abuse, unlawful
discrimination or criminal conduct. In such instance, relevant Education Code
and criminal statutes shall be in effect. To promote prompt and fair resolution
of the complaint, the following procedures shall govern the resolution of
complaints against unit members:

27.1.1    No negative and/or unsatisfactory evaluation shall be based
upon information or material of a derogatory or critical nature
which has been received by any member of the administration
from any parent, non-administrative employee of the District, or
any other citizen unless the following procedures have been
followed.

27.1.2    If any information is placed in unit member's personnel file by
administration regarding a formal complaint the provisions of
Article 7.11, Personnel Files, shall be in effect.

27.2    Definitions:

27.2.1    An informal complaint is a verbal or written expression of
dissatisfaction or concern and the direct supervisor believes that
the complaint requires administrative action beyond being
attuned to a potential problem or problem area. An informal
complaint shall not be used as basis for evaluation and it shall
not be placed in the unit member's personnel file.

27.2.2    A formal complaint is a written expression of dissatisfaction
which requires an action, as per 27.4.

27.3    Informal Complaint Procedure:

27.3.1 Every effort should be made to resolve a complaint at the
earliest possible stage. Whenever possible, the complainant
should communicate directly to the unit member in order to
resolve concerns.

109

27.3.2    If the complainant is unable or unwilling to resolve the complaint
directly with the unit member, he/she may submit an oral or
written complaint to the unit member's direct supervisor or the
principal. Should the administrator who receives the complaint
not be directly responsible for the unit member, he or she shall
refer the complaint to the administrator who is the individual's
direct supervisor.

27.3.3    In the event an administrator receives a complaint regarding a
unit member, the administrator shall weigh the validity of the
complaint and the seriousness of the complaint.

27.3.4    The direct supervisor shall do one or more of the following within
fifteen (15) work days of receipt of the complaint:

27.3.4.1    Speak to the unit member of the complaint to
establish the facts, indicating an administrative
concern if appropriate.

27.3.4.2    Speak generally to the faculty about the problem or
the potential problem.

27.3.4.3    Hold a meeting with the complainant and the
affected unit member.

27.4 Formal Complaint Procedure:

27. 4.1 If the complaint is not resolved through any of the above, the
direct supervisor may ask the complainant if he/she wishes to
make a formal written complaint. If the complainant is unable to
prepare the complaint in writing, administrative staff shall help
him/her to do so. Such complaint shall be filed within ten (10)
working days of the notification by the direct supervisor.

27. 4.1.1 A formal written complaint shall include:

1.    The full name of the unit member involved.

2.    A brief but specific summary of the
complaint and the facts surrounding it.

3.    A specific description of any prior attempt to
discuss the complaint with the unit member
and the failure to resolve the matter.

4.    In the complainant's opinion, the action(s)
that would remediate the problem.

110

27.4.2    The complaint shall be shown to the unit member and discussed
with the unit member within five (5) school days after receipt of
the complaint.

27.4.3    Should the unit member or the direct supervisor decide that
there should be a meeting concerning the complaint with the
complainant, either the unit member or the direct supervisor
shall submit a written request for such a meeting within three
(3) days to the unit member and the complainant. Either party
may decline to attend the meeting.

27.4.4    Should both complainant and the unit member agree to a
meeting, it shall be scheduled within ten (10) days by the direct
supervisor.

27.4.4.1    An Association Representative may be present at
said meeting at the request of the unit member.

27.4.4.2    The role of the direct supervisor shall be to:

1.    Establish the purpose of the meeting,

2.    Clarify facts,

3.    Propose a resolution to the problem if the
complainant and the unit member are unable
to reach resolution.

27.4.4.3    Five (5) days after the meeting is held the direct
supervisor shall send a brief written summary of
the meeting to both the unit member and the
complainant stating the facts and resolution of the
incident established at the meeting.

27.4.5    If either party declines the meeting within five (5) days of the
request, the direct supervisor shall then investigate the charge
and issue a written report. The report shall be provided to both
parties within ten (10) days after the meeting has been declined.
The written report shall be a written summary of facts and
resolution.

27.4.6    Appeal at the Board level may be made by either party.

27.5 Complaints which are withdrawn and shown to be false shall neither be
placed in the unit member's personnel file nor be utilized in any evaluation or
disciplinary action against the unit member.
