ARTICLE 1 - AGREEMENT

1.1    The    Articles    and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding

agreement by and between the Igo-Ono-Platina Union School District (hereinafter
referred to as "District") and the Igo-Ono-Platina Teachers Association CTA/NEA
(hereinafter referred to as “Association”).

1.2    This    agreement    is    entered    into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the

Government Code. 1

2.1 The District confirms its recognition of the Association as the exclusive representative for
those certificated employees in the representation unit recognized by the District per its
resolution at the meeting of the Governing Board of June 12, 1980. The unit excludes
substitutes, management, supervisory, and confidential employees.

3.1    Any grievance which may arise between an employee and the District with respect to the
interpretation or application of any of the terms of this Agreement, except as otherwise
provided in this Agreement, shall be determined by the provisions of this Article. Grievant as
used in this Agreement is defined as an employee of the District.

3.2    Grievance Procedure

Step One:

The initial step in the adjustment of a grievance shall be a discussion between
the Grievant or his representative and the Principal who will answer within ten
(10) calendar days. This step shall be started within thirty (30) calendar days
of the date of the action complained of, or the date the grievant became aware
of the incident which is the basis for the grievance.

Step Two:

If a grievance is not resolved in the first step, the second step shall be
presentation of the grievance, in writing, to the Principal, who shall answer, in
writing, within twenty (20) calendar days. The second step shall be taken
within ten (10) calendar days of the date of the answer in Step One. The
written presentation shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
circumstances involved, the pertinent dates, the decision rendered at the
previous step, the section of the Agreement alleged to be violated, and the
specific remedy sought.

Step Three:

If a grievance is not resolved in the second step, the third step shall be the
presentation of the grievance, in writing, to the Superintendent or designee,
who shall answer, in writing, within twenty (20) calendar days. The third step
shall be taken within ten (10) calendar days of the date of the answer in Step
Two. The written presentation shall be a clear, concise statement of the
grievance, the circumstances involved, the pertinent dates, the decision
rendered at the previous step, the section of the Agreement alleged to be
violated, and the specific remedy sought.

Step Four:

If a grievance is not resolved in the third step, the fourth step shall be referral
by the Association to mediation within twenty (20) calendar days of the
answer in Step Three. Whenever a grievance is referred to mediation, either
party shall request that the California State Mediation and Conciliation
Service refer a State Mediator. The State Mediator shall assist the parties in
the resolution of the grievance in the same manner as that which is normally
used in the mediation of interest disputes. Referral to Step Five shall not
occur until the Mediator has released the parties from the mediation process.

Step Five:

If a grievance is not resolved in the fourth step, the fifth step shall be referral
by the Association to arbitration within twenty (20) calendar days of the

3

An Arbitrator shall be appointed on each occasion that a grievance is
submitted to arbitration. In the event that the District and the Association are
unable to agree on the selection of an Arbitrator, they shall request the State
of California Mediation and Conciliation Service to nominate five (5) persons
to be the Arbitrator. The District and the Association each will alternately
challenge two (2) of such nominees, the party having the first challenge to be
determined by lot. The remaining nominee shall be accepted as the Arbitrator
and his compensation and expenses shall be borne equally by the District and
the Association. The District and the Association shall pay the compensation
and expenses of their respective appointees and witnesses. At Association
request District shall release Grievant from duty to participate in arbitration
proceedings. Expense items such as court reporters, transcripts, or room rent,
when requested by either the Arbitrator or by both parties, shall be borne
equally by the parties. Whenever such items are requested by only one of the
parties, the expense shall be borne by that party.

Am Arbitrator shall hold such hearings and shall consider such evidence as to
the Arbitrator appears necessary and proper. The decision of the Arbitrator
shall be final and binding on the District, the Association and the Grievant, and
shall not in any way add to, disregard or modify any of the provisions of this
Agreement.

Failure by the Grievant to meet any of the aforementioned time limits will
result in forfeiture of the grievance. Except, however, that the
aforementioned time limits may be extended by written mutual agreement.

Any employee may present grievances in accordance with this Article without
the intervention of the Association, so long as the adjustment is reached prior
to Arbitration and is not inconsistent with the terms and conditions of this
Agreement and further provided that the District shall not agree to a
resolution of the grievance until the Association has received a copy of the
grievance and the proposed resolution and has been given the opportunity to
file a response.

A Grievant shall in no way interfere with the right of the District to proceed in
carrying out its management responsibilities subject to a final decision on the
grievance. In the event the grievance involved an order, requirement or other
directive, the Grievant shall fulfill or carry out such order, requirements or
directives, pending the final decision of the grievance.

All documents resulting from the processing of a grievance shall be kept in a
separate grievance file and shall not be placed in an employee's personnel file.

ARTICLE 4 - HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

4.1    Full-time employees shall work one hundred eighty-three (183) days including one hundred
eighty (180) instructional days during each school year. The school year calendar shall be
developed by mutual agreement of the District and Association. The annual scheduled parent
conferences shall be conducted during minimum school days.

4.2    Beginning with the 1986-87 school year the minimum annual instructional minutes are as
follows:

Kindergarten    36,000    minutes per year

Grades 1 - 3    50,400 minutes per year

Grades 4 - 8    56,000 minutes per year

The District Superintendent/Principal shall establish the actual class schedule including
starting and ending times as well as recesses and lunch periods, after consultation with the
Association.

4.3    Unit members shall report for work at least thirty (30) minutes prior to the beginning of their
first assigned class, and shall work on campus as assigned by the Principal with the exception
of a thirty (30) minute duty-free lunch period until thirty (30) minutes after the end of their
last assigned class.

4.4    On work days of non-student attendance, the reporting times of all members of this
representation unit may be modified by the District.

4.5    In addition to the above working time, all members of the representation unit maybe assigned
by the District to one back-to-school night, one open house, faculty meetings (not to exceed
to (2) per school month), advisory committee meetings (not to exceed ten (10) per school
year), parent conferences, appointments with the administration, supervision of activities, and
curriculum committee meetings.

4.6    Minimum days shall begin at the completion of the legally mandated minimum instructional
time, subject to availability of buses. The minimum days for Platina may be modified by
mutual agreement between the District and Association. Other minimum days may also be
scheduled by mutual agreement between the District and Association.

4.7    Whenever any employee needs and requests release from classroom duties for purposes of
preparation time and the Superintendent/Principal is available, such relief maybe provided by
the Superintendent/Principal at his sole discretion.

ARTICLE 5 - CLASS SIZE

5.1    The District shall strive to maintain class enrollments not to exceed the following:

K - 3    30    students

(also applies to combination classes)

4- 8    32    students

In the event that the above class size goals are exceeded for more than fifteen (15) school
days in a twenty (20) school day period the Superintendent/Principal shall meet with affected
teachers to discuss, and shall make every reasonable effort to provide for, an equitable
distribution of students.

5.2    In the event that any class exceeds the above class size goals (based upon the official District
class assignment list), an additional $ 125.00 per pupil above the stated goals shall be allocated
to the teacher, subject to the following procedures;

a.    The additional funds may be used to purchase aide time, instructional
equipment, supplies, or materials, consistent with District purchasing and
hiring procedure.

b.    The additional funds shall be allocated upon a class exceeding the above
stated class size goal for more than fifteen (15) school days in a twenty (20)
school day period. The funds are a one-time allocation per each school year
for each numerical increment of students above the goal.

5.3    Each teacher at the Igo-Ono School who is assigned a class consisting of more than one (1)
grade shall be allocated $250.00 per school year. Such funds shall be utilized pursuant to
section 5.2 a. above. Such funds shall be in addition to any funds which the teacher may
receive under Section 5.2.

5.4    The District shall attempt to equalize the distribution of instructional aide time allocated to
teachers during any school year. It is understood that various factors may limit the District's
ability to equalize such aide time (such as funding restrictions, classified employee layoff
procedures, geographic considerations, and instructional program differences).

ARTICLE 6 - TRANSFER AND ASSIGNMENT PROCEDURES

6.1    General Provisions:

In accordance with Education Code Sections 35035, the Superintendent may transfer
employees within the District, limited only by the specific and express procedures contained in
this Article. Accordingly, only alleged procedural violations of this Article maybe processed
through the grievance procedure of this Agreement. In making transfers, consideration will
be given to such factors as experience, training, and credential of potential transferees, duties
and requirements of the vacancy and the length of service of potential transferees.

6.2    Definitions:

a.

b.

A "transfer" is the relocation of a unit member from one school to another within the

District or a change of grade level assignment within the same school.

A "vacancy" is a position within this representation unit occurring when the current
certificated school staff have been assigned to fill all available positions, and the
District determines that an additional or replacement employee is required.

6.3 Voluntary Transfers:

a.    During the regular school session, the District shall post in each school a list of
vacancies (describing grade level, job requirements, location and duties) for positions
within the representation unit for the following school year. The list shall contain the
closing date and procedures for submitting a request for transfer. No regular
assignment to fill such a vacancy may be made until after this closing date.

b.    To be considered for vacancies which occur during the summer recess period,
employees shall file with the District Office a District form which indicates a desire to
transfer to a particular school or assignment.

c.    If a request for transfer is not granted, the employee may schedule a meeting with the
Superintendent to discuss the reasons why the transfer was not granted. Upon further
request of the employee, the District shall put the reasons for the transfer in writing.

6.4 Involuntary Transfers:

An employee who does not request a transfer may not be transferred until given an
opportunity (a telephone call or a letter if the employee is not available) for a personal
discussion with the Superintendent to discuss the reasons for the transfer. Upon the further
request of the employee, the District shall put the reasons for the transfer in writing. The
employee shall also be given an opportunity to be considered for other vacancies which are
available at the time of the impending transfer for which the employee is qualified. All such
transfers shall normally be completed by June 30 of each school year. In the event of an
occurrence after this date, such as a death, resignation, leave of absence, change in

7

enrollment, or an emergency, transfers may be made or changed by the District.

ARTICLE 7 - EMPLOYEE EVALUATION PROCEDURES

7.1    General Procedures:

A joint committee shall be formed to develop a recommended employee evaluation procedure.
The committee shall consist of one upper grade and one primary grade teacher (appointed by
the Association) and the Superintendent/Principal. The recommendations of the Committee
shall be submitted to the parties to this Agreement.

7.2    Employee Personnel Files:

An employee may inspect material in his/her personnel file which may serve as a basis for
affecting the status of his/her employment except materials which:

a.    are obtained prior to his/her employment,

b.    were prepared by identifiable examination committee members, or

c.    were obtained in connection with a promotional examination.

An employee may inspect such materials in his/her personnel file, with the exception of the
above specified items, during the normal business hours of the District Central Office at times
other than when the employee is required to render service.

No materials of a derogatory nature, except the above specified items, may be placed in an
employee's personnel file without allowing the employee an opportunity to review and
comment thereon. An employee shall have the right to enter, and have attached to any such
derogatory statement, his/her own comments thereon. The review and comment upon
materials of a derogatory nature shall take place during the normal business hours of the
District Office and at times when the employee can be spared from duty. The employee shall
submit an advance written or oral request to the supervisor to leave the nonnal place of work
during assigned duty times for such review and comment.

All material placed in an employee's personnel file shall be dated and signed by the
contributor.

The employee personnel file referred to in this section is the official personnel file of the
employee which shall be maintained in a lockable file in the central administrative offices of
the District.

7.3    Public Complaints:

Except those involving criminal matters, all citizens or parent complaints shall first be referred
to the unit member involved for a conference. In the event that a citizen or parent does not

wish to confer with the unit member, the complaint shall be put in writing, with a copy being
provided to the unit member.

Pursuant to Section 7.2(c), unit members shall be given an opportunity to make a written
response to each complaint which shall be attached and filed with the complaint. District
administrators shall in no way be restricted from conferring with parents and citizens
concerning a complaint.

ARTICLE 8 - SAFETY CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

8.1    The District shall confer with and advise any employee assaulted by a student or a student's
parent while in the discharge of his/her duties. An assault shall be defined, as in the penal
code, as an unlawful attempt, coupled with a present ability to commit a violent injury on the
person of another.

8.2    Any case of assault shall be promptly reported to the employee's immediate supervisor, who
shall immediately report the incident to the appropriate law enforcement agency. Such
employee then shall submit a complete written report of the incident to the District.

8.3 When absence arises out of or from such assault or injury, the terms and conditions of the
Workers' Compensation Insurance and appropriate leave provisions of this Agreement shall
apply.

8.4 The District desires to maintain a safe place of employment for its employees, and will make
all reasonable provisions necessary for the safety of District employees in the performance of
their work.

8.5    Whenever any unsafe condition becomes known to District employees such condition will be
reported promptly to the District's Superintendent/Principal.

8.6    Upon becoming aware of unsafe conditions, the Superintendent/Principal will take all
necessary and appropriate actions in order to correct or improve any unsafe or hazardous
condition. 11

ARTICLE 9 - LEAVES

9.1    Rules for General Absences:

Unit members shall not be absent during a work day except on an approved leave of absence,
as defined herein.

If an employee needs to be absent from duty, notice must be given to the District Office.
Except in the event of an emergency, the District Office shall be notified prior to the day of
absence. It is the responsibility of the employee to see that the class roll book, lesson plans,
and other pertinent material necessary to conduct the class are made available to the school.

9.2    Sick Leave:

Unit members employed five (5) days per week shall be entitled to ten (10) work days leave
of absence for illness or injury with full pay, for each school year of service. Unit members
employed less than five (5) days per week shall be entitled to a proportionate amount of sick
leave.

9.2.1    Employees may accumulate unused sick leave without limitation.

9.2.2    The District may require a doctor's verification following any absence due to illness or
injury as a condition of payment of sick leave. Except in the event of a concerted
activity, the District shall pay the cost of the examination. In the event that such
doctor's verification does not provide the District with sufficient information, the
employee shall obtain another statement from his/her physician that verifies that sick
leave was for the reasons stated.

9.2.3    Employees returning to work from Sick Leave from surgery or a serious illness, upon
the request of the District, must provide a doctor's release certifying medical
permission to return to work.

9.2.4    When a unit member's employment terminates and more sick leave has been used than
earned, the amount used but not earned shall be deducted from the final pay warrant.

9.2.5    When an employee is absent from duties on account of illness or accident for a period
of five (5) school months or less, the amount deducted from the salary due the
employee for any month in which the absence occurs shall not exceed the sum actually
paid any substitute employee employed to fill the position during the absence; or if no
substitute employee was employed, the amount which would have been paid to the
substitute had he/she been employed.

9.3    Pregnancy Leave:

Unit members are entitled to use sick leave as set forth in Section 9.2 for disabilities caused or
contributed to by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and recovery therefrom, on the same
terms and conditions governing leaves of absence for other illness or medical disability. Such

12

leave shall not be used for child care, child rearing, or preparation for childbearing, but shall
be limited to those disabilities as set forth above. The length of such disability leave, including
the date on which the leave shall commence and the date on which the duties are to be
resumed, shall be determined by the unit member and the unit member’s physician, provided
that such verification clearly demonstrates to the District that such leave is solely for
pregnancy-related disability purposes. In the event that such doctor's verification does not so
demonstrate to the District, the employee shall obtain another statement from her physician
which verifies that such leave is solely for pregnancy-related disability purposes.

9.4    Child Care Leave:

Unit members may be granted parental child care leave of up to one (1) year for the purpose
of preparing for and caring of a newly bom or newly adopted child.

9.4.1    Such leave is without pay and benefits of any kind, including, but not limited to, any
paid leaves.

9.4.2    Request for such leave must be made at least thirty (30) work days (except in the
event of an unforeseen circumstance) prior to the requested beginning date in the case
of a newly bom child and as far in advance as possible in the case of an adoption. In
the event of such unforeseen circumstance, the leave request shall be submitted as
soon as possible prior to the leave. The beginning date and duration of such leave
shall be at the discretion of the Board of Trustees.

9.5    Industrial Accident and Illness Leave:

Unit members shall be eligible for leave of absence because of industrial accident or illness
which the District's industrial accident insurance carrier considers a valid claim. Allowable
leaves shall be for not more than sixty (60) service days in any one (1) fiscal year for the same
accident and shall commence with the first (1st) day of absence.

9.5.1    Leave of absence under this provision shall not be accumulated from year to year.
When the Industrial Accident or Illness Leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the
employee shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due him/her for the
same illness or injury.

9.5.2    Employees shall be paid such portion of the salary due them for any month in which
absence occurs, as when added to the temporary disability indemnity under the
California Labor Code, will result in payment to them of not more than their full
salaries.

9.5.3    Leave of absence applied for under this provision shall be reduced by one (1) day for
each day of authorized absence, regardless of a temporary disability indemnity award
to the employee.

9.5.4    Industrial Illness and Accident Leave is to be used in lieu of Sick Leave. When

13

entitlement to Industrial Illness and Accident Leave has been exhausted, entitlement to
Sick Leave shall then be used. If an employee is receiving a temporary disability
indemnity, the employee shall be entitled to utilize only so much of his or her sick
leave and vacation leave which, when added to his/her temporary disability indemnity,
will result in a payment to him/her of no more than a full day's wage or salary. The
Employer in turn shall issue the appropriate salary warrants for payment of salary and
shall deduct normal retirement and other authorized deductions.

9.5.5    Unless travel outside of California is authorized by the District, employees receiving
benefits under the policy during a period of illness or injury shall remain in the State of
California.

9.5.6    The District may require a written statement from a physician verifying an employee's
absence under this leave and his/her ability to return to work.

9.6    Bereavement Leave:

9.6.1 A unit member shall be entitled to a maximum of three (3) days (or up to five (5) days
if travel out-of-state or more than two hundred fifty (250) miles one way is required)
leave of absence without loss of salary for the death of any member of his/her
immediate family.

9.6.2    Member of the "Immediate Family" is defined as the mother, father, grandmother,
grandfather, or a grandchild of the employee or of the spouse of the employee, and
the spouse, son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law, brother, or sister of the
employee, or anyone living in the immediate household of the employee.

9.7    Jury Duty:

Unit members are entitled to leave to serve on a jury.

9.7.1    Employees receiving notice to appear for jury duty will confer as soon as possible
with the immediate supervisor.

9.7.2    When an employee is granted leave for jury duty, he/she shall endorse any juror's fees,
excluding mileage, parking, and meal allowance to the District.

9.7.3    In the event that the employee is excused from jury duty prior to the last half of
his/her work day, the employee shall return to duty.

9.8    Personal Leave:

9.8.1 Personal Necessity:

Up to seven (7) days of sick leave per year may be used by the bargaining unit

14

member at his/her election for any of the following reasons:

a.    Death or serious illness of a member of his/her immediate family when
additional leave is required beyond that provided under Bereavement Leave.

b.    Accident involving the person or property of the employee, or the person or
property of a member of the immediate family.

9.8.2    Personal Leave:

Bargaining unit members shall be allowed up to three (3) days per year for personal
leave. During such leave, the cost of the substitute shall be deducted from the
bargaining unit member’s salary or the bargaining unit member may request that the
leave be subtracted from his/her sick leave.

Personal leave may be extended at the discretion of the Superintendent. Such
extended leave shall be charged to sick leave. Each request shall be considered on its
own merits, and the approval or denial of the leave shall not set a precedent for other
requests.

9.8.3    All requests for Personal Necessity Leave and Personal Leave shall be in writing and
should be presented to the Superintendent at least five (5) days before the date of the
leave, except in the event of unforeseen circumstances. In the event of an unforeseen
circumstance, the leave request shall be submitted as far in advance as possible.
Requests shall state the period of time to be absent and personal necessity requests
must state the nature of the personal necessity.

9.8.4    Employees shall not be required to secure advance permission for leave taken for: (1)
death or serious illness of a member of his/her immediate family; (2) accident,
involving his/her person or property, or the person or property of a member ofhis/her
family. Employees utilizing this option must notify the District Office and arrange for
appropriate substitutes.

9.8.5    "Member of the Immediate Family" shall be as defined in the Bereavement Leave
provision of this Agreement.

9.9 Association Leave:

9.9.1 The association is entitled to one (1) day of paid leave per school year for the purpose
of conducting Association business, subject to the following conditions:

a.    Such leave is non-accumulative.

b.    Except by mutual agreement between the District and the Association, such
leave must be utilized on a full day basis.

c.    The Association shall notify the Superintendent /Principal at least three (3)

15

working days in advance.

d.    Such leave is contingent upon the District being able to secure an acceptable
substitute employee.

e.    The Association shall reimburse the District for the cost of any substitute
employed to replace persons utilizing this leave.

9.10 Leave of Absence Without Pay:

9.10.1 Unit members may request a leave of absence without pay for a period of up to one
year.

9.10.2    The Superintendent may approve such leaves of up to ten (10) working days. I .eaves
of greater than ten (10) working days require the approval of the Board. Requests for
leave may be based on illness, or personal reasons.

9.10.3    Employees requesting such leaves shall present a letter to the Superintendent
indicating the reasons for the request. A request for an extended leave shall be
submitted before March 15 of the year prior to the leave. Exceptions to the March 15
deadline may be granted by the Superintendent for unforeseen circumstances.

9.10.4    The Board of Trustees may grant up to one year's leave. The Board may extend such
leave up to one (1) additional year.

9.10.5    A unit member on unpaid leave shall not receive a year's service credit on the salary
schedule.

ARTICLE 10 - COMPENSATION

10.1    Wages:

Employees covered by this Agreement shall be paid wages according the attached salary
schedules in Appendix "A".

The annual salaries set forth in this agreement shall be paid in eleven monthly installments,
with the first installment for the fiscal year to be paid on the last working day of August. If an
employee’s employment terminates before the end of the fiscal year and the employee has
been overpaid, any overpayment shall be withheld from the employee’s final paycheck.

10.2    Insurance Benefits:

10.2.1    Each full-time employee shall be eligible to receive medical, dental, vision, salary
protection, and life as listed in Articles 10.2.2-10.2.6. Except as otherwise provided
below and as provided in Appendix "B", part-time employees who work at least half
time shall be eligible to receive a pro-rata premium contribution for the following
coverages. Notwithstanding the foregoing, however, a part-time employee may elect
to participate in less than all insurance benefits and apply the District's unused
premium allocation to the benefits elected up to their full premium cost. If the
District's premium allocation exceeds the premium cost of the elected benefit, there
will be no additional cash payment to the employee. Employees who work less than
half-time are not eligible to receive any District insurance contribution or coverage.

10.2.2    The District agrees to contribute $8,280 annually ($690 monthly) for each eligible
bargaining unit member for the cost of Shasta Trinity Schools Insurance Group health
benefits. Unit members shall choose a medical plan from the tiered active rates.
Excess premium benefits will be paid to the member employees on their payroll
checks. Employees may participate in an IRS 125 plan for out-of-pocket premium
costs of health and welfare benefits.

10.2.3    Delta Dental plan with orthodontia for eligible employees and covered dependents.

10.2.4    Vision Service Plan "C"; $15.00 deductible for eligible employees and covered
dependents.

10.2.5    The Standard Salary Band Salary Protection Insurance for eligible employees.

10.2.6    The Standard $25,000 Flat Rate Benefit life insurance plan (up to age 70) for eligible
employees.

An employee on an unpaid leave of absence may participate at the employee's option and
expense in the above insurance coverages during the period of the leave, subject to the

10.3 Retiree Health and Welfare Benefits

Effective July 1, 2005, eligible retirees will receive $550 per month for 10 years or until age
65, which ever occurs first, toward the cost of health and welfare benefits.

Eligibility Requirements

To participate in this program, an employee must:

a.    Be a member of the certificated bargaining unit.

b.    Have been a permanent certificated employee of the District for the equivalent often
(10) years immediately prior to such retirement. Equivalency can be obtained by:

(1)    Full-time service

(2)    Combination of part-time and full-time service equaling ten (10) full years of
service

(3)    Part-time service equaling ten (10) full years

Leave without pay shall not be considered in the calculation of the ten (10) year service
requirement, but such leave shall not constitute a break in service.

Paid sick leave shall be calculated toward the satisfaction of the ten (10) year service
requirement.

Extended sick leave (beyond the paid sick leave time) shall not be used in the calculation of
the ten (10) year service requirement, but shall not constitute a break in service.

Bargaining unit members eligible for disability retirement shall not be eligible to participate in
this program.

Bargaining unit member acceptance of this program is irrevocable. Further, it is understood
and agreed by the parties that this program is for the benefit of bargaining unit members who
wish to retire from active employment with the District and, therefore, the bargaining unit
member is responsible to ascertain the provisions and coverages of the various retirement
plans without assistance from the District. It is also understood and agreed by the parties that
the District shall have no responsibility other than as set forth herein, particularly with regard
to ascertaining the specifics of the various retirement plans available to retiring bargaining unit
members who wish to take advantage of this program.

Procedures

Any bargaining unit member wishing to avail himself/herself of this program shall, prior to
April 15th, write a letter of request to the Superintendent. This letter shall outline the facts
relative to having met the eligibility requirements of the program.

Having been found qualified for participation in this program, the benefits shall commence
July 1 st. There shall be no gap in coverage.

10.4    Automobile Mileage Expense Reimbursement:

Effective as of the date of ratification of this Agreement, a unit member who is authorized in
advance by the supervisor to use his/her personal automobile in the performance of duties
shall be reimbursed at the current IRS rate per mile. To be eligible for such mileage
reimbursement, an employee must follow District approval and claim procedures.

10.5    Personal Property Loss Reimbursement:

10.5.1 A unit member who is authorized by the District to use his/her own personal property
in the classroom as part of the institutional program shall be reimbursed for lost,
damaged, or stolen items, subject to the following provisions:

a.    Such reimbursement shall apply only to items included on a current inventory
approved by the Superintendent/Principal.

b.    The reimbursement shall be limited to the replacement value as approved by
the Superintendent/Principal on the above current inventory, less any
insurance reimbursement received by the employee.

c.    No reimbursement shall be provided for items lost, damaged, or stolen due to
negligence on the part of the employee.

APPENDIX A - Certificated Salary Schedule

1. General Provisions Applicable to Salary Schedules

1.1    In establishing the placement of a new certificated employee, credit for public school
certificated experience prior to employment by the Igo-Ono-Platina Union School
District will be recognized on a year-for-year basis for up to seven (7) years.

1.2    Only graduate or upper division semester units which were taken after the date the
Bachelor's Degree is received from an accredited college or university shall be
counted for initial salary placement.

1.3    An employee shall advance one step annually upon completion of one academic year
of paid certificated experience subject to the following conditions:

a.    Step advancement credit each year shall be limited to the maximum number of
steps on the certificated salary schedule.

b.    An employee shall be credited with an academic year of certificated service
whenever the employee has been employed on a regular contract and is in a
paid status in the District for a minimum of 135 teaching days during the
preceding school year.

1.4    College units must be upper division or graduate level courses. Lower division units
may apply toward class advancement on the salary schedule only if the Superintendent
determines them to be of value to the present or a planned assignment and approved
them in advance. In the event that the Superintendent does not approve a course, the
employee may appeal the decision in writing within ten (10) work days to a review
committee. The committee shall consist of the Superintendent or designee and two
District teachers appointed by the Association. After review of the matter, the
committee shall by majority vote make a final and binding decision to approve the
course or not.

1.5    Transcripts, official grade cards, or verification of completion of courses, and District
intent forms containing evidence of units earned shall be turned in to the District
office on or before September 10. In the event the above documents are not available
before September 10, a temporary certificate may be submitted by the teacher. This
certificate will be a personal letter which indicates the title of the course, the grade,
the number of units earned, the name of the school, the date attended, and the
instructor's name. This letter shall be accepted by the District Office as a true
statement until the arrival of the required documents.

1.6    Placement and advancement on the salary schedule shall be determined by semester
units. For the purpose of converting quarter units to semester units, each quarter unit
shall constitute 2/3 of one semester unit.
