ARTICLE I
RECOGNITION

The Hueneme Elementary School District, (hereinafter “District”) recognizes the Hueneme
Education Association/California Teachers Association/National Education Association
(hereinafter “Association”) as the exclusive representative of all certificated unit members,
(hereinafter “unit member(s)”) of the District, serving under regular contract, for the purpose of
meeting and negotiating. The parties agree that excluded from such recognition are
management, confidential, and supervisory unit members, as defined in the Education
Employment Relations Act.

ARTICLE 2
DEFINITIONS

A.    “Day” shall mean any day in which the District Administration Office is open for
business.

B.    “School Day” shall mean any day during which students are required by the District to
attend school.

C.    “Work Day” shall mean any day unit members are required to be on duty.

D.    “Seniority” shall be defined as a unit member’s length of continuous service to the
District. Continuous service shall not be broken by a paid leave. Continuous service
shall not be broken by an approved unpaid leave. Unit members who are tied in
seniority shall have the tie broken by lot.

ARTICLE 3
EMPLOYEE RIGHTS

A.    The District shall not treat unit members in a manner that is arbitrary, capricious, or
discriminatory.

B.    The District shall not discriminate against a unit member on the basis of race, color,
creed, age, sex, national origin, political affiliation, physical handicap, or membership in
an employee organization.

C.    A unit member will have ten (10) work days to withdraw a letter of resignation prior to it
being accepted by official action of the Board of Trustees.

D.    Unit member shall have the same rights as citizens to address the Board. The
procedures are set forth in Board Bylaws 9322 and 9323, Oral Communications to the
Board. 1

1

E.    Paraprofessionals

1.    The District may assign a paid Paraprofessional to a certificated employee. The
unit member shall be involved in the interview and selection process, except in
cases of lateral transfer (i.e., same job, different location). If the teacher involved
has expressed a legal and compelling reason not to place the applicant in the
teacher’s classroom, such reason shall be given due consideration by the
District.

2.    The District may assign or the unit may recruit non-paid volunteer teacher
assistants in accordance with above.

3.    All Paraprofessional shall be under the direction of a certificated person.
Instructional decision making, including planning and evaluation of materials and
students, shall be limited to certificated personnel.

4.    The unit member may request and the principal shall convene a meeting of the
unit member, paraprofessional, and principal or coordinator to discuss any issues
relating to the work situation.

5.    Paraprofessionals shall not be used in place of a unit member, except as
provided by law.

F.    Except in cases of emergencies, regular classroom teachers shall not have the primary
responsibility for performing necessary and required medical procedures, such as
catheterization, suction, injection, gavage feeding and draining.

G.    Except in cases of late or mid-year enrollment, the District will provide the regular
classroom teacher with notice in advance of placement in the classroom of any pupil
legally identified as severely handicapped in order to assist in preparation and planning
for the pupil.

H.    Upon request of a regular classroom teacher, the District administration will conduct an
analysis of the impact upon class size of the inclusion full time of a pupil legally
identified as severely handicapped. Such analysis will involve instructional workload,
physical space limitations, and overall classroom behavior.

I.    During the period of the agreement, the District will maintain Board Policy 4040 -
Employee Use of Technology that provides unit members with information on
appropriate uses of District-provided technology and Internet access.

ARTICLE 4
HOURS

A. The maximum length of the unit member’s work day shall be 7 % hours, including lunch
and breaks.

2

1.    There shall be a minimum of 45 minutes duty-free lunch period for each unit
member except on rainy days when it may be reduced to a minimum of 30
minutes. On any day that a rainy day schedule has been implemented at a
school site, requiring teachers to shorten lunch and recess schedules, the
teacher will be allowed to leave at the close of the site instructional day.

instructional day when personal necessity matters (such as:    medical,    dental, legal

appointments, insurance claims, family emergencies, school, and Association business)

the District.

ARTICLE 6
TRANSFERS

Transfer refers to the movement of a tenured unit member between schools or between grade
levels within the school. There are two (2) types of transfer: voluntary and involuntary. The
purpose of the outlined transfer procedures is to resolve transfers with the least amount of
involuntary movement.

A. Definitions

1.    Voluntary Transfer shall include any transfer where a unit member, at their own
discretion has elected to apply to an open position at the school site or within the
District during the designated Transfer Rounds.

2.    Involuntary Transfer shall include the following:

a.    Position Closure exists when an individual unit member’s position is closed
or redesignated, requiring the involuntary transfer of a unit member to
another position.

b.    School Closure exists when a school is closed.

c.    Reassignments exist when changes in enrollment or programming may
require the reorganization of the placement of unit members within a school
or the district.

5

d. Involuntary Transfer for cause may be initiated under exceptional
circumstances. It is the intent of the District to limit these transfers after
other remedies have failed. The unit member will be notified in writing at
least 15 days prior to the transfer. The notice shall include a statement of
reason for the transfer. The unit member may request a meeting with the
Superintendent to discuss the transfer. Involuntary transfers for cause shall
not be arbitrary, capricious or discriminatory. Unit members who have been
involuntarily transferred for cause shall not receive priority in the transfer
process.

3. Transfer Cycle refers to the duration of time from the initial in-school transfers
through the completion of round 2.

B. Procedures

1.    Notice of vacancies shall be posted on the Association bulletin board, included in
the school site bulletin, when possible announced in a faculty meeting, and
forwarded to the Association at the time they are posted.

2.    In-school vacancies shall include the grade and/or subject(s), as well as the
deadline for application.

3.    District vacancies, after in-school changes have been made, district vacancies
shall include the position description, location(s), grade/subject(s), and credential
requirement (as applicable).

4.    Vacancies after completion of District transfer rounds need not follow posting nor
transfer clause. The position shall be subject to posting and transfer during the
next transfer cycle.

5.    The District Personnel Office shall assemble all applications received within the
window period and develop a list of teachers who have applied for each opening,
in order of District seniority. The lists shall be distributed to principals and HEA
representatives. Principals shall utilize the appropriate list to contact teachers
regarding the transfer. Transfer selection will

continue to be based upon appropriate credentials and District seniority. Once
contacted by phone and email, teachers must decide whether or not to accept a
transfer opportunity within two (2) school days. The decision to accept or decline
the position is binding. If the opportunity is declined, the principal will contact the
next teacher on the list. The process is repeated until the opening is filled, or all
the teachers on the list have declined the opening.

6.    Teachers who apply for, and do not receive a transfer shall be given the reasons
in writing upon request.

C.    Voluntary Transfer Procedures

1.    In-School Vacancies:

a.    Initial projected vacancies at each school site shall be posted no later than
the second Monday in March, and closed the last Friday in March.
Teachers may apply for in-school vacancies directly to the principal using
the District Voluntary Transfer Application Form (Appendix B), and shall
have two days from the posting to accept the position. If more than one
unit member applies for the same vacancy, the unit member with the
greatest District seniority shall be granted the position, providing the unit
member possesses the required credential. Principals shall maintain a file
of all in-school vacancy applications.

b.    In-school vacancies resulting from reassignments or the District transfer
process in Rounds I and II, shall be posted and announced at the school
site as they occur and granted to the unit member at that site with the
greatest District seniority.

2.    District Vacancies:

a.    Round one (1) of the District transfer process shall begin on the Monday
prior to spring vacation. Application deadline is the Friday prior to spring
vacation. It is intended that all round one (1) positions be filled and the
round closed on the second Friday following Spring vacation. Round two
(2) shall begin on the second Monday of May. Application deadline is the
following Friday. It is intended that all round two (2) positions be filled and
the round closed on the fourth Friday of May. There will be a one week
period following each round to allow in-school positions to be filled.

b.    Forms are available from the School Office Manager, the Personnel
Office, and in Appendix B. A teacher may apply for more than one
opening. Teachers shall receive a written verification of receipt of the
application form from the District.

unit member for transfer.

ARTICLE 8
PERSONNEL FILES

A.    Unit members may review materials contained in their personnel files at any time during
normal business hours outside of instructional time.

B.    Such material is not to include ratings, reports, or records which:

1.    Were obtained prior to the employment of the person involved.

2.    Were prepared by identifiable examination committee members.

3.    Were obtained in connection with a promotional examination.

C.    Information of a derogatory nature, except material mentioned in Section B above, shall
not be entered or filed unless and until the unit member is given notice and an
opportunity to review and comment thereon. Unit members shall have the right to enter
and have attached to any such derogatory material their own comments.

D.    The person who causes material to be placed in a file shall sign and date the material.

E.    A unit member may authorize, in writing, his/her representative to review the material in
his/her personnel files. If material is proven to be false, it shall be removed upon
request of the unit member.

13

F.    Personnel files mean those files kept in the District Office.

G.    Any derogatory material kept in the unit member’s file in the principal’s office must either
be used in the unit member’s evaluation, entered into the unit member’s District file or
be removed from the principal’s file at the conclusion of the evaluation. The principal’s
file may be reviewed upon the request of the unit member during the last three (3)
weeks of each school year. The unit member shall have the right to enter and have
attached to any derogatory material his/her own comments.

H.    Procedure for Review of Files

1.    The personnel files in the District Office are in the custody of the Superintendent
or designee. They are only open to District personnel on a need-to-know basis.

2.    Unit members or unit member representatives with written authorization signed
by the unit member may review their files by the following procedure:

a.    Call the Personnel Office at least 24 hours prior to the time the materials
are to be reviewed. This will allow time to make the file available and for
the material listed in “B” above to be removed.

b.    The personnel file may not be removed from the office by the unit member
or the representative as it is the property of the school district.

c.    Upon written request by the unit member or the unit member’s
representative, material in the file will be copied by the District. Within two
(2) days of the request, the material will be available for pickup at the
office. The cost of copies shall be 50 per page after the first three (3)
pages.

d.    Other than the unit members, the District shall keep a log indicating the
persons who have examined a personnel file, as well as the dates such
requests were made. Such log shall be available for examination by the
unit member or his/her authorized Association representative.
Authorization must be obtained from either the superintendent or the
administrator in custody of the files. The contents of all personnel files
shall be kept in the strictest confidence by the District.

I. Materials not in the unit member’s District file may not be used against a unit member
unless the incident occurred within thirty (30) working days prior to notification to the
unit member.

ARTICLE 9
EVALUATION

The purpose of unit member’s evaluation and of unit member’s evaluation conference

are:

1.    To help maintain high standards within the District and thus guarantee the best
possible educational opportunity for the children of the Hueneme School District.

2.    To establish a policy upon which the Board, administration, and unit members
can work together with confidence, solidarity, and sincerity.

3.    To insure due process and protect the rights of unit members.

General Guidelines

1.    Permanent unit members shall be evaluated once every two years. Permanent
unit members shall not be evaluated more frequently than every other year
unless their last evaluation was unsatisfactory. An unsatisfactory evaluation shall
result in carryover evaluation the subsequent year, but such evaluation will be
limited to evaluation areas that were rated unsatisfactory.

2.    Extension of the interval used for evaluation of permanent unit members will be
five (5) years if the following conditions exist:

a.    Is a certificated employee who has been employed with the Hueneme
School District for at least 10 years.

b.    Is “Highly Qualified” as defined under ESEA/NCLB.

c.    Meets standards in the previous evaluation

3.    Prior to October 15 in the school year in which an evaluation is to be conducted,
the evaluator shall conduct a pre-evaluation conference with each unit member
to be evaluated.

4.    The evaluation procedure for permanent unit members shall, at the option of the
unit member, be completed prior to February 15. The evaluation procedure for
other permanent unit members made pursuant to this Article shall be completed
and transmitted to the evaluatee sixty (60) calendar days before the last school
day of each school year in which the evaluation takes place.

5.    For temporary and probationary teachers, the first formal observation shall be
completed by October 25. The second formal observation shall be completed by
February 15. The Teacher Evaluation Report will be completed thirty days
before the last day of school. The evaluation of temporary and probationary
teachers must be completed and transmitted to the evaluate 30 days prior to the
close of the school year.

6.    Evaluations shall be conducted by the school principal, assistant principal, or in
the case of itinerant members, by the designated District administrator.

7.    Evaluations may include a specification of unit member strengths to the extent
that they are known to the evaluator.

8.    The written evaluation of the unit member shall be kept in the unit member’s
personnel file. The personnel file shall be available for examination by the unit
member or his Association representative. The content of all personnel files shall
be kept in strictest confidence, and shall be accessed on a need to know basis.

C. Standard Evaluation Procedures

1.    Temporarv/Probationarv Teachers: Temporary and probationary teachers shall
be evaluated using the HSD Teacher Evaluation Report (Appendix E-1) The
evaluation of the first year probationary and temporary teachers shall focus on
three (3) standards, Standard 1 (Engaging and Supporting All Students in
Learning), Standard 2 (Creating and Maintaining Effective Environments for
Student Learning) and Standard 5 (Assessing Student Learning). The evaluation
of second year probationary and temporary teachers shall focus on Standard 3
(Understanding and Organizing Subject Matter for Student Learning), Standard 4
(Planning Instructional and Designing Learning Experiences for All Students) and
Standard 5 (Assessing Student Learning). In addition, each non-permanent unit
member and his/her evaluator will meet and mutually agree on a plan for
Standard 6 (Plan for Developing as a Professional Educator). The evaluation
form for temporary and probationary teachers is contained in Appendix E-1. A
Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program Consulting Teacher shall be
assigned to every temporary and probationary teacher and shall provide
professional assistance in meeting tenure standards through observation and
conferencing, the teaching of demonstration lessons, etc.

2.    Permanent Teachers: Permanent teachers shall be evaluated using the HSD
Teacher Evaluation Report (Appendix E-1) and shall focus on a teacher selected
indicator from two standards upon which evaluation will be based. If the unit
member and his/her evaluator are unable to agree upon two standards then each
party shall select one standard. In addition, each permanent unit member and
his/her evaluator shall also meet and mutually agree on a plan for Standard 6
(Plan for Developing as a Professional Educator). Standard 6 shall not be
evaluated in any formal observations and shall not be included in the final
evaluation.

3.    Evaluation Observations and Post Observation Conferences

a. There shall not be more than four (4) observations. If the District wishes
more than four (4) observations, the unit members shall be entitled to the

16

presence of the Association Representative during the observation and
post observation conference.

b.    Formal observations shall be no less than fifteen (15) minutes nor more
than sixty (60) minutes in length.

c.    Formal classroom observations shall be preceded by at least a 48-hour
notice that the observation is going to occur.

d.    Within three (3) work days after observation, the evaluator shall schedule
a post observation conference to review the evaluator’s observations with
the unit member.

d. Prior to the formal evaluation summary, all preliminary observation
conferences shall include recommendations, if necessary, as to areas of
improvement in the performance of the evaluatee. In the event the
evaluatee is not performing his/her duties in a satisfactory manner
according to the standards prescribed by the District, the evaluator shall
notify the evaluatee in writing of such fact and describe such
unsatisfactory performance. The District shall thereafter confer with the
evaluatee and shall make concerted efforts to assist the unit member to
correct cited weaknesses, including but not limited to specific
recommendations and adequate release time for the unit member to
observe similar classes in other schools at District expense. If a unit
member’s performance is found unsatisfactory, he/she may receive
necessary demonstrations by the evaluator or PAR Consulting Teacher of
the proper techniques or procedures in question.

4.    The evaluator should not base his/her evaluation of a unit member on any
information which has not been subject to verification by the evaluator.

5.    The final evaluation shall not contain any statement, materials, or evidence of
which the unit member has not been previously made aware.

6.    The evaluation of unit members shall not be included or be based upon the
success or lack thereof of the instructional or clerical assistant in the
performance of tasks assigned by the unit member, except as it may relate to
maintaining professional relationships.

7.    The evaluation and assessment of unit members’ competence pursuant to this
Article shall not include the use of publishers’ norms established by standardized
tests. This provision shall not prohibit unit members from being evaluated on the
progress of pupils on academic content standards as measured by criterion
referenced assessments and required by Education Code Section 44662. The
District shall apply and implement the evaluation provisions contained herein in
an equitable and reasonable fashion.

17

8.    The written evaluation shall be completed and signed by the evaluator before it is
signed by the unit member.

9.    The unit member shall be allowed to affix to the written evaluation a rebuttal of
any portion of the evaluation he/she feels inaccurate, unfair, or incomplete.

LEAVES

A.    The benefits provided unit members by Sections 44962 through 44985, 44800, and

B.    Sick Leave

Each full-time unit member is entitled to ten (10) days of paid sick leave per school year
for illness, disability, pregnancy, pregnancy-related disability, or injury. A unit member
who is employed less than full time is entitled to a proportionate amount of sick leave,
per his/her contract.

1.    Unused sick leave shall accrue without limit from school year to school year.

2.    Absence for pregnancy is treated as any other sick leave. A physician’s
verification is necessary thirty (30) calendar days prior to the expected leave so
that a competent substitute may be provided. Sick leave arising from pregnancy
shall commence at the time the unit member’s physician certifies that she is no
longer physically able to perform her regular duties.

a.    A pregnant unit member, at her option, may use up to eight (8)
consecutive calendar weeks of sick leave, not to include winter or spring
break. The presumption is that the unit member is physically unable to
teach approximately two (2) calendar weeks before expected delivery; as
determined by the employee’s physician, and six (6) calendar weeks after
expected delivery. If this presumption is insufficient to cover periods of
actual illness, additional sick leave shall be provided if verified by the
physician.

b.    A unit member may utilize only sick leave for absence related to childbirth
or may use a combination of sick leave and child rearing leave.

20

3.    The District may require a physician’s certification if a unit member is absent for
five (5) or more consecutive days stating that:

a.    The unit member is unable to perform duties and should remain on leave;
or

b.    The unit member was unable to perform duties but now may return to
duty.

4.    The District shall periodically provide each unit member with a written statement
of:

a.    His/her accrued sick leave total.

b.    His/her sick leave entitlement for the school year.

5.    Sick Leave Donation Program

Permanent unit members who are experiencing long-term or catastrophic illness,
and have exhausted fully paid sick leave may request direct donations of
accumulated, unused sick leave from District permanent, certificated and
classified employees. The eligibility of temporary or probationary unit members
to participate in the program shall be evaluated by the parties on a case-by-case
basis.

a.    Unit members wishing to receive donations of sick leave shall provide the
District with a written request to participate in the program. The request
shall include a description of the illness necessitating the donation, and an
estimate of the time period required for recovery. A verification of the
information in the request from the unit member’s physician shall be
attached. Family members or Association representatives may draft a
request in cases where illness prevents the unit members from completing
a request. Illness associated with normal, uncomplicated pregnancy and
delivery shall not constitute a long-term illness for the purpose of this
program.

b.    The District shall publish and post a request for donation notice at each
District work site, and the request shall be announced at a faculty meeting.
The notice shall specify a window period for receipt of donations of fifteen
(15) work days from the date the notice is published. The notice shall
include the name of the affected unit member and the nature of their
illness only if verbal authorization is received directly from the unit
member. As an alternative to the posted request procedure, a unit
member may notify the District of unit members/employees who have

21

offered to donate sick leave to them, and the District will verify the
information and transfer the sick leave pursuant to program guidelines and
pursuant to program guidelines and restrictions.

c.    Employees may donate a maximum equivalent of five (5) days of sick
leave for the affected unit member. Certificated employees may donate
sick leave to other certificated employees in not less than one-half (0.5)
day increments. Certificated employees may donate sick leave to
classified employees in not less than one-half (0.5) day increments.
Classified employees may donate sick leave to certificated employees in
not less than four (4) hour increments. Certificated employees may not
donate sick leave if, as a result of the donation, their balance of
accumulated sick leave falls below fifteen (15) days. Donations of sick
leave shall be submitted to the District Personnel Office on a form
provided by the District. Unit members may receive up to a maximum of
one hundred (100) days of donated sick leave. The one hundred day limit
may be exceeded in exceptional circumstances. Donations shall be
transferred to the leave balance of the unit member requesting the
donation on a day-to-day basis, regardless of the daily salary of the
employees donating and receiving donations. The District shall provide
certificated employees donating leave with a verification of sick leave
hours transferred to the affected unit member. Any donated sick leave not
utilized by the affected unit member at the conclusion of the illness shall
be returned to the sick leave balance of the certificated employees who
provided donations on a prorated basis.

d.    Donated sick leave shall be treated as regular, accumulated sick leave for
affected unit members pursuant to collective bargaining agreements and
Board Policy governing the use of sick leave, however; it is the intent of
the parties that the sick leave donation program serve as a safety net for
unit members experiencing long-term or catastrophic illness as opposed to
frequent day-to-day absences. Donated days of sick leave shall be
utilized prior to, but not in addition to the use of extended illness provisions
detailed in Article ll-D. Donated sick days may not be used for personal
necessity pursuant to Article ll-F.

e.    Unit members may utilize accrued sick leave to attend to the needs of a
child or spouse who is experiencing a serious, potentially terminal illness.
Unit members who wish to utilize sick leave for this purpose must submit a
written request to the Superintendent or designee. The request must
include written verification to the child/spouse’s physical condition from an
attending physician. Unit members who have exhausted all accrued sick
leave may access the sick leave donation program to attend to the needs
of a seriously ill child/spouse.

C. Child Rearing Leave

Child Rearing Leave is leave granted upon termination of sick leave arising from
pregnancy or for the adoption of a child pursuant to the provisions of the California
Family Leave Act.

1.    Compensation

a.    Child Rearing Leave is leave without pay.

b.    No advancement on salary schedule will be granted while on this type of
leave unless the unit member has taught seventy-five percent (75%) of the
year.

2.    Length of Leave

a.    Child Rearing Leave may be either one, two, or three trimesters in length.
A one trimester leave is first granted and the next following trimester or
trimesters.

b.    A two or three trimester leave shall equate to the balance of the trimester
in which the child rearing leave is first granted and the next following
trimester or trimesters.

c.    The unit member has the option of taking a child rearing leave of one or
two trimesters as defined above. A child rearing leave of three trimesters
may be granted by the District.

3. Return to Service

a.    The unit member shall give written notice of intention to return to work
thirty (30) calendar days prior to the expiration of the leave.

b.    If a unit member, on child rearing leave, returns at the end of either the
first or second trimester of the leave, he/she shall have the right to be
returned to the same assignment occupied prior to the leave, except in
exceptional circumstances.

c.    If a unit member on child rearing leave returns at the end of the third
trimester of the leave, he/she shall have the same return rights as other
unit members returning from other types of leaves.

23

4.    Form of Request

Applicants for child rearing leave shall file a written request with the
superintendent, on the form provided by the District, at least sixty (60) days prior
to the beginning of the period for which child rearing leave is requested.

5.    Vacancies

Vacancies created by a child rearing leave of one or two trimesters shall not be
considered vacancies for purposes of the transfer article.

6.    Immediate Return    to Duty

If a unit member is on leave for childbearing or child rearing and in the event of a
miscarriage or death of a child subsequent to childbirth, or other change of
status, the unit member may request an immediate assignment to a unit position.
If there is a vacancy for which a unit member is qualified, at the sole judgment of
the superintendent or the superintendent’s designee, the District will assign the
unit member to a position as soon as a position is available.

D.    Family Illness Leave

1.    In any school year, unit members may utilize up to five (5) days of accrued sick
leave to attend to an illness/hospitalization of the unit member’s child, parent or
spouse. The District may request medical verification of the family member’s
condition for unit member absences of fie (5) or more consecutive days.

2.    Serious. Potentially Terminal Family Illness

In addition to the leave provided in Section B-6, unit members may utilize
accrued sick leave to attend to the needs of a child or spouse who is
experiencing a serious, potentially terminal illness. Unit members who wish to
utilize sick leave for this purpose must submit a written request to the Assistant
Superintendent, Human Resources. The request must include written verification
of the child/spouse’s physical condition from an attending physician. Unit
members who have exhausted all accrued sick leave may access the sick leave
donation program to attend to the needs of a seriously ill child/spouse.

E.    Extended Illness Leave

1.    Extended Illness Leave shall be governed by applicable provisions of the
Education Code, including Education Codes 44977 and 44978.1.

2.    When a unit member has exhausted all available sick leave, including
accumulated sick leave, and continues to be absent from duty on account of
illness or accident, whether or not the absence arises out of or in the course of

24

employment, the unit member shall receive an additional five (5) months of
extended illness leave. Unit members on extended illness leave receive
differential pay, i.e., their regular contract salary reduced by a deduction for the
cost of the substitute employed to replace them. A unit member shall not be
provided more than one five (5) month extended illness leave per illness or
accident. However, if the school year terminates before the five (5) month period
is exhausted, the employee may use the balance of available leave in the
subsequent school year. Extended illness leave is for long-term illness, and is
not available for day to day absences. The District shall require a physician’s
verification for absence during this leave.

4. When a unit member is not medically able to resume their duties at the end of the
five (5) month extended illness leave, if not placed in another position, they shall
be placed on a re-employment list for a period of 24 months (probationary
employee) or 39 months (permanent employee). During this 24 or 39 month
period, if the unit member is medically able to return to duty as verified by a
physician, they shall be returned to employment in the first available position for
which they are credentialed and qualified.

Shall be in compliance with the Education Code 44984. The following summary of

Education Code 44984 is provided for information purposes only and is not meant to be

entitled to only the amount of unused leave due to him/her for the same illness or
injury.

1.    At the election of the unit member, up to ten (10) days of accrued sick leave per year
may be used in cases of personal necessity.

2.    Unit members are required to provide written notification prior to taking the leave,
using the appropriate form (See Appendix F), except in the following
emergencies.

a.    Death or serious illness of a member of the immediate family.

b.    Accident, involving the person or property, or the person or property of a
unit member’s immediate family.

3.    This leave may be used only for matters of compelling personal necessity. This

extend District holiday periods.

Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources or designee.

and must include the reason for the leave, regardless of the length of the leave.
The District may grant or deny Personal Business Leave.

4.    Personal Business Leave days are not deducted from the unit member’s sick
leave balance. The regular substitute rate will be deducted from the unit
member’s salary for each day of leave. Absences without prior written approval
will result in salary deduction at the unit member’s daily rate of pay.

K.    Sabbatical Leave

A Sabbatical Leave is leave granted to unit members for professional study, research,
or travel which is directly related to the present or prospective service of the unit
member.

27

1.    Eligibility

The unit member must have completed at least seven (7) years of service to the
District since initial hiring or seven (7) years of service since his/her last

2.    Criteria for Granting Leave

Criteria for the granting of sabbatical leave shall be based on the following
without respect to race, color, creed, or age:

a.    Value of the leave to the individual

b.    Specific needs of the District

c.    Maintenance of a reasonable distribution of applicants by schools.

Sabbatical leave shall count toward retirement, with retirement and annuity
contributions to be deducted from payroll warrants in the usual manner.

a.    The unit member who is on sabbatical leave shall receive fifty percent

b.    Health and Welfare Benefits authorized for regular unit members will be

c.    Deductions in salary placement, as requested by the unit member on

sabbatical leave in accordance with Education Code 44041, will continue
in effect as though    the    unit member were on regular full-time service.

on a form provided by the District. (See Appendix G).

ARTICLE 16
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Definitions

1.    A “grievance” is a claim by a grievant that there has been a violation,
misinterpretation, or misapplication of a provision(s) of this Agreement, which
adversely affects the grievant.

2.    A “grievant” shall be an individual unit member, a group of unit members, or the
Association when it grieves on behalf of specified individuals or regarding an
Association right provided by this Agreement.

Purpose

1.    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative
level, solutions to the claims which may arise and affect the “Terms of this
Agreement.” Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal
and private as may be appropriate at any level of the procedures.

2.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the
time limits specified at each level should be considered to be maximum and
every effort should be made to expedite the process. Failure by the grievant to
meet any time limits constitutes a waiver of the grievance. Failure by the District
to meet any time limits allows the grievant to proceed to the next step. At any
level of the procedure, the time limits may be extended by mutual agreement.

3.    The District’s processing and discussing the merits of an alleged grievance by a
member shall not constitute a waiver by the District of a defense that the dispute
is not grievable.

4.    When it is necessary for a representative designed by the Association to
investigate a grievance, attend a grievance meeting/hearing, or serve as a
witness during the day, he/she shall, upon notice to his/her principal, or
immediate supervisor by the president of the Association, be released without
loss of pay as per Article 13, in order to permit participation in the foregoing
activities.

5.    All documents, communications, and records dealing with the processing of a
grievance shall be filed in a separate grievance file and shall not be kept in the
personnel file of any of the participants. This information shall not be transmitted
to future prospective employers and it shall not be considered in future
promotions within the District.

6.    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by the Superintendent, any member or
representative of the administration, or any other participant in the grievance
procedure by reason of such participation.

38

C. Steps in the Grievance Procedures

1.    Level I

a.    Within thirty (30) work days after the occurrence of the act or omission
giving rise to the grievance, the grievant must present the grievance in
writing on the appropriate District form to his/her immediate supervisor
and the Association. The grievance shall include the date of violation and
the specific section of the Agreement allegedly violated.

b.    This statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
circumstances involved, and the specific remedy sought.

c.    The supervisor shall communicate his decision to the unit member in
writing within ten (10) work days after receiving the grievance. If the
supervisor does not respond within the time limits, the grievant, may
appeal to the next level.

d.    Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal
conference and the grievant may appear with an Association
representative.

2.    Level II

a.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I, the decision may
be appealed within ten (10) work days on the appropriate form to the
superintendent or designee and the Association.

b.    This statement shall include a copy of the original grievance and appeal,
the decisions rendered, and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for
the appeal. Either the grievant or the superintendent may request a
personal conference within the above time limits. The grievant may
appear with an Association representative.

c.    The superintendent or designee shall communicate a decision to the
grievant within ten (10) work days. If the superintendent or designee does
not respond within the time limits provided, the grievant may appeal to the
next level.

3.    Level III

a. In the event that the unit member is not satisfied with the recommendation(s)
of the superintendent, the decision may be appealed in writing within ten (10)
work days to the Board of Trustees and a hearing requested. The unit
member may appear at the hearing with an Association representative.

39

b.    As an alternative to the Level III (a), the grievant may request the
Association to submit the grievance to arbitration. If the Association
occurs with the grievant’s request for arbitration, the Association shall,
within ten (10) work days of the conclusion of level II, submit a written
request for arbitration to the superintendent.

c.    The Association and the District shall attempt to mutually select an
arbitrator. If an arbitrator is not mutually selected within ten (10) work
days, the parties shall request a panel of five (5) arbitrator’s names from
the State Conciliation Service. Parties shall alternately strike names from
the panel. Striking order shall be determined by lot.

d.    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator shall be shared equally by the
District and the Association. All other expenses, including fees and costs
of witnesses and conferees, shall be shared by the party incurring them.

e.    The arbitrator shall have no power to add to, subtract from, or modify the
terms of this Agreement.

f.    The arbitrator’s decision shall be binding on both parties.

ARTICLE 23
JOB SHARING

A.    Definition - “Job Sharing” shall refer to two (2) teachers on regular contracts sharing
one (1) teaching assignment. The job share may be other than a 0.50/0.50 FTE split as
long as the two assignments equal 1.0 FTE (i.e., 0.40/0.60 FTE split).

B.    Two (2) teachers shall share an assignment for a minimum of one (1) year.

D. Only permanent teachers may apply for a job share assignment. If a permanent unit
member wishes to job share, and is unable to locate another permanent unit member to
share an assignment, the unit member wishing to job share may request the District hire
a temporary teacher to job share with the permanent unit member. Job shares between
permanent and temporary teacher will not be approved for more than two years.
Applications for a job share assignment for the following school year shall be filed with
the District no later than April 1 notwithstanding exceptional circumstances. The
application must state the name of the teacher who currently holds the position to be
shared.

D.    All requests for job sharing shall be reviewed by the principal and the superintendent or
designee. The superintendent or his designee shall make a recommendation to the
Board.

E.    The Board shall approve or deny the request and notify, in writing, the personnel office,
principal, and the applicants of its decision.

F.    Notwithstanding other provisions of this Agreement, job sharing teachers’ wages,
benefits, and paid leave shall be prorated relative to the actual time worked. In no event
shall the total amount of health and welfare benefits for the job sharers exceed the
amount the District would have paid the position had not been shared.

46

G.    Job sharing teachers shall receive experience credit for salary schedule advancement
prorated based upon the FTE share. Job sharing teachers must accumulate the
equivalent of at least 75% of a single year’s full time experience credit to advance on
the salary schedule. Such credit may be earned over multiple years (i.e., job share
assignment is 0.30 FTE, experience credit for salary schedule advance occurs after
third year in the job share).

H.    Upon request of the two teachers, a job sharing assignment may be renewed provided
the two teachers notify the District in writing prior to April 1. In the event the two
teachers fail to notify the District of their desire to continue the job sharing assignment,
or in the event the District does not approve the continuance of the assignment, the
teachers shall be returned to full-time assignments.

I.    If a teacher on a regular contract is in a job-sharing assignment and elects to return after the
first year to full-time teaching, the teacher will be returned to his/her original school/position. If a
teacher on a regular contract is in a job sharing assignment for more than one (1) year and
elects to return to full-time teaching, the teacher will be assigned to the first available full¬
time teaching position for which he/she is certified and competent and in conformance
with Article 6 of this Agreement.

J.    The District shall provide interested applicants with a list of criteria which should be
satisfactorily addressed in the application.

ARTICLE 24
SUMMER SCHOOL

A.    The District shall provide unit members with information on summer school employment
opportunities. The information shall include the proposed school calendar, work
schedule, locations, and prorated salary information.

B.    Unit members teaching in the summer school program shall receive their prorated daily
wage rate, based upon the seven (7) hour, fifteen (15) minute (435 minutes) regular
year contract work year. The summer school salary shall be calculated using the
member’s prorated daily wage rate, multiplied by the total number of summer school
work days, which shall include one (1) preparation day.

C.    Summer school employment shall continue to be contingent upon adequate enrollment.

D.    Summer school unit members are entitled to one (1) day of summer school sick leave.
The summer school sick leave day may be used for the illness of the unit member or the
unit member’s spouse or child, or for emergency personal necessity as outlined in
Article 11-G-2-a/b. The unit member shall be paid the difference between his/her daily
rate and the cost of the substitute. Additional absences shall result in full summer
school daily rate deductions. Summer school substitutes shall be compensated at the
regular year, full daily substitute rate. Summer school is a limited term instructional

47

program, generally serving at-risk students. Therefore, continuity of instruction is
critical. The parties agree that summer school teacher attendance is a central
component of that continuity. Summer school unit members who do not use the
summer school sick leave day shall have one (1) day of sick leave added to their
regular accrued sick leave balance.

E.    If more than enough applications are received for summer school employment,
preference shall be given to teachers who did not teach the prior year or years. The
intent is to rotate assignments. The district may use the discretion in placing unit
members in summer school positions which require specialized instructional or
leadership skills. If two or more candidates are judged equal on the basis of rotation
and specialized instructional or leadership skills, then District seniority will apply as tie
breaker.

F.    Summer school teachers will teach a prescribed curriculum using specific instructional
strategies developed by the District.

G.    Teachers employed in the summer school program shall attend summer school training
on the prescribed curriculum and instructional strategies to be utilized in the summer
school program. Unit members shall be compensated for summer school training at
their prorated daily wage rate, paid on a non-contract extra-time basis

H.    Unit members may apply to share a single summer school assignment, provided the
joint application clearly defines the specific days for which each job share is
responsible. Both teachers shall attend summer school training and receive
compensation pursuant to Section G. Summer school job share partners shall be
responsible for ensuring that continuity of instruction is maintained throughout the
summer school term. Job share teachers will not be eligible for summer school sick
leave. In placing job share applicants, the District shall use the “highest” summer
school priority of the two (2) job share partners.

ARTICLE 25

PEER ASSISTANCE AND REVIEW (PAR) PROGRAM

A. Purpose

1.    The Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program allows exemplary teachers to
assist certain permanent and beginning teachers in the areas of subject matter
knowledge, teaching strategies, teaching methods, and classroom management.

2.    The extent of the program’s assistance and review depends on whether the
participating teacher is a beginning teacher, a volunteer permanent teacher, or a
permanent teacher who has received an unsatisfactory evaluation in one or more
areas of teaching methods and/or instruction. The program’s assistance shall be
provided through Consulting Teachers as described in detail in Sections C and

48

D-2 of this document. This assistance shall not involve the participation in, nor
the conducting of the annual evaluation of certificated unit members as set forth
in Article 9 of the Agreement and Education Code 44660, et seq., except for
making available to the evaluator the results of a unit member’s participation in
the Program.

B. Definitions

1.    “Participating Teacher”

A unit member who is a classroom teacher who either volunteers, or is required
by this Agreement to participate in the program.

2.    “Consulting Teacher”

An exemplary teacher selected to provide program assistance to the participating
teachers and who meets the qualifications listed in subsection D.2.a.

3.    “Beginning Teacher”

Any unit member having probationary or temporary status, or any District
teaching intern participating in a program established according to Education
Code Sections 44305, et seq. and 44325, et seq.

4.    “Voluntary Participating Teacher”

Any unit member with permanent status who requests assistance from the
consulting teacher, or any unit member with permanent status whose last
performance evaluation was rated satisfactory and who selects the alternative
evaluation under Section 9-B of the Agreement and who elects to have a
Consulting Teacher assist in that Alternative Evaluation Program.

5.    “Participating Teacher with an Unsatisfactory Evaluation”

A unit member with permanent status whose most recent performance evaluation
contained an unsatisfactory evaluation in one or more areas of subject matter
knowledge, teaching strategies, and/or teaching methods and instruction, as
specifically designated by Article 9, Section A-4-a-d of the Agreement, and
standards “1”, “2”, “3”, and “4” on the Permanent Teacher Evaluation Report
form.

6.    “Joint Panel”

The Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program will be administered by a panel
consisting of seven members, four selected by the Association and three
appointed by the District. A panel member’s term shall be three years, except

the first terms will be determined by lot on the basis of two two-year terms, two

three-year terms, and three four-year terms.

C. Program Outline

1. For Participating Teachers with an Unsatisfactory Evaluation

a.    Any permanent teacher with an unsatisfactory evaluation in one or more
area listed in Section B-5 must participate in the program.

b.    The Consulting Teacher’s assistance and review shall focus on the
specific areas recommended for improvement by the Participating
Teacher’s evaluator after the Participating Teacher receives the
unsatisfactory rating. (Refer to Article 9, C-3-e)

1.    These recommendations shall be written, aligned with student
learning, clearly stated, and consistent with Education Code
Section 44662. These recommendations shall be considered as
the performance goals required by Education Code Sections
44664(a) and 44500(b) (2).

2.    The Principal and Consulting Teachers assigned to the
Participating Teacher shall meet and discuss the recommended
areas of improvement outlined by the Principal and the types of
assistance that should be provided by the Consulting Teacher.

3.    The Consulting Teacher and the evaluating principal are expected
to establish a cooperative relationship and shall coordinate and
align the assistance provided to the Participating Teacher.

c.    The Consulting Teacher and the Participating Teacher shall meet to
discuss the plan for assistance. Thereafter, the Consulting Teacher will
provide assistance, which shall also involve multiple classroom
observations of the Participating Teacher.

d.    Before April 1, the Consulting Teacher shall complete a written report
regarding the teacher’s participation in the program consisting of: (1) a
description of the assistance provided to the Participating Teacher; and (2)
an analysis of the Participating Teacher’s improvement resulting from
assistance in the targeted areas. This report shall be submitted to the
Joint Panel. The Consulting Teacher shall also prepare a written
summary of the assistance provided to the Participating Teacher which
shall be submitted to the Joint Panel for review/approval and then
forwarded to the evaluating principal. The principal may attach the
summary to the Participating Teacher’s evaluation.

50

e.    After reviewing the report, the Joint Panel shall determine whether the
teacher would benefit from continued participation in the Program.

f.    The Joint Panel will make an annual report to the Governing Board and
the Association regarding the impact of the program, improvements to be
made in the program, and any recommendations regarding program
participants, including forwarding the names of the permanent teachers
with unsatisfactory evaluation who, after sustained assistance, would not
benefit from continued participation in the program.

g.    The teacher will continue participating in the program until the Joint Panel
decides that the teacher will not benefit from continuation in the program,
or the teacher receives a satisfactory evaluation, or the teacher is
separated from the District. The evaluating principal and the District have
the sole authority to determine whether the Participating Teacher has
been able to demonstrate satisfactory improvement.

1. The Consulting Teacher’s report, as defined in subsection C-1-d
above, shall be made available to the District for placement in the
personnel file.

2.    For Beginning Teachers

a.    A Consulting Teacher will be assigned to each Beginning Teacher to
provide assistance. In the first year, the Consulting Teacher shall
concentrate the assistance in the area of the District’s Teaching
Standards. In the second year, the Consulting Teacher will focus the
assistance in any areas which may be listed by the evaluating principal as
needing improvement and/or assistance.

b.    The Consulting Teacher and the evaluating principal shall have a
cooperative relationship, and shall coordinate the assistance provided to
the Beginning Teachers.

c.    Because the participation of Beginning Teachers is not legally mandated,
neither the Consulting Teacher nor the Joint Panel will make a written
report or forward the names of any individual beginning teacher who
participates in the program. The Joint Panel will, however, annually report
on the overall effectiveness of the program for beginning teachers.

3.    For Voluntary Participating Teachers

a. Any unit member with permanent status who requests assistance from the
Consulting Teacher, or those teachers participating in an alternative
evaluation program set forth in Section 9-D of the Agreement may utilize

the services of a Consulting Teacher as one option in the alternative
system.

b. Because permanent teachers with satisfactory performances are not
mandated by law to participate in the program, neither the Consulting
Teacher nor the Joint Panel will report on any volunteer participation in the
program. Any reports to the Governing Board or the Joint Panel will be
made only as required by the individual plan. All other reports and
recommendations will be governed by Article 9 of the Agreement.

D. Governance and Program Structure

1. Joint Panel

a.    The Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program will be administered by
a panel consisting of seven members, four selected by the Association
and three appointed by the District. A panel member’s term shall be three
years, except the first terms will be determined by lot on the basis of two
two-year terms, two three-year terms, and three four-year terms.

b.    The panel will make all decisions through consensus in the areas of
appointments, reports and recommendations to the Governing Board and
program plan and budget. Failing consensus, decisions will be made by
majority vote. Five of the seven members will constitute a quorum for
purposes of meeting and conducting business.

c.    The Joint Panel’s primary responsibilities involve establishing the annual
program and budget, and selecting and overseeing the Consulting Teachers.
In addition, the Joint Panel is responsible for:

1.    Submitting to the Governing Board and the Association an annual
evaluation of the program’s impact, including recommendations
regarding Participating Teachers with unsatisfactory evaluations,
and if necessary, forwarding names of individuals who, after
sustained assistance, would not benefit from continued participation
in the program.

2.    Assigning the Consulting Teachers;

3.    Reviewing Consulting Teachers’ reports on Participating Teachers
with permanent status referred to the program because of
unsatisfactory evaluations;

4.    Evaluating the effectiveness of the Consulting Teachers in the role
of Consulting Teacher.

52

5.    Coordinating with the District to provide training for Consulting
Teachers, for panel members, and where appropriate, for
Participating Teachers;

6.    Establishing internal operating procedures and regulations
necessary to carry out the requirements of the Education Code and
this document, including a procedure for selecting the Joint Panel’s
chair.

d. The Panel shall use the following procedure for establishing the annual
program and budget:

1. By May 1 of each fiscal year, the Joint Panel will establish a
program and budget for the succeeding year, which will include:

a.    The estimated state revenues for the program;

b.    The estimated expenditures, involving:

(1.) Projected number of Participating Teachers

(2.) Projected (full and part-time) number of Consulting
Teachers needed to service the projected need.

(3.) Released time for the panel and Consulting Teachers.

(4.)    Pay for Joint Panel members

(5.)    Pay for Consulting Teachers

(6.) Projected training costs

2. Consulting Teacher

a. Minimum qualifications for Consulting Teacher:

1.    A credentialed classroom teacher with permanent status and at
least five years of recent teaching experience.

2.    Demonstrated exemplary teaching ability, as indicated by, among
other things, effective communication skills, subject matter
knowledge, and mastery of a range of teaching strategies
necessary to meet students’ needs in different contexts.

3.    Ability to work cooperatively and effectively with other teachers and
administrators.

53

b.    Consulting Teacher positions shall be filed by the District posting the
position. Each applicant will be required to submit a completed
application, which shall include at least three references from individuals
who have direct knowledge of the applicant’s abilities for the position. A
reference from a principal is preferred, although not necessary. All
applications and references will be treated with confidentiality and will not
be disclosed except as required by law. The Joint Panel will make the
selection, which will be forwarded to the Superintendent. The panel’s
procedures for selecting Consulting Teachers shall include provisions for
classroom observation of Consulting Teacher candidates.

c.    A Consulting Teacher’s term will be three years. A Consulting Teacher
may request to return to regular teaching duties prior to conclusion of the
three-year term, and they will be returned to regular duties at the
beginning of the next school year. The District will return the teacher to
the position held before assuming the Consulting Teacher position. This
guarantee to return to the same position shall not entitle the teacher to any
rights greater than would be attained by remaining in the position.

3. Consulting Teacher shall provide assistance to Participating Teachers in the
areas of subject matter knowledge, teaching strategies, teaching methods, and
classroom management. This assistance may include, but is not limited to the
following activities:

a.    Providing consecutive assistance to improve in the specific areas targeted
by the evaluating principal or the District Teaching Standards

b.    Meeting and consulting with the principal or designee regarding the nature
of the assistance being provided.

c.    Observations of the Participating Teacher during periods of classroom
instruction

d.    Allowing the Participating Teacher to observe the Consulting Teacher or
other selected teachers

e.    Attending specific training in specified teaching techniques or in
designated subject matter

f.    Demonstrating good practices to the Participating Teacher

g.    Maintaining appropriate records of each Participating Teacher’s activities
and progress

E. Other Provisions

1.    Functions performed by unit members under this document shall not constitute

2.    Unit members who perform functions as Consulting Teachers or panel members

TEACHER-ADMINISTRATION LIAISON COMMITTEE

Education has expanded rapidly in the past decade in complexity, diversity, and
technology. All principals’ responsibility for maintaining effective communication with
staff and community is increasingly demanding. In some instances, the size of school
staff has grown to the point where more formalized lines of communication between
staff and building administrator needs to be established. The need for this is generally
recognized by both the building administrator and the staff.

It is characteristic of professionals that they recognize the importance of open channels
of communication in solving their mutual problems, meeting their mutual responsibilities,
and cooperating in many decision-making processes essential to effective school
operation.

In keeping with the above purposes, a Teacher-Administration Liaison Committee may
be established at each school if in the administrator’s and faculty s judgment such a
committee would facilitate the work.

1.    The committee shall be elected by the non-administrative certificated personnel
at each school.

2.    The principal may meet and confer with the Committee upon request on matters
within the scope of their authority.

3.    In conjunction with the principal, the Committee may have the authority to
schedule faculty meetings.

4.    The committee may have the authority to place items on the agenda of the
regular faculty meetings.

5.    The committee may use up to 30 minutes at any faculty meeting to report on
school activities.

6.    Changes in school procedures originated by the building administrator should, if
at all feasible, first be discussed with the committee.

7.    The committee may discuss with the principal other items as determined
necessary by the committee, including, but not limited to, school budget,
equipment, available supplies, etc.

STRS Supplemental Benefit Program
August 2002

The original STRS Defined Benefit Program provides eligible California certificated
employees with a monthly retirement allowance, calculated based on the employee’s
age at retirement years of service and highest 12 month's salary (final compensation).
The employee and employer make contributions to the STRS Defined Benefit Program
based upon a percentage of the employees' annual contract salary, In the past,
additional income earned from extra duty assignments/stipends, for example, hourly
extra time pay or summer school pay were not subject to STRS contributions, but did
not enhance future retirement benefits.

Assembly Bill 2700 established the STRS Defined Benefit Supplemental (Supplemental)
Program. The STRS Supplemental Program provides full time, eligible California
certificated employees with an additional retirement allowance, above and beyond the
regular Defined Benefit Program allowance, based upon employee and employer
contributions from income earned from extra duty assignments For less than full time
employees, the Supplemental Program provides an opportunity for extra duty pay to be
applied toward full time service credit in the Defined Benefit Program, up to 100% of full
time rt part time contract salary and extra duty pay equal 100% of a teacher's full time
contract salary. The Supplemental Program allowance is paid as a lump sum
distribution at the time of retirement or a monthly annuity for a fixed period of years
following retirement, based upon total employer and employee contributions to the
Supplemental Program during active employment.

In May 2002, representatives of the Hueneme Elementary School District and the
Hueneme Education Association met to negotiate the impact of AB 2700. The parties
utilized a summary of the provisions of AB 2700 developed by the Ventura County
Superintendent of Schools Office to categonze the types of extra duty pay and other
non salary compensation available to HEA unit members, and determine whether
District/unit member STRS contributions would be directed to the Defined Benefit or
Supplemental Program. A copy of the summary, Compensation Creditable Under AB
2700 Summary is attached to this agreement. The summary refers to the Defined
Benefits Program as the DB account, and the Defined Benefits Supplemental Program
as the DBS account. The summary categorized nine (9) different types of extra duty
and non salary compensation, examples of the category, and whether STRS
contributions from the extra duty pay are transferred to the Supplemental Program, or
remain in the Defined Benefit Program (in which case the extra duty pay is included in
