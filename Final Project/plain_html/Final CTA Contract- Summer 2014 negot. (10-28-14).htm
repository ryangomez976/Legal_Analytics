ARTICLE 1 - Agreement

1.1    This Agreement is made and entered into by and between the Board of
Education of Chawanakee Unified School District, which together with its
administrative staff and representatives shall be referred to in this Agreement as the
“District,” and the Chawanakee Teacher’s Association, CTA/NEA, the certificated
employees’ exclusive representative, which together with its officers and
representatives shall be referred to in this Agreement as the “Association”.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, sections 3540-
3549 of the Government Code, which shall be referred to as the “EERA.” (See
Appendix A.)

1.3    This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2013 until
June 30, 2016.

1.4    Any individual contract between the District and a unit member shall be
subject to and consistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 2 - Recognition

2.1 The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all
certificated employees of the District excluding: superintendents, principals,
and any certificated employees who are responsible for hiring, firing, or
evaluating other certificated employees, and any substitute teachers, or is
designated as management by the District.

2.2

The parties to this Agreement recognize that the duties and work performed by
the certificated employees in the bargaining unit shall generally be performed
by unit members. The parties also recognize that in some instances, non-
certificated, non-unit individuals may possess valuable skills and knowledge,
which they can impart to students. Nothing in this article precludes the
District from using such individuals to supplement but not supplant the regular
instructional programs of the District.

ARTICLE 3 - Definitions

3.1    “Assault” means unlawful and unprovoked attempt to commit a verbal of

physical injury on another person(s).

3.2    “Adjunct duties" are those supervision responsibilities that occur outside the

classroom, divided equitably amongst teaching staff

3.3    “Association” means the Chawanakee Teachers Association, CTA/NEA, its

Officers, and representatives. The Association is the exclusive representative
of the certificated bargaining unit in the District.

3.4    “Contract year” for certificated employees is 180 days with students, plus 3

days without students.

3.5    “Daily Rate of Pay” means the unit members annual scheduled salary divided

by the number of duty days required by this Agreement.

3.6    “Days" means the days the District office is open for business.

3.7    “Dependent" means any person who qualifies to be covered by the employee’s

medical insurance benefits.

3.8    “District” is the Chawanakee Unified School District, its Board of Education,

Administration, and other designated representatives.

3.9    “Duty Day(s)” means day(s) during which unit members are required by

contract to render service.

3.10    “Hourly Rate of Pay” means the daily rate of pay divided by 7.5 daily duty
hours.

3.11    “Immediate Family” shall include: the mother, father, grandmother,
grandfather, or grandchild of the employee or the employee’s spouse, the
employee’s domestic partner, son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law,
brother, or sister; any relative or dependent minor living in the employee's
immediate household.

3.12    “Immediate Supervisor” means the unit member’s administrator, supervisor,
or line manager employed by the District who has direct responsibility for
supervising the Unit Member.

3.13    “Unit Member" means any District certificated employee who is included in
the appropriate unit as defined in Article 2

3.14    “Instructional Day(s)” means any day(s) pupils are present for instruction.

3.15    “Intern” is an employee performing certificated services pursuant to an intern
credential issued by CTC.

3.16    “Involuntary Transfer/Reassignment” is a District-initiated process to change
the grade or subject assignment or school site of an employee.

3.17    “Outside the duty day” is before or after the duty day (7.5 hour day) of the
unit member.

3.18    “Permanent Employee” is an employee who has completed probation within
the district pursuant to Ed Code.

3.19    “Probationary” is a certificated employee’s status that has not reached
permanent status by definition in the Ed Code.

3.20    “Pupil-Free Day” means a certificated contracted work day when students are
not present for instruction.

3.21    “Site” means a building or location where unit members work.

3.22    “Seniority” means the unit member’s first day of paid service in accordance
with Ed Code.

3.23    “Temporary” is a status assigned to a non-probationary or permanent
employee by definition in the Ed Code.

3.24    “Voluntary Transfer” is an employee-initiated process to change the grade or
subject assignment or school site of the employee.

ARTICLE 4 - Negotiations Procedure

4.1    As soon as possible in the year in which this Agreement expires, the

Association and District shall submit their initial proposals to each other for a
successor Agreement to the District. The District shall give proper public
notice of such proposals at the first school board meeting following the
submission of the proposals.

4.2    Each party may open 3 Articles each year for the purposes of re-openers. The

District shall give proper public notice of such proposals at the first school
board meeting following the submission of the proposals.

4.3    The parties shall commence to meet and negotiate on re-openers or a

successor Agreement beginning no later than five (5) days after the
completion of the public notice requirements listed above. Any Agreement
reached between the parties shall be reduced to writing and signed by them,
unless the timelines are extended by mutual consent. The formalized
schedule above does not preclude the parties meeting informally, by mutual
consent, to discuss the current District state of affairs, and/or current contract
management issues prior to formal sunshine procedures.

4.4    Within thirty (30) days of ratification of the Agreement by both parties herein,

the District shall have sufficient copies prepared and delivered to the
Association for distribution to each unit member in the District plus one (1)
per site for use by the Association. The District shall give a copy of the
Agreement to any new bargaining unit members it hires during the term of the
Agreement. Said copies may be provided in electronic form.

ARTICLE 5 - Maintenance of Standards

5.1    The Board shall not reduce or eliminate any compensation or health and

welfare benefits contained in this agreement, which were enjoyed by unit
members as of the effective date of this Agreement unless otherwise
provided by the express terms of this Agreement.

5.2    The provisions of this Agreement shall not be interpreted or applied in a

manner, which is arbitrary, capricious, or discriminatory. Rules, which, are
designed to implement this Agreement, shall be uniform in application and
effect. (See Appendix A, for reference to codes.)

ARTICLE 6 - Savings

6.1    If any provision of this Agreement, or any application of this Agreement to any

unit member or group of unit members is held to be contrary to law by a court
of competent jurisdiction oris modified by legislative action, then such
provision or application shall not be deemed valid and subsisting, except to
the extent permitted by law; but all other provisions or applications shall
continue in full force and effect.

6.2    It is further agreed that within ten (10) days of receipt of notification of the

court’s decision or legislative modification, negotiations shall commence
regarding matters related to such provision.

ARTICLE 7 - Concerted Activities

7.1    All of the rights, powers, or authority, which the District had under law prior to

the execution of this Agreement, are retained except as those rights, powers,
and functions or authority are specifically abridged or modified herein.

7.2    No Strike/No Lockout

7.2.1    During the term of this Agreement, the Association, in
consideration of the terms and conditions of this Agreement, will not
engage in or instigate any strike, work stoppage or any concerted
refusal to perform contractual work duties as required by this
Agreement.

7.2.2    During the term of this Agreement, the District, in
consideration of the terms and conditions of this Agreement, will not
authorize any lockout of bargaining unit members covered by this
Agreement.

ARTICLE 8 - Grievance Procedure

8.1    Definitions

8.1.1    A “grievance” is a claim by one or more unit members or
the Association that there has been a violation, misinterpretation, or
misapplication of a provision of this Agreement.

8.1.2    The “grievant” is the unit member, unit members, or the
Association making the claim.

8.2    Purpose

8.2.1 The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest
possible administrative level, equitable solutions to the problems,
which may from time-to-time arise affecting the welfare or working
conditions of unit members. Both parties agree that these
proceedings will be kept as informal and confidential as may be
appropriate at any level of the procedure. Before moving any
grievance to Level 1, an informal conference with the immediate
supervisor is required. (Unless grievance is regarding the immediate
supervisor, in which case the matter is referred directly to Level 2)

8.3    Procedure (Refer to Grievance Form, Appendix “C”)

8.3.1    Level 1 Immediate Supervisor

8.3.1.1    A grievance shall be presented in writing to the immediate supervisor.
Should they desire, the grievant would be responsible for notifying the Association of
the status of the grievance at this time. Statute of time in which to file a grievance
shall be ten (10) duty days after the time the employee discovers or becomes aware of
the alleged violation. No grievance shall be filed when discovery or awareness of the
alleged contract violation occurs more than one (1) year after the alleged contract
violation. The Supervisor shall respond to the grievance within five (5) duty days.

8.3.1.2    If the grievant is not satisfied with the response to the resolution of the
grievance, or if any timeline applicable at this level is not met, the grievant may request
that the grievance go to Level 2 within ten (10) duty days.

8.3.2    Level 2 Superintendent

8.3.2.1    Upon receipt of a grievance, the Superintendent or her/his designee, shall
meet with the grievant and, if desired by the grievant, an association representative,
within seven (7) duty days of receipt of the grievance. The Superintendent or her/his
designee shall provide a written response to the grievance within five (5) duty days of
the meeting.

8.3.3    Level 3 Board of Trustees

8.3.3.1    If the grievant is not satisfied with the response of Superintendent, or if any
timeline applicable to the resolution of the grievance at this Level is not met, the
grievant may request that the grievance goes to Level 3 within ten (10) duty days.

8.3.3.2    The appeal shall be in writing, shall be accompanied by copies of the
grievance and decisions at Levels 1 and 2.

8.3.3.3    The Board of Trustees shall meet with the grievant as a Board at the next
regularly scheduled board meeting following receipt of the appeal. The grievant may
be represented if he/she so chooses and may present witnesses on her/his behalf.

8.3.3.4    The Board of Trustees shall render a decision in writing and will set forth
its findings on the issues submitted by the grievant within ten (10) duty days following
the conclusion of its review, but in no event, no longer than twenty (20) duty days after
the board meeting. The decision of the Board of Trustees will be final.

8.4    Time Limits

8.4.1    Time limits provided for at each level shall begin the day
following receipt of the grievance appeal or written decision. If
necessary, timelines may be extended by mutual written consent.

8.5    No Reprisals

8.5.1    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by the District or by
any member or representative of the administration or the Board
against any grievant, or any other participant in the grievance
procedure.

8.6    Miscellaneous

8.6.1    When it is necessary for an employee to attend a
grievance meeting or hearing during the duty day, she/he shall be
released without loss of pay in order to permit participation in such
activities. Upon completing participation in such activities, the
employee shall immediately return to their regular work place.

8.6.2    All documents, communications and records dealing with
the processing of a grievance shall be filed in a separate grievance
file. The fact that an employee has filed a grievance will not be
kept in the personnel file of any unit member.

8.6.3    Forms for filing grievances, serving notices, taking
appeals, making reports and recommendations, and other

necessary documents shall be prepared jointly by the District and
Association and given appropriate distribution.

8.7    Public Complaints

8.7.1    Any public complaint about a unit member shall be
reported to the unit member by the administrator receiving the
complaint, within five (5) duty days of receipt.

8.7.2    If the matter is not resolved at Level 1, the informal
discussion level, the complainant (grievant) may begin Level 2. See
Appendix B for a copy of the current District Complaint Procedure.

8.7.3    Complaints which are withdrawn, shown to be false, or
are not sustained by the grievance procedure shall neither be
placed in the unit member's personnel file nor utilized in any
evaluation, assignment, or disciplinary or dismissal action against
the unit member.

8.7.4    All information or proceedings regarding any complaint
shall be kept confidential by the District.

ARTICLE 9 - Organizational Security

9.1 Dues Deduction

9.1.1    The right of payroll deduction for payment of membership
dues, initiation fees, and general assessments shall be accorded
exclusively to the Association. Association members who currently
have authorization cards on file for the above purposes need not be
reauthorized. Membership dues, initiation fees, and general
assessments, upon formal written request from the Association to
the District, shall be increased or decreased without reauthorization
from unit members.

9.1.2    Any unit member who is a member of the Association or
who has applied for membership, may sign and deliver to the
District an authorization for deduction of membership dues, initiation
fees, and general assessments of the Association. Pursuant to such
authorization, the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues
from the regular salary check of the unit member each month for ten
(10) months

9.1.3    The District agrees to remit all sums deducted pursuant to
section 9.1.1 promptly to the Association, accompanied by an
alphabetical list of unit members, for whom such deductions have
been made, including their names, work locations, and any changes
from the list previously furnished.

ARTICLE 10 - Association Rights

10.1    The Association has the right under the Educational Employment Relations
Act to represent bargaining unit members in their employment relations with
the District. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as a waiver of such
rights.

10.2    Mail Facilities

10.2.1    The Association shall have the right to use the District
mail service and unit member mailboxes for communications to unit
members.

10.2.2    The Association shall have the right to use the District
electronic mail service and unit member electronic mailboxes for
communications outside of the duty day.

10.3    Bulletin Boards

10.3.1    The Association shall have the right to post notices of
activities and matters of Association concern on bulletin boards.

10.4    Use of Buildings and Equipment

10.4.1    The Association shall have the right to use school
buildings, sites, and equipment during all reasonable hours for
meetings and other Association activities.

10.4.2    The Association shall have the right to use District
educational technology equipment and/or studios so long as such
use does not interfere with the District’s regular instructional
program. In the event any cost or damage accrues to the District
under this provision the Association shall reimburse the District that
cost. All equipment shall remain on premises. Any supplies and
use of materials shall be supplied by the Association

10.5    Access to Worksite

10.5.1    Authorized representatives of the Association shall have
the right to transact official Association business on school property
and utilize District facilities outside of the duty day.

10.6    Access to Information

10.6.1    The District, upon request by the Association, agrees to
furnish to the association, within five (5) days, the financial
resources and certificated and classified staffing of the District. Such
information shall include, but not be limited to: annual financial
reports and audits, budgets, interim reports, J-90s, assignment
location of certificated personnel, proposed budgetary requirements
and allocations, agendas and minutes of all Board of Trustees’
meetings and all attachments thereto at the time of distribution to
the Board of Trustees.

10.7    Release Time

10.7.1    The Association President or designee shall be provided
release time to conduct Assoc, business within District jurisdiction at
no loss of salary or benefits.

10.7.2    No more than five (5) Bargaining Team members shall
be provided release time for negotiations at no loss of salary or
other benefits.

10.7.3    Grievants, witnesses, and Association representatives
shall be provided release time for grievance processing at no loss of
salary or other benefits.

ARTICLE 11 - Transfer and Reassignment

11.1 Voluntary Transfer/Reassiqnment

11.1.1    A transfer is the movement of a unit member from one
work location to another work location, or from one program to
another program such as year round education, restructured
schools, or reconfiguration. The transfer may include a change in
grades or subject areas as long as the move involves changing
work sites.

11.1.2    A reassignment is the movement of a unit member from
one subject area to another subject area, one grade level to another
grade level, or from one configuration to another such as team
teacher, restructuring, or other reconfiguration within the same
school site.

11.1.3    Criteria to be considered by the District when selecting
unit members for voluntary or involuntary transfers and/or
reassignments shall include: Certification of unit member; training
and experience; needs of the District, school program and
students. If all of the above factors are equal, District seniority shall
be the determining factor as policy adopted by the Board of
Trustees.

11.1.4    A transfer request shall not be denied arbitrarily,
capriciously, or without basis in fact.

11.1.5    If a unit member’s request for a voluntary transfer is
denied, the unit member, upon request, shall be granted a meeting
with the administrator who denied the request to discuss the
reasons for the denial. Following the meeting the unit member may
request and shall receive written reasons for the denial.

11.2    Involuntary Transfer/Reassignment

11.2.1    Involuntary transfer/reassignment shall be made only for
the following reasons: A decrease in the number of pupils which
requires a decrease in the number of unit members; Class Size;
elimination of prog ram (s), credentialing and/or funding; or school
closings.

11.2.2    If a decrease in the number of pupils or the elimination of
program(s) and/or funding occurs, the District shall seek volunteers
prior to making any involuntary transfer/reassignment. If an
involuntary transfer/reassignment becomes necessary, the unit
member with the least District seniority with the appropriate
credential shall be transferred or reassigned.

11.2.3    Unit members who are transferred/reassigned during the
school year shall be allowed 4 days of release time, or 4 days of
preparation time outside the work day at the substitute teacher rate,
or a combination thereof, not to exceed a total of 4 days.

11.2.4    The District shall provide assistance in moving a unit
member’s material whenever a unit member is
transferred/reassigned.

11.3    Notification of Assignment

11.3.1 When vacancies are known prior to the end of the
instructional year, unit members shall be given written notice by
June 30th of next year’s assignment. Such notice shall specify the
building, grade, grade level, and subject area to which the unit
member will be assigned. In addition, such notice shall explain the

nature of special problems, which may be experienced by pupils
assigned to the unit member.

11.4 Vacancies

11.4.1    A vacancy is any position that does not have a unit
member assigned to it. This includes any vacated, promotional, or
newly created position, including positions created by
reconfiguration or restructuring.

11.4.2    Upon knowledge of vacancies, the District shall post in
all school buildings, and may also post online and/or via email, a list
of all vacancies, which occur during the school year and for the
following school year. This list shall contain the following:

11.4.2.1    A closing date which is at least ten (10) working days following the
posting date unless the vacancy occurs within 20 days of the first day of class.

11.4.2.2    A job description.

11.4.2.3    Credentials and qualifications necessary to meet the requirements of the
position.

11.4.3    No assignment to fill the vacancy shall be made until
after the closing date unless the vacancy occurs within 20 days of
the first day of class.

11.4.4    Present employees must notify the district office in
writing via regular mail or hand delivered to Human Resources (date
stamped), prior to date of closing, if interested in position. It is the
unit members responsibility to confirm that the notice to the district
office was received.

11.4.5    The District shall, upon request of the unit member,
deliver in writing, the reason(s) for the unit member not receiving the
vacancy.

ARTICLE 13 - Evaluation

The primary purpose of any employee evaluation is to improve the educational
process of the District and to develop the highest professional competence on the part
of each employee.

13.1    Frequency of Evaluation

13.1.1    Probationary/Temporary Employees

Each probationary and/or temporary employee shall be evaluated
on a continuing basis. A formal evaluation shall be completed each
school year. These employees will also receive interim evaluations
on or before December 1 and on or before February 1. All final
evaluation conferences for probationary/temporary employees shall
be completed by February 15.

The immediate supervisor shall meet with first-year probationary
employees within thirty (30) calendar days of the close of the first
quarter. At this meeting, the immediate supervisor shall review the
teacher’s performance in the first semester and inform the teacher
whether any problems were noted. If any problems are identified,
the immediate supervisor and teacher will begin developing a
written assistance plan designed to allow the unit member to make
the improvements necessary.

13.1.2    Permanent Employees

Each permanent employee shall be evaluated on a continuing basis.
Permanent employees performing satisfactorily shall be formally
evaluated at least once every other year except as noted below:
Upon agreement of the evaluator and the employee, permanent
employees who have been employed by the district at least ten
years, who are highly qualified as defined in 20 USC 7801, and
whose previous evaluation rated the employee as meeting
standards may be evaluated every five years. Either the employee
or the evaluator may withdraw consent at any time.

Permanent employees given a rating of "Unsatisfactory or Needs
Improvement" on the Formal Evaluation Summary shall be formally
evaluated each school year.

13.1.2.1    Permanent employees receiving an "unsatisfactory" Formal Evaluation
shall be referred to the PAR program. This rating of "unsatisfactory" is not intended to
indicate that the employee has been proven to be unsatisfactory as per Education
Code dismissal proceedings.

13.1.2.2    In order for a Teaching Standard to be unsatisfactory overall, 50% or
more of the elements in that Standard must be marked "unsatisfactory." This rating
shall be determined on the Final Evaluation of Teaching Performance included in
Appendix K.

13.2    Areas of Evaluation

13.2.1 The District shall evaluate and assess certificated
employee performance as it reasonably relates to students’
progress toward district standards of expected achievement for their
grade level in each area of study using the California Standards for
the Teaching Profession CSTP). See Appendix K and the state
adopted content standards.

Areas of Evaluation are:

1. Engaging and supporting all students in learning

2. Creating and maintaining effective environments for student learning

3.    Understanding and organizing subject matter for student
learning

4.    Planning instruction and designing learning experiences for all
students

5.    Assessing student learning

6.    Developing as a professional educator

13.2.2    The first observation may focus on CSTP standards 1-3
and the second observation may focus on CSTP standards 4-

6. The formal evaluation will include all standards.

13.2.3    Employees shall be evaluated on the fulfillment of duties
as defined in their job descriptions. Upon request, employees will be
provided a copy of their job description.

13.3    Areas Excluded from Evaluation

The evaluation and assessment of employees' performance pursuant to this Article
shall not include:

13.3.1    the use of students' standardized test scores, unless

otherwise    allowed    by    law.

13.3.2    the personal life or lifestyle of the unit member, their
personal opinions, scholarly, literary, or artistic endeavors outside of
the school day.

13.3.3    any aspect of the educational program over which the
unit member has no authority or ability to correct deficiencies.

13.4    Notice to Employees

No later than October 1, of the year in which the formal or informal evaluation is to take
place, the District shall give employees a copy of the evaluation procedures, the
criteria upon which the evaluation is to be based, the Governing Board's Established
Standards of Expected Pupil Achievement at each grade level in the area of study, and
the identity of their evaluator.

13.5    Evaluation Timeline

Teachers to be evaluated during a particular school year shall meet with
their    evaluator    by    October    15th to discuss:

13.5.1    Objectives and standard to be achieved

13.5.2    Any constraints, which the unit member believes, may
inhibit her/his ability to meet the objectives and standard
established.

13.5.3    During the course of the evaluation period circumstances
may change which require modification of the original objectives and
standard. The unit member may initiate a change of these
objectives and standards.

13.6    Classroom Observations/Post Observation Conferences

Each evaluation plan shall include a minimum of two formal classroom observations,
and each formal classroom observation shall be a minimum of thirty minutes. Prior to
the first observation, the District shall give the employee at least two days notice. As
soon as practicable within ten days after each formal classroom observation, the
evaluator shall hold a post observation conference. In addition to the formal
observation, the evaluator may informally observe the employee.

With agreement of both the evaluatee and the evaluator, the number of formal
observations for tenured employees may be reduced from two to one.

13.7    Formal Evaluation Summary

13.7.1    Post observation conferences for permanent employees
shall be completed by April 30. All final evaluation conferences for
probationary/temporary employees shall be completed by February

15. The evaluator shall give a copy of the final evaluation to the
employee thirty (30) days prior to the end of the school year. The
evaluator and the evaluatee must sign all copies. The final
evaluation form shall contain ratings of “Proficient, Satisfactory,
Needs Improvement” or “Unsatisfactory.”

13.7.2    If a permanent employee is given a rating of
“Unsatisfactory" or “Needs Improvement” in one of the six
Teaching Standards, s/he shall receive assistance including but not
limited to the following:

13.7.2.1    Referral to PAR as described in Appendix D

13.7.2.2    Specific recommendations for improvement in all other areas.

13.7.2.3    Techniques to measure improvement.

13.7.2.4    Time schedule to monitor progress.

13.7.3    In preparing the final evaluation form for placement in

ARTICLE 19 - Hours and Adjunct Duties

19.1    Length of Workday / Work Year

The school year will consist of 180 student-instructional days and three (3) non-
instructional work days for a total of 183 duty days. It is the intent of the District to
keep the student instructional year intact. Any potential changes to this schedule would
be part of a Memorandum of Understanding accompanying this contract.

Certificated employees will receive a full year of service credit for
STRS. Should any additional monies become available, the district agrees to meet to
discuss reinstatement of the certificated furlough days as its first priority prior to
additional monies being allocated.

19.1.1    The District recognizes that the varying nature of a unit member’s day-to-
day professional responsibilities does not lend itself totally to an instructional day of
rigidly established length. The minimum school day assignment for all teachers will be
a 7 1/2 hour duty day, including a minimum thirty (30) minute duty free lunch period.

19.1.2    All unit members must report to their duty at least fifteen (15) minutes
before instruction begins and they may leave no earlier than fifteen (15) minutes after

the regular student day, provided they complete the same established minimum duty
time and their daily schedule is consistent on a daily basis.

19.1.3 During the Fall and Spring semesters, schools will calendar a minimum
workday for purposes of parent outreach: i.e. Back to School Night, Open House,
parent conferences, Academic Showcase, etc. Additionally, if the current school
calendar includes classroom instruction on the Wednesday before Thanksgiving, or
the Friday before the Christmas vacation, these shall also be minimum days. These
dates will be determined in accordance with the adoption of the annual District
calendar.

19.2 Work Load/Adjunct Duty

19.2.1    No unit member shall be required to be on duty more than two and one-half
(2 1/2) hours without being provided a relief break of at least ten (10) minutes.

19.2.2    All adjunct duties within the workday, which do not require full faculty
participation, shall be equitably distributed among unit members.

19.2.3    Any regularly scheduled services by a unit member beyond contractually
specified time, which involves non-classroom supervision of pupils, shall be on a
voluntary basis or paid per stipend schedule. (See contract article 23.4)

19.2.4    For stipend requests not specifically outlined in contract, the District and
Association will meet collaboratively on an as-needed basis with the requesting site
leader(s) to discuss the relative value of the work requested and to make a
determination as to how the addition of the new work will impact the total stipend
allotment.

19.3    Preparation Time

19.3.1    Unit members shall utilize the remainder of the regular workday for faculty
meetings, in-service meetings, IEP team meetings, meetings with parents and/or staff
members, and/or planning and preparation for their assignment The establishment of
such days may require that there be some adjustment in other duty days to assure that
minimum instructional minutes are provided at all times as defined in the California
Education Code. Teachers receive preparation time either as part of weekly early
release days, or as a preparation period during the instructional day. This preparation
period is part of the teacher workday, and although considered non-instructional time,

it is not duty-free time. As such, the preparation period may only be used for
instructional or other work-related preparation. Unit members may not use their
preparation period for outside work or for personal business. Unit members who agree
to teach during their preparation period for a full school year will be compensated an
additional one-eighth (1/8) of their regular salary.

19.3.2    In the event coverage is needed for a bargaining unit member for any
period of time less than one-half of the contract day (for example, an absence of one
instructional period is needed to attend a sports event or a doctor's appointment),
another bargaining unit member who has prep time during the absence may, with the
approval of the site administrator, cover the absence in lieu of his/her prep time for that
day, and receive a stipend from the District at the rate of $25.00 per hour. Should the
absence be subject to the language in Article 21: Leaves, the appropriate dock shall
be made the to bargaining unit member’s accrual.

19.4    Participation on Committees

19.4.2    Unit members’ participation on all District committees shall be on a voluntary
basis and they may be granted release time during their workday at no loss of pay or
benefits to fulfill the obligations of their appointment.

21.1 Leave Right

shall be entitled to    return    to the same position and

assignment she/he had prior to the leave. A unit member returning

21.2 Sick Leave

21.2.1 Unit members employed on a full-time basis shall be
entitled to accumulate 13 days in the 2010-2011 school year, 12
days in the 2011-2012 school year, and 12 days thereafter (All new
hires for the 2010-2011 will accrue 12 days) per school year of sick
leave, which shall be available to the unit member from the first
work day of each year. Sick leave may be used by the unit member
for illness, injury, or quarantine of the unit member or the unit
member’s immediate family. Sick leave, which is not used, shall
accumulate from year-to-year without limit.

21.2.2    Unit members who work less than full-time and/or work
during summer session shall be entitled to accumulate and use sick
leave for all such service at a rate that is in the same proportion to
the full-time entitlement as their part-time employment bears to full¬
time employment.

21.2.3    In addition to all sick leave entitlement a unit member
may accumulate within the District, she/he shall also be entitled to
all unused sick leave, which may have been accumulated while
employed in a position requiring certification qualifications in another
school district.

21.2.4    The District shall provide each unit member with an
accounting of the number of days of sick leave she/he is entitled for
the current school year. This information is found on the paystub
monthly.

21.2.5    Upon exhaustion of all accumulated sick leave, a unit
member who would otherwise qualify for sick leave under the
provisions of this Article shall be entitled to additional non-
accumulated differential leave for a period, not to exceed 100 days
or five (5) school months. The amount deducted for leave purposes
from the unit member’s salary shall be the amount actually paid a
substitute employee employed to fill the position during the leave, or
if no substitute is employed, the amount which would have been
paid a substitute.

21.2.6    The District shall not require medical verification of
illness or injury of three (3) days or less, except in cases of
reasonable suspicion of abuse of this leave provision. The District

work, if there exists a reasonable suspicion that the person is not
able to return to full service.

21.2.7    A unit member will use the District-approved procedure
to report the absence and/or request a substitute teacher.

21.2.8    A unit member who is absent for one-half (1/2) day or

leave; and if the absence exceeds more than one-half (1/2) day, a
full day shall be deducted from accumulated leave.

21.2.9    A unit member may be docked the substitute’s pay if the
employee fails to notify the District of the employee’s intent to return
to work at least by 3:00 p.m. of the work day to which the unit
member is assigned if such failure results in a substitute being
secured.

experiences a catastrophic illness or injury which requires the

and the employee has exhausted all available sick leave and other

credits.

21.3.2    In making such a request, the employee shall provide
verification of the catastrophic injury or illness.

21.3.3    Verification shall be made by means of a letter, dated

21.3.8    The Superintendent or designee shall ensure that all

21.8    Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

21.8.1    The Federal Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA)/California
Family Rights Act (CFRA) provides up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-
protected leave to eligible employees for certain qualifying family
and medical reasons. The District will maintain the employee’s
group health plan coverage for the duration of this leave. Leave is
also available for pregnancy, childbirth and related medical
conditions under the California Pregnancy Disability Leave Act
(PDLA) and District policy. The following is synopsis of the
provisions and/or information that applies in most cases.

21.8.2    Eligibility for FMLA/CFRA Leave

12 months service with employer and 1250 hours of service in the
last twelve months. For eligibility purposes, full-time teachers are
deemed to meet the 1250 hour test. Even if you are not eligible for
FMLA/CFRA leave, if you are disabled by pregnancy, childbirth, or
related medical conditions, you are entitled to take a pregnancy

disability leave of up to five (5) months depending on the duration of
disability. If you are CFRA eligible, you have a right to take both a
pregnancy disability leave and a CFRA leave for the reason of the
birth of your child and in order to care for the child.

21.8.3 Reasons for Leave

Birth of child or in order to care for such child.

Placement of child with employee for adoption or foster care.

To care for serious health condition of spouse, child, or parent.

Serious health condition of employee that keeps him/her from performing job functions.

21.8.4    Duration of Leave

12 weeks within 12-month period (based on the initial date of the
qualifying event). If for birth, adoption, or foster care placement,
leave must be initiated within one year of the event. 12 weeks is in
addition to pregnancy disability leave. If leave is taken for an
employee’s own serious health condition or for the purpose of caring
for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition, an
employee may take leave intermittently or on a reduced leave
schedule if medically necessary. If leave is taken for the birth,
adoption, or placement of a child for foster care, an employee may
take such leave intermittently or on a reduced leave schedule on
two occasions without regard to the duration of such leave. The
third and all subsequent occasions must be for a minimum of two
consecutive weeks duration. The District may temporarily assign an
employee requesting intermittent leave to an alternative position
with equivalent pay and benefits which better accommodates the
employee’s intermittent leave schedule.

21.8.5    Terms of Leave

Use of accrued vacation time or other accumulated paid time off (i.e.
personal necessity) is required for an employee who takes leave for
the birth, adoption or foster care placement of a child or for the
purpose of caring for a spouse, child, or parent with a serious health
condition. Use of sick leave, vacation and other accumulated paid
time off (i.e. differential leave) is required for an employee who
takes leave for his/her own serious health condition. Paid leave and
requested unpaid FMLA/CFRA leave will run concurrently. No
accrual of vacation while on FMLA/CFRA leave is allowed. For
purposes of retirement plans, the District shall not be required to
make plan payments during leave period and the leave period shall
not be counted for time accrued under the plan.

21.8.6    Advance Notice and Medical Certification

30 days notice required for foreseeable events; for unforeseen
events, as soon as possible. The District requires medical
certification of an employee’s own or the employee’s family
member’s serious health condition stating: date condition began;
probable duration; statement that condition warrants family member

care. Certification must be provided within 15 days. In case of
leave for own serious health condition, employee must have
attending physician issue a fitness for duty report, releasing
employee to resume work with or without reasonable
accommodation.

COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

LEVEL 1 - INFORMAL DISCUSSION

The first step in the complaint procedure shall be an informal discussion between the
parties in order to resolve the issue. For example, in the case of a parent/guardian
with a complaint about his/her child’s teacher, the first step would require the
parent/guardian to informally discuss the issue with the teacher. Complaints made
directly to the principal or supervisor shall be referred to the employee. If a private
informal conference is unacceptable to the grievant or employee, the employee’s
principal or supervisor shall offer to attend the informal conference.

LEVEL 2 - WRITTEN STATEMENT

If the informal discussion did not resolve the issue, the grievant shall within ten (10)
working days prepare a written statement of the complaint and submit it to the
appropriate employee. The written statement shall include a clear, concise account of
the complaint; the persons involved, and the name and address of the grievant. Within
five (5) working days the employee will add his/her written response to the statement

and conduct a conference with the grievant to review the complaint and
response. Failure of the grievant to submit a written statement shall be considered a
dropping of the complaint.

LEVEL 3 - SITE ADMINISTRATOR / SUPERVISOR

If the grievant remains dissatisfied following the discussion and review at Level 2,
he/she may submit within ten (10) working days following the completion of Level 2, a
written grievance to the site administrator or supervisor. The written grievance shall
include a copy of the Level 2 written complaint; the persons involved, and the name
and address of the grievant. The site administrator or supervisor shall notify the
employee of the advancement to Level 3 within the ten (10) day period. The site
administrator or supervisor shall submit a written response to the grievant within ten
(10) working days of receiving the Level 3 notice.

LEVEL 4 - DISTRICT ADMINISTRATION

If the grievant remains dissatisfied following the site administrator/supervisor response
at Level 3, he/she may submit the complaint within seven (7) working days of receiving
the response to the district superintendent or designee for review. The request for
review shall include a copy of the Level 2 written complaint; the employee’s and site
administrator/supervisor written responses; the actions previously taken; the persons
involved; and the name and address of the grievant.

The grievant shall submit the complaint directly to the superintendent or designee who
may hold a conference with all interested parties to the complaint within five (5)
working days of the complaint’s receipt unless there is mutual agreement that more
time is needed. The superintendent shall submit a written response to the grievant of
the decision in the matter within five (5) working days after the conclusion of the
conference or ten (10) working days after receipt of the complaint, whichever comes
first.

LEVEL 5 - BOARD OF TRUSTEES

If the grievant remains dissatisfied following the decision at Level 4, he/she may
submit a letter within seven (7) working days after the receipt from Level 4, requesting
a review of the decision by the Board of Trustees. The written request shall include a
copy of the Level 2 written complaint; the reason(s) why the grievant has not been
satisfied at the lower levels of the process; the actions previously taken; the persons
involved; and the name and address of the grievant. The Board of Trustees shall
place the review of the decision on the next regularly scheduled agenda if the request
is received within seven (7) days following the grievant’s receipt of the decision from
Level 4. The Board shall review the decision and the complaint and shall render its
final decision during the board meeting.

1.0    Program Components

1.1    There shall be a Peer Assistance and Review (PAR) Program, hereafter

teachers assist other teachers in the areas of subject matter knowledge, teaching
methodology, and teaching strategies. PAR shall have three components:

1.1.1    Permanent Teacher Peer Assistance Program:

This component shall provide peer assistance to permanent teachers who receive an
"unsatisfactory" evaluation in the area of teaching methods or instruction.

1.1.1.1    Teachers receiving an "unsatisfactory" on any one of the first 5 teaching
standards evaluation according to contract, section 13.7.2 in the area of teaching
methodology and / or instruction will be referred to PAR by the principal or designated
evaluator for assistance under this program.

1.1.2    Voluntary Teacher Peer Assistance Program:

This component shall provide assistance to teachers who volunteer for

the Program.

1.1.2.1    Teachers desiring assistance in improving their teaching practice may

the authority to accept or reject such referrals. The PAR Panel shall have the authority

subject to the availability of resources.

1.1.2.2    If a teacher is accepted into PAR as a volunteer, documentation will not be
placed into the personnel file.

has been evaluated to be "unsatisfactory" or other areas deemed in need of
assistance by the peer coach during the period of assistance;

4.6.3    Opportunities for the teacher receiving assistance to observe exemplary
practice, either by the peer coach or other exemplary teachers;

4.6.4    District-provided professional development opportunities as recommended
by the Peer coach and approved by the PAR panel;

4.6.5    Conference attendance including, as necessary, in the company of the peer
coach, to facilitate reflection on how this experience fits into the Individual Learning
Plan;

4.6.6    Other forms of assistance which the peer coach and the Panel may identify
and provide; and

4.6.7    The parties understand that every possible subject matter competency may
not be available within the corps of coaches, and therefore, it shall occasionally be
necessary to secure additional assistance to fully address identified deficiencies. In
such cases, the peer coach shall maintain prime responsibility for the Individual
Learning Plan, but may function more like a case carrier who assures the availability of
appropriate resources.

4.6.8    Communication and consultation between the teacher and the Peer coach
with the principal / evaluator shall be ongoing.

4.7    Nothing in this article precludes the principal/evaluator or District from doing
informal observations nor from notifying the teacher verbally and / or in writing
regarding incidents or events related to the teacher's fulfillment of his or her
professional obligations.

4.8    Written peer review reports will be submitted to the Panel by the Peer Coach
after 10 hours. The coach will share all written and verbal evaluation reports during a
conference with the teacher, prior to submission to the Panel. A copy of the written
reports will be provided to the principal/evaluator.

4.9    The peer coach shall submit an oral and written final report regarding the
teacher's participation in the PAR to the Panel no later than March 1* of the year of
participation or no earlier than 6 months after placement in PAR. This teacher's report
shall describe the measures of assistance provided to the teacher and describe the

results of the assistance in the area or areas recommended for improvement. The final
report shall become a part of the permanent teacher's permanent personnel file. The
teacher shall have the opportunity to attach his or her comments.

4.9.1    The teacher and principal / evaluator may be present for the coach's
presentation and will be given an opportunity to respond to the report.

4.10    The Panel shall deliberate whether:

4.10.1    The teacher is now "proficient" in the areas evaluated, or

4.10.2    The Panel and peer coach do not consider that further assistance and
remediation will be successful with reasons in support of this conclusion.

4.11    Notwithstanding the above, and while the term of this assistance shall
normally be for one school year, the assistance may be extended to a second year if
the Panel believes progress is being made, although the teacher may not have
returned to a "proficient" level of performance

4.12    The deliberations of the Panel shall be closed and confidential. Panel
decisions shall be based on the information provided by the coach, the principal /
evaluator, and the teacher or his or her Association representative.

4.13    Neither the teacher, coach, nor principal/evaluator may be present during
deliberations of the Panel, which are confidential. The Panel may request additional
information from any teacher involved in the Program.

4.14    The decision of the Panel shall be reported to the teacher, the coach, the
principal/evaluator, and the president of the teachers association, if requested by the
teacher, in conference with the Superintendent or designee.

4.15    The Panel shall report their findings to the Board of Education

5.0    Teacher Due Process Rights

5.1    The    teacher    shall be entitled to review all reports generated    by the peer coach

prior to their    submission to the Panel and to have affixed thereto his    or her comments.

To effectuate this right, the peer coach shall provide the teacher being reviewed with
copies of such reports at least ten (10) working days prior to any such meeting.

5.2    The teacher shall have a right to be represented by the Association in any
meetings of the Panel to which they are called, and shall be given a reasonable
opportunity to present his or her point of view concerning any report being made.

5.3    The    teacher    shall have the right to timely progress report.

5.4    The    teacher    shall have the right to present reasons why a specific peer coach

should be replaced and another coach substituted and to have those reasons
considered. The teacher shall have the right to petition the PAR panel for a coach from
outside the district.

5.5    A teacher shall not have access to the grievance process to challenge the
content of reports, or decisions by the Panel, but may file responses which shall
become part of the official record of the intervention.

5.6    This Program in no manner diminishes the legal rights of bargaining unit
members.

6.0    Voluntary Teacher Peer Assistance Program

6.1    A teacher may volunteer for the peer assistance program. The PAR Panel will
determine whether the teacher may participate in the program. All participants referred
to the PAR Program based on an unsatisfactory evaluation will be served prior to
accepting any voluntary participants.

6.2    The coach and teacher will meet to determine the volunteer teacher's needs
and jointly develop an Individual Learning Plan. This plan will draw from the course of
assistance that is available through the PAR Program enumerated in 1.4.6 and include
timelines.

6.3    Coaches will provide oral and written feedback documentation to the volunteer
teacher. Communication between a voluntary participant and his or her coach
concerning participation in the Program shall remain confidential.

6.4    Documentation will not be placed in the personnel file only so long as
participation continues on a voluntary basis.

6.5    The volunteer teacher may terminate his or her participation in the Program at
any time.

7.0    Miscellaneous Provisions

7.1    Expenditures for the Program shall not exceed revenues received from the
state for the PAR Program.

7.2    Funds shall be set aside to allow for release days and/or conferences as
developmental tools for panel members, teachers and peer coaches assigned to the
Program.

7.3    Any monies remaining in the PAR program at end of year will remain in the
PAR budget for the upcoming year.

7.4    It is the intent of the District and the Association that this Article remain in
effect for as long as specific state funding for the California Peer Assistance and
Review Program for Teachers is received by the District. If state funding for the PAR
Program is eliminated, this article shall expire and have no force or effect without the
need for further action by either the District or the Association. The District shall notify
the Association in writing that the PAR Program has been eliminated.

7.5    The District and Association agree that this Article shall be reopened if either
the Education Code section 44500 et. Seq. Or the State's implementation guidelines or
regulations are modified in any manner that adversely impacts a term of the Article.

The parties further agree that this Article may be reopened at any time by mutual
agreement.

hereinafter referred to as “District”, and the Chawanakee Teachers Association
hereinafter referred as “Association”. This agreement reflects a change and
clarification of the former North Fork Early Retirement Plan and affects all employees,
certificated and classified.

The conditions of the CUSD Early Retirement Benefit shall be as follows:

Eligibility

1    Employees hired prior to July 1, 1992, who are between the ages of 50 and 61
are eligible for this benefit.

2    The employee must meet STRS qualifications for retirement.

3    The employee must have 10 years of service with CUSD, exclusive of time
granted for service in other districts.

4    The employee must enter retirement out of CUSD.

5    Certificated employees born prior to January 1, 1943, must be in column 5 of the
salary schedule to qualify.

6    Certificated employees born after December 31, 1942, must be in column 5 of
the salary schedule, plus 30 units to qualify.

7    Any employee who receives a disability retirement under STRS shall be eligible
for the CUSD Early Retirement Benefit.

8    In order to be eligible for the Chawanakee Early Retirement Program, the
employee must notify the District of their intent to retire on or before April 15 of the
year in which retirement is to become effective.

Article I Early Retirement Stipend

A.    Payments

1 Stipend shall be a maximum of $100.00 per month for Certificated to be initiated
as follows:

a.    Year one after retirement, no payments.

b.    Year two after retirement, $50.00 per month for Certificated.

c.    Beginning year three and for life, $100.00 per month for Certificated.

B.    Early Death Benefit

1    A minimum of $1000.00 shall be paid under this benefit.

2    In the event the employee is deceased before payments total $1000.00, the
employee’s beneficiary shall receive a lump sum amount which is equal to $1000.00
minus payments made under this benefit.

Article II Health Benefits

A.    Payments

1    Payment for benefits shall be limited to that amount being paid for current
employees of CUSD.

2    At age 65 or more, a retiree with at least five years of service to the Distnct will
receive $2.78 per month times years of service (12 month basis), to be applied to the
District’s health insurance policies covering the retiree. Maximum payment under this
provision is $100.00 per month.

3    Retirees on the CUSD Early Retirement Benefit may elect to use a portion or all
of each monthly payment for health benefits after age 65. Should District paid health
benefits terminate before age 65, then the retiree may at the termination of those
benefits elect to use a portion or all of each monthly payment to pay the cost of such
health benefits.

B.    Early Death Benefit

1 If the employee is deceased before benefits under this agreement cease, the
District shall continue benefits, at District expense, for three months for the eligible
dependent of the employee.

Article III Funding

A.    The District shall fund payments for the CUSD Early Retirement Benefit from the
District’s General Fund on an ongoing basis as such obligations occur.

B.    The District shall not be required to maintain any separate fund to support the
CUSD Early Retirement Benefit.
