PREAMBLE

The District and the teachers are mutually committed to keeping class size at the lowest
levels possible keeping in mind financial resources, staffing and student needs.

The District and the teachers are working together to provide an educational program,
which is varied and rich in curriculum for all students. To that end both recognize a priority for
maintaining workable class sizes, for providing up to date texts and sufficient instructional
supplies.

ARTICLE I

AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement (agreement) by and between the Governing Board of the Wilmar School
District (Board) and the Wilmar Teachers Association/CTA/NEA (Association), an
employee organization.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Section 3540.1(h) of the
Government Code.

ARTICLE V

PROFESSIONAL DUES OR FEES AND PAYROLL DEDUCTIONS
Any teacher who is a member of the Association, or who has applied for membership,
may sign and deliver to the Board an assignment authorizing deduction of unified
membership dues, initiation fees and general assessments in the Association.

Pursuant to such authorization, the Board shall deduct one-eleventh of such dues from
the regular salary check of the teacher each month for eleven (11) months.

Deductions for teachers who sign such authorization after the commencement of the
school year shall be appropriately prorated to complete payments by the end of the
school year. For those teachers who have prior membership in the Association, the
amount deducted shall equal the full dues of the Association. Teachers joining the
Association for the first time shall have the appropriate portion of the dues prorated
over the months remaining from the date of authorization. The Association agrees to
indemnify and hold the Board harmless in the event of litigation brought by the
Association member due to the terms of this section.

With respect to all sums deducted by the Board pursuant to authorization of the
employee, the Board agrees to promptly remit such monies to the Association upon
receipt from the Sonoma County Office of Education, accompanied by an
alphabetical list of teachers for whom such deductions have been made.

The Association agrees to furnish any information needed by the Board to fulfill the
provisions of this Article.

Upon appropriate written authorization from the teacher, the Board shall deduct from
the salary of any teacher and make appropriate remittance for any voluntary
deduction plan or programs jointly approved by the Association and the Board, for
which the Sonoma County Office of Education is procedurally set up to handle.

ARTICLE VI

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
A grievance is defined as a formal written statement by a unit member and/or the
Association that the District has violated an express term of this Agreement and that
by reason of such violation, his or her rights have been adversely affected. All other
matters and disputes of any nature are beyond the scope of these procedures. Also
excluded from these procedures are those matters so indicated elsewhere in this
Agreement.

The respondent in all cases shall be the District itself rather than any individual. The
filing or pendency of a grievance shall not delay or interfere with implementation of
any District action during the processing thereof.

A “day” is a day in which the central administration office of the District is open for
business.

Informal Stage

Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant shall attempt to resolve it by

Formal Stage
Level 1

Within twenty (20) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the
grievance, or within twenty (20) days of when the grievant or the Association could
reasonably have known of the act or omission, the grievant must present his/her
grievance in writing to the Superintendent.

The written statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, including
the specific provisions of this Agreement alleged to have been violated, the
circumstances involved, the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the
specific remedy sought.

Either party may request a personal conference with the other party. The
Superintendent shall communicate a decision to the grievant in writing within ten (10)
days after receiving the grievance and such action will terminate Level 1.

Level II

In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the recommendation(s) of the
Superintendent, he/she may appeal the decision to the Board of Education within ten

(10) days of the Superintendent’s decision in 6.5.1.3.

This appeal shall include at least a clear, concise statement of the decision rendered,
and the reasons for appeal.

The Board of Education will consider the appeal within 20 days or at the next
regularly scheduled meeting of the board provided that the parties have at least 10
days prior notice of the hearing date. The time period begins on the date the appeal is
personally received by the Superintendent. The parties may mutually agree to a
hearing date, which is beyond the limits set forth in this section.

The Board of Education shall communicate its decision to the grievant and the
Association within ten (10) days after consideration. The decision of the Board shall
be final and binding.

Failure to Meet Time Limits

If a grievance is not processed by the grievant in accordance with the time limits set
forth in this Article, it shall be considered settled on the basis of the decision last
made by the District. If the District fails to respond to the grievance in a timely
manner at any level, the running of the time limit shall be deemed a denial of the
grievance and termination of the level involved, and the grievant may proceed to the
next step.

Time limits hereunder may be lengthened or shortened in any particular case only by
mutual agreement.

The parties will attempt, in good faith, to adjust time limit problems, which occur
above Level 1 as a result of the summer recess.

Association Representation

The grievant shall be entitled upon request to representation by the Association at all
grievance meetings. In situations where the Association has not been requested to
represent the grievant, the District will not agree to a final resolution of the grievance
until the Association has received a copy of the grievance and the proposed
resolution, and has been given the opportunity to state its views on the matter. Ten
(10) days will be considered an opportunity in this instance.

Reasonable Release Time

Reasonable release time will be provided the grievant and/or the Association designee

Grievance Files

The District’s records dealing exclusively with the filing and processing of a
grievance shall be maintained separately from the grievant personnel file. All records
used in this grievance procedure which may have derived from the official personnel
files maintained by the District, will be returned to those files without indication that
they had been used in this grievance procedure.

Group Grievance

Should the Association and the District feel that the significant characteristics of a
number of individual grievances are sufficiently alike that it would be in the best
interest of time to hear this group of grievances as one (1), they may mutually agree
to do so. In such instances, a group grievance would be filed at Level 1 of the
procedure.

Forms

Forms for filing grievances, serving notices, taking appeals, making reports and
recommendations, and other necessary documents will be prepared jointly by the
Principal/Superintendent and the Association and given appropriate distribution by
the Association so as to facilitate operation of the grievance procedure. The costs of
preparing such forms shall be borne by the Board.

No Reprisals

No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the Principal/Superintendent or by any
member or representative of the administration or the Board against any aggrieved
party, any party in interest, any member of the Association or any other participant in
the grievance procedure by reason of such participation. Similarly no reprisals of any
kind will be taken by the grievant, any unit member or the Association against the
Principal/Superintendent, Board Member, Board Representative, parent, or other
participant in the grievance by reason of such participation.

ARTICLE VII

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

Mail Facilities

The Association shall have the right to use the District mail service and unit member
mailboxes for communications to unit members without interference, censorship, or
examination of such communications by the employer.

Bulletin Boards

The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities and matters of the
Association concern on an Association bulletin board in the Teacher’s Room.

Use of Buildings and Equipment

The Association shall have the right to use school facilities and equipment during all
reasonable hours for meetings and other Association activities provided such does not
interfere with classroom instruction.

Access to Worksite

Authorized representatives of the Association shall have the right to transact official
business on school property and utilize District facilities at all reasonable times

provided that such activities or use do not interfere with classroom instruction and
provided they register upon arrival at the school office.

7.5    Access to Information

Upon request by the Association, the District agrees to provide within 5 days, the
Association with copies of all public documents/information which are necessary to
the Association’s duty to represent the unit members of the District. In the alternative
the District shall inform the Association where such information is available.

ARTICLE VIII

PUBLIC AND PARENTAL COMPLAINT PROCEDURE

8.1    Parents or guardians of pupils enrolled in the District may present informal (oral)
and/or formal (written) complaints regarding members of the bargaining unit to the
District. Parents or guardians should be encouraged to present informal (oral)
complaints first with the employee who is the subject of the complaint, or with that
employee’s Principal/Superintendent, prior to presenting any formal (written)
complaint to the Board.

8.2    Informal (oral) complaints:

No record of any informal (oral) complaint shall be placed in the personnel file of a
member of the bargaining unit unless:

8.2.1    The Principal/Superintendent conducts an investigation about the complaint. Such
investigation shall include a conference with the complainant, the employee, and the
employee’s representative either individually or collectively.

8.2.2    The member of the bargaining unit has been given prior notice of the informal (oral)
complaint including the name(s) of the complainant(s) and any record to be filed such
that the member of the bargaining unit has a reasonable opportunity (ten calendar
days) to present relevant information to his or her Principal/Superintendent.

8.3    Formal (written) complaints:

The District shall forward as soon as practicable to the member of the bargaining unit
any formal (written) complaint regarding that member. A member of the bargaining
unit shall forward as soon as practicable to the Principal/Superintendent any formal
(written) complaint received by the member regarding that member. No record of
any formal (written) complaint or the complaint itself shall be placed in the personnel
file of a member of the bargaining unit unless:

8.3.1    The Principal/Superintendent conducts an investigation about the complaint. Such
investigation shall include a conference with the complainant, the employee, and the
employee’s representative either individually or collectively.

8.3.2    The member of the bargaining unit has been given prior notice of the formal (written)
complaint including the name of the complainant(s) and any record to be filed such
that the member of the bargaining unit has a reasonable opportunity (ten calendar
days) to present relevant information to his or her Principal/Superintendent.

8.4    No record of any complaint shall be kept if an investigation by the District shows that
the complaint has no merit, and/or the District believes that no record shall be
maintained.

8.5    Anonymous complaints shall not be processed pursuant to the provisions of this
section.

ARTICLE IX

TRANSFER AND REASSIGNMENT POLICIES

9.1    Definition    -“Reassignment” shall mean the movement of a teacher from one grade

level to another. This provision includes movement to and from a class that combines
two grade levels.

9.2    Upon initial employment, assignment as to grade level shall be made by the
Superintendent or designee.

9.3    In the event reassignment becomes necessary, the Superintendent shall post a notice,
giving the grade level(s) affected, and shall solicit applications from among current
staff members to fill the vacancy.

9.3.1    If no application is forthcoming, the following criteria will be employed in
determining the reassignment.

9.3.1.1    Credential, experience, recent training and seniority supporting possible success in
the assignment.

9.3.1.2    Special consideration as expressed by both the employer and the employee relating to
staff relationships.

9.3.2    Prior to making a final decision the Superintendent shall consult in a meeting with the
teacher who may be reassigned.

9.3.3    The teacher being reassigned shall have the right to appeal the reassignment to the
Superintendent.

9.3.3.1    The teacher shall submit his/her reasons for appeal in writing to the Superintendent
and shall meet with the Superintendent to discuss his/her concerns.

9.3.3.2    In the event the unit member is not satisfied with the result of his/her meeting with
the Superintendent, he/she may meet with the Board to discuss his/her concerns
regarding the reassignment.

9.4    Teachers who are reassigned during the school year shall be allowed up to 2 days of
release time for preparation prior to the effective date of reassignment and the District
shall provide a day of release time in recognition of the extra effort involved in a
school year reassignment. For teachers reassigned prior to the start of the school
year, the District shall provide a day of release time during the year in recognition of
the time needed during the summer.

9.5    Prior to the end of a school year, every reasonable effort will be made to notify
teachers, in writing, of their tentative teaching assignments and work locations for the
coming school year. For teachers being relocated the District shall provide a day of
release time during the year in recognition of the time needed during the summer.

The District shall provide assistance in the moving of the teacher’s materials to the
new work location.

9.5.1 If tentative teaching assignments or work locations are changed during the summer,
teachers shall be contacted by mail or personally for the purpose of discussing

reassignment by July 1, except in unusual circumstances. Teachers mailing addresses
will be on file with the District.

9.6    The parties recognize that as a result of special circumstances beyond the control of
the district, class assignments may change after July 1. Those teachers affected by
such changes will be contacted immediately.

9.7    In the event of a vacancy or opening, the District shall notify current staff members of
such at the same time the position is advertised to the public. Current staff members
may apply for the position within the same time frame made available to the public.
The decision of the Board to hire from the outside or to voluntarily reassign a current
staff member is the decision of the Board with input from the Superintendent.

ARTICLE XI

EVALUATION

The principal objective of an evaluation procedure is to maintain and improve the
quality of education, and to assist all teachers in maintaining/improving their
professional skills.

Areas of Evaluation

The immediate supervisor shall evaluate and assess certificated employee
competency under the following criteria:

The Instructional techniques and strategies used by the employee.

The employee’s adherence to District curricular objectives and progress of students
towards District achievement goals.

The establishment and maintenance of suitable learning environment, within the
scope of the employee’s responsibilities.

Other instructional and non-instructional duties necessary to the total teaching
process.

The specific employee - evaluator approved performance objectives designed to
implement the District criteria in sections 11.2.1—11.2.4 above.

Frequency of Evaluation

Every probationary teacher shall be evaluated by the Superintendent/Principal, in

Q

writing, at least once each year. Permanent Teachers shall be evaluated at least every
other year. Teachers who have a new class assignment may be evaluated that year.
Superintendent/Principal shall, in accordance with the law, be trained for
evaluating/assessing the professional competence of teachers and be certified as to
competency under Education Code Section 35160.5(a).

11.4    Instructional Goals and Objectives

11.4.1    The Superintendent and the unit member shall meet in a pre-observation conference
within the first seven (7) weeks of instruction. The purpose of the conference will be
to:

1)    Discuss and attempt to mutually agree on the unit member’s goals and objectives;
and

2)    To discuss the District’s goals and objectives for its course of study, expected
student achievement, and unit member performance criteria; and

3)    To allow the Superintendent to familiarize the unit member with the observation
and evaluation forms; and

4)    To set up an observation and evaluation calendar for that unit member.

11.4.2    Each unit member, in cooperation with the evaluator, shall develop objectives in at
least two of areas in the California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTP).

11.4.3    Agreement between the evaluator and unit member shall be sought to be reached at
the beginning of the observation/evaluation cycle on the following elements:

1)    The number, priority and appropriateness of the employee’s specific objectives;

2)    Standards of performance and assessment procedures related to forms used;

3)    Any Admini strative/Di strict support agreed upon for the employee;

4)    Modifications, if any, of the employee’s objectives. Mitigating factors to
achieving desired levels of performance may be listed by the employee should an
agreement fail to be reached with the evaluator. That list of constraints may, upon
request, be attached to the observation and final evaluation written reports.

11.4.4    Each member shall submit his/her objectives to the evaluator no later than fifteen (15)
workdays after the Superintendent-Principal has provided the paperwork.

11.5 Observation

At least twice during the year, the Superintendent shall formally observe each unit
member being evaluated. The first observation shall be no later than January 15, and
the second—no later than March 15. Each formal observation shall be at least thirty
(30) minutes in duration.

11.5.1    Each observation done shall be followed by a conference, within ten(10) workdays,
between the unit member and the evaluator to discuss the activities and behavior
observed as well as data collected and to prepare a written report which will be
attached to the resulting evaluation.

11.5.2    Each member shall sign the formal observation; however, such signature indicates
that the observation report has been discussed with the member and does not
necessarily constitute agreement with the judgment of the evaluator.

11.5.3    If a member receives an objectionable written observation report, he/she shall be
entitled to not less than one (1) additional observation and conference other than
those provided elsewhere in this section. Within five (5) working days of receiving
the initial written observation report, the member shall request in writing the
additional observation and conference. The evaluator will schedule an additional

observation and conference within five (5) working days.

11.5.4    A reasonable number of additional classroom observations, formal or informal, with
or without notice, may be conducted for any reasonable period of time at the
discretion of the evaluator. When such additional observations are documented a
copy shall be given to the unit member who may request a conference to discuss the
report. Upon such request, a meeting shall be granted. The member shall have the
right to attach a written response.

11.5.5    As appropriate, the member’s evaluator shall make specific recommendations,
including specific timelines, as to how to correct any deficiencies noted in the
observation report.

11.6 Evaluation

Evaluations shall be completed at least 30 days before the last school day. In the
event any certificated employee has received an unsatisfactory evaluation, the
Principal shall evaluate the member twice a year until the member achieves a positive
evaluation or is separated from the district.

11.6.1    The written evaluation report shall be based on the member’s performance in the
areas of:

1)    Instructional and non-instructional duties; and

2)    Attainment of the agreed upon objectives that are consistent with applicable
District goals and standards and

3)    The member’s professional conduct within the scope of his/her assigned
responsibilities.

11.6.2    The information used on the evaluation report, other than that obtained through
observation by the evaluator, shall be verified and identified as to source.

11.6.3    If a member is evaluated in the area outside of his/her teaching credential, the
evaluation form shall so state.

11.6.4    The certificated employee’s evaluator shall take affirmative action to advise and
counsel the employee regarding any cited deficiencies in the evaluation report. The
unit member shall make every effort to improve in those areas noted in the report.
Failure to do so may constitute grounds for dismissal. Such action shall include
specific recommendations for improvement and direct assistance in implementing
such recommendations.

11.6.5    If the member is directed to seek such remedial assistance, the District shall pay for
the registration or tuition fees, materials necessary, and a substitute teacher, if
necessary, providing Education Code section 44664(b) has been invoked in writing.
The member will not receive professional growth credits for purposes of salary
schedule movement in this instance.

If the evaluator has recommended, and the employee has acted to seek remedial
assistance, then the district shall grant professional growth credits for subsequent
salary schedule movement, providing the member has borne whatever tuition and
expenses that may have been required to receive college/university credits or their
equivalents, and the provisions of Article XII have been met.

11.6.6    If subsequent remedial action eliminates the negative evaluation and/or the identified
deficiencies, the evaluation(s) citing such deficiencies shall have a notice to that
effect attached to the member’s permanent personnel file with the report.

ARTICLE XIII

LEAVES

13.1    General Provisions

A.    Upon written request the Board may grant a unit member’s request for a paid or
unpaid leave. In such request, the unit member shall specify the beginning and
ending date of the requested leave.

1) The unit member shall submit the request for leave as soon as possible prior to
the anticipated beginning date.

B.    The granting of any paid or unpaid leave by the Board shall not be precedent with
regard to any other request for leave by any other unit member.

C.    Part-time unit members shall be entitled to a pro rata share of leaves as otherwise
provided in this contract.

D.    For purposes of this article, “immediate family” shall be defined as in Ed Code
44985.

E.    An unpaid leave means a leave without pay and without fringe benefits unless
otherwise expressly provided by the Board.

1) Unit members who have been granted an unpaid leave, may at their own
expense maintain their health/fringe benefits. In order to do so, the unit
member must pay the premium-amounts by the first of each month to the
District.

13.2    Sick Leave

14

A.    Unit members shall be entitled to 10 days of sick leave each year.

B.    Unused, but earned sick leave shall be accumulated from year to year unless
otherwise provided by law.

C.    Accrued sick leave may be used as a credit for retirement. Such practice is
dependent on the rules and regulations of the retirement system and state law.

D.    Absence of 1/2 day or less shall be charged as a half day and absence of more
than a half day shall be charged as a full day.

E.    Sick Leave will be credited to returning unit members as of July 1 of each
contract year. By October 30 the District will provide each unit member a
statement of Sick Leave credit and total Sick Leave accumulation.

F.    Extended Sick Leave - As provided in the Education Code, unit members are
entitled for a long term illness to differential pay for a period not to exceed 5
months or 100 calendar days. Differential pay is determined by the unit
member’s daily rate minus the cost of a replacement teacher whether or not one is
employed.

G.    Personal Necessity

1)    Unit members may use up to 10 days each school year of their accumulated
sick leave for purposes of personal necessity.

2)    Personal Necessity is defined as personal business which cannot be postponed
to non-school time; accident, injury, or illness of a member of the immediate
family; or extension of bereavement leave;

3)    At least one day of notice is required for use of personal necessity except in
emergency situations.

I.    Personal Necessity Leave days are noncumulative.

J.    Pregnancy Leave:

1)    The District shall provide a unit member disabled due to pregnancy,
miscarriage, or childbirth or recovery there from, leave pursuant to sick leave.

2)    The length of such illness leave shall be determined (beginning and ending
dates) by the employee and her physician.

13.3    Bereavement Leave

Unit members shall be granted leave up to 3 days for bereavement involving the death
of one’s immediate family. If travel one way exceeds 300 miles, a leave of up to five
days shall be granted.

13.4    Industrial Accident/Illness Leave

Such leave is limited to 60 days and is covered by the provisions of Education Code
Section 44984.

13.5    Sabbatical Leave

Such leave may be granted by the Board and is subject to the provisions of the
Education Code Sections 44966 et seq.

13.6    Child Rearing Leave

Unit members may be granted an unpaid leave for up to one year for the purpose of
child rearing.

ARTICLE XV

SAFETY

Safe Working Conditions

The District shall make a reasonable effort to maintain safe and healthy working
conditions so that teachers will not be required to perform tasks that endanger their
health, safety, or well-being.

Teachers are encouraged to report conditions which they feel are hazardous or unsafe
to the Superintendent in writing.

The District shall comply with provisions of the California Occupational Safety and
Health Act, as amended (California Labor Code 67300, et seq.) and regulations
relating thereto (8 California Administrative Code Section 330, et seq.). In the event
a CAL-OSHA Compliance Inspection Engineer comes to the District as a result of the
implementation of California Occupational Safety and Health Act, as amended,
and/or regulations relating thereto, the following provisions will take affect:

The Association President or his/her designee shall be immediately contacted and
released with pay to participate in an opening conference between the District’s
representative and a CAL-OSHA Compliance Inspection Engineer.

The Association President or his/her designee shall be afforded release time with pay
to accompany the District representative, if any, and the CAL-OSHA Compliance
Inspection Engineer as the engineer conducts his/her walk-around inspection.

The Association President or his/her designee shall be afforded release time with pay
to be present at a closing conference between any District representative and CAL-
OSHA-Compliance Inspection Engineer.

The District shall, within five (5) working days, provide to the Association, a copy of
any correspondence received by the District from CAL-OSHA regarding such
inspection.

ARTICLE XVI

CLASS SIZE

The District shall attempt to maintain all classroom enrollments at a maximum of

16

twenty three (23) per TK-3 classroom teacher, and a maximum of twenty-nine (29)
students per 4-6 classroom teacher (not including Special Day Class students) subject
to the financial conditions of the District, availability of qualified teachers, and the
best interest of the students.

16.1.1    RSP students shall be included in calculation for total enrollment considered for each
teacher, (e g., mainstreaming).

16.1.2    If the District is unable to comply with the maximum class sizes in 16.1, then TK-6
classroom teachers shall be provided 1.5 hours of aide time daily.

16.2    Should a class size exceed 30, the affected teacher and the Superintendent will meet
within two (2) days to consider alternatives designed to reduce the size of the affected
class.

16.2.1    In the event a mutually acceptable solution is not found, or a solution is identified that
would substantially affect other teacher assignments, the matter will be brought
before the full faculty for discussion and a recommendation.

16.2.2    The Superintendent will consider the recommendation of the total staff in making an
appropriate recommendation to the Board of Education. The teacher may also make a
recommendation to the Board.

16.3    It is agreed that if no other arrangement is mutually agreeable the use of additional
aide time to reduce the impact of the larger class size will be provided by the District.

16.4    Midyear Student Placement

For children enrolled during the course of the school year, the Superintendent-
Principal shall consult with appropriate staff about classroom placement of the
student when there is more than one (1) option. Whenever possible teachers will
have one (1) day of notice prior to actual attendance of the student. It is understood
that in some circumstances, students will be immediately placed on a tentative basis
and that consultation and final placement will occur subsequently. Any background
information available to the District regarding the student will be made available to
the Teacher.
