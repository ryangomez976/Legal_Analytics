Article 1 — Agreement and Duration

1.1    The article and provisions contained within this Agreement constitute a bilateral and
binding agreement by and between the Burlingame School District [District] and the
local chapter of the California Teachers Association which is the Burlingame Education
Association [Association],

1.2    This agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, commencing with Section
3540 of the Government Code of the State of California.

1.3    The provisions will apply to those unit members who are designated in Article 2.2.1

negotiations during this term shall be as follows:

Article 3 — Hours of Employment

3.1    Unit members are to be present at their respective duty assignments or engaged in other

school duties as directed by their immediate supervisors prior to the start of the first
class at the unit member's school site.

assigned to Burlingame Intermediate School (BIS) shall receive at least 350

breaks during the student instructional day. At elementary school sites, these

breaks, lunch break and teacher duty-free preparation periods. The unit

days, which normally will be approved by the Board of Education.

Article 5 — Grievance Procedure

5.1    Definition of Terms

5.1.1    A "grievance" is a claim by a unit member, a group of unit members, or the
Association, that there has been a violation, misinterpretation or misapplication
of the provisions of this contract.

5.1.2    A "grievant" is a member of the bargaining unit or the Association, asserting a
grievance.

5.1.2.1    Both parties agree that prior to Level 4, the grievant(s) may represent
his/herself/themselves, or may select the Association for the purpose of
representation. The grievant(s) will be present at all proceedings in the
processing of a grievance.

5.1.2.2    All grievants will be named when a grievance is filed.

5.1.3    A "day(s)" will be any day in which the grievant is required to render service to
the District. Upon request of the grievant, a grievance may be processed during
any days the District Office is open for business provided all relevant parties are
available.

5.1.4    An "immediate supervisor" is the supervising administrator having immediate
jurisdiction over the grievant(s).

5.2    Purpose

5.2.1 The purpose of this procedure is to use every step of the process in a problem
solving manner and, secure, at the lowest possible administrative level
resolution to issues of interpretation of the provisions of this contract which
may from time to time arise and affect a unit member.

5.3    Procedure

5.3.1 Informal Level

5.3.1.1 Before filing a formal grievance, and within twenty (20) days after the
occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the grievance, or when
the grievant reasonably should have known, the grievant will attempt to
resolve the grievance in a conference with his/her/their immediate
supervisor. The grievant will inform the supervisor that the conference
is the informal level of the grievance procedure.

5.3.2 Formal Level

5.3.2.1    Level One. If the grievant(s) is not satisfied with the disposition of the
grievance at the informal level, and within ten (10) days after the attempt
to resolve the grievance at the informal level, the grievant may file a
formal grievance in writing, on the appropriate form, to her/his/their
immediate supervisor. The form will include statements indicating:

1.    how the grievant has been adversely affected;

2.    the specific section of the contract allegedly violated; and

3.    the specific remedy sought by the grievant to resolve the
grievance.

The immediate supervisor will hold a problem solving conference with
the parties involved and communicate his/her decision to the unit
member in writing within ten (10) days of the filing of the grievance. A
copy of the response will be sent to the president of the Association.

5.3.2.2    Level Two. If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of his/her
grievance at Level One, or if no written decision has been rendered
within ten (10) days, the grievant may appeal the decision to the
superintendent or designee within ten (10) days of the receipt of the
Level One decision. That statement will include a copy of the original
grievance, the decision rendered, and a clear and concise statement will
be submitted on the prescribed form.

5.3.2.2.1    The superintendent or designee will hold a problem solving
conference with the parties involved and communicate a
decision to the grievant in writing within ten (10) days of the
filing of the appeal. A copy of the response will be sent to the
president of the Association.

5.3.2.2.2    If the superintendent or designee does not respond within
limits provided, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

5.3.3 B oard of Trustees - Level 3

5.3.3.1 Level Three. If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of
his/her grievance at Level Two, or if no written decision has been
rendered within ten (10) days, the grievant may appeal the decision and
meet with the Board of Trustees at the next regular Board meeting. If
there is insufficient time for either party to schedule and prepare, the
appeal will be scheduled for the following Board meeting. At least three

(3) days prior to the meeting, a copy of the grievance and the decisions

rendered will be made available to the Board members. Any
information provided to the Board will also be given to the grievant.

5.3.3.2 A facilitator, to be selected by the Board, who is versed in problem

solving techniques will ensure that the meeting proceeds consistent with
the following:

5.3.3.2.1    The format must allow for dialogue between the grievant, the
Board and relevant parties to ensure that the Board receives all
pertinent information. All discussions will be "off the record"
to encourage open discussions and protect the interests of all
parties should the issue be submitted to arbitration.

5.3.3.2.2    The grievant and, a representative if one has been requested
by the grievant, will be provided an opportunity to present a
statement of the issues giving rise to the grievance and the
proposed remedy.

5.3.3.2.3    The superintendent and/or designee, will be provided an
opportunity to present a statement about the responses to the
grievance at each level.

5.3.3.2.4    Within ten (10) days following the hearing, the Board will
provide written notification of its proposed resolution. If the
grievant is not satisfied with this resolution, he/she may
request that the Association submit the grievance to
arbitration.

5.3.4 Arbitration - Level 4

5.3.4.1    The Association may submit a written demand for arbitration pursuant to

4.3.4.2, and send a copy of the demand to the superintendent. Only
issues which were processed and handled in accordance with the
grievance procedure of this Article are subject to arbitration.

5.3.4.1.1    The grievance is resolved if a written request for arbitration is
not submitted within ten (10) days after receipt by the grievant
of the decision from the Board;

5.3.4.1.2    Prior to requesting a panel of names of arbitrators, the
Association and the superintendent will attempt to agree on an
arbitrator.

5.3.4.2    The Association may request that the California State Conciliation
Service supply a panel of seven (7) names of arbitrators experienced in
public sector grievances.

5.3.4.3    Within ten (10) days of the selection of the arbitrator, the superintendent
and the Association will attempt to agree in writing upon the issue or
issues and will disclose evidence to be submitted to the arbitrator. If
they are unable to agree upon a submission statement, the arbitrator will
determine the issues by referring to the written grievances and responses
at each level.

5.3.4.4    In the event there is a dispute between the District and the grievant as to
arbitrability of any issue(s), the question of arbitrability will be ruled
upon by the arbitrator prior to hearing the issue(s) of the grievance.

5.3.4.5    The arbitrator will have no authority to make a decision based upon
arguments not disclosed in Levels One and Two. The arbitrator will
render a written decision on the submitted issue(s) within thirty (30)
days after the close of the hearing; or if an oral hearing has been waived
by both parties, within thirty (30) days after the final submission of
written evidence and final arguments.

5.3 .4.6 The District and the Association agree that the jurisdiction and authority
of the arbitrator so selected in opinions he/she expresses, will be
confined exclusively to the interpretation of the express provision or
provisions of this Agreement at issue between the parties. The arbitrator
will have no authority to add to or subtract from, alter, amend, or modify
the provisions of the Agreement. The decision of the arbitrator within
the limits prescribed, will be final and binding upon the parties to the
dispute.

5.3.4.7 The fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing will be borne
equally the District and the Association. All other expenses will be
borne by the parties incurring them. Unless the parties mutually agree to
share the expenses, the cost of the services and expenses of the court
reporter will be paid by the party requesting the same. If, however, one
of the parties declines to share the expenses of the court reporter and
subsequently requests a transcript of the arbitration proceedings or a
copy thereof, that party will be required to reimburse the other party
one-half of the cost of the court reporter's services and expenses. The
cost of any transcript or any copy thereof requested by either will be
borne by the party requesting the same.

5.3.5 Miscellaneous Provisions

5.3.5.1 Relevant employee witnesses, the grievant(s) and representative will be
provided release time without loss of pay for the purpose of participating
at grievance conferences with appropriate District representatives, and
testifying at the grievance arbitration hearing. Witnesses may be placed
on on-call status and required to attend the arbitration hearing for only
so long as his/her testimony is taken.

5.3.5.2    One half (1/2) day release time will be provided for up to five (5) unit
members who may be involved in the arbitration hearing. The cost of
the release time will be shared equally by the District and the
Association.

5.3.5.3    All documents, communications, and records dealing with the
processing of a grievance will be filed separately from the personnel file
of the grievant(s) and maintained in the office of the superintendent.

5.3.5.4    No reprisals of any kind will be taken by any District representative
against the grievant or any representation of the grievant(s) by reason of
their bringing a grievance or participating in a grievance; nor will any
reprisals of any kind be taken by the Association against either the
grievant(s), representative of the District, or any participation in the
grievance procedure by reason of such participation or decisions.

5.3.5.5    If two or more members of the Association have the same grievance in
issue and fact, then the grievance will be consolidated for purposes of
hearing and decision; provided further, that the rights of the parties are
not thereby prejudiced.

5.3.5.6    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possibly,
the time limits specified at each level should be considered as
maximums and every effort should be made to expedite the process.

The time limits may, however, be extended by mutual written
agreement.

5.3.5.7    If any unit member represents him/herself, any proposed resolution will
not be agreed upon the District until the Association has been provided a
copy and allowed an opportunity to respond.

5.3.5.8    No grievance will be arbitrated (Level Four) without the consent of the
Association.

5.3.5.9    Arbitrators may not award remedies which require a direct money
payment (payout) by the District of more than $5,000 to the grievant or
other unit members similarly situated even if they were not grievants. In
cases where the potential aggregate award exceeds $5,000, the grievant
or Association may seek remedy in any court of competent jurisdiction;
the District waives any requirement for utilization/exhaustion of the
grievance procedure in such cases.

Article 6 — Teacher Safety, Health and Protection

6.1    The safety and health of pupils and employees is of utmost concern to the Board. To
ensure that exposure to unsafe or unhealthy conditions is minimized, certificated unit
members are encouraged to be safety conscious in their own actions and to report, in
writing, any alleged unsafe or potentially unsafe or unhealthy conditions to their
immediate supervisor. The immediate supervisor is to forward such report to the
superintendent and the superintendent is to respond to the unit member via the
immediate supervisor within ten [10] working days as to whether or not action will be
taken on the reported condition. If no action is to be taken, the superintendent will
indicate, in writing, the reason for such inaction.

6.1.1    The District will at all times maintain and enforce standards of safety and
sanitation in conformance with state law.

6.1.2    Unit members and students will not be required to work under unsafe or
hazardous conditions or to perform tasks which may endanger their health,
safety, and their emotional well- being.

6.1.2.1    Unit members will not be required to re-enter the school building during
a bomb threat or other hazardous conditions which may endanger health,
safety, and emotional well-being until an all clear by an official has been
given.

6.1.2.2    In the case of a natural disaster and/or a declared state of emergency,
procedures outlined in the Civil Disaster: Emergency Preparedness
policy adopted in 1990 will be followed.

6.1.3    The Board will, upon recommendation of the student's teacher, exclude from the
class any student who, in the teacher's opinion as a lay person has an infectious
or contagious disease which would cause his attendance to be adverse to the
welfare of other students.

6.1.3.1 A student excluded as above will not be entitled to return until the
condition no longer exists, as certified by an appropriate medical
practitioner.

6.1.4    Each school site will provide access to a telephone at all times to all unit
members.

6.2    Unit members will immediately report cases of verbal/non-verbal threat of violence
and/or physical assault and/or harassment suffered by them in connection with their
employment. This report will be submitted to their immediate supervisor who will
immediately report the incident to the police or other appropriate authority.

6.2.1    The unit member will provide the superintendent with written notice of the fact.
The superintendent will transmit such report to the Board. The Board and the
superintendent will comply with any request from the unit member for
information in their possession related to the incident and persons involved, and
will otherwise cooperate with the unit member in the event of a civil or criminal
proceeding.

6.2.2    Unit members will not forfeit any sick leave or personal leave when absence
arises out of or from an assault or injury while acting in the discharge of their
duties.

6.2.2.1 Industrial accident leave will be provided by the Board.

6.3    Unit members may use and apply such amount of force as is necessary and reasonable
to protect themselves from attack or to quell a disturbance threatening physical injury to
others. Unit members will inform their principal of such an incident.

6.4    In order for the unit members to carry out their responsibilities regarding discipline,
the principal will meet with his/her faculty at the beginning of the school year, but
no later than October 1, for the purpose of jointly discussing with the unit members
the guidelines and procedures for student behavior, appropriate consequences, and
administrative follow-up in the school. The principal will establish a written procedure
for handling discipline in his absence to preserve the consistency of such procedure.

6.4.1    A student who acts in such a way that the unit member believes learning cannot
take place in his/her classroom will be removed from the classroom upon the
unit member's recommendation.

6.4.2    A unit member may suspend a student from his or her classroom for the day of
the occurrence and the following day. He/she will report the suspension to the
principal and send the student to the principal for appropriate action.

Article 8 — Just Cause/Due Process

The Burlingame School District and Burlingame Education Association mutually agree that the
high professional standards of the District will be adhered to and will be applied in a
consistent, fair manner to support professional accountability. It is the intent of this section to
provide a supportive environment for unit members. Due process will be separate from the
professional assessment plan. The District may discipline a unit member only for just cause.

8.1    Progressive Di scipline

In handling professional accountability the following progressive discipline will be
used. Progressive discipline means the level of discipline will be tailored to fit the
severity of the offense; therefore, the serious nature of the offense may require by
passing progressive discipline steps.

8.1.1 The following progressive discipline procedures will be applied except where
the serious nature of the offense may require the District to directly impose a
written warning, a written reprimand, or suspension without pay.

8.1.1.1    Verbal Counseling/Waming

Verbal counseling/warning may result in a post-conference summary
memorandum. The post-conference summary memorandum will not be
placed in the unit member's personnel file.

8.1.1.2    Written Warning

Subject to Article 8.1.1 above, written warnings will not be used unless
the unit member has been verbally warned about similar actions within
the last twenty-four (24) months. Written warnings will not be placed in
the unit member's file.

8.1.1.3    Written Reprimand

Subject to Article 8.1.1 above, a written reprimand will not be used
unless the unit member has received a written warning about similar
actions within the last twenty-four (24) months. The unit member will
sign the reprimand to acknowledge receipt, and a copy may be placed in
the unit member's personnel file. The Association member has 20
workdays in which to respond and the response will be attached to the
reprimand in the personnel file as per contract Article 9.3.

8.1.1.4    Suspension Without Pay

Subject to Article 8.1.1 above, suspension will not be used unless the
unit member has received a written reprimand about similar actions
within the last twenty-four (24) months. No unit member will be
suspended more than fifteen (15) working days during a school year. In
all instances, however, the length of a suspension will relate to the
severity of the action and/or suspension history of the unit member.

person by the superintendent or designee or by certified mail. A copy will be
provided to the Association president. The notice of suspension will contain:

based;

8.1.2.2    A statement of the cause(s) for which action is recommended;

8.1.2.3    Where applicable, the Education code section, policy, rule, regulation,
or directive violated;

8.1.2.4    Penalty proposed and effective date;

8.2    Notice After Action Has Been Taken

8.3    Appeal Procedure

8.3.1    Right to Appeal

Only suspension without pay may be appealed to arbitration pursuant to Article
5, Section 5.3.4 (pg. 10). If timely appealed, the penalty will not be applied
until the arbitrator's decision is rendered, except for just cause necessitating the
immediate removal of the unit member from the worksite. At the arbitration,
documentation supporting the suspension, including written reprimands, may be
subject to review by the arbitrator.

8.3.2    Request for Appeal

The unit member must request an appeal by delivering written notice of appeal to the
superintendent in person or by certified mail within ten (10) working days after receipt
of the notice of suspension or written reprimand. If the unit member does not demand
an appeal within the above timeline, the suspension without pay may be imposed
immediately by the superintendent or designee.

All information or proceedings regarding any actions or proposed actions pursuant to
this Article will be kept confidential by the parties to the extent permitted by law.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE TIME LIMITS

PURPOSE: The purpose of the grievance procedure is to use every step of the process in a problem
solving manner and, secure, at the lowest possible administrative level resolution to issues of
interpretation of the provisions of this contract which may from time to time arise and affect a unit
member. The time limits listed below are established in the Contract between the Burlingame School
District Board of Education and the Burlingame Education Association.

DEFINITIONS:

1.    A “grievance” is a claim by a unit member, a group of unit members, or the Association, that
there has been a violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of the provisions of this contract.

2.    A “grievanf ’ is a member of the bargaining unit or the Association, asserting a grievance.

3.    A “day(s)’ will be any day in which the grievant is required to render service to the District. Upon
request of the grievant, a grievance may be processed during any days the District Office is open
for business provided all relevant parties are available.

4.    An “immediate supervisor” is the supervising administrator having immediate jurisdiction over
the grievant(s).

INFORMAL RESOLUTION: Unit member must initiate a conference with the immediate supervisor
within TWENTY (20) working days of the time he/she had knowledge of the act or omission giving rise
to the grievance. If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of the informal grievance or no
decision has been rendered within TEN (10) days, the grievant may appeal the decision to the next level.

FORMAL WRITTEN GRIEVANCE LEVELS: The unit member must submit, to the immediate
supervisor, “Grievance Form - Level 1” within TEN (10) working days after the conference at the
informal level.

DECISION ON FORMAL WRITTEN GRIEVANCE: Decisions by the immediate supervisor.

Sexual Harassment

The Board of Trustees prohibits sexual harassment of district employees and job applicants. The Board also
prohibits retaliatory behavior or action against district employees or other persons who complain, testify or
otherwise participate in the complaint process established pursuant to this policy and administrative
regulation.

(cf. 0410 - Nondiscrimination in District Programs and Activities)

(cf. 4030 - Nondiscrimination in Employment)

The Superintendent or designee shall take all actions necessary to ensure the prevention, investigation, and
correction of sexual harassment, including but not limited to:

1.    Providing training to employees in accordance with law and administrative regulation

2.    Publicizing and disseminating the district's sexual harassment policy to staff
(cf. 4112.9/4212.9/4312.9 - Employee Notifications)

3.    Ensuring prompt, thorough, and fair investigation of complaints

4.    Taking timely and appropriate corrective/remedial action(s), which may require interim separation of the
complainant and the alleged harasser and subsequent monitoring of developments

All complaints and allegations of sexual harassment shall be kept confidential to the extent necessary to carry
out the investigation or to take other subsequent necessary actions. (5 CCR 4964)

Any district employee or job applicant who feels that he/she has been sexually harassed or who has
knowledge of any incident of sexual harassment by or against another employee, a job applicant or a student,
shall immediately report the incident to his/her supervisor, the principal, district administrator or
Superintendent.

A supervisor, principal or other district administrator who receives a harassment complaint shall promptly
notify the Superintendent or designee.

Complaints of sexual harassment shall be filed in accordance with AR 4031 - Complaints Concerning
Discrimination in Employment. An employee may bypass his/her supervisor in filing a complaint where the
supervisor is the subject of the complaint.

(cf. 4031 - Complaints Concerning Discrimination in Employment)

Any district employee who engages or participates in sexual harassment or who aids, abets, incites, compels,
or coerces another to commit sexual harassment against a district employee, job applicant, or student is in
violation of this policy and is subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

(cf. 4117.4 - Dismissal)

(cf. 4118 - Suspension/Disciplinary Action)

(cf. 4218 - Dismissal/Suspension/Disciplinary Action)

Sexual Harassment

Definitions

Prohibited sexual harassment includes, but is not limited to, unwelcome sexual advances, unwanted requests
for sexual favors, or other unwanted verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature made against
another person of the same or opposite sex in the work or educational setting when: (Education Code 212.5; 5
CCR 4916)

1.    Submission to the conduct is made explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of the individual's
employment.

2.    Submission to or rejection of such conduct by the individual is used as the basis for an employment
decision affecting him/her.

3.    The conduct has the purpose or effect of having a negative impact upon the individual's work or has the
purpose or effect of creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment. The conduct is
sufficiently severe, persistent, pervasive, or objectively offensive so as to create a hostile or abusive working
environment or to limit the individual's ability to participate in or benefit from an education program or
activity.

4.    Submission to or rejection of the conduct by the other individual is used as the basis for any decision
affecting him/her regarding benefits, services, honors, programs, or activities available at or through the
district.

Other examples of actions that might constitute sexual harassment, whether committed by a supervisor, a
co-worker, or a non-employee, in the work or educational setting, include, but are not limited to:

1.    Unwelcome verbal conduct such as sexual flirtations or propositions; graphic comments about an
individual's body; overly personal conversations or pressure for sexual activity; sexual jokes or stories;
unwelcome sexual slurs, epithets, threats, innuendoes, derogatory comments, sexually degrading descriptions,
or the spreading of sexual rumors

2.    Unwelcome visual conduct such as drawings, pictures, graffiti, or gestures; sexually explicit emails;
displaying sexually suggestive objects

3.    Unwelcome physical conduct such as massaging, grabbing, fondling, stroking, or brushing the body;
touching an individual's body or clothes in a sexual way; cornering, blocking, leaning over, or impeding
normal movements

Prohibited sexual harassment may also include any act of retaliation against an individual who reports a
violation of the district's sexual harassment policy or who participates in the investigation of a sexual
harassment complaint.

Training

Every two years, the Superintendent or designee shall ensure that supervisory employees receive at least two
hours of classroom or other effective interactive training and education regarding sexual harassment. All

newly hired or promoted supervisory employees shall receive training within six months of their assumption
of the supervisory position. (Government Code 12950.1)

The district's training and education program for supervisory employees shall include information and
practical guidance regarding the federal and state laws on the prohibition against and the prevention and
correction of sexual harassment, and the remedies available to the victims of sexual harassment in
employment. The training shall also include all of the content specified in 2 CCR 7288.0 and practical
examples aimed at instructing supervisors in the prevention of harassment, discrimination, and retaliation.
(Government Code 12950.1; 2 CCR 7288.0)

In addition, the Superintendent or designee shall ensure that all employees receive periodic training regarding
the district's sexual harassment policy, particularly the procedures for filing complaints and employees' duty
to use the district's complaint procedures.

Notifications

A copy of the Board policy and this administrative regulation shall: (Education Code 231.5)

1.    Be displayed in a prominent location in the main administrative building, district office, or other area of
the school where notices of district rules, regulations, procedures, and standards of conduct are posted

2.    Be provided to each faculty member, all members of the administrative staff, and all members of the
support staff at the beginning of the first quarter or semester of the school year or whenever a new employee
is hired

(cf. 4112.9/4212.9/4312.9 - Employee Notifications)

3.    Appear in any school or district publication that sets forth the school's or district's comprehensive rules,
regulations, procedures, and standards of conduct

All employees shall receive either a copy of information sheets prepared by the California Department of Fair
Employment and Housing (DFEH) or a copy of district information sheets that contain, at a minimum,
components on: (Government Code 12950)

1.    The illegality of sexual harassment

2.    The definition of sexual harassment under applicable state and federal law

3.    A description of sexual harassment, with examples

4.    The district's complaint process available to the employee

(cf. 4031 - Complaints Concerning Discrimination in Employment)

5.    The legal remedies and complaint process available through DFEH and the Equal Employment
Opportunity Commission (EEOC)

6.    Directions on how to contact DFEH and the EEOC

7.    The protection against retaliation provided by 2 CCR 7287.8 for opposing harassment prohibited by law or
for filing a complaint with or otherwise participating in an investigation, proceeding, or hearing conducted by

DFEH and the EEOC

In addition, the district shall post, in a prominent and accessible location, DFEH's poster on discrimination in
employment and the illegality of sexual harassment. (Government Code 12950)

SMART GOALS - TEMPLATE

SMART goals help improve achievement and success. A SMART goal clarifies exactly what is
expected and the measures used to determine if the goal is achieved and successfully
completed.

A SMART goal is:

Specific (and strategic): Linked to position summary, departmental goals/mission, and/or
overall school/district goals and strategic plans. Answers the questions-Who? What?

Measurable: The success toward meeting the goal can be measured. Answers the question-
How?

Attainable: Goals are realistic and can be achieved in a specific amount of time and are
reasonable. Answers the question-How?

Relevant (results oriented): The goals are aligned with current tasks and projects and focus in
one defined area; include the expected result. Answers the question- Why?

Time framed: Goals have a clearly defined time-frame including a target or deadline date.
Answers the question-When?

Examples:

Not a SMART goal:

•    Student will improve their writing skills.

Does not identify a measurement or timeframe, nor does it identify why the improvement is
needed or how it will be used.

SMART goal:

•    By June 2013, 90% of my third grade students will know their multiplication facts from
0-12 with 100% accuracy, as measured by classroom assessments conducted bi-monthly
(provide standard content if applicable).

Burlingame SD | 1000 | AR 1312.1 Community Relations
Complaints Concerning District Employees

The Superintendent or designee shall determine whether a complaint should be considered a complaint
against the district and/or an individual employee, and whether it should be resolved by the district's process
for complaints concerning personnel and/or other district procedures.

(cf. 1312.2 - Complaints Concerning Instructional Materials)

(cf. 1312.3 - Uniform Complaint Procedures)

(cf. 4144/4244/4344 - Complaints)

To promote prompt and fair resolution of the complaint, the following procedures shall govern the resolution
of complaints against district employees:

1.    Every effort should be made to resolve a complaint at the earliest possible stage. Whenever possible, the
complainant should communicate directly to the employee in order to resolve concerns.

2.    If a complainant is unable or unwilling to resolve the complaint directly with the employee, he/she may
submit an oral or written complaint to the employee's immediate supervisor or the principal.

3.    All complaints related to district personnel other than administrators shall be submitted in writing to the
principal or immediate supervisor. If the complainant is unable to prepare the complaint in writing,
administrative staff shall help him/her to do so. Complaints related to a principal or central office
administrator shall be initially filed in writing with the Superintendent or designee. Complaints related to the
Superintendent shall be initially filed in writing with the Board.

4.    When a written complaint is received, the employee shall be notified within five days or in accordance
with collective bargaining agreements.

5.    A written complaint shall include:

a.    The full name of each employee involved

b.    A brief but specific summary of the complaint and the facts surrounding it

c.    A specific description of any prior attempt to discuss the complaint with the employee and the failure to
resolve the matter

6.    Staff responsible for investigating complaints shall attempt to resolve the complaint to the satisfaction of
the parties involved within 30 days.

7.    Both the complainant and the employee against whom the complaint was made may appeal a decision by
the principal or immediate supervisor to the Superintendent or designee, who shall attempt to resolve the
complaint to the satisfaction of the person involved within 30 days. Parties should consider and accept the
Superintendent or designee's decision as final. However, the complainant, the employee, or the
Superintendent or designee may ask to address the Board regarding the complaint.

8.    Before any Board consideration of a complaint, the Superintendent or designee shall submit to the Board a
written report concerning the complaint, including but not limited to:

a.    The full name of each employee involved

b.    A brief but specific summary of the complaint and the facts surrounding it, sufficient to inform the Board
and the parties as to the precise nature of the complaint and to allow the parties to prepare a response

c.    A copy of the signed original complaint

d.    A summary of the action taken by the Superintendent or designee, together with his/her specific finding
that the problem has not been resolved and the reasons

9.    The Board may uphold the Superintendent's decision without hearing the complaint.

10.    All parties to a complaint may be asked to attend a Board meeting in order to clarify the issue and present
all available evidence.

11.    A closed session may be held to hear the complaint in accordance with law.

(cf. 9321 - Closed Session Purposes and Agendas)

(cf. 9323 - Meeting Conduct)

12.    The decision of the Board shall be final.

Any complaint of child abuse or neglect alleged against a district employee shall be reported to the
appropriate local agencies in accordance with law. Board policy and administrative regulation.

(cf. 5141.4 - Child Abuse Prevention and Reporting)
