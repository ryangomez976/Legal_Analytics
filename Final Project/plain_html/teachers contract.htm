ARTICLE 1
AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
('Agreement") by and between the Mark Twain Board of Trustees (“District" or "Board") and
the Mark Twain Unit of the Angels Copper Educators / California Teachers Association /
National Education Association (“Association").

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Section 3540-3549, of the
Government Code.

ARTICLE 2
RECOGNITION

2.1    Inclusions

The Board recognizes the Association to be the exclusive representative for the following
unit of non-supervisory certificated employees:

All Permanent Teachers
All Provisional Teachers
All Part-Time Teachers
All Counselors

2.2    Exclusions

The Board further recognize that the jurisdiction of the Association shall not include
management personnel, as defined by Chapter 10.7, Section 3540.1(c), (g) and (m) of the
Government Code, or substitute employees.

2.3    Membership    of the individual employee    in ACE/CTA/NEA is    not required.

2.4    The Board    and the    Association    (the    "parties")    recognize    and    declare    that providing a quality

education for the children of Mark Twain Union Elementary School District is their mutual aim,
and that the character of such education is influenced by the quality and morale of the teaching
services.

2.5    The parties recognize that the development of educational policies and programs can best be
accomplished by teachers, administrators, and the Board working together.

2.6    The Board has agreed to negotiate in good faith with respect to wages, hours, and other
terms and conditions of employment, and now, having reached agreement on all such matters,
desires to execute this contract covering such an agreement.

2.7    The parties, following extended and deliberate professional negotiations, have reached certain
understandings which they desire to memorialize.

ARTICLE 3
ACADEMIC AND PERSONAL FREEDOM

3.1 Unit members shall be entitled in private life to full rights of citizenship. The Board shall not
inquire into, nor predicate any adverse action upon, a teacher's private, political, and
organizational activities or preferences unless they adversely affect classroom performance,
providing that these activities do not violate any reasonable District regulation and/or state or
federal law.

3.2. Academic Freedom

3.2.1    Teachers must be free to teach and students free to learn. Teachers must have access to,
and be free to explore and discuss, issues and divergent points of view in a
responsible manner. (Refer to BP 1312.2)

3.2.2    The District shall not interfere in a unit member's freedom to exercise professional
judgment in speech, skills creativity, and materials while teaching the curriculum and
managing the classroom according to District-adopted content, state and federal law, and
professional ethics.

3.3 Personal Freedom

3.3.1    Teachers must be free to evaluate, criticize and/or advocate personal points of view
concerning policies and/or programs of the school. Teachers must be free to assist
their colleagues when their academic or professional freedoms are violated.

3.3.2    Teachers must be employed, promoted, or retained without discrimination or
harassment regarding their personal opinions or their scholarly, literary, or
artistic endeavors.

reassignment in accordance with Articles 17-18.

6.2.2    The Screening and Selection Committee shall be composed of three (3) teachers, one (1)
administrator and one (1) Board member except that ACE and the administration may
agree to proceed with more or fewer members.

6.3.1    If the District anticipates offering Summer School, the District will establish a Summer

Director who shall be a teacher in the district. Other members of the Committee shall
include an administrator appointed by the District, the Superintendent, one (1)

Board member and two (2) additional teachers.

6.3.2    By April 30th, the Committee shall determine the goals, curriculum and budget for
the summer session subject to the approval of the Board of Trustees. To the
extent feasible, the summer session shall be financially self-supporting. The Board
shall approve the guidelines.

6.3.3    The hiring process will begin immediately following the adoption of the guidelines.

In selecting teachers for the summer session, the Superintendent and the Director shall
give preference to teachers already employed by the District commensurate with the
goals of the program and the background and experience of the applicants. The final
staff selections shall be approved by the Board of Trustees.

6.4.1.2    This Committee will conduct a paper screening and background check of the
applicants. All acceptable applicants will be forwarded to the Interview
Committee.

6.4.2    Phase II - Interview Committee

6.4.2.1    The Interview Committee will consist of eight (8) representatives. The
committee will include three (3) teachers (one [1] carried over from Phase
I), one (1) administrator, two (2) School Site Council parent
representatives, one (1) Board Member, and one (1) classified member. The
teachers, parents, and classified member will represent the school where the
principal vacancy exists.

6.4.2.2    The Association will select the teacher representatives. The Board and
Superintendent will select the administrator and the Board Member to serve. The
full-time classified staff members of the school will determine their
representative. The School Site parent representative will be selected by
parent members of the School Site Council.

6.4.2.3    The Interview Committee will recommend all acceptable candidates (no fewer

10

than two [2] candidates) to the Superintendent. The Interview Committee
will meet with the board to discuss the recommended candidates; the
recommended candidates will not be identified by priority.

6.4.2.4 Should none of the candidates be regarded as acceptable, the position will be
readvertised and the process reimplemented, beginning with Phase I.

6.4.3 Phase III - Final Interview

The Superintendent and the two (2) Board members will interview all recommended
finalists. The Superintendent will make the final recommendation to the Mark Twain

ARTICLE 7
PROCEDURAL RULES AND REGULATIONS

7.1    Negotiations Committee

The Negotiations Committee shall consist of four (4) teachers, one (1) Board member, and
one (1) administrator. This Committee shall be responsible for all negotiations which relate to
the bargaining unit contract.

7.2. Negotiations for the renewal of the Contract shall commence not less than ninety (90) or
more than one hundred fifty (150) calendar days prior to the end of the current contract.

7.3    At any time, the District or the Association may, by mutual agreement, initiate negotiations for
individual contract items. This shall be initiated by submission of a written request to the other
party, at which time the negotiation process shall commence as stipulated by Article 7.

7.4    Conduct Governing Bargaining Sessions:

The following rules and regulations shall guide the conduct during all bargaining sessions
between the Association and the Board or its designated representative(s), except as provided in
Article 7, Subsection 7.1.6.5:

7.4.1    Negotiations shall be conducted in an atmosphere of good faith and mutual respect
for those representing their respective agencies. Negotiations regarding all matters
shall be conducted by the Negotiations Committee as stipulated in Article 7.

7.4.2    Each party shall retain the right to establish and implement rules governing its own
conduct outside negotiating sessions.

7.4.3    It shall be understood that the Association and the Board's designated
representative(s) shall serve in the bargaining process as agents of their respective
agencies and shall reach tentative agreement based only upon instructions received by
those agencies.

11

7.4.4    The committee bargaining sessions are private sessions. However, either party shall have
the right to bring in any consultants it deems necessary. Such consultants shall be
responsible to and directed by their sponsor.

7.4.5    Either side may caucus as necessary during the committee bargaining sessions.

7.4.6    Information regarding budget preparations, audits and fiscal accounting, enrollment,
proposed policies as they may relate to the Education Code, amendments to policies
and all other matters as may be of interest except items which are confidential, shall be
submitted to the Association representative upon written request. In some
instances, when the preparation and gathering of such information requires time, the
Association may request that the materials be gathered within a reasonable time and
stipulated date.

7.4.7    It is understood by the Association and the Administration and the Board's
representative that certificated personnel serving on Board and/or administratively
appointed study panels, commissions or committees, do not represent the recognized
Association. The results of such studies shall be subject to negotiations upon proper
notice as declared elsewhere in these regulations.

7.4.8    Amendments to these procedural rules and regulations shall be addressed as outlined in

Article 7.

7.5 Negotiation sessions may be called by either party through the following procedure:

7.5.1    The Board or the Association may initiate negotiations upon submission of a written
request to the other party. The written request shall include a proposed date and
agenda for the initial meeting.

7.5.2    At the initial meeting, a specific negotiations schedule and agenda shall be mutually
approved. Items to be discussed shall be restricted to the agenda.

7.5.3    Additional negotiating sessions shall be conducted until such time as agreement or
impasse is reached.

7.5.4    Each bargaining session shall last so long as both parties agree to remain and each
agenda item shall be discussed and negotiated for whatever duration of time as may be
determined by the two (2) parties.

7.5.5    Negotiating sessions shall take place only by mutual consent. There shall be one set of
mutually agreed upon minutes.

7.5.6    Either party may declare postponement of an agenda item when, in its judgment, more
time is required to prepare for that item. Deferment of an item shall be made no more
than once without the consent of the other party.

7.5.7    Upon reaching agreement on an item, the item shall be reduced to writing prior to
the end of the negotiating session. Both parties shall review the written agreement

and shall sign the agreement as representatives of their respective agencies only when
they are satisfied that said agreement clearly states the intent of both parties,
clearly identifies how the agreement shall be implemented, identifies the effective
date of the implementation, and truly represents the instructions of both parties. Said
agreement shall be tentative until both agencies have ratified the agreement.
Presentation of the tentative agreement to the Board and the Association shall be
made by mutual consent. Upon final adoption, written copies of the agreement shall
be supplied by the District to the Administrator, the representative of the Association
and each member of the Board.

7.5.8    When the Board or the Association rejects a tentative agreement, said agreement shall
be referred back immediately to the bargaining process for further negotiations prior
to entering into any form of dispute processing.

7.5.9    News releases shall be made by mutual agreement.

7.6 Resolution of Persistent Disagreement

7.6.1    "Persistent disagreement" or "impasse" means an impasse as defined in Chapter 10.7,
Section 3540.1(f) of the Government Code, as amended.

7.6.2    Impasse may be declared provided thirty (30) calendar days have elapsed since
the issue(s) that are at impasse were initially submitted for negotiations and the
parties mutually agree that an impasse exists and mutually agree to submit the issues(s)
to the impasse procedure.

7.6.3    A good faith effort will be made to find a person to serve as mediator.

7.6.4    If the parties cannot agree upon a mediator within five (5) working days, then
a fact finding panel shall be formed. Each party will select, within five (5) working
days, a representative to the panel. The third member, who shall serve as
chairman, will either be mutually agreeable to the representatives of the parties or,
if agreement is not reached within ten (10) working days, then he/she will be
selected from a list provided by the American Arbitration Association (AAA)
or the California Conciliation Service (CCS).

7.6.5    The panel shall hold hearings, make inquiries and investigations that it deems
necessary. The panel shall make findings of fact and terms of agreement which will
be binding on all parties. Such findings may be made public upon mutual agreement
of the Board and Association.

7.6.6    The cost of the chairman of the panel shall be equally shared by the parties.

Each party shall pay for its own representative.

ARTICLE 9
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

9.1    Definitions

9.1.1    A "grievance" is a claim by one (1) or more unit members of the Association that there
has been a violation, misinterpretation or a misapplication of any provision of this
Agreement.

9.1.2    The "grievant" is the unit member, unit members or the Association making the
claim.

9.1.3    A "party in interest" is any person who might be required to take action or against
whom action might be taken in order to resolve the grievance.

9.1.4    A "day" is any day in which the grievant is required by contract to render service.

9.1.5    The "immediate supervisor" is the site principal.

9.1.6    The "grievance facilitator" is the grievance processor.

9.2    Purpose

9.2.1 Level 1 - Immediate Supervisor

9.2.1.1    In the event that a grievant believes that there is a basis for a
grievance, he/she or the Association shall first informally discuss the alleged
grievance with the Site Administrator with the objective of resolving
informally.

9.2.1.2    If, as a result of the informal discussion with the Site Administrator, a
grievance still exists, the grievant may invoke the following formal
grievance steps:

9.2.1.2.1 Within 10 days after the informal complaint, a grievance shall be
presented in writing to the immediate supervisor, the

days of receipt of the grievance. The immediate supervisor shall
provide a written disposition of the grievance, including the
reasons therefore, to all parties of interest within ten (10) days of
such meeting.

agreeable recognized resolution center, be assigned to assist the parties in
the resolution of the grievance.

9.2.3.2    The mediator, within ten (10) days of the assignment, shall meet with the

grievance.

9.2.3.3    If an agreement is reached, the agreement shall be reduced to writing and shall
be signed by the grievant, the Association and the District. This agreement
shall be non-precedential and shall constitute a settlement of the grievance.

9.2.3.4    In the event that the grievant, the Association and the Superintendent or
his/her designee have not resolved the grievance with the assistance of the
conciliator/mediator within ten (10) days of the first meeting held by the
conciliator/mediator, the Association may terminate Level 3 and the grievance
may proceed to Level 4, unless an extension of time has been agreed to in
writing by all parties at interest.

9.2.4 Level 4 - Binding Arbitration

9.2.4.1    If the Association proceeds to arbitration, it shall notify the District in
writing. Within ten (10) days of such notification, representatives of
the District and the Association shall attempt to agree upon a mutually
acceptable arbitrator and obtain a commitment from said arbitrator to serve.

If the parties are unable to agree upon an arbitrator within the specified
period, the Association shall file a Demand to Arbitrate with the American
Arbitration Association. The selection of the arbitrator and the arbitration
proceedings shall be conducted under the Voluntary Arbitration Rules of
the American Arbitration Association. The Association and the District shall
each pay one-half (1/2) of any charges required by the American Arbitration
Association for services rendered.

9.2.4.2    The arbitrator's decision shall be in writing and set forth the findings of fact,
reasoning, and conclusions of the issues submitted. The arbitrator shall be
without power or authority to make any decision which requires the
commission of an act prohibited by law and which is violative of the terms of
this Agreement. However, it is agreed that the arbitrator is empowered to
include in any award such financial reimbursement or other remedies as
he/she judges to be proper. The decision of the arbitrator will be submitted
to the Association and the Superintendent and will be final and binding on all
the parties. If any question arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such
question will be ruled upon by the arbitrator only after he/she has had an
opportunity to hear the merits of the grievance.

9.2.4.3    All costs for the service of the arbitrator, including but not limited to per diem
expenses, travel and subsistence expenses and the costs of the hearing room,
will be borne equally by the District and the Association. All other
costs, except for released time for the grievant(s). Association
representative(s) and witnesses, will be borne by the party incurring them.

9.3 Time Limits

9.3.1    Time limits provided for each level shall begin the day following the receipt of the
grievance, appeal or written decision.

9.3.2    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time limits
should be made to expedite the process. The time limits may, however, be

9.3.3    In the event a grievance is filed within thirty (30) days prior to the last teaching day
of any year, and if left unresolved, harms the grievant, the time limits set forth herein
will be reduced so that the procedure may be exhausted prior to the end of the school
year or as soon as is practicable.

9.3.4    Grievance related to safety shall commence with the Superintendent level.

9.4    Rights of Representation

A grievant may be represented at all stages of the grievance by (an) Association
representative(s), and/or any other person selected by the grievant.

9.5    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by the District or by any member or representative of
the administration of the Board against any grievant, any party in interest, any bargaining
unit member, the Association, or any other participant in the grievance procedure by
reason of such participation.

9.6    Miscellaneous

9.6.1    The Association, either in its own behalf, or on behalf of more than one affected
unit member, may initiate a grievance at Level 2.

9.6.2    If a grievance arises from action or inaction of the District at a level above the
principal or immediate supervisor, the grievant shall submit such grievance in writing

ARTICLE 10
COMPLAINT POLICY

10.1    Parents are always welcome to visit the school and to attend school board meetings. It is
recognized that parents may, at times, have complaints regarding curriculum and/or personnel.

10.2    All such complaints should be initiated by a conference with the complainant and teacher. If a
complaint is made directly to the Board of Trustees or to an individual Board member, the
complainant shall be advised to take the matter to the appropriate school staff member. This will
be the unit member involved, and/or the site administrator.

10.3    In dealing with such complaints, the following procedures shall be used:

10.3.1    STEP ONE: When a complaint is directed to or received by an administrator, the unit
member shall be notified expeditiously of the nature of the complaint in order for an
equitable solution to be found. The unit member and the appropriate administrator shall
meet and mutually agree on a possible resolution of the matter. The unit member may
have a representative at this meeting.

10.3.2    STEP TWO: If the complaint is not satisfactorily resolved, the complaintant
shall submit his/her complaint in writing to the administrator concerned stating the
reasons for the complaint. The administrator shall meet with and provide a copy of the
written complaint to the unit members against whom the complaint is registered. This
meeting shall be held to explore the nature of the complaint and shall take place within
five (5) working days of the receipt of a written complaint. The unit member may have a
representative at this meeting. The administrator shall then meet with complainant at a
mutually convenient time not to exceed ten (10) working days after receipt of the written
complaint. Within fifteen (15) working days of this meeting, the administrator shall
provide a written response to the complaintant with a copy to the unit member stating the
reasons for the decision.

10.3.3    STEP THREE: In the event the matter is not yet resolved, the complainant may file a
written appeal to the Superintendent within ten (10) working days of the final meeting in
Step Two. The written appeal shall include the reasons for the appeal and a copy of the

18

site administrator’s decision. The Superintendent may then meet with complainant, the
administrator, and the unit member involved within ten (10) working days of the receipt
of the written appeal. The unit member may have a representative at this meeting. The
Superintendent shall conduct an objective investigation into the complaint. Within five

(5) working days of the conclusion of the investigation, the superintendent shall
communicate his/ her decisions with supporting reasons in writing to the complainant, to
the administrator, and to the unit member involved.

10.3.4 STEP FOUR: Within ten (10) working days of receiving the decision of

The Superintendent, the complaintant may file a written appeal, accompanied by a copy
of the Superintendent’s decision, to the Board of Education.

The Board may uphold the Superintendent’s decision without granting the complainant a hearing.

The Board may request that all parties to a complaint attend a Board meeting in order to clarify the issue

and present all available evidence.

A closed session may be held to hear the complaint in accordance with the law.

The Board’s decision is final.

10.4    Complaints that are withdrawn or shown to be false, shall neither be placed in the unit member’s
personnel file nor utilized in any evaluation or disciplinary action against the unit member.

10.5    Due process steps and procedures are as follows:

10.5.1    All charges will be made in writing and signed by the individual(s) presenting the
accusations.

10.5.2    The charges as presented in writing shall be shared with the certificated staff member
at least eight (8) teaching days prior to the Board of Education meeting. The
staff member(s) shall also be notified of the right to representation at this time.

10.5.3    A charge by the public will be reviewed and discussed in executive or closed
session with the employee's (s') right to attend and be represented, unless the
employee(s) request in writing a public hearing of these charges.

10.5.4    The Board and/or Superintendent will make no public comment regarding any
charges until after a thorough review and investigation has been conducted as
defined in this section.

teacher for counsel fees incurred by his/her defense, and shall reimburse the teacher for any
loss of salary.

development and peer assistance. This shall be a Peer Assistance and Review (PAR)
Program, hereafter referred to as “Program”, for all teachers. Teachers referred to or who
volunteer for the Program are viewed as valuable professionals who deserve to have the best
resources available provided to them in the interest of improving performance to a successful
standard. The Program shall have three components: Participating Teachers, Peer Assistance
and Review Panel (PAR), and Consulting Teachers.

ARTICLE 17
TRANSFER AND REASSIGNMENT

17.1 Definitions

17.1.1    A "transfer" is a change in regular work location from one school or facility to
another.

17.1.2    A "reassignment" is a change in grade level in grades K-6 or a change in subjects in
grades 7-8.

17.1.3 A "vacancy" is any position that does not have a unit member assigned to it. This
includes any vacated, promotional, or newly created position.

17.1.4 "Seniority" is defined as the unit member's initial date of service in the bargaining
unit.

17.1.4.1    Unit members with the same initial date of service shall have their
seniority number determined by lot.

17.1.4.2    The lottery shall be conducted in the presence of at least two (2)
Association representatives.

17.1.4.3    Once this lottery is used to determine a unit member's seniority, that
seniority shall remain in effect while employed in the District.

17.1.4.4    Seniority shall be the determining factor in granting all transfers,
assignments and reassignments.

17.2 Exceptions

This article shall not apply in the following cases:

17.2.1    A unit member returning from unpaid leave after forty-five (45) days or longer.

17.2.2    Any District-initiated transfer or reassignment agreed to in writing by the unit
member.

17.3    Vacancies

17.3.1    Upon knowledge of vacancies, the District shall deliver to the Association and
post in all school buildings a list of all vacancies which occur during the school year
and for the following school year.

17.3.2    During the summer months, notices of a vacancy shall be provided to all unit members
via e-mail. For those unit members who have provided written notice to the District
prior to the start of a summer, the District shall, during that summer, also provided
notice to those unit members by U.S. Mail.

17.3.3    Each vacancy listed shall include the following information:

17.3.3.2    Grade level or subject matter assignment;

17.3.3.3    Credential requirements.

17.3.4    There will be a closing date of at least five (5) working days after the posting of any
vacancy except during the summer when it will be ten (10) working days. The
Administration may post in-house and outside the District concurrently. No outside
applicant shall be selected to fill a vacancy if there is a qualified unit member applicant
(See Section 17.4.7).

17.3.5    No assignment to fill a vacancy shall be made until the closing date.

17.3.6    Unit members who have transfer requests on file will be notified of any vacancies
which occur in the area of their transfer request.

17.3.7    If a unit member already has a transfer request on file, it is not necessary to
make a further request for the position.

17.4    Emnlovee-Initiated Transfer and Reassignment

17.4.1    A unit member may request a reassignment or a transfer to any vacancy for which
he/she qualifies.

17.4.2    A request for reassignment or transfer may be made at any time, whether or not a
vacancy exists.

be notified by the District of the application; however, if the transfer is a possibility, the
sending principal will be notified.

17.4.5    In making unit member-initiated transfers and reassignments, the administration shall

teachers. If all factors other than District seniority are satisfied, the most senior
qualified unit member shall be transferred or reassigned.

unit member not receiving the vacancy. The District's decision is final unless a
grievance regarding the process is filed and sustained.

17.4.7    No outside applicant shall be selected to fill a vacancy if there is a qualified unit
member applicant.

17.5 District-Initiated Transfers and Reassignments

17.5.1    District-initiated transfers and reassignments shall be made only for the following
reasons:

17.5.1.1    A decrease in enrollment which requires a decrease in the number of unit
members.

17.5.1.2    Changes    in attendance boundaries or patterns.

17.5.1.3    Resignations or retirements.

17.5.1.4    Leaves.

17.5.1.6    The unit member's experience, credential(s), major and minor fields of
study. District seniority, and strengths and weaknesses of available
teachers shall be considered. If all factors other than District seniority
are satisfied, the least senior qualified unit member shall be transferred or
reassigned.

17.5.2    If the need for transfer or reassignment is known before the end of a school year, a
unit member who is to be transferred or reassigned must be notified in writing no later
than twenty (20) working days before the end of the school year.

17.5.3    A District-initiated transfer or reassignment shall take place only after a
conference between the unit member and his/her supervisor has been held to discuss the

proposed transfer or reassignment. A unit member may have up to three (3) duty days
to plan for the meeting and has the right to request that an Association
representative be present during this discussion.

17.5.4    A unit member who is transferred or reassigned under this section during the school

preparation and moving prior to the effective date of the transfer or reassignment.

17.5.5    The District shall provide assistance in moving a unit member's material whenever a
unit member is transferred/reassigned.

17.5.6    If a District-initiated transfer or reassignment is to be made during the summer recess,

member may have up to three (3) calendar days to plan for this meeting and has the
right to request that an Association representative be present during this discussion.

17.5.7    A unit member being transferred or reassigned under this section shall receive written

17.6.2    If the need for the assignment change(s) occur during the summer break, the District

be reassigned.

problems.

ARTICLE 20
LEAVES OF ABSENCE

20.1 Sick Leave

All ten (10) month certificated employees shall be entitled to eleven (11) days sick leave
per school year. Sick leave is accumulated year by year and shall be transferred from district to
district. The District may require a physician's verification of illness and fitness for duty if an
employee has been absent for five (5) or more consecutive days. The District shall notify, in
writing, all certificated employees on or before September 30 of each year of the total number of
days of accumulated sick leave. All accumulated sick leave shall be applied to the teacher's
retirement as recognized by state statute. Sick leave shall be used only for illness, injury,
medical/dental appointments, or quarantine of the employee as provided for in the Education
Code.

20.2 Personal Necessity Leave

20.2.1    An employee may use up to seven (7) days of leave provided in Sick Leave of this

Article in any school year in cases of personal necessity, including any of the

following:

20.2.1.1    The death of a member of the employee's immediate family when the
number of days of absence exceeds the limit provided in Bereavement
Leave policy. "Members of the immediate family" means the mother, father,
son, daughter, brother or sister of the employee or spouse of the
employee, spouse, grandparent, grandchild, foster children, any person
who has served as a foster parent or legal guardian to the employee.
Permission to use this policy for other relatives because of extenuating
circumstances may be granted by the employee's site principal.

20.2.1.2    An accident or imminent danger involving the employee's property or the
person or property of a member of the employee's immediate family.
Such accident must (1) be serious in nature; (2) involve circumstances the
employee cannot reasonably be expected to disregard; and (3) require the
attention of the employee during his/her assigned hours of service.

20.2.1.3    An illness of a member of the employee's immediate family as defined in

19.2.1.1 above, serious in nature which, under the circumstances the
employee cannot reasonably be expected to disregard, and which requires
the attention of the employee during his/her assigned hours of service.

20.2.1.4    Paternity leave upon the birth or adoption of a child.

20.2.1.5    Other personal necessities to be allowed at the discretion of the
employee's site principal.

20.2.2    Necessity Leave and Personal Necessity Leave Pav

20.2.2.1 The total number of days allowed in one school year for such leave or leaves
shall not exceed seven (7) days.

20.2.2.2

The days allowed shall be deducted from, and may not exceed, the
number of sick leave days to which the employee is entitled.

20.2.2.3 Payment for such absences shall be made only upon clarification by the
administrator that the absence was due to a situation designated as a
personal necessity within the meaning of this section. The employee
shall be required to sign a statement that such absence was due to a
personal necessity. Such statement shall be approved for payment by the
administrator and shall be filed with the District Office. If a difference
exists as to the above definition of Personal Necessity Leave, the
employee may file a grievance as provided herein.

20.3    Health Leave

Any certificated employee of the District may be granted a leave of absence for reasons of
health at the expiration of all accumulated sick leave. Such leave is to be specified for a
period of not less than the remainder of the fiscal year. A statement of need by a qualified
physician shall be required by the Board. Probationary or tenure status of an employee
requesting health leave will not be affected if the provisions for application are fulfilled. An
employee on health leave will receive the difference between his/her per diem pay and that of
his/her replacement for the duration of the leave. Before return from leave, a statement must be
furnished from the physician indicating that the returning employee can return to full-time
employment.

20.4    Personal Business Leave

All certificated employees may have three (3) days of leave each year at no loss of pay and
with no deduction from sick leave. This leave is not cumulative and shall be granted except in
unusual circumstances. The employee shall give the administration at least twenty-four (24)
hours notice prior to taking Personal Business Leave. This leave shall not be a part of
Personal Necessity Leave.

20.5    Industrial Accident and Illness Leave

All employees are eligible for Industrial Accident and Illness Leave because of occupational
injury or illness. Refer to State Compensation Law.

20.6    Bereavement Leave

A leave of absence with pay and without deductions from accumulated sick leave, not to
exceed three (3) days, shall be granted to a certificated employee on account of the death of
any member of his/her immediate family or any relative living in the immediate household of the
certificated employee. Any additional days requested beyond those provided by the
Bereavement Leave policy must be handled through the provisions under Section 19.2,
"Personal Necessity Leave."

20.7    Maternity Leave

The Board shall authorize a certificated employee to take a leave of absence for maternity
reasons for up to one (1) year maximum for each birth or adoption. Application for leave of

37

absence due to pregnancy should be made by letter to the Board. The beginning date of leave
shall be determined by the employee and the employee's physician. The maternity leave shall
terminate when the employee's physician certifies that the employee is medically able to return to
work or at a date that is mutually agreeable to the employee and the District. In case of
miscarriage, an individual on maternity leave may be considered for immediate placement
provided a position for which the individual is qualified exists and statement from the
attending physician indicated that the individual is able to return to the profession
without detriment to her health. Probationary or tenure status of an employee requesting
maternity leave will not be affected if the provisions for application and extension of leave are
fulfilled.

20.8    Jury Duty

Personnel serving on jury duty will be paid up to the amount or difference between their
regular earnings and any amount received as juror's fees.

20.9    Court Witness

Employees may be absent from duties in order to serve as a court witness without loss of pay.

All fees, other than mileage, shall be reimbursed to the District.

20.10    Military Service

Leaves of absence for military service shall be granted as provided in California state law
governing military service training.

20.11    Leaves of Absence for Other Purposes

20.11.1    Leaves of absence, with or without pay, may be granted by the Board upon the
recommendation of the Superintendent to permanent employees of the District for study,
travel, special service, or other purposes when approved in advance.

20.11.2    Study for which leaves may be granted shall be limited to study in an accredited
institution of higher learning or under a fellowship grant from a recognized
foundation or research organization engaged in education, research, or research related
to the teacher's major or minor field of study or current teaching assignment. Plans for
such study shall be submitted to the Superintendent in writing in advance and shall be
so designed as to improve the individual's skill and competence in his/her area of
service.

20.11.3    Leaves of travel may be granted for the following reasons:

20.11.3.1    Travel conducted by an accredited institution of higher learning for
which course credit is granted by the institution.

20.11.3.2    Travel plans which have been submitted in advance to the board and
approved. Such plans shall constitute a predetermined program designed
to improve the individual's competence.

20.11.4    Special service leaves may be granted for full-time service when such service is

38

directly related to the teacher's area of work. Such leaves shall be limited to special
service performed for or with an institution of higher learning, a foundation or
research organization, a state education department, the United States Office of
Education, the Peace Corps, or similar institutions or organizations, plans for which
shall have been submitted in writing and approved in advance. Such plans shall
constitute a predetermined program designed to improve the individual's
competence.

20.11.5    Time during which the individual is on leave for study, travel, special service or
other purposes as described in this Article, may be credited toward increments. Sick
leave allowance and accrual shall be determined by the Board in advance of leave.
Seniority shall be retained. The individual on leave may elect to maintain all benefits.
Specific arrangements are to be negotiated with the Superintendent. Upon return from
leaves of absence, as indicated above, teachers shall submit to the Superintendent
evidence of fulfillment of the purpose for which the leave was granted.

20.11.6    Application for leaves of absence specified above are to be submitted to the
Superintendent on prescribed forms. Such applications shall certify (1) purpose of
leave; (2) plans for the use of the leave; (3) date upon which the individual shall
return to service; and (4) agreement that failure to return to active service upon
expiration of the leave granted shall be deemed by the Superintendent and the
individual to constitute a resignation, unless such failure is the result of illness or
some other factor that precludes the good intentions of the teacher to return to
service on the date of expiration.

20.11.7    At the expiration of a leave of absence, the certificated employee who has been
granted such leave shall be reinstated.

20.11.8    If the leave is granted with pay, the teacher shall subsequently serve for at least one
(1) year with the District unless otherwise mutually agreeable.
