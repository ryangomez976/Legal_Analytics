AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, hereinafter referred to as the "Agreement", is entered into this
13th day of February, 2013, by and between the MOUNTAIN VALLEY UNIFIED SCHOOL
DISTRICT and the MOUNTAIN VALLEY TEACHERS ASSOCIATION UNIT OF TRINITY
COUNTY TEACHERS ASSOCIATION, affiliated with the California Teachers Association and
the National Education Association, hereinafter referred to as "Association".

The term, "Agreement", as used herein means the written binding agreement provided
under statue. 1

1

The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all full-time
and part-time certificated employees of the District - excluding management,
confidential and supervisory employees, as defined in Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540 -
3549 of the California Government Code - for the purpose of meeting and negotiating.
The District Superintendent, Building Principals, and Counselors who serve in excess
of 50% of their time in such capacity shall be considered management and not
members of the bargaining unit.

No other group or organization or representative thereof shall be recognized or
permitted to engage on behalf of any employee included in the bargaining unit in any
activities concerning wages, hours, or terms and conditions of employment.

The parties of the Agreement recognize that the duties and work performed by the
certificated employees in the bargaining unit described above shall be performed by
unit members. The District, with the approval of the Association, may hire specialists
to support the instructional program in accordance with Ed Code.

ARTICLE 5 - CLASS SIZE

The parties recognize that class size is (1) a significant factor in pupil achievement; (2)
an important term of condition of employment; and (3) a major cost variable, subject to
staff availability, physical facility limitations, student transportation problems, special
pupil needs, the grade level and/or subject and other factors. For these reasons, the
parties affirm this intention to consider class size as a matter of mutual importance and
consideration.

With the exception of Physical Education, Work Experience, Drama and Music (Band
and Chorus), the total number of pupils per teacher shall not exceed at the high school
156 enrolled students per teacher.

The ratio of pupils to classroom teacher at the 4th - 8th levels will not exceed thirty (30)
pupils per teacher, with the exception of split grade classes, in which the ratio will not
exceed 28 to 1. The ratio of pupils to classroom teacher at the K-3 level will not exceed
25 pupils per teacher, with the exception of split grade classes, in which the ratio will
not exceed 23 to 1. If the pupil per teacher ratio exceeds 30 to 1, or 28 to 1 in split-
level classes for grades 4th - 8th, or 25 to 1 or 23 in split-level classes for grades K-3,
the Board and administration will make every reasonable effort to rectify the situation
within 20 working days. The teachers over the maximum will have the assistance of a
temporary aide.

The criteria for establishing the maximum number of student work stations for a
specific class are: space, enrollment, safety, type of class and the equipment and
furniture physically present, and each constitutes a controlling factor in the
establishment of student work stations.

5.4.1    Space    is    defined    as an area in which the student has sufficient room and

adequate ventilation and heat to perform the required learning tasks.

5.4.2    Provisions for safety shall be determined by the activities involved in a
particular class and the nature of the potential hazards present.

5.4.3    The amount and type of equipment and furniture physically present shall
be determined by the equipment needed to provide proper instruction.

5.4.4    The    District    will    attempt to maintain the actual number of students in all

classes below the maximum level.

ARTICLE 6 - TRANSFER/ASSIGNMENT/RE-ASSIGNMENT

6.1    A transfer refers to any action that results in the movement of a teacher from one
school to another. A re-assignment refers to any change in grade level or assignment to
another subject matter area. A transfer may be teacher-initiated (voluntary) or District-
initiated (involuntary). A vacancy is a newly created or vacated position within the
unit after transfers/re-assignments have been made.

6.2    The District Superintendent has the authority to transfer teachers.

6.3    A transfer of an employee shall not result in a reduction of the teacher's salary,
seniority or any fringe benefit.

SECTION 1 - VOLUNTARY TRANSFER

6.4    To another position: Prior to the last teaching day in March of each school year,
teachers may apply for transfer to another position. Teachers desiring transfer shall
request placement, in writing, through the Building Principals, stating their reasons for
transfer. The request shall then be sent to the Superintendent.

6.5    To a vacant position: The Superintendent shall deliver to the Association and each
teacher by name, post in all school buildings a list of the vacancies for any promotional
positions or new positions, which occur during the school year and for the following
school year upon knowledge of the vacancies. The list shall contain a closing date for
submitted requests for transfer which is at least ten (10) working days following the
posting date. No assignment to fill vacancies shall be made until after the closing date.
The District shall notify all teachers' during summer recess of any posted openings,
which may arise during the summer. Each teacher will provide their summer address if
it is different than their regular address. If a teacher already has a transfer application
on file, it is not necessary to make a further application in order to be considered for
any vacancies for which the teacher may have applied

6.6    No outside applicant shall be selected to fill a vacancy if there is a unit member
applicant that meets the qualifications of the job description.

6.7    For the purposes of selecting which unit member shall be voluntarily transferred and
/or re-assigned in order to meet the needs of the District, the District shall consider the
training, experience, major and minor fields of study, credential, length of service in
the school and in the District, past evaluations, advanced degrees and other criteria as
listed in the job posting.

6.8    If two or more teachers have equal qualification, the decision shall be based upon
District-wide seniority. A teacher's seniority is established by the initial date of service
to the District. Those teachers whose first day of service to the District is the same
shall have their seniority established by lot. The District shall conduct the lottery in a
fair, equitable and reasonable manner with Association representatives present. Once a
teacher's seniority has been established by lot, such seniority date shall remain with that
teacher as long as that teacher is employed by the District.

6.9    Whenever an employee's request for transfer is denied, the District Superintendent
shall, upon request, and within three (3) days, give such employee a written statement
of the reason(s) for such denial. The teacher shall then be granted, upon request, a
meeting with the Administrator denying the request to discuss the reasons for the
denial. The teacher may be accompanied by a representative of his or her choice.

SECTION II - INVOLUNTARY TRANSFERS/REASSIGNMENTS

6.10    The Superintendent maintains the right to assign certificated employees to positions
based upon qualifications, enrollment, specialized services, opportunities for
performance improvement and the educationally related needs of the District. If the
District determines that all the items referred to in the prior sentence are equal, then the
least senior teacher shall be reassigned. Employees being assigned to a different site
shall be given reasons in writing for such assignments. Employees may request
assignment to one of the posted vacancies.

6.11    An involuntary transfer shall not be made arbitrarily or capriciously. It may be made
for the following reasons: A decrease in the number of pupils which requires a
decrease in the number of unit members, class size considerations, elimination of
program(s), decreased funding, change in school configuration or school closing. Such
transfers will be subject to annual review, upon the request of the teacher.

6.12    Any member of the unit who is to be involuntarily transferred/reassigned shall be
informed by the Superintendent or designee, in writing, of the reason or reasons for the
transfer. If the involuntarily transferred/reassigned unit member so requests, a
conference shall be held between the unit member, an Association member chosen by
the teacher, and Superintendent or designee prior to the effective date of the
transfer/reassignment. The unit member may request the site principal’s attendance at
the conference. This committee will render a written recommendation to the
Superintendent.

6.13    If a change of enrollment or funding requires a decrease in staff or departmental
personnel at a particular school or within a particular funded program, the District shall
seek volunteers prior to making any involuntary transfers. If an involuntary transfer
becomes necessary, the qualified teacher with the least seniority shall be transferred.

ARTICLE 7 - SAFETY

Employees will not be required to perform tasks that endanger their health or safety.

7.1.1    Qualified    and    trained    unit members may consent to provide and conduct

necessary specialized health care procedures.

The Teachers shall report unsafe conditions to the Principal. The Principal or his/her
designee shall promptly investigate the unsafe condition, and if he/she concurs the
condition is unsafe, he/she shall take appropriate action to correct the condition.

Any case of verbal or physical assault upon a teacher in the performance of the
teacher's duties shall be promptly reported to the Superintendent or his/her
representative. The Superintendent shall obtain advice from Counsel and thereafter
advise the teacher of his/her rights and obligations with respect to such assault. The
Superintendent shall render necessary assistance to the law enforcement and judicial
authorities per Education Code 48900.7.

No Principal, Administrator, parent, guardian or other person shall upbraid, insult or
abuse any teacher of the Public Schools in the presence or hearing of a pupil or other
school personnel; anyone who so violates a teacher is guilty of a misdemeanor
(Education Code 44811 and 44812).

A bargaining unit member may suspend a pupil from her/his class for the day of the
suspension and the following day for any act that disrupts, diminishes, or threatens the
safety of the education process according to Ed Code as long as alternative means of
discipline have been attempted. 11

ARTICLE 10 - PERSONAL AND ACADEMIC FREEDOM

10.1    It is the policy of the District that all instruction shall be fair, accurate, objective,
appropriate to the age and maturity of the students, and sensitive to the community
needs and the needs and values of our diverse cultures and heritages. Academic
freedom is essential to the fulfillment of this policy and the district acknowledges the
fundamental need to protect teachers from any censorship or restraint which might
interfere with the teacher’s obligation to pursue truth in performance of their teaching
functions. Accordingly:

10.1.1    A teacher shall have reasonable freedom in the classroom presentations
and discussions and may introduce political, religious or otherwise
controversial material, provided that said material is relevant to the course
content and within the scope of the law.

10.1.2    In performing teaching functions, teachers shall have reasonable freedom
to express their opinions on all matters relevant to the course content in an
objective manner. A teacher, however, shall not utilize his/her position to
indoctrinate students with his/her own personal, political or religious
views.

10.2    The personal life of a teacher is not an appropriate concern of the district for purpose of
evaluation or disciplinary action unless it prevents the teacher from performing his/her
assigned duties.

10.3    A teacher shall be entitled to full right of citizenship. No religious or political
activities, or lack thereof, shall be used for purpose of evaluation or disciplinary action
unless said activities violate local, state or federal law.

ARTICLE 12 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

SECTION 1 - DEFINITIONS

12.1    A grievance is a claim by one or more teachers or the Association that there has been a
violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of one or more provisions of this
Agreement.

12.2    A grievant is one or more certificated employees of the District, including the
Association or representatives there of.

12.3    A day is any day on which the certificated staff is required to perform services by this
contract.

12.4    Grievants, witnesses, and Association representatives shall be provided release time for
grievance processing at no loss of salary or other benefits.

SECTION 2 - PURPOSE

12.4    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to the problems which may arise from time to time. Both parties
agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal and confidential as may be
appropriate at any level of the procedure.

12.5    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any teacher having a
grievance to discuss the matter informally with any appropriate member of the
administration, and to have the grievance adjusted without intervention by the
Association, provided that the adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this
Agreement.

12.6    The time limits specified at each level should be considered to be maximums. These
time limits may be extended or limited by mutual agreement due to holidays and/or end
of school year.

SECTION 3 - PROCEDURE

LEVEL ONE - INFORMAL

12.7    A grievant and/or the Association shall first discuss the grievance with the Principal or
immediate Supervisor.

29

LEVEL TWO- FORMAL

12.8    Within thirty (30) days after the grievant is aware of or should have been aware of the
occurrence of the act giving rise to the grievance, the grievant must present his/her
grievance in writing to his/her Principal or immediate supervisor.

12.9    This written notice shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
circumstances involved, the specific article, section or clause of the Agreement
allegedly violated, the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the specific
remedy sought.

12.10    The Principal shall communicate his/her decision to the grievant in writing within five
(5) days after receiving the grievance. If the Principal does not respond within the time
limits, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

12.11    Within the above limits, either party may request a personal conference.

LEVEL THREE

12.12    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level Two, he/she may within ten

(10) days appeal the decision in writing to the Superintendent.

12.13    The written appeal request shall include a copy of the original grievance and appeal,
the decisions rendered and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

12.14    The Superintendent shall communicate his/her decision to the grievant within ten (10)
days. If the grievant is not satisfied with the Superintendent's decision at this level or if
the Superintendent does not respond within the time limits provided, the grievant may
appeal to the School Board within ten (10) days of the receipt of the Superintendent's
decision or the expiration of the time limits, as outlined in 12.13.

LEVEL FOUR

12.15    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level Two/Three, he/she may within
ten (10) days submit a request in writing to the Association for arbitration of the
dispute. A copy of the request will be simultaneously submitted to the Superintendent.

12.16    Within ten (10) working days or as soon as possible after the District receives written
notice of the Association’s decision to arbitrate, the parties shall agree upon an
arbitrator.

12.17    If agreement on an arbitrator is not reached within said (10) days, they shall request the
State Conciliation Service to supply a panel of five (5) names of persons experienced
in hearing grievances in public schools. Each party shall alternately strike a name until
only one name remains. The order of the striking shall be determined by lot.

fact, reasoning, and conclusions on issues submitted.

12.18.3    The Arbitrator may hear and determine only one grievance at a time unless

12.19.3    Affirmative action will be taken within fifteen (15) school days after

ARTICLE 17 - IMPACT OF LAYOFFS

SECTION 1 - NOTIFICATION PROCEDURES FOR LAYOFFS

17.1    If the District is considering the layoff of any unit member due to the lack of funds,
declining enrollment or the reduction and/or elimination of any program or service,
including management positions, which would potentially lead to the layoff of any unit
member, it will so notify both the Association and all unit members who could be
affected by such action no later than March 15. The layoff notice shall be in writing
and will include an identification of specific position(s) within the District, an
identification of all unit members who could be affected, and the specific reason for
such proposed action.

17.2    At the request of the Association, prior to the issuance of any notices of intent to layoff
unit members due to reduction of, or elimination of any programs and/or services,
including management positions which could potentially lead to the layoff of any unit
member, the District shall enter into discussion with the Association within ten (10)
working days or at a mutually agreed upon time in accordance with the procedures
contained in Section El of this Article.

SECTION 2 - CRITERIA FOR DETERMINING LAYOFFS

17.3    If it is determined by the District that the basis exists, subject to provisions of the
Education Code, to layoff certificated employees due to program reduction, program
elimination or declining enrollment, employees will be laid off in accordance with the
following provisions:

17.3.1    The teachers so affected shall have seniority rights to replace less senior
teachers, who, in turn, shall be laid off in the inverse order of seniority,
except as provided in 17.3.3 below.

17.3.2    Part-time employees shall be laid off first (providing that the distinction
between part-time and full-time employees does not violate a provision of
the Education Code) except those employees who involuntarily have part-
time status due to previous layoff proceedings and who have not been
given an opportunity to become full-time; such teachers shall be treated as
having full-time status for purposes of this Article, except as provided in

17.3.3 below.

17.3.3    The District shall not layoff an employee if it retains a junior employee in
a position which the senior employee is certificated and competent (based
on familiarity and/or training in the subject matter to be taught) to render.

45

17.3.4    Criteria for determining seniority for unit members with the same initial
date of service shall be:

17.3.4.1    Relevant teaching credentials.

17.3.4.2    Experience and/or training in teaching the particular subject
area to be filled.

17.3.4.3    Should the application of criteria 1 - 2 not result in
determining seniority for teachers with the same first day of
service, seniority shall then be determined by lottery.

Should the lottery be needed, it shall be conducted by the
Superintendent in presence of at least two (2) Association
representatives and the employees so affected (at their
discretion).

17.3.5    The parties recognize the District's right to determine its curricular needs
for the following year.

17.3.6    The District will provide, upon request by the Association, written
rationale for any part-time and/or split assignments that may result from
reduction of particular kinds of services.

17.4    Layoffs due to lack of funds.

17.4.1    The District may layoff certificated employees for the lack of funds as
determined by the Board of Trustees, subject to provisions of the
Education Code. The District shall provide the Association with pertinent
facts relative to the lack of funds.

17.4.2    Prior to taking any action regarding layoff for the lack of funds, the
District shall give the Association thirty (30) days advance notice and if
the Association desires to negotiate regarding the impact of such layoffs,
the District and the Association shall meet and negotiate on such issues.

17.4.3    Part-time employees will be laid off first followed by full-time employees
according to the inverse order of their seniority without regard to program
or services (providing that the distinction between part-time and full-time
employees does not violate a provision of the Education Code) except as
noted in 17.3.2 above.

17.5    Additional Provisions

17.5.1 The District shall furnish the Association with a current seniority list by no
later than October 1 of each year. This seniority list shall include the
names of all certificated employees of the district, all types of credentials

interruption of services including dates and nature of such breaks in
service.

ARTICLE 22 - PAR

PURPOSE

The teacher peer assistance and review program is intended as a critical feedback mechanism that
allows exemplary teachers to assist fellow teachers in the development of subject matter
knowledge, teaching strategies, or both.

Rules and Procedures for the Joint Committee

1. JOINT COMMITTEE

The Joint Committee serves as the governing body for the program and determines program
guidelines that are consistent with terms of the Collective Bargaining Agreement. The Joint
Committee consists of 5 members selected by the Association, who shall compose the majority
of the Committee, and 3 members, who shall be school administrators, selected by the District.

The make-up of the Committee will consist of the three district principals, two teachers from
Hayfork High, two from Hayfork Elementary and one from either Valley High School,
Community Day School, Hyampom Art Magnet School or Home School.

2. MEETINGS

The Joint Committee will establish a meeting schedule as needed. The Committee will select a
chairperson. Minutes will be kept of each meeting.

Meetings will take place after regular school hours and teachers will be paid at their negotiated
hourly rate.

3. RESPONSIBILITIES

Responsibilities shall include:

1.    Provide annual training for Joint Committee members and “coaches”.

2.    Establish committee rules of procedures.

3.    Select consulting teachers.

4.    Select trainers if needed.

5.    Review the work of consulting teachers.

The Joint Committee should consider the agreed upon intent on the Peer Assistance and Review
Program and the funding received to support the program. All aspects of the program should be
considered, including:

• Number of unsatisfactory evaluation referrals.

52

•    Number of probationary teachers to receive assistance.

•    Number of permanent volunteer participants.

•    Training needs of the consulting teachers.

•    Training needs of the Joint Committee.

•    Release time needed by consulting teachers. Joint Committee and participating teacher(s).

•    Compensation for Peer Assistance and Review participants as agreed upon in the Collective
Bargaining Agreement.

•    Administrative Costs, if any.

4. CONSULTING TEACHER SELECTION PROCESS

Application forms for the position of consulting teacher shall be available from the District, the
Association and at the sites. At any time the District or the Association recognizes a need, they shall
jointly notify all employees in the District that the Peer Assistance and Review Program Joint
Committee is seeking applications for consulting teachers.

Employees may submit a completed application form within the required time lines established by the
Joint Committee for the application process.

Based upon a review of the applications, the Joint Committee shall create a list of qualified, potential
consulting teachers.

5. SELECTION OF TRAINERS AND TRAINING PROVIDERS

The Joint Committee is responsible for providing any necessary annual training for Joint
Committee members and for providing training for consulting teachers prior to their participation
in the Peer Assistance and Review Program.

6. BUDGET DEVELOPMENT AND ADMINISTRATION

The Joint Committee is responsible for developing the annual budget for the Peer Assistance and
Review Program and administering the budgetary dollars available to the program. Money can not
exceed amount received.

Rules and Procedures for Consulting Teachers

1. QUALIFICATION

Must be a permanent certificated employee with a positive current evaluation.

2.    DUTIES

Consulting teachers shall assist participating teachers by demonstrating, observing, coaching,

conferencing, referring, or by other activities, which, in their professional judgment, will assist

the participating teacher.

•    Meet regularly (specifically defined in conjunction with time allowed) for
observations/discussions with participating teacher.

•    Maintain a written log of contacts and specific support given to participating teacher.

•    Document all observations, visitations and meetings held.

•    The consulting teacher shall meet with the referred participating teacher to discuss the Peer
Assistance and Review Program, to establish mutually agreed upon performance goals,
develop the assistance plan and develop a process for determining successful completion of
the Peer Assistance and Review Program.

•    The consulting teacher shall conduct multiple observations of the participating teacher during
classroom instruction and provide specific, immediate feedback after each visit.

•    The consulting teacher shall monitor the progress of the referred participating teacher and
shall submit to and discuss with the referred participating teacher periodically written reports.

•    The consulting teacher shall continue to provide assistance until he or she concludes that the
teaching performance of the participating teacher is satisfactory, or more time is needed, or
that an alternate plan is appropriate.

•    A copy of the consulting teacher’s report shall be submitted to and discussed with the
referred participating teacher to receive his or her signature before it is submitted to the Joint
Committee. Joint Committee will design the reporting form.

•    The consulting teacher shall submit a final report to the Joint Committee.

3.    TERM

•    A teacher may serve in this role as long as he/she decides and/or at the discretion of the Joint
Committee.

•    A teacher may not be appointed to an administrative position in the District while serving as
a consulting teacher for two (2) full years after serving as a consulting teacher.

4.    COMPENSATION

•    In addition to the regular salary, a consulting teacher shall receive a stipend.

54

•    If the consulting teacher is on full release, per diem salary will be paid for each day worked
over the contract year.

•    Consulting teachers shall be reimbursed for all travel expenses approved by the Joint
Committee.

•    Consulting teachers shall have a budget determined by the Joint Committee to purchase
supplies and materials based on a plan submitted by and approved by the Joint Committee
and forward to the business office.

•    Expenditures will not exceed the monies received for the program.

5. FINAL REPORT TO JOINT COMMITTEE

The consulting teacher shall submit a final report to the Joint Committee no later than 10 days

following the last day of their “action plan”.

6. REPORTS TO PARTICIPATING TEACHERS

Consulting teachers shall submit to and discuss with the referred participating teachers periodic
written reports.

7. DUE PROCESS

All communication between the consulting teacher and participating teacher shall be confidential
and without written consent of the participating teacher shall not be shared with others.
