ARTICLE I

PREAMBLE AND RECOGNITION

The following Collective Bargaining Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between
the CAYUCOS SCHOOL DISTRICT (“District” or Employer”) and the CAYUCOS
TEACHERS ASSOCIATION/CTA/NEA (“Association” or Exclusive Representative”). This
Agreement is entered into pursuant to Government code section 3540 et seq. (“Act”).

Section 1 — Bargaining Unit

The district recognizes the association as the sole and exclusive representative for all certificated
personnel (“employee,” “employees,” “teacher,” or “teachers”). Excluded from the bargaining
unit are all management, confidential, and supervisory employees as designated by the district
board of trustees (“Board”), substitutes, and summer school employees.

Section 2 — Negotiations Relationship

The association agrees to negotiate exclusively with the representative or representatives
designated by the district. The district agrees to negotiate exclusively with the representative or
representatives designated by the association.

ARTICLE III
MANAGEMENT RIGHTS
Section 1 — Management Powers. Rights, and Authority

It is understood and agreed that, except as limited by the terms of this agreement, the district
retains all of its powers and authority to direct, manage and control to the extent allowed by the
law. Included in, but not limited to those duties and powers are the right to: determine its
organization; direct the work of its employees; determine the time and hours of operation;
determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided, and the methods and means of
providing them; establish its educational policies, goals and objectives; secure the rights and
educational opportunities of students; determine staffing patterns; determine the number and
kinds of persomiel required; maintain the efficiency of district operations; determine district
curriculum; design, build, move or modify facilities; establish budget procedures and determine
budgetary allocations; determine the methods of raising revenue; contract our work when present
employees are not available to perform the work; and take any action on any matter in the event
of any emergency as provided in section 3 of this article. In addition, the district retains the right
to hire, classify, assign, evaluate, promote, demote, terminate and discipline employees. This
recital in no way limits other district powers as granted by the law.

Section 2 — Limitation

The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the district,
the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof, and the use of
judgment and discretion in connection therewith, shall be limited only by the specific and
express terms of this agreement, and then only to the extent the specific and express terms are in
conformance with law. Application of the district’s power, rights, and authority as set forth in
section 1 of this article shall not be arbitrary, capricious or discriminatory.

Section 3 — Emergencies

In case of emergency, the district retains its right to suspend those portions of the agreement
affected by the emergency condition for the period of time required by the emergency. Examples
of emergencies are national, state, or county declared emergencies, natural disasters and fiscal
crises. For the purposes of this Section, a “fiscal crisis” shall be defined as an event or events that
results in or arises out of a “negative certification” of the District’s budget by the San Luis
Obispo County Office of Education (“SLOCOE”) and will trigger immediate reopeners on those
provisions subject to suspension.

ARTICLE IV
WAGES

Section 1 — Salary Schedules

Unit members serving in an administrative capacity shall have their base daily rate adjusted by
5% for each day of administrative service when the member is removed from the classroom to
serve as an administrator and a substitute is retained to cover the unit member’s assignment.
Adjustments shall be in Vi day increments.

The 2013-2014 Certificated Salary Schedule shall be increased 3% effective 1/8/14, no
retroactive payment agreement is included. Extra-Curricular Stipends are set forth in
Appendix B.

The 2014-2015 Certificated Salary Schedule shall be increased 2% effective the first day of the
2014-2015 contract year.

For the 2015-2016 school year, the Certificated Salary Schedule for regular faculty shall be open
for negotiations based upon the budget, deficit reduction and COLA.

After the 2015-2016 school year unit members placed at the final step and column of the salary
schedule for more than two years shall receive a 1% annual increase or the negotiated increase,
whichever is higher.

Section 2 — Mileage Reimbursement

Employees specifically authorized in advance by the district to use their personal cars in
fulfilling a specific work assignment shall be reimbursed at the district approved rate. It is
understood and agreed that employee travel between home and work sites is exempt from this
provision. It is further understood and agreed that this reimbursement shall be payment in full for
all costs relating from the use.

Section 3 — Payroll Deductions

Subject to approval by the SLOCOE, the district shall provide each employee with payroll
deductions for:

A.    Association Membership Dues

B.    Credit Union

C.    Tax Sheltered Annuities

D.    Insurance plans offered by the district

E.    Other programs approved by the    SLOCOE

6

Bargaining unit members who desire the district to remit payment for any of the above purposes
shall complete a form authorizing or continuing authorization of payroll deduction for the
amount in question. At the beginning of each school year, the district will provide each employee
with a copy of the employee’s previous year’s form.

Section 4 — Payroll Schedule

Subject to approval by SLOCOE and except by mutual agreement, regular employees shall be
paid in 10 equal payments no later than the last working day of the month.

Employees shall have the option to be paid in 12 equal payments. The employee’s cost of the
fringe benefit program shall be deducted on a 10-month basis. An employee shall be paid in 10
equal payments unless an option to be paid in 12 equal payments is filed no later than September
10 of the school year for which the option is to become effective.

Should an employee opt for a 12-month pay cycle, that option shall remain in effect for a
minimum of one full year and continue until the employee requests the payroll office in writing
by September 10 of the school year to return to a 10-month pay cycle.

Section 5 -- Hourly Rate

Unit members who are assigned to instruct outside of the regular contract by administration shall
be paid their regular hourly rate. The amount of time shall be agreed upon in advance and in
writing. Unit members who voluntarily accept extra duty assignments such as after school
enrichment shall be paid at the agreed upon curriculum rate or their regular hourly rate
whichever is less.

shall receive one additional month’s coverage for each eight days of unused sick leave beyond
the 100-day qualifying amount.

ARTICLE VI
HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

Section 1 — Calendar

The District shall consult with the association annually on the preparation of the school calendar
for the succeeding work year. Days and hours of student attendance will be determined by the
district.

Section 2— Work Year

Effective the 2014-2015 school year except as provided elsewhere in this agreement, Cayucos
Teachers Association bargaining unit members will add two non-instructional days to the
certificated employee work schedule. The bargaining unit member work year will include 186
days. One day will be provided prior to instructional days solely for individual classroom/work
instructional preparation. One day will be provided after instructional days solely for individual
classroom work area instructional cleanup. The intent of these days is to provide specific
preparation/cleanup time dedicated to maximize other non-instructional staff development
activities.

Section 3 — Adjunct Duties (Professional)

The district may require work outside the regular assigned work day for professional activities
such as parent and student conference, faculty meetings, in-service training, special school
functions and other agreed upon duties without additional compensation.

Section 4— Workday

Employees shall be on the work site no less than 30 minutes before the first students arriving
are scheduled to attend class (8:00 a.m.) and remain on the work site 30 minutes after the latest
scheduled dismissal of students (3:30 p.m.) Variations in these hours for individual employees
may be detennined by the principal provided total time is not increased. Early dismissal days are
considered part of the regular work day and include activities such as faculty/staff meetings,
professional development, committee meetings and department meetings. Employees are
expected to remain on campus until 3:30 p.m. unless prior arrangements have been made with
the principal. Occasional variations in hours may be determined by mutual agreement between
the association and the principal for special purposes.

Section 5 — Preparation Time

For 1.0 FTE employees the district shall make every attempt to schedule preparation time during
the school day. Any change to the time structure shall be by mutual agreement between the
principal and the respective bargaining unit member(s).

10

Section 6— Professional Committees

The district in coordination with the association will establish professional committees on an
annual basis. Each employee is expected to participate in an equal number of committees based
upon the FTE status. Committees include, but are not limited to: SST, Faculty Advisory
Graduation, Middle School Activities, Safety, Curriculum, Communications, and Assessment.

Section 6A—Faculty Advisory Committee

The district in coordination with the association shall maintain substantive faculty involvement
in the recruitment, screening, interview and selection process for certificated faculty members.

Section 7— Part-Time Employees

A part-time employee’s assignment shall be based upon a ratio of the employee’s assigned time
to the assignment of time of a regular (FTE) full-time employee. The status of the employees
will be determined at the time of the work agreement with a clear FTE percentage.

A part-time employee is responsible for attendance at all staff meetings, staff development, work
groups, or any other activities or functions at which the attendance of full-time employees is
required when the activity or function occurs on a day that the employee is regularly scheduled
to work. 11

ARTICLE VII
EVALUATION PROCEDURES

Section 1 — Frequency

All certificated employees shall be evaluated in writing at least once each school year. The
district shall consult with the association annually regarding the evaluation instrument. The
evaluation shall include, but is not limited to, at least two classroom observations conducted by
the evaluator, which may include the inspection of any instructional materials, including but not
limited to, lesson plans, student work and student progress reports. Such observations and
inspections may be made at the discretion of the evaluator with or without notice to the
employee. Each observation shall be followed by a post-observation conference with the
employee.

Section 2 — Objectives and Plan

No later than the end October of each school year, an evaluator shall meet with each certificated
employee to mutually agree upon each employee’s respective goals and objectives for that
school year and to discuss the elements and criteria upon which the employee’s evaluation shall
be based. The specific goals and objectives for the employee shall be based upon the California
Standards for the Teaching Profession. The district’s determination of the specific elements and
criteria for teacher evaluations shall be final and shall be placed in writing to the employee at
that time.

Section 3 — Signing

The evaluator shall meet with the employee to review the final evaluation at a meeting which
shall take place at least thirty days prior to the end of the school year. The employee being
evaluated shall sign and be given a copy of any evaluation reports made on the employee’s
performance. The signing of the report shall not necessarily indicate that the employee is in
agreement with the report. The employee shall have the right to have attached to the file copy of
the evaluation reports any written comments the employee desires.

Section 4 — Personnel File

No material adverse to an employee shall be placed in an employee’s personnel file without the
employee being given an opportunity to review the document and attach a rebuttal.

Section 5 — Peer Review and Peer Assistance Program

The parties agree to adopt and implement the Peer Review and Peer Assistance Program based
upon state funding of the program.

ARTICLE VIII
LEAVES

Section 1 — Leaves of Absence — General Provisions

A.    Teachers are required to apply in writing for all leaves of absence except sick leave and
leaves for which prior approval is prohibited by law. Except in emergencies, leave of
absence may not commence prior to written approval.

B.    Except as provided by statute or by express provision of district policy, the district retains
the right to withhold approval of any leave requested.

C.    The district at any time may require adequate confirmation of stated reasons for leave,
and any false statement made to support a request for leave shall be grounds for
withholding leave and for such other disciplinary action as the district deems appropriate.
A teacher on paid leave of absence shall not be gainfully employed by any employer
except as approved by the district.

D.    Where applicable, following any leave, a doctor’s statement attesting to the teacher’s
fitness to resume employment may be required before the teacher returns to work. The
district may require confirmation by a doctor of the district’s choice and expense before a
teacher is eligible to return to work.

E.    A teacher who fails to return to work at the expiration of approved leave shall be deemed
to be absent without leave.

F.    The district will provide for employee absences as authorized by law and as negotiated
between the district and the employee organization.

Section 2— Sick Leave

Full-time employees will receive one day of sick leave for every month worked. Teachers and

other ten-month employees will receive 10 days sick leave per year.

A. An employee employed less than full-time shall be entitled to that proportion of sick
leave as the number of hours the employee is employed per month bears to those
employed full-time.

13

B.    Pay for any day of such sick leave absence shall be the same as the pay which would
have been received had the employees served during the day. Credit for sick leave need
not be accrued prior to taking the sick leave by the employee and a sick leave absence
may be taken at any time during the year.

C.    If an employee does not take the full amount of sick leave allowed in any year under this
section, the amount not taken shall be accumulated from year to year.

D.    Unit members returning to work from sick leave involving three (3) or more consecutive
days or multiple sick leaves of any duration involving the same or related illness or
condition, may be required to provide a physician’s release verifying the unit member’s
illness and the unit member’s fitness to return to service, before the unit member can
return to duty. If such an absence or absences occur at the end of a school year, such
verification may be required before the unit member may return to duty the next school
year. If an employee is required to provide a physician’s release pursuant to this Article,
the district shall reimburse the employee for the out-of-pocket costs of obtaining the
physician’s release which are not otherwise reimbursed or compensated under the
employee’s applicable medical insurance coverage. No additional sick leave shall be
charged to the employee who is absent from work for the sole purpose of obtaining a
physician’s release.

Section 3 — Use of Sick Leave for Pregnancy

An employee shall be entitled to utilize sick leave for pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and the

recovery.

A.    Leave shall not be used for child care, child rearing, or preparation for child rearing, but
shall be limited to the causes set forth above.

B.    The length of the leave, including the date on which the leave shall commence and the
date on which the employee shall resume duties shall be determined by the employee’s
medical specialist. The employee shall be entitled to return to a position comparable to
that held at the time the leave commenced.

Section 4— Transfer of Sick Leave

The district hereby adopts the provisions of Education Code section 44979 which provides for

the transfer of accumulated sick leave from an employee’s previous school district in the manner

prescribed when the employee is initially employed by the district.

14

Section 5 — Extended Illness Leave

When a unit member has been absent due to illness or injury and has exhausted all available sick
leave, and continues to be absent due to illness or injury, the employee may utilize extended
illness leave for an additional period not to exceed five (5) months. During this five-month
period, the district may subtract from the salary due to the unit member the amount actually paid
to a substitute employed to fill the position during the absence or, if no substitute is employed,
the amount that would have been paid to a substitute had one been employed. If no substitute is
hired, the deduction shall be the regular daily substitute rate. If the extended illness leave
continues past twenty days and no substitute is hired, the deduction shall be the long term
substitute rate thereafter, until such leave is exhausted. During the extended illness leave period,
the unit member shall receive not less than half of his/her normal daily pay. A unit member shall
not be provided more than one (1) five-month extended illness leave period per illness or injury.
However, if a school year terminates prior to the exhaustion of the five-month period, the unit
member may take the balance of the five-month period in the subsequent school year for the
same illness or injury. Upon return from extended illness leave, a unit member must provide a
physician's release verifying the unit member’s illness and the unit member’s fitness to return to
service, before the unit member can return to duty.

Upon the exhaustion of the extended illness leave, if the unit member is unable to return to duty,
the unit member shall be placed on unpaid leave of absence pending further disposition subject
to any rights applicable under the state and federal family medical leave acts.

Section 6 — Use of Sick Leave for Personal Necessity

The district hereby adopts the provisions of Education Code section 44981 which allows for the
use of up to seven of the ten days of sick leave granted per year in cases of personal necessity.
Days used for personal necessity shall be deducted from the employee’s accumulated sick leave.

A. Personal necessity leave is available for a matter of compelling personal importance, or
an emergency situation, that is unavoidable and of a serious nature involving
circumstances which the employee cannot be expected to disregard and which may not
be conducted at a time other than during the employee’s regular duty hours, including but
not limited to the following:

1.    Death or illness of a member of the employee’s immediate family as defined in this
Article.

2.    Accident, involving the employee’s person or property or the person or property of a
member of the employee’s immediate family as defined in this Article.

3.    Appearance in court as a litigant; or as a witness under an official order.

4.    Nationally recognized religious holidays of the employee’s religion.

5.    Other reasons at the discretion of the superintendent or designee.

15

B.    Personal necessity leave may not be taken to extend a school holiday, vacation, or recess
period or for any recreational purpose.

C.    Except for the circumstances set forth in this section, the employee shall make a request
for personal necessity leave in advance of the time that the leave is to be utilized.

D.    Unit members may use days designated as “personal necessity” leave days for purpose of
tending to “personal business” or “emergencies.” “personal business” or “emergency”
leave may be used for any of the circumstances listed in subsection A above, or for any
other legal or business purpose which is unavoidable and is of an important and serious
nature involving circumstances which the unit member cannot be expected to disregard
and which may only be conducted during school hours. In order to utilize the “personal
business” or “emergency” leave day, a unit member must submit an application and
verification of the need for the leave which shall consist of a written statement signed by
the employee attesting that the requested day of leave will or has been used for one of the
circumstances listed in subsection A above, or for another legal or business purpose
which is unavoidable and is of an important and serious nature involving circumstances
which the unit member cannot be expected to disregard and which may only be
conducted during school hours. The unit member need not disclose any further
information on the application. Wherever possible, a unit member shall request this leave
in advance of the time the leave shall be utilized. Abuse or falsification of this request as
to purpose or use shall subject the unit member to discipline, including dismissal.

Section 7— Industrial Accident Leave

The district hereby adopts the requirements set forth in Education Code section 44984 for
certificated employees.

A.    The allowable leave shall be 60 working days in any one fiscal year for
the same accident.

B.    An employee must have completed one calendar year of service with the
district to qualify for industrial accident leave.

C.    No deduction of accumulated sick leave shall be made while the employee
is on industrial accident leave. If the employee is still absent after the end
of the 60 day leave, the mandatory provisions of Education Code section
44984 shall apply.

D.    Wage loss benefit checks from Worker’s Compensation must be endorsed
over to the district by the employee as long as the employee is on a paid
leave — industrial leave, sick leave, vacation leave, etc.

16

Section 8— Bereavement Leave

Each employee is entitled to a leave of absence, not to exceed three days, or five days if travel of
more than 200 miles is required on account of the death of any member of the immediate family.
No deduction shall be made from the salary of the employee nor shall the leave be deducted from
leave granted by other sections of the Education Code or provided by the district.

A. “Member of the immediate family” as used in this Article means the mother,
father, grandmother, or grandfather of the employee or of the spouse of the
employee, and the spouse, son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law, brother,
brother-in-law, sister, sister-in-law, or grandchild of the employee, or any person
living in the immediate household of the employee.

Section 9— Health or Hardship Leave

A health or hardship leave may be granted upon the written application to the superintendent and
approval by the board up to a maximum of 30 employee workdays. The employee shall receive
the employee’s regular rate of pay less the cost of a substitute if the application is approved by
the board.

Section 10 — Sabbatical Leave

Sabbatical leave may be granted upon the recommendation of the superintendent and approval of
the board. The board may at its discretion grant pay for sabbatical leave.

Sabbatical leave for professional study or approved travel may be granted every other year to any
certificated employee of the district who has rendered seven consecutive years of service to the
district and who meets the following requirements:

A.    Applicant has filed a request with the district not later than April 1.

B.    Applicant has submitted evidence that the proposed professional study shall be
designed to enlarge applicant’s understanding of educational psychology, to
improve facility in teaching techniques, to broaden experience in special fields, or
to do research.

C.    Applicants for sabbatical leave under approved travel shall submit a brief
statement of the proposed itinerary showing specific ways in which the trip will
contribute to the improvement of the applicant’s services with the respect to the
particular educational field in which the employee is engaged. A detailed report
shall be submitted on completion of the trip attesting to the satisfactory
fulfillment of this requirement.

D.    The employee must file with the district a suitable bond indemnifying the district
for any salary paid the employee during the period of sabbatical leave in the event
the employee fails to return and to render two full years of service in this district

17

following sabbatical leave, or in the event employee fails to carry out the program
of study or the itinerary of the trip approved by the board. Failure of an employee
to return and render service or to complete the scheduled program of study or
travel shall not result in a forfeiture of the bond when the failure is due to death,
or certification by a physician that failure was due to physical or mental disability.

E.    At the expiration of the leave of absence, the employee shall, unless otherwise
agreed, be reinstated in the position held at the time of the granting of the
sabbatical leave, or in a position within the scope of the employee’s certification.

F.    An employee returning from sabbatical leave will progress on the schedule the
same as if the employee had remained in active service.

G.    The number of employees on sabbatical leave during any one year shall not
exceed one.

H.    Applications must be accompanied by a certificate of health signed by a
physician, indicating that the applicant is in satisfactory health.

Section 11— Military Leave

Military leave may be granted upon the recommendation of the superintendent and approval by
the board after one full school year of service to the district. This leave is without pay.

Section 12 — Parenthood Leave

At any time during the eight months prior to becoming a natural, adoptive, or foster parent or
within 30 days thereafter, an employee who is not eligible for or docs not wish to use sick leave
for pregnancy, may be granted an unpaid leave of absence under this section.

A.    The employee shall request such leave as soon as practical, but under no
circumstances less than 30 workdays prior to the date on which the leave is to
begin. Such request shall be in writing and shall include a statement as to the
dates the employee wishes to begin and end the leave.

B.    The determination as to the date on which the leave shall begin and the duration
of such leave shall be made at the discretion of the superintendent when
considering the scheduling and replacement problems of the district. The duration
of such leave shall consist of no more than 12 consecutive months and shall
automatically terminate on June 30 in the school year in which such leave is
granted. An extension of leave may be granted, not to exceed an additional 12
months.

C.    The employee is not entitled to the use of any accrued sick leave or other paid
leave while on unpaid leave.

18

D.    There shall not be a diminution of employment status during parenthood leave
except that no employee shall be entitled to compensation, step increment, or the
accrual of seniority for layoff or reduction in force purposes, nor shall the time
taken count toward credit for probationary periods. An employee who is granted
parenthood leave may purchase, at the employee’s expense, district health
insurance for the length of the leave.

E.    If an employee is on parenthood leave and experiences a miscarriage or the death
of the infant the employee may request an assignment to a unit position. If there is
a vacancy for which the employee is qualified, the District will assign the
employee to a position as soon as practical.

Section 13— Adoption Leave

The District shall provide up to three days’ leave without loss of pay or benefits to an employee
for matters pertaining to adoption or placement of a foster child in the employee’s home,
including travel, receipt of child, or legal responsibilities associated with the adoption or foster
placement. Such leave shall not be charged to sick leave.

Section 14 — Catastrophic Leave

An employee may request that the district grant catastrophic leave benefits in the form of sick
leave credits as set forth in Education Code section 44043.5. Requests for and donations of sick
leave credits shall be made pursuant to Education Code section 44043.5, except that no donation
of sick leave time may result in a reduction of the donor’s sick leave balance to fewer than 10
days. This applies to certificated unit members only.

Section 15— Disability Leave

An employee who has filed an application for a disability determination through S I RS may
request to be placed on a leave of absence pending STRS action on the application. The leave
shall be granted for a period not to exceed 30 calendar days following the date of STRS action on
the application. The leave shall be unpaid unless the employee otherwise qualifies for another
paid leave as provided by this section.

Section 16— Family Medical Leave Act Benefits

An employee shall be eligible for Family Medical Leave Act benefits pursuant to state and
federal law. In order to be granted leave benefits pursuant to this section, the employee must
have been employed by the district for one calendar year, and must have served in at least 60%
of a regular full-time assignment, as reflected by the employee’s wage rate based on appropriate
salary schedule placement. In addition, the reason for the leave itself must satisfy the statutory
requirements.

A. Leave benefits granted pursuant to this section are concurrent with sick leave benefits. In
cases of financial hardship, a qualifying employee may request that the district extend the
District’s contributions set forth in article V, for up to 12 weeks following exhaustion of
the employee’s extended sick leave benefits.

ARTICLE IX
DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

Section 1 — General Provisions

Discipline shall be imposed upon a certificated employee pursuant to the terms of this article as

authorized by Government Code section 3543.2(b).

A.    Disciplinary action shall be for just cause and shall be administered in accordance with
the provisions of this article. Disciplinary actions taken pursuant to the provisions and
procedures of this article are intended to be corrective rather than punitive.

B.    The degree of severity of the disciplinary action should be reasonably related to the
nature of the offense committed by the employee and should take into account any prior
disciplinary action or actions imposed on the employee. When the district determines it
to be appropriate, disciplinary action imposed pursuant to the provisions of this article
will be preceded by an oral and/or written warning. Oral and written warnings do not
constitute disciplinary action and will not be placed in the personnel file,

C.    “Discipline” shall mean a letter of warning, letter of reprimand or a suspension without
pay of up to 15 work days when any of these actions is taken as a result of an offense
committed by the employee.

D.    The following are not considered disciplinary actions and are specifically excluded from
the provisions and procedures of this article: an oral warning, an incident report, or the
deduction of pay for being absent without leave,

E.    The provisions and procedures of this article shall not apply to a “Notice of
Unprofessional Conduct” or a “Notice of Unsatisfactory Performance” as set forth in
Education Code section 44938 or to any proceeding that arises out of the issuance of such
a notice. Also specifically excluded from the provisions and procedures of this article are
actions taken by the district as part of the process of evaluation of an employee’s work
performance or that are related to the placement of materials in the district’s personnel
file pursuant to the provisions of article VII.

F.    No disciplinary action shall be taken for any cause that arose prior to the employee
becoming a permanent employee of the district or for any cause that arose more than two
years preceding the date of the notice of disciplinary action unless the cause was
concealed or not disclosed by the employee when it reasonably could be assumed that the
employee should have disclosed the facts to the district.

20

Section 2 — Pre-Discipline Investigation

Any proposed disciplinary action shall be brought to the attention of the superintendent. The
superintendent or designee shall investigate the matter and shall hold an informal meeting with
the employee or, if requested by the employee, with the employee and a representative within
five work days after the matter has been brought to the superintendent’s attention.

A.    The employee shall be informed of the right to be accompanied to the meeting by a
representative. If the employee elects not to be represented by a representative, the
employee shall sign a statement to that effect.

B.    As a part of the investigation, the employee shall be notified in writing of the allegations
and shall be given an opportunity to respond and to comment on the appropriate
disposition.

Section 3 — Notice of Discipline

Within five work days of the conclusion of the investigation, the superintendent or designee shall
give the employee written notice of the intended disciplinary action.

A.    The notice of disciplinary action shall be personally served upon the employee and shall
be signed for and dated upon receipt, or it shall be sent by United States certified mail,
return receipt requested, addressed to the employee at the employee’s last known
address.

B.    Where the employee has utilized the services of a representative during the investigation,
the representative shall also be sent a copy of the notice.

C.    The notice of disciplinary action shall inform the employee of the charges as well as the
effective date of discipline, which shall be not less than six work days after service of the
notice. The notice shall contain a statement in ordinary and concise language of the
specific acts and omissions upon which the disciplinary action is based, a statement of
the cause for the action taken, a listing of dates of related oral and/or written warnings, if
any, and if it is claimed that an employee has violated a district rule or regulation, the
rule or regulation shall be set forth in the notice.

21

D.    The notice shall inform the employee of the right to appeal the disciplinary action and the
time in which the appeal and request for a hearing must be filed, which shall be not less
than five work days after service of the notice. If the disciplinary action is a letter of
warning or a letter of reprimand, the appeal shall be to the Board of Trustees or a
conciliator as provided by article X, at Level Three. If the disciplinary action is a
suspension, the appeal shall be to the Board or to an arbitrator pursuant to Code of Civil
Procedure section 1280, et scq.

E.    The notice shall also contain a form, the signing and filing of which shall constitute the
appeal and request for a hearing. Failure to file the appeal and request for a hearing as set
forth in the notice shall constitute a waiver of the right to an appeal and the discipline
shall be final.

Section 4 — Appeal of Disciplinary Action

If the employee files the appeal and request for hearing as required by section 3 of paragraph E
of this article, the following shall apply:

A.    If the employee elected not to be represented as set forth in section 2, paragraph A, of
this article, or if the exclusive representative declines to proceed before a conciliator or
an arbitrator as set forth in paragraph B of this section, the hearing shall be conducted by
the board pursuant to the following:

1.    The burden of proof to support the discipline rests with the district.

2.    The employee, and a representative if the employee desires, may present evidence
or argument to the board, or to a hearing officer designated by the board, prior to
the board making a decision.

3.    Following the appeal hearing, the board shall adopt, modify, or reject the
discipline.

4.    The board’s decision shall be in writing and shall set forth the findings of fact,
conclusions, and reasons for the board’s determination.

5.    If the board either modifies or rejects the discipline, the employee’s personnel
records shall be adjusted to reflect the board's decision.

6.    The decision of the board shall be final.

7.    Nothing contained within this procedure shall be interpreted to deny an
employee’s right to seek judicial review of a disciplinary action following the
completion of the procedures contained in section 4, paragraphs A through A.6,
of this article.

B.    If the employee is represented by the exclusive representative, the appeal shall be heard
by a conciliator (or arbitrator), at the request of the exclusive representative. The
exclusive representative shall have 10 days from receipt of its copy of the notice to
inform the district of its election to proceed before a conciliator (or arbitrator).

22

Section 5 — Miscellaneous Provisions

When the superintendent determines that the needs of the district so require, an employee may be
placed on administrative leave or may be suspended immediately on an emergency basis prior to
the completion of the procedures set forth in sections 3 or 4 of this article.

A.    Any proposed suspension of an employee, except an immediate suspension made
pursuant to section 5 of this article or the laws of the State of California or the United
States of America, shall be stayed until completion of the appeal process if the proposed
suspension is challenged. In that case, the suspension and any denial of compensation
shall be an issue in the appeal hearing, if one is requested by the employee.

B.    All information and proceedings regarding any of the above actions or proposed actions
shall be kept as confidential as possible by all parties to the proceeding. The notification
to the employee and to the employee’s representative as set forth in section 3, paragraphs
A and B, of this article shall not be considered a violation of the terms of this paragraph.

Section 6 — Complaints Against Unit Members

Written complaints against an employee shall be brought to the employee’s attention within five
school days of receipt if in the opinion of the superintendent or principal it may be considered for
inclusion in the employee’s personnel file.

A.    At the request of either party, the superintendent or principal shall attempt to hold a
meeting between the complaining person and the employee prior to the placement of the
complaint in the personnel file.

B.    An employee, on request, is entitled to representation in the meeting. The employee shall
be informed of the right to be accompanied to the meeting by an association
representative. If the employee elects not to be represented by an association
representative, the employee shall sign a statement to that effect.

C.    If management determines that the complaint is substantiated and shall be included in the
employee’s personnel file, management will attach a summary of its own findings. The
employee shall be notified of the pending placement in the personnel file and shall be
allowed to file a written response.

D.    If the employee disputes the complaint, the employee may request a hearing with the
superintendent whose decision shall be final or the association may initiate the grievance
process at the conciliation step.

E.    All information or proceedings regarding any complaint shall be treated by the district as
confidential.

F.    The provisions do not apply to any written communication concerning any investigation
done by law enforcement when the district has been requested in writing by law
enforcement not to inform the employee of the investigation.

ARTICLE X
DISPUTE RESOLUTION PROCEDURES

Section 1 — Definitions

A.    A “grievance” shall mean a written complaint that there has been an alleged violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of a provision of this agreement.

B.    A “grievant” shall mean an employee or the exclusive representative who files a
grievance in accordance with the procedures in this article.

C.    A “day” shall mean a day when the employee is required to be present and to render
service to the district.

Section 2— General Provisions

A.    Every employee shall have the right to present grievances in accordance with these
procedures with or without representation. Nothing contained in this article shall be
construed to prevent any individual employee from discussing a problem with a
representative of the district and having it resolved without filing a grievance as provided
herein.

B.    The failure of the grievant to act within the prescribed time limits stated in this article
will act as a bar to any further appeal. The failure of the district to give a decision within
the time limits shall permit the grievant to proceed to the next step. By mutual agreement
between employer and employee, time limits stated herein need not apply between the
end of the employee work year and beginning of employee work year.

C.    Hearings and conferences under this procedure shall be conducted at a time and place
which will afford opportunity for all persons entitled to be present to attend and will be
held, insofar as possible, after the regular hours of instruction or during the non-teaching
time of personnel involved.

D.    Any investigation or other handling or processing of a grievance by any party to the
grievance shall minimize interference with or interruption of the instructional program.

E.    Decisions rendered at levels two and three of the grievance procedure will be in writing
setting forth the decision and supporting rationale and will be transmitted to the grievant
and the association. Time limits for appeal provided in each level shall begin the day
following the receipt of the written decision.

F.    When it is necessary for a grievant to testify in a grievance hearing called by the
supervisor during the student day the grievant will be released without loss of pay in
order to participate. Any employee who is requested by the supervisor to participate in a
grievance hearing during the student day will be accorded the same right.

G.    The time limits on the filing and processing of grievances may be extended only by
mutual written agreement.

24

H. All materials concerning a teacher’s grievance shall be kept in a file separate from the
teacher’s personnel file.

I    Names of all aggrieved parties shall appear on all documents related to the settlement of

the grievance.

J.    In the course of investigation of any grievance, the representative of the association will
report to the site administrator of the building being visited and will state the purpose of
the visit immediately upon arrival.

K.    References to specific administrators shall also include “or designee.”

L.    Subject to reasonable regulation, association grievance representatives shall be granted
access to district employees. Access shall not interfere with or distract from duties or
district operations.

M.    No grievance or grievances shall be filed regarding:

1.    The discharge of a probationary teacher.

2.    The failure or refusal of the district to rehire or retain in employment any
probationary employee.

3.    The decision of the district granting or refusing to grant any sabbatical leave of
absence.

4.    The contents of an evaluation.

5.    Discipline pursuant to the Education Code or article IX of this agreement.
Section 3 — Levels in the Grievance Procedures

Level One:    The    Grievant    shall    first    discuss    the    grievance    with    the immediate supervisor in an

effort to resolve the matter informally.

Level Two:    Any    employee,    who, after attempting an informal resolution, still thinks a

grievance exists, may reduce the matter to writing within 15 work days after the act or condition
giving rise to the grievance or within 15 work days of when the grievant should have known of
the act or condition giving rise to the grievance. The written grievance will be presented on a
district form and shall include:

A.    The name of the employee filing the grievance.

B.    A listing of the provision or provisions of the agreement alleged to have been
violated.

C.    A statement describing how the district is alleged to have violated the agreement
(including all names, dates, and places necessary for a complete understanding of
the grievance), the decision rendered at level one, and the remedy sought.

D.    The immediate supervisor shall issue a preliminary written response to the
grievance within 10 days of receipt of the written grievance.

25

Level Three: Within five days of the preliminary written response, the grievant may request that
the grievance be submitted to mediation/arbitration (“med/arb”) by the California State
Mediation and Conciliation Service. A copy of the request shall be provided to the
Superintendent at the time it is filed. The association shall control its participation and financial
responsibility to bargaining unit members for the process through internal procedures.

A.    The med/arb shall be limited solely to the interpretation and application of this agreement
to the precise issue or issues submitted. The med/arb shall not determine any other issue
or issues. Where the district has made a judgment involving the exercise of discretion, the
conciliator shall review the decision solely to determine whether the decision has violated
the agreement. The conciliator shall not substitute the conciliator’s judgment for that of
the district.

B.    After a hearing on the merits of the grievance, the conciliator shall render a written
decision which sets forth findings of fact, reasoning, and conclusions on the precise issue
or issues submitted. The conciliator shall not add to, subtract from, modify, alter, or
amend any provisions or procedures contained in this agreement. The conciliator’s
determination shall state whether the grievance is approved or denied, and if approved,
the proper remedy, and the basis for such remedy.

C.    The decision and award of the conciliator shall be submitted to the association and to the
board for review and implementation.

D.    Any costs for the services of the conciliator and the cost of any hearing room shall be
borne equally by the district and the association. All other costs shall be borne by the
party incurring them.

ARTICLE XII
CONCERTED ACTIVITIES

It is agreed and understood that there will be no strike, work stoppage, slow-down, or other
interference with the operations of the district by the association or by its officers, agents, or
members during the term of this agreement, including compliance with the request of other
employee organizations to engage in such activity.

Section 1 — Association Obligations

The association recognizes the duty and obligation of its representatives to comply with the
provisions of this agreement and to make every effort toward inducing all employees to do so. In
the event of a strike, work stoppage, slow-down, or other interference with the operations of the
district by employees who are represented by the association, the association agrees to take all
necessary steps in good faith to cause those employees to cease such action.

Section 2 — Violation

It is agreed and understood that any employee who violates this article may be subject to
discipline up to and including termination by the district.

Section 3 — Breach of Agreement

It is understood that in the event section 1 of this article is violated, this agreement shall be
breached and the district may elect to withdraw any rights, privileges or services provided for
herein from any employee or the association.

ARTICLE XIII
MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

Section 1 — Term of Agreement

Except as noted below, this agreement shall remain in full force and effect up to and including
June 30, 2016 and thereafter shall continue in effect year by year unless one of the parties
notifies the other in writing no later than April 15 of each year of its request to modify, amend,
or terminate the agreement. Subject to public notice provisions, the parties agree to commence
negotiations on a successful agreement no later than June 1, 2016. If the enactment of a statute or
the issuance of a binding judicial decision serves to amend, modify, or repeal a term and
condition of employment, as that phrase is used in the act, which is set forth in this agreement,
the affected provision or provisions shall be reopened for negotiations without regard to the two
article limitation.

Section 2— Completion of Negotiations

During the term of this agreement, the association expressly waives and relinquishes the right to
meet and negotiate with respect to any subject or matter whether referred to or covered in this
agreement or not, even though each subject or matter may not have been with the knowledge or
contemplation of either or both the district or the association at the time they met and negotiated
this agreement, and even though such subjects or matters were proposed and later withdrawn.

Section 3 — Copies of Agreement

Within 60 calendar days of the execution of this agreement by the district and the exclusive
representative, the district shall have copies prepared for distribution to each employee.

Section 4 — Americans with Disabilities Act

When the district is required to accommodate a member of the bargaining unit pursuant to the
provisions of the Americans with Disabilities Act, the superintendent shall notify the president of
the exclusive representative prior to implementation of the accommodation.

h.    Units and degrees will be accepted only from accredited colleges and universities.

i.    Advanced training units to be credited for salary schedule placement shall be
related to the teaching assignment. The superintendent may approve courses
which, in the superintendent’s opinion, lead to the improvement of classroom
instruction and/or job performance or to an additional educational credential.

j.    Units are defined for the purposes of this appendix as semester units. Three
quarter units equal two semester units.

k.    An employee who anticipates column advancement on the salary schedule for the
following school year must notify the superintendent’s office by April 15th of the
preceding school year on a form provided by the district. Failure to observe this
time deadline or the September 10th “receipt of transcripts or grade cards”
deadline shall cause the units to be held to the next school year.

l.    An official transcript of courses completed, an official grade card, or a statement
signed by the instructor or the registrar of the college or university that the course
has been satisfactorily completed will be required no later than the last workday
before September 10th to be applicable for salary schedule placement purposes for
the school year. Failure to observe this deadline shall cause the crediting of the
units to be held to the next school year.
