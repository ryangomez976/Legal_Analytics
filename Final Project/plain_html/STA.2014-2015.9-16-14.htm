Grievance Procedure

Definitions:

1)    A “grievance” shall be limited to an alleged violation of this Agreement,
or of officially adopted Board pohcy within the scope of negotiation,
personally and adversely affecting the grievant.

2)    A “grievant” is any employee covered by this agreement who is
personally and directly involved in, and affected by an action or
omission, which is the basis for the dispute, or the Association, and
who files a grievance in accordance with the requirements of this
article.

3)    A “party of interest” is any person or persons making the claim or the
person who might be required to take action or against whom action
might be taken in order to resolve the claim.

4)    “Immediate supervisor” is the administrator who has been designated
by the district to adjust grievances and who exercises regular
responsibility for the grieving employee.

5)    “Day” means school day during which the grieving teacher is required
to be present.

Purpose

1)    The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible
administrative level, equitable solutions to the problems that may
from time to time arise concerning the apphcation or interpretation of
this agreement.

2)    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any
teacher having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with any
appropriate member of the administration, and to have the grievance
adjusted without intervention by the Association, provided that the
adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement.

Time Limits

1) Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as
possible, the time hmits specified at each level should be considered to
be maximums and the parties are expected to expedite the process.

Procedure

Informal Discussion

A teacher shall first present the grievance to the immediate supervisor within
twenty (20) days after the grievant knew or should have known of the act or
condition giving rise to the grievance. The parties will confer in an endeavor
to settle the grievance informally. Resolution of problems in such informal
conferences shall not be binding upon the parties in subsequent grievances.

Level One^ Formal Complaint to Immediate Supervisor

If the dispute is not resolved informally, the aggrieved teacher may submit a
grievance in writing to the immediate supervisor within ten (10) days after
the Informal Discussion.

Said grievance, to be considered by the immediate supervisor, must clearly
specify:

a)    A clear, concise statement of the provisions within the Agreement
that is alleged to have been violated.

b)    A hst of persons involved (including conferees, if any).

c)    The circumstances, including the date of occurrence and the
manner in which said provision(s) has been violated.

d)    An outline of actions taken to adjust the complaint.

e)    Supporting documents, if any desired by the grievant.

£) A hst of specific actions which the aggrieved person(s) beheves
would best remedy the grievance.

g) The reason(s) why the decision of the informal
discussion/conferences was not satisfactory.

Superintendent and the Association. Within ten (10) days of receipt of such

Level Two: Review by District Superintendent

If the aggrieved teacher has instituted his/her grievance with a person

Evaluation Procedures

The purpose of the District’s evaluation procedures is to improve classroom
instruction and to maintain professional standards. The process is based
upon the California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTP). The
evaluation procedures shall evaluate and assess the teacher’s competency as

educator.

During the first week of each school year; certificated employees will receive
notice regarding them status on the evaluation timeline.

Probationary

Every probationary certificated employee shall be evaluated by the
administration, in writing, at least once each school year. The written

observation shall be during the first sixty (60) school days. The second

completed at least thirty (30) days before the last scheduled workday.

administration, in writing, at least once every other year. Administrators
may elect to evaluate more frequently those teachers showing need for
improvement. The written evaluation shall include at least one formal
observation of no less than 30 minutes. The observation shall be during the
first one-hundred (100) school days but no sooner than the 20b student
attendance day. The final written evaluation shall be completed at least
thirty (30) calendar days before the last scheduled workday.
