DISTRICT RIGHTS

1.    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers
and authority to direct, manage and control the District to the full extent of the law.
Included in, but not limited to, those duties and powers is the exclusive right to:

and hours of operation; determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided,
and the methods and means of providing them; establish its educational policies,
goals and objectives; insure the rights and education opportunities of students;
determine staffing patterns; determine the number and kinds of personnel required;
maintain the efficiency of District facilities; establish budget procedures and
determine budgetary allocation; determine the methods of raising revenue; contract
out work; and take reasonable action on any matter in the event of an emergency.

In addition, the District retains the right to hire, classify, assign, evaluate, promote,
terminate, and discipline employees.

responsibilities by the District, and the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and
practices in furtherance thereof, shall be in conformance with existing law and
limited only by the specific and express terms of this Agreement, and then only to
the extent such specific and express terms are in conformance with law.

determination of whether or not the policies and practices referred to in this

ARTICLE 5

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

1.    All Association business, discussions, and activities will be conducted
by unit members or Association officials outside established instructional time.
Instructional time shall be defined as the time in which the unit member has a class.
The Association shall have the right to use District equipment and machines outside
of instructional time for lawful Association business, provided that all supplies
used shall be paid for by the Association.

2.    The Association may use the school mail boxes, the electronic mail
service, unit member electronic mailboxes and bulletin board spaces designated by
the Superintendent, subject to the following conditions:

a.    All postings for bulletin boards or items for school mail boxes
must contain the date of posting or distribution and the identification of the
organization, together with a designated authorization by an Association
representative.

b.    A copy of said postings must be delivered to the
Superintendent/designee at the time of posting.

c.    The Association will not post or distribute information which is
obscene or libelous of the District or its personnel, subject to the immediate
removal by the District of the right to post or distribute for a period of at
least one full semester.

for consideration under the appropriate heading, i.e., "old business" or "new

ARTICLE 10

HOURS

1.    The length of the unit member work day, including preparation time,
lunch, relief period, and time required before and after school shall not exceed
seven hours and forty-five minutes.

2.    The starting and dismissal time for unit members (i.e., the work day),
shall be no earlier than thirty minutes before the first scheduled teaching period
and not later than thirty minutes following the dismissal of the last regularly
scheduled period of the day.

3.    In addition, unit members are responsible for other instructional-day
duties (i.e., parent conferences, faculty meetings, back-to-school nights, special
program meetings, counseling students) beyond the unit member's normal work
day as defined in Paragraph 2, and yard duty at elementary and middle school sites
assigned on an equitable basis.

work year shall be 182 duty days, comprised of 180 instructional days; and 2 staff
development days which shall be scheduled as follows: one and one-half (1.5) days

following the last student attendance day of the school year. By agreeing to move
the .5 staff development day from after the last student attendance day to prior to
the first student attendance day, the District's intent is to provide additional time
for individual teacher classroom preparation prior to the start of the instructional
school year except as determined by the District. The salary schedule for 2013/2014

ARTICLE 12

CERTIFICATED EMPLOYEE EVALUATION/CLASSROOM OBSERVATIONS
The District and KUTA recognize that a system of periodic evaluation is essential to
assist certificated employees in developing competency and realizing their potential.
The parties also recognize that information gathered through such an evaluation
system will help the Board of Education when making decisions regarding a
certificated employee's continued employment.

GENERAL PROIVSIONS

1.    At the beginning of each new school year, the District will conduct an
orientation meeting with all new certificated employees to explain District
procedures for teacher evaluation, record keeping, classroom discipline, evaluation
of students, requisition of supplies and other information which the District deems
pertinent and which may be a basis for an evaluation.

2.    The evaluator shall be a site or program administrator and other
administrators selected by the evaluator to assist and/or conduct classroom
observations and evaluations. The written observations and evaluation required
pursuant to paragraphs 3 and 5 below shall be prepared on the KUSD Certificated
Lesson Observation form attached to this Agreement as Appendix 4 (a) and the
evaluation required pursuant to paragraph 6 and 7 below shall be prepared on the
KUSD Annual Certificated Evaluation form attached to this Agreement as Appendix
4(b).

3.    Prior to the initial observation [form attached to this Agreement as Appendix
4 (a)], and subsequent to each observation, the evaluator shall meet with certificated

Prior to the initial observation, the evaluator shall review the documents and
procedures for the evaluation process with the certificated employee. Post¬

later than ten (10) school days following the observation.

4.    In the case of an unsatisfactory observation(s), the evaluator shall take positive

(c)    Provision of additional resources, when applicable, may be utilized to

ARTICLE 27

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

1.    Definitions:

a.    "Grievance" is an allegation by one or more unit members that
he/she has been adversely affected by a violation of this collective bargaining
Agreement.

b.    A "Grievant" may be any certificated non-management
employee of the District covered by the terms of this Agreement, or the Association.

c.    A "day" is any day in which the central administrative office of
the Kerman Unified School District is open for business.

d.    The "immediate supervisor" is the lowest level administrator
having immediate jurisdiction over the grievant and who has been designated to
adjust grievances.

2.    Processing of Grievances:

a.    Informal Level - An aggrieved person will discuss the
grievance with the immediate supervisor within ten (10) days of the occurrence of
the alleged act or omission with the objective of resolving the matter informally.

b.    Formal Level I - Failing to resolve the difficulty through
informal means, the grievant may, within five (5) days from the informal
conference, register a formal grievance. The grievance shall be in writing, on forms
provided by the District, with copies going to the Association, the immediate
supervisor, and the Superintendent/designee, stating the following:

07-05

49

(1)    Statement of the Grievance - listing the specific actions and
events alleged to violate this Agreement and the provisions violated;

(2)    Steps taken to resolve the difference through informal means;

(3)    Steps the grievant recommends the District take to remedy the

grievance.

The immediate supervisor shall communicate a decision in writing to
the grievant, with a copy to the Superintendent/designee and the Association
within five (5) days after receiving the grievance.

c.    Level II - In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the
decision at Level I, he/she may appeal the decision to the Superintendent/designee
within five (5) days after receiving the Level I decision. The written appeal shall
contain the following:

(1)    A copy of the original grievance

(2)    The decision rendered at Level I

(3)    A clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

The Superintendent/designee shall confer with the grievant and shall
communicate a decision in writing to the grievant, with a copy to the Association
and the immediate supervisor, within five (5) days after receiving the appeal.

d.    Level III - In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the
decision at Level II, he/she may appeal the decision to the Board of Trustees within
five (5) days after receiving the Level II decision. A copy of the appeal shall also be
submitted to the Association. The grievant shall furnish the Board with a full report
of the grievance. The Board, at its next regularly scheduled meeting, shall review

07-05

50

the records and, if it finds it necessary, shall hear additional testimony or receive
additional evidence. The decision of the Board shall be rendered within seven days
after hearing the grievance and shall be communicated in writing to the grievant,
the Association, and the Superintendent.

e. Level IV - In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the
decision at Level III, he/ she may, within 10 days of the Board's decision request
that the Association Grievance Committee, at its discretion and within 15 days of
the Board's decision submit in writing to the Superintendent a notice of intent to
forward the dispute to arbitration. The Association Grievance Committee and the
District shall attempt to agree on an arbitrator. If no agreement can be reached, the
parties shall request the State Conciliation Service to supply a list of five (5) names
of persons experienced in hearing grievances in public schools. Each party shall
alternately strike a name until only one (1) name remains. The remaining panel
member shall be the arbitrator. The order of striking shall be determined by lot.

(1)    The Association Grievance Committee has the sole authority
and discretion to approve or disapprove any grievance for processing beyond Level
III of the Article.

(2)    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator in the hearing shall be
borne equally by the District and the Association. All other expenses shall be borne
by the party incurring them.

(3)    The arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear evidence and
render a decision on the issue or issues submitted. If the parties cannot agree upon

07-05

a submission agreement, the arbitrator shall determine the issues by referring to the
written grievance and the answers thereto at each step.

(4)    The arbitrator shall have no power to add to, subtract from, or
modify the terms of this Agreement.

(5)    After a hearing, the arbitrator shall submit in writing to all
parties his/her findings of fact, reasoning, and conclusions on the issues submitted.

(6) The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding

on all parties, subject only to judicial review.

3. Representation:

a.    No employees shall be required to be represented by the

b.    An employee may request the Association to represent

c.    If an employee pursues a grievance without the intervention of

(2)    Conferences and hearings in the grievance processing

STIPENDS:

1    One-time incentive for new employees hired to teach in difficult to fill subject areas: Any
unit member hired on or after July 1, 2006, who possesses a clear credential in math,
science or special education, and who accepts a full-time teaching assignment in the
subject area of that credential, shall receive a one-time only incentive stipend of $2000.

2    Master's Degree: Teachers with an earned Master's Degree shall receive an additional
one thousand dollars ($1,000).

3    Longevity: Only those bargaining unit members who are paid pursuant to the Certificated
Salary Schedule shall be eligible for the longevity stipends set forth below.

a.    Upon completion of twenty (20) consecutive years of full-time service to the District,
effective July 1, 2006 any unit member frozen on the Certificated

Salary Schedule at Step 13 of Class V (BA + 90) who continues teaching in a full-time
assignment shall receive an annual stipend of $1250.

b.    Upon completion of twenty-five (25) consecutive years of full-time service to the
District, effective July 1, 2006 any unit member frozen on the Certificated

Salary Schedule at Step 13 of Class V (BA + 90) who continues teaching in a full-time
assignment shall receive an annual stipend of $1750.

c.    Upon completion of thirty (30) consecutive years of full-time service to the District,
effective July 1, 2006 any unit member frozen on the Certificated

Salary Schedule at Step 13 of Class V (BA + 90) who continues teaching in a full-time

102

assignment shall receive an annual stipend of $2250.

d. For purposes of longevity stipends, any Board approved leave of absence, whether
paid or unpaid, shall not be considered a break in service. However, to be eligible for
longevity, the unit member must have provided the requisite years of full-time service.

4 Speech Language Pathologist: Shall earn an additional 7% of the unit member's annual
base salary paid monthly.

INFORMATION:

1    Once the unit member has an intern, preliminary or clear credential he/she will be
appropriately placed on the salary schedule based on his/her years of experience and
college units.

2    Teachers who are employed by the District for the first time and who have previous K-12
school teaching experience which require a credential (clear, preliminary, intern,
emergency) shall be placed on the salary schedule in the appropriate class and at the step
commensurate with their years of teaching experience. Under no circumstances shall new
hires be credited with more than 10 years of experience.

3    Any teacher hired on or after July 1, 2006, in Class BA shall not advance more than five
steps as long as he/she remains in Class BA.

4    Class advancement will be determined by the number of verified units submitted. The
number of classes a teacher moves in any school year shall be commensurate with the
number of verified units submitted by the teacher.
