ARTICLE 1 (Recognition)

The District recognizes the Hope District Teachers' Association (Association) as
the exclusive representative of all classroom teachers in the District (including resource
specialists, SDC teachers, speech teachers and reading teachers). The recognized unit
excludes managers, supervisors, confidential employees, substitutes and classified
employees.

This Agreement is entered into pursuant to the Educational Employment
Relations Act, Government Code section 3540 et seq.

ARTICLE 2 (Association Rights)

Section 2.1 Communications

The Association shall have the right to use District-provided bulletin
boards (one at each campus), mail boxes, e-mail (for announcements of
meetings) and other means of communication, provided such use is
consistent with District procedures/regulations. At the time of posting or
distribution, the principal and the Superintendent shall be provided with
copies of the material posted/distributed. Any communication posted on a
bulletin board, sent through school means of distribution, or placed in
employee mail boxes shall be dated and identified as an Association-
authored document.

Section 2.2 Reasonable Access

The Association shall have the right to reasonable access to certificated
employees at their place of assignment. Such access however, shall not
interfere with the efficient operation of the school or with employee
performance, as determined by the immediate supervisor. Authorized
Association representatives shall check in with the school principal (or
designee) and confirm the availability of an employee prior to contacting
any employee at the school site.

Section 2.3 Use of District Facilities

The Association may utilize designated meeting rooms at reasonable
times. The approval of the Superintendent or designee shall be required
for the use of school facilities. Approval shall not be withheld
unreasonably. The President of the Association, or other person
designated in writing in advance by the Association, shall make all
requests for the use of school facilities.

ARTICLE 3 (District Rights)

It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority
to direct, manage, and control to the full extent of the law. Included in, but not
limited to, the District's powers and authority are the exclusive right to: determine
its organization; direct the work of its employees; determine the times and hours
of operation; determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided and the
methods of providing them; establish its educational policies, goals and
objectives; ensure the rights and educational opportunities; determine staffing
patterns; determine the number and kinds of personnel required; maintain the
efficiency of District operations; determine the curriculum; build, move or modify
facilities; establish budget procedures and determine budgetary allocation;
determine the methods of raising revenue; and take action in the event of an
emergency. In addition, the District retains the right to hire, classify, assign,
evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline employees pursuant to just cause.

The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties, and responsibilities
of the District; the adoption of policies, rules, regulations, and practices in
furtherance thereof; and the use of judgment and discretion in connection
therewith, shall be limited only by the specific and express terms of this
Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific and express terms are in
conformance with law. This article shall not be subject to the grievance
procedure.

4.2    Initial Salary Placement

Initial step placement shall be based on year-for-year credit of previous
teaching experience in accredited public or private schools, up to a
maximum of four (4) years of credit.

Teachers’ movement down the salary schedule (step movement) requires
teaching at least 75% of the school days in any one school year.

Teachers’ movement down the salary (step movement) requires that the
sum of the FTE equivalents meet or exceed the next annual step.
Advancement will be calculated once each year in July.

A.    Column placement shall be on the basis of the number of semester
units completed prior to the first day of the employee’s work year.

B.    Units completed prior to the issuance of the Bachelor’s Degree
shall be counted as post-bachelor degree units only if certified by
the registrar of the institution that they are graduate level courses
and are applicable to the teaching credential, Master’s Degree or
Doctorate.

C.    Upper division and graduate courses must be approved in advance
and be related to the field of education.

4.6 Additional Compensation

A.    Team Leaders

Each school principal may appoint two Team Leaders, one upper
grade teacher and one lower grade teacher. The duties of the Team
Leaders shall include providing leadership in the areas of ordering
instructional materials, conducting meetings, scheduling teacher
aide hours, assist the principal with other scheduling needs such as
duty schedules, attend Curriculum Council meetings, and other
duties as assigned by the principal. The Team Leaders will be
compensated for their year of service in the amount of $1750 per
year, paid in monthly increments.

B.    Outdoor Education

Fifth and sixth grade teachers shall be compensated for attending
overnight camp as part of the curriculum (for example, CIMI,

Astro Camp, Rancho El Chorro and Sage Hill). Teachers who
attend overnight camp shall receive compensation of $100/night.
Compensation shall be made upon receipt of verification of
attendance from the site principal.

C.    Retirement Incentive

The District shall offer a retirement incentive for any teacher
eligible to retire who submits a letter of retirement received by
February 28, 2011. The District shall pay a one-time lump sum
equivalent to the cost of one year of STRS service credit. This
offer is limited to three people and will be granted in chronological
order based on the date of receiving the letter.

ARTICLE 6 (LEAVES)

6.1    Definition of Immediate Family

Members of the immediate family, as used in this Agreement, mean the
mother, stepmother, father, stepfather, grandmother, grandfather, or a
grandchild of the employee or of the spouse or domestic partner (as
defined by State law) of the employee, and the spouse, son, son-in-law,
daughter, daughter-in-law, brother, or sister of the employee, or any
relative living in the immediate household of the employee.

6.2    Sick Leave

Each full-time employee is entitled to ten (10) days of sick leave per
school year for personal illness or injury. Unit members who are
employed less than full time are entitled to a proportionate amount of sick
leave, as are unit members who are employed full time for more than ten
(10) months per year. Employees may use up to one-half of their allotted

sick leave per year to attend to an illness of a child, parent, spouse or
domestic partner of the employee.

Employees may accumulate unused sick leave without limitation.

6.3 Extended Sick Leave

6.3.1    During each school year when an employee has exhausted all
available sick leave, including all accumulated sick leave and
catastrophic leave, and continues to be absent from his or her
duties on account of illness or accident for an additional period of
five school months, whether or not the absence arises out of or in
the course of employment, the amount deducted from the salary
due him or her for any of the additional five months in which the
absence occurs shall not exceed the sum that is actually paid a
substitute employee employed to fill his or her position during his
or her absence or, if no substitute employee was employed, the
amount that would have been paid to the substitute had he or she
been employed.

6.3.2    For purposes of subsection 6.3.1:

a.    The sick leave, including accumulated sick leave, and the
five-month period shall run consecutively;

b.    An employee shall not be provided more than one five-
month period per illness or accident. However, if a school
year terminates before the five-month period is exhausted,
the employee may take the balance of the five-month
period in a subsequent school year.

6.3.3    When an employee has exhausted all available sick leave,
including accumulated sick leave, and continues to be absent on
account of illness or accident for a period beyond the five-month
period provided in subsection 6.3.1, and the employee is not
medically able to resume the duties of his or her position, the
employee shall, if not placed in another position, be placed on a re¬
employment list for a period of 24 months if the employee is on
probationary status, or for a period of 39 months if the employee is
on permanent status.

6.4 Verification of Sick Leave Use

Normally, verification will not be required for short term absences. A
doctor’s certification or other acceptable form of verification may be
required however, for absences exceeding five (5) days, situations where

6.5 Industrial Accident and Illness

In accordance with Education Code section 44984, industrial accident and

illness leave shall be in effect for all employees according to the following

provisions:

6.5.1    Allowable leave shall be for not more than sixty (60) days during
which the schools of the District are required to be in session or
when the employee would otherwise have been performing work
for the District in any one fiscal year for the same accident.

6.5.2    Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

6.5.3    Industrial accident or illness leave shall commence on the first day
of absence.

6.5.4    When an employee is absent from his/her duties on account of an
industrial accident or illness, he/she shall be paid such portion of
the salary due him/her for any month in which the absence occurs
as, when added to his/her temporary disability indemnity under
Division 4 or Division 4.5 of the Labor Code, will result in a
payment to him/her of not more than his/her full salary.

6.5.5    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be reduced by one (1) day
for each day of authorized absence regardless of a temporary
disability indemnity award.

6.5.6    When an industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next
fiscal year, the employee shall be entitled to only the amount of
unused sick leave due him/her for the same illness or injury.

6.5.7    Upon termination of the industrial accident or illness leave, the
employee shall be entitled to the benefits provided in Education
Code Sections 44977, 44978 and 44983, and for purposes of each
of these sections, the employee’s absence shall be deemed to have
commenced on the date of termination of the industrial accident or
illness leave, provided that if the employee continues to receive
temporary disability indemnity, he/she may elect to take as much
of his/her accumulated sick leave which, when added to his/her
temporary disability indemnity, will result in a payment to him/her
of not more than his/her full salary.

verify the absence by submitting a completed and signed district
absence form to his/her immediate supervisor.

6.6.3    The intent of personal leave is not for purposes of personal
convenience, for the extension of a holiday or vacation period, for
concerted activities or for matters, which can be taken care of
outside of working hours.

6.6.4    The employee shall provide advance notification for personal
necessity leave except in urgent situations.

6.11    Maternity Leave

Maternity leave of absence shall be granted to any employee who is
required to be absent from duty because of pregnancy, miscarriage,
childbirth, and recovery therefrom.

The length of the maternity leave, including the date on which the leave
should commence and the date on which the employee shall resume
duties, shall be determined by the employee and her physician, to coincide
if feasible, with the beginning of a term or other appropriate break.

The employee is entitled to use accumulated sick leave, including
extended sick leave, when physically disabled or otherwise incapacitated
from performing duties because of pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth, and
recovery therefrom. After the exhaustion of all paid leave, the employee
will be on unpaid leave status.

A certificate of good health from the attending physician shall be
submitted prior to reinstatement from maternity leave.

6.14 Catastrophic Leave Program

6.14.1    When an employee or a member of his/her immediate family
experiences a catastrophic illness or injury which requires the
employee to take time off from work for an extended period of
time, and the employee has exhausted all available sick leave and
other paid time off, he/she may request donations of accrued sick
leave credits. (Education Code 44043.5)

6.14.2    Creation

acting for all unit members (full and part-time certificated
employees), have created a Catastrophic Leave Bank.

The Catastrophic Leave Bank shall be funded in accordance with
the terms below.

For the purposes of this section, a "day" shall be any day a unit
member is expected to be on duty as determined by the terms of
this Agreement.

Certificated employees who are retiring from the District may
contribute up to ten (10) days of unused sick leave to the
Catastrophic Leave Bank.

Days shall be contributed and withdrawn from the Bank without

6.14.3 Eligibility and Contributions

All permanent unit members on active duty with the District are
eligible to contribute to the Catastrophic Leave Bank. Unit
members are eligible to participate if they are permanent or have
been consecutively employed for more than two years and have
contributed at least one day of sick leave to the bank.

Participation is voluntary and in compliance with the provisions of
Education Code section 44043.5.

The donating unit member must, after the donation, retain a
minimum of one year’s worth of their sick leave. Full time
donating members, therefore, must retain ten (10) days of
accrued, unused sick leave from prior accumulations. Donating
unit members working 50% time must, after the donation, retain
a minimum of five (5) days of accrued, unused sick leave from
prior accumulations. Donating unit members working 60% time
must, after the donation, retain a minimum of six (6) days of
accrued, unused sick leave from prior accumulations. This
calculation will be pro-rated to correspond with the work
schedule of other part-time employees as well.

Contribution to the Catastrophic Leave Bank, on the appropriate
form, shall be authorized by the unit member. The donating unit
member shall execute and file with the District a form authorizing
and irrevocably assigning the donated day(s) to the bank.

Donated sick leave not utilized by an employee shall remain in the
bank.

Sick leave previously authorized for contribution to the bank shall
not be returned if the unit member is released or resigns from the
district for any reason.

The minimum rate of contribution by each unit member shall be
one (1) day of sick leave which shall be deemed to equate to the
legal minimum required by Education Code Section 44043.5.

The catastrophic leave bank shall not accrue more than 150 days.

A unit member whose sick leave is exhausted may withdraw from

bank.

ARTICLE 7 (Grievance Procedure)

7.1    Definitions

7.1.1    A “grievance” is an allegation that there has been a violation of
one or more of the specific provisions of this Agreement.

7.1.2    “Grievant” is an employee who has been adversely affected by a
violation of the Agreement, or the Association.

7.1.3    “Day” is any day in which the administrative offices of the District
are open for business.

7.2    Informal Level (Step 1)

Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant shall attempt to
resolve it by an informal conference with the principal. Such conference
shall be held within fifteen (15) days of the occurrence of the act or
omission giving rise to the grievance or fifteen (15) days after the grievant
knew or should have known about the occurrence, whichever is later.

7.3    Formal Level (Steps 2-4)

7.3.1 Step 2

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at the Step 1
conference, then within ten (10) days of the conference with the
principal the grievant must present the grievance in writing to the
principal. The written grievance shall contain a clear, concise
statement of the grievance, including the specific provision or
provisions of this Agreement allegedly violated, the circumstances
involved, the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the
specific remedy sought.

The principal shall communicate his/her decision to the grievant in
writing within ten (10) days after receiving the written grievance.

337396.1 H0060-008

19

If the principal does not respond within the time limits, the
grievant may appeal to the next level.

Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal
conference. If a personal conference is held, each party may invite
one person to assist or to be present in the conference.

7.3.2    Step 3

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Step 2, then
within ten (10) days of receipt of the principal's decision or the
date the response should have been given if no decision has been
made by the principal, the grievant may appeal the grievance in
writing to the Superintendent or designee. The appeal shall
contain the complete grievance statement, the principal's decision
at Step 2, and a written explanation as to why that decision is not
acceptable

The Superintendent or designee shall communicate his/her
decision to the grievant in writing within ten (10) days after
receiving the written grievance. If the Superintendent or designee
does not respond within the time limits, the grievant may appeal to
the next level.

Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal
conference. If a personal conference is held, each party may invite
one person to assist or to be present in the conference.

7.3.3    Step 4

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Step 3, the
grievant may within ten (10) days following receipt of the decision
in Step 3 submit the matter to mediation using the services of the
California State Mediation and Conciliation Service.

7.3.4    Step 5

If mediation does not resolve the dispute, the grievant may within
ten (10) days following the conclusion of mediation submit a
written appeal to the Board. The appeal shall contain a clear,
concise statement of the grievance and the reason(s) for appealing
the matter to the Board. The Board will consider the grievance at
the next regularly scheduled Board meeting, or as soon thereafter
as is practicable. The hearing will be held in closed session unless
otherwise requested by the grievant. After consideration of the

337396.1 H0060-008

20

grievance appeal, the Board will issue a decision regarding the
matter. The Board’s decision will be binding on the parties.

7.4 General Provisions

7.4.1    Should the grievant fail to adhere to the time provisions for
appealing a grievance, the last decision rendered will be
considered final.

7.4.2    An employee shall have a right to representation by the
Association at all steps of the grievance procedure.

ARTICLE 8 (Hours/Work Year)

8.1    Hours of Work

The parties recognize that the professional responsibilities of employees
include: teaching; lesson planning and preparation; grading and record
keeping; meeting with students, parents, and District personnel; program
development; professional growth activities; and participating in adjunct
duties incidental to the District’s programs. The parties also recognize
that the professional nature of these responsibilities does not lend itself
to a work day of a rigidly established length.

Employees are each day to be at their work location thirty minutes prior
to the convening of the school (instructional) day, and are to spend a
reasonable amount of time after the close of the student school day, to
take care of student needs, attend scheduled parent or administrative
meetings, and participate in adjunct duties. Unless prior notification has
been given to the principal, employees shall normally be at their
assigned sites until 3:30 p.m.

Employees shall be entitled to a 45 minute duty-free lunch period to be
taken as near noon as possible, except when rainy weather or other
operational circumstances makes such time period impractical.

8.2    Non-Student Work Days

On days when employees are scheduled to work but pupils are not
scheduled to be present, the school-based hours will be those of a regular
school day, unless otherwise agreed upon by the teacher(s) and the
Superintendent or designee.

8.3    Report Card Days

The District shall provide up to two days per year to complete report
cards. Teachers with 23 or less students shall be entitled to one day, and
teachers with 24 and above shall be entitled to two days. Report card days

337396.1 H0060-008

21

shall not be attached to weekends or holidays. All days shall follow the
normal protocol for approval. These release days shall be non-transferable
and must be used within the 180 day school calendar (i.e., instructional

year).

8.4    Faculty Meetings

Faculty meetings at each school normally shall not exceed 60 minutes.
Ordinarily, there will be no more than three agenda meetings a month.
These meetings shall occur on Tuesdays, except in a situation that may
occur which could not reasonably have been anticipated.

8.5    Thursday Meetings

Grade-level meetings for instructional planning, student assessment, or
professional development may be scheduled no more than one time per
month for all faculty at each school.

No more than two district-wide grade level meetings will be scheduled
per school year. A district-wide grade level meeting will take the place
of a site-based grade level meeting in the month that it is scheduled.

A district-wide meeting on Thursday will end no later than 3:30 p.m.

8.6    Adjunct Duties

Adjunct duties, such as yard duty and parking lot duty, will be
assigned on an equitable basis within each school site among the
teachers recognized in this contract. In addition, an attempt will be
made to keep the assigned duties equitable district-wide.

8.7    Work Year

The work year for unit members will be 184 days.

All schools will have seven (7) consecutive minimum days to be used in
the fall for parent conferencing purposes. Five (5) additional days will
also be provided in the spring.

ARTICLE 9 (Evaluation)

9.1    Evaluation Procedures

The District retains sole responsibility for the evaluation and assessment
of each teacher working in the District. Any grievance related to the
evaluations of a teacher shall be limited to a claim that the following
procedures have been violated.

9.2    Evaluation/Supervision

Evaluation and assessment shall be made on a continuing basis, at least
once a year for probationary personnel and every other year for permanent
personnel.

337396.1 H0060-008

24

The Evaluator shall meet with each employee for whom he or she has an
evaluation responsibility for that year and establish the employee's goals
for the year, normally by October 15 of the applicable school year. The
evaluator and the employee shall attempt to agree on the goals. In the
event agreement cannot be reached on the goals, the employee may attach
a written statement to the decision of the evaluator.

Permanent personnel who receive an unsatisfactory evaluation shall be
assessed annually until they receive a satisfactory evaluation or are
separated from the District.

The formal evaluation shall be in writing and one form will be used
district-wide. If the employee is performing satisfactorily according to the
District’s standards, he/she shall be notified in writing of such a fact. In
those cases in which the employee is not performing satisfactorily, the
evaluator shall make specific recommendations for improvement of the
employee’s performance. An employee who receives an unsatisfactory
performance rating will be assigned to and participate in, the District’s
Peer Assistance and Review Program.

A copy of the evaluations shall be provided to the employee no later than
thirty (30) days before the last day of school. The employee shall have the
right to respond to the evaluation. The response shall become a permanent
attachment to the employee’s personnel file. A meeting shall be held
between the employee and the evaluator to discuss the evaluation before
the last day of school.

9.3    Evaluation Procedures for Permanent Employees

There shall be at least one formal scheduled classroom observation during
each evaluation period. Each formal scheduled classroom observation
shall be followed by a conference between the evaluator and teacher
within ten (10) school days of the observation.

9.4    Evaluation Procedures for Probationary Employees

9.4.1    Within the first four to six weeks of initial employment,
probationary teachers shall be counseled regarding the district's
teacher evaluation procedures and criteria.

9.4.2    As part of their annual evaluations, probationary teachers shall be
formally observed and given constructive criticism at least twice
per year, more often when necessary. Probationary teachers

will be observed more frequently if the applicable administrator
believes this would improve the performance of the employee.

9.4.3    Within ten (10) school days of each formal observation, a
conference shall be held between the evaluator and the
probationary teacher. The evaluator shall provide a written
notation of any deficiencies and suggestions for improvement and
shall offer the teacher positive help in achieving the needed
improvement. The evaluator also will identify college courses
and/or in-service programs for probationary teachers who need
such training or assistance.

9.4.4    The probationary teacher shall take action to correct any cited
deficiencies, based upon the evaluator's recommendations.

9.4.5    The evaluator shall make a record of all formal observations and
conferences and shall attach all necessary support material to the
evaluation summary.
