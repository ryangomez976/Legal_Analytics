ARTICLE I:    AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
Agreement (herein called “Agreement”) by and between the Governing Board of the
Lindsay Unified School District (hereinafter called the “District”) and the Lindsay
Teachers Association, CTA/NEA (hereinafter called the “Association”).

1.2    This Agreement shall supersede any rules, regulations, or practices of the District, which
are or may in the future, be contrary to or inconsistent with its terms.

1.3    The provisions of this Agreement shall not be interpreted or applied in a manner that is
arbitrary, capricious, or discriminatory. Rules that are designed to implement this
Agreement shall be uniform in application and effect.

1.4    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of
Government Code (hereinafter called the “Act”).

ARTICLE II:    RECOGNITION

2.1    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all certificated
employees employed within the district excluding those employed and compensated in
the positions listed on the Certificated Management/Guidance Salary Schedule.

2.2    Substitutes and part-time/casual employees as defined in Article 3, Definitions, shall also
be excluded from the unit.

2.3    Teachers employed on temporary or restricted contracts shall be included as part of the
certificated bargaining unit and shall be entitled to all rights and benefits herein set forth.
This shall not, however, be construed as granting such teachers statutory rights not
expressly set forth in law, including but not limited to continued employment rights.

7.1.1    The purpose of the procedure is to attempt to secure equitable solutions
to grievances. All parties agree that these proceedings should be kept
informal and confidential, and that the grievant and immediate supervisor
should attempt to resolve the grievance at the informal level.

7.1.2    The filing of a grievance shall in no way interfere with the right of the
Board to proceed in carrying out its management responsibilities subject
to the final decision of the grievance.

7.1.3    Nothing    contained    herein will be construed as limiting the right of any

grievant to discuss a grievance informally with his/her immediate
supervisor or to have the grievance adjusted, prior to Step 4, without
intervention of the Association, provided that the adjustment is not
inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement. If a grievant resolves a
grievance at steps 1, 2, or 3 without the assistance or intervention of the
Association, the Association shall be provided a summary of the
grievance and the resolution and the Association may submit a written
response to the District regarding the grievance resolution. However, the
grievance will be deemed resolved upon the agreement of the grievant
and the District, unless it violates the terms of this agreement. Such an
agreement shall be non-precedential and shall constitute a settlement
only of the grievance.

7.1.4    The filing of a grievance shall not reflect unfavorably upon the grievant, or
upon the supervisor with whom it may be filed.

7.1.5    The grievant shall have the right to request the presence of other
employees who are material witnesses at any step of this process as is
reasonably necessary to explain or resolve the grievance. These names
shall be made available to both parties upon request. Such witnesses
shall be in addition to the conferee that either party may select.

each level of the grievance procedure, it is recognized that multiple
grievance claims must be processed in a sequential manner.
Consequently, at each level of the procedure, grievance claims shall be
assigned consecutive numbers, based upon the time and date on which
written grievances are received. Administrative personnel shall process
such numbered grievances in a sequential manner, following a pattern

7.4 Grievance Procedure

7.4.1    STEP    ONE

The teacher shall meet with the immediate supervisor or designee to
discuss the potential grievance, in an attempt to resolve it informally. The
teacher and/or the immediate supervisor may bring a conferee to this
informal meeting. If the potential grievance is not resolved at this level,
the grievance shall be presented in writing using the grievance form
within thirty (30) days of the occurrence that gave rise to the grievance.
The written grievance must include the specific action and events alleged
to violate this Agreement and the article(s) violated; the steps taken to
resolve differences through informal means; and the specific steps the
grievant recommends the District take to remedy he grievance. The
supervisor shall respond in writing to a written grievance within five (5)
days thereafter and shall forward a copy to the Association and to the
Superintendent.

7.4.2    STEP    TWO

If the grievant is not satisfied with the response in Step One, or if no
disposition has occurred pursuant to the provisions of Step One, within
five (5) days thereafter the written grievance may be presented to the
District Superintendent. The Superintendent shall, upon request, meet
promptly with the grievant, during regular business hours, to discuss the
grievance, and shall respond in writing to the grievant within seven (7)
days of receipt of the grievance appeal.

7.4.3    STEP    THREE

If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition at Step Two or if the
Superintendent has not rendered a decision within the stated time limit,
the grievant may request that the District petition the California State
Conciliation and Mediation service for the assistance of a mediator. The

ARTICLE XXI: DUE PROCESS FOR DISCIPLINE LESS THAN DISMISSAL

21.1    Disciplinary action in the form of dismissal shall be in accordance with the appropriate
provisions of the Education Code (“EC”) and for “just cause” as defined in the EC and/or

21.3    The procedures set forth herein will be utilized for discipline less than dismissal. Conduct
that is of such a nature that injures or threatens to injure the safety of pupils or other
employees or causes substantial disruption to the educational program can justify
skipping steps in the progressive discipline process and/or warrant the District
proceeding directly to termination. It is mutually acknowledged that unusually serious
behavior and/or exceptional conditions can result in full or partial bypass of one or more
levels of “progressive discipline” identified in 21.4, below.

21.4    Any procedural defects regarding the process for discipline less than dismissal will be
