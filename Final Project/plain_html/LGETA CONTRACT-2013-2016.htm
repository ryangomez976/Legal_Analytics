1.6 Domestic Partners:

California Family Code section 297 provides that "domestic partners are two adults
who have chosen to share one another's lives in an intimate and committed relationship
of mutual caring." A domestic partnership is established in California when both persons
file a Declaration of Domestic Partnership with the Secretary of State, and at the time of
filing, all of the following requirements are met:

•    Both persons have a common residence.

•    Neither person is married to someone else or is a member of another domestic partnership
with someone else that has not been terminated, dissolved, or adjudged a nullity.

•    The two persons are not related by blood in a way that would prevent them from being
married to each other in this State.

•    Both persons are at least 1 8 years of age.

•    Either of the following:

o Both persons are members of the same sex.

o One or both of the persons is/are over the age of 62 and meet the eligibility
criteria under Title II of the Social Security Act as defined in 42 U.S.C. Section
402(a) for old-age insurance benefits or Title XVI of the Social Security Act as
defined in 42 U.S.C. Section 1381 foraged individuals.

•    Both persons are capable of consenting to the domestic partnership.

The definition of "common residence" means that both domestic partners share the
same residence. It is not necessary that the legal right to possess the common residence
be in both of their names. Two people have a common residence even if one or both
have additional residences. Domestic partners do not cease to have a common residence
if one leaves the common residence but intends to return.

1.6.1 Affidavit Requirements

For a domestic partner to be eligible for district benefits, the
employee and domestic partner would need to present the notarized
Declaration of Domestic Partnership filed with the Secretary of State’s Office to
the District’s Benefit Office. The employee is required to notify the district’s
Benefits Office of a change in domestic partnership status.

Article 4. Hours of Employment

4.1    The regular K-5 work day shall include thirty (30) minutes prior to the beginning of the
assigned instruction and fifteen (15) minutes after the ending of the instructional day,
but not less than 7 1 /2 hours including lunch, preparation time, recess/break, and time
required before and after school. Any exception to this must be approved by the site
administrator.

At the middle school 6-8, the regular 7 1 /2 hour workday shall include thirty minutes
prior to the beginning of the assigned instructional day. Should a scheduling issue
arise, e.g. Zero period, 7th period, the solution shall be reached by mutual agreement
of the teachers and administration.

4.2    All unit members must remain after their last school assignment as long as necessary to
meet their responsibilities to the students and the educational program. Teachers shall
be required to participate in teaching related duties which shall include staff meetings,
grade level or department level meeting, Back-to-School-Night, Open House, parent/
teacher conferences, district or local in-service. There shall be no more than five (5)
required meetings per month unless mutually agreed upon by those involved. The
following shall be voluntary with some teacher representation: PTA or home/school
association meetings, task force, board meetings, and school site council meetings.
Additional meetings will be required for people holding a stipend position in the district.

4.3    Middle school teachers shall have one duty-free teaching period per day, aside from
lunch, set aside for preparation, planning, counseling, and conferencing.

Beginning with the 2000-2001 school year, the District will furnish a certificated teacher to
provide one duty-free 45-minute preparation period per week for each 4th/5th grade FTE
(full-time equivalent). FTEs benefiting from the State CSR (Class Size Reduction)
program do not qualify for this preparation period.

The intent of the preparation time is two-fold:

•    To enrich the instructional program for students.

•    To provide teachers in 4th/5th grades with time to develop classroom plans
or materials.

The preparation time teacher shall be responsible for planning, preparation of materials,
and conduct of lessons. The presence of the regular classroom teacher shall not be
required during the preparation time teacher’s program.

A schedule shall be developed allowing each 4th/5th credentialed regular education FTE
(full-time equivalent) 45 minutes of preparation time per week. During elementary
conference periods, a schedule shall be prepared allowing half of the eligible 4th/5th grade
teachers their regularly scheduled preparation period during the fall conference time
and the remaining half during the spring period, resulting in a loss of one scheduled
preparation period.

The preparation time teacher will follow district guidelines to procure a substitute in
case of illness or other absence.

Preparation time is considered on-campus preparation time.

Article 5

Grievance Procedure

5.1    The purpose of a grievance procedure is to process a claim of grievance and to secure a
resolution at the administrative level closest to the aggrieved. This grievance procedure
shall not be construed as in any way hindering, discouraging, or denying the settlement
of complaints outside the structure of the grievance procedure.

5.2    Definition of Terms

5.2.1

Grievance — an allegation by a grievant that there has been a misinterpretation,
or a violation of the specific provisions of this agreement.

5.2.2

Grievant -- an individual unit member in the bargaining unit covered by this
agreement who alleges a grievance, or the Association. If the Association alleges
a grievance, it must carry such grievance on behalf of a grieved unit member or
unit members.

5.2.3

Day -- a day in which the central administrative offices of the District are open
for business.

5.2.4

Immediate Supervisor -- the administrator having immediate jurisdiction over
grievant.

informally resolve the grievance.

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision of Level II, or if no written

The parties shall request a list of arbitrators from the California

State Mediation and Conciliation Service and National Academy of
Arbitrators. The Association's representative and the District's

overturns the arbitrator's decision which was in favor of the grievant,
the above fees will be paid by the District. All other costs will be
borne by the party incurring them.

5.3.4.3

The arbitrator shall have no power to alter, amend, change, add to, or
subtract from any of the terms of this agreement but shall determine
only whether or not there has been a violation of the agreement.

5.3.4.4

If any question arises as to the arbitrability of a grievance, such
question will be ruled upon before proceeding on to the merits of the
grievance.

Article 6. Safety

6.1    It is the responsibility of the certificated unit member to report unsafe, unsanitary or
physically hazardous conditions to his/her immediate supervisor. The unit member shall
confirm this report in writing, so that inspection and corrective action may be taken as
deemed necessary by the Administration. In situations that fall within the scope of
District employment, unit members may use reasonable force and means to protect
themselves from attack, protect another person or property from attack, quell a
disturbance threatening physical injury to others, or obtain possession of weapons
upon the person or within control of another.

6.2    Unit members shall not knowingly be required to perform tasks which represent a clear
and/or present danger to the health or safety of the unit member. However, such
prohibition shall not be construed in such a manner that certificated unit members
neglect their responsibilities to provide for student safety.

6.3    Unit members shall immediately report cases of assault suffered by them in connection
with their employment, including cases of attack by any pupil, to their principal or other
immediate supervisor.

6.4    In cases of notification of assault and/or battery, the principal or immediate supervisor
shall promptly report the same to the appropriate law enforcement authorities and shall
notify the Superintendent.

6.5    In cases where a unit member has suffered attack, assault and/or battery, the unit
member shall have the right to contact the District Attorney's office for the purpose
of filing charges.

6.6    A teacher may suspend a pupil from his/her class in accordance with the current State
law. A copy of the current law will be maintained in the District Policies and Procedures
Manual under Pupil Information and will be updated by any subsequent changes in

the law during the year.

6.7    The District shall provide liability insurance covering each unit member acting within the
scope of his/her employment (ten million dollars or more per occurrence).

6.8    The District will notify the Association of any reported assault or battery if they occur
within the scope and performance of the unit member’s employment.

6.9    Unit members who are injured by assault or battery in the scope of their employment
may apply for Worker's Compensation coverage provided that they file the report as
required by law.

6.10    The District will reimburse a unit member for out-of-pocket loss, damage or destruction of
personal property of the unit member sustained in situations that fall within the scope of
District employment if the District is notified within 30 days of the loss. For the intent of
this section, "personal property" is defined as eyeglasses, hearing aids, dentures, watches,
or necessary tools of the trade such as cameras or tape recorders. Reimbursement shall
not exceed $100.00 per year per unit member. However, such losses shall not be
reimbursed if the loss is a result of contributory negligence on the part of the unit member.

Article 7.

Evaluation Procedures

7.1    Purpose

It is the intent of the Los Gatos Union School District to develop a comprehensive system
of unit member performance evaluation which results in the improved performance of unit
members relative to their areas of responsibility. Further, the unit member evaluation
system is intended to commend effective service, to provide clear direction and
opportunity to improve for those in need of assistance, and to include due process for all.

7.2    Performance Areas

Evaluation of unit member competence will include an assessment of the following:

7.2.1    engaging and supporting all students in learning

7.2.2    understanding and organizing subject matter for student learning

7.2.3    creating and maintaining effective environments for student learning

7.2.4    planning instruction and designing learning experiences for all students

7.2.5    assessing student learning

7.2.6    developing as a professional educator

7.3    Responsibility for Conducting Evaluation

Building principals and immediate supervisors shall serve as primary evaluators. A list of
unit members to be evaluated, and the name of the primary evaluator, will be prepared
and distributed to unit members by October 1 of each school year.

7.4    Frequency of Evaluations

7.4.1    Each probationary unit member shall be evaluated at least annually; each
permanent unit member with less than five (5) years of service in the District
shall be evaluated at least once every two (2) years. Permanent unit members
with five (5) or more years of service in the District and whose performance is
satisfactory shall be evaluated once every three (3) years. Permanent unit
members with five (5) or more years of service in the District and whose
performance is satisfactory may be evaluated by the alternative evaluation system
for as long as the evaluator and evaluatee mutually agree to the same. Nothing
herein shall preclude more frequent evaluation.

7.4.2    Timetable for implementation of the District Professional Standards for Teachers
Flaving Probationary 1, 2, and Temporary Status Section 7.1 0, will be followed
for evaluation of probationary and temporary teachers.

7.4.3    Teachers will be given a copy of the LGUSD Professional Standards when they
accept employment in the District. Staff development activities for new
teachers will be held on a combination of release time and after school time.

7.6.1.3    The district administrator responsible for certificated personnel will be

Article 8.

Class Size

8.1    The District will survey each school community to make enrollment projections in February
of each year for the following year. The District will update this information regularly and
communicate it to the immediate supervisor of each school to facilitate on-site planning.
The immediate supervisor shall communicate the enrollment projection he/she receives to
the appropriate school staff.

8.2    Students in Special Day Classes who are "mainstreamed" into regular K-8 classrooms for
part of a school day and who cause the ratios referred to in sections 8.3.1 and 8.3.5 to be
exceeded, shall cause the allocation of aide time only when the affected teacher and the
principal agree that the mainstreaming is of a significant duration or nature to warrant the
allocation of aide time.

8.3    The annual ratio of pupils to the total number of classroom teachers at each school site
shall not exceed the following:

8.3.1    Kindergarten - Third    25

8.3.2    Fourth - Eighth    28    (district-wide    average)

8.3.3    Sufficient staff will be assigned at the seventh and eighth grade level so that the
school's average class size shall not exceed 28. Sufficient staff will also be
assigned so that the average size of sixth grade core classes shall not exceed 28.
Teacher preparation periods shall not be included in these calculations.

8.3.4    Special Day Class teachers, elementary music teachers, nurses, librarians, and
district specialists will not be utilized in the computation or application of the
above class sizes.

8.3.5    For middle school physical education, the gender of both teachers and students may
be mixed in order to accommodate an optimal class size of 40 or less. In the event
that optimal class size is exceeded, 8.6 of the contract will be implemented.

8.4    If the ratios are exceeded, the assigned teachers may submit written alternatives for
consideration by the immediate supervisor.

8.4.1    The immediate supervisor shall discuss with appropriate staff members the
alternatives which might better meet the needs of the students, the teachers, and
the instructional program.

8.4.2    After alternative solutions to the instructional needs of the District have been
explored with the affected teachers, the immediate supervisor shall determine the
most appropriate action to take based on the enrollment of the school site, the
grade level, the content of the courses, methodology of instruction, the
learning abilities of the students, the needs of the teachers, the needs of the
students, and the financial ability of the District to pay.

8.5    Ratios shall not be maintained by assigning children of the same family to different schools.

8.6    If an individual K-5 class exceeds the class size ratio by two or more students and the unit

member may appeal the problem to the Superintendent in an effort to resolve the matter,

school principal.

Article 9.

Assignment, Reassignment and Filling of Vacancies

9.1    Definitions

9.1.1    "Transfer" is determined as a movement, either voluntary or involuntary, of a
district unit member from one school site to another school site; or in the
case of the middle school, from one subject area department to another subject
area department; or in the case of K-5 grades, from one grade level to another.

9.1.2    "Employee-initiated transfer" is defined as a transfer that is requested by a
District unit member.

9.1.3    "Employer-initiated transfer" is defined as a transfer that is not initiated or
requested by a District unit member.

9.1.3.1    An “involuntary” transfer for:

1)    Declining enrollment

2)    Filling a vacancy(ies)

3)    Accommodating the special staffing needs and/or
requirements of any school(s) or department(s)

4)    Reduction or termination of a program(s)

5)    Initiation or expansion of a program(s)

6)    School closure

7)    Administrative necessity

9.1.4    "Vacancy" is defined as a position that the District intends to fill and for which
there is no credentialed personnel available, including those on leave (Education
Code 44920). If a vacancy occurs on or prior to May 1 5th, notice of such vacancy
shall be posted at all schools and the District Office.

9.1.5    An "opening" is defined as any change in a position that the District intends to
fill through voluntary requests for transfers. Notification of an opening shall be
communicated in writing or verbally at a staff meeting in the school where the
opening exists. Intraschool transfers shall be given first consideration. If the
opening is not filled by an intraschool transfer, it will be opened to those unit
members who expressed interest on the January survey; the District will
assume responsibility for this notification.

9.2    Assignment to a school is determined in one of the following manners:

9.2.1    Teacher remains at school due to prior placement.

9.2.2    Teacher is reassigned due to the reduction of teachers at a particular school
because of declining enrollment or the closing of a school (involuntary transfer).

9.2.2.1    Unit members who are involuntarily transferred shall receive physical
assistance in moving from the classroom at the previous school to the
classroom in the newly assigned school. Should relocation be
necessary, they shall also be offered professional packing of the
classroom or compensation at the rate of $200 per relocation.

When it has been determined that a school must reduce staff due to declining enrollment,

9.3.1    When it is anticipated that a staff reduction is to take place, a preliminary meeting
with the entire staff will take place for the purpose of informing the staff of the
potential reduction and discussing the impact of that reduction upon the staff
members.

9.3.2    When it has been determined that a school must reduce staff due to declining

faculty meeting to discuss the need for a reduction in staff assigned to that site.
The discussion will include the instructional implications for the following year's
staffing. The principal will solicit in writing a volunteer or volunteers to move to
another school and will consider any responses to this request in making his
recommendation to the Superintendent.

9.3.3    No unit member shall be transferred more than once in any three (3) year period,

Article 1 5.

Peer Assistance and Review (PAR)

Mission Statement:    To    improve    student    learning    by    supporting    the    development    of    an    inspired,

dedicated and highly qualified teaching force.

15.1 Purpose:

The LGETA and LGUSD are continually striving to provide the highest possible quality of
teaching in order for students to succeed in learning. Therefore, the parties agree to
cooperate in the design and implementation of programs to improve the quality of instruction
through expanded and improved professional development and peer assistance. Referred
teachers, voluntary participants and first or second year teachers are viewed as valuable
professionals who are eligible to participate in this program in the interest of improving
performance to an effective standard.

15.1.1 The District and Association agree that the Peer Assistance and Review (PAR)
program shall focus on the support and development of teachers with an emphasis on
teachers who are new to the profession. The PAR program will continue to supplement
the district sponsored programs for teachers who are new to the district, new to the
profession, and/or experienced.

The PAR Joint Panel will meet annually to review the Program goals and budget for each school
year; this Article shall remain in force as long as state funds are available. As state funds are no
longer available to fund this article, should funding be restored, both parties agree to open
negotiations for the purpose of considering any adjustments to the existing language.

15.2    Definitions:

Joint Panel (JP): Comprised of teachers and administrators whose function is to oversee
the Peer Assistance and Review Program.

Referred Participating Teacher (RPT): A teacher with permanent status who receives
assistance from the PAR program as a result of an unsatisfactory evaluation as defined in
Article 7.5.7.

Voluntary Participating Teacher (VPT): A teacher with permanent status who elects
to receive assistance from the PAR program. In addition, teachers who receive one or two
“ineffectives” may choose to participate in accordance with the evaluators suggestions and
still be considered voluntary.

New Teacher Participants (NTP): Any first or second year, temporary or probationary
teacher, will automatically participate in the new teacher support offered through PAR.
Consulting Teacher (CT): A Consulting Teacher is a teacher who provides assistance
to any teacher participating in the PAR program.

15.3    Joint Panel

1 5.3.1 The Joint Panel shall consist of 5 members, the majority of whom shall be

permanent, effective, certificated classroom teachers who are chosen to serve
by other classroom teachers through Association procedures. The other 2 members
shall be appointed by the Superintendent. A Panel member’s term shall be two years;

however, initially, two teachers will serve three-year terms and one of the district

1 5.3.2 The Joint Panel shall establish its own meeting schedule. Four members of the Panel

time for members of the Joint Panel for meeting, training, and conducting classroom
observations of Consulting Teachers candidates and shall pay for any substitutes.

1 5.4 Consulting Teachers

15.4.1 A Consulting Teacher is a teacher who provides assistance to a Participating Teacher

pursuant to the PAR Program. The qualifications for the Consulting Teacher shall be set
forth in the Rules and Regulations, provided that the following shall constitute minimum
qualifications:

15-3

1 5.4.1.1 A credentialed classroom teacher with permanent status.

1 5.4.1.2 Substantial recent experience in classroom instruction.

1 5.4.1.3 Shall demonstrate exemplary teaching abilities, as indicated by, among
other things, effective communication skills, subject matter knowledge,
and mastery of a range of teaching strategies necessary to meet the
needs of pupils in different contexts.

1 5.4.2 Consulting Teacher positions may be full-, part-, or release time. The Personnel
office shall post Consulting Teacher positions. Each applicant shall be required to
submit a completed application, which shall include at least three references from
individuals who have direct knowledge of the applicant's abilities for the position,
including at least one letter of support from a District principal or Instructional
Supervisor and copies of the teacher’s last two summative evaluations. The
Panel's procedures for selecting Consulting Teachers shall include provision for
interviews and classroom observations of candidates. The Joint Panel will make
the selection, which will be forwarded to the Superintendent. All applications and
references will be treated with confidentiality and will not be disclosed except as
required by law.

1 5.4.3 The Joint Panel will assign Consulting Teachers.

1 5.4.4 A Consulting Teacher's term will be two years, and s/he may reapply for additional

terms. If a Consulting Teacher's assignment involves being released from the classroom,
upon completion of the assignment, unless the Consulting Teacher requests otherwise,
the District will make every effort to return the teacher to the school in which the
teacher taught before assuming the Consulting Teacher position. In the first year of the
program only, the Joint Panel may create both 2-year and 3-year terms in order to
stagger the entry of Consulting Teachers into the Program to maintain continuous level
of experience. Consulting Teachers will be required to attend training programs designed
to prepare them for their role. This training will take place on combination of released
time and non-work time, with appropriate compensation.

1 5.4.5 The Consulting Teachers will work together to plan and implement training for

teachers who are new to the district and/or to teachers who are voluntary participants
in the program.

15.4.6 Consulting Teachers shall provide assistance to Referred Participating Teachers in
the six areas outlined in Article 7.2. For Referred Participating Teachers this
assistance may include any of the following activities:

a.    providing consultative assistance to improve in the specific areas identified by
the Evaluator(s);

b.    meeting and consulting with the Evaluator(s) or designee regarding the nature
of the assistance being provided; observing the Referred Participating Teacher
during periods of classroom instruction;

c.    demonstrating teaching for the Referred Participating Teacher or arranging
opportunities for the Referred Participating Teacher to observe other teachers;

15-4

d.    facilitating the Referred Participating Teacher's access to specific training in
specified teaching techniques or in designated subject matter; other activities
appropriate to the Referred Participating Teacher’s needs and interests.

e.    serve as a member of the Remediation Team for a Referred Participating Teacher.

1 5.4.7 Support provided by Consulting Teachers to New Teacher Participants or Voluntary
Participating Teachers may include providing consultative assistance to improve in
areas identified by the participating teacher, as well as the activities listed in
c and d above.

1 5.4.8 Functions performed pursuant to this Article by bargaining unit members shall not
constitute management or supervisory functions. The Consulting Teacher shall
continue all rights of bargaining unit members.

1 5.4.9 Consulting Teachers shall assist Participating Teachers by demonstrating, observing,
conferencing, referring to additional resources and/or by other activities, which, in
their professional judgment, will assist the Participating Teacher. The Consulting
Teacher, in the case of a Referred Participating Teacher, shall consult with the
evaluator in developing the assistance plan and continue communication as needed.

1 5.4.10 The Consulting Teacher will submit a final report to the Joint Panel regarding the

Referred Participating Teacher’s progress in the Peer Assistance and Review program.
A copy of this report shall be submitted to and discussed with the Referred Teacher
to receive his/her input and signature before it is submitted to the Joint Panel. The
Referred Participating Teacher's signing of the Report does not necessarily mean
agreement, but rather that he/she has received a copy of the report. The Referred
Teacher shall have the right to submit a written response, within ten (10) days,
and have it attached to the final report. The report becomes part of the Referred
Teacher’s permanent file.

1 5.4.11 Teachers who provide assistance and review, including panel members, shall
have the same protection from liability and access to appropriate defense as
other public school employees under the Government Tort Claims Act
(Division 3.6 commencing with Section 810 of Title 1 of the California
Government Code).

15.5 Participating Teachers

1 5.5.1 A Referred Participating Teacher is a teacher with permanent status who receives
assistance in any of the six areas outlined in Article 7.2 as a result of an
unsatisfactory evaluation, as defined in Article 7.5.7.

1 5.5.2 The purpose of participation as a Voluntary Participating Teacher in the PAR Program
is for peer assistance only and such participation shall not cause any written reports
to be produced. Participation of the VPT in the PAR program shall be confidential.

The VPT may terminate his/her participation in the PAR Program at any time.
Participation in the New Teacher Participation is mandatory, confidential, and shall
not cause any written reports to be produced.

1 5.5.3 A VPT may request a particular Consulting Teacher. The Joint Panel will make all

reasonable attempts to honor this request. Referred Participating Teachers will have first
priority; Voluntary Participating Teachers whose evaluator suggested voluntary
participation in the PAR program will receive second priority. A different Consulting
Teacher may be selected to work with the Voluntary Participating Teacher at any time
during the process when requested to do so by the Participating Teacher or the
Consulting Teacher, if possible.

1 5.5.4 All Proceedings and materials related to evaluations, reports, and other personnel

matters shall be strictly confidential. Therefore, Joint Panel members and Consulting
Teachers may disclose such information only as necessary to administer this Article.

Article 1 8. District Rights

18.1    The exercise of the powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the District
as set forth below, and the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in
furtherance thereof, and the use of judgment and discretion in the connection therewith,
shall be limited only by the specific and express terms of this Agreement, and then only
to the extent such specific and express terms are in conformance with law.

18.2    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority to
direct, manage and control to the full extent of the law. Included in but not limited to
those duties and powers are the exclusive right to: determine its organization; direct
the work of its unit members; determine the times and hours of operation; determine
the kinds and levels of services to be provided and the methods and means of
providing them; establish its educational policies, goals and objectives; insure the
rights and educational opportunities of students; determine staffing patterns; determine
the number and kinds of personnel required; maintain the efficiency of district operations;
determine the curriculum; build, move or modify facilities; establish budget procedures
and determine budgetary allocation; determine the methods of raising revenue;
contract out work; hire, classify, assign, evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline
unit members; and take action on any matter in the event of an emergency. An
emergency is defined as any act of God or other unforeseen casualties that may have a
significant impact on the education process.

1 8.3 The District retains its right to amend, modify or rescind policies and practices referred

to in this Agreement in cases of emergency. If the District determines that an emergency
exists and it modifies or suspends any portion of this Agreement, it shall meet with the
exclusive representative within ten (10) days to discuss the impact that the declared
emergency has on this Agreement.

18.4 The Association agrees that they are not the sponsoring group for non-school
sponsored tutorials held in classrooms after school hours. For district purposes,
all after-hours building use by persons or organizations other than district/school
sponsored events will be subject to the regular Use of Facilities procedures as
outlined in Board Policy and Administration Regulations 1330.

Education Code Section 40054, which prohibits private use of school facilities for
profits, is incorporated herein by reference.
