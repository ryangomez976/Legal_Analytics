ARTICLE 1: AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained in this Agreement constitute a binding
agreement (“Agreement”) by and between the Hollister School District
(“District”) and the Hollister Elementary School Teachers
Association/California Teachers Association/National Education Association
(“HESTA”), an employee organization.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to the Educational Employment
Relations Act (“the Act”), Government Code Sections 3540-3549.3.

1.3    Any contract between the District and an individual unit member shall be
subject to and consistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement, and
a copy of the individual contract will be sent to the HESTA President prior to
submitting it to the individual.

ARTICLE 2: RECOGNITION

The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all
certificated employees of the Hollister School District, excluding all those positions
designated as Management, Supervisory, Confidential, and Substitute Teachers.

ARTICLE 3: DISTRICT RIGHTS

3.1 District Powers And Authority

To the extent limited by Section 3.2 below, the District retains all of its powers
and authority to direct, manage, and control to the full extent of the law.
Included in but not limited to those duties and powers are the exclusive right
to: determine and administer policy; determine its organizations; direct the
work of its employees; determine the times and hours of operation; determine
the kinds and levels of services to be provided and the number and kinds of
personnel necessary for the District’s efficient operation; determine the
methods and means of providing such services; establish its educational
policies, goals, and objectives; ensure the rights and educational opportunities
of students; determine staffing patterns; maintain the efficiency of District
operations; determine the curriculum; build, move, close, or modify facilities;
establish budget procedures and determine budgetary allocations; determine
the methods of raising revenue; contract out non-bargaining unit work
including extra duty work; and take action on matters in the event of an
emergency. In addition, the Board retains the right to hire, classify, assign,
evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline employees.

3.2

Limitations

The District’s exercise of these powers, rights, authority, duties, and
responsibilities, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations, and practices in
furtherance of and the use of judgment and discretion in connection with these
powers, rights, authority, duties, and responsibilities shall be limited only by
the terms and conditions of this Agreement and the District’s obligations under
the law, including the Educational Employment Relations Act.

3.3 Declaration Of Emergency

The declaration of an emergency shall be limited to natural disasters and/or
events over which the District has no control. In no event shall the District
declare an emergency for purposes of evading the provisions of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 4: ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

4.1    Right To Represent

HESTA shall have the exclusive right to represent members of the unit in
employment relations with the District, pursuant to Government Code 3540,
et seq. This shall include the right to represent individual employees in
meetings with District management that are investigatory in nature or may
lead to adverse action.

4.2    Right To Associate

The Board and HESTA recognize the right of employees to form, join, and
participate in the activities of an employee organization and the equal
alternative right of employees to refuse to form, join, or participate in employee
organization activities.

4.3    Legal Rights

The Board agrees not to impose or threaten to impose reprisals on employees
or HESTA, discriminate against employees or otherwise interfere with,
restrain, or coerce employees because of their exercise of rights guaranteed by
this Agreement.

4.4    Communication And Use Of Facilities

4.4.1 HESTA shall have the right to District equipment for the use of
conducting Association business. These rights include the right to
use unit members’ mailboxes at the sites. HESTA shall have the
right to post notices regarding matters of Association concern on
school bulletin boards, at least one of which shall be provided in each

announcements at the conclusion of faculty meetings. HESTA’s
Executive Board shall be permitted to use the district email to
announce general assemblies, contact surveys, and contract votes
upon written request from the HESTA President or designee.

4.4.2    Use Of Facilities

HESTA shall have the right to use District facilities for Association
meetings when there is no conflicting previously scheduled use.
HESTA shall clear facility use with the site Principal or designee for
school site usage, and with Education Services for use of the District
Office. This request shall be made within a reasonable time prior to
usage. HESTA will return the facility to its prior condition upon
completion of usage.

4.5    Board Agendas

4.5.1    The District shall provide to HESTA one paper copy of the regular
Board agenda, including supporting documents, at least three (3)
calendar days prior to the meeting. If a Special Board meeting is
called, the HESTA President or designee will receive a paper copy of
the agenda and supporting documents at the meeting. The
unapproved minutes of the regular and Special Board meetings will
be posted to the District website within ten (10) working days.

4.5.2    Upon written request of HESTA, the District will provide
information, including but not limited to the preliminary budget,
administrative regulations, and other public documents to HESTA
in its role as the exclusive representative. If the District incurs costs
in providing this information, the parties will negotiate a reasonable
fee that may be charged. The District shall provide HESTA with the
opportunity to inspect said public documents. Any information shall
be provided via electronic means if the District keeps such data in an
electronic format.

4.6    Employee Names

The District shall provide HESTA, within 20 instructional days of the first
instructional school day, a hst with all unit members’ (including new hires’)
names, assignments, addresses, emails, and telephone numbers HESTA shall
be given the same information for all subsequent certificated new hires within
ten (10) instructional days of employment. None of the documents submitted
to HESTA shall contain bargaining unit member Social Security identification.

4.7

Association President's Release Time

The Association will be provided release time according to Education Code
Section 44987.

4.8    Association Leave

In addition to the leave provided in Section 4.7 above, the District shall grant
a maximum of 20 days per work year for Association business, upon one
working day notification and upon request in writing to the District
Superintendent and/or designee by the Association’s President or designee.
Within 30 calendar days of the submission of a District invoice, the Association
shall reimburse the District for all costs of the substitute or the amount that
would have been paid if the substitute had been employed. This leave shall
not be used to prepare for or conduct any concerted activity or litigation against
the District.

4.9    Agency Fee

HESTA shall be accorded the right to agency fee as allowed by law. HESTA
will indemnify and hold harmless the District for any cost to the District in
defending the enforcement of the fee.

Any unit member who is a member of a religious body whose traditional tenets
or teachings include objections to joining or financially supporting employee
organizations shall not be required to join or financially support the
Association as a condition of employment; except that such unit member shall
pay, in lieu of a service fee, sums equal to such service fee to one of the following
non-religious, no-labor organization, charitable funds exempt from taxation
under Section 501(c)(3) of Title 26 of the Internal Revenue Code:

a.    Foundation to Assist California Teachers;

b.    Christa McAuliffe Institute for Education Pioneering;

c.    Hilda Maehling Grants Program;

d.    Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial Scholarship Fund; and

e.    Hollister Elementary School Teachers Association Scholarship Fund.

4.10    Notice To Association Regarding Program Improvement Status

When the District receives formal written notice from the state or federal
regulatory agencies that a school site has been placed into or has qualified to
leave a mandated California accountability program or a mandated status
under the “No Child Left Behind” amendments to the Federal Elementary and

Secondary Education Act, the District shall give the Association written notice
within a reasonable time. Under most circumstances, the District shall give
the written notice to the Association within thirty (30) calendar days after
receiving a formal written notice from either the state or federal agency.

4.11 Waiver Of Education Code And State Administrative Regulations

Before seeking a waiver of the Education Code or State Code of Regulations,
the District will make available such request for waiver to the Association and
seek the Association’s approval.

ARTICLE 5: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

Both the District and HESTA agree that everyone concerned will benefit when
prompt, informed, and confidential resolution of problems and grievances is
encouraged. Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as informal as
may be appropriate at any level of the procedure. Accordingly, the following procedure
to accomplish these purposes is established.

5.1    Definitions

5.1.1    A “grievance” is any alleged violation, misinterpretation, or

misapplication of any specific provision^) of this Agreement in
writing and which clearly identifies itself as a grievance.

5.1.2    A “grievant” is a unit member or group of unit members or HESTA who

allege that they have been adversely affected by a violation,
misinterpretation, or misapplication of a specific provision of this
Agreement and who file a claim.

5.1.3    A “day” for purposes of this article is any contractual work day.

5.1.4    The “immediate supervisor” is the lowest level administrator having

immediate jurisdiction over the grievant(s) and who has the
authority to adjust grievances.

5.1.5    A “representative” shall be a person chosen by HESTA to represent the

grievant.

5.2    Procedure

5.2.1 Informal Level(Mandatorv)-Immediate Supervisor

Within 30 contractual days after the event that was the basis for the
grievance, or within 30 contractual days of the time when the grievant

reasonably should have known of the event that gave rise to the
grievance, the grievant shall notify in writing, including email, his or
her immediate supervisor (or responsible administrator), and HESTA
grievance chair, of the potential grievance and shall request a face-to-
face meeting. The supervisor shall meet with the grievant within ten
(10) contractual days of the receipt of the written notification or email.
At the grievant’s option, the grievant may be accompanied by a HESTA
representative during this meeting. The objective of this meeting shall
be to resolve the matter informally.

After the informal face-to-face meeting, the immediate supervisor (or
responsible administrator) shall provide a written response to the
grievant within seven (7) contractual days of the informal meeting. If
the immediate supervisor (or responsible administrator) does not
respond within the timelines, the grievance shall proceed automatically
to the next step. The timelines may be extended by mutual agreement
in written form.

5.2.2 Level One Immediate Supervisor/Human Resources Administrator

If the grievant is not satisfied with the written response or no written
response was received within the informal level timeline, the grievant,
within ten (10) contractual days, shall submit a written statement of the
grievance on the appropriate form (see Appendix E-2) to his/her
immediate supervisor (or responsible administrator), the Human
Resources Administrator and the HESTA grievance chair. The
statement shall include a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
specific provision(s) of the contract allegedly violated, the circumstances
involved, the informal response provided, the specific remedy sought,
and the date of the alleged act or omission. The grievant, immediate
supervisor (or responsible administrator), and Human Resources
Administrator shall meet within seven (7) contractual days of
submission of the grievant’s written statement. The grievant may be
accompanied by a representative of HESTA. If the grievant prefers to
represent himself or herself, a copy of the grievance will be sent to
HESTA.

Within seven (7) contractual days after the meeting to review the
grievance, the immediate supervisor (or responsible administrator)
shall state his/her decision in writing on the appropriate form (see
Appendix E-3) and shall forward a copy of the decision to the Human
Resources Administrator HESTA and the grievant. If the immediate
supervisor does not respond within the timelines, the grievance shall
proceed automatically to the next step. The District or grievant may
waive the right to meet in person and the District may proceed with

rendering a written decision no later than fourteen (14) contractual days
from when the Level One grievance is received. The timelines may be
extended by mutual agreement in written form.

5.2.3    Level Two - Sunerintendent/Designee

If the grievant is not satisfied with the written disposition of the
grievance at Level One or if no written decision has been rendered
within the level one timeline described in 5.2.2, the grievant may file a
written appeal of the grievance on the appropriate form (see Appendix
E-4) with the Superintendent, with a copy to HESTA grievance chair,
within seven (7) contractual days after the written decision at Level
One.

Within seven (7) contractual days after delivery of the appeal, the
Superintendent or his/her designee will confer with the grievant in an
effort to resolve the problem. An Association representative may be
present at this conference. Within seven (7) contractual days after the
grievance conference, the Superintendent/designee shall submit his/her
decision in writing on the appropriate form (see Appendix E-5) to
HESTA grievance chair, with a copy to the grievant and a copy to the
immediate supervisor involved in Level One.

The District or grievant may waive the right to meet in person and the
District may proceed with rendering a written decision no later than
fourteen (14) contractual days from when the Level Two grievance is
received. If the Superintendent, or designee, does not respond within the
timelines, the grievance shall proceed automatically to the next step.
The timelines may be extended by mutual agreement in written form.

5.2.4    Level Three - Arbitration

If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of the grievance at
Level Two, or if no written decision has been rendered within the Level
2 timeline described above in 5.2.3, the grievant may request in writing
that HESTA submit the grievance to arbitration. HESTA, by written
notice to the Superintendent within fourteen (14) contractual days after
receipt of the Superintendent’s response (Appendix E-5) may submit the
grievance to arbitration using the appropriate form (See Appendix E-6).
At all times during a Level Three grievance, the grievant shall be
represented by a HESTA member or its designated representative.

5.2.4.1    The parties shall first attempt to reach agreement on the

selection of the arbitrator. If the parties are unable to reach
agreement, they shall request a fist of five arbitrators from
the California State Mediation and Conciliation Service.

5.3 General Provisions

5.3.1    It is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible. The
number of days indicated at each level should be considered as a
maximum and every effort should be made to expedite the process. The
timelines specified may be extended by mutual consent of the parties.

5.3.2    The District will not take reprisals affecting the employment status of
any unit member, party in interest, any HESTA representative, or any
other participant in the procedure by reason of such participation.

5.3.3    Any records pertaining to a grievance shall be kept in a confidential file
separate from the grievant’s official District personnel file. Such records
shall not be utihzed in evaluations or in any recommendation for job
placement. While in the process or within 30 days of the settlement of
the grievance, a member of the HESTA’s Executive Board and/or
grievant shall have access to the confidential grievance file.

5.3.4    A teacher may present grievances to the District and have such
grievances adjusted, without HESTA’s intervention, as long as the
district has informed the HESTA grievance chair on each level of the
grievance procedure. The adjustment shall not be inconsistent with the
terms of this Agreement. The District shall not agree to a resolution of
a grievance until HESTA has received a copy of the proposed resolution
and has been given the opportunity to file a response.

5.3.5    A grievant and/or a HESTA representative will be released from duty
without loss of pay to attend a scheduled grievance meeting with a
District representative. Any unit member who is requested by the
District to appear in such investigation, meeting, or hearing as a witness
will be accorded the same right.

5.3.6    If a grievance arises from the action of authority higher than the
principal, the grievance may be filed with the appropriate management
person. The parties agree, however, that it is beneficial to all parties

-9-

concerned that any grievance be initiated at the lowest appropriate
level.

5.3.7    In the event a grievance is filed at such a time that it cannot be
processed through all levels of procedure by the last working day of the
school year, the time limits set forth in this Article may be reduced upon
mutual agreement, so that the procedure may be completed before the
end of the school year, or as soon thereafter as practicable.

5.3.8    Any grievance regarding summer school, extended school year, and
extended work year assignments shall be filed at level II.

Forms for fifing grievances and other related documents will be developed and
amended jointly by the District and HESTA and shall be available at the District
Office, each school site, and through the HESTA executive board.

ARTICLE 6: LEAVES

6.1    Definitions

6.1.1    Sick Leave Days - Members receive ten (10) sick leave days (6.2.1.1) annually.
Any unused sick leave days are cumulative each year. Unused sick leave days
can be used for retirement. The more sick leave days accumulated the greater
the service credit years in retirement.

6.1.2    Personal Necessity Leave Davs - Education Code 44891 grants members seven

(7) personal necessity days per year. Any of these days used (6.2.2 ) are
deducted from the ten (10) annual sick leave days. Unused personal necessity
days do not accumulate from year to year, that is, each member starts with
seven (7) new personal necessity days at the beginning of the new work year.

6.1.3    Discretionary Leave Davs - Discretionary leave days (6.2.2.1.5 ) are personal
necessity days, and up to five (5) may be used annually. These days, as they
are personal necessity days, are deducted from the ten (10) annual sick leave
days.

6.2 Paid Leaves Deducted From Sick Leave

6.2.1 Personal Illness And Injury (Sick Leave)

6.2.1.1 Annual Earned Sick Leave

Full-time unit members shall be entitled to 10 days of
personal illness, injury, or quarantine leave with full pay

-10-

for each school year. Unit members who work less than
full-time shall be entitled to that portion of the 10 days
leave as the number of hours per week of scheduled duty
relates to the number of hours for a full-time member in a
comparable position. A unit member may use earned and
accrued personal illness and injury leave under
Sections 6.2.1.1, 6.2.1.2, and 6.2.1.3 for illness or injury or
quarantine of a member of the unit member’s immediate
family, as defined in Section 6.3.2.2

6.2.1.2    Accumulated Earned Sick Leave

If a unit member does not use the 10 days of leave
authorized in Section 6.2.1.1, the amount of unused leave
shall accumulate from year to year without limitation.

6.2.1.3    Monthly Statement Of Sick Leave

On a monthly basis, the District shall provide each unit
member with an accounting of the number of days of sick
leave the unit member has accumulated, plus the number
of days that the unit member is entitled for the current
school year.

6.2.1.4    Transfer Of Accumulated Sick Leave

In addition to all sick leave entitlement a unit member may
accumulate within the District, as required by Education
Code Section 44979, an eligible unit member is entitled to
all unused sick leave that may have been accumulated
while employed in a position requiring certification
qualifications in another school district.

6.2.1.5    Extended Sick Leave With Difference Pay

When a unit member has exhausted all available sick
leave, including the leave described in Sections 6.2.1.1,

6.2.1.2, 6.2.1.4, and any leave granted under Section 6.4,
and continues to be absent on account of the unit member’s
own illness or injury for an additional period of five school
months, whether or not the absence arises out of or in the
course of the unit member’s employment, the amount
deducted from the salary due the unit member shall not
exceed the sum that is actually paid a regular substitute
employed to fill the absent unit member’s position or, if no
substitute was employed, the amount that would have been

-11-

paid to a regular substitute if one had been employed, or
fifty percent (50%) pay. The sick leave described in
Sections 6.2.1.1, 6.2.1.2, and 6.2.1.4, and the five-month
period described in this Section 6.2.1.5, shall run
consecutively. A unit member shall not be provided more
than one five-month period of leave per illness or accident.
If a school year terminates before the five-month period is
exhausted, however, the unit member may take the
balance of the five-month period in a subsequent school
year. (Education Code Section 44977)

6.2.1.6 Return To Work

A unit member who returns to work from an extended sick
leave with difference pay must give the District 15 working
days’ notice of the unit member’s intent to return. In order
to minimize disruption of the educational program, a unit
member who is unable to return to work pursuant to this
section until within 15 working days prior to the end of the
semester or prior to the end of the school year may be
assigned other duties outside of the unit member’s
classroom, including substitute duties, at the unit
member’s regular rate of pay, and shall wait until the
following semester or the following school year to resume
his/her regular duties. At the District’s discretion, a unit
member may be returned to the same position as the unit
member held that same school year prior to the leave or
may be assigned to any assignment in which the unit
member is credentialed to serve.

6.2.1.7    Fitness For Duty

A medical doctor’s verification and clearance to return to
work may be required for any absence of five (5) days or
more for illness, accident, or quarantine. If requested by
the District, a unit member shall not return to work until
she/he submits a medical doctor’s authorization to return
to work. The District will pay for the cost of any portion of
the certification requested by the District that is not paid
by medical insurance.

6.2.1.8    Reemployment List

When a unit member has exhausted all the available sick
leave described in Sections 6.2.1.1, 6.2.1.2, 6.2.1.4, and

6.2.1.5, and continues to be absent on account of illness or
accident, and is not medically able to resume the duties of
the unit member’s position, the unit member shall be
placed on a reemployment list. Probationary unit members
shall be placed on the list for a period of 24 months and
permanent unit members shall be placed on the list for a
period of 39 months. When the unit member is medically
able to return during the 24 or 39 months, the District shall
assign the unit member to a position for which the unit
member is credentialed and qualified. If at the end of the
24 or 39 month period, the unit member is not able to
resume the duties of the unit member’s position, the unit
member shall be dismissed. (Education Code 44978.1)

6.2.2 Personal Necessity Leave

6.2.2.1 Personal necessity leave may be used for:

6.2.2.1.1    Personal loss (death). This can be in addition to
Bereavement Leave.

6.2.2.1.2    Accident or sudden illness in the unit member’s
immediate family. Leave granted shall be no less
than one-half day per occurrence or more than six

(6) days total. “Immediate family” is defined in
Section 6.3.2.2. The unit member’s immediate
supervisor may require that the need for the
leave be substantiated in writing prior to
approval.

6.2.2.1.3    Accident involving a unit member’s person or
property, or the person or property of a member
of the unit member’s immediate family, as
defined in Section 6.3.2.2

6.2.2.1.4    Appearance in court as litigant or as a witness.

6.2.2.1.5    Discretionary Leave - During any school year, a
unit member may use at his/her election, up to
five (5) days of Personal Necessity Leave for any
reason that the unit member feels is sufficiently
compelling to warrant his/her absence from
his/her normal duties. Discretionary leave
requires a minimum of 24-hour prior notification
to and approval by the immediate supervisor.
Approval shall be granted if the District

determines that a qualified substitute is
available and no unusual hardship to the school
program is anticipated. The Superintendent or
designee may grant, at his/her discretion, up to
two (2) additional days subject to the above
provisions.

6.2.2.1.6 Other requests not covered by the above must be
submitted to the Superintendent or designee for
consideration. The Superintendent's or
designee’s decision shall be binding.

6.2.2.2 No more than three (3) days of personal necessity may be
taken in succession. The Superintendent or designee may
grant, at his/her discretion, additional days in succession,
beyond the three (3) days, for extraordinary circumstances.

6.2.2.3    Personal necessity leave shall be limited to circumstances
that necessitate the unit member’s immediate attention,
and cannot reasonably be taken care of after work hours or
on weekends. Personal necessity leave may not be used to
avoid attendance at a staff development; extend holidays
or vacation periods; or participate in a demonstration, work
slowdown, strike, similar work stoppage, or other
concerted activity against the District during work time.

6.2.2.4    The unit member requesting the leave shall indicate the
type of the personal necessity leave on the Monthly
Absence Report Form. When requesting Discretionary
Leave, which requires prior approval of the supervisor, the
unit member shall verify the use of this leave by signing
the form.

6.2.3 Pregnancy Disability Leave

6.2.3.1 Use Of Sick Leave

recovery therefrom on the same terms and conditions
governing leaves of absence from other illness or medical
disability. This leave shall not be used for child care, child
rearing, or preparation for child bearing, but shall be
limited to those disabilities described above.

upon a medical doctor's verification of a catastrophic medical
condition of illness or injury as defined above in 6.5.1.1. The written
decision by the Catastrophic Sick Leave Committee can be appealed
to the Association's Executive Board. Within ten (10) duty days of
receiving the appeal, the Executive Board's decision shall be given,

ARTICLE 8: TRANSFERS

8.1 Definitions

8.1.1    A transfer is the relocation of a unit member from one school site to
another.

8.1.2    Voluntary transfers are initiated by the unit member.

8.1.3    Involuntary transfers are initiated by District and not agreed to by
unit member.

8.1.4    An assignment, is the placement of a unit member at a specific site
and position. For the purposes of this Article, a “position” refers to
the assigned grade-level, subject, or specialized duties. A change in
assignment is any change in a position at the same job site. The
assignment is at District’s sole discretion.

8.1.5    A vacancy is any position remaining unfilled after the process of
assignment defined in Section 8.2.1, or a position declared vacant for
filling during the school year.

8.1.6    Date of hire means the date the unit member first rendered paid
service to the District in probationary status as defined by Education
Code Sections 44845 and 44846, or their successor provisions.

-37-

8.1.7 Reassignment Pursuant to Education Code Section 44955 is a change
to a different program and/or a movement from one work site to
another and/or a change in position at the same job site as a result of
layoff pursuant to Education Code Section 44955.

8.2 Voluntary Transfer - Posting And Filling Of Vacancies

8.2.1    Unit members will respond in writing with their intent to return for
the following year and their assignment preferences to the Human
Resources Department by March 15 of each school year. On or before
March 15, the Human Resources Department will notify all
probationary and permanent unit members of their employment
status with the District for the following year.

All newly created positions for the new TK-8 and magnet schools will
not be subject to the assignment procedure in this Section 8.2.1, but
will be posted as vacancies. Once a position at these new schools has
been initially filled through the posting of vacancies, the regular
school assignment process will apply to that position.

8.2.2    All site positions not assigned will be considered vacant and will be
subject to the posting and filling provisions below. The Human
Resources Department will make every attempt to assign unit
members requesting transfers on the intent-to-return form to their
submitted grade and school site preferences. If unable to grant a unit
member’s first choice for placement, the Human Resources
Department will include in the written notification of temporary
placement the reason(s) why the first choice was unavailable.

All vacancies will be posted electronically, and sent to all unit
members via District email and will include school site, approximate
grade level, and any specific requirements or desired skills. The
postings will also be located on the District website, and in a
prominent location at each school site and in the District Office for a
minimum of five (5) working days prior to filling the vacancy. The
filing date will be listed unless the position is posted as “Open Until
Filled.” In addition to the posting, unit members will be notified of
position(s) by e-mail. Interested members will submit their names,
in writing on an in-house application, for open or new positions. Unit
members wishing to receive vacancy notices mailed to them outside
the regular contractual workdays may request this notification in
writing by providing the Human Resources Office with a mailing
address. In addition, vacancies must be posted on District’s Web site
and at each summer school site during the summer school period.

Positions becoming available due to the long term absences of a unit
member during the school year need not be posted. Positions
becoming available within the last five (5) days before the first day
of school may be filled as the District sees fit.

8.2.3    Criteria For Selection Of Voluntary Transfer Applicants

8.2.3.1    All voluntary transfer applicants will be interviewed by
Principals and may be interviewed by the Superintendent
or designee. Principal interviews may be conducted by
telephone. Selection of a voluntarily transferring unit
member for an open position will be based upon
credential(s), date of hire in District, and the unit
member’s qualifications by verifiable academic training
and/or verifiable experience for the specific requirements
in the position’s posting.

8.2.3.2    After April 15, however, there will be no priority provided
to an applicant over other qualified applicants on the basis
of the above criteria. (Refer to Education Code Section
35036.)

8.2.3.3    All unit members will have equal opportunity to apply for
and be considered for openings at other school sites as they
occur, taking into account the transfer’s disruption to the
instructional program during the school year. A current
unit member applicant will be considered on an equal basis
with any applicant for any vacancy that occurs after April
15 for the following school year.

8.2.3.4    In addition, the District will not select an applicant who
applies to a school that is ranked in deciles 1 to 3, inclusive,
on the Academic Performance Index, if the Principal of the
school refuses to accept the applicant. (Refer to Education
Code Section 35036.)

8.2.3.5    Approval of voluntary transfer applications is solely within
the District’s discretion.

8.2.4    By May 25, the Human Resources Office wifi notify each unit
member of his/her tentative school designation for the following year.

8.2.5    Any change from the Section 8.2.4 tentative school designation wifi
be made only on the basis of unpredictable changes in student
enrollment or unexpected staff vacancies. All unit members involved
will be contacted as soon as the change is known to be necessary.

8.3 Involuntary Transfers

8.3.1    The District will attempt to meet its needs and all grade placements
through voluntary transfer. A unit member who did not request a
transfer to fill a vacant position may be selected by the District to fill
a vacant position. An involuntary transfer may be initiated on the
basis of school closure or consolidation, reduction in school site staff,
or when the Superintendent or designee concludes that the transfer
is in the best interest of the District (Ed Code 35035(c). Except for
transfer in the best interest of the District, involuntary transfers will
not be punitive in nature.

8.3.2    In the event that an involuntary transfer appears necessary, the
Human Resources Department will confer with all unit members
being considered for an unfilled assignment to attempt to provide a
solution to the need.

Unit members being involuntarily transferred will be given priority
consideration over other unit members seeking voluntary transfers
for a vacant position if the unit member has notified the District of
his/her interest in writing. Except for transfer in the best interest of
the District (Ed Code 35035(c), the decision as to which unit member
will be involuntarily transferred will be based on the following
criteria:

•    in the best interests of the District;

•    the unit member’s credential to perform the required services;

•    the unit member’s    qualifications by training and/or

experience;

•    legal mandates.

If two or more unit members    are equally qualified after considering

the above criteria, the unit members with the most recent date of
hire will be transferred.

8.3.3    In the event of an involuntary transfer, the Superintendent will
inform the affected unit member in writing of the reason(s) for the
involuntary transfer.

8.3.4    Except for transfers in the best interest of the District (Ed Code
35035(c), an involuntarily-transferred unit member will be given
priority of returning to his/her previous assignment the following
year if the unit member has notified the District in writing of his/her

-40-

interest. The involuntarily-transferred unit member will be given
priority over new hires and voluntary transfers.

8.3.5    Priority/first priority will mean that unless the District states in
writing additional requirements for that position prior to the position
being advertised, the involuntarily transferred unit member will be
given the assignment.

8.3.6    No unit member will be involuntarily transferred twice within two

(2) successive years. This provision does not apply to transfers due to
relocation of Special Education services. However, in addition to the
District relocation stipend as stated in 8.4.1., these Special
Education unit members will receive an additional stipend of $1,000
their second year of involuntary transfer. Should a Special Education
Unit Member be involuntarily transferred three (3) or more years
successively, the unit member will also receive the relocation stipend
and the additional stipend of $1,000 for each successive year of
involuntary transfer to different school sites. Any Special Education
Unit Member that remains at the same site for two (2) years or more
is no longer eligible for this stipend, unless in the future, they are
involuntarily transferred twice within two (2) successive years.
Special Education unit members included in this section are SDC,
Mild/Moderate, Moderate/Severe, RSP, and LSH Specialists.

8.3.7    Involuntarily transferred unit members may be interviewed by the
receiving principal. Any unit member being involuntarily
transferred may prepare a written statement expressing their
concerns about the transfer, and this written statement will be
included in the unit member’s personnel file.

8.4 Classroom Moves

8.4.1 Any unit member being involuntarily transferred, voluntarily
transferred, or reassigned to a different school site or whose change in
assignment as defined in Article 8.1.4 or Article 8.1.7 results in a
classroom move at the same site as defined in Article 8.1.7 will receive

instructional materials required by the site administrator. The unit
member and principal shall mutually agree on the dates to be worked.

permanent unit members at least once every two years.

10.1.2    A unit member with permanent status may be evaluated every five
(5) years if that individual:

•    has been employed in the District for at least ten (10) years,

•    has    been    certified    as    “highly    qualified”    according to state and

federal regulations

•    has    achieved an overall “satisfactory” rating in the previous

evaluation, and

•    has obtained the written agreement of the evaluator.

-46-

This designation must be put in writing and placed on file with the
Human Resources Office by filling out Form “Five-Year Evaluation
Cycle Agreement” in Appendix D.

A unit member may be removed from this frequency of evaluation at
any time upon the request of either the unit member or the evaluator,
and returned to a regular cycle as specified in Section 10.1.1. The
unit member will not be given a summative evaluation during the
year they are taken off of the five (5) year cycle.

10.1.3    When any permanent unit member has received an “overall
unsatisfactory” summary evaluation, the unit member shall be
evaluated annually until a satisfactory evaluation is achieved, or the
teacher is separated from the District.

semester shall begin with an evaluation on the first year of returning
to duty.

10.2 Evaluation Criteria

The evaluation shall be based on the following criteria:

10.2.1    The progress of students toward the applicable state-adopted
academic content standards as measured by state-adopted criterion
referenced assessments and other multiple measures.

10.2.2    The performance of non-instructional duties and responsibilities
normally associated with the unit member’s assignment. (See
California Standards for the Teaching Profession (“CSTP”)
Standard “7.”)

10.2.3    Adherence to curricular objectives. (See CSTP Standards “3”
and “4.”)

10.2.4    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning

ARTICLE 16: SAFETY

16.1    Unit Member’s Duty To Report Conditions Endangering Health Or Safety

16.1.1    Unit members shall not be required to work under unsafe conditions
or to perform tasks or teach under conditions that endanger their
health or safety as determined in writing that the specific condition
is unsafe by the appropriate federal, state, or local agency.

16.1.2    If a unit member believes that an unsafe condition exists, the unit
member shall submit a written report to the site administrator or
designee as soon as possible. The written report shall include the
location and nature of the condition believed to endanger health or
safety.

16.2    District’s Response To Reports Of Conditions Endangering Health Or Safety

Within ten (10) working days after the unit member submits a report, the
District will determine whether an unsafe condition or any condition
endangering health or safety exists, and shall report its decision to the unit
member in writing. If the District determines that unsafe conditions exist, the
District will remedy the conditions as soon as possible. If no remedy is
available the District will find another work area for the affected unit member.

16.3    Unit Member’s Assignment When Students Dismissed

If the District dismisses students from the school as a result of a condition
endangering health or safety within a school building, unit members shall not
be required to remain in the building. Instead, the District may assign unit
members to other instructional activities.

16.4    CAL-OSHA

To the extent required by law, the District shall comply with provisions of the
California Occupational Safety And Health Act, as amended (California Labor
Code 6300, et seq.), and regulations relating to that Act (California Code of
Regulations, Title 8, Section 330, et seq.). This provision shall not be subject
to Article 5: Grievance Procedure.

16.5    District Safety Committee

different school sites to serve on the District Safety Committee. The HESTA
bargaining unit members shall be provided reasonable release time to attend
District Safety Committee meetings. The District shall communicate the

16.6 Notice About Planned Construction

construction that may reasonably disrupt the classroom learning environment.

Annually, the site administrator shall designate in writing one unit member
as the Administrator Designee. In a prominent place at the site, the site

16.11    Unit Member’s Suspension Of Student From Class
(Education Code Section 489101

A unit member may suspend any student from the unit member’s class for any
of the acts enumerated in Education Code Section 48900 for the day of the
suspension and the day following. The unit member shall immediately report
the suspension to the site administrator or the site administrator’s designee.
The unit member shall send the student to the administrator or the site
administrator’s designee for appropriate action. As soon as possible the unit
member shall ask the student’s parents or guardians to attend a parent-
teacher conference regarding the suspension. A District administrator shall
attend the conference if requested by the unit member or parent/guardian. The
student shall not be returned to the class from which he/she was suspended
during the period of the suspension without the concurrence of the unit
member who suspended the student and the administrator. The student will
not be placed in another regular class during the period of the suspension.
However, if the student is assigned to more than one (1) class per day, this
restriction applies only to other regular classes scheduled at the same time of
day as the class from which the student was suspended.

16.12    Unit Member’s Role In Suspending: Student From School

When a unit member believes a student should be suspended from school or
expelled for violating District or school rules, the unit member must complete
a student referral form and submit it to the site administrator. On the referral
form, the unit member must include prior attempts to correct the student’s
behavior. The site administrator shall determine whether suspension from
school or other disciplinary action is appropriate. The site administrator shall
provide the unit member with a copy of the referral form showing the site
administrator’s disposition of the referral.

reasonable action or action they deem appropriate as permitted by District
