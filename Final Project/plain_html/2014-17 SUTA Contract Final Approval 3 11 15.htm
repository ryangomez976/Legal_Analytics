ARTICLE I

A.    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
("Agreement") by and between the Governing Board of the Stanislaus Union School District
("District") and the Stanislaus Union Teachers Association/ California Teachers Association/ National
Education Association ("Association"), an employee organization.

B.    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all certificated employees

excluding the following:    Superintendent,    Assistant    Superintendent, Directors, Principals, Vice-

Principals, Managers, Nurses, Speech Language Pathologists, Psychologists, Program Specialists,
Occupational Therapists, Physical Therapists, Adaptive Physical Education Teachers and Substitute
Teachers. Summer School Teachers not employed by the contract with the District during the school
year are also excluded.

C.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Section 3540-3549 of the Government Code
("Act").

D.    This Agreement shall be in full force and effect through June 30, 2011. The Association shall present
its proposals for a successor contract to the Governing Board no earlier than its first regular meeting in
May 2011. Both parties agree that this Agreement shall remain in effect until negotiations are
completed regardless of expiration date of the present Agreement.

E.    Negotiations for each school year may be initiated on Article II - Salary and Related. Article III -
Health and Welfare Benefits, plus three (3) additional unspecified articles per party per year at any
time mutually agreeable to the parties.

SALARY AND RELATED

A. Salary

A unit member who holds an emergency-permit or a long-term (or short-term) waiver authorization
and has not earned a basic teaching credential shall initially be placed on Step 1, Column I, and shall
remain frozen at that cell until he/she completes a teacher preparation program and earns a basic
teaching credential. Upon completion, the unit member shall move to Step 2 of the teachers’ salary
schedule provided he/she has worked the minimum number of days as specific in subsection B(5)
below.

B. Salary Related

1.    The District will pay $900.00 annually to the holder of a Master's Degree.

2.    The District will allow a unit member new to the District six (6) years of non-district teaching
experience for salary schedule placement purposes.

3.    A unit member who works for the District for ninety-one (91) or more consecutive days will
receive a full step increase in the subsequent year. A unit member who works for the District
fewer than ninety-one (91) consecutive days will remain at the same step in the subsequent year
unless said unit member has worked in two or more districts and will receive one full year
service credit for State Teachers Retirement System purposes.

4.    The District shall provide $600 per K-6 site and $1,200 for Prescott Senior Elementary School to
provide stipends for unit members who perform academic duties outside of the instructional day.
The maximum stipend that a unit member shall receive is $50 per activity. A District and an
Association representative at each site shall meet to determine the number of “advisorships” per
site. If these site representatives cannot make a determination, a SUTA representative and the
Superintendent/Designee shall determine the allocation for the site. Their decision shall be final.

5.    The District shall pay unit members with twenty-one (21) years of credited service in the District
one (1) additional step on the salary schedule. One year of service shall be defined as ninety-one
(91) or more consecutive days of contracted service during the school year.

6.    Staff Development Buy Back Days

a.    The District shall pay a stipend of $230 per unit member per “day” for staff development
attendance pursuant to Education Code Section 44987.

b.    A “day” shall be defined as 6 hours and 30 minutes (including lunch and breaks).

c.    Unit members shall attend staff development activities for the equivalent of one full “day”
to receive a stipend of $230. The District may conduct staff development activities in full-
day and/or part-day sessions.

d.    A staff development “day” shall be scheduled on a day outside the negotiated work year or
during time outside the negotiated work day.

e.    The stipend may be increased if sufficient revenue is generated to offset the expense of
non-instructional certificated employees who attend staff development sessions, but the
stipend per teacher shall not be more than $250 per day.

f.    Each attending unit member shall sign the appropriate District form which shall document
the unit member’s attendance at staff development activities.

7.    For the 2003-2004 school year and succeeding school years, the District will pay $30.00 per
hour to employ unit members to instruct students in approved programs beyond the instructional
day. Such programs include but are not limited to: home and hospital instruction, summer
school, and after school remediation programs funded through summer school funding or other
grants.

8.    In addition the District shall provide stipends to unit members, in Grades K-8, who sponsor
academic or co-curricular activities unrelated to current after-school recreation activities. The
maximum amount allowed per activity shall not exceed nine-hundred ($900.00) dollars. To be
approved for the entire amount the sponsor of the activity must show proof of thirty (30) hours
of student contact time. If the thirty (30) hours is not met, the amount received shall be prorated
on an hourly basis. All such activities must be pre-approved by the Site Administrator who shall
also verify the stipends tot eh District upon completion of the activity.

1.    College units toward salary schedule advancement are given for units taken that

apply to a higher degree, that apply to a credential, or that will serve to augment, improve,
maintain, and/or reinforce teaching skills.

2.    A unit member will not receive credit toward higher classification for units earned on or before
the date of receiving a bachelor’s degree.

3.    Upper division and graduate level courses or workshops from an accredited college or university
may be accepted toward salary schedule advancement.

ARTICLE III
HEALTH & WELFARE BENEFITS

A.    The amount of the negotiated health and welfare benefit shall be available to full-time
unit members and may be used toward the purchase of health and welfare benefit
programs which shall be determined by the Governing Board of the Stanislaus Union
School District, based upon recommendations of the District Insurance Committee. Unit
members, who are contracted for less than a full day and for the entire work year, shall
be known as part-time. The part-time member’s health and welfare benefit program will
be prorated on the actual hours for which the part-time unit member is contracted.

B.    Health and welfare benefits may be augmented at any time after:

1.    The District Insurance Committee has determined an appropriate carrier;

2.    The District Insurance Committee has made its recommendation to the Governing
Board; and

3.    The Governing Board has approved the recommendation.

C.    A unit member may select one or more coverages from the list of available benefits. In
the event a unit member's request to add to his/her health and welfare benefit plans
exceeds the District's contribution as specified in paragraph A above, the District will
grant the request after the unit member signs a payroll deduction authorization.

D.    Married couples employed by the District may combine the District's contribution
specified in paragraph A above.

E.    The District shall make available to all eligible unit members an Internal Revenue Code
Section 125 Health and Welfare Benefit Plan.

ARTICLE IV
WORKDAY

A.    A unit member's work day begins 15 minutes before the instructional day for his/her
pupils. A unit member may leave after the instructional day provided the unit member's
departure will not conflict with any previously scheduled school related activity. If the
District schedules a half-day kindergarten program, the hours of a unit member who
teaches an afternoon kindergarten session may be adjusted by the
Superintendent/Designee to meet the needs of the District. The hours of a unit member
who teaches a morning kindergarten session will be the same as a unit member who
teaches primary.

B.    It is recognized by both the District and the Association that the job of teaching varies
from day-to-day and that in addition to teaching during the day, unit members are also
required to perform other professional duties outside the instructional day.

1.    By way of illustration, these professional duties include all activities required by
law. These duties include, but are not limited to, the following:

Faculty meetings, department meetings, staff development activities,
District and site curriculum meetings. District and site advisory committee
meetings, program-project or District meetings, parent conferences (at
home or school), back-to-school night, open house, athletic and social
events, field trips, school committee meetings, the supervision of
organized student groups, the supervision of students 15 minutes prior to
the start of the instructional day. The supervision of students immediately
prior to and after the students' day and yard duty.

2.    Other professional duties set forth in B1 will be equitably assigned by the school
administrator according to the needs of the school.

C.    Unit members in grades 7 and 8 shall be assigned a prep period within the seven period
instructional day. The prep period shall be the same length as the regular class period.

D.    Unit members in grades 7 and 8 may be required to substitute during their prep periods.
If a unit member substitutes during his/her prep or volunteers to take students for
another unit member and that absent unit member is docked pay based on his/her
absence, the unit member/volunteer shall be paid at his/her hourly rate.

E.    Evening functions, exclusive of performances, shall be scheduled to end by 9:00 p.m.

F.    Relief breaks shall be provided to unit members who teach longer than two and one-half
(2 1/2) hours without a break.

G. Each regularly employed full-time unit member shall be provided with at least a thirty
(30) minute duty free lunch period each day.

ARTICLE VI
EVALUATION PROCEDURE

A.    In accordance with Education Code 44660, members will be evaluated utilizing the
California Standards for the Teaching Profession. Observation and Summary
Evaluation Forms are Appendices A, B, and C.

B.    All probationary unit members shall be evaluated at least once per year. The evaluation
shall be completed by the site administrator and/or District administrator prior to
February 1st of each school year. A summary evaluation for probationary unit members
shall include at least two scheduled and two unscheduled observations. A post¬
observation conference shall be held not more than ten (10) working days after each
observation.

C.    1. All permanent unit members receiving satisfactory evaluations shall be evaluated

at least every other year. All evaluations of tenured unit members shall be
completed and reduced to writing with a copy being provided to the unit member
not later than thirty (30) days before the last school day scheduled on the school
calendar adopted by the governing board for the school year in which the
evaluation takes place.

2.    An unsatisfactory in any area of expectation occurs when a majority of descriptors
is unsatisfactory. An improvement plan for that area of expectation shall be
developed.

3.    Any permanent member receiving an unsatisfactory evaluation in one or more
area(s) of expectation on the evaluation form shall be annually evaluated in that
area until the unit member achieves a satisfactory evaluation.

4.    Any permanent member receiving an unsatisfactory evaluation in three of the first
five areas of expectation will be referred to the Peer Assistance and Review Panel
(PAR).

5.    A summary evaluation shall include at least one scheduled and one unscheduled
observation. A post-observation conference shall be held not more than ten

(10) working days after the final observation.

6.    Notwithstanding Section C (1), a permanent unit member may be evaluated at least
every five (5) years if the following criteria are satisfied:

a. The permanent unit member has a minimum of 10 years continuous
employment with the District.

b. The permanent unit member is "highly qualified" under the No Child Left
Behind Act.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A. Definitions

ARTICLE X

1.    A "grievance" is an allegation by SUTA and/or by one or more members of the
bargaining unit that there has been a violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication
of a provision of this Agreement.

2.    A "grievant" is SUTA and/or a member(s) of the bargaining unit provided for in
this Agreement.

3.    An "immediate supervisor" is the administrator who assigns, reviews, and directs
the work of the grievant.

4.    A "day" is any day unit members are required to report for work. During the
summer a "day" shall be defined as any day the District Office is open for business.

B. Time Limits

Each party involved in a grievance shall act quickly so that the grievance may be
resolved promptly. Every effort should be made to complete action within the time limits
contained in the grievance procedure, but with the written consent of both parties, the
time limitation for any step may be extended.

C. Informal Discussion

1.    Initially, the grievant shall meet with his/her immediate supervisor and attempt to

resolve the grievance informally.

2.    The grievant may proceed with either a or b below.

a.    The grievant shall clearly indicate that the meeting is for the purpose of
informally discussing a potential grievance. The immediate supervisor shall
respond to the informal discussion within five (5) days.

b.    The grievant, the Association on behalf of the grievant, or the District may
request that this optional level be invoked. Upon request, a meeting shall be held

between the grievant, immediate supervisor, a representative appointed by the
Association, and a representative appointed by the District. The purpose of this
meeting shall be to resolve the grievance in a problem solving mode. In the
event such resolution does not result, the grievant may proceed to Level I of the
Grievance Procedure.

Statements and offers of compromise made during the problem solving meeting
shall be considered settlement efforts, and shall not be considered admissions,
should further steps of the Grievance Procedure be pursued.

D. Level I (Immediate Supervisor)

1.    A formal grievance may be initiated no later than twenty (20) days after the
grievant knew or reasonably should have known of the event or circumstance

2.    A formal grievance shall be initiated in writing on a form prepared by the District,
and shall be filed with the immediate supervisor.

3.    Within fifteen (15) days after the filing of the formal grievance, the immediate

grievant and to SUTA.

E.    Level II (Superintendent)

1.    If the grievant is not satisfied with decision rendered at Level I, he/she may appeal
the decision within ten (10) days to the Superintendent/Designee.

2.    The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance, the decision rendered at

3.    Within fifteen (15) days, the Superintendent/Designee, shall investigate the
grievance and give his/her decision in writing to the grievant and to SUTA.

F.    Level M (Mediation)

1.    If the grievance is not settled at Level II, the Association may submit the grievance
to mediation. The Association must notify the District that it wishes to proceed to
mediation within fifteen (15) days after receipt of the Superintendent/Designee’s
decision or fifteen (15) days after the Superintendent/Designee should have issued a
decision in writing if no decision is issued.

2.    The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance, the decision rendered at
Levels I and n, and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

3.    The parties shall request a mediator from the State Conciliation and Mediation
Service.

G. Level IV (Arbitration)

1. If mediation is utilized and if the grievance is not settled in a maximum of two (2)
mediation sessions, the Association may submit a request in writing to the
Superintendent for arbitration. Any such request must be made within fifteen (15)
days of the date of the last mediation date.

2.

The Association and the District should attempt to agree upon an arbitrator. Should

ARTICLE XIX
ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY

A.    The District shall, upon appropriate written authorization from any employee represented
by the Stanislaus Union Teachers Association, deduct and make appropriate written
authorization for dues.

1.    The right of payroll deduction for payment of membership dues, initiation fees, and
general assessments shall be accorded exclusively to the Association. Association
members who currently have authorization cards on file for the above purpose need
not be re-solicited. Membership dues, initiation fees, and general assessments,
upon formal written request from the Association to the District, shall be increased
or decreased without re-solicitation and authorization from unit members.

2.    With respect to all sums deducted by the District pursuant to Section A.l, the
District agrees to remit such money promptly to the Association accompanied by an
alphabetical list of unit members, including their names, addresses, and work
location for whom such deductions have been made, and indicating any changes in
personnel from the list previously furnished.

3.    Any unit member who is a member of the Association or who has applied for
membership may sign and deliver to the District an assignment authorizing
deduction of membership dues, initiation fees, and general assessments of the
Association.

B.    Organizational security within the scope of employee representation by the Association,
requires an employee, as a condition of continued employment, either to join the
Stanislaus Union Teachers Association, or to pay the organization a service fee in an
amount not to exceed the standard initiation fee, periodic dues, and general assessments
of the organization for the duration of the agreement.

1.    With respect to all sums deducted by the District, whether for membership dues or
agency fee, the District agrees to remit such money promptly to the Association
accompanied by an alphabetical list of unit members for whom such deductions
have been made, categorizing them as to membership or non-membership in the
Association, and indicating any changes in personnel from the list previously
furnished.

2.    The Association and District agree to furnish to each other any information needed
to fulfill the provisions of this Article.

C.    Any unit member who qualifies as a religious objector shall not be required to join or
financially support the Association as a condition of employment. To receive a religious
exemption, the unit member must submit a reasonably detailed written statement
establishing the basis for the religious exemption.

ARTICLE XIX

1.    A religious objector shall pay, in lieu of an agency fee, a sum equal to such agency
fee to one of the following examples of non-religious, non-labor organization,
charitable funds exempt from taxation under Section 501 (c)(3) of Title 26 of the
Internal Revenue Code:

(a)    Foundation to Assist California Teachers (FACT)

(b)    The United Way

(c)    Other, as agreed to by the Association and the District

canceled checks indicating the amount paid, date of payment, and to whom
payment in lieu of the agency fee has been made. No in-kind services may be
received for payments, nor may the payment be in a form other than money such as
the donation of used items. Such proof shall be presented on or before the due date
for cash dues/fees each school year.

demands, or suits, or any other action arising from the provisions contained in this
Article.
