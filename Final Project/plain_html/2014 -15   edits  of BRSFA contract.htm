ARTICLE 1: RECOGNITION

1.1 The Board recognizes the Belmont-Redwood Shores Faculty Association/ California
Teachers Association/National Education Association (“Association”) as the exclusive
representative of all certificated employees of the Board, including temporary unit members
and part-time unit members, but excluding summer school teachers, substitute teachers,
management, supervisory, and confidential employees.

1-1

2.1 The right to manage the school district, and to direct its employees and operations, is vested
in and reserved by the District and shall be unrestricted except that exercise thereof may
not supersede any right or benefit expressly provided for in this Agreement.

2-1

3.1    Any unit member covered by this Agreement may sign and deliver to the District an
assignment authorizing deduction of Association membership dues. Such authorization
shall continue in effect from year to year unless revoked. Pursuant to such authorization,
the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues from the regular salary warrant of
said unit member each month for 10 months. Deductions for unit members who sign
such authorization after the commencement of the school year shall be appropriately
prorated to complete payments by the end of the school year.

3.2    For the term of this Agreement, all unit members shall either join the Association or pay
to it a service fee in an amount not to exceed the standard initiation fee, periodic dues,
and general assessments required for Association membership. Newly hired unit
members shall either join or pay the service fee within 30 days of hire.

3.3    For each unit member who is subject to the service fee arrangement described above, and
who has not paid such service fee to the Association, the District, each month of
employment during the term of this Agreement, shall deduct from the salary due such
employee an amount equal to the monthly dues which are deducted from the salary of
Association members.

3.4    With respect to all sums deducted by the District pursuant to this Article, the District
agrees to promptly remit such monies to the Association accompanied by an alphabetical
list of names of the employees for whom such deductions have been made. The
Association and the District agree to furnish each other any information needed to fulfill
the provisions of this Article.

3.5    If a unit member does not have sufficient funds due to the unit member to provide for the
payment of dues or service fees after all other authorized or mandatory deductions or
garnishments have been made, no such sums shall be deducted and the Association shall
assume the duty of direct collection from the unit member. The Association shall assume
the same responsibility in all cases where no deductions have been made because a unit
member’s earnings are insufficient during any pay period to pay such dues or service
fees.

3.6    Any unit member covered by this Agreement who is a member of a religious body whose
traditional tenets or teachings include objections to joining or financially supporting
employee organizations shall not be required to join, maintain membership in, or
financially support any employee organization within the meaning of this Article except
that, once such a unit member has submitted evidence to the parties which proves that a
unit member sincerely holds such beliefs, a unit member will be required, in lieu of a
service fee to pay sums equal to such service fee either to a non-religious, non-labor
organization or charitable fund exempt from taxation under Section 501(c)(3) of Title 26
of the Internal Revenue Code, chosen by such employee from the following:

3.6.1    American Cancer Society.

3.6.2    Easter Seal Society for Crippled Children and Adults.

3.6.3    American Heart Association.

ARTICLE 5: EVALUATIONS

5.1 The purpose of evaluation is to maintain high quality education in the District through
recognition of performance levels. Every reasonable effort will be made to provide
assistance to certificated unit members in need of improving professional skills and
achieving the standards of performance.

5.1.1    All certificated unit members shall be evaluated by the principal or immediate
supervisor under the provisions of the Education Code Sections 44660, 44662,
44663, and 44664, et al.

5.1.2    All certificated unit members shall confer with the evaluator by October 15 each
year. Certificated unit members in the Beginning Teacher Support Assistance
(BTSA) program may extend to November 1.

5.1.3    All certificated unit members shall be observed annually and provided feedback
by their evaluators.

5.1.3.1    Tenured certificated unit members will be involved in the evaluation
process every other year; probationary and temporary unit members
will be involved in the evaluation process every year.

5.1.3.2    Tenured certificated unit members shall receive at least two a formal
observations, with one observation completed by January 20. and the
other by March 1.

5.1.3.3    Temporary and probationary certificated unit members shall receive at
least three two formal observations, with two one observations
completed by January 20, and a third prior to March 1.

5.1.3.4    Observations may be made on a random basis. Additional
observations may be made where necessary in the judgment of the
evaluator. Evaluatee may request further observation by the evaluator.

5.1.3.5    A conference for the purpose of providing feedback will be held.
Conferences for the purpose of this section shall be held within seven
working days following the observation. Conferences will be held at
the convenience of the evaluatee whenever possible.

5.1.3.6    A copy of the written OBSERVATION SUMMARY by the evaluator
will be given to the unit member. A copy of the OBSERVATION
SUMMARY is to be kept by the evaluator, and one copy placed in the
unit member’s personnel file. The unit member being evaluated is to
sign the OBSERVATION SUMMARY. This signature does not
indicate approval of the content, but rather that the summary has been
received by the unit member.

ARTICLE 6: TRANSFER

6.1.1    Transfer

A transfer shall consist of a unit member’s change in work location from one
school or work site to another school or work site within the District. Such a
transfer does not encompass the process of assignment to a specific position and
responsibilities within the school, or department, or work location. A unit
member assigned to more than one work site shall be considered as being
transferred only when moved from one District-wide program to another program.

6.1.2    Assignment/Reassianment

Assignment/Reassignment is the designation of a specific position or
responsibilities within a school, department, or work location.
Assignment/Reassignment includes the placement of an employed bargaining unit
member in a specific school or work locations as well as the change of a unit
member’s position or responsibilities within the same school, department, or work
location. An Assignment/ Reassignment does not include an extra-curricular duty.

6.1.3    Itinerant Teacher

An itinerant teacher is a unit member who travels from school site to school site
and performs his or her employment duties at multiple school sites.

6.1.4    Vacancy

Vacancy is defined as any new or existing opening which occurs after the onsite
staffing process has been completed. Refer to Section 6.4.1.

6.2 General Provisions

6.2.1    All transfers and assignments shall be based on the following criteria: credentials,
seniority, demonstrated strengths, experience in subject matter and/or grade level,
qualifications, the articulated needs of the District, the welfare of the students, and
special skills listed in the vacancy announcement. The criteria are not listed in
order of importance, nor are they to be considered of equal value as long as the
criteria are applied in the same manner to all applicants for a particular position.
When only internal applicants are being considered and all of the qualifications
are equal, the Administrator shall consider seniority as a significant factor in
making the final selection for the transfer or assignment. When internal and
external applicants are being considered, and all qualifications are equal,
permanent unit members seeking a transfer to an open position within the District
will have priority over new hires.

6.2.2    The District will make a good faith effort not to transfer or change the assignment
of unit members more frequently than once every two consecutive years. When

Assignment And Change In Assignment

6.3.1    Once each year, a unit member will complete the “Intent to Return” form, which
shall include a provision for stating a unit member’s preferences for assignment
within the school, department, or work unit and any desire for a general transfer.
Site administrator or immediate supervisor will thereafter make assignments of
unit members based on the above criteria in Section 6.2, taking into account the
unit member’s preferences as stated in the intent form for assignments and
transfers. If the site administrator or immediate supervisor intends to change a
unit member’s assignment, the unit member shall be notified of that change and
provided an opportunity to conference with the administrator before the final
decision is made.

6.3.2    A unit member may not be assigned two consecutive years or two grade levels
above or below the present assignment without the consent of the unit member.
In the middle school, the District will mitigate the impact of any unit member
involuntarily assigned to four or more preparations, to teaching outside the scope
of the unit member’s credential, or to more than two classrooms. The form of
mitigation may include, but not be limited to, the following: release from adjunct
duties and duty schedule; assignment of additional classroom assistance; or
assignment of a classroom for exclusive use. In addition, if the involuntary
assignment to four or more preparations reoccurs the following year, the
assignment will be rotated among appropriate staff.

6.3.3    Failure of a unit member to complete the intent to return form by February 15, or
failure of a unit member on leave to notify the District of the intent to return by
February 1, will result in the loss of assignment preferences and voluntary transfer
rights in this article. The Association and the District will encourage unit
members not returning to provide the District with timely notice to ensure the best
opportunity to fill the position.

6.3.4 Positions that become vacant during the school year (the first work day through
the last workday) shall be filled as a temporary assignment by a new hire. This
position shall then be posted as vacant for the following school year. “Temporary

6-2

assignment” denotes the temporary placement of the individual in the District;
and it does not imply employment status as a temporary unit member.

6.3.5 By no later than April 1 of each year, the principal will discuss next year’s
tentative assignment with each returning permanent staff. Positions held by unit
members of temporary or probationary status will be listed as filled, subject to the
retention or reelection for the following year. All positions filled as a temporary
assignment as defined by Subsection 6.3.4, will be designated as vacant. Any
positions not filled after the assignment process will be considered a vacancy and
posted within the District, starting April 15.

6.4 Voluntary Transfers - Posting And Filling Of Vacancies

6.4.1    Beginning April 15, all positions that are not filled after the assignment process is
completed will be posted as vacancies for at least five working days in the District
administration office and at each work site. In addition, any position that
thereafter becomes vacant will be posted, up to two weeks before the first day of
instruction for the next school year. The vacancy notice shall include the site or
location, grade level or subject matter assignment, the qualifications and special
skills required, and the date for closing applications, except for middle school
vacancies where the notice may be generic. During the summer vacation period,
vacancy notices will be posted on the District’s web site and at the District office.

6.4.2    All internal applicants meeting the criteria are entitled to a conference with the
site administrator making the recommendation to fill the vacancy. These
conferences will be completed before the District interviews candidates new to
the District. Internal applicants will be notified of a decision within twenty (20)
working days of the conference. The selection will be made using the above-listed
criteria in 6.2.1 and the qualifications listed in the vacancy announcement.

6.5 Involuntary Transfer Based On District Need

6.5.1    Involuntary transfer may be initiated at any time based on the District’s legitimate
needs other than those listed in Section 6.6 below.

6.5.2    If feasible, involuntary transfers initiated by the District will not be finalized until,
if requested by the unit member, a conference has been held between the
employee and the Assistant Superintendent.

6.5.3    Unit members being transferred or reassigned involuntarily shall have the right to
indicate preferences from a list of vacancies. If the unit member’s preference
cannot be honored, the reason(s) for the denial shall be given in writing by the
Superintendent. If agreement on the transfer is not possible, the Superintendent,
or a designee, shall make the decision with regard to the transfer.

ARTICLE 7: LEAVES OF ABSENCE

7.1.1    Unit members will be notified of the amount of their accumulated sick-leave days

Under no circumstances should any unit member be absent from school without

7.1.3    If at all possible, absence from school should be anticipated.

Failure to provide adequate notice shall be grounds for denial of leave

secretary during the school day so that the substitute may be advised of
the unit member’s plans for the next day.

Unit members may not use Personal Business Leave for any of the Teacher
Training Days as defined in Section 13.6 of this Agreement.

7.2    Absence From Duties Due To Illness Or Injury

7.2.1 Sick Leave

Each full-time unit member shall be allowed 10 days of absence due to personal
illness or injury each year. Unit members working less than five days per week
shall be entitled to a proration of 10 days. Any days not used shall be
accumulated by the unit member for use if necessary during succeeding years.

7-1

7.2.2 Difference Pay

After all earned sick-leave days at full pay have been used and the unit member
continues to be absent because of an illness or accident for an additional period of
five school months, the unit member shall receive the difference between the unit
member’s own salary and the amount paid a substitute at the current per diem
substitute rate, up to a total of five school months. This provision is not intended
to cover illness or accidents that require an absence of short duration. The sick
leave, including accumulated sick leave and the five-month differential, shall run
consecutively. A unit member shall not be provided more than one five-month
period per illness or accident. In order to qualify for difference pay, a physician’s
certification is required for each illness or accident.

7.2.3    Leave For Illness Of A Child. Parent. Or Spouse/Domestic Partner (Labor Code
Section 233faY)

7.2.3.1    In any school year a unit member may use up to a maximum of five

spouse/domestic partner.

7.2.3.2    As used in this section, “child” means a biological, foster, or adopted
child, a stepchild, a legal ward, or a child of a person standing in loco

stepparent, or a legal guardian.

7.2.3.3    This section does not extend the maximum period of leave to which a
unit member is entitled under the Family and Medical Leave Act or the
California Family Rights Act and the District’s implementing policies,
regardless of whether the unit member receives sick leave
compensation during that leave.

7.2.4    Upon a reasonable basis for belief of misuse of leave under this section, the
District may require written verification of the illness, which may include a
physician’s statement by the employee’s physician, or at the District’s discretion
and expense, verification by a physician selected by the District.

7.2.5    When any absence exceeds five consecutive work days, the District may require
that the unit member submit a statement signed by a physician describing the
cause of the absence, the nature of the illness, and the expected date of return to
work. Before resuming work, the District may require the unit member to provide
written verification of ability to carry on the particular duties of the assignment.

7.6.1    Allowable leave shall be for 60 days when the schools of the District are required

7.6.2    Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

7.14 Catastrophic Illness Benefit

On a case-by-case basis and with mutual agreement of the Association and the District,
any unit member may donate accumulated and unused eligible leave credits to another
unit member when that unit member or a member of his or her family suffers from a
catastrophic illness or injury.

7.14.10 Definitions

7.14.10.1 “Catastrophic illness” or “injury” means an illness or injury that is
expected to incapacitate the unit member for an extended period of
time, or that incapacitates a member of the unit member’s family,

7-9

and that incapacity requires the unit member to take time off from
work for an extended period of time to care for that family
member, and taking extended time off from work creates a
financial hardship for the unit member because all of his or her
sick leave and other paid time off has been exhausted.

7.14.10.2    “Eligible leave credits” means sick leave accrued to the donating
bargaining unit member. One (1.0) full day of eligible leave credit
shall provide one day of full pay to the receiving party, whether
that receiving party is on sick leave differential or has exhausted
all leave.

7.14.10.3    “Family members” shall be as defined in Section 7.4.4 of this
Article for personal necessity leave.

7.14.11 Eligibility

Eligible leave credits may be donated to a unit member for a catastrophic
illness or injury if all of the following requirements are met:

7.14.11.1    The unit member who is, or whose family member is, suffering
from a catastrophic illness or injury requests that eligible leave
credits be donated and provides verification of catastrophic injury
or illness as required by the District.

7.14.11.2    The District determines that the unit member is unable to work due
to the unit member’s, or his or her family member's catastrophic
illness or injury.

7.14.11.3    The unit member requesting donation of sick leave has exhausted
all accrued paid leave credits, excluding any differential leave.

7.14.12 Procedure

7.14.12.1    A unit member who wishes to receive the catastrophic illness
benefit must request in writing to either the Association or the
District that sick leave donations be solicited on his or her behalf.
The request must be accompanied by a verification of the
catastrophic injury or illness.

7.14.12.2    Donations will be solicited by a joint announcement of the
Association and District on behalf of a specifically-named
individual who meets the requirements for this benefit.

7.14.12.3    The minimum donation shall be one day leave credit. However,
the sick leave may be donated in one-half (0.5) day increments for

ARTICLE 12: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

12.1    Purpose

The purpose of this grievance procedure is to process a claim of grievance and to secure,
at the administrative level closest to the grievant, solutions to problems that may from
time to time arise under this Agreement.

12.2    Definitions

12.2.1    A “grievance” is defined as an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or
misapplication of a specific article or section of the Agreement.

12.2.2    A “working day” is any day on which the District Office is open to the public for
business.

12.2.3    A “grievant” is a person or class of persons, covered by the terms of this
agreement, asserting a grievance.

12.2.4    A “representative” is any person selected by the grievant or the employer to
assist in representation at any stage of the grievance process.

12.3 Timelines

12.3.1    To insure the prompt resolution of grievances, specific time limits have been
established; however, they may be extended as necessary with the written
consent of both parties.

12.3.1    If at any level or step the grievance is not resolved to the satisfaction of the
grievant, it may be appealed to the next step in accordance with the established
procedure.

12.3.2    If a grievance is not processed by the supervisor at any step in accordance with
the time limits of this article, the grievant may appeal to the next step.

12.3.3    If a grievance is not processed by the grievant at any step in accordance with the
time limits of this article, it shall be deemed withdrawn.

12.4 General Provisions

12.4.1    Should the processing of any grievance require that a unit member be released
from the unit member’s regular assignment, the unit member shall be released
without loss of pay or benefits.

12.4.2    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by or against any participant in the
grievance procedure by reason of such participation.

12.4.3    All parties to the grievance procedure shall make available to other parties
involved all pertinent information not privileged under the law in its possession

12-1

or control which is relevant to the issues raised by the grievance. Both parties
agree that grievance proceedings will be kept confidential as may be appropriate
at any level.

12.4.4    Any grievance which arose prior to the effective date of this Agreement shall not
be processed.

12.4.5    Until final disposition of a grievance takes place, the grievant is required to
conform to the original direction of the grievant’s administrator.

12.4.6    The processing of all grievances and other necessary documents shall be filed
separately from the personnel files of the participants and shall be accessible
only to the parties of interest and the aggrieved person(s).

mutually agreed to by the Association and the Superintendent or designee. Such
forms shall be duplicated by the Superintendent or designee and made available

Association shall attempt to agree upon a mutually acceptable
arbitrator and to obtain a commitment from the arbitrator to serve. If
the parties are unable to agree upon an arbitrator or to obtain a
commitment from the arbitrator mutually agreed upon, a request for a
list of arbitrators may be made to the State Mediation and Conciliation
Service by either party. A certified court reporter shall be employed to
record verbatim the entire arbitration hearing. The cost of the
arbitrator and court reporter shall be borne equally by the parties.

The function of the arbitrator shall be:

•    to hold a hearing concerning the grievance; and

•    to render an advisory award in writing within 30 days after the
close of the hearing, which award shall set forth findings of fact,
reasoning, and conclusions on the issues submitted. If oral
hearings before the arbitrator have been waived by the parties, the
arbitrator shall render his advisory award 30 days following receipt
of the written statements and proof of the parties.

Once the arbitrator has been selected, hearings shall commence and be
held at the convenience of the arbitrator. Hearings shall be confined to
working days unless otherwise agreed to by the parties in writing.

Neither the District nor the grievant shall be permitted to assert
evidence before the arbitrator that was not previously disclosed to the
other party. The arbitrator shall consider only those issues that have
been properly carried through prior steps as required by the provisions
of this grievance procedure. The arbitrability of an issue shall be
determined prior to a hearing on the merits of the case.

The arbitrator shall not render any award that conflicts with or alters
this Agreement. It is understood, however, that the arbitrator shall
interpret the Agreement in accordance with acceptable rules of
contract construction and the laws of the State of California and the
United States of America.

The arbitrator is empowered to include in any award such financial
reimbursements or other remedies as judged to be proper.

Each party shall bear the full costs for its representation in the
arbitration.

A copy of the arbitrator’s award shall be submitted to the
Superintendent or designee and the Association. The District shall
then implement the award, or, within 10 working days of receipt of the
award, submit the award to the Governing Board for appropriate
disposition.

History of the California Standards for the Teaching Profession

The Belmont-Redwood Shores District Teacher Evaluation is based on the California Standards
for the Teaching Profession (CSTP). The six major teaching standards and their supporting
standards were adopted by the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing, the State
Superintendent, and the California Department of Education in 1997. These standards are
included in the district evaluation process as a way of helping teachers reflect on their teaching,
assessing their strengths and areas of growth, and to help move their teaching practice forward.

The Evaluation Process

Tenured Teachers

•    Tenured teachers will be involved in the evaluation process every other year, and will
participate in two formal observations.

•    By October 15, there will be a conference between the evaluator and the teacher in which
the evaluation process is explained and goals are set.

•    By January 20, there will have been one classroom observation by the evaluator,
followed by a conference.

•    Prior to March 1, a second evaluation and conference will be completed.

•    Thirty calendar days before the end of the school year, the teacher will receive a
Summative Report.

Probationary and Temporary Teachers

•    Probationary and Temporary Teachers will be involved in the evaluation process every
year and will participate in three formal evaluations.

•    By October 15, there will be a conference between the evaluator and the teacher in which
the evaluation process is explained and goals are set. (Teachers in the BTSA program
may extend to November 1).

•    By January 20, there will have been two classroom observations by the evaluator,
followed by a conference.

•    Prior to March 1, a third observation and conference will be completed. In addition, the
teacher will receive a Summative Report.
