ARTICLE 1:

AGREEMENT

The articles and provisions in this agreement constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement (“Agreement”) by and between the Governing Board or Board of Trustees -
referred to as “Board” of the Oakley Union School District (“District”) and the Oakley
Union Teachers Association, CTA/NEA, an employee organization.

ARTICLE 2:_DURATION OF AGREEMENT

This Agreement shall become effective as of the date of its execution and shall
continue in full force until Midnight, June 30, 2014. This Agreement shall not be
amended orally and it is expressly understood that it shall expire on the date indicated
and no portion of the Agreement shall continue in effect after that date.

ARTICLE 3:_RECOGNITION

The District recognizes the Association as the representative designated by teachers
for purposes of collective bargaining. As designated representative, the Association
shall represent certificated personnel engaged in full or part-time teaching under
contract with the District, including the following: General Education Classroom
Teachers, Special Day Class Teachers, Teachers on Special Assignment and
Instructional Support Providers.

ARTICLE 4:    DEFINITIONS

1.    The term "teacher" refers to any professional employee who is in the bargaining unit
as defined above and who is covered by this Agreement.

2.    A “pilot” is a temporary program concluding on a specific date. Both parties will
meet at least 30 days prior to termination date to decide whether to extend or revise
the pilot. Upon mutual agreement, the pilot will become contractual.

3.    The “end of the instructional day” is defined as the latest dismissal time at a
school, excluding Kindergarten.

4. “Prep” is time provided during the instructional day when teachers are not
responsible for student instruction or supervision.

ARTICLE 5:

NEGOTIATIONS PROCEDURES

1.    In the Spring of the calendar year in which this Agreement expires, the Association
shall present in writing its initial proposals. Proposals shall be presented to the
District at an open public meeting. The District will respond within 30 calendar days.

2.    Negotiations shall be mutually scheduled.

3.    The District agrees to release members of the Association from their scheduled
duties for a maximum of one-hundred-eighty (180) hours to serve on the
Association's bargaining team without loss of pay or benefits. It shall be understood
negotiations are normally scheduled for a full work day, at minimum, and substitutes
shall be compensated entirely at District expense. The District and Association may
employ, at their sole discretion, consultants to assist in the negotiations process. It
is expressly understood for negotiations scheduled during the regular work day that
neither negotiating team shall exceed six (6) individuals including any consultants,
and shall at all times include at least four (4) unit members. Any of the above
restrictions may be waived by mutual consent.

4.    District agrees to provide the Association with the placement of all unit members on
the negotiated salary schedule when requested.

ARTICLE 6:    ACADEMIC    FREEDOM

1.    All instruction shall be fair, accurate, objective, and appropriate to the age and
maturity of the student(s) and sensitive to community needs. Academic freedom is
essential and the District acknowledges the fundamental need to protect unit
members from any censorship, which might interfere with the performance of their
teaching functions.

2.    A unit member shall have reasonable freedom in classroom presentations and
discussions and may introduce appropriate political, religious or otherwise
controversial material relevant to course content.

3.    In performing teaching functions, unit members shall have reasonable freedom to
express their opinions in an objective manner. In accordance with current Board
policy, unit members shall not utilize their positions to indoctrinate students with their
own personal, political, and/or religious views.

ARTICLE 7:

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

1.    The Association shall have the right to post notices of activities and materials of
organizational concern on Association bulletin boards, one of which shall be
provided in each school in an area frequented by teachers. The Association may
use District mail services and employee mailboxes for Association communications.

2.    The Association shall have the sole and exclusive right to have membership dues,
initiation and service fees deducted for members of the bargaining unit. The District
shall, upon appropriate written authorization from any employee in the unit, deduct
and make appropriate remittance to CTA for dues and service fees.

3.    Members of the bargaining unit who are not members of CTA on the effective date
of this Agreement and employees who become members of the bargaining unit
shall, either within thirty (30) days of the date of this Agreement or their employment,
apply for membership in and execute the appropriate written authorization for dues
or service fee deduction as a condition of their employment.

4.    In the event any member of the bargaining unit revokes an authorization for
deduction of dues or service fees, the District shall notify the Association within five
(5) workdays of receipt of that revocation. Without the reinstatement of the
authorization, the District shall terminate the employee ten (10) workdays from the
date of notification to the Association.

5.    If any member of the bargaining unit belongs to a recognized religious sect, which
forbids its members to belong to or pay a representational fee to any employee
organization, that employee shall be exempted from this provision upon the
presentation to the District of a notarized statement declaring such fact. An amount
equal to the representational fee shall be donated to a recognized charity.

6.    The Association agrees to indemnify and hold the District harmless from any and all
claims, demands or suits or any other action arising from the provisions of Sections
2, 3, 4 and 5 above.

7.    If an authorized representative of the Association desires to transact business on
school premises, he shall first report to the school office. The transaction of
Association business shall not take place during the teacher work day and shall be

limited to the duty-free lunch period or to that time prior to or after the teacher work
day.

ARTICLE 8:    GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A grievance is a formal written allegation by a unit member or the unit who claims to
have been adversely affected by an alleged violation of the specific provisions of this
Agreement. Any member of the bargaining unit shall have the right to present a
grievance as specified below. The intent of these procedures shall be to settle the
matter being grieved at the lowest possible level. The steps of the procedure shall be:
INFORMAL DISCUSSION:

Principal/Supervisor: A unit member who believes he has a grievance shall discuss it
first with his Principal or supervisor within five (5) school days of the event in an attempt
to resolve the matter informally.

Level 1: Principal/Supervisor

1.    If, as a result of this informal discussion, the matter is not resolved, the grievant shall
set forth his grievance in writing within five (5) school days of the date of the informal
discussion and present it to the Principal or supervisor specifying:

a.    The specific nature of the grievance and the contract line, clause or section
alleged to have been violated;

b.    The results of previous discussions relative to the allegation(s);

c.    The date and time of presentation to the Principal or supervisor;

d.    The relief sought in the matter.

2.    The Principal or supervisor shall communicate his decision to the employee in writ¬
ing within five (5) school days after receiving the written grievance. The decision
shall include:

a.    A summary of previous discussions held on the matter;

b.    The rationale behind the decision.

Level 2: Superintendent

If the grievance remains unresolved, the grievant may, no later than five (5) school
days after receipt of the Principal or supervisor’s decision, request a review by the
Superintendent. All previous documents shall be submitted to the Superintendent with
a request for review and he shall render his decision no later than ten (10) school days

after receipt of the appeal. The Superintendent may indicate a designee to review the

Principal or supervisor’s decision.

Level 3: Board

If the grievance is still unresolved, the grievant may, no later than five (5) school

days following receipt of the Superintendent’s review of the Principal or supervisor’s

decision, appeal to the Board.

1.    The employee shall submit all previous documents to Board with his appeal.

2.    The Board shall hear the matter at a regular meeting in Closed Session, unless the
grievant desires otherwise, within twenty (20) school days of receipt of the request.

3.    The employee shall be entitled to representation at the hearing and shall have the
right to make a presentation to the Board on his allegations.

4.    The Board shall inform the employee, in writing, regarding its decision in the matter
within five (5) school days of the hearing.

Level 4: Arbitration

1. In the event the matter remains unresolved, the grievant may, no later than fifteen
(15) calendar days after receipt of the Board’s decision, appeal to the arbitration
procedure described below. Such procedures shall be subject to the following
limitations:

a.    Except for memoranda of understanding signed and agreed upon by the parties,
this Agreement sets out expressly all the restrictions and obligations assumed by
the parties and no implied restrictions or obligations are inherent in this
Agreement or were assumed by the parties in entering into this Agreement.

b.    In considering whether a matter is subject to arbitration, a fundamental principle
shall be that the Board retains all its rights to manage the school system, subject
only to the express limitations set forth in this Agreement.

c.    No matter will be considered arbitrable unless the issue constitutes a grievance
under the definition of a grievance set forth above. No court or arbitrator shall or
may proceed under any presumption that a request to arbitrate is arbitrable
except under such conditions.

d.    It is agreed between the parties that no court or arbitrator shall direct arbitration
on any other issue between the parties to the Agreement and such arbitration

shall be expressly limited to procedural matters contained in this Agreement. It is
further agreed the award of the arbitrator shall be restricted to the determination
of whether or not there has been a violation of a specific procedure in one of the
sections of this Agreement, and the steps, if any, to be taken by the Board to
provide relief to the grievant.

e.    The arbitrator shall limit himself to the issues submitted and shall have no
authority in any case to add to, subtract from, or alter any provision of this
Agreement.

f.    The arbitrator's award shall be binding upon the parties.

2. An appeal to arbitration shall state in reasonable detail the nature of the dispute and
the specific remedy sought by the grievant. It shall also include copies of all
previous documents related to the dispute. Within ten (10) calendar days of receipt
of the appeal to arbitration, the parties to this Agreement shall meet at a mutually
agreeable time and endeavor to mutually agree upon an arbitrator to hear the case.
If the parties fail to agree upon an arbitrator, they shall jointly request the California
State Conciliation Service to furnish a panel of five (5) capable arbitrators. Each
party shall have the right to reject one (1) panelist. The Association and the Board
shall alternately strike names from the agreed panel, with the Association striking the
first name in the odd numbered cases, and the remaining individual shall be the
arbitrator. The fees and expenses of the arbitrator shall be borne equally by the
Board and the Association. It is understood the Association shall be the sole and
exclusive employee organization designated to represent grievants in the bargaining
unit during the term of this Agreement. In the event a grievant is represented by
himself or by an attorney, the Association shall be notified upon receipt of the
request for review at the Superintendent’s level and shall be permitted to be present
at all meetings. Copies of all correspondence shall also thereafter be forwarded to
the Association. It is understood:

a. Failure to file or appeal grievances within the specified time limits shall constitute
a waiver and settlement of the grievance.

b.    Failure of the Board’s representative to answer within the time limits provided at
any step of this procedure will automatically advance the grievance to the next
step of this procedure.

c.    Nothing contained in this Agreement shall prevent either the Board or the
Association from agreeing to submit a grievance directly to arbitration.

d.    Neither the Board nor the Association shall subject any grievant, or employees
appearing on behalf of or in opposition to such a grievant, to reprisals for
participation in the grievance procedure.

e.    Grievance papers shall not be filed in employee’s file.

f.    Assignments and applicable rules and regulations of the Board shall remain in
effect through the grievance process.

g.    No member of the bargaining unit shall be penalized in salary or fringe benefits
for participation in grievance or arbitration hearings held during such member’s
work day.

ARTICLE 9:    DEDUCTIONS

Members shall authorize the District to make necessary deductions for membership

dues. Plans and programs may include, but are not limited to, annuities, IRS Section

125 programs, credit unions, savings bonds, scholarships, and charitable donations.

ARTICLE 10:    WORKDAY

Section 1: WorkWeek

1.    The average five day workweek shall consist of 37.5 hours.

2.    Unit members shall be entitled to a duty-free lunch period of at least 35 minutes.

3.    Unit members must arrive at their school site a minimum of 30 minutes prior to the

start of the instructional day.

Section 2: Instructional Responsibilities

1. The following minimum instructional minutes shall be offered:

•    40,500 minutes per year in Kindergarten

•    50,400 minutes in Grades 1-3

•    54,000 minutes in Grades 4-5

•    57,600 minutes in Grade 6

•    59,400 minutes in Grades 7-8.

7

2. Unit members shall not be required to attend more than three (3) activities such as
an open house, back to school night and evening conferences. The site
administrator shall provide for staff input prior to a final determination of the
activities. Date and time of the activities shall be established, when possible, prior to
the end of the school year for the following year.

Section 3: Preparation Time

1.    The District shall provide one (1) daily preparation period out of the seven period day
for each full-time sixth, seventh and eighth grade position while assigned to the
middle school. On a non-minimum day, the minimum block for this will be forty-five
(45) minutes.

2.    Unit members may volunteer to substitute during their preparation period, but no
teacher shall be required to substitute during their preparation period, except in
cases of emergency. If substitutes are not available, the Principal or
supervisor/designee may request teachers not in class to substitute. Teachers
utilized to substitute for others shall be compensated per Appendix B Supplemental
Pay.

Section 4: Other Responsibilities

1.    Unit members shall perform assigned duties.

2.    The District may convene staff meetings and site committee meetings as needed
which should conclude within 75 minutes of the end of the school’s instructional day
or the end of the CLT on modified Wednesdays at K-5 schools. At least two (2)
working days notice shall be given for these meetings, five (5) work days whenever
possible.

3.    For the middle school ONLY, if adequate volunteer supervision for extracurricular
activities cannot be arranged five (5) days prior to the scheduled events, the
Principal or supervisor, after seeking volunteers, may assign employees to supervise
with at least two (2) working days notice. No employee shall be assigned more than
twice in a school year.

4.    Unit members shall attend pre-scheduled IEP, SST and committee meetings as well
as make necessary parent contacts, including conferences. These “meetings” shall

be scheduled with at least two (2) working days notice, five (5) work days whenever
possible.

ARTICLE 11:    LEAVES

Section 1: Sick Leave

1. The District shall provide for compensated leave of absence from duty for any
unit member who is absent from duty because of accident, illness or quarantine,
as follows:

a.    Unit members who are employed on a full-time basis are entitled to ten (10) days
of sick leave each school year, seven of which can be personal necessity,
commencing on the first day of employment.

b.    Unit members who are employed less than a full year shall be entitled to a pro¬
rata share of ten (10) days per year.

c.    Unused sick leave shall accrue from year to year.

2.    The District reserves the right to specify the manner of verifying the legitimacy of
leave taken and the discipline to be enacted for misuse of sick leave. The District
may require a physician's verification of illness if misuse is suspected, when a
teacher has used sick leave for five (5) or more consecutive days, or when the
pattern of absences indicates possible abuse. Employees may receive a warning for
the first misuse or abuse dependent upon the severity.

3.    The District and OUTA wish to comply with Education Code §44977 and Education
Code §44978.1. In summary these sections provide the following:

a.    Unit members who are absent because of illness or accident beyond their
accumulated sick leave entitlements will receive their daily rates of pay less
differential pay for up to five (5) school months per incident. Differential pay is
currently $145/day and will be adjusted annually. Please call the Human Resources
office for the differential pay amount as the District may annually adjust this amount.

b.    Sick leave, including accumulated sick leave, and the five-month period shall run
consecutively.

c.    An employee shall not be provided more than one five-month period per illness
or accident. However, if a school year terminates before the five-month period is

exhausted, the employee may take the balance of the five-month period in a
subsequent school year.

d.    When a certificated employee has exhausted all available sick leave, including
accumulated sick leave, and continues to be absent due to illness or accident for a
period beyond the five-month period, and the employee is not medically able to
resume the duties of his or her position, the employee shall be placed on a
reemployment list for a period of 24 months if the employee is on probationary
status, or for a period of 39 months if the employee is on permanent status. The 24-
month or 39-month period shall commence at the expiration of the five-month period.

e.    When the employee is medically able, during the 24- or 39-month period, the
certificated employee shall be returned to the next available vacancy for which he or
she is credentialed and qualified.

4. Whatever the claim of disability, no day of absence shall be considered a sick leave
day on which unit members or the Association have engaged in a concerted work
stoppage, unless the unit member was legitimately absent and provides verification
as the District may require.

Section 2: Bereavement Leave

1. Bereavement leave shall be afforded to employees up to five (5) days for the death
of any member of the employee's immediate family.

Section 3: Personal Necessity Leave

Sick leave may be used as personal necessity leave for the following:

1.    Death or serious illness to a unit member's immediate family.

2.    Accident involving the unit member's person or property or the personal property of a
member of the teacher’s immediate family.

3.    Required court appearance. The site administrator must be given at least two (2)
days prior notice.

4.    Discretionary Leave

Discretionary leave days shall not be used as part of a concerted work stoppage.
The administrator may not grant approval of these days if the leave will extend a
school holiday or break. The site administrator must be given at least two (2) days
prior notice, unless the reason is for illness of a family member. These days do not

in

accumulate beyond the fiscal year. These days shall be deducted from personal
necessity days.

a.    Two (2) days may be taken at the discretion of the employee.

b.    Three (3) additional days may be taken at the discretion of the employee
for family/personal obligations (i.e. illness of a child, legal business) which do not
otherwise meet Personal Necessity criteria. The current established daily substitute
rate as stated in Appendix B shall be deducted.

5.    A unit member may take five (5) days of leave upon the birth or adoption of a child.
The site administrator must be given at least two (2) days prior notice.

6.    The District reserves the right to specify the manner of verifying personal necessity
exists in each case in which such leave is requested.

Section 4: Maternity Leave

A unit member may take sick leave for maternity reasons, subject to the following

conditions [Note: For further information regarding maternity leave, contact your site

administrator or the District personnel office]:

1.    Maternity leave shall begin on the date indicated in a written statement signed by a
duly licensed physician or other competent practitioner that the unit member is no
longer able to perform her duties.

2.    The unit member may be required to submit medical certification of her continued
fitness to perform all assigned duties during her pregnancy.

3.    Maternity leave shall end on the date indicated in a written statement signed by a
duly licensed physician or other competent practitioner that the unit member is
physically able to return to work.

Section 5: Jury Duty

1.    Unit members shall request leave no less than two (2) working days after receiving
notice that they have been called to serve as a juror. This leave shall be granted
with pay. Unit members shall turn over to the District the portion of the stipend
received for such duty, which is not for mileage expenses or transportation.

2.    A teacher who is called for jury duty on one of his/her work days, but through action
on his/her part is able to defer jury service to a non work day shall receive $95 per
day for his/her jury duty service to a maximum of five (5) days. Appropriate

verification of deferral and service must be provided prior to payment. He/She may
also keep the stipend for this same period of time.

Section 6: Leave Without Pay

1.    Unit members may apply for leave without pay. The District will consider such
requests individually. Requests for unpaid leave must be submitted by May 1 of the
fiscal year prior to the year in which the leave is to be taken and shall be limited to a
maximum of two (2) years. The District shall set the duration, be it one (1) or two (2)
years, at its sole discretion. Personal leaves will not be granted to accept
employment in another school or district or will be cause for termination of the
current leave. Unit members may also apply as soon as possible for unpaid leave in
connection with maternity or emergencies for less than one (1) year. The District
shall act upon such requests at its sole discretion. The following conditions shall
apply to unpaid leaves of absence:

a.    Unit members who are granted unpaid leave, or whose position is otherwise
vacated, shall retain no rights to the position vacated. The District shall assign
returning individuals to positions which serve the best interests of the District's
educational program.

b.    Unit members who are granted unpaid leave may continue to participate in the
health and welfare benefits at their sole expense.

c.    Unit members who are on an unpaid leave at the end of the school year must
notify the district of their intentions for the following year by the date established
on the “Teacher Assignment Preference” form.

2.    Unit members may take unpaid leave for personal business, subject to the following
stipulations:

a.    Personal business leave shall not exceed ten (10) days in any one (1) school
year.

b.    Circumstances permitting, notification of intent to take personal business leave
shall be submitted to the immediate supervisor a minimum of twenty-four (24)
hours in advance.

3.    Unit members shall have a minimum of one-half (1/2) day’s salary or sick leave
deducted for all leaves taken during the instructional day.

19

4.    All unauthorized leaves shall be deemed a breach of contract and shall result in
salary deduction.

5.    The Association shall be allowed twelve (12) days of leave for the purpose of
conducting Association business. The Association shall request this leave at least
five (5) days in advance. Additional days may be granted but shall require mutual
agreement by the Association and the Superintendent or his/her designee. The
Superintendent’s or his/her designee’s decision to deny a request shall not be
subject to the grievance procedure. The Association shall reimburse the District for
the actual cost of the substitute teacher.

ARTICLE 12:    TEACHING    CONDITIONS

1.    The District shall keep the schools reasonably supplied, equipped and maintained.

2.    The District will attempt to maintain reasonable class size.

3.    If significant imbalances in classes arise after the opening of school, the Principal or
supervisor and the affected teachers will meet to discuss possible solutions.

4.    Following the first two weeks of the school year, teachers may bring problems with
regard to classes to the attention of the Principal or supervisor by initiating an
informal discussion, the purpose of which shall be to explore the problems and the
alternatives available for its possible solution. The final decision in the matter shall
be the sole prerogative of the Principal or supervisor.

5.    Teachers shall also have the right to initiate consideration of student placement
changes subsequent to the first two weeks of school. The final decision shall rest
with the Principal.

6.    Unit members shall be expected to dress in a professional manner.

7.    As a unique medical need of a child may arise appropriate site and the district
administrators will confer with the child’s teacher(s) to discuss and attempt to
address the individual needs of the child wellbeing. Other than in a life threatening
situation a teacher’s consent shall be obtained prior to the administration of
medication.

ARTICLE 13:

ASSIGNMENT

1.    An assignment is the grade level and/or subject matter taught by the teacher. A
reassignment is a change in the grade level and/or subject matter being taught by
the teacher. A transfer is a move from one site to another.

2.    Current unit members and those returning from unpaid leave may request a
voluntary reassignment or transfer annually by submitting a request to the District, in
writing, using the Teaching Assignment Preferences form distributed in the Spring of
the current teaching year.

3.    The District shall make assignments for the ensuing school year as soon as
administratively possible and through a cooperative effort of teachers and
administrators. If consensus is not attained, the site administrator’s decision will
prevail.

4.    In assigning teachers, the District shall consider the educational needs of students
as well as teacher preference, qualifications, certifications, experience, training and
length of service in the District. Current unit members that volunteer for an opening
shall be considered before outside applicants unless an opening occurs during the
instructional year.

5.    In the event of an involuntary transfer, the teacher may solicit the reason(s) in writing
from his/her site administrator. If the teacher is still concerned about the involuntary
transfer he/she will be encouraged to meet with the Superintendent or his/her
designee for further information and planning.

6.    A unit member who is involuntarily reassigned or transferred after the first day of
school shall be given as much notice as possible and shall be give three (3) days
release time to prepare for the new assignment. This paragraph shall not apply to
the opening of a new school.

7.    After the first day of school a unit member who is involuntarily reassigned,
transferred or is moved as part of the opening of a new school will be provided
assistance in moving of the teacher’s materials, if necessary.

8.    Shifting of students to create combination classes when increases or shortfalls in
enrollment occur shall not be considered a change in assignment.

9. Written notification of openings after preliminary assignments are made shall be
provided to OUTA and to designated site representatives.

ARTICLE 14:    EVALUATIONS

1.    Purpose: The evaluation process shall be a positive and constructive tool for the
purpose of continuing to improve teacher strengths and helping certificated
employees become more effective in their professional roles.

2.    Process: By October 15, the employee shall know, prior to the pre-conference, the
system, method, criteria, and evaluator to be used in determining the employee’s
performance.

b.    The District and OUTA shall mutually agree to all forms used in the observation
and evaluation process.

c.    A pre-conference will be held.

d.    Observations shall be at least 15 minutes in duration and shall be completed by
March 15.

e.    Permanent employees shall be evaluated a minimum of every two (2) years.
However pursuant to Education Code §44664(a)(3), eligible employees may be
evaluated every 5 years. The provisions of the law are as follows:

1.    Must have permanent status

2.    Employed in the district for at least 10 years

3.    Deemed “high qualified”, per NCLB

4.    Previous evaluations marked as meeting or exceeding standards

5.    Mutual agreement between the employee and the evaluator

6.    The employee or the evaluator may withdraw consent at anytime

7.    The decision to participate or not participate in this opportunity shall not be
subject to the grievance process

f.    Non-permanent employees will have at least two (2) observations.

g.    Permanent employees shall be evaluated a minimum of every two (2) years.

h.    A written report shall be provided within five (5) working days of the observation.

i.    A post-conference will be held for all non-permanent employees and others by
request.

j. The evaluator may make informal, unannounced observations of the employee’s
performance. If the evaluator observes anything that he/she believes needs
improvement or remediation or deserves commendation, he/she shall so notify
the employee in writing.

3.    Other Provisions

a.    The employee shall have the right to respond in writing to the appraisal and
written recommendations of the evaluation. The employee may include variables
and/or constraints which may have affected their performance.

b.    If a dispute is resolved in favor of the evaluatee, the evaluation shall reflect the
resolution of the dispute.

c.    Upon request, the administrator will conduct one formal observation in the
subject area of the employee’s choice.

d.    All evaluations shall be based upon documented/substantiated information.

e.    The employee shall have the right to see documentation/substantiation prior to
inclusion in the evaluation.

f.    The evaluation shall be subject only to the appeals procedure and shall not be
subject to the Grievance Procedure.

4.    Improvement Plan:

If deficiencies are noted, a plan will be implemented which shall include specific
recommendations for improvement. A plan may include direct or indirect assistance,
attendance at conferences/workshops, and/or release time to observe other classes.
Upon request, the administrator will conduct or arrange for demonstration lessons.
A reasonable amount of time shall be provided for the correction of noted
deficiencies.

5.    Appeals Procedure:

In the event a certificated employee believes there is a violation of the District
evaluation policy or disagrees with the substance of an evaluation, he/she may
appeal. Prior to an appeal, the employee should attempt to resolve the problem in a
conference with the administrator.

a. The employee may, within five (5) working days of receipt of the evaluation,
submit a written request to the Superintendent for review. The Superintendent

shall immediately forward the request to his/her designee and shall inform the
employee as to who his/her designee is. This request will indicate the specific
problem, the decision rendered by the supervisor, and the remedy being sought.

b.    Within ten (10) working days, the Superintendent’s designee will meet with the
employee.

c.    Within ten (10) working days of the meeting, the Superintendent’s designee will
render a decision.

d.    If the employee is not satisfied with the decision, he may appeal the decision
within five (5) working days to the Superintendent.

e.    The Superintendent will review the problem and will render a decision within ten
(10) working days of receipt of the appeal. The Superintendent’s decision will be
final.

ARTICLE 15:    PEER    ASSISTANCE    &    REVIEW

The District and OUTA will cooperate in the design and implementation of the

Oakley Peer Assistance Review Program (OPARP).

1.    Purpose

The purpose of the OPARP is to improve the quality of instruction through expanded
and improved professional development and peer assistance. The program will
serve as a critical feedback mechanism to allow experienced teachers to assist
teachers in need of development in subject matter knowledge and/or teaching
strategies.

2.    Panel

A Peer Assistance Review Panel comprised of four teachers selected by OUTA, and
three administrators selected by the District, will be chaired by the Assistant
Superintendent of Curriculum and Instruction who will serve as a non-voting
member. Not withstanding the above, annually the District may modify the size of the
panel provided that the change is consistent with the requirements of Education
Code §44502 and does not become smaller than the original composition of four
teachers and three administrators.

a. The Panel will select Peer Counselors in accordance with Education Code 44501
and 44502, as needed, and will be responsible for adopting rules and procedures

17

to effect the provisions of this Article which will include defining "unsatisfactory
evaluation," determining compensation, setting meeting schedules, providing
training, etc.

b.    The Panel will work with Peer Counselors to develop remediation plans for
participating teachers including, at a minimum, written performance goals aligned
to the District’s student learning goals and a time frame for regular reporting.

c.    The Panel will further create a plan which will offer, but not be limited to,
observations of other teachers, additional staff development, demonstration
lessons, and other individual assistance from available resources.

d.    The Panel will periodically evaluate the program and make adjustments as
needed to improve service delivery.

e.    The Panel, Peer Counselors, and others involved in this process are reminded of
the sensitivity of information they will receive. This information is highly
confidential and must be shared carefully at all times.

3.    Site administrators will conduct additional evaluations and observations during the
review year and share these with the peer counselors. The program will foster and
strongly encourage a cooperative relationship between the Peer Counselor,
Participating Teacher, and Site Administration. Written records of plans, progress,
and evaluations or observations will be placed in the teacher’s personnel file.

4.    Participating teachers include teachers referred as a result of unsatisfactory
evaluations (Referred) and volunteer teachers seeking peer assistance and not
performance review (Volunteer).

5.    After a year of support a referred teacher who has received a negative evaluation
and has not shown improvement will be referred to the Assistant Superintendent of
Human Resources. Voluntary teacher participants will not be referred beyond the
panel.

6.    It is agreed expenditures received by the District:

a.    Will be used for the design and implementation of the OPARP;

b.    Five percent (5%) or less of the funds may be expended for administrative
services; and

c. Funds received may also be expended for support of non-permanent teachers or
interns, and professional development.

ARTICLE 16:    DUE    PROCESS FOR DISCIPLINE

1.    This Article provides due process for the disciplinary actions outlined below. It does
not affect other personnel actions which may be governed by the Education Code or
other procedures not specified. This Article shall be used at the sole discretion of
the District.

2.    Disciplinary action is limited in this Article to suspensions with or without pay. Any
suspension shall be limited to a maximum of ten (10) work days.

3.    The disciplinary action must be substantiated and based upon just cause. The
disciplinary action must also be according to the principles of progressive discipline.
However, the District shall have the right to skip any step of progressive discipline in
the event the unit member has committed a serious offense. In all cases, any
disciplinary action taken by the District shall be commensurate with the offense.

4.    Discussions with unit members regarding disciplinary action shall be conducted in an
appropriately private setting apart from individuals not directly involved in the issue.
Unit members shall have the right to be accompanied by an Association
representative at any investigative meeting where the results of the meeting may
lead to a suspension using this article meeting and where disciplinary action is
discussed.

5.    Any proposed suspension of a bargaining unit member shall be preceded by written
notice of the charges and the right to appeal to the Superintendent or designee
within five (5) days of receiving that notice. Included in the charges shall be the
specific facts underlying those charges.

6.    If the unit member appeals the suspension, a conference shall be scheduled within
10 working days to discuss the matter with the Superintendent or designee. The
Superintendent shall then determine the action to be taken by the District and shall
notify the unit member in writing of such action.

7.    If the employee is not satisfied with the action taken by the Superintendent, he may
appeal to the Board of Trustees by submitting a written notice of appeal within five
(5) working days of receipt of the Superintendent’s report. The Board of Trustees

will review the Superintendent’s findings, and the unit member’s appeal and shall
issue a written decision within (30) working days. The Board may elect to meet with
the employee and the District staff. The Board’s decision may be appealed through
the grievance process outlined in Article 8. There shall be no loss of pay to a unit
member until after the completion of the appeal process if the action is challenged.

Members of the bargaining unit may request to retire early subject to the terms and
conditions of this Article. It is understood that the option to retire early shall be the sole

service under the provisions of the State Teachers Retirement System, of which ten

receive health benefits for five (5) years or until his/her 65th birthday which ever
occurs first.

3.    An employee who has completed a minimum of twenty (20) years of active full-time
service in the District, shall receive health benefits for ten (10) years or until his/her
65th birthday which ever occurs first.

4.    The employee must draw from the State Teachers Retirement System during the
period of participation.

5.    The full cost (up to the current established district contribution - $637) of Board
selected medical insurance for participating employees shall be paid by the District
for the period of benefits provided for under this article.

ARTICLE 22:    PART-TIME    EMPLOYMENT

Part-time employment is defined as a single employee working less than a full day.
Two (2) persons sharing one full-time job is not part-time employment.

Bargaining unit members may request to work part-time for a given school year,
subject to the terms and conditions below. Annually, the District shall review the needs
of the District in relation to such requests as have been received for part-time
employment and shall grant such requests for part-time employment as are deemed to
be in the best interest of the District. The granting of such requests shall be the sole
prerogative of the Board, subject to the following provisions:

1.    After initial approval has been granted for participation, the employee may not
request revocation of part-time status during the year.

2.    In order to be eligible to participate, the employee shall be a permanent employee.

3.    Requests for participation shall be submitted to the District in the Spring of the year
preceding the year in which participation is to take place. Normally, such requests
shall be based upon half-time work on a daily basis or full-time work on a half-year
basis and shall list the specific assignment desired by the applicant.

4.    Once an assignment is set for a given year of participation, it shall not be modified
except by mutual consent.

5.    The employee shall contribute to the STRS on a pro-rata basis.

6.    The District shall make a contribution to the STRS for the employee on a pro-rata
basis.

7.    The employee shall be compensated on a pro-rata basis at the appropriate step and
column of the current salary schedule. Employees shall not move to the next step
on the salary schedule unless 75% of a full year has been worked.

8.    The employee shall receive a pro-rata share of the fringe benefits provided for in this
Agreement.
