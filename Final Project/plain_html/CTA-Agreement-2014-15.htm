SCHOOL’S AGREEMENT

CERTIFICATED PERSONNEL

THIS AGREEMENT, made and entered into this 18th day of December, 2007, between the
Northern Humboldt Union High School District, hereinafter referred to as the School or
District and the Northern Humboldt Union High School Teachers’ Association (CTA/NEA),

hereinafter referred to as the Association shall be as follows:

ARTICLE I
Purpose

1.1    The purpose of this Agreement is to comply with the Education Employment Relations
Act (EERA), Chapter 10.7 of the California Government Code, as it requires and sets
forth the requirements for meeting and negotiating in California schools.

1.2    This Agreement is the entire, final, complete, full, and fair agreement between the parties
and supersedes all prior agreements, written or oral, and any representations heretofore
made hereon by the parties or their representatives and concludes the meet and negotiate
process on any and all items, whether included in this Agreement or not, for the term of
this Agreement.

1.3    The parties to the Agreement recognize the non-assignable adjunct duties as specified in
Appendix C or non-assignable extra duties performed by Unit Members shall be
performed only by Unit Members unless there are no Unit Members who are qualified,
available, and interested in performing the work, in which case, the District, at its
discretion, may utilize non-bargaining Unit volunteers or pay and assign non-bargaining
Unit personnel.

ARTICLE 2

Association Recognition

2.1    The District recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all its
certificated employees employed in the following classifications:

A.    Classroom Teacher (including RST and Special Day Teachers),

B.    Certificated School Nurse,

C.    Counselor,

D.    Speech and Hearing Specialist,

E.    Librarian,

F.    Temporary Teacher,

G.    Teachers on leave or sabbatical,

as recognized by the District in its Resolution No. 70653 (1) dated at 5:00 p.m.,
on May 6, 1976, as was submitted to the EERB (now PERB) by Certified Mail on
May 10, 1976. The bargaining unit members are referred to as Unit Members.
Specifically excluded from recognition are school administrators and
management, confidential and supervisory personnel, day-to-day substitutes, adult
education staff, young mothers’ program, home study, persons who evaluate, and
summer school staff.

2.2    Effective the date of this Agreement (February 13, 2001), the school nurse position
within the District may become a classified position in the event that there are no
qualified certificated applicants for the position. If the hired employee receives a
credential, s/he will be placed on the certificated salary schedule. The current employee
in one of these positions was hired as a certificated employee and cannot have his/her
employment status (certificated) changed by the District.

ARTICLE 3
Term

3.1    The term of this Agreement shall be from July 1, 2014 through June 30, 2015.

3.2    Both the District and the Association shall sunshine their monetary re-openers
simultaneously at the April Board of Trustees meeting.

3.3    The District and the Association agree to ongoing non-monetary negotiations throughout
the contract year in an effort to be proactive with issues that arise. While no limit on the
number of non-monetary issues has been established, the intent is to keep these
negotiations to four or less per contract year. These non-monetary negotiations may also
be used to revise contradictory or outdated contract language.

ARTICLE 4

Management Rights

It is understood and agreed the District reserves and hereby retains all its powers,
authority, and control to the full extent permitted by law, except as specifically delegated
or limited by the expressed provisions of this Agreement. The District's authority to
direct, manage, and control is defined as matters involving hiring, directing the work
force, determining the utilization of facilities and equipment, determining District
policies, District goals or objectives, developing the District budget, modifying the
revenues, and determining the existence of an emergency, raising revenues, and
determining the educational curriculum. The exercise of such direction, management,
and control shall be consistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

Nothing stated in this Article shall be construed to mean that the Association has waived
its right to bargain issues or the impact or effect of issues within the scope of
representation under the EERA nor has the Association waived its right to consult with
the District.

ARTICLE 5
Unit Members’ Responsibilities

Unit Members will devote such time as may be necessary to prepare to teach their
assigned subjects effectively.

Each Unit Member shall be responsible to have adequate plans or instructions available
to substitute teachers to allow day-to-day substitute teachers to continue the educational
continuity for at least a two-day absence.

Once each school year each Unit Member shall prepare and file with the school principal
a classroom discipline plan and a copy of the class grading policy.

Teacher dress shall reflect their professional status in the community. In the event that
there is an issue regarding the dress of an employee, the site administrators will address
the concern on an informal basis.

Certificated faculty are expected to assist in promoting the school as a safe and positive
environment through such measures as being a presence out in the halls between classes,
as well as at lunch, before and after school, and during passing time between classes;
opening classroom doors while classes are changing; encouraging students to get to class
on time; being a visible presence on the campus during the school day as able; and
through addressing students on inappropriate behaviors as they are noticed. The intent of

this provision is to have all faculty share in developing and promoting a positive school \
culture and climate through campus supervision as a part of our professional
responsibility.

6.5 General Provisions

6.5.1    The procedural time limits may be extended only by a mutual
written agreement.

6.5.2    In the interest of prompt and orderly settlement of a grievance,
grievances not timely submitted or not pursued within the time
limits or pursuant to the procedures contained herein, shall be
dismissed.

6.5.3    Any grievance not timely or properly referred to the next higher
step shall be deemed settled on the basis of the decision rendered at
the preceding step.

6.5.4    As necessary, the Grievant and needed witnesses will be given
release time to attend the arbitration hearing under Step 5.

6.5.5    No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the District, its officers,
agents or employees against any Grievant, or any other person, as a
result of filing, not filing, participating in, or not participating in,
any grievance. No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the
Association, its officers, agents or employees against any Grievant,
or any other person, as a result of filing, not filing, or participating
in, or not participating in, any grievance.

6.5.6    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of
any person having a grievance to discuss the matter informally
with any appropriate member of the administration and to have the
grievance individually adjusted without intervention by the
Association or resort to this process.

procedure, except Step 5, by the Grievant alone or, at the
Grievant’s option, by the Association or its representative. The
Association shall have the right to be present and to state its views at all
stages of the grievance procedure. Representation of Step 5 will be by the
Association.

6.5.8    If    a grievance arises from action or inaction on the part of a

member of the administration at a level above the Principal or
immediate supervisor, the Grievant shall submit the grievance to
the Superintendent and the Association directly and the processing
of such grievance will be commenced at the fourth step.

6.5.9    The    Association may process a grievance through all steps of the

grievance procedure even though an individual Grievant does not
wish to appeal at Step 2 or higher.

6.5.10    All    documents,    communications    and records dealing with the

processing of a grievance will be filed in a separate grievance file
and will not be kept in the personnel file of any of the participants.

6.5.11    In    the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be

processed through all the steps in the Grievance Procedure by die
end of the school year and, if left unresolved until the beginning of
the following school year, could result in harm to a Grievant, the
time limits set forth herein may be reduced by mutual agreement
so that the procedure may be completed prior to the end of the
school year or as soon as practicable following the close of the
school year.

9.2    Evaluation Standards for Teaching Unit Members with Classroom Assignments

Each Unit Member with classroom assignments shall be evaluated and assessed in
accordance with and based upon the California Standards for the Teaching
Profession. Standards 9.2.1-9.2.5 per classroom observation(s) and 9.2.6 will be
included in summary evaluation.

9.2.1    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard One: Engaging and
Supporting all Students in Learning

A.    Connecting students' prior knowledge, life experience, and
interests with learning goals.

B.    Using a variety of instructional strategies and resources to respond
to students’ diverse needs.

C.    Facilitating learning experiences that promote autonomy,
interaction, and choice.

D.    Engaging students in problem solving, critical thinking, and other
activities that make subject matter meaningful.

E.    Promoting self-directed, reflective learning for all students.

9.2.2    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard Two: Creating and
Maintaining Effective Environments for Student Learning (Methods/Stralegies)

A.    Creating a physical environment that engages all students.

B.    Establishing a climate that promotes fairness and respect.

C.    Promoting social development and group responsibility.

D.    Establishing and maintaining standards for student behavior.

E.    Planning and implementing classroom procedures and routines
that support student learning.

F.    Using instructional time effectively.

-26-

9.2.3    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard Three: Understanding
and Organizing Subject for Student learning (Subject matter)

A.    Demonstrating knowledge of subject matter content and student

B.    Organizing curriculum to support student understanding of subject
matter.

C.    Interrelating ideas and information within and across subject
matter areas.

D.    Developing student understanding through instructional strategies
that are appropriate to the subject matter.

E.    Using materials, resources and technologies to make subject
matter accessible to students.

9.2.4    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard Four: Planning

A.    Drawing on and valuing students' backgrounds, interests, and

C.    Developing and sequencing instructional activities and materials
for student learning.

D.    Designing short-term and long-term plans to foster student
learning.

E.    Modifying instructional plans to adjust for student needs.

9.2.5    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard Five: Assessing
Student learning (Methods and Strategies)

A.    Establishing and communicating learning goals for all students

B.    Collecting and using multiple sources of information to assess
student learning.

C.    Involving and guiding all students in assessing their own learning.

D.    Using the results of assessments to guide instruction.

E.    Communicating with students, families, and other audiences about
student progress.

9.2.6    California Standards for the Teaching Profession Standard Six: Developing as a

development.

B.    Establishing professional goals and pursuing opportunities to grow
professionally.

C.    Develops and maintains open channels of communication

D.    Maintains an appropriate atmosphere of mutual respect
among pupils, teachers and staff and abides by the Code of
Ethics of the teaching profession.

E.    Adheres to District and school regulations.
