ARTICLE IV:

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS

The parties mutually recognize the rights of all employees covered hereby to join and
participate in the lawful activities of the Association or other employee organizations of their

REASSIGNMENT/TRANSFER

9.1    GENERAL

9.1.1    A reassignment is defined as a change of grade-level or department within a
school. A transfer is defined as a change of school, not including movement to
positions outside the bargaining unit.

9.1.2    Reassignments/Transfers shall be subject to the review and approval of the site
administrator with the vacancy. An employee desiring a reassignment/transfer
for the following school year shall notify his/her site administrator through the
Statement of Intent Process, initiated by the District in December of each year.
An employee may update his/her Statement of Intent through June 30.

9.1.3    Site administrators shall not block a transfer that would otherwise take place,
except for good and sufficient reason based upon the educational needs of
students and programmatic needs of the receiving school as determined by the
site administrator.

9.1.4    The District shall post a notice of vacancies on an open, accessible bulletin
board in the district office, district website, and through e-mail to all certificated
staff.

9.2    VOLUNTARY REASSIGNMENTS/TRANSFERS

9.2.1    A unit member may request a voluntary reassignment/transfer to take effect
during the school year outside of the Statement of Intent Process window. If
so, the request shall be sent to the Personnel Office. See Appendix G.

9.2.2    An employee who requests a reassignment through the Statement of Intent
process shall be given first consideration for any available openings at their
current school. Voluntary reassignments within the current school staff shall
be considered first before accepting transfers.

9.2.3    To be eligible for a voluntary reassignment/transfer, the employee must
have an acceptable record of satisfactory ratings on all five (5) components
of the summary evaluation during the previous school year, and must be a
permanent employee or be eligible for tenure status at the conclusion of the
current school year. The employee must also possess the credential(s) to
perform the service.

9.2.4    No employee shall be pressured to seek a voluntary reassignment/transfer.

9.2.5    If a voluntary reassignment/transfer is denied, the employee may request and
shall be granted a written statement regarding the specific reasons for denial.

9.2.7    No position shall be filled until after the closing date for application specified on

28.2    Certificated employees may be disciplined only for “just cause” resulting from violations of

progressed through “Step 1 - Verbal Warning or Conference Summary”, “Step 2 - Written
Warning”, and “Step 3 - Written Reprimand”, except for conduct which is of such a nature
that it injures or threatens to injure the safety of pupils, employees, or others, or causes
substantial disruption to the educational program, or as otherwise required or provided by
law.
