ARTICLE 1.    AGREEMENT

1.1    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
(“Agreement”) by and between the Governing Board of the Riverbank Unified School District
(“Employer”) and the Riverbank Teachers Association/CTA/NEA (“Association”), an
employee organization.

1.2    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code (“Act”).

1.3    This Agreement shall remain in effect from July 1, 2013, through June 30, 2016, or until a
successor agreement is reached.

1.4    For the 2015/2016 year, the entire agreement is open for review for the July 1, 2016 through
June 30, 2019 contract duration.

1.4.1 For the 2013/2014, 2014/2015 & 2015/2016 years, each side may reopen salary, health
& welfare benefits and two (2) articles each.

1.5    In the last year of this Agreement, the Association shall present its full written “sunshine”
proposal to the District no later than the last Board meeting in May. The District shall then
submit its full written “sunshine” proposal no later than June 30th. A public hearing shall be
held at the next regular meeting of the Board of Trustees following each sunshine process to
effectuate the purposes of Government Code Section 3547. Negotiations shall then begin as
mutually agreed.

ARTICLE 3.    MANAGEMENT    RIGHTS    AND DISTRICT POWERS

3.1    It is understood and agreed that the District retains its powers and authority to direct and
control to the full extent of the law. Included in, but not limited to, those duties and powers are
the rights to: Direct the work of its employees; determine the method, means and services to be
provided; establish the education philosophy and the goals and objectives after consultation
with certificated staff; insure the rights and educational opportunities of students; determine the
staffing patterns in accordance with this Agreement; determine the classification of positions;
maintain the efficiency of the District operation; determine the curriculum; build, move or
modify the facilities; develop a budget; develop and implement budget procedures; determine
the methods of raising revenue; and contract out work. In addition, the Board retains the right
to hire, assign, evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline employees according to the
Education Code; and, to take action on any matter in the event of an emergency.

3.2    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the
District, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof, and the
use of judgment and discretion in connection therewith, shall be limited only by the specific
and express terms of this Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific and express
terms are in conformance with the laws of the State of California.

ARTICLE 4.    GRIEVANCE    PROCEDURE

4.1    Representation Rights

4.1.1    A grievant may have an Association representative at any grievance level if he/she
desires.

4.2    Definitions

4.2.1    A “grievance” is any alleged violation of this written Agreement.

4.2.2    A “grievant” may be: any certificated non-management employee of the District, or the
Association covered by the terms of this Agreement.

4.2.3    A “day” is any day in which the central administrative office of the Riverbank Unified
School District is open for business.

4.2.4    The “immediate supervisor” is the lowest level administrator having immediate
jurisdiction over the grievance issue.

4.3    Informal Level - Level I

4.3.1    Before filing a formal grievance, the grievant shall attempt to resolve it by an informal
conference with his/her immediate supervisor.

4.4    Formal Level - Level II

4.4.1    Within thirty (30) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the
grievance, the grievant must present the grievance, in writing to his/her immediate
supervisor. Failure to file in a timely manner waives employee right to file under these
provisions.

4.4.1.1    This statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the
circumstance(s) involved, the decision rendered at the informal conference,
and the specific remedy sought.

4.4.1.2    The administrator shall communicate his/her decision to the employee in
writing within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance. If the administrator
does not respond within the time limit, the grievant may appeal to the next
level.

4.4.1.3    Within the above limits, either party may request a personal conference.

persons experienced in hearing grievances in public schools. Each party shall

4.4.4.3 The fees and expenses of the arbitrator shall be borne equally by the District

ARTICLE 5.    ORGANIZATIONAL    SECURITY

5.1 Any unit member who is a member of the Association, or who has applied for membership,
may sign and deliver to the District an assignment authorizing deduction of unified
membership dues, initiation fees and general assessments in the Association. Pursuant to such
authorization, the District shall deduct one-twelfth of such dues from the regular salary check
of the unit member each month for twelve (12) months. Deductions for unit members who sign
such authorization after commencement of the school year shall be appropriately pro-rated to
complete payments by the end of the school year.

5 .2    Any unit member who is not a member of the Association, or who does not make application

for membership within thirty (30) days from the date of commencement of assigned duties
within the bargaining unit, shall become a member of the Association or pay to the Association
a fee in an amount equal to membership dues, initiation fees and general assessments, payable
to the Association in one lump sum cash payment in the same manner as required for the
payment of membership dues. In the event that a unit member shall not pay such fee directly to
the Association, the Association shall so inform the District and the District shall immediately
begin automatic payroll deduction as provided in Education Code section 45061 and in the
same manner as set forth in this Article.

5.3    Any unit member who is a member of a religious body whose traditional tenets or teachings
include objections to joining or financially supporting employee organizations or who holds
strong moral convictions against joining or financially supporting employee organizations shall
not be required to join or financially support the Association as a condition of employment;
except that such unit member shall pay, in lieu of a service fee, sums equal to such service fee
to one of the following non-religious, non-labor organizations, charitable funds exempt for
taxation under Section 501(c) of Title 26 of the Internal Revenue Code, FACT, Heart
Association, American Cancer Society, a foundation program for educational purposes for
students in need from the Riverbank Unified School District when established, or to any
qualifying organization mutually agreed upon. Such payment shall be made on or before
October 31, of each school year.

5.3.1    Proof of payment and a written statement of objection along with verification of beliefs
pursuant to paragraph 5.3 above, shall be made on an annual basis to the District as a
condition of continued exemption under the provisions of paragraphs 5.3 and 5.3.1 of
this Article. Proof of payment shall be in the form of receipts and/or cancelled checks
to whom payment in lieu of the service fee has been made. Such proof shall be
presented on or before October 31 of each school year to the Association.

5.3.2    Any unit member making payments as set forth in paragraph 5.3 above, and who
requests that the grievance or appeal provisions of this agreement be used on his/her
behalf, shall be responsible for paying the reasonable costs of using the grievance or
appeal procedures.

5.4    The District agrees promptly to remit to the Association all sums deducted by the District
pursuant to the authorization of the unit member, and will provide the Association with a list of
deductions upon request.

ARTICLE 9. CLASS SIZE

9.1    The parties acknowledge the importance of class size and commit to maintain class sizes that
are comparable to other local school districts and provide solutions which meet the educational
needs of students within the classroom.

9.1.1    For grades 4 and 5 and K-3 not a part of the Class Size Reduction Program, the District
shall make reasonable efforts to maintain a maximum of 31 students per class,
excluding music and physical education.

9.1.2    For grades 6, 7, and 8, the District shall make reasonable efforts to maintain a school
wide staffing ratio of 26 students per teacher [number of students divided by number of
FTE (classroom teachers) equals staffing ratio],

9.1.3    For grades 9 through 12, the District shall make reasonable efforts to maintain a school
wide average class size of 33.3 students per class, excluding music, physical education
and NJROIC.

9.1.4    The maximum enrollments do not include programmatic groupings which may occur
during the student instructional day.

9.2    Should the District not be able to maintain the maximums in 9.1.1 and 9.1.2, the teacher(s)
specifically affected may meet with the school administration to develop strategies and
assistance needed to provide sound education for students in such classes. The teacher(s)
affected may request and be granted representation from the Association.

9.2.1 The classroom teacher shall be issued a pay warrant with an amount equal to $5 per day
per student for each student in excess of the maximums for a period of more than 10
days.

9.3    This article may be reviewed by the parties and shall be subject to a reopener if additional
funds are allocated by the state for the lowering of class sizes at the 4-12 grade levels.
