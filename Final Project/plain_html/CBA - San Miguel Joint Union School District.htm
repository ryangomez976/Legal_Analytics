Article I
Introduction

A.    This Article and the provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement by and between the San Miguel Joint Union School District ("District" or
"Employer") and the San Miguel Teachers Association ("Exclusive Representative" or
"Association"), an employee organization.

B.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Sections 3540-3549 of the Government
Code ("the Act").

C.    This Agreement shall supersede any rules, regulations, or practices of the District
which are inconsistent with its terms. Rules designed to implement this Agreement
shall be uniform in application.

Article II
Recognition

A. The Employer recognizes the Association as the Exclusive Representative of all
credentialed bargaining unit members exclusive of management, supervisory,
confidential and substitute employees (“Unit Members”).

Article III
Salary

A.    The Certificated Salary Schedules for the school years covered by this Agreement
are attached as Appendix A. The 2011-2012 school year Salary Schedule shall be the
same as the 2010-2011 Salary Schedule. The 2012-2013 school year Salary Schedule
shall be the same as the 2011-2012 Salary Schedule.

B.    Step Placement and Longevity. For the term of this Agreement, step increments
shall be granted to those Unit Members who qualify.

1.    When a Unit Member is initially hired by the District, the Unit Member shall be given
up to four years of credit for prior teaching experience. The four years of teaching
must have been within the preceding ten years. The Unit Member’s maximum
placement shall be at Step 5 on the Salary Schedule.

2.    Time spent in unpaid leave status shall not count toward advancement on the Salary
Schedule.

3.    Placement on the first step of the Salary Schedule is based on the completion of a
bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university with no additional
approved units and with no creditable years of teaching experience.

C. Column Placement. For the purposes of this Article, the following terms or
conditions are defined:

•    Units are defined as semester units. Quarter units are converted to semester
units by applying the factor of 2/3.

•    For Salary Schedule advancement only, Continuing Education Units and
credit for study other than accredited college units shall be granted at the rate
of 54 hours of study or class work for one semester unit equivalent; for
example 18 hours of Unit Member contact plus 36 hours of home or other
study or activity.

•    Except for pass-fail courses, courses shall not be eligible for salary credit
unless earned with a grade of “C" or higher.

•    Verifying completion of course work will be done by submitting transcripts or
other official documents.

•    Units and degrees will be accepted only from accredited colleges and
universities. For purposes of this Article and Appendices, accredited colleges
and universities are defined as those from which units are accepted by the
California Commission for Member Preparation and Licensing.

•    With respect to college units taken for credit on the salary schedule, the units
must be upper division or graduate level courses. A Unit Member may
request credit for a lower division course which is applicable to his/her
assignment.

•    Units to be credited for salary placement shall be related to the Unit Member’s
assignment. The Superintendent may approve courses that are not related to
the Unit Member’s assignment but that, in the Superintendent’s opinion, lead
to the Unit Member’s improvement of classroom instruction, job performance,
communication, child development, or additional educational credential or
degree.

District, their transcripts will be evaluated by the Superintendent to determine which
graduate courses completed, in addition to those required for a bachelor’s degree,
will be accepted for salary placement purposes.

a.    It is the Unit Member’s responsibility to provide the District with verification of
request for official transcripts within the first 30 days of paid service. Any transcripts
received after 90 days will not be used for salary placement for the current school
year and a result in payroll deductions if the initial placement was incorrect.

b.    Graduate units related to the Unit Member’s teaching assignment earned prior to
the time the Unit Member received a bachelor’s degree shall be granted for salary
schedule placement at the discretion of the District.

2.    Advancement For Salary Placement. Units shall be credited as they are earned,
subject to the conditions specified below.

a. Notice of intent to qualify for a higher class must be filed with the District by
May 1 of each year.

Article V
Hours

A. Unit Members shall be on duty and shall perform assigned tasks as directed by the
District. The length of the Unit Member’s workday, including preparation time, lunch
and relief periods, and time required before and after school shall not exceed seven and
one-half hours per day which shall normally be scheduled from 8:00 a.m. until 3:30 p.m.

Early departures and/or arrivals require the approval of the Superintendent or designee
on a case-by-case basis.

B.    The District shall be entitled to require a Unit Member to engage in extra, co-
curricular, and adjunct duties beyond the regular workday for which the Unit Members
shall receive no additional compensation.

1.    Such duties shall be limited to meetings and parent conferences, Back-to-School
Night, and open house. Staff meetings shall be only held once a month, unless due
to a special exception. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph A of this Article,

site staff and the Principal, general faculty meetings may be held in the afternoon

obligation to walk their students to the bus.

who attends a night function will be provided with “Flex time” that does not impact
the educational program and is scheduled at a time that is mutually agreeable to the
Unit Member and the Superintendent or designee.

C.    All Unit Members shall be assigned a duty-free uninterrupted lunch period equal to
the student lunch period but not less than 30 minutes.

Article VI
Leaves of Absence

A. Every full-time regular Unit Member shall be entitled to ten days of sick leave for
each year of employment.

1.    A Unit Member may use accumulated sick leave at any time during the school year
for accident, illness, or quarantine of the Unit Member or his/her immediate family.

a.    Unused sick leave shall accrue from school year to school year.

b.    The District shall provide each Unit Member with a written statement of
accumulated and credited sick leave for the current school year as soon after the
beginning of the school year as possible.

2.    Sick leave shall be taken on an hourly basis in units of one hour for any absence
from assigned duties. Unit Members shall normally contact the District prior to 6:30

a.m. on the date of absence.

3.    Female Unit Members shall be entitled to utilize sick leave for the period of time
required to be absent due to pregnancy or childbirth.

a.    The period of leave, including the date upon which the leave shall begin, shall be
determined by the Unit Member and her doctor based solely on the Unit Member's
physical ability to render service to the District.

b.    A statement from the Unit Member's doctor as to the beginning date of the leave
shall be filed with the Superintendent.

c.    The date of the Unit Member's return to service shall be based upon her doctor's
analysis and written verification of the Unit Member's physical ability to render
service to the District.

4.    If at the beginning of the school year the Unit Member's accumulated sick leave total
was less than five school months (i.e., 100 days), and if the accumulated sick leave
is used up within the school year, the Unit Member shall be entitled to paid extended
sick leave beginning on the first day following exhaustion of regular and
accumulated sick leave on the following basis:

a. Upon written verification of illness or incapacity by a physician, the amount of
salary deduction in any month shall not exceed:

(1) The sum which was actually paid a substitute in the position, or

Page -8-

(2) If no substitute was employed, the amount which would have been paid to a
substitute had one been employed for the remainder of the illness up to a total
period of five school months.

b. The maximum period of time for which a Unit Member may be granted a paid
leave of absence combining regular sick leave, accumulated sick leave, and
extended sick leave shall be five school months.

Personal Necessity Leave

B.    Unit Members may request to utilize up to seven days of the ten days of sick leave
allowed pursuant to this Article in cases of personal necessity.

1.    Personal necessity leave shall be granted upon request for the following reasons:

a.    Death or serious illness of a member of the immediate family.

b.    Accident involving the person or property of the Unit Member or the person or
property of a member of the immediate family. The accident must be of such a
serious nature that the immediate presence of the Unit Member is required.

c.    Required appearances in court during normal work hours under official order as a
litigant or witness.

d.    Such other reasons as approved by the Superintendent on a case-by-case basis.

2.    Up to three days of this leave may be taken for an unspecified reason.

3.    A Unit Member shall not be required to secure advance permission for leave taken
for the reasons set forth in paragraphs B.1 .a.-b. of this Article. However, in all other
instances, advance written notice of the Unit Member's intention to take such leave
shall be required.

Bereavement Leave

C.    Every Unit Member shall be entitled to three days of paid non-cumulative leave of
absence, or five days if travel in excess of 350 miles one way is involved, because of
the death of any member of the Unit Member's immediate family. 1

1.    The District shall require the use of bereavement leave before personal necessity
leave days are used for purposes allowed in this paragraph.

2.    This leave shall not be deducted from sick leave.

Industrial Accident and Illness Leave

D. A Unit Member shall be entitled to industrial accident or illness leave for any job-
related accident or illness in the amount of up to 60 days during which school is

Page -9-

required to be in session or when the Unit Member would otherwise have been
performing work for the District in any one fiscal year for the same accident. Such leave
shall not be cumulative.

1.    Such benefits shall be in addition to other sick leave benefits provided by the District.

2.    When entitlement to this leave has been exhausted, other sick leave shall be
utilized.

3.    Unit Members shall report all job-related injuries and illnesses to the District within
24 hours of the occurrence, whenever possible, regardless of whether or not medical
attention is required or whether or not time is lost from work. In the event the Unit
Member's injury or illness prevents the filing of the report within the time limit, the
report shall be filed as soon as possible.

4.    When a person employed in a position requiring certification qualifications is absent
from duty because of an industrial accident, the Unit Member shall be paid such
portion of the salary due the Unit Member for any month in which the absence
occurs as, when added to temporary disability indemnity, will result in a payment of
not more than the Unit Member's full salary.

5.    For each day that the Unit Member is absent and receiving Workers' Compensation
payments, the District shall charge the Unit Member's available leave only for that
portion of a day represented by the difference between the full day's pay and the
amount of daily pay represented by the Workers' Compensation payment endorsed
to the District.

6.    When an industrial accident or illness leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the
Unit Member shall be entitled to only the amount of unused leave due for the same
illness or injury.

7.    During any paid leave of absence, the Unit Member shall endorse to the District the
temporary disability indemnity checks received on account of the industrial accident
or illness. The District, in turn, shall issue the Unit Member's appropriate salary
warrants for payment of the Unit Member's salary and shall deduct normal retirement
and other authorized contributions.

Jury Leave

E.    Each Unit Member shall be entitled to paid leave for as many days of jury duty as
are required of the Unit Member by the court. The Unit Member shall sign over or pay
to the District any fees paid for jury duty less any allowance for transportation, parking,
and meals.

Catastrophic Leave

Immediate Family Defined

I.    For the purpose of this Article, immediate family shall be defined as: mother, father,

stepbrother, or stepsister of the Unit Member, or any person living in the immediate

granted for persons not listed above.

Provisions for Fringe Benefits

J.    Unit Members on paid leaves of absence shall have health and welfare benefits as

amount of money equal to the premiums for the various fringe benefits.

Return From Leave

K. Upon return from Disability-Pregnancy, Extended Illness, or Industrial Accident
leave, an effort will be made to reinstate the Unit Member to the position held at the time
leave was granted or to as nearly identical a position as possible. If it is determined by
the Superintendent that, in order to meet the educational-related needs of the District, it
is necessary to place the returning Unit Member in a different position, such placement
shall be made.

Article VII

Certificated UNIT Member Evaluation Procedures

A. These provisions constitute the procedures to be utilized for the evaluation and
assessment of certificated Unit Members as set forth in Education Code sections
44660, et seq., or its successor, commonly referred to as the "Stull Bill."

1.    The evaluation shall relate to:

a.    The progress of pupils toward the standards established pursuant to Education
Code §44662(a) and, if applicable, the state adopted academic content standards as
measured by state adopted criterion referenced assessments;

b.    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the Unit Member;

c.    The Unit Member's adherence to curricular objectives; and

d.    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment within the
scope of the Unit Member's responsibility.

2.    Evaluations shall take place at least once each year for probationary Unit Members,
at least once every other year for permanent Unit Members and at least every five
years for permanent Unit Members who meet all of the criteria in Education Code
§44664(a)(3):

•    Employed by the District for 10 years;

•    Teaching in a position required to be filled by a “highly qualified professional”
by the federal No Child Left Behind Act of 2001;

•    Previous evaluation rated the Unit Member as meeting or exceeding

Grievance Procedure

Miscellaneous Provisions

A.    A grievance shall mean an allegation by one or more Unit Members or the
Association that there has been a misinterpretation or misapplication of the specific
terms of this Agreement.

B.    Any Unit Member may, at any time, present grievances to the District and have such
grievances adjusted without the intervention of the Exclusive Representative as long as
the adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement; provided, however,
that the District shall not agree to a resolution of the grievance until the Exclusive
Representative has received a copy of the grievance and the proposed resolution and
has been given an opportunity to file a response.

C.    Each formal grievance shall be preceded by an informal attempt to settle whatever
problem exists at the appropriate level prior to the filing of the formal grievance.

1. Grievances will contain the following minimum information:

Page -17-

•    Name of grievant(s);

•    Date of filing;

•    Date of alleged contract    violation;

•    Section(s) of contract allegedly violated;

•    Synopsis of relevant facts;    and

•    Relief requested.

2. Grievances not filed in accordance with these criteria may be rejected as improperly
filed. Such rejection shall not extend the time limitations herein.

D.    Once a grievance has been initiated, all matters of dispute relating to it which occur
during the processing of the grievance shall become a part of and be resolved in the
grievance proceeding. Once a grievance has been resolved or a final decision
rendered, a grievant or the Association shall not be entitled to initiate a new grievance
on any matter or occurrence which properly could have been included in the first
grievance.

E.    At all formal levels of the grievance procedure, the grievant shall provide the
Exclusive Representative with all details and copies of correspondence relative to the
grievance. No reprisal of any kind will be taken by the District against any aggrieved
person or participant in the grievance procedure by reason of such participation.

F.    All documents, communications, and records dealing with the processing of a
grievance will be filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in the personnel
file of any of the participants.

G.    Failure of the grievant, the grievant's representative, or the Association to adhere to
the time limits of this Article shall constitute a waiver of the grievance and acceptance of
the District's action or decision at the appropriate level.

H.    Time limits may be extended or shortened by mutual agreement of the Association
and the District.

Level One

I. Within 20 school days of the occurrence or knowledge of the alleged
misinterpretation or misapplication of the Agreement, the grievant or the Association
must present the grievance in writing to the Superintendent. Within ten school days of
receipt of the grievance by the supervisor, the supervisor shall communicate a clear and
concise decision and the reasons for such decision to the grievant or the Association in
writing. If the supervisor does not respond within the time limit, the grievant or the
Association may appeal to the next level.

Level Two - Grievance Mediation

J. The parties shall request the services of a mediator from the California State
Conciliation Service within five school days following written notice from the grievant or
the Association that he/she is not satisfied with the decision at Level One. The parties
shall attempt to mediate a settlement to the grievance. In no instance will the form or

Page -18-

matter of the discussion and/or proposal during the mediation process be revealed.

Only the terms of a settlement, if any, may be revealed.

Level Three - Option One - School Board Appeal

K.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level Two, the grievant or
Association shall appeal to the Board of Trustees within five school days of the issuance
of the decision or the deadline within which such decision was to be made. The Board
shall hold a hearing on the matter and make a determination on the grievance.

Level Three - Option Two - Mediation/Arbitration

L.    If the Exclusive Representative and District are unable to find a resolution at Level
Two through the mediation process, the matter may be submitted to the mediator for
his/her binding decision on the grievance.

M.    The arbitration shall be limited solely to the interpretation and application of this
Agreement to the precise issue(s) submitted for arbitration. The arbitration shall not
determine any other issue(s). Where the District has made a judgment involving the
exercise of discretion, the mediator shall review such decision solely to determine
whether the decision has violated the Agreement and the arbitrator shall not substitute
his/her judgment for that of the District.

N.    After a hearing on the merits of the grievance, the mediator shall render a written
decision which sets forth findings of fact, reasoning, and conclusions on the precise
issue(s) submitted. The mediator shall not add to, subtract from, modify, alter, or
amend any provisions or procedures contained herein. The mediator shall not issue
statements of opinion or conclusions not essential to the determination of the issue(s)
submitted. No grievance(s) shall be filed regarding:

1.    The discharge of a probationary Unit Member;

2.    The failure or refusal of the District to rehire or retain in employment any
probationary Unit Member;

3.    The decision of the District granting or refusing to grant any sabbatical leave of
absence; and

4.    The contents of an evaluation.

P. The decision and award of the mediator shall be submitted to the Exclusive
Representative and District Board of Trustees for review and implementation.

Appendix C
Job Sharing Leaves of Absence

A. Permanent (tenured) classroom Unit Members who have satisfactory evaluations
may apply to the Board of Trustees of the District no later than March 1 of the preceding
school year for consideration of a job-sharing leave of absence under the following
conditions:

1.    Each Unit Member of a team submitting a job-sharing leave request must meet
all the credential and job description requirements for the requested position.

Further, the District must be able to employ a suitable replacement in the position
being left vacant.

2.    The job-sharing leave request must include a work schedule that is educationally
sound, as determined by the District.

3.    The job-sharing leave request must not cause the District to expend additional
funds.

4.    The leave for a job-sharing assignment, as well as any request for renewal of a
job-sharing assignment, is subject to annual approval by the Board of Trustees of
the District. Request for renewal must be submitted no later than March 1.

5.    The District's health and welfare contribution shall be shared equally (or in a
percentage that matches the work year of each Unit Member) by the job-sharing
team. Any Unit Member contributions that are necessary to maintain coverage must
be received by the District in a timely manner. If allowed by the insurance provider,
and if there is no additional cost to the District, one of the two part-time Unit
Members may decline coverage, thereby allowing the second Unit Member to have
full coverage.

6.    Step advancement on the salary schedule shall occur every other year for each
Unit Member who completes the equivalent of at least 75 percent of a full school
year of service within any two-year period of job-sharing leave.

7.    The recommendation of the Superintendent/Principal must accompany the initial
and subsequent renewal of job-sharing leave requests to the Board of Trustees of
the District.

8.    The District may apply any reasonable limit on the number of job-sharing
assignments it deems necessary.

9.    In the event that one Unit Member of the job-sharing team is unable to complete
the work term as specified and approved in the job-sharing leave request, the
remaining Unit Member shall immediately assume the full-time position for the
remainder of the school year.

10.    Both Unit Members of a job-sharing team will attend Open House and Back-to-
School Night unless extenuating circumstances, as determined by the District,
prevent the fulfillment of these responsibilities. Both Unit Members will attend
