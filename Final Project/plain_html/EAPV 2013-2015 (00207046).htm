ARTICLE 1
PREAMBLE

A.    The articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
Agreement by and between the Pleasant Valley Joint Union Elementary School
District ("District") and the Education Association of Pleasant Valley, CTA/NEA
("Association"), an employee organization.

B.    Tliis Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, §§ 3540-3549 of the
Government Code which shall be referred to as the "EERA".

C.    This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from the date of ratification

until June 30, 2015. Agreement signed by EAPV and PVJUESD on 8/12/2013, and Board
approved on 8/14/2013.

D.    Any individual contract between the District and a unit member shall be subject
to and consistent with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 3
ASSOCIATION AND MEMBER RIGHTS

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

A.    Association representatives shall have reasonable access to unit members
during the normal workday provided the association representative makes
his/her presence known to the principal/superintendent prior to contacting the
unit member on duty. Contact with employees should be limited to non-
instructional hours.

B.    The Association will have the right to use teacher mail boxes and designated
bulletin boards. The District will provide a bulletin board for Association use.

The Association will have the right to the use of District facilities pursuant to
EERA, Government Code Section 3543.1 (b). The Association's communication
with employees shall not include District's e-mail system which is reserved for
official District business communications and is not to be used for any business
unrelated to the District's business.

C.    The District will deduct from the pay of Association members, and pay to the
Association, the normal and regular monthly membership dues as voluntarily
authorized in writing by the employee on the employer form subject to the
following conditions:

1.    Deductions will be made only upon submission on a District-approved
form of a duly executed and revocable authorization by the employee.

2.    Tine District will not be obligated to put into effect any new, changed, or
discontinued deductions until the pay period commencing 15 days or
more after such submission.

3.    Any changes in deductions hereunder during the term of the Agreement
will be made pursuant to employee authorization.

MEMBER RIGHTS

A.    Teachers will be notified of their tentative assignment for the subsequent school
year by May 1 of the current school year.

B.    A teacher may rescind a resignation at any time before Board action is taken on
the resignation.

becomes effective or who enrolls during the term of this Agreement will pay
membership dues to the Association for the duration of this Agreement. This
provision will not deprive any teacher of the right to terminate membership at any
time. Nor will this deprive any member or former member who has resigned
membership during the term of this Agreement from the right to terminate the
deduction of dues upon the expiration of this Agreement provided the demand to

ARTICLE 4

MANAGEMENT RIGHTS AND EMPLOYEE DISCIPLINE

MANAGEMENT RIGHTS

A.    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its power and authority
to direct and manage to the full extent of the law. Included in, but not limited to,
those duties and powers are the right to: determine its organization; direct the
work of its employees; determine the times and hours of operation; determine
the level, means, and kinds of services to be provided; establish its education
policies, goals, and objectives; ensure the rights and educational opportunities of
students, determine staffing patterns; determine the number and kinds of
personnel required; contract out work or transfer work out of the unit subject to
the limitations set forth in paragraph C of this Article; maintain the efficiency of
employer operations; determine the curriculum; build, move, or modify facilities;
establish budget procedures and determine budgetary allocation; determine the
methods of raising revenue; and take action on any matter in the event of an
emergency. In addition, the District retains the right to hire, classify, assign,
evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline employees.

B.    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties, and
responsibilities by the District; the adoption of policies, rules, regulations, and
practices in furtherance thereof; and the use of judgment and discretion in
connection therewith, will be limited only by law and by the specific and express
terms of this Agreement, and then only to the extent the specific and express
terms are in conformance with the law.

EMPLOYEE DISCIPLINE

This Article does not apply to other personnel actions which may be governed by the
Education Code or other statutes including, but not limited to, employee evaluations,
employee termination and employee non-reelection.

A.    Progressive discipline shall be utilized unless the incident giving rise to the discipline is of
such nature that immediate or more severe action is appropriate, or where progressive
discipline is not likely to correct the conduct involved.

B.    Discipline shall be for just cause only.

C.    The following provisions apply to suspensions without pay of less than ten (10) days:

1. Before a suspension without pay is imposed, the employee shall be given a
written statement of charges indicating the facts and causes alleged, the
disciplinary action proposed, and notice of the right to appeal such discipline by
filing a grievance at Level Three (3) of Article 10 governing Grievance Procedures
of the Contract.

ARTICLE 6
HEALTH AND WELFARE BENEFITS

A.    The District will continue to provide current medical, prescription, vision and dental
programs on a composite rate basis. The District's contribution toward employee health
and welfare premiums will be "capped" at $7,816.70 per employee, applied on a tenthly
basis. All costs in excess of the District's monthly contribution shall be paid by
employees through payroll deductions. The District and the Association agree that there
shall be no increase or decrease in the District's contribution for health and welfare
benefit premiums for the term of the contract.

fifty percent of the premium contribution for a full-time employee.

C.    Employees will be enrolled in insurance programs on the first day of the month
following the date of employment. Benefit coverage will terminate on the last day
of the month following the termination date. Benefit coverage will be reduced to
the appropriate level on the first of the month following the reduction in hours.

D.    Tlie parties recognize that the provisions of the Consolidated Omnibus Budget
Reduction Act of 1985 ("COBRA") are applicable to unit members. As such,
teachers are governed by the rights and responsibilities set forth in the law.

ARTICLE 7
HOURS

A.    The professional responsibilities of unit employees include teaching; lesson
planning and preparation; grading and record keeping; meeting with students,
parents, and District personnel; improvement of teaching skills, and participating
in adjunct duties incident to the District's programs. The professional nature of
these responsibilities does not lend itself to a workday of rigidly established
length,

B.    The normal workday for teachers will begin at 8 a.m. and end at 3:15 p.m. Current full-
time teachers will not have their work hours adjusted without their written agreement
unless provided otherwise in this Agreement. Part-time, temporary teachers or full-time
teachers hired after July 1, 2009 may be assigned work hours as early as 7 a.m. or as
late as 4:15 p.m. but the workday will not exceed 7 hours and 15 minutes per workday
and will not require more than 315 student instruction minutes per workday. Employees
shall have an 18 minute break from 10:00 a.m. to 10:18 a.m. Class instruction shall
resume at 10:18 a.m. Based upon the needs of the District, the Superintendent shall be
allowed to change the starting time of this break, however, the Superintendent shall
provide one day's notice of any change, except in case of an emergency, where notice
shall not be required. Unless otherwise agreed, the lunch period will run from 12:00 to
12:45 on a regular day and from 12:30 to 1:15 on a minimum day. On a regular day,
the warning bell shall ring at 12:40 and the teachers shall open their doors for class at
12:45.

C.    The work year for full-time teachers will be 185 days, 181 days of which will be pupil
contact instructional days. The four additional days shall be utilized primarily for the
purpose of preparation. However, the days are work days for which the employee is paid
and as such, the District retains the right to direct the employees and to assign tasks as
necessary during those days. The administration may hold up to seven hours of
meetings on the two workdays immediately prior to the first student attendance day.

The Association will be consulted annually on the school calendar prior to its adoption by
the District.

D.    Co-curricular assignments are considered to be mandatory requirements of the job. As is
the case with adjunct duties, teachers are expected to remain beyond the minimum on¬
site hours, as necessary, to perform the duties.

1.    Volunteers will be sought first. If there are insufficient volunteers, the site
administrator will assign teachers to perform the duties. The assignments
will be made in a fair and equitable manner.

2.    The number of co-curricular assignments will be substantially similar to
the amount given in prior years.

ARTICLE 8
EVALUATION PROCEDURES

A.    The following procedures shall be utilized for the evaluation and assessment of
certificated employees consistent with California Education Code Sections 44660-44665
and the California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTP).

1.    The governing board or its designee shall evaluate and assess each certificated
employee's performance as it reasonably relates to:

a.    The progress of students towards the District's standards of expected pupil
achievement;

b.    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the employee;

c.    The employee's adherence to curricular objectives;

d.    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment
within the scope of the employee's responsibilities;

2.    The Superintendent/Principal shall formally evaluate each certificated employee at
least once each school year and shall transmit the evaluation to the evaluatee by
February 15.

B.    Prior to November 15 for each school year, the Superintendent/Principal and the
employee shall meet for a pre-observation conference to discuss the employee's
instructional goals and objectives for the lesson(s) to be evaluated and the evaluation
process.

C.    The Superintendent/Principal shall utilize at least two (2) formal, scheduled, classroom
observations for a time period of not less than 30 minutes, using a separate copy of the
Classroom Observation Report (Appendix B-l) for each observation. The
Superintendent/Principal shall meet with the employee regarding the formal classroom
observations as soon as practicable following each formal observation. The
Superintendent/Principal may also utilize informal unscheduled classroom observations
as part of the evaluation process and shall meet with the employee to discuss those
informal classroom observations as soon as practicable after the informal observations.

D.    The Superintendent/Principal shall utilize the Teacher Evaluation Report (Appendix B-2)
to complete the employee's evaluation. If the employee's written evaluation indicates
that he/she is not performing satisfactorily in any area, the Superintendent/Principal
shall describe the specific areas of unsatisfactory performance in the Teacher Evaluation
Report.

E.    The employee and the Superintendent/Principal shall meet to review the Teacher
Evaluation Report as soon as practicable after its completion.

ARTICLE 9
LEAVES

A.    Personal Illness and Injury Leave. Employees regularly employed on a full-time
basis who are absent from duty due to their own illness or injury, will be granted
10 workdays per year as personal illness leave. Earned but unused sick leave
days will be accumulated from year-to-year.

1.    Employees serving less than a full school year, and/or employed on less
than a full-time basis, will be entitled to personal illness leave in the same
proportion that the time served bears to a school year of full-time service.

2.    An employee's sick leave will be exclusive of all days he/she is not
required to render service to the District.

3.    Whenever an employee is absent from duty on account of his/her own
illness or injury for a period of time in excess of the current year's sick
leave to which he/she is entitled, he/she will be allowed full compensation
for the period, if any, of his/her accumulated sick leave earned, but
unused in previous years of service with the District, and/or the lesser of
the following amounts for a period of not to exceed five school months
during the current school year; said period being exclusive of the number
of days of accumulated sick leave paid at full salary.

4.    If an employee has no accumulated sick leave from previous years and
takes all or a portion of the sick leave allowable for the current school year
and subsequently fails to serve the District for the full school year, an
amount equivalent to the number of days of used, but unearned, will be
deducted from the final salary warrant drawn payable to the employee.

B.    Personal Necessity. At his/her election, an employee may request to utilize up to
seven days of sick leave allowed pursuant to Education Code Section 44981 in
cases of personal necessity.

1.    Leave may be granted upon request for a portion of a workday required to
cover emergency occasions that are unavoidable and of a serious nature
involving circumstances that the employee cannot be expected to
disregard and that may not be conducted at a time other than regular duty
hours.

2.    The 7 days of leave under this section may be utilized by an employee
without having to state a reason, subject to the following restrictions:

a.    The days will be limited to the equivalent of the employee's
contractual workday and will be charged in one-half day blocks of time.

b.    No day before or day after any holiday, or winter, spring, or
summer recess may be utilized.

ARTICLE 10
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Definitions:

A.    A "grievance" is an alleged violation of the specific term of this Agreement. A
grievance may be filed by an employee, employees or the Association. Actions
to challenge or change the policies of the District as set forth in the rules and
regulations or administrative regulations and procedures, so long as these are
consistent with the terms of the Agreement, must be undertaken under separate
legal processes. Other matters for which a specific method of review is provided
by law are not within the scope of this procedure. Neither the content of an
employee's Performance Evaluation nor the employment status of probationary
employees will be subject to the provisions of this Article.

B.    A "day" is a day in which school is open. In the event a grievance is being
processed after the close of the school year, at the option of a grievant, the
grievance will be suspended until the commencement of the new semester.

C.    Informal Level. Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant will attempt
to resolve it by an informal discussion with the grievant's immediate supervisor.

D.    Formal Level.

Level I: Within twenty (20) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving
rise to the grievance, the grievant must present the grievance in writing on the
appropriate District designated form to the Superintendent. This will be a clear,
concise statement of the grievance, the circumstances involved, the specific
proviston(s) of the Agreement alleged to have been violated, and the specific
remedy sought. The Superintendent will communicate a decision to the
employee in writing within 20 days after receiving the grievance. If the
superintendent does not respond within the time limits, the grievant may appeal
to the next level. Within the above time limits, either party may request a
personal conference with the other party.

E.    Level II: If the grievant is not satisfied with the Superintendent's decision, the
grievant may, within 15 days (or within 15 days of the date the Superintendent's
decision should have been issued, if no decision was issued), appeal the matter
to Grievance Conciliation with the agreement of the Association.

1.    The grievant will submit a signed statement setting forth in a clear,
concise manner the reasons for the appeal and whether a hearing is
requested. The statement will also include a copy of the original
grievance as well as all other subsequent written documentation regarding
the grievance.

2.    The District will then schedule a meeting with a conciliator from the
California State Mediation and Conciliation Service as soon as reasonably
possible for all parties of interest.

ARTICLE 11
SAFETY AND STUDENT DISCIPLINE

A.    A teacher may suspend a pupil from her/his class for the day of the suspension
and the following day pursuant to Ed Code section 48910.

B.    Should the District request or require that a unit member transport a pupil or
pupils, the District will provide full primary liability coverage for any liability which
may occur during the assignment.

C.    The District will protect teachers from loss of personal teaching property while
acting in the discharge of their duties. The District will fully reimburse teachers
for losses resulting from theft or damage to their property, provided that the
District has been made aware of and has approved the use of the property prior
to the theft or damage.

D.    The District will provide teachers with prior written authority to take pupils on
school sponsored activities and field trips, with the District bearing liability for any
personal injuries, deaths, or damage to personal or real property arising during
the course of an activity or field trip.

E.    Teachers will immediately report cases of assault suffered by them in connection
with their employment to the Superintendent, who will immediately report the
incident to the sheriff.

F.    Pursuant to Ed Code section 49079, the District will provide teachers, who have
a need to know, with information regarding any student whose actions have, or
could have, led to the suspension or expulsion of the student.
