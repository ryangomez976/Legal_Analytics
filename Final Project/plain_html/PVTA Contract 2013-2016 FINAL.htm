ARTICLE 1

Agreement

1.    This Agreement (Agreement) constitutes a binding bilateral contract by and between
the Portola Valley School District (“District”) and the Portola Valley Teachers
Association, an affiliate of the California Teachers Association, National Education
Association (“Association”).

2.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code. The term “Agreement” as used herein means the written
Agreement provided under Section 3540.1 (h) of the Government Code.

ARTICLE 3

Membership and Payroll Deductions

1.

Any employee who is a member of the Association, or who has applied for
membership, may sign and deliver to the District an assignment authorizing deduction
of unified membership dues. Such authorization shall continue in effect from year to
year unless revoked in writing between July 1 and July 31, of the final year of the
contract. Pursuant to such authorization, the District shall deduct one tenth of such
dues from the regular salary check of the employee over a ten month period.
Deductions for employees who sign such authorization after the commencement of the
school year shall be appropriately prorated to complete payments by the end of the
school year. The District agrees to remit such monies to the California Teachers
Association, accompanied by an alphabetical list of employees from whom such
deductions have been made.

2.

Unit members must, as a condition of continued employment with the District, fulfill
his/her financial obligation to the Association during the term of this Agreement as set
forth in this Article.

3.

The employee may elect to have her/his salary paid in either ten (10) monthly
payments, the first payment due on the last working day of September and the final
payment due on the last working day of June; or in twelve (12) monthly payments, the
first payment due on the last working day of September and the final payment due on
the last working day of August. No payroll deductions will be made with payments
issued in July and August.

4.

Any unit member who is not a member of the Association, or who does not make
application for membership within 30 days of the effective date of this Agreement, or
within 30 days from the date of commencement of assigned duties within the
bargaining unit, shall become a member of the Association or pay to the Association a
service fee as determined by the Association in an amount not to exceed the standard
initiation fees, periodic dues, and general assessments, payable to the Association in
one lump-sum cash payment in the same manner as required for the payment of

the Association, the Association shall so inform the District, that the requirements of
Section 5 below have been met and the District shall immediately begin automatic
payroll deduction as provided in Education Code Section 4506 and in the same
manner as set forth in this Article. There shall be no charge to the Association for
such mandatory service fee deductions.

ARTICLE 5

Grievance Procedure

1.    Purpose

1.1.    The purpose of this grievance procedure is to secure at the lowest possible
level, resolution of grievances arising under this Agreement. Both parties
agree that these proceedings shall be kept as informal and confidential as may
be appropriate at any level of this procedure.

1.2.    Nothing contained herein shall be construed as limiting the right of any member
of the unit to have a grievance adjusted without intervention by the Association
as long as the adjustment is reached prior to arbitration and such adjustment is
not inconsistent with the terms of the master contract. If any unit member
presents a grievance on her/his own behalf, the District shall not agree to a
resolution of the grievance until the Association has received a copy of the
grievance and the proposed resolution and has been given the opportunity to
file a response. The District shall provide the grievant with the appropriate
printed forms.

2.    Definitions

2.1.    A grievance is an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the
express terms of this Agreement. Actions to challenge or change the terms of
this Agreement shall not be considered a grievance.

2.2.    A grievant is a unit member, unit members, or the Association making a claim
on behalf of one or more unit members, or the Association on its own behalf.

3.    Procedure

3.1.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the
timetable specified at each level should be considered as a maximum and
every effort should be made to expedite the process. The time limit specified
may be, however, extended by mutual agreement.

3.2.    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be processed
through all the steps in this grievance procedure by the end of the school year,
and could result in irreparable harm to the grievant, the District, or the
Association, an effort should be made to reduce, by mutual agreement, the
time limits set forth herein so that the procedure may be exhausted prior to the

3.2.1.    Informal Level

A grievant shall first identify the alleged violation on a “Notice of Intent to
File a Grievance” form (see Appendix A), submit the form to the
principal or immediate supervisor, and discuss the grievance within five
(5) duty days after the alleged violation or discovery of the alleged
violation or, if grievant desires, through her/his chosen representative
with the objective of resolving the matter informally.

3.2.2.    Level One

3.2.2.1.    If, within five (5) duty days after this informal discussion, the
grievant has not received a written disposition or is not satisfied
with the disposition of the grievance, she/he may file a formal
grievance with the principal or immediate supervisor in writing on
a “Statement of Grievance - Level One” form (see Appendix At
approved by the District and Association with a copy
simultaneously provided to the Association. If the grievant
requests a conference with the principal or immediate
supervisor, the grievant and the principal or supervisor shall
meet to discuss the grievance within five (5) duty days after the
written grievance is filed.

3.2.2.2.    Within five (5) duty days after receipt of the “Statement of
Grievance” form by the principal or immediate supervisor, or if a
conference was requested within five (5) duty days, the principal
or immediate supervisor shall render her/his formal decision in
writing with a copy simultaneously provided to the Association.

3.2.2.3.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the disposition of the
grievance, or if no disposition has occurred within the Level One
timelines set forth above, the grievance may be appealed to
Level Two within five (5) duty days on the “Statement of
Grievance Form-Level Two” form (see Appendix A) to the
Superintendent with a copy simultaneously provided to the
Association.

3.2.3.    Level Two

3.2.4.2.    The parties shall select a mutually acceptable arbitrator who
shall be bound by the rules and procedures of the American
Arbitration Association (AAA) or the California State Mediation
and Conciliation Service (CSMCS). If the parties are unable to
agree upon an arbitrator, the parties shall request a list of
arbitrators from AAA or CMSCS. An arbitrator shall be selected
by the following procedures: The Association and the employer’s
representative shall select the arbitrator from the American
Arbitration Association or California State Mediation and
Conciliation Service list by eliminating names until one remains.
The first option of elimination shall alternate. The one remaining
name shall be the arbitrator. The process of striking names shall
occur within five (5) days of receipt of the list from the American
Arbitration Association or California State Mediation and
Conciliation Service by both parties. All grievances reaching the
arbitration level shall be numbered. The odd-numbered
grievances will give the District first elimination; even numbered
grievances will give the Association first elimination.

3.2.4.3.    Any question which arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance
shall be ruled upon by the arbitrator first before hearing the
merits of the grievance.

grievance procedure by reason of such participation.

Administration at a level at or above the principal or immediate supervisor, the

ARTICLE 6

Association and Unit Member Rights

1.    Representation

The Association has the right under the Educational Employment Relations Act to
represent bargaining unit members in their employment relations with the District.
Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as a waiver of such rights.

2.    District Services

The Association shall have the right to use the District mail/email service and unit
member mailboxes for communications to unit members without interference,
censorship, or examination of such communications by the District, except where
contrary to law. The Association shall also have the right to post notices of activities
and matters of Association concern on an Association bulletin board. If the District
develops a District-wide electronic bulletin board, it shall establish a designated space
for the Association on the board. All members with regular access to the board shall
have access to the Association space.

3.    Use of Buildings and Equipment

The Association shall have the right to use District buildings, sites, and equipment
during all reasonable hours for meetings and other Association activities.

4.    Access to Worksite

Authorized representatives of the Association shall have the right to transact official
Association business on school property and utilize District facilities at reasonable
times provided that such activities or use do not interfere with classroom instruction,
the duties and responsibilities of District employees, or school site functions.

5.    Access to Information

Within ten (10) duty days from the receipt of a written request from the Association for
available public information or specific employee data pertinent to the development of
collective bargaining proposals, the District will either provide the requested

ARTICLE 10
EVALUATION PROCEDURE
Overview

The District and PVTA are committed to a teacher evaluation system that is comprehensive,
promotes exemplary teaching, and provides ample opportunities for self-reflection and
professional growth. Unit member evaluations encompass all aspects of teacher roles and
responsibilities described in the California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTP),
2009.

The California Standards for the Teaching Profession, 2009, (see appendix I.) will continue
as the basis for the evaluation process in the Portola Valley School District. “The 2010
Continuum of Teaching Practice (see appendix J.) is aligned with the CSTP, builds on
California’s pre-service Teaching Performance Expectations and sets forth a description of
teaching that respects the diverse ways teachers pursue excellence in teaching and learning.”
(Continuum of Teaching Practice, p. 2) Unit members will be evaluated using the five
indicators described in the Continuum document plus a zero point, “not evident” column, has
been added creating a six (0-5) point rubric: Not Evident, Emerging, Exploring, Applying,
Integrating, and Innovating. The District will use the rubrics from the Continuum of Teaching
Practice as a tool for self-reflection, goal setting, and inquiry into practice to guide this
evaluation process. “A formative assessment system has three essential components:
standards, criteria and evidence of practice. Standards refer to the CSTP and are in alignment
with the P-12 academic content standards. Criteria refer to indicators of teaching practice.
Evidence of practice includes multiple sources of data, and student work analyses and is used
to make valid self-assessments on the Continuum of Teaching Practice. The Continuum of
Teaching Practice is one component of a comprehensive formative assessment system for
teachers, based on the CSTP.” (Continuum, p. 2)

All Portola Valley School District teachers should strive to perform at the highest levels
(Integrating and Innovating) of this continuum in all areas. Those unit members who are not
performing at their highest levels should take the steps necessary to grow and improve their
practice. The District and PVTA are committed to providing support to all probationary and
permanent status unit members through the Consulting Teacher Program. The District
recognizes that “a teacher may be at Level 1 or 2 in some elements of the CSTP and Levels 3
and 4 in others, no matter how many years she or he has been in the profession. Because a
teacher’s growth is developmental, one may return to an earlier level temporarily if there are
changes in a teacher’s career, such as new course content, grade level, school, or student
demographics. In fact, it is not uncommon for teachers to self-assess and find they have
moved from right to left on the Continuum of Teaching Practice in response to new teaching

ARTICLE 11

Transfer/Reassignmerit Procedures

Definitions

1.    T ransfer

1.1.    A transfer shall be defined as the change of a unit member’s work location from
one school or worksite to another school or worksite within the District. Such a
transfer does not encompass the process of reassignment within the present
school, department, or work location. A unit member assigned to more than
one work site shall be considered as being transferred only when moved from
one District-wide program to another program.

2.    Reassignment

2.1.    A reassignment shall be defined as the redesignation of or change in a specific
assignment or responsibilities within a school or department or work location.
The definition of reassignment shall include changes in grade-level and/or
subject-area instructional responsibilities within the same school, department,
or work location but not the initial placement of a newly employed bargaining
unit member in a specific school or work location. Changes in assignments to
extracurricular activities or adjunct duties shall not be considered reassignment.

2.2.    A reassignment made to ensure the retention of certificated unit members with
seniority greater than those laid off shall not be considered a transfer unless it
necessitates the change of a unit member’s work location from one school or
worksite to another school or worksite within the District.

3.    Nothing in this Article limits the District’s authority to make transfers, assignments, and
reassignments pursuant to Education Code Section 44955(c).The Superintendent has
final authority to transfer unit members.

4.    Notification of Staffing and Teaching Assignment

Overview

The District and PVTA are committed to a teacher evaluation system that is comprehensive,
promotes exemplary teaching, and provides ample opportunities for self-reflection and
professional growth. Unit member evaluations encompass all aspects of teacher roles and
responsibilities described in the California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTP),
2009:

1.2    Connecting learning to students’ prior knowledge, backgrounds, life experiences, and
interests

1.3    Connecting subject matter to meaningful, real-life contexts

1.4    Using a variety of instructional strategies, resources, and technologies to meet students’
diverse learning needs

1.5    Promoting critical thinking through inquiry, problem solving, and reflection

1.6    Monitoring student learning and adjusting instruction while teaching

Creating and Maintaining Effective Environments for Student Learning

2.1    Promoting social development and responsibility within a caring community where each

2.4    Creating a rigorous learning environment with high expectations and appropriate support

2.5    Developing, communicating, and maintaining high standards for individual and group

2.6    Employing classroom routines, procedures, norms, and supports for positive behavior to

2.7    Using instructional time to optimize learning

3.2    Applying knowledge of student development and proficiencies to ensure student

3.3    Organizing curriculum to facilitate student understanding of the subject matter

3.4    Utilizing instructional strategies that are appropriate to the subject matter

Revised CSTP

The California Standards for the Teaching Profession, 2009, (see appendix L.) will continue
as the basis for the evaluation process in the Portola Valley School District. “The 2010
Continuum of Teaching Practice (see appendix M.) is aligned with the CSTP, builds on
California’s pre-service Teaching Performance Expectations and sets forth a description of
teaching that respects the diverse ways teachers pursue excellence in teaching and learning.”
(Continuum of Teaching Practice, p. 2) Unit members will be evaluated using the five
indicators described in the Continuum document plus a zero point, “not evident” column, has
been added creating a six (0-5) point rubric: Not Evident, Emerging, Exploring, Applying,
Integrating, and Innovating. The District will use the rubrics from the Continuum of Teaching
Practice as a tool for self-reflection, goal setting, and inquiry into practice to guide this
evaluation process. “A formative assessment system has three essential components:
standards, criteria and evidence of practice. Standards refer to the CSTP and are in alignment
with the P-12 academic content standards. Criteria refer to indicators of teaching practice.
Evidence of practice includes multiple sources of data, and student work analyses and is used
to make valid self-assessments on the Continuum of Teaching Practice. The Continuum of
Teaching Practice is one component of a comprehensive formative assessment system for
teachers, based on the CSTP.” (Continuum, p. 2)

All Portola Valley School District teachers should strive to perform at the highest levels
(Integrating and Innovating) of this continuum in all areas. Those unit members who are not
performing at their highest levels should take the steps necessary to grow and improve their
practice. The District and PVTA are committed to providing support to all probationary and
permanent status unit members through the Consulting Teacher Program. The District
recognizes that “a teacher may be at Level 1 or 2 in some elements of the CSTP and Levels 3
and 4 in others, no matter how many years she or he has been in the profession. Because a
teacher’s growth is developmental, one may return to an earlier level temporarily if there are
changes in a teacher’s career, such as new course content, grade level, school, or student
demographics. In fact, It is not uncommon for teachers to self-assess and find they have
moved from right to left on the Continuum of Teaching Practice in response to new teaching
contexts, responsibilities, challenges, or even deeper understanding of the work.” (Continuum,
p. 3)

C.    Evaluation Criteria

The evaluation of unit members shall be based upon the unit member demonstrating the
following:

1.    Progress on the California Standards for the Teaching Profession, 2009, and Continuum of
Teaching Practice.

2.    The performance of non-instructional duties and responsibilities, including student supervisory
duties, collaborating with colleagues, interacting with parents and students at school
programs, events and activities, and participating in professional meetings.

3.    Student progress toward grade level/district academic standards, either as stipulated in the
National Common Core State Standards or as determined by the unit member and the
evaluator in consultation.

D.    Observation

1. Permanent-status unit members shall be observed on a frequent basis, approximately once a
month for short periods of time, approximately 15 minutes per visit. The evaluator will provide
brief, informal written feedback to the permanent-status unit member following each informal
visit.

E.    Final Summative Evaluation

1.    The final written summative evaluation of the unit member shall be transmitted to the unit
member no later than the dates established in Evaluation Process Steps: permanent-status
Unit Members below.

2.    Within one (1) week of receipt of the final written summative evaluation, narrative and
assessment form, by the unit member, the evaluator shall meet with the unit member to
discuss the final summative evaluation.

3.    The unit member shall sign the evaluation. However, such signature does not signify
agreement.

4.    The unit member may file a written response to the evaluation which shall be attached as a
permanent part of the evaluation.

Consulting Teachers - The District will provide support to temporary, probationary, and
permanent status teachers through consulting teachers. These exemplary teachers will be
selected by the District and paid at their hourly rate to support unit members. Probationary
teachers will be provided with up to 40 hours of consulting teacher support during the school
year. The evaluator and probationary teacher may increase this level of support by mutual
agreement. Permanent and temporary status teachers may also request up to 20 hours of
consulting teacher support during the school year. These requests must be pre-approved by
the evaluator.

Step 2: The evaluator (principal, dean of students, program specialist, assistant

15 of the evaluation year. During this meeting, the evaluator will review the CSTP standards
and Continuum of Teaching Practice rubrics with the unit member and establish an evaluation
blog. The unit member shall complete a self-assessment, indicating their placement on the
rubric in each of the CSTPs. The evaluator shall provide input into this process, and the
evaluator and unit member shall collaboratively agree to the unit member’s placement on the
various rubrics. Progress metrics shall be determined by the unit member and evaluator in
consultation.

Step 3: The unit member shall select one area within each standard they would like to work
on for the year. The evaluator will meet with the teacher and identify three or four top
priorities from these for focus areas. The evaluator and the unit member shall meet in
January or February to discuss the unit member's progress in the three or four focus areas.
Unit member shall provide ongoing evidence/material documentation of progress toward the
successful achievement of previously agreed upon professional goals via posts to their
evaluation blog.

Step 4: The evaluator will make several brief (approximately 10-15 minutes), unannounced
visits during the school year. Each time the evaluator visits the classroom, s/he will provide
the unit member with informal, brief written feedback via the blog. Evaluator shall inform the
unit member, at this time, of any deficiencies that might place the unit member in jeopardy of
failing to achieve the agreed upon professional goals.

Step 5: The evaluator and unit member shall meet some time between mid-April and the end
of May to revisit the self assessment focus areas and determine current placement. The
evaluator’s notes, unit member’s reflections, student work samples, and assessments will all
be considered when making this placement.
