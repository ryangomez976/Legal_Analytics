ARTICLE 1

AGREEMENT

1.1 The Articles and paragraphs contained herein constitute the agreement (“Agreement”)

between the Governing Board of the Jacoby Creek School Charter District (“Board”) and
the Jacoby Creek Teachers’ Association (JCTA”), an employee organization, pursuant to
Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the Government Code (“Act’*).

This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2007 until June 30,

2010.

ARTICLE 2
RECOGNITION

2.1    Recognition. The Board confirms its recognition of the JCTA as the exclusive

representative for that unit of employees defined as follows:

All certificated employees, excluding management, confidential, supervisory and
substitute employees.

2.2 Non-Bargaining Unit Personnel. The parties to this Agreement recognize that the duties
and work performed by the bargaining unit described above shall be performed only by
unit members unless there are no unit members who are qualified ana interested in
performing the work, in which case the District may utilize appropriate certificated non¬
bargaining unit personnel.

ARTICLE 3

NEGOTIATION PROCEDURES

3.1    The JCTA and the Board shall annually present to each other a list of intended areas of

negotiations for the following school year no later than March 8th.

3.2 As soon after March 8th, as possible, both parties will submit specific initial proposals for
public notice.

3.3 Bargaining will commence as soon as possible after the initial proposals have been

“sunshinea”. A good faith effort will be made by all parties to arrive at an agreement
before end of the school year. Recognizing the vagaries of funding in public education,
we realize this is not always achievable.

3

3.4 Meetings shall occur at mutually convenient times and places. It is understood that the
Association has the right to reasonable release time to negotiate during their regularly
Contracted work day. Both parties will make reasonable accommodation for schedules
of participants to facilitate the process.

ARTICLE 4

MAINTENACE AND APPLICATION OF STANDARDS

4.1 The Board shall not reduce or eliminate any benefits or professional advantages which
are mandatory topics of bargaining and which were enjoyed by unit members as of the
effective date of the Agreement unless otherwise provided by the express terms of this
Agreement, a subsequent agreement of the parties, or completion of the impasse
procedures.

4.2 This Agreement will supersede any rules, regulations or practice of the Board which are
or may in the future be contrary to or inconsistent with its terms. It shall not supersede
applicable state or federal law. Only mandatory changes in federal or state law shall
diminish the provisions of this contract.

4.3 The provisions of this Agreement and rules which are designed to implement it shall be
uniform in application and effect.

ARTICLE 5

NON-DISCRIMINATION

5.1    No Discrimination. No party to this Agreement shall discriminate against any employee
or official of the District on the basis or race, color, creed, age, sex, national origin,
political affiliation, marital status, sexual orientation, physical handicap, membership or
non-membership in an employee organization or participation or non-participation in the
activities of an employee organization, all as may be prohibited by applicable state or
federal law.

5.2    Employment Interviews. Employment application forms and oral hiring interview
procedures shall not refer to membership in or preference for the exclusive
representative, any other employee organization or no representative.

SAVINGS

6.1    If any provision of this Agreement is held to be contrary to law by a court of competent
jurisdiction, then such provision shall not deemed valid except to the permitted by law,
but all other provisions of this Agreement shall continue in effect.

6.2    It is further agreed that within 10 davs of either party’s request following notification of
the court’s decision, the parties shall begin negotiations regarding matters related to such
provision.

7.1    Improvements in benefits included in this Agreement which are brought about by the
amendment or addition of statutory guarantees now provided in California or federal law
shall be incorporated into this Agreement.

statutory guarantees incorporated into this Agreement shall obligate the parties within ten
(10) days of either party’s request to negotiate. (In no case shall an amendment or repeal
of statutory guarantees automatically reduce this agreement unless that amendment or

ARTICLE 9

CONTRACT MAINTENANCE

9.1    Definitions

9.1.1    A “grievance” is a claim by one or more unit members or the JCTA that there has
been a violation, misinterpretation or misapplication of a specific provision of this
Agreement.

9.1.2    The “aggrieved” party is the person or persons making the claim.

9.1.3    A “day” is any day in which a unit member is required to render services to the
District.

9.1.4    A “party of interest” is any person who might be required to take action or against
whom action might be taken in order to resolve the claim.

9.2    Purpose. The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative
level, equitable solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise in the
interpretation or application of this Agreement. Both parties agree that these proceedings
will be kept as informal and confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the
procedure.

9.3    Procedure

9.3.1    Informal Problem Solving- Step 1

9.3.1.1 Prior to the filling of any grievances form, the teacher, teachers, and/or

Association designated representative shall schedule a conference with the
Administrator to discuss the problem that could precipitate a grievance.
The Administrator shall have five (5) working days to consider the
problem, at which time the Administrator will be present in writing a
proposed solution. The Administrator may schedule a conference for this
purpose.

9.3.2 An individual unit member may be accompanied at all stages of this
procedure by a JCTA designated representative(s).

9.3.2    Formal Problem Solving (grievance) -Step 2

If an acceptable solution is not achieved at the informal step, or if no timely
disposition has been made, a grievance shall be presented in writing to the
Administrator using the grievance form (Appendix D). The Administrator shall
meet with the grievant and the JCTA designated representative within five (5)
days of receipt of the written grievance form. The Administrator shall provide a
written disposition of the grievance, including all reasons there, to all parties of
interest within five (5) days of such meeting.

6

9.3.3    Formal Problem Solving (grievance) - Step 3

If the grievant or JCTA is dissatisfied with the disposition or if no timely disposition has
been made at Step 2, it may have the matter reviewed by the Board. The JCTA may
cause the matter to be heard by placing the matter on the Board agenda. The Board shall
provide a written response within twenty-five (25) days of the Board meeting to all
parties of interest.

9.3.4    Formal Problem Solving (grievance) - Step 4

9.3.4.1    If the aggrieved party is not satisfied with the disposition of the grievance, or if no
disposition has occurred within twenty (20) days of such a meeting, the aggrieved
party may request JCTA submit the grievance to arbitration.

9.3.4.2    If JCTA proceeds to arbitration, within fifteen (15) days it shall notify the Board
in writing. Within ten (10) days of such notification, representatives of the Board
and JCTA shall request a panel of five (5) arbitrators from the California
Conciliation and Mediation Service. The selection of the arbitrator and the
arbitration proceedings shall occur pursuant to the alternative strike method
between the parties.

9.3.4.3    The arbitrator’s decision will be in writing and will set forth the findings of fact,
reasoning and conclusions of the issues submitted. The arbitrator will be without
power or authority to make any decision which requires the commission of an act
prohibited by law or which is violative of the terms of this Agreement. However,
it is agreed that the arbitrator is empowered to include in nay award such as
financial reimbursement or other remedies as she/he judges to be proper.

The arbitrator shall review and consider the Board’s action taken at Step 3 prior to
making a decision.

The decision of the arbitrator will be submitted to JCTA and the Board and will
be final and binding on the parties.

If any question arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such question will be
ruled upon by the arbitrator only after he/she has had an opportunity to hear the
merits of the grievance.

7

9.4.4 All costs for the service of the arbitrator, including, but not limited to, per diem
expenses, travel and subsistence expenses, and the cost of any hearing room, will
be borne equally by the Board and the Association. All other costs, except for the
release time for the grievant(s), JCTA representatives and witnesses, will be borne
by the party incurring them.

9.4    Time Limits

9.4.1    Step 1, the informal part of this process, must be begun no more than forty-five
(45) days after the event giving rise to the grievance first occurs or could
reasonably been known to have occurred.

9.4.2    Time limits for each level shall begin the day following receipt of the grievance,
grievance appeal or written decision.

9.4.3    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time
limits specified at each level should be considered to be maximums and every
effort should be made to expedite the process. The time limits may, however, be
extended by mutual agreement.

9.4.4    In the event a grievance is filed at such time that it cannot be processed through
all the steps in this procedure by the end of the school year and, if left unresolved
until the beginning of the following school year, could result in harm to an
aggrieved person, the time limits set forth herein will be reduced so that the
procedure may be exhausted prior to the end of the school year, or as soon as is
practical.

9.5    No Reprisals. No reprisals of any kind will be taken by either party against any person

for participation or non-participation in this process.

9.6    Miscellaneous

9.6.1    As necessary, a JCTA representative will be released without loss of pay to attend
a grievance meeting or hearing. Any unit member who is requested to appear as
witnesses will be accorded the same right.

9.6.2    All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a
grievance will be filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in the
personnel file of any of the participants.

9.6.3    Upon mutual agreement of JCTA and the Administration, any grievance may be
taken directly to the Board.

9.6.4    Upon mutual agreement of JCTA and the Board, any grievance may be taken
directly to arbitration.

8

9.6.5 A unit member may at any time, present grievances to the employer, and have
such grievances resolved, without the intervention of JCTA, as long as the
resolved is consistent with the terms of this Agreement. If an employee presents a
grievance on her/his own behalf, JCTA shall have the right to be present and state
its views at all grievance meetings. The Administrator or Board shall not agree to
resolution of the grievance until JCTA has received a copy of the grievance and
the proposed resolution and has been given opportunity to file a response.

9.7 Expedited Arbitration. At the option of JCTA or the District, the arbitration may be held
under the Expedited Rules of the American Arbitration Association. Notice of such
option shall accompany the request for a list of arbitrators.

ARTICLE 10
DUES DEDUCTION

10.1    The JCTA shall have the exclusive right of payroll deduction for payment of
organizational dues. Members who currently have authorization cards on file need not be
resolicited. JCTA dues and fees, upon formal written request from JCTA to the District,
shall be increased or decreased without resolicitation and authorization from unit

members.

10.2    Upon authorization from the unit member, the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of
the Association dues and fees from the regular salary check each month. Deductions for
unit members who sign such authorization after the start of the school year shall be
appropriately prorated to complete the payment by the end of the school year.

10.3    By the end of each month, the District will remit such moneys to the Association, and
provide an alphabetical list of all unit members for whom dues have been deducted,
stating the amount so deducted and identifying any changes that may have occurred from
the previous month’s list.

ARTICLE 11
ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

11.1 Mail Facilities. JCTA shall have the right to use the District mail service and unit

member mail boxed for communication to unit members without interference, censorship,
or examination of such communications by the employer. Materials which are sealed and
marked “confidential” will be treated as confidential communications.

11.2    Bulletin Boards. JCTA shall have the right to post notices of activities and matter of
Association concern on Association bulletin boards located in the staff work room and
the lounge.

11.3    Use of Building and Equipment. JCTA shall have the right to use school facilities and
equipment, without charge, except for materials used, during all reasonable hours for
meetings and other Association activities. Normal scheduling procedures will be
followed.

11.4    Access to Work Site. Authorized representatives of the JCTA/CTA/NEA shall have the
rights to transact official Association business on school property and utilize District
facilities at all reasonable times, provided that such activities do not interfere with
classroom instruction, performance of employee duties, or other previously scheduled
uses of school facilities.

11.5    Access to Information. The Board, upon written request from JCTA, agrees to furnish
the Association, within five (5) days, all available documents concerning the financial
resources and professional staffing of the district. In addition, the District, upon request,
agrees to provide any other appropriate information that the JCTA deems necessary to
fulfill its role as exclusive representative. If the District is unable to meet the time lines,
they will notify the Association in writing when the information will be available.

11.6    Appointment to District Committees. JCTA shall select voluntary teacher representatives
to the following:

-    School Site Council

-    PAR/Staff Development Committee

-    District Advisory Committee

-    Charter District Review Committee

-    Any ad hoc committees establish by the district or any other committee

established under the Education Code.

11.7    Association Business. The Association President or his/her designee shall be provided a
maximum of three (3) days release time to transact Association business at no loss of
salary or other benefits for the duration of this Agreement. The cost of the substitute
shall be borne by the Association.

11.8    School Board Agenda. JCTA shall have a regular time for general communication on the
School Board agenda.

JCTA may request that a specific item be placed on the School Board agenda for a
regular meeting. The request must be written form, and must be submitted at least six (6)
working days before the regular meeting of the Board.

Every unit member retains the right to make comments during the public or community
comments portion of any Board meeting.

ARTICLE 12

REASSIGNMENTS

12.1    General Principle. The parties believe in encouraging voluntary transfers, while
recognizing to need for a teacher to experience an assignment for a least a few
consecutive years to develop their program.

12.2    Definitions

12.2.1    A reassignment is the movement of a probationary or permanent unit member
from one subject area to another subject area or one grade level to another grade
level at the same work location.

12.2.2    A vacancy is any position not held by a unit member which the Board seeks to
fill.

12.2.3    Seniority is defined as the unit member’s initial dates of service in the bargaining
unit with the following provisions.

12.2.3.1    If a unit member is assigned to a non-bargaining unit position
within the District, that unit member does not accrue seniority for
the purposes of this Article while working on that assignment.

12.2.3.2    A unit member’s seniority shall accrue during layoff.

12.2.3.3    All other qualifications being equal, decisions on notice of filling
assignments, vacancies, on filling voluntary assignments or
imposing all involuntary reassignments shall be based on seniority.

12.3    Notice of Assignment

12.3.1 Each unit member shall be given written notice not later than May 25th of the next
year’s assignment. Such notice shall specify the position, grade level and/or
subject area to which the unit member will be assigned. This assignment shall be
firm barring major unforeseen or contingent circumstance. (Under “contingent
circumstance” for example, a teacher may be told they will teach kindergarten
unless there are not a specified number of kindergarten students, in which case the
assignment will be a K-l combination.) Should either of these circumstances
occur, the District shall confer with the teacher involved and the Association,
giving the reasons, in writing, for the proposed change. An attempt will be made
to find alternative solutions. All this will occur at the earliest reasonable date
after the District is aware of the problem.

11

12.3.3 Except for voluntary or involuntary reassignments, the District will attempt to

maintain teachers in the same assignment under paragraph 12.3.1 for at least two
and one-half full years. A change in assignment in a shorter period may occur as
a result of, but is not limited to, the need to shift teachers following a change in
the school’s grade level structure from one year to the next or the return of a
teacher from a leave. If this occurs, volunteers will be solicited by posting before
any reassignments are made.

If a teacher is to be moved more than once in a two and one-half year period ,
prior to May 20th an administrator, board member(s), the affected teacher(s) and
the Association will meet at the request of any of the aforementioned participants
to discuss the issue in a good-faith attempt to reach resolution. A real effort will
be made to keep reassignments to a minimum and to distribute reassignments
equitably among the staff, considering the nature of assignment, the interests of
the affected unit members, and, in particular, the seniority of the unit members (if
the least senior member has been moved in the past two and on-half years, the
more senior unit members will be considered).

12.4 Vacancies

12.4.1    The Administrator shall deliver to the Association and post a list of vacancies and
new positions that occur during the school year and for the following year upon
knowledge of the vacancies, including the following:

12.4.1.1    A closing date of at least ten (10) working days following the
posting date. In emergencies this date may be waived by a joint
decision of the Administrator and the Association. If this
emergency occurs outside of the regular school year, this decision
may be made, jointly, by the Administrator and two of the five
designees, authorized for this purpose. The Association will
provide the Administrator with a list of the currently authorized
representatives.

12.4.1.2    A job description.

12.4.1.3    The qualifications necessary to meet the requirements of the
position.

12.4.2    No assignment to fill the vacancy or newly created position shall be made until
the closing date.

12.4.3    No outside applicant shall be selected to fill the vacancy or newly created position
if there is a qualified unit member applicant.

12

12.4.4    If a unit member’s request for reassignment is denied, the unit member shall be
entitled to a meeting with the Administrator and shall receive written reasons for
the denial within five (5) days following said meeting, and copy shall be sent to
the Association.

12.4.5    The District shall notify each unit member during summer recess or period of
leave of any posted openings which may arise during summer recess or period of
leave. This notification shall be made by regular mail. If a waiver has been
granted to shorten the closing date time the District will attempt to notify each
Association member by telephone, followed by notification by regular mail.

12.5    Voluntary Reassignment

12.5.1    A unit member may submit a request for a reassignment (including the
reassignment of certificated support staff, such as the music teacher, reading specialist,
etc.) To the District at any time, whether or not a vacancy exists. A unit member may
also submit a request for a reassignment subsequent to the posting of a vacancy notice
pursuant to this Article.

12.5.2    If a unit member’s request for reassignment is denied, the unit member shall be
entitled to a meeting with the Administrator to discuss the reasons. The unit
member may request and shall receive written reasons for the denial within five

(5) days following said meeting, and a copy shall be sent to the Association.

12.5.3    A reassignment request shall not be denied arbitrarily or without basis in fact.

12.5.4    Unit members returning from leave shall be afforded all rights provided under this
section.

12.6    Involuntary Reassignment Support

12.6.1    Involuntary reassignments are made fill a vacancy from within after the start of
the school year.

12.6.2    The District shall seek and consider volunteers prior to making an involuntary
reassignment. The posting process of a vacancy shall be pursuant to this article.

12.6.3    Involuntary reassignment shall not be made arbitrary or without a basis in fact.

12.7    Reassignment Support

12.7.1 Unit members who are reassigned within the current school year shall be allowed,
as needed by the teachers, up to five (5) days of release time for preparation prior
to the effective date of the reassignment.

13

12.7.2    The District shall provide assistance in moving of the unit member’s material
whenever a unit member is reassigned.

12.7.3    Unit members returning from leave shall be afforded all rights provided under this
section.

ARTICLE 14
EVALUATION

14.1 Evaluation Procedure

14.1.1    Probationary and temporary unit members shall be evaluated each school year.
Permanent unit members shall be evaluated every other year. Unit members with
permanent status who have been employed at least ten (10) years with the school
district, are highly qualified, as defined in twenty (20) U.C.S. Sec. 7801 (ESEA),
and whose previous evaluation rated the employee as meeting or exceeding
standards, shall be evaluated every five years if the unit member and evaluator
consent to this schedule. Should the evaluator withdraw consent, notice and
identifiable cause shall be provided to the unit member in a timely manner.
Classroom observation and conferences during the off year is encouraged and
welcomed.

14.1.2    Unit members to be evaluated during a particular school year shall be furnished a
copy of the evaluation procedures, advised of the criteria upon which the
evaluation is to be based. The evaluator is the Principal/Superintendent. See
appendix for form D.

14.1.3    The unit member being evaluated and the evaluator shall meet prior to the
evaluation to discuss:

14

14.1.3.1    Yearly goals (as related to professional standards).

14.1.3.2    A schedule of observations, conferences and final evaluation date.
In the event of a disagreement over the objectives, standards and/or
evaluation schedule, the unit member and the evaluator shall:

14.1.3.2.1    Make a good-faith effort to resolve the differences
themselves.

14.1.3.2.2    If the differences are not resolved, each shall have
the opportunity to state their position on the
matter(s) in dispute, and to have a written statement
attached to the evaluation form.

14.1.3.3    The unit member shall have the right to identify any constraints
which the unit member believes may inhibit his/her ability to meet
the objectives and standards established.

14.1.4    During the course of the evaluation, circumstances may change which

require modification of the original yearly goals. The unit member may
initiate a change of these objectives and standards in the manner described
in section 14.1.3.

14.1.5 The evaluation process shall include the following activities:

14.1.5.1    Classroom observation shall last at least thirty (30) minutes; shall be made
known to the unit member at least two (2) days prior to their occurrence;
and shall be followed by an evaluation conference within three (3) days of
the observation. A written report will be generated prior to the conference
as a result of that observation, and discussed at that conference. During
the conference this report will be discussed, and if necessary amended.

14.1.5.2    In preparing, the final evaluation form for placement in the unit member’s
personnel file, the evaluator shall rely primary upon data collected through
classroom observation and conferences. Any deficiencies which may have
been brought to the attention of the unit member, and subsequently
corrected, shall not be included in the final evaluation form. No negative
evaluation may be included that was not noted on observation reports.

14.1.5.3    The evaluation process may also include informal visits and “walk
throughs” on an unscheduled basis.

15

14.1.5.4    A unit member shall not be evaluated on or held accountable for any
aspect of the educational program over which the unit member has no
authority or ability to correct.

14.1.5.5    A final evaluation conference between the unit member and evaluator
shall be held no later than thirty (30) days, as provided by Education Code
44663, prior to the end of the school year to discuss the content of the final
evaluation form. In the event the unit member disputes the content, the
unit member may prepare a written statement which shall be attached and
incorporated into the final evaluation. The final evaluation shall contain
three ratings: satisfactory, needs improvement, and unsatisfactory.

14.1.5.6    Unit members shall not be required to participate in the evaluation(s)
and/or observations of other unit members.

14.1.5.7

The evaluation of unit members, pursuant to this Article, shall not include
or be based upon the following:

14.1.5.7.1    Standardized achievement test results.

14.1.5.7.2    Results of any utilized for the purpose of a School
Improvement Plan.

14.1.5.7.3    Achievement of objectives stated in Individual Education
Programs (IEPs) of special education students.

14.1.5.7.4    Utilization of any specific teaching techniques (ex:
Discovery method. Cooperative learning, 7 Step, Target
Teaching) unless agreed to by the unit member being
evaluated.

14.1.5.7.5 The success, or lack thereof, of an instructor or clerical aide
in the performance of tasks assigned by the unit member.

14.1.6 The evaluation and observation forms shall be mutually agreed upon by the staff
and Principal/Superintendent (Appendix D).

14.2 Personnel Files

14.2.1    There shall be a single personnel file for each unit member. Personnel files shall
be kept in the central administrative office of the District.

14.2.2    Materials in the personnel file of a unit member shall be made available for
inspection by the unit member involved. Upon authorization by the unit member,
an Association representatives may review the unit member’s file or accompany

16

the unit member in his/her review of the file. Unit members shall have the right
to inspect and obtain a copy of personnel file materials, upon request.

14.2.3    Information of a derogatory nature shall not be entered or filed unless and until
the unit member is given notice and opportunity to review and comment thereon.
A unit member shall have the right to enter, and have attached to any such
derogatory statement, their own comments thereon. Such review, and any
preparation of comments in response to the material and/or statement, shall take
place during normal business hours, and the unit member shall be released from
duty for this purpose without salary reduction.

If the unit member alleges that the material is false and/or hearsay, a personnel
session before the Board may be initiated within 30 days to determine the validity

14.3 Personnel and academic Freedom

14.3.1 It is the policy of the District that all instruction shall be fair, accurate, objective,

community needs and the needs and the needs and values of our diverse cultures
and heritages. Academic freedom is essential to the fulfillment of this policy and
the District acknowledges the fundamental need to protect unit members from any
censorship or restraint which might interfere with the unit member’s obligation to
pursue truth in performance of their teaching functions. Accordingly:

14.3.1.1 A unit member shall have reasonable freedom in the classroom
presentations and discussions and may introduce political.

17

religious, or otherwise controversial materials, provided that said

14.3.1.2 In performing teaching functions, unit members shall have

views.

14.3.2    The personal life of a unit member is not an appropriate concern of the District for
the purpose of evaluation or disciplinary action unless it prevents the unit member
from performing his/her assigned duties.

14.3.3    A unit member shall be entitled full rights of citizenship, and no religious or

of evaluation or disciplinary action unless said activities violate local, state, or

ARTICLE 16
PUBLIC CHARGES

16.1 Parents or guardians of pupils enrolled in the District may be present oral and/or written
complaints regarding teacher. Every effort should be made to resolve complaints at the
earliest possible step. No record of any complaint shall be placed in the personnel file
nor disciplinary action taken against any member of the bargaining unit unless the
following procedures have been followed and the complaint results in grounds for
employee discipline:

16.1.1    Step One- Informal (oral) Complaints. Any complaint to the Administrator shall
be reported to the teacher by the Administrator receiving the complaint within
four (4) days of receipt of the complaint. Complainants will be encouraged to
attempt to orally resolve concerns with the teacher personally.

16.1.2    If the complainant, the teacher, the Administrator or the Association believes the
allegations warrant a meeting, the Administrator shall schedule a meeting to
resolve the complaint. A representative of the unit member’s choice may be
present at the meeting.

20

16.1.3 If the complainant does not attend the mutually agreed upon meeting, the matter
will be dropped and the complaint shall not be used by the District in any
evaluation or disciplinary action against the teacher. Given unforeseen
circumstances, the meeting may be rescheduled on a mutually agreed date.

16.2 Step 2 - Formal (written) Complaint

16.2.1    If the matter is not resolved at the meeting to the satisfaction of the complainant
within ten (10) days of the meeting, the complaint may be put in writing and the
original submitted to the teacher with a copy to the Administrator. If no written
complaint is made the complaint is dropped.

16.2.2    A written complaint must include the name of the teacher involved and a brief but
specific summary of the complaint and the facts surrounding it. It must also
include a specific description of the prior attempt to resolve the complaint with
the teacher involved.

16.2.3    If the complaint remains unresolved after review by the Administrator the unit
member may appeal the matter to the Board or the Administrator may transfer the
matter to the Board.

16.2.4 No party to a complaint may address the Board, either in closed or open session,
unless the Board has received the Administrator’s written report concerning the
complaint. The report shall contain, but not be limited to:

a. The name of the unit involved.

b.    A brief but specific summary of the complaint and the facts
surrounding it, sufficient to inform the Board and the unit
member(s) of the precise nature of the complaint, and to allow unit
member(s) to prepare a defense.

c.    A copy of the signed original complaint.

d.    A summary of the action taken by the Administrator.

16.2.5 All parties to the complaint, including the Administrator, may be asked to attend a
Board meeting or part of such meeting for the purpose of presenting all available
evidence and allowing every opportunity for explaining and clarifying the issue.

All complaints shall be addressed in a closed session of the Board unless the unit
member requests that the issue be addressed in open session.

21

16.2.6    The unit member shall receive up to one-half day of release time for the
reviewing, dating and initialing of the written complaint and the preparation of a
response. This response shall be attached to the complaint.

16.2.7    Complaints which are withdrawn, shown to be false, or are not determined to be
grounds for employee discipline shall neither be placed in the teacher’s personnel
file nor utilized in any evaluation or disciplinary action against the teacher, and
shall be destroyed.
