ARTICLE I

AGREEMENT

1.0    The articles and provisions contained herein constitute a binding agreement (“Agreement”) by and
between the Governing Board of the Firebaugh-Las Deltas Unified School District (“Board”) and the
FLDUTA/CTA/NEA (“Association”), the employee organization.

2.0    This agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-3549 of the Government
Code (“Act”).

3.0    This agreement will remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2016

ARTICLE II
RECOGNITION

1.0    The Board recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of all full time regular and
probationary certificated employees, excluding District Superintendent, Assistant Superintendent,
Principals/Vice-Principals/Learning Directors, Counselors, Speech Therapists, and Director of
Student Services, Director of Health Services, Director of Educational Technology, Supervisory
Employees, and Management/Confidential Employees for the purpose of meeting and negotiating.

2.0    The Association in turn, recognizes the Board as the duly elected representative of the people and
agrees to negotiate exclusively with the Board through the provisions of the Rodda Act. The
Association further agrees that it, its members and agents shall not attempt to negotiate privately
or individually with any Board Member or manager,

3.0    New Classifications created or positions added to classes shall be subject to negotiations between
the Board and the Association to determine if they are to be included in the bargaining unit.
Disputed cases shall be submitted to the PERB and shall not be subjected to the grievance
procedure contained in this contract.

4.0    The Board recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative for the unit of employees
recognized by the Board at the Regular Board Meeting of May 18, 1976, and the recognition
agreement dated May 18, 1976, which is incorporated in this agreement as Appendix A.

ARTICLE IV

NEGOTIATION PROCEDURES

1.0    No earlier than March 1 of the calendar year and after compliance with Section 3547 of the
Governing Code in which this agreement expires, the Board Representative shall commence to
meet and negotiate in good faith with the Association on negotiable items provided that the
Association has presented its negotiable agreement in keeping with the conditions of the Rodda
Act. Meetings will be held within fifteen (15) business days from receipt of a written request.

2.0    The Board and Association agree to meet and negotiate commencing at 1:00 P.M. of each mutually
agreed upon working day in order to negotiate, reach agreement and reduce to writing the various
issues contained within the scope of negotiations as defined in the California Rodda Act.
Agreement reached by negotiations shall be subject to ratification by the Board and Association at
the earliest opportunity subsequent to agreement but in no case later than fifteen days subsequent
to agreement.

3.0    Each party may have present at negotiating sessions not more than two person acting as
consultants. If the chief negotiator for either party is other than an employee, the party must
certify' before the commencement of negotiations that the chief negotiator acts as spokesman. The
chief negotiator shall not transfer authority except in case of emergency.

4.0    It is agreed by both parties that agreement will be made by the end of each bargaining session on
the agenda for the following bargaining session. Schedule of date and time for the following
meeting or meetings will be made at the beginning of each negotiating session.

5.0    The District agrees to provide paid release time as set forth below for no more than five members
of the Firebaugh-Las Deltas Unified School District Teachers Association Collective Bargaining
Team.

6.0    The District agrees to commence meetings at 1:00 P.M. and pay employees for the released time
between 1:00 and 4:00 P.M. so long as the number of meetings does not exceed two per week. At
such times when it is mutually agreed that more than two meetings per week are necessary, the
meetings will commence subsequent to 4:00 P.M. The District and the Association agree to
commence meetings at mutually acceptable times if the negotiation process extends beyond the
regular school teaching year.

7.0    Members of the Firebaugh-Las Deltas Unified School District Teachers association Bargaining
Team shall be responsible for notifying their immediate supervisor of meeting times and dates and
requesting release time no later than the beginning of the work day following the scheduling of the
meeting.

8.0    The Board and Association agree that the persons acting as chief negotiators for the respective
parties shall carry the full authority of the respective party and shall be certified to make
proposals and counterproposals and to sign a tentative agreement or full contractual agreement.

9.0    Each party’s group or team of negotiation representatives shall consist of not more than five
members. Reasonable released time is defined as the number of hours remaining in the teacher
work day subsequent to 1:00 P.M. of each work day or during regular teaching hours.

ARTICLE V
COMPLETION OF MEET AND NEGOTIATION

1.0    During the term of this Agreement, the District and the Association expressly waives and
relinquishes the right to meet and negotiate and agrees that neither party shall be obligated to
meet and negotiate with respect to any subject or matter whether referred to or covered in this
Agreement or not, even though each subject or matter may not have been within the knowledge or
contemplation of either or both the District or the Association at the time they met and negotiated
on and executed this Agreement, and even though such subjects or matters were proposed and
later withdrawn.

2.0    This agreement shall supersede any rules, regulations or practices of the Board which shall be
contrary to or inconsistent with its terms. The provisions of the Agreement shall be considered
part of the established policies of the Board.

3.0    This Agreement shall constitute the full and complete commitment between both parties and shall
supersede and cancel all previous agreements both written and oral. This Agreement may be
altered, changed, added to, deleted from or modified only through the voluntary, mutual consent of
the parties in a written and signed amendment to this Agreement.

ARTICLE VI

ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

1.0    The Board authorizes the Association to use the District’s facilities and buildings at times other
than hours of student instruction as long as the Association submits the appropriate Civic Center
Act form to the immediate supervisor of the facility or building. In emergencies the Board may
authorized the Association to use the District’s facilities and buildings during normal working
hours as long as the Association declares in writing that the use of such facilities and buildings
constitute an emergency and as long as the use of such buildings and facilities does not interfere
with the instructional program.

2.0    The immediate supervisor of the facility or building may grant the Association use of District
equipment as long as such use is in accordance with the procedures provided for in the Civic
Center Act and as long as the use of such equipment does not interfere with the normal student
instruction or work production of the District. The Association shall pay for the cost of all
materials and supplies incident to each use.

3.0    The Association agrees to leave facilities, buildings and/or equipment used in a clean and orderly
condition.

4.0    The Association shall use designated District bulletin boards located in the Faculty Room of each
school for posted communications to its members. District mail boxes may also be used for
communications. Each piece of material distributed or posted shall be dated and bear the name

5.0    All Association communications to employees other than notices of meetings must be submitted to
the Superintendent’s Office on the same day of distribution to the staff.

6.0    The Association agrees that its officials, including Building Representatives, shall not
communicate with employees during regular teaching hours and shall conduct normal Association
business at times other than the respective employees’ teaching hours. Association business will
be permissible following faculty meetings.

7.0    Association meetings shall not commence until the end of the school day for students.

8.0    The Board agrees to provide a paid leave of absence not to exceed three days per year for one
officer of the Association for the purpose of attending the Association annual conference.

9.0    The Board agrees to post Board Meeting Agendas on District bulletin boards located in the Faculty
Room of each school not less than 24 hours prior to each Board Meeting. The Board further agrees

Meeting.

the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues from the regular salary check of the unit
member each month for ten (10) months. Deductions for unit members who sign such
authorization after the commencement of the school year shall be appropriately prorated to
complete payments by the end of the school year.

1.0 PERSONAL ILLNESS AND INJURY LEAVE

1.1    Accumulation - Sick Leave

Members of the bargaining unit employed five days a week shall be entitled to ten days
leave of absence for illness or injury for a year of service. Members of the bargaining unit
employed less than five days a week for a year of service, shall be entitled to that proportion
of ten days’ leave of absence for illness or injury as the number of days of employment per
week bears to five. The sick leave entitlement for the year shall be credited to the member
of the bargaining unit at the beginning of the year. Unused sick leave shall be accumulated
from year to year,

1.2    Eligibility - Sick Leave

To be eligible to apply for sick leave absence with pay, the employee shall be in a paid
status and scheduled for work on the day(s) of absence.

1.3    Use of Unearned Sick Leave

If an employee resigns, retires, or is terminated and has used more sick leave than was
earned, the amount used but not earned shall be deducted from the final warrant of the
employee.

1.4    Request for Paid Sick Leave Absence

Members of the bargaining unit must notify the District of absence as soon as the necessity
to be absent becomes known to the employee but in no instance later than 7:00 A.M. of the
day of the absence.

charged to sick leave in the same manner as other illness absences.

physician certifies that the employee is temporarily disabled and must return to
work not later than the beginning of the week following the physician’s verification
that the employee is released to return to duty. An employee who wishes to remain
absent from duties beyond, the period of disability may request an unpaid child
rearing leave.

8.0    INDUSTRIAL ACCIDENT AND ILLNESS LEAVE

8.1    Members or the bargaining unit who sustain an injury or illness arising directly out of and in
the course and scope of their employment shall be eligible for a maximum of sixty (60) working
days paid leave in any one fiscal year. This leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

8.2    Payment for wages lost on any day shall not, when added to an award granted under the

Industrial accident and illness leave shall be reduced by one day for each day of authorized
absence, regardless of a compensation award made under the workers’ compensation. When
an industrial accident or illness leave occurs at a time when the leave will overlap into the
next fiscal year, the employee shall be entitled to only that amount of leave remaining at the
end of the fiscal year, in which the industrial injury or illness occurred, for the same illness or
injury.

8.3    Industrial accident or illness leave is to be used in lieu of normal sick leave benefits. When
entitlement to industrial accident or illness leave under this section has been exhausted,
entitlement to other sick leave will be used. If, however, the employee continues to receive
temporary disability indemnity under the Worker’s Compensation laws of this state at the
time of the exhaustion of benefits under this section, the employee may elect to take as much
of the employee’s accumulated and available sick leave, which, when added to the Workers’
Compensation award, results in a payment of not more than the employees regular salary.

comply with the medical verification and reporting provisions of the sick leave section (1) of
this Article.

authorization to return to work, an employee on Industrial Accident or IllnesB leave may be
reinstated in a position in the same class without loss of status or benefits.

10.0    SABBATICAL LEAVE

10.1    Purpose

10.1.1    Sabbatical leaves are intended for certificated personnel who have as their primary

10.2    Eligibility

10.2.1    A certificated employee is eligible to apply who served the Firebaugh-Las Deltas
Unified School District as a full-time employee for seven (7) consecutive years and
has not yet reached his sixty first birthday before July 1st. An employee is to be given
only one sabbatical leave during each seven (7) year period.

10.3    Extent and Distribution of Leaves

10.3.1    The number of employees absent on sabbatical leave, at one time, shall not exceed
two percent (2%) (to the nearest whole number) of the total number of certificated
employees in the bargaining unit.

10.3.1    Priority shall be given according to seniority, whether previous sabbatical leave has
been granted, order of application, and unusual circumstances such as research
projects, grants, etc.

procedure.

10.4.2    Applications for sabbatical leave must be submitted (in triplicate) to the principal
and shall include a full statement of the purposes (study, travel, and research),
expected benefits and outcomes, and plans for use of leave. The principal will
forward the application to the Superintendent.

ARTICLE IX

TRANSFERS

1.0    A transfer is defined as a change from one (1) school to another.

1.1 Certificated positions will be posted and tenured teachers will be given first consideration
when adjusting schedules. If the tenured teacher disagrees with the transfer, an informal
hearing will take place between all interested parties.

2.0    Voluntary Transfers

2.1

Requests for transfer shall be made in writing by the teacher on or before March 15th. Such
requests represent consent to transfer and may be acted upon without further consultation
with the teacher. As a matter of routine, identified professional staff vacancies will be
posted at the various schools in the District and the District Office, and a notice of vacancies
during the summer months shall be sent to the Association and interested individuals who
requested voluntary transfer.

2.2

The Superintendent, or his/her designated representative, will give consideration to the
preference requested but may deny transfer if, in his/her opinion, it is in the best interest of
the District.

2.3

Upon request of applicant, verbal reasons for denial of a transfer shall be supplied to
him/her.

3.0 Involuntary Transfer

3.1

The District reserves the right to transfer certificated staff to meet the needs of the District.

3.2

A teacher reassigned to a newly-formed class, or a class for which advance planning and
preparation was not made, after the first teaching day of a school year shall be allowed
three (3) days (teaching days) to prepare for the new assignment. The reassigned teacher
shall have the support of a substitute teacher during the first three (3) instructional days of
service in the new assignment. During the same three (3) days, the newly-assigned teacher
shall have the right to leave the classroom as he/she deems necessary, not to exceed 50% of
the day.

3.3

The District shall provide assistance in moving the transferred teacher's personal teaching
materials to the new location.

3.4

Before the implementation of an involuntary transfer, the Superintendent, or his/her
designated representative, shall meet with the teacher being transferred to review needs
and reasons for such transfer. Upon written request of transferred teacher, written reasons
shall be supplied to him/her.

CERTIFICATED EMPLOYEE EVALUATION PROCEDURES

1.0 These provisions constitute the procedures to be utilized for the evaluation and assessment of the
instructional performance of certificated employees as set forth in California Education Code Section
44660, et. seq., or its successor, commonly referred to as the “Stull Bill.”

1.1    Any established standards of expected student achievement adopted by the Board at each
grade level in each area of study shall be utilized in the evaluation process.

1.2    The evaluation shall relate to:

1.2.1    The progress of pupils toward the Board’s established standards.

1.2.2    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the employee.

1.2.3    The employee’s adherence to curricular objectives.

1.2.4    The performance of those non-instructional duties and responsibilities including
supervisory and advisory duties, as prescribed by the Board.

1.2.5    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment within the
scope of the employee’s responsibility.

1.3    The assessment of an employee’s performance shall be made on the Summary Evaluation
Form, attached as Appendix C to this Agreement.

1.4    Probationary employee evaluations shall be made on a continuing basis at least once each
school year and shall be concluded by not later than thirty (30) days before the last school day
scheduled on the school calendar, except for any remediation processes and procedures.

1.4.1    An evaluation containing an “unsatisfactory” rating shall be given to the employee not
later than sixty (60) days before the last school day, absent unusual circumstances.

1.5    Permanent employee evaluations, except for certain employees with ten (10) or more year’s
experience as set forth in section 1.5.2 below, shall be made on a continuing basis at least once
every other school year and concluded by not later than thirty (30) days before the last school
day scheduled on the school calendar, except for any remediation processes and procedures.

1.5.1    An evaluation containing an “unsatisfactory” rating shall be given to the employee not
later than sixty (60) days before the last school day, absent unusual circumstances.

1.5.2    Permanent Employees with at least ten years of experience in Firebaugh-Las Deltas
Unified School District and who have evaluations that Meets or Exceeds Standards
may be evaluated using the self-directed evaluation, as attached hereto, not less than
once every five (5) years. Permanent unit members receiving an Unsatisfactory or
Needs Improvement shall be evaluated at least once every year until they achieve a
Meets or Exceeds Standards, or unless further evaluation will not be productive.

of each certificated employee in the school. Except as provided below or due to unforeseen
circumstances which the chief site administrator determines constitutes a reason to change
evaluators, the person who meets with the employee for the pre-observation conference shall be

ARTICLE XVIII

GRIEVANCE

SECTION 1.0 - Definition of a Grievance

A grievance is a formal written allegation by a grievant that he/she has been adversely
affected by a grievant that he/she has been adversely affected by a violation, misapplication
or misinterpretation of the specific provisions of this Agreement. Actions to challenge or
change the policies of the District as set forth in the rules and regulations or administrative
regulations and procedures must be undertaken under separate legal processes. Other
matters for which a specific method of review is provided by law, by the rules and regulations
of the Board of Education, or by the administrative regulations and procedures of this school
district, are not within the scope of this procedure.

SECTION 2.0 - Information Level

Before filing a grievance, the grieved person will first discuss the potential grievance with the
immediate supervisor within ten business days after the occurrence giving rise to the grievance,
with the objectives of resolving the matter informally.

SECTION 3.0 - Formal Level

Level 3.1

3.1.1    Within ten (10) business days after the informal conference, if no agreement is reached, the
grievant must present his/her grievance in writing on the appropriate form to his/her immediate
supervisor.

This statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the circumstances
involved, the decision rendered at the informal conference and the specific remedy sought.

3.1.2    The supervisor shall communicate his/her decision to the employee in writing within ten
(10) business days after receiving the grievance. If the supervisor does not respond within the time
limits, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

3.1.3    Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal conference.

Level 3.2

3.2.1 If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I, he/she may within five (5)
business days, appeal the decision on the appropriate form to the Superintendent or his/her
designee. Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal conference.

3.2.2 The Superintendent or his/her designee shall communicate his/her decision in writing to
the grievant within five (5) business days. If the Superintendent or his/her designee does not respond
within the time limits provided, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

Level 3.3

3.3.1 If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level II, he may within five (5) business
days appeal the decision on the appropriate form to the Board of Trustees. The statement shall
include a copy of the original grievance and appeal, the decisions rendered and a clear, concise
statement of the reasons for the appeal.

32

3.3.2    The Trustees will review the statements of the parties involved in the grievance as well as
the records presented at each level of the grievance procedure. If, upon review, the Trustees find
they are unable to render a final determination on the records presented, the Trustees may reopen
for the taking of additional evidence.

3.3.3    The trustees will communicate their decision in writing to the grievant within thirty (30)
business days.

3.3.4Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal conference.

Level 3.4

3.4.1The grievant may appeal the Board’s decision for a final and binding determination. The
grievant must notify the Board within thirty (30) days that he/she is appealing to the courts.

4.0 Miscellaneous

4.1 A teacher may be represented at all stages of grievance procedure by himself /herself or, at
his/her option (except at the informal level) by the Association.

4.2    When it is necessary for a representative of the Association to attend a grievance
