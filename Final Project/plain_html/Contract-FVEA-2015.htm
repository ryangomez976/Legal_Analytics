ARTICLE II:

DISTRICT RIGHTS

A POWERS

It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority to
direct, manage, and control to the full extent of the law. Included in, but not limited to,
those duties and powers are the exclusive right to: determine its organization; direct the
work of its Bargaining Unit Members; determine the times and hours of operation;
determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided, and the methods and means of
providing them; establish its educational policies, goals, and objectives; insure the rights
and educational opportunities of students; determine staffing patterns; determine the
number and kinds of personnel required; maintain the efficiency of District operations;
determine the curriculum, build, move, or modify facilities; establish budget procedures
and determine budgetary allocation; determine class size; determine the methods of
raising revenue; contract out work; and take action on any matter in the event of an
emergency. In addition, the Board retains the right to hire, classify, assign, transfer,
evaluate, promote, terminate, and discipline Bargaining Unit Members; however, any
suspension of a Bargaining Unit Member shall be in accordance with the provisions of
Education Code Section 44934.

B EXERCISE OF POWERS

The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the
District, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations, and practices in furtherance thereof,
and the use of judgment and discretion in connection therewith shall be limited only by
the specific and express terms of this agreement, and then only to the extent such specific
and express terms are in conformance with law.

C DISTRICT RIGHTS TO AMEND, MODIFY, OR RESCIND

The District retains its right to amend, modify or rescind policies and practices referred to
in this Agreement as they are necessary to meet the needs of an emergency. These
amendments, modifications, or rescissions shall continue until the emergency is deemed
by the District to be terminated. During emergency situations (exclusive of concerted
activities) the Bargaining Unit Member shall not be required to perform services that will
directly cause serious bodily injury. The determination of whether or not an emergency
exists is solely within the good faith discretion of the Superintendent or designee, and is
expressly excluded from the provisions of Article m. In the event of an emergency, the
Superintendent or designee will inform the Board of Trustees, Management Team
members, the Association, and the newspapers. (Emergency herein shall be defined
according to Black's Law Dictionary revised 4th Edition, page 615.)

ARTICLE TTT:

GRIEVANCE

A DEFINITIONS

1.    A "grievance" is a formal written allegation that the grievant has been adversely
affected by a violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the provisions of
this Agreement. (If the allegation is that a District Policy, a District
Administrative Procedure, or a section of the Education Code have been violated,
misinterpreted, or misapplied, see Problem Solving Procedure.)

2.    A "grievant" may be any member of the bargaining unit represented by the
Association or the Association itself under the following conditions:

a.    One conferee may be selected by the Association to assist in presenting
and processing the grievance.

b.    The Association shall observe the time limits as if it were the original

grievant.

3.    "Conferee" is the individual selected by the grievant to assist him/her in
presenting and processing the grievance.

4.    A "day" is any day on which the central administrative office of the Fountain
Valley School District is open for business except winter, spring, and summer
recesses.

5.    The "immediate supervisor" is the lowest level administrator having jurisdiction
over the grievant and who has the authority to resolve the particular grievance. If
a Bargaining Unit Member initiates the grievance with an individual other than
the site administrator, when in fact the site administrator/supervisor has the
authority to resolve the grievance, then that individual shall advise the grievant to
re file the grievance with the site administrator/supervisor.

B    TIME LIMITS

Within thirty (30) days after the occurrence of the act of omission or when discovery of
said act or omission giving rise to the grievance could have occurred the grievant shall
present the grievance in writing on the appropriate form to the lowest level supervisor
who can resolve the grievance.

C    INFORMAL CONFERENCE

Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant should attempt to resolve it by an
informal conference with the immediate supervisor.

3

D FORMAL

1. Level I

a.    Following the time specification under Section B of this Article, the
grievant shall present the grievance in writing on the appropriate form to
the immediate supervisor. This statement shall be a clear, concise
'statement of the grievance, citing the specific section of this Agreement
allegedly to have been violated and/or misinterpreted and/or misapplied,
the circumstances involved, the decision, if any, rendered at the informal
conference, the specific remedy sought.

b.    The supervisor shall communicate the decision to the Bargaining Unit
Member in writing within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance. If
the supervisor does not respond within the time limits, all fees and
expenses of the arbitrator and hearing shall be borne by the District. If the
grievant or Association does not respond within the time limits, the
grievance will be dropped.

c.    Within the above time limits either the grievant or the supervisor may
request, and shall be granted a personal conference with the other party.

2. Level II

a.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I, the grievant
may, within ten (10) days, appeal the decision on the appropriate form to
the Superintendent or designee. If the grievance was filed with the
Superintendent or designee at Level I, then it is understood that Level II
can be waived. The grievant may proceed to Level in.

b.    This statement shall include a copy of the original grievance, the decision
rendered, and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the appeal.

c.    The Superintendent or designee shall communicate the decision to the
grievant on the appropriate form within fifteen (15) days. If the Superin¬
tendent or designee does not respond within the time limits all fees and
expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be borne by the District.

3. Level HI

a.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level n, he/she may,

within fifteen (15) days after a decision by the Superintendent or designee,
request in writing that the grievance be submitted to conciliation. If the
grievant requests conciliation, the parties will, within fifteen (15) days
after receiving the request, contact the California Conciliation Service (if
available from State of California) and set a meeting with a conciliator for
as soon as possible for all parties.

4

4.

Level IV

a.    If the grievant is not satisfied with the result of Level m conciliation,
he/she may, within fifteen (15) days of the conclusion of Level HI
conciliation, request in writing that the Association submit his/her
grievance to arbitration. A copy of this request shall be given to the
Superintendent.

(1) If a grievant decides to pursue arbitration contrary to the advice of
the Association, said grievant shall equally share the costs of the
arbitration with the District.

b.    The Association, by written notice to the Superintendent, within the time
period specified in (4) above may submit the grievance to arbitration.

Prior to submission the Association or Superintendent may request and
shall be granted up to three (3) hours of meeting time to resolve the

grievance.

c.    The grievant and the District shall attempt to agree upon an arbitrator. If within
ten (10) days no agreement can be reached, they shall request the American
Arbitration Association (hereinafter referred to as "AAA") to supply a panel of
five (5) names of persons experienced in hearing grievances in public schools.
The selection of an arbitrator shall be determined by a striking process in which
the first party to strike shall be determined by a flip of a coin; then each party
shall alternately strike a name until one name remains. The remaining panel
member shall be the arbitrator.

d.    The arbitrator shall proceed under the Voluntary Labor Arbitration Rules

of the AAA.

e.    The arbitrator will be without power or authority to make any decisions
which require the commission of an act prohibited by law or which is in
violation of the terms of this Agreement.

f.    The arbitrator will conduct a hearing and render an advisory award to both
parties in accordance with the rules of the AAA.

g.    All fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be borne
equally by the District and Association except where stated in Level lb..

Level He., of this article. Cost of substitutes for hearing shall be borne by
the District. Any additional expenses shall be borne by the party incurring
them.

h.    After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to make
written arguments, the arbitrator shall submit in writing to all parties
the findings and recommendations.

i.    The arbitrator's decision will be in writing and will set forth findings of
facts, reasoning, and conclusions of the issues submitted within (30) days.

5.

Miscellaneous

a.    Should a grievant choose to pursue a grievance without the intervention of
the exclusive representative, then, prior to any level, the District shall
convey a copy of the grievance and its proposed resolution to the
Association and shall allow the Association seven (7) days to comment
thereon. In such cases time limits will be extended appropriately.

b.    Any arbitration in which the Association is not officially involved, the
Association shall be given prior notification of the date, time and location
of the hearing(s) and shall be able to have an observer present at
Association expense.

c.    The grievant and conferee(s) shall be provided reasonable release time at
District expense for processing of the grievance.

d.    Upon request, the District and Association shall make available any
documents or district records not privileged under law which are necessary
to the resolution of the grievance.

e.    No documents, communications, or records dealing with the processing of
a grievance shall be kept in the personnel file of any of the participants.

f.    A Bargaining Unit Member shall have the right to be accompanied by a
conferee(s) at any or all of the levels/steps of this procedure.

g.    When a grievance is settled prior to arbitration, the decision shall not be
inconsistent with the terms and conditions of this contract.

h.    The Association shall receive copies of all grievances filed with the
District with the name of the grievant blocked out to protect privacy within
ten (10) days of filing.

i.    Timelines at any level/step may be extended by mutual consent of both
parties.

j.    All communications, notices, and papers required to be in writing shall be
served personally and copy dated or by certified United States mail.

k.    It is understood that the filing of a grievance by any Bargaining Unit
Member shall not reflect unfavorably upon the unit member.

l.    At Levels I, II, IE and IV, it is understood that in order to protect the
confidentiality of Bargaining Unit Members names of individual grievant
shall not be made public without their written consent.

ARTICLE IV:

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS

A    RIGHT TO JOIN ORGANIZATION

The District and the Association recognize the right of Bargaining Unit Members to form,
join, and participate in lawful activities of Bargaining Unit Member organizations and the
equal alternative right of Bargaining Unit Members to refuse to form, join, and participate
in Bargaining Unit Member activities.

B    ACTIONS AGAINST BARGAINING UNIT MEMBERS

The District shall not take any action against a Bargaining Unit Member that is prohibited
by law.

C    STAFF LOUNGES

The District shall provide each school with a staff lounge. The staff lounge shall include
adequate tables and chairs for the staff, a bulletin board for certificated notices, and an
Association bulletin board. Each staff lounge shall be designated for staff only as
determined and posted by each site staff.

D    RESTROOMS

During working hours, each school shall provide restrooms, separate from the nurse's
office, for adult use only. Restrooms shall be well ventilated. Women's restrooms shall
have feminine hygiene supplies available.

E    REST AREA

Where possible, each school shall provide a rest area, designated by the site
administrator, separate from the nurse's office, for adult use only.

F    STUDENT DISCIPLINE

Each staff shall develop a well-defined procedure for student discipline which shall be
cooperatively implemented and reviewed every four years. The site administrator shall
have final approval of this procedure.

G    SPLIT ASSIGNMENTS

Bargaining unit members who request to split an assignment shall receive the same
consideration as other employees who apply for a position.

9

H    STUDENT PLACEMENT

1.    At the end of each school year, teachers and appropriate specialists shall work in
grade level groups to place students in classes for the following year.

2.    The teachers and principal shall review all class lists for appropriateness of
student placement and needed changes will be made prior to the start of school.

I    STAFF DEVELOPMENT COMMITTEE

This committee, under the direction of the administrator of curriculum/instruction, shall
be formed annually by March 15th for the purpose of providing input for planning,
publicizing, and evaluating District staff development programs, as well as approving
applications for the district Staff Development Fund. This committee will be composed
of a teacher from each school selected by the staff at each site whose name will be
forwarded by FVEA to the Assistant Superintendent by March 1, one (1) site
administrators, and a District administrator. Prior to the end of each school year, the
committee will develop a plan to gather input on the evaluation of current year’s staff
development activities including subject matter and delivery, and on suggestions for the
coming year’s staff development needs.

J    GRADE 6-8 COMMITTEE

On an as needed basis the Assistant Superintendent, Instruction will form a 6-8
committee of interested bargaining unit members and administrators for the purpose of

sharing interests and common concerns.

K    SITE BUDGET

Staff members shall be informed by the site administrator regarding the allocation and
distribution of the site budget within 30 days of receipt from the district.

L    ADMINISTRATOR’S SCHEDULE

Bargaining unit members shall be notified of each site administrator’s weekly off-site
meeting schedule. The administrator may determine the notification method. Staff will be
notified at the beginning of the year the primary and secondary administrative designee.

M    KEYS

The District shall make room keys available to Bargaining Unit Members at least two
weeks prior to the return of students.

ARTICLE V:

1.    Any unit member who is a member of the Fountain Valley Education

Association/CTA/NEA, or who has applied for membership, may sign and deliver
to the District the appropriate deduction form for unified membership dues,
initiation fees, and general assessments in the Association. Upon authorization,
the District shall deduct one-tenth (1/10) of such dues from the regular salary
check of the unit member each month thereafter.

2 MEMBERSHIP CATEGORIES ARE ESTABLISHED AS FOLLOWS:

Category 1 -

For those teachers whose teaching assignment is more than 60% of a
normal assignment, except for faculty employed as pre-school, Head Start,
and child care teachers whose salaries are less than the minimum teacher
salary for the district in which they are employed.

Category 2 -

For those teachers whose teaching assignment is greater than 1/3, but not more
than 60% of a normal assignment, or faculty employed as pre-school, Head
Start, or child care teachers whose salary in the district in which they are
employed is less than the minimum salary paid regular teachers in such district.

Category 3 -

EVALUATION

The provisions of this article outline the procedures to be utilized in the evaluation of certificated
employees as set forth in Education Code Sections 44660-44665 and the California Standards for

A    PURPOSE

The evaluation process shall be a positive and constructive tool for the purpose of
continuing to improve teacher strengths, helping Bargaining Unit Members develop

B    GOALS

1.    Standard

The primary goal of evaluation is the improvement of student instruction and
achievement. Therefore, the standards listed below are used to assess certificated
employee effectiveness in the following:

•    Engaging and supporting all students in learning

•    Creating and maintaining effective environments for student learning

•    Understanding and organizing subject matter for student learning

•    Planning instruction and designing learning experiences for all students

•    Assessing student learning

•    Developing as a professional educator

See Appendix A for supporting statements related to each standard.

2.    Alternative

The primary goal of the alternative evaluation is professional growth and
development leading to improvement of student instruction and achievement.
Participants in the alternative evaluation must continue to meet the teaching
standards as listed above.

ARTICLE XTTT:    TRANSFER AND    ASSIGNMENT

A DEFINITIONS

1.    A transfer refers to any District action which results in the movement of a
probationary or tenured Bargaining Unit Member from the position he/she holds
immediately preceding such action to another position within the same job
classification but at a different school.

2.    All personnel are employees of the District and not of a particular school. The
District shall have the final prerogative in determining the assignment of
certificated personnel according to the needs of the District, interests of the
children and the individual teacher, subject to the provisions of this Article.

3.    A transfer may be Bargaining Unit Member initiated (voluntary) or District
initiated (involuntary).

B VOLUNTARY TRANSFER

A Bargaining Unit Member desiring a voluntary transfer shall submit the request
according to the following procedures:

1.    Any Bargaining Unit Member may apply for a voluntary transfer by submitting a
transfer request form to the Personnel Division either when a vacancy is posted or
in advance of the posting of a vacancy.

2.    The names of those individuals requesting a transfer shall be prepared and sent to
the school of their request. When a vacancy becomes available, the hiring
supervisor shall establish an interview time with the individual(s) requesting a
transfer to that location before the position has been filled.

3.    A decision to apply for or not to apply for a voluntary transfer shall be the
Bargaining Unit Member's independent decision.

C INVOLUNTARY TRANSFER

1. Bargaining Unit Members may be involuntarily transferred when any one of the
following conditions exist:

a.    Credential Authorization

b.    Opening and closing of schools

c.    Declining and increasing enrollment

d.    School or staff needs

e.    Principal and Bargaining Unit Member agreement based on Bargaining
Unit Member request.

55

2.    In making transfer decisions pursuant to paragraphs l.c and l.d of this section the
immediate supervisor shall utilize the following criteria:

a.    Student Needs: The Bargaining Unit Member either (1) has skills, training,
education and/or experience which are uniquely suited to meeting the needs of
students at a different school, or (2) cannot meet the needs of the students (e g.,
grade level, special education, bilingual, bicultural, gifted) at the Bargaining
Unit Member's present school.

b.    Educational Program Needs: The Bargaining Unit Member either (1) has
strengths, abilities, experience in programs, use of materials and/or
teaching levels that uniquely qualify the unit member to meet the needs of
the educational program at a different school, or (2) lacks the strengths,
abilities, or experience in programs, use of materials and/or teaching levels
that exist and/or are operational or will be operational in the Bargaining
Unit Member's school.

c.    Seniority: Except as otherwise provided in paragraph C.9, when none of
the above applies or when the above criteria equally apply to more than
one unit member, the Bargaining Unit Member involuntarily transferred
shall be the Bargaining Unit Member with the least District seniority.

3.    Performance Related Transfers: The immediate supervisor may transfer a
Bargaining Unit Member if the Bargaining Unit Member's performance impedes
the instructional program by reason of the Bargaining Unit Member's inability or
unwillingness to work cooperatively toward the common goals of the school.

4.    At the time they receive notice of intent to involuntary transfer, the Bargaining
Unit Member may request written reasons for the involuntary transfer.

5.    Before any involuntary transfer is effected, the Bargaining Unit Member must be
advised, through personal notice by the principal or supervisor initiating the
request for transfer, that an involuntary transfer is being recommended.

6.    Upon request, an opportunity shall be provided for the Bargaining Unit Member
to meet with the recommending supervisor to discuss the proposed transfer.

7.    The supervisor shall consider alternative solutions that the Bargaining Unit
Member may propose.

8.    A current list of the District's vacant positions shall be made available for each
Bargaining Unit Member being considered for an involuntary transfer.

9.    Bargaining Unit Members who have been involuntarily transferred shall not be
subject to another involuntary transfer until they have completed two (2) school
years at the site to which they were transferred. If the Bargaining Unit Member is
transferred to a school which closes, this does not apply.

10.    If the condition which necessitated an involuntary transfer changes and that position
becomes available again, the Bargaining Unit Member who was involuntarily

change over the summer months, which would cause the position to be reinstated.

11.    Bargaining Unit Members who are being involuntarily transferred for the ensuing

ARTICLE XV:

PAR PROGRAM/MENTOR TEACHER PROGRAM

A. PURPOSE OF PAR PROGRAM

The Fountain Valley Education Association and the Fountain Valley School District are
continuously striving to provide the highest possible quality of education. In order for students
to succeed in learning, teachers must succeed in teaching. Therefore, the parties agree to
cooperate in the design and implementation of programs to improve the quality of instruction
through expanded and improved professional development and peer assistance. Teachers
referred to or who volunteer for the Peer Assistance and Review Program are viewed as
valuable professionals who deserve to have the best resources available provided to them in the
interest of improving performance to a successful standard.

The Peer Assistance and Review Program (PAR) allows exemplary classroom teachers to
assist permanent teachers in the areas of: 1) subject matter knowledge and/or 2) teaching
methods/instruction/strategies. The program will include clear written performance goals
for the participant, which are consistent with District learning standards of multiple
classroom observations, and sufficient staff development to assist participants’ skills and
knowledge. Temporary and probationary teachers will be provided support and
assistance by means outside of this Article. The extent of this program’s assistance and
review depends upon whether the participating teacher is a volunteer permanent teacher,
or a “referred teacher,” e.g., a permanent teacher who has received a needs to improve
rating in either: 1) subject matter knowledge or 2) teaching methods/instruction/strategies,
or in both areas. Assistance shall be provided through consulting teachers as described in
this Article. It shall not involve the consulting teacher in the evaluation of the
participating teacher, except that the report of the referred teacher’s participation shall be
made available as part of the evaluation process.

Effective July 1, 2000, the Peer Assistance and Review Program for Teachers shall
become fully operational and shall supplement the Mentor Teacher Program. The Peer
Assistance and Review Program shall establish a teacher peer assistance and review
system as a critical resource mechanism that allows consulting teachers to assist
participating teachers in gaining knowledge in subject matter and/or teaching strategies.

Effective July 1, 2001, the Mentor Teacher Program shall become inoperative. Until July
1, 2001, the language in Article XV of the current contract describing the Mentor Teacher
Program shall stay in effect.

B DEFINITIONS

1.    “Classroom teacher” or “teacher” shall mean any permanent member of the
bargaining unit covered by certificated evaluation requirements in Article XII.

2.    “Participating teacher” shall mean a permanent classroom teacher member of the unit
who either volunteers or is required to participate in the program.

3.    “Referred teacher” or “Participating teacher with an unsatisfactory evaluation” shall mean
a unit member with permanent status whose most recent performance evaluation
contained a needs to improve rating in either: 1) subject matter knowledge and/or 2)
teaching methods/instruction/strategies, or both, pursuant to Article XII of this agreement.

4.    “Voluntary teacher” shall mean a permanent unit member whose most recent
evaluations have been satisfactory and who is not required to participate in this
program because of a needs to improve rating in either: 1) subject matter knowledge,
and/or 2) teaching methods/instruction/strategies. Such voluntary participation is for
the purpose of professional growth utilizing the assistance of a consulting teacher.

5.    “Consulting teacher” is an exemplary classroom teacher meeting the requirements of
this article, selected by the PAR Panel to provide program assistance to a referred
teacher. (A consulting teacher shall not provide assistance under this article to more
than two teachers annually.)

6.    “Curriculum specialist” is an exemplary teacher meeting the requirements of this
article, selected by the PAR Panel to provide staff development, curriculum
development, and teacher assistance as assigned.

7.    “Evaluator” is the certificated administrator appointed by the District to evaluate a
permanent teacher.

The election will be run by the Association in cooperation with the District.

b.    Teacher members of the panel shall serve for a two (2) year term.

c.    The initial panel shall draw lots to determine the one (1) and two (2) year terms.

d.    To be eligible to serve on the panel, the teacher must have the same qualifications
as those for a consulting teacher.

e.    The panel shall establish its own meeting schedule. A quorum requires five (5) of
the seven (7) members be present. Such meetings will take place after the Regular
workday. Panel members shall be released as necessary to perform selection
responsibilities. Teacher panel members shall receive a stipend of $250.00
annually.
