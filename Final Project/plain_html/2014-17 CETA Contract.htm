ARTICLE I

RECOGNITION/AGREEMENT AND REOPENERS

1.    The Board recognizes the Association as the exclusive representative of
certificated employees for the purpose of meeting and negotiating as defined in
the Rodda Act, Senate Bill 160, including the following:

•    contractual teachers (temporary)

•    contractual teachers (probationary)

•    contractual teachers (permanent)

•    school nurse

•    counselor

•    speech therapist

•    this provision specifically excludes    management personnel including the

Superintendent, Principals and Assistant-Principals. Day-to-day substitute
teachers are also not represented by this Association.

2.    The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement (“Agreement”) by and between the Governing Board of the Cardiff
School District (“Board”) and the Cardiff Elementary Teachers
Associaton/CTA/NEA (“Association”), an employee organization.

3.    It is understood that both parties to this contract support this agreement and will
do everything necessary to assure the implementation of the contract provisions.

4 The Association agrees that neither it nor any employee in the bargaining unit
shall engage in any refusal to work including any strike, work stoppage, slow
down, or refusal to fully and faithfully perform job duties and responsibilities
during the term of this Agreement including compliance with request from other
employee organizations to do so. The District shall be entitled, in the event this
provision is violated, to withdraw any rights, privileges or services provided for in
this Agreement.

5.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Section 3540-3549 of
the Government Code (“Act”).

6.    The parties may reopen this Agreement by mutual consent.

7.    The Agreement, shall become effective upon the date of final ratification by the
parties and shall remain in force and effect up to and including June 30, 2017.
The parties may annually reopen negotiations for any two (2) issues as well as
health and welfare benefits and salary for the term of this agreement. The
parties may reopen other articles by mutual consent.

ARTICLE II

MANAGEMENT RIGHTS AND DISTRICT POWERS

1.    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority
to direct and control district matters to the full extent of the law. Includes, but not
limited to those duties and powers, are the rights to: direct the work of its
employees; determine the method, means and services to be provided; establish
the educational philosophy, goals, and objectives; insure the rights and
educational opportunities of students; determine the staffing patterns, determine
the number and kinds of personnel required; determine the classification of
positions, maintain efficiency of the district operation; determine the curriculum,
build more, or modify the facilities, develop and implement budget procedures;
determine the methods of raising revenue; and contract out work. In addition, the
District retains the right to hire, assign, evaluate, promote, terminate, and
discipline employees, and to take action on any matter in the event of an
emergency.

2.    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties, and
responsibilities by the District, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and
practices in furtherance thereof, and the use of judgment and discretion in
connection therewith, shall be limited only by the specific and express terms of
this Agreement, and then only to extent such specific and express terms are in
conformance with the laws of the State of California.

3.    The Board of Education will amend its written policies and procedures and take
such other action by resolution or otherwise as may be necessary to give full
force and effect to the provisions of this Agreement. The Board of Education
retains its right to amend, modify, or rescind policies or practices referred to in
this Agreement in cases of emergency. The determination of whether an
emergency exists is solely within the discretion of the Board of Education and is
expressly excluded from the provisions of Article II. Emergency shall be defined
as a natural disaster, National emergency, act of God, epidemic, or other visible
catastrophe. When the Board determines that the emergency no longer exists,
all policies and practices which were amended, modified, or rescinded during the
emergency will be changed back to the original provisions of this contract.

ARTICLE III

DEFINITIONS

1.    “Bargaining Unit Member” refers to any employee who is included in the
appropriate unit as defined in Article I and therefore covered by the terms and
provisions of this Agreement.

2.    “School Days” means days during which students are required to be in
attendance.

3.    “Paid Leave of Absence” means that a bargaining unit member shall be entitled:

A To receive wages and all fringe benefits, including, but not limited to,
insurance and retirement benefits;

B.    To return to the same assignment which he/she occupied immediately
preceding the commencement of the leave, if said assignment is available;
and

C.    To receive credit for annual salary increments provided during his/her
leave. Such paid leaves include: Sick Leave, Industrial Accident Leave,
Personal Necessity Leave, Bereavement Leave, Jury Leave, and In-
Service Leave.

4 “District-approved Unpaid Leave of Absence” means that a bargaining unit

member shall be entitled to the same benefits accorded bargaining unit members
who are on paid leave, except wages, for a period not to exceed twenty (20)
working days. After a period of twenty (20) working days, the employee is
entitled to pay for the district health benefits, providing the insurance carrier
agrees, and will receive no other district benefits.

5.    “Immediate Family” means the mother, father, son, daughter, brother, sister,
brother-in-law, sister-in-law, spouse, mother-in-law, father-in-law, grandparent,
grandchild, foster child, or any person who has served as foster parent or legal
guardian to the bargaining unit member, or any relative living in the immediate
household of the bargaining unit member. At the Superintendent’s discretion, the
definition of immediate family may be extended because of extenuating
circumstances.

6.    “CETA” means the Cardiff Elementary Teachers’ Association as used in the
terms and provisions of this Agreement.

7.    “Association” means Cardiff Elementary Teachers’ Association/California
Teachers Association/National Education Association.

8.    “Board” means the elected Board of Trustees of the Cardiff Elementary School
District. 5

NEGOTIATIONS PROCEDURES

1.    No later than the end of October of each school year, the Association will make
public their “Notice of Contract Articles” to be included in negotiations. Any
agreement reached between the parties shall be reduced to writing and signed,
with the intent that it be implemented before the end of the fiscal year.

2.    Either party may utilize the services of outside consultants.

3.    The Board and Association may discharge their respective duties required by the
Agreement by means of authorized officers, individuals, representatives, or
committees.

4.    Negotiations shall take place at mutually agreeable times and places.

5.    The Association shall designate as many as five (5) representatives to attend
negotiations and impasse proceedings.

6.    The Board shall furnish the Association with copies of all budgetary and other
information that is necessary for the Association to fulfill its role as the exclusive
bargaining representative.

7.    Not later than October 15th, the Board shall furnish the Association with the
placement of personnel on the respective salary schedules as of October 1st 6

ARTICLE V

PERSONNEL FILES

1.    The Board shall not base any adverse action against a bargaining unit member
upon materials which are contained in such bargaining unit member’s personnel
file unless the materials have been placed in the file within a reasonable period of
time following the incident giving rise to such materials and the bargaining unit
member had been notified at such time that such materials were being placed in
the file.

2.    A bargaining unit member shall be provided any negative or derogatory material
before it is placed in his/her personnel file. He/she shall also be given an
opportunity to initial and date the material and to prepare a written response to
such material.

3.    Upon written authorization by the bargaining unit member, a representative of the
Association shall be permitted to examine, and/or obtain copies of materials in
the bargaining unit member’s personnel file, one time per year, per employee.
Additional requests for copies shall be at the employee’s expense.

4.    The person or persons who draft material to be placed in a bargaining unit
member’s personnel file shall sign the material and signify the date on which
such material was drafted.

5.    The District shall keep a log indicating the persons other than District
Administrative Staff who have requested to examine a personnel file as well as
the dates such requests were made. Such log shall be available for examination
by the bargaining unit member or an Association Representative, if so authorized
by the bargaining unit member.

6.    Access to the personnel files shall be limited to the members of the District
Administrative Staff on a need-to-know basis. Board of Education members may
request the review of a bargaining unit member’s file at a personnel session of
the entire Board of Education. The contents of all personnel files shall be kept in
the strictest confidence.

7 A bargaining unit member’s personnel file shall not be forwarded to any other
district without the bargaining unit member’s consent.

8. The District shall maintain the bargaining unit member’s personnel files at the

District’s central office. Any files kept by the bargaining unit member’s immediate
supervisor shall not contain any material not found in the District’s files except
records of observations kept only between evaluation periods.

and bargaining unit member mailboxes for communications to bargaining unit
members. The foregoing rights are guaranteed by the State Collective
Bargaining Law, but are subject to reasonable regulations by the District.

3.    Names, addresses and telephone numbers of all bargaining unit members shall
be provided without cost to the Association no later than October 15th of each
school year.

ARTICLE VIII

CONTRACT VIOLATION PROCEDURE

1 Definitions

A.    A “contract violation” is a formal written claim by one or more bargaining
unit member or the Association, that there has been a violation,
misinterpretation or misapplication of a provision of this Agreement. A unit
member may file, on behalf of the Cardiff Elementary Teachers’
Association, a contract violation alleging a violation of Association rights
under a specific provision of this Agreement.

B.    The “grievant” is the bargaining unit member, unit members, or the
Association making the claim.

C.    A “day” is a day in which the central administration office of the District is
open for business.

D.    The “immediate supervisor” is the lowest level administrator having
immediate jurisdiction over the bargaining unit member who has been
designated by the District to adjust contract violations. Beginning at Step
2 of the Contract Violation Procedure, a teacher or teachers filing a
contract violation may choose to be represented by the Cardiff Elementary
Teachers Association.

2. Time Limitation and Waiver

A.    A contract violation shall not be valid for consideration unless it is
submitted in writing, on the prescribed form (Appendix A), to the building
principal setting forth the facts and specific provisions of the Agreement
allegedly violated and the particular relief sought within twenty (20) days
after the teacher or teachers through the use of reasonable diligence,
should have had knowledge of the occurrence that gave rise to the
contract violation. Failure to file any contract violation within such period
shall be deemed a waiver thereof.

Failure to appeal a contract violation from one step to another within the
time periods hereafter provided shall constitute a waiver of the contract
violation.

B.    Computation of Time: In computing any period of time prescribed or
allowed by procedures herein, the time of the act, event, or default for
which the designated period of time begins to run shall not be included.
The last date of the period so computed shall be counted, unless it is a
Saturday, a Sunday, or a legal holiday, in which event the period runs until
the end of the next day which is not a Saturday, a Sunday, or a legal
holiday.

10

C. Filing and Postmark: The filing or service of any notice or document

herein shall be timely if it bears a postmark of the United States mail within
the applicable time period

D Failure by the District or its representatives to issue a decision within the
time periods provided herein shall constitute a denial of the contract
violation and the teacher may appeal to the next step.

Procedure

A.    Step One: Prior to filing a formal written contract violation the grievant
shall first make an effort to adjust the alleged contract violation informally
with his/her immediate supervisor.

B.    Step Two: If the contract violation is not resolved through informal
discussion with the immediate supervisor pursuant to Step One, the
grievant shall submit the prescribed contract violation form within the
twenty (20) day time limitation provided in Section 2.A. of this Article.

Within ten (10) days after receipt of the written contract violation, the
immediate supervisor shall issue a decision in writing to the employee.

C.    Step Three: In the event the contract violation is not resolved at Step
Two, the decision rendered may be appealed to the Superintendent within
ten (10) days after the decision at Step Two. The Superintendent or
his/her designee shall set a time to meet regarding the contract violation
within seven (7) days after receipt of the appeal. Within five (5) days after
the meeting, the Superintendent or his/her designee shall issue a decision
in writing to the grievant.

D.    Step Four: In the event the contract violation is not resolved at Step
Three, the grievant may, within ten (10) days after the decision at Step
Three, submit a request in writing to the Superintendent for arbitration of
the dispute.

1)    The grievant and the District shall attempt to agree upon an
advisory arbitrator. If no agreement can be reached, they shall,
within five (5) days after the request for arbitration, request the
State Conciliation Service to supply a panel of five (5) names, each
party shall alternately strike a name until only one (1) name
remains. The remaining panel member shall be the advisory
arbitrator. The order of the striking shall be determined by lot.

2)    The fees and expenses of the arbitrator and the hearing shall be
borne equally by the District and the Association. However, if the
Board rejects, in whole or in part, the arbitrator’s recommendation
at Step Five, the District shall pay all fees and expenses of the
arbitrator. All other expenses shall be borne by the party incurring
them.

3)    The arbitrator shall, as soon as possible, hear evidence and render
a decision on the issue or issues submitted to him/her. If the
parties cannot agree upon a submission agreement, the arbitrator
shall determine the issues by referring to the written contract
violations and the answers thereto at each step and the Agreement.

4)    The arbitrator will have no power to add to, subtract from or modify
the terms of the Agreement or the written policies, rules, regulations
and procedures of the District.

5)    After a hearing and after both parties have had an opportunity to
make written arguments, the arbitrator shall submit in writing to all
parties his/her findings and recommendations which shall be
advisory to the parties.

E.    Step Five: In the event that either party is not satisfied with the
recommendation or recommendations of the arbitrator, that party may
appeal the decision, in writing, within ten (10) days to the Board of
Trustees.

1) The Board alone has the power to render a final and binding

determination of a contract violation. The recommendation of the
arbitrator shall only be advisory and if, upon review of the written
decision, the Board of Trustees determines that it is unable to
render a final determination on record, it may reopen the record for
the taking of additional evidence. The Board shall render a final
and biding decision within thirty (30) days after additional evidence
is taken.

F.    This procedure is not cumulative and this Article shall be the exclusive
provision and remedy for the determination of all contract violations. A
teacher instituting any action or proceeding, either by himself/herself or
through a representative, in a court of law or equity, state or federal, or
before an administrative tribunal, the subject matter of which constitutes a
grievance, shall immediately thereupon waive any and ail rights under this
Article.

ARTICLE IX

PROFESSIONAL DUES OR FEES AND PAYROLL DEDUCTIONS

1.    All bargaining unit members will sign and deliver to the Board, an assignment
authorizing deduction of unified membership dues in the Association. Such
authorization shall continue in effect from year to year unless revoked in writing
by the teacher between July 1 and September 10 of any school year. Pursuant
to such authorization, the Board shall deduct one-tenth (1/10th) of such dues from
the regular salary check of the teacher each month for ten (10) months.
Deductions for teachers who sign such authorization after the commencement of
the school year shall be appropriately prorated to complete payments by the end
of the school year. This will remain in effect as long as CETA/CTA/NEA is the
exclusive representative. Any dispute as to dues deduction shall be between the
individual teacher and the Association, and the Association agrees to save and
hold the District harmless from any and all claims against the District by the
teacher arising out of dues deduction under this section.

2.    With respect to all sums deducted by the Board pursuant to authorization of the
employee for membership dues, the Board agrees to remit such moneys to CTA
accompanied by a list of teachers for whom such deductions have been made.

3.    The Association agrees to furnish any information needed by the District to fulfill
the provisions of this Article.

4.    Upon appropriate written authorization from the teacher, the Board shall deduct
from the salary of any teacher and make appropriate remittance for annuities,
credit union, savings bonds, charitable donations, or any other plans or programs
approved by the Board.

5.    Any unit member who is not a member of the Association or who does not make
application for membership within thirty (30) days from the commencement of
assigned duties within the bargaining unit, as a condition of continued
employment shall become a member of the Association or pay the Association a
fair share service fee. The amount of the fee shall not exceed the dues that are
payable by members of the Association and shall cover the cost of negotiation,
contract administration, and other activities of the Association that are germane
to its functions as the exclusive bargaining representative. Payment of the fair
share service fee shall be made either in a lump sum prior to November 1 of
each year, or through payroll deduction in the same manner as prescribed for
monthly payroll deductions for membership dues.

ARTICLE X

1.

TEACHING HOURS

Except as modified herein, the workday for bargaining unit members shall be
seven and one-half (7 1/2) hours per day, or a total of thirty-seven and one-half
(37 1/2) hours per week of school, based on related District service. The
bargaining unit members’ workday shall be structured and directed by the
Building Principal in conformance with this Article.

2.

The bargaining unit members’ workday will begin at least twenty (20) minutes
before his/her first instructional assignment and conclude no sooner than twenty
(20) minutes after the school site dismissal time.

3.

It is understood by the parties that individual bargaining unit member’s service to
the district is his/her primary professional obligation.

4.

In addition to the basic workday as described in Section 1, bargaining unit
members are required to perform duties associated with the education profession
such as yard duty, sixth grade camp, parent conferences, Back-to-School Night
and Open House. Full attendance at sixth grade camp shall be compensated
with two (2) compensatory days.

5.

Bargaining unit members, during the term of this Agreement, shall normally be
provided a forty (40) minute duty-free lunch period except for emergencies such
as inclement weather, and any unavailability of other employees to supervise
pupils.

6.

In order to accommodate school/district meetings, the administration may
designate one day per week, which may extend past the regular workday.

7.

The bargaining unit member’s work year, of not more than one hundred eighty-
five (185) days, shall be established annually by the Board of Trustees after
consultation with the Association regarding the composition of the calendar. For
each school year, the Board of Trustees may extend the work beyond one
hundred eighty-five (185) days within its discretion as long as it pays bargaining
unit members per diem for additional days actually worked.

8.

The last student attendance day of the year shall be a minimum day for students.

ARTICLE XII

LEAVES

1. Paid Leaves

A.    Sick Leave: Every bargaining unit member shall be entitled to ten (10)
days of sick leave each year of employment.

1)    Unused sick leave accrues from school year to school year.

2)    By October 15th, every bargaining unit member shall receive a sick
leave allotment credit, equal to his/her sick leave entitlement for the
school year. A bargaining unit member may use his/her credited
sick leave at any time during the school year.

3)    The Board may require a physician’s verification of any illness at
Board expense.

4)    The Board shall provide each bargaining unit member with a written
statement of:

a)    His/her accrued sick leave total; and

b)    His/her sick leave entitlement for the school year.

5)    The Board shall provide for leave of absence from duty for any
bargaining unit member of the District who is required to be absent
from duties because of a disability related to pregnancy,
miscarriage, childbirth, and a recovery therefrom. The length of the
leave of absence, including the date on which the leave shall
commence and the date on which the bargaining members shall
resume duties, shall be determined by the bargaining unit and the
bargaining unit member’s physician. Disabilities caused or
contributed to by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth and recovery
there from are, for all job-related purposes, temporary disability
insurance or sick leave plan available in connection with
employment by the school district. All employment policies and
practices of the District shall be applied to disability due to
pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth and recovery from on the same
terms and conditions applied to other temporary disabilities.

B.    Extended Sick Leave (Education Code Sections 44977. 44998 & 44987.1)
During each school year, when a member of the bargaining unit has
exhausted all available sick leave, including all accumulated sick leave,
and continues to be absent from his/her duties on account of illness or
accident for an additional period of five school months, whether or not the
absence arises out of or in the course of employment, the amount
deducted from the salary due him/her for any of the additional five (5)
months in which the absence occurs shall not exceed the sum that is
actually paid a substitute employee employed to fill his/her position during
his/her absence or fifty percent (50%) whichever is the less amount.

16

For purposes of this provision sick leave, including accumulated sick
leave, and the five-month period shall run consecutively. An employee
shall not be provided more than one five-month period per illness or
accident. However, if a school year terminates before the five-month
period is exhausted, the employee may take the balance of the five-month
period in a subsequent school year. No other paid or partial paid illness or
accident leave shall be granted by the District.

When a member of the bargaining unit has exhausted all available sick
leave, including accumulated sick leave, and continues to be absent on
account of illness or accident for a period beyond the five-month period
provided immediately above, and the member of the bargaining unit is not
medically able to resume the duties of his/her position, the member shall
no longer be employed by the District but shall be placed on a
reemployment list for a period of 24 months if the employee is on
probationary status, or for a period of 39 months if the member is on
permanent status. The 24-month or 39-month period shall commence at
the expiration of the five-month period of partial paid sick leave.

When the member is medically able, as determined by a physician
selected or agreed to by the District, during the 24-month or 39-month
period, the member shall be returned to employment in a position for
which he or she is credentialed and qualified. If the member does not
return to work during the 24-month or 39-month period, the member shall
be taken off the reemployment list and shall lose all reemployment rights
to the District.

C. Industrial Accident Leaves:

1)    Bargaining unit members will be entitled to industrial accident leave
according to the provision in Education Code Section 44984 for
personal injury which has qualified for worker’s compensation.

2)    Such leave shall not exceed sixty (60) days during which the
schools of the District are required to be in session or when the
bargaining unit member would otherwise have been performing
work for the District in any one fiscal year for the same industrial
accident.

3)    The District has the right to have the bargaining unit member
examined by a physician designated by the District at District
expense to assist in determining the length of time during which the
bargaining unit member will be temporarily unable to perform
assigned duties and the degree to which a disability is attributable
to the injury involved.

17

4)

For any days of absence from duty as a result of the same
industrial accident, the bargaining unit member shall endorse to the
District any wage loss benefit check from the Workers’
Compensation Insurance Administrators which would make the
total compensation from both sources exceed one hundred percent
(100%) of the amount the bargaining unit member would have
received as salary had there been no industrial accident or illness.

5) If the bargaining unit member fails to endorse to the District any
wage loss disability, indemnity check received on account of the
industrial accident or illness as provided above, the District shall
deduct from the bargaining unit member’s salary warrant the
amount such disability indemnity actually paid to and retained by
the bargaining unit member.

D. Personal Necessity Leave: Every bargaining unit member shall be
entitled to use up to eight (8) days of his/her paid sick leave
allotment during each school year in case of personal necessity.

1)    “Personal Necessity” means any circumstances that are
serious in nature which cannot be expected to be
disregarded, which necessitate immediate attention and
which cannot be dealt with during off-duty hours without
causing undue inconvenience to the bargaining unit
member.

2)    A bargaining unit member shall not be required to secure
advance permission to use Personal Necessity Leave in the
cases enumerated below:

a)    Death or serious illness of member of his/her
immediate family;

b)    Accident, involving his/her person or property, or the
person or property of member of his/her immediate
family.

3)    A bargaining unit member must receive prior District
approval for any Personal Necessity Leave which is not
specifically excluded in Section D 2 above (Appendix C).

4)    Personal Necessity Leave cannot be utilized for the
purposes of work slow-down or strike against the District.

H. Catastrophic Leave:

1)    The District and the Association agree to the establishment
of a Catastrophic Leave Bank (“Bank”) for Unit Members.

The intent of this Bank is to provide additional financial
protection to those Unit members of the District who incur a
period of prolonged illness or hospitalization, not to include
routine maternity issues. A catastrophic injury or illness shall
be defined as: any injury or illness which incapacitates a
Unit Member for an extended period of time, based upon
competent medical evidence.

2)    To be an eligible participant of the Bank, a certificated
employee must have at least one (1) year of service (at least
75% of the school year), must have at least ten (10) days of
accumulated sick leave, and must donate one (1) day of sick
leave (1 day = current daily hours assigned) during the open
enrollment period and one (1) day each year thereafter, for
five years. A member shall not be required to contribute
after 5 years unless the bank reserve falls below 40 days. At
that point, all members of the Bank must contribute on a
yearly basis until the bank is above 40. No member shall
contribute more than one (1) day to the Catastrophic Leave
Bank in any one open enrollment period.

New members wishing to enter the Bank will be required to
donate one (1) day during the open enrollment period in the
year they join regardless of the total number of days in the
Bank. Following the initial enrollment, a Unit Member may
only join the Bank during the annual open enrollment period
during the month of September. Employees hired after
September 1 or returning from a approved leave of absence
will have an open enrollment period of thirty (30) days
following the date of employment.

3)    The sick leave day contribution will be authorized on the
appropriate form (Appendix F-1) to be completed each year
during open enrollment and continue under the provisions
addressed below or until canceled by the Unit Member. No
sick leave may be surrendered or drawn for the purpose of
summer school.

Donations of sick leave days to the Bank shall be
irrevocable. Sick leave which is donated under this section
shall be deducted from the accrued sick leave authorized
under Section XII.I.A above and shall not be designated to a
specific unit member for his/her exclusive use.

20

A Unit Member who wishes to donate sick leave shall submit
a “Certificated Sick Leave Bank Deposit” form (Appendix
F-1) to the Payroll Department. This form authorizes the
donation to the Bank. No withdrawal shall be effective until
approved by the Joint Catastrophic Sick Leave Bank
Committee. The decisions of the Committee shall not be
subject to the grievance procedure, but may be reviewed
upon appeal to the Committee.

4)    A Unit Member electing to use the Catastrophic Leave Bank
shall complete an appropriate form (Appendix F-2) in order
to make a draw on the bank. The Unit Member must submit
this form to the Catastrophic Leave Bank Committee for
processing. The Unit member must provide an attending
physician’s statement which verifies the catastrophic illness
or injury and gives an estimated date of return to work.

In the event that the Unit Member is personally unable to
apply for catastrophic leave, an immediate family member or
Unit Member’s agent may make the request for the
applicant.

5)    The Committee shall be composed of four (4) members, two

(2) of whom are appointed by the District and two (2) of
whom are appointed by the Association. The Catastrophic
Leave Bank Committee shall be responsible for receiving
leave requests, verifying validity of requests, approving the
full or less than full amount requested or denying requests,
communicating its decision to affected unit members and the
Superintendent, and soliciting donations of sick
leave/vacation days from eligible unit members. A Unit
Member dissatisfied with any action or decision made by the
Committee concerning the Catastrophic Leave plan herein
provided, may submit a request for an appeal with
documentation. No request for appeal shall be considered
by the Committee unless the request for appeal is submitted
no later than ten (10) days after the action or decision in
question. The Committee shall have no jurisdiction to hear
any request which is not submitted within the required time
frame.

The Committee shall review timely matters which are
submitted to it. The Committee shall prepare a written report
regarding the matter submitted to it.

The report shall be submitted to the District Board of
Education as an information item.

The Committee shall be responsible for informing Unit
Members of solicitation for donations earmarked for the
Bank. The decision of the Committee shall not be subject to
the grievance procedure, but may be reviewed upon appeal

The maximum number of work days allowed for a member of

ARTICLE XIII

TRANSFERS

1.    A transfer refers to any District action which results in the movement of a
bargaining unit member from one school to another. A transfer may be unit
member-initiated (“voluntary”) or district-initiated (“involuntary”).

2.    Voluntary Transfers: A bargaining unit member may request a voluntary transfer
to take effect during the school year or at the beginning of the next school year.

In either event, the request shall be made on a “Request for Transfer” form
(Appendix B) and sent to the district office.

A.    Any request that is made for a transfer to take effect during the school
year shall be made within ten (10) days of posting the notice of vacancy.

B.    Management, when making voluntary transfers of bargaining unit
members, should consider the following criteria:

1)    credentials to perform required services;

2)    suitability to fill vacant position.

C.    Voluntary transfer requests shall be given priority consideration.

D.    If a voluntary transfer request is denied, upon request the bargaining unit
member shall be provided with the specific reason for the denial.

3.    Involuntary Transfers: Involuntary transfers shall be based exclusively on the
legitimate, educational-related needs of the District.

A.    Bargaining unit members to be involuntarily transferred shall have the right
to indicate preferences from a list of vacancies and the District shall
consider such requests.

B.    A bargaining unit member who is to be involuntarily transferred shall be
given the reasons for the transfer upon request.

4.    Notice of vacancies shall be posted for at least seven (7) calendar days on the
employee bulletin board in each building. Such notices shall be posted as soon
as the District Administration determines that a vacancy exists and shall include
the position description and location, grade level or subject matter assignment
and credential requirement. When such vacancies occur during a non-teaching
period, such as the summer vacation period, a notice of such vacancy shall be
mailed to all bargaining unit members.

ARTICLE XIV

CERTIFICATED EMPLOYEE EVALUATION

1.    The District management shall evaluate all permanent bargaining unit members
no less than once every two years, and all temporary or probationary unit
members no less than once each year.

2.    The evaluator shall be the bargaining unit member’s immediate supervisor or any
other full-time certificated management personnel designated by District
management.

3.    Those bargaining unit members who are regularly scheduled to be evaluated will
be so notified by the appropriate management person by no later than the
fifteenth (15th) working day of each school year. Such notice will contain a brief
explanation as to the procedures for evaluations.

4.    The evaluation procedure shall consist of at least one (1) formal observation and
an informal observation(s), and also a final written evaluation. A formal
observation shall be at least thirty (30) minutes and should include a pre-
conference and post-conference, whenever feasible. If any observation report
identifies a “needs improvement” or “unsatisfactory" area, the employee shall
receive an additional observation and observation report, if possible, prior to the
preparation of the final written evaluation.

5.    The final evaluation conference will be held no later than thirty (30) calendar days
prior to the end of the work year. The evaluator will present the written
evaluation and discuss the matter with the bargaining unit member. The
bargaining unit member must sign the evaluation signifying only that he/she has
read the document and has been provided the opportunity attaching a written
response which shall become a part of the permanent records.

6.    The established contract violation resolution procedure of the District may be
utilized for processing any disputes which arise over evaluation procedures. It is
agreed that the substance and content of any evaluation shall not be grievable.

ARTICLE XVI

TEACHER SAFETY

1.    The district will consider, upon recommendation of the student’s teacher,
excluding from a class, any student who suffers from a contagious or infectious
disease; or suffers from a physical or mental disability which could cause, by
his/her attendance, to be detrimental to the welfare of other students; or acts in
such a way that good cause exists for such student’s exclusion. The District will
act upon the bargaining unit member’s recommendation within five (5) days or
less. Should the District fail to follow the bargaining unit member’s
recommendation, upon written request it shall provide the unit member with its
reason in writing.

2.    The Board shall not take any action against a bargaining unit member who uses
reasonable restraint to protect himself/herself or a student while acting within the
scope of his/her duties.

3.    Bargaining unit members shall immediately report cases of assault suffered by
them in connection with employment to the principal or other immediate
supervisor.

4.    As used in this Article, “In connection with their employment” shall include any
voluntary activities (such as field trips) provided that the proper procedures and
permission were approved prior to voluntary action.

5.    The District shall protect bargaining unit members from loss of personal property
while acting in the discharge of their duties. The District shall reimburse
bargaining unit members for such losses resulting from any property being lost,
stolen, damaged, soiled, or destroyed.

equivalent of at least one-half (1/2) of the number of days of service

5)    The member will be paid a pro-rata share of his/her full-time salary.

absence from their full time teaching position.

7.    Teachers electing to remain in the Job Sharing Program subsequent to the first
year shall resign their full time position prior to March 1 and shall in the same
action be rehired as part-time employees at the agreed upon percentage level.
This action shall not constitute a break in service but shall effect a reduction of
tenure rights to the percentage of the shared teaching position.

8.    Teachers wishing to return to full time teaching will be rehired full time with full
tenure rights in positions for which they are qualified before outsiders are hired.
Teachers returning to full time positions will be reassigned at the same time as
others returning from leaves of absence.

9.    Salaries, leave benefits, health and welfare benefits, and contributions to State
Teachers Retirement System shall be proportionate to the time served.

10.    Adjunct duties of teachers will be required as designated in the teacher’s contract
proportionate to the contract percentage to which they are assigned. In addition,
the teachers may be required by the principal to attend meetings (faculty
meetings, parent conferences, planning meetings and/or professional
development) which do not occur during their work duty time. Such required
meetings will be compensated at the teacher hourly rate.

ARTICLE XXIV

PEER ASSISTANCE AND REVIEW PROGRAM

1.    The District and the Association agree that the District in any school year,
beginning on July 1, 2000, may implement the California Peer Assistance and
Review Program for Teachers (PAR Program) under California Education Code
Sections 44500-44508 consistent with the provisions in the Article. As
specifically cited by the Legislature, the PAR Program is intended to be a “critical
feedback mechanism that allows exemplary teachers to assist veteran teachers
in need of development in subject matter knowledge or teaching strategies, or
both.” The District and the Association also agree that the PAR Program in any
school year may be contingent upon the actual receipt by the District of adequate
funding from the State specifically designated for the PAR Program. The District
may take any action so that the District does not lose any funding under the PAR
Program as long as such action is consistent with this Article and there is prior
consultation with CETA Leadership. Pursuant to Education Code Section
44506(c), the District also may expend PAR program funds from the State for
any other allowable purpose which includes, but is not limited to, any program
that supports the training and development of new teachers as long as there is
prior consultation with the CETA Leadership.

2.    For any school year which the District determines that a teacher or teachers will
participate in the PAR Program, the Superintendent or designee shall establish a
Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel composed of two permanent
classroom teachers and the Superintendent or designee. The Joint Teacher -
Administrator Review Panel shall schedule its meetings. To have a meeting,
two-thirds (2/3) of the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel must be
present and one (1) member must be the Superintendent or designee.

•    The two permanent classroom teachers shall be selected by the
classroom teachers in a secret ballot election conducted by the
Superintendent and the Association. Classroom teachers at Cardiff
School will select one (1) member, and classroom teachers at Ada Harris
School will select one (1) member. In order to be on the ballot, a
permanent teacher must submit a written communication to the
Superintendent at least three (3) calendar days prior to the election
expressly indicating a desire to be on the ballot, and also expressly
indicating a commitment to perform all the required duties of the Joint
Teacher - Administrator Review Panel for the school year. The
Superintendent or designee and Association President may seek
applications for the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel.

•    The teacher on the ballot receiving the most number of votes from the
classroom teachers actually voting at each school shall be appointed to
the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel. The ballots shall be
counted by the Superintendent or designee and the Association President
or designee.

40

3.    The duties of the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel include the
following: Select no more than three (3) consulting teachers by majority vote for
the school year consistent with this Article and applicable law no later than
October 15. The number of consulting teachers shall be determined by the
number of teachers participating in the PAR Program which, in any year, may
require no consulting teachers. As required by Education Code Section
44502(c)(2), before a consulting teacher is selected, the Joint Teacher-
Administrator Review Panel shall conduct at least one (1) classroom observation
of the candidate which shall be arranged and scheduled by the Superintendent or
designee.

•    Review and recommend whether to combine the District’s authorized
Education Code Section 44503(d)

•    Assign each consulting teacher to a specific program participant(s) with
the approval of the Superintendent or designee. If a program participant
disagrees with the consulting teacher assigned to him/her, an appeal may
be made in writing to the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel.

Any such appeal must be made within five (5) calendar days and must
specify the reason(s) for the appeal. The Joint Teacher - Administrator
Review Panel shall make the final decision on whether to continue the
assignment of the consulting teacher.

•    Make confidential written recommendations to the Governing Board
regarding participants in the PAR Program, including forwarding the
names of any participants in the PAR Program who, have sustained
improvement. These written recommendations will be reviewed in closed
session. Members of the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel
shall not disclose the confidential written recommendations except to the
Superintendent or designee and the Governing Board.

•    Annually evaluate the impact of the PAR Program and submit
recommendations for improvement to the Governing Board and the
Association no later than June 15th. This annual evaluation may include,
but is not limited to, interviews or surveys of program participants.

4.    Any consulting teachers selected by majority vote of the Joint Teacher -
Administrator Review Panel shall meet the following criteria:

•    Any permanent classroom teacher who has experience as classroom
teacher for at least five (5) out of the last seven (7) years.

•    Demonstrated exemplary teaching ability, as indicated by, among other
things, effective communication skills, subject matter knowledge, and
mastery of a range of teaching strategies necessary to meet the needs of
pupils in different contexts.

41

•    No disciplinary action by the Governing Board or any administrator within
the last four (4) years including no Notice of Unprofessional Conduct
under Education Code Section 44938(a), no Notice of Unsatisfactory
Performance under Education Code Section 44938(b), and no evaluation
document designating any needs improvement or unsatisfactory
performance.

•    Not a member of the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel who
meet all the eligibility criteria.

5.    The duties of any consulting teachers shall include the following:

•    Professional assistance to program participants to improve teaching skills
and subject matter knowledge. Education Code Section 44500(b)(4)
provides that there shall be a cooperative relationship between the
consulting teacher and the principal with respect to the process of peer
assistance and review. The professional assistance should include, but
not limited to, specific written suggestions for improvement, sample
instructional materials, possible specific lesson plans, and advice on
available resources.

•    Prepare written peer review reports of program participants and submit
them to the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel no later than May
31st. the peer review reports shall be based upon clear performance goals
established at the beginning of the peer review process which shall be in
writing, aligned with pupil learning, and consistent with Education Code
Section 44662 (a provision in the Stull Act.)

•    Peer review reports also shall be based on no less than four (4) classroom
observations of at least thirty (30) minutes each for each program
participant, and on no less than two (2) conferences with each program
participant regarding classroom observations. Since the classroom
observations require release time for the consultant teacher, such
observations must be scheduled with the approval of the principal.

6.    A permanent classroom teacher shall become a program participant under any of

the following two (2) conditions:

•    During the previous school year the teacher received any unsatisfactory
rating on an evaluation report completed by an administrator.

•    The teacher volunteers to participate and the Joint Teacher -
Administrator Review Panel and the Superintendent or designee agrees to
the participation.

If, in any school year, there are no program participants, the
Superintendent or designee, after consultation with CETA Leadership,
may determine to provide professional assistance under the PAR program
generally to all classroom teachers, to reduce or eliminate consulting
teachers, and to utilize PAR Program monies for various activities such as
curriculum development or staff development.

42

7.    Peer review reports completed by the consulting teacher shall be placed in the
personnel file of the program participant, the Joint Teacher - Administrator
Review Panel, and the Superintendent or designee. A volunteer program
participant may determine that any peer review report shall be placed in his/her
personnel file. Peer review reports are confidential documents which should be
treated as such. Both the program participant and the Superintendent or
designee may submit a written response which will be attached to the peer
review report in the personnel file prior to the end of the ten (10) day period. A
written response by the program participant is the sole remedy to the peer review
report. A peer review report is not a substitute for an evaluation by an
administrator.

8.    The Superintendent or designee may remove a teacher selected to the Joint
Teacher - Administrator Review Panel, and may terminate the selection of a
consulting teacher, after consultation with the Joint Teacher-Administrator
Review Panel. A replacement shall be selected as soon as possible consistent
with the relevant provision in this Article.

9.    The two (2) classroom teachers selected to the Joint Teacher - Administrator
Review Panel and any consultant teachers selected by the Joint Teacher -
Administrator Review Panel are not considered to be either management or
supervisory employees for purposes of the Educational Employment Relations
Act.

10.    The two (2) permanent classroom teachers on the Joint Teacher - Administrator
Review Panel will be required to perform duties related to their assignment on
the Joint Teacher - Administrator Review Panel outside of the regular word day,
each teacher shall receive hourly pay for PAR duties performed outside of the
regular work day.

11.    Since any consulting teacher will be required to perform duties related to his/her
assignment as a consulting teacher outside of the regular work day, each shall
receive an annual stipend of $1,500 for each teacher who is a participant in the
PAR Program and who is actually served by the consulting teacher for an entire
school year. This amount shall be prorated if the service is for less than an entire
school year. However, if the funding actually received during the school year
from the State specifically designated for the PAR Program is less than budgeted
and anticipated amount, then the stipend will be decreased by an amount
proportional to the decrease in State funding.

12.    Program participants shall receive no additional compensation because of their
participation in the PAR Program.

13.    The remaining funds actually received during the school year from the State
specifically designated for the PAR Program may be used by the Superintendent
or designee for various activities such as curriculum development or staff
development, substitutes and other educational activities, as long as there is
prior consultation with CETA Leadership.

Only provisions 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 are subject to the Grievance Procedure.
