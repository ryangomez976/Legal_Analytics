ARTICLE I: AGREEMENT

A. This is an Agreement made and entered into this 1st day of July, 2007 between the Nevada City School
District (hereinafter referred to as “District”) and the Nevada City Faculty Association,
CTA/NEA(hereinafter referred to as “Association”). This Agreement is entered into pursuant to the
provisions of the Rodda Act.

ARTICLE II: RECOGNITION

A. The District recognizes the Nevada City Faculty Association as the exclusive representative for all
certificated employees of the District, except Charter School teachers, all substitutes, management,
confidential and supervisory employees.

ARTICLE III: DISTRICT RIGHTS

A.    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its powers and authority to direct, manage and
control the District to the full extent of the law.

B.    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authorities, duties, and responsibilities by the District, the
adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof, and the use of judgment and
discretion in connection therewith, shall be limited only by the specified and expressed terms of this
Agreement, and then only to the extent such specific and expressed terms are in conformance with the law.

ARTICLE IV: ASSOCIATION RIGHTS

A.    Association business will be conducted by unit members outside established work hours. District property
may be used when use of facilities will not interfere with the school program and/or duties of unit members
and will not interfere with the right of employees to refrain from participation.

B.    The Association may use the school mailboxes and designated bulletin board spaces subject to the following
conditions:

1.    All Nevada City Faculty Association official postings shall be signed by an authorized Association
representative.

2.    The Association will not post or distribute information which is defamatory of the District or its
personnel, subject to immediate removal by the District. The Superintendent, as soon as possible, will
inform the President of the Nevada City Faculty Association that information has been removed from
the bulletin board and school mailboxes.

C.    Two Association representatives will exclusively receive time off from duties for the processing of
grievances past Level I of the grievance procedure, Article XIX herein. Hearings shall be outside of
instructional time.

1. Twenty-four (24) hours prior to release from duties for grievance processing the designated
representative shall inform his immediate supervisor in order that an adequate substitute may be
obtained if such is necessary. Time off shall be limited solely to representing a grievant in a conference
with a management person, beyond Level I. Prior to taking release time for processing grievances, the
employee shall get approval from the immediate supervisor.

D.    The Association may use District equipment at reasonable hours when not in use for school business. The
Association will provide its own materials and supplies.

ARTICLE V: ACADEMIC FREEDOM

A. Freedom of individual expression shall be encouraged by the Board to all its teachers, subject to the

following:

1.    Discussion and analysis of controversial issues shall be conducted within the guidelines of the State
Framework and District approved curriculum including Board policies.

2.    Parent or community member viewing instructional material selected by individual teachers shall not
interfere with the teacher’s ability to perform teaching duties.

3.    Parents of a student and community members residing within the school district having concerns
about the continued use of basic text, instructional material or additional individual teacher selected
material shall be referred to the District Textbook Review Committee.

ARTICLE VI: SAFETY CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

A. Unsafe conditions affecting the health and safety of the staff shall be reported to the principal immediately to
be considered for correction by the District.

ARTICLE VII: WORK YEAR

A.    The calendar shall reflect 180 instructional days and three additional teacher work days.

1.    The calendar to be agreed upon mutually.

2.    The day before Thanksgiving, Christmas break, and the last day of instruction shall be a minimum day.
Community Day Instructor shall be compensated at the daily rate of pay for each of these days worked
(total of 1 Zi days).

3.    The three additional teacher work days shall be comprised of two pre-service day (not to be used for
staff development) and one parent conference day. One of the pre-service days shall be a “Flex
Teacher Contract Day.”

4.    Beginning July 1, 2007, an additional teacher workday (“Flexible Teacher Contract Day”) will be
added to the calendar. (This will be approximately a .6% increase on the salary schedule.) This date
will be calendared before the first day of school. Certificated unit members shall work the day that is
scheduled (“Flexible Teacher Contract Day”) or shall submit a time sheet to reflect seven (7) hours of
make-up time, stemming from this non-student work day. The “make-up” time must be approved by
the site administrator and can only reflect time the unit member worked from August 1 until the
scheduled “Flex Teacher Contract Day”. If the unit member does not work on the “Flex Teacher
Contract Day,” and does not complete a time sheet by the start of school, the unit member shall
process paperwork to use a personal day for the “Flex Teacher Contract Day”.

B.    Buy Back Days

1.    The Parties agree to implement the following Staff Development Buy-back Program and add two (2)
additional Staff Development days to the work year.

a.    This implementation plan is contingent upon current legislation being continued and effective,
and that the District will be funded at a minimum of $270 per teacher per day of buy-back time.

b.    The current certificated salary schedule shall reflect compensation of certificated staff at the rate
of their regular per diem for each of the two additional work days.

c.    In the event that the above State funding is reduced, the regular calendar year shall be reinstated
and the certificated salary schedule shall be reduced to reflect the removal of the additional days
of instruction compensation. The parties will meet to reevaluate the calendar structure.

2.    Extended Day: The District will provide a third, voluntary, Staff Development day through five (5)
one and three quarter hours (1.75) sessions outside of the contract day. Teachers must attend four (4)
of the five (5) inservice opportunities to be eligible for compensation which shall be a stipend based
upon the individual teacher’s per diem rate compensation. Part-time employees who choose to

participate in the extended day program must work all four (4) extended days at one and three quarter
hours (1.75) per day to receive one full day’s compensation.

ARTICLEVIII: WORKDAY

A.    The normal work day for all bargaining unit members shall consist of seven (7) consecutive hours, excluding
a thirty (30) minute, duty-free, daily lunch break. Individual flexibility in hours may be coordinated with the
site administrator. Beginning and ending hours are dependent on the student instructional day schedule.

B.    The District shall fully comply with implementing the maximum SB 813 instructional minutes.

C.    In addition to the requirements of the normal work day, all bargaining unit members are required to satisfy
certain responsibilities and duties. These responsibilities, activities, functions and/or requirements shall
include, but are not necessarily limited to, faculty meetings (limited to one hour), IEP meetings, inservice
meetings/programs, parent-teacher conferences, Back-to-School Night, Open House, currently traditional
holiday activities, but shall not be deemed to include student supervision responsibilities which commence
more than thirty (30) minutes before the commencement of the instructional day.

D.    On holidays, Fridays, and days when students are released early and no formal requirements (i.e., inservice,
parent conferences) are scheduled for teachers, the teacher workday shall be adjusted to end after final
dismissal of students.

E.    The District shall provide Seven Hills certificated staff an eight period instructional day including one period
set aside for teacher planning in addition to a lunch period. Certificated staff will be assigned throughout the
day to deal with discipline referrals. This w ill be achieved by assigning some staff members to Advisement
periods and/or through the designation of a Dean of Students or Assistant Principal.

F.    Snow Days: Certificated unit members shall submit a time sheet to reflect seven (7) hours of make-up time,
stemming from a non-student attendance snow-day. The make-up time would need to be approved by the
site administrator.

ARTICLE IX: EVALUATION

A. The District’s evaluation procedures will attempt to provide enough flexibility within the system to allow for
differences in actions, while offering enough direction to identify common expectations. The program of
evaluation and assessment shall apply to all bargaining unit employees according to the employee’s purpose
of employment.

1.    Objectives

a)    Unit members to be evaluated during a particular school year shall be furnished with a copy of the
evaluation procedures and forms (see exhibits) and advised of the guidelines upon which the
evaluation is to be based (see exhibits), no later than one week after fall scheduled parent
conferences of the year in which the evaluation is to take place. The goals and objectives and
standards of performance of the employees will be related to the adopted District goals and
curriculum.

b)    The employee should assess the skills, and/or knowledge of each assigned class. These factors will
affect the identification of objectives, procedures for their attainment, and the methods of
assessment.

c)    Each employee shall submit initial goals and objectives and standards of performance, in writing, to
the administration 20 school days after the beginning of the student calendar year. Either party may
request a meeting to finalize the goals and objectives no later than 30 days after the beginning of the
student year. During the year, modifications of the original objectives and standards may be
necessary. The employee or administration may request a change in these objectives and standards
during the school year by mutual agreement.

2.    Evaluation Procedure:

a)    Observations: Every employee shall be observed at least one time a year during a year of formal
evaluation. This provision does not limit the number of observation and/or conferences any
employee may receive or request. Classroom observations for the purpose of evaluation shall be as
follows:

(1)    Observations shall last at least 30 minutes or preferably, one full teaching lesson.

(2)    A written memo, relative to the observation, shall be given to the employee within seven
working days (see exhibits).Either party may request a conference to discuss items relative to
the observation.

(3)    In the event that an administrative directive is on the observation memo, the employee shall be
provided with a description of the areas needing improvement. The evaluator will confer with
the employee and make specific written recommendations regarding areas of improvement in
the employee’s performance. An employee who receives an unsatisfactory observation shall be
entitled to a subsequent observation and/or conference in an effort to rectify any problems that
were cited. Should subsequent observations reduce or eliminate the deficiencies identified in
the observation, the evaluator shall indicate in writing on the observation memo that
deficiencies have been reduced. The employee will sign this memo and have an opportunity to
attach a written response.

(4)    If a unit member will receive a single observation for evaluation purposes, it shall occur no
later than March 15.

b)    Formal Stull Evaluation: Each employee shall be evaluated every year. At the administrator’s
suggestion, a permanent employee may mutually agree to be formally evaluated every other school
year as set forth in the Education Code. If an employee is scheduled to be formally evaluated during
a particular school year, but is granted a leave of absence, such evaluation shall take place during the
first year of return. Procedures for formal evaluation shall be as follows:

(1)    Evaluations (conferences and documentation presentation) shall be completed no later than 30
days prior to the end of the instructional year.

(2)    The employee’s signature acknowledges only that the employee has received the document.
The employee may attach a written statement which shall become part of the permanent
record.

(3)    If seven (7) of eight (8) Standards (Items 1-6, 9-10) on the Evaluation Summary (Exhibit C)
are marked unsatisfactory, then the unit member shall be referred to the Peer Assistance and
Review Program.

(4)    Additionally, according to AB 954, the evaluation and assessment may occur at least every
five years. Eligible bargaining unit members must have permanent status, have been employed
at least 10 years with the school district, are highly qualified, as defined, and whose previous
evaluation rated the employee as meeting or exceeding standards. The teacher and evaluator
must mutally agree to the extended time line and either party may withdraw this consent at any
time.

3. Miscellaneous:

a)    No member of the bargaining unit other than the employee being evaluated shall participate in the
normal evaluation process.

b)    The employee has the right to have a witness present during any of the evaluation procedures

excluding unannounced classroom observation time.

c)    Any information used in the evaluation must be information obtained through direct observation by
the evaluator or information contained in the personnel file.

d)    An official personnel file for each employee shall be maintained at the District Office. Materials in
the employee’s personnel file shall be made available for inspection by the employee, with the
exception of documents shielded under Education Code Section 44031. With prior written
authorization by the employee, a representative may review the employee’s file or accompany the
employee in review of the file. Employees shall have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of
personnel file materials upon request. All materials placed in an employee’s personnel file shall be
dated and signed by the person who prepared the materials. No evaluation material shall be placed in

the file, without written notification to the employee. The employee has the right to respond and
attach a written statement to the file.

ARTICLE XII: FRINGE BENEFITS

A.    Qualified employees shall select a Central Valley Trust health, dental and vision plan. The total dollar
premium for the above benefit package will be as follows and will establish a dollar cap contribution by
the District: effective September 1, 2004 the cap shall be $637.67 per month/121y (yearly cap of
$7,652.00).

1.    The District shall provide the following fringe benefit coverage of all bargaining unit members
working 75 percent or more of full time and their eligible dependents (i.e., for purposes of health
benefit coverage only, 75% of full time is 75% X # of contracted days X 7 hours) except when a 7
hour employee is hired to complete an otherwise full-time assignment for that year.

2.    Employees working less than 75% or more of full time may elect to be covered by existing
insurance programs so long as they pay their prorated share of the premiums by payroll deduction.
The District shall contribute a prorated amount of the cap equal to the percentage worked by the
employee.

3.    Retired District unit members may retain the District health benefit package at their own expense,
regardless of age, at the same monthly premium rate as regular employees. Retirees who elect
Medicare coverage as the primary insurer at age 65 or thereafter may continue to use CVT as the
secondary insurance carrier at their own expense and will pay the prevailing rates for such coverage.
Health premium payments shall be made monthly, in advance, and arrangements shall be made with
the District Business Office prior to retirement.

B.    Other current employees may elect to continue with existing coverage or any other plan allowed by CVT
and the employee would be responsible for paying any increased cost for that coverage above the
District’s contribution.

C.    Continuing Education and Professional Growth is encouraged for all regular full time and part time
employees. A maximum of $300 will be allowed for full time certificated employees and pro-rated for
part time employees for college courses, other classes, and conferences with prior approval by the
District (see also Article XX E5, PAR). Allowable reimbursable expenses may include registration fees
or tuition, books, mileage expenses, substitute cost and other approved expenses. Educational courses
must enhance school district educational programs. Receipts must be presented before reimbursement.

1.    The employee must submit a Pre-Authorization Form approved by the school site principal.

2.    The employee can be reimbursed within approximately two (2) weeks for registration, tuition, and
books, mileage, and other approved expenses with receipts.

3.    For accountability, course completion documentation or evidence of attendance must be given the
District Office. If the course or conference is not completed, the individual must reimburse the
district within 120 days. Payroll deduction authorization must be signed.

ARTICLE XIII: FAIR SHARE

It is agreed by the parties to this contract that there shall be an agency fee provision as provided for in
Government Code Sections 3540.1 (1) and 3546.3 and E.C. 45061.

A.    Any unit member who is a member of the Nevada City Faculty Association or who has applied for
membership, may authorize their deduction of unified membership dues, initiation fees and general
assessments in the Association. Pursuant to such authorization, the District shall deduct one tenth
(l/10th) of such dues from the regular salary check of the employee each month for ten months.
Deductions for the employee who signs authorization after the commencement of the school year shall
be prorated to complete payments by the end of the school year.

B.    Any unit member who is not a member of the Nevada City Faculty Association or who does not make
application for membership within 30 days of the effective date of this agreement or within thirty (30)
days from the date of commencement of assigned duties within the bargaining unit shall become a
member of the Association or pay a fee in an amount equal to unified membership dues, initiation fees
and general assessments payable to the Association in one lump sum cash payment. In the event that a
unit member shall not pay such fee directly to the Association, or authorize payment through payroll
deduction as provided in section A, the Association shall so inform the District and the District shall
immediately begin automatic payroll deduction as provided in Education Code section 45061 and in the
same manner as set forth in Section A of this Article. There shall be no charge to the Association for
such mandatory agency fee deductions.

C.    As per 3546.3 of the Government Code, any unit member who is a member of a religious body whose
traditional tenets or teachings include objections to joining or financially supporting employee
organizations or who have conscientious objections to joining or financially supporting employee
organizations shall not be required to join or financially support NCFA/CTA/NEA as a condition of
employment except such unit member shall pay, in lieu of a service fee, sums equal to unified
membership dues, initiation fees and general assessments to the following non-religious, non-labor
charitable funds exempt from taxation under Section 501 (c) (3) of Title 26 of the internal Revenue
Code:

a.    United Way of Western Nevada County

b.    Nevada City Schools Foundation

c.    Red Cross of Western Nevada County

d.    Nevada County Hospice of the Foothills

e.    Nevada City School PAL (Pupil Assistance Liaison)

The Association shall inform the District of all non-members, religious/conscientious exemptions. In the
event that a unit member shall not pay such fee directly to the Association, or authorize payment through
payroll deduction as provided in Section A, the Association shall so inform the District and the District
shall immediately begin automatic payroll deduction as provided in Education Code section 45061 and
in the same manner as set forth in Section A of this Article. There shall be no charge to the Association
for such mandatory agency fee deductions.

D.    The association agrees to furnish any information needed by the District to fulfill the provisions of
section C of this Article.

E.    The sole function of the District as contained in this Article is to provide for the collection of dues
through payroll deduction. Any employee/Association concern, which might arise relating to this
Article, is an internal affair of the Association and shall not affect the District

F.    The Association agrees to pay the District all reasonable legal fees and legal costs incurred in defending
against any court action and/or administrative action before the Public Employment Relations Board
challenging the legality or constitutionality of the agency fee provisions of this Agreement or their
implementation. The Association shall have the exclusive right to decide and determine whether any
such action or proceeding shall or shall not be compromised, defended, tried or appealed.

ARTICLE XV: LEAVES OF ABSENCE

A. Sick Leave:

1.    Each bargaining unit member shall be entitled to ten (10) days paid Sick Leave each year. Unused Sick
Leave shall accrue from school year to school year. Sick Leave entitlement shall be prorated for part-time
employees.

2.    Upon returning from leave, the bargaining unit member will submit a statement of the number of Sick Leave
days used and the reason for the absence on a form provided by the District.

3.    In cases where the District has a reasonable suspicion of abuse of this leave provision in which case, a
medical verification could be requested at any time.

4.    The District may require a unit member to present a medical release to return to duty from leave due to
serious illness or injury.

5.    The District shall provide each unit member with a written statement of the accrued total amount of Sick
Leave available to the member. This statement shall be provided no later than October 1 of each school year.

6.    Acknowledging that use of regular and accumulated Sick Leave as stated herein reduces a unit member’s
Sick Leave entitlement for his/her own illness or injury and potentially reduces a unit member’s retirement

allowance, a unit member may use, in any school year, up to one-half (1/2) of their annual allowance of Sick
Leave credited per Paragraph A1 above (ie. 5 days) and any or all accumulated Sick Leave to care for a child,

B.    Differential Pav Leave:

1.    During each school year when a unit member has exhausted all earned Sick Leave, including all accumulated
sick leave, additional non-accumulative leave shall be available for a period not to exceed five (5) school
months.

2.    The amount deducted from the bargaining unit member’s salary during the five (5) month period shall be the
amount actually paid a substitute employee to fill the position during the leave. If no substitute is employed,
the amount deducted shall be the amount that would have paid the substitute had a substitute been employed.

3.    A bargaining unit member shall not be provided more than one five (5) month period per illness or accident.
However, if a school year is terminated before the five (5) month period is exhausted, the employee may take
the balance of the five (5) month period in a subsequent school year.

4.    When a bargaining unit member exhausts all available leave, including accumulated Sick Leave and the five

(5) month Differential Pay Leave, and continues to be absent on account of illness or accident, and is not
medically able to resume the duties of his or her position, the employee shall, if not placed in another
position, be placed on a re-employment list for a period of 24-months if the employee is on probationary
status or 39-months if the employee is on permanent status.

5.    When the unit member is medically able, during the 24 or 39-month period, the employee shall be returned to
employment in a position for which he or she is credentialed and qualified.

C.    Industrial Accident Leave

1.    Unit members will be entitled to Industrial Accident Leave according to the provisions of Education Code
section 44984 for personal injury which has qualified for workers’ compensation benefits under the
provisions of the Schools Insurance Group.

2.    Industrial Accident Leave shall not exceed sixty (60) working days in any one fiscal year. Such leave shall
not be accumulated from year to year. Industrial Accident Leave shall commence on the first day of absence
and shall not be charged to Sick Leave.

3.    When Industrial Accident or Illness Leave overlaps into the next fiscal year, the unit member shall be entitled
to take only the amount of unused leave remaining for the same illness or injury.

4.    The District has the right to have the unit member examined, at the District’s expense, by a qualified
physician selected by the member from a panel of three physicians. The physician will be utilized to assist
the District in determining the length of time the unit member will be unable to perform his or her assigned
duties and the degree to which the teacher is disabled from the injury.

5.    The unit member shall endorse to the District all workers’ compensation benefit checks from Schools
Insurance Group. The District shall, in turn, issue the employee the appropriate salary warrants for payment
of the employee’s full salary, less the normal taxes and other authorized deductions.

6.    If the unit member fails to endorse to the District any workers’ compensation benefit check received on
account of the industrial accident or illness, the District shall deduct from the unit member’s salary warrant
the amount of such disability indemnity actually paid to the unit member.

7.    Return to work programs established by the District shall be consistent with current contract language.

D.    Pregnancy Disability Leave:

1.    Unit members are entitled to use Sick Leave (Paragraph A) and Differential Pay Leave (Paragraph B) for
disabilities caused or contributed to by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth and recovery therefrom on the
same terms and conditions governing leaves of absence for other illness or medical disability.

2.    A unit member absent as a result of pregnancy disability may choose to be placed on unpaid leave during the
period of pregnancy disability.

3.    The unit member and her physician or practitioner shall determine as far in advance of the anticipated date of
childbirth as is feasible the date on which her pregnancy will disable her from the performance of her duties.
She shall then report that date to her site administrator so that substitute services may be arranged.

4.    A unit member and her physician or practitioner shall determine and report to the unit member’s site
administrator the date on which she is likely to be physically capable of returning to her duties following the
termination of her paid or unpaid pregnancy disability leave. In case of suspected abuse, the District may
require verification of such disability through a physical examination of the employee, at the District’s
expense, by a physician selected by the member from a panel of three physicians who have been
recommended by the District.

5.    The unit member on leave for pregnancy disability shall be entitled to return to the same position or a
position comparable to that held at the time the leave commenced.

6. At the conclusion of all paid leave and any unpaid leave taken as a result of disabilities caused or contributed
to by pregnancy, a unit member shall be entitled to utilize up to twelve (12) weeks of unpaid Family Medical
Leave Act/Califomia Family Rights Act (“FMLA/CFRA”) leave to care for her child. During this period of
up to twelve (12) weeks of unpaid FMLA/CFRA leave, the District shall continue to pay its maximum
monthly contribution toward the cost of the employee’s health and welfare benefits and the employee shall be
obligated to pay her monthly contribution toward the cost of such benefits by the tenth (10th) of each month.

E. Family Care Leave

1.    Unit members may take unpaid leave for a total of twelve (12) weeks in a twelve (12) month period to care
for a newborn or newly adopted child, for the unit member’s serious health condition or for a seriously ill
family member. Family members shall include spouse, son, daughter or parent.

a.    Serious health condition involves either:

1.    Inpatient care in a medical facility; or

2.    Continuing treatment by a health care provider that includes:

(a)    A period of incapacity requiring absence from work or other regular daily activities of more
than three consecutive calendar days, and any subsequent treatment or period of recovery,
provided that there is medical treatment on a least two occasions or a regimen of continuing
treatment;

(b)    Incapacity due to pregnancy;

(c)    Incapacity due to a chronic serious health condition that is, a condition that requires periodic
visit for treatment, continues over an extended period of time and may have episodic periods of
incapacity;

(d)    Permanent and long-term incapacity due to a condition for which treatment may not be
effective;

(e)    Any absence to receive multiple treatments either for restorative surgery or for a condition that
would likely result in a period of incapacity of over three calendar days in the absence of
treatment, such as chemotherapy and dialysis.

b.    Son or daughter means biological, adopted or foster child, step-child, legal ward, or child of person in
loco parentis who is under 18, or if older, incapable of self care because of a mental or physical
disability.

c.    Parent means biological parent or person who stands or stood in loco parentis, and does not include
parents in law.

2.    In the case of the unit member requesting unpaid leave for care of an ill or injured family member, the unit
member must submit, with the request for the leave, a statement from the treating physician or practitioner
that includes the date the condition commenced or will commence, the probable duration of the condition,
the need for the employee’s care of the individual, and the estimated length of time the employee’s care is
needed.

3.    An employee on FMLA leave because of his or her own serious illness shall use all accrued paid Sick Leave
and Differential Pay Leave at the beginning of any otherwise paid FMLA leave period and such paid leaves
used for a unit member’s own serious health condition shall run concurrently with and reduce the total of
unpaid FMLA leave on a day by day basis. For any portion of the 12 weeks of FMLA not covered by paid
leave, the District shall continue its contribution for group health insurance for any remaining portion of the
12 weeks for which the employee is on unpaid leave.

4.    Acknowledging that the use of regular and accumulated Sick Leave as stated in Paragraph A5 could
potentially reduce a unit member’s retirement allowance and would reduce a unit member’s Sick Leave
entitlement for his/her own illness or injury, a unit member may use Sick Leave to the extent set forth in

Paragraph A5 to care for a child, parent or spouse with a serious health condition. Paid leaves used for this
purpose shall run concurrent with the twelve (12) weeks of unpaid family care leave.

5.    A unit member is required to give at least thirty (30) days advance notice before FMLA leave is to begin if
the need for the leave is foreseeable. When thirty (30) days notice is not possible, the unit member shall
give as much notice as practicable.

6.    Unit members may request an additional period of unpaid leave for the employee’s own serious illness or to
care for an ill family member. The provision of such leave is at the discretion of the Board of Trustees of
the District.

7.    On timely return to work following the expiration of the FMLA leave period, an employee is entitled to the
same or a comparable position.

F.    Bereavement Leave:

b.    An accident involving the unit member’s person or property, or the person or property of the employee’s

ARTICLE XIX: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

A.    Definitions

1.    A “grievance” is a formal written allegation by a unit member who has been adversely affected by a
violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the specific provisions of this Agreement. Actions to
challenge or change the policies of the District as set forth in the rules and regulations or administrative
regulations and procedures must be undertaken under separate legal process.

2.    A “day” is a day in which the District Office is open for business.

3.    The “immediate supervisor” is the lowest level administrator having immediate jurisdiction over the
grievant.

4.    ‘Time limits” are as set out in this procedure and may be extended by mutual written agreement.

5.    “Representation” means employees may avail themselves of the Association Representation at any and
all steps of this procedure.

6.    No reprisals shall be taken by any member or representative of the District or the Association against
any grievant, party of interest, member of the bargaining unit or other participant in the grievance.

7.    Group grievance, i.e., involving the same or similar circumstances, would be resolved in total by
processing one of the individual grievances as selected by the Association.

8.    The Association has a right to file a grievance on behalf of its members.

B.    Informal Level

1.    Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant shall attempt to resolve it by an informal

conference with the grievant's supervisor.

C.    Formal Level

1.    Level I

a.    Within fifteen (15) days after the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the grievance, or
after the grievant should have had reasonable knowledge of such occurrence or act, the grievant
must present such grievance in writing on the appropriate form to the immediate supervisor. This
statement shall be a clear, concise statement of the grievance, the circumstances involved, the
decision rendered at the informal conference, and the specific remedy sought.

b.    The supervisor shall communicate a decision to the employee in writing within five (5) days after
receiving the grievance. If the supervisor does not respond within the time limits, the grievant may
appeal to the next level.

c.    Within the above time limits, either party may request a personal conference with the other party.

2.    Level II

a.    In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level I, the grievant may appeal the
decision on the appropriate form to the Superintendent or designee within five (5) days. This
appeal should include a copy of the original grievance, the decision rendered, and a clear, concise
statement of the reasons for the appeal.

b.    The Superintendent/designee shall communicate a decision within eight (8) days after receiving
the appeal. Either the grievant or the Superintendent/designee, may request a personal conference
within the above time limits. If the Superintendent/designee does not respond within the time
limits, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

3.    Level III

a. In the event the grievant is not satisfied with the recommendation(s), the grievant may appeal the
decision in writing within five (5) days to the Board of Trustees.

b.    The Board alone has the power to render an administrative determination of the grievance within a
reasonable period of time after having given full opportunity for all parties to present such
testimony, witnesses and/or evidence as each party deems appropriate.

c.    Upon conclusion of all presentations, the Board of Trustees shall separate themselves from all
advocating parties and consider their decision. Upon reaching their decision, the Board of Trustees
shall reassemble with all advocating parties and verbally announce the decision. The Board shall
provide the advocating parties with a draft written copy of the decision and rationale for such
decision.

d.    The grievant, if not satisfied with the Board’s decision, may seek civil redress.

D. Miscellaneous

1. If a grievance arises from action or inaction on the part of the administration at a level above the
immediate supervisor, the aggrieved party shall submit the grievance in writing directly to the
Superintendent and begin at Level II.

ARTICLE XX: PEER ASSISTANCE REVIEW

The Nevada City Faculty Association and the Nevada City School District agree to cooperate in the design and
implementation of a peer assistance program. Teachers referred to the program deserve to have the best
resources available provided to them in the interest of improving performance to a successful standard.

A.    Joint Committee

1.    The Joint Committee shall consist of eleven (11) members, the majority of whom shall be permanent
certificated employees who are chosen to serve by the Association for a term of 3 years, one from each
NCE and Gold Run, two from Deer Creek and Seven Hills. All District Administrators shall serve on the
Joint Committee.

2.    Two-thirds of the members of the Joint Committee, including at least one administrator, must be present.
Such meetings may take place during the regular teacher workday. Teachers who are members of the
Joint Committee shall be released from their regular duties to attend meetings, without loss of pay or
benefits.

B.    Referred Participating Teachers

1.    A Referred Participating Teacher is a teacher with permanent status who receives assistance as a result
of an unsatisfactory final Stull evaluation. In making the referral, the evaluating principal shall state in
writing the areas of deficiency and a history of interventions attempted with the Referred Participating
Teacher. The areas of deficiency that trigger a PAR referral are employee performance as it reasonably
relates to:

a.    The progress of pupils towards the established Board standards of expected pupil achievement at
each grade level in each area of study.

b.    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the employee.

c.    The employee’s adherence to curricular objectives (planning and preparation.)

d.    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment, within the scope of the
employee’s responsibilities.

2.    The Joint Council shall select a Consulting Teacher from the panel of Consulting Teachers provided to
work with the Referred Teacher. Each Referred Participating Teacher shall receive no less than ten (10)
hours of assistance per semester from the Consulting Teacher.

3.    Assistance shall normally be for one school year. The Joint Committee may recommend additional
intervention.

The Joint Committee

The Joint Committee serves as the governing body for the Peer Assistance and Review Program (PAR) and

determines program guidelines that are consistent with the terms of the Collective Bargaining Agreement.

A.    Composition

The Joint Committee shall consist of eleven (11) members, the majority of whom shall be permanent
certificated employees who are chosen to serve by the Association for a term of 3 years, one from each
NCE and Gold Rim, two from Deer Creek and Seven Hills. All District Administrators shall serve on
the Joint Committee.

B.    Meetings

1.    The Joint Committee shall meet in June of each year to determine if any individual certificated
employee shall be referred to the committee.

2.    The joint Committee shall establish its own meeting schedule. To meet, two-thirds of the
members of the Joint Committee, including at least one administrator, must be present.

3.    Decisions shall be made by consensus where possible. Action must be taken on affirmative
vote of at least 5 members, the majority of which are certificated employees. Members shall not
vote in any matter that represents a professional or personal conflict of interest.

4.    The Committee shall select a chairperson for the Committee and keep minutes.

C.    Responsibilities

1.    Determine the number of Consulting Teachers needed each year based on need.

2.    Issue applications to and select the panel of Consulting Teacher.

3.    Notify, in writing, applicants for Consulting Teacher of the selection or non-selection for the

program.

4.    Provide annual training for the Joint Committee members and Consulting Teachers.

5.    Send notification, in writing, of participation in the PAR program to the Referred Teacher, the
Consulting Teacher, and the site principal.

6.    Notify the Referred Teacher of the assigned Consulting Teachers.

7.    Administer the budgetary dollars available to the program and allocate funds to the Consulting
Teacher within budget established by the district.

8.    Request periodic written progress reports, and review documentation and/ or the final report
submitted by the Consulting Teachers.

10.    Meet with the Referred Teacher if so requested.

11.    Make final recommendation to the Governing Board regarding the Referred Teacher's progress
in the PAR Program.

12.    Develop firm timelines, consistent with the Collective Bargaining Agreement and statutory
evaluation timelines.

13.    Evaluate annually the impact of the program and make recommendations to the Association
and Board for program improvements.

14.    Serve as ad hoc committee to determine Staff Development needs and make recommendations
to the CCC.

D.    Number of Consulting Teachers

The Joint Committee shall annually determine the number of Consulting Teachers needed to
implement the Peer Assistance and Review Program. All aspects of the program should be considered
including:

1.    Number of unsatisfactory evaluation referrals,

2.    Training needs of the Consulting Teachers and Joint Committee,

3.    Release time needed by Consulting Teachers, joint Committee and Referred Teacher(s),

4.    Administrative costs and compensation for the Consulting Teacher and the Joint Committee
participants as agreed upon in the Collective Bargaining Agreement,

E.    Consulting Teacher Selection Process

Application forms for the position of Consulting Teacher shall be available from the District and the
Association. At any time the District and the Association recognize the need, they shall jointly notify
all employees in the District that the PAR Joint Committee is seeking applications for Consulting
Teachers. The candidates meeting the basic job requirements may be selected to participate in an
interview process.

F.    Budget Development and Administration

1.    The Joint Committee is responsible for developing the annual budget for the Peer Assistance
and Review Program and administering the budgetary dollars available to the program, as
approved by the Board of Trustees. Consideration needs to be given to the following:

a.    On-going comprehensive training for Consulting Teachers and the Joint Committee,

b.    Adequate compensation and supplies for the Consulting Teachers, Joint Committee and
the chairman,

c.    Adequate release time for the Consulting Teachers and the Joint Committee,

d.    Administrative support of the Joint Committee and the Consulting Teachers,

e.    Professional development costs for the Participating Teachers including adequate
release time.

2.    Remaining funds shall be allocated to Staff Development and a Voluntary Peer Support
Program.

G.    Annual Evaluation of the PAR Program

The Committee shall annually evaluate the impact of the Peer Assistance and Review Program in order
to improve the program. This evaluation may include:

1.    Interviews of the Consulting Teacher and Participating Teacher.

2.    Surveys of both Consulting Teacher and Participating Teacher.

The Joint Committee shall submit the evaluation and any recommendations for improvement to the
Governing Board and the local Association.

the Consulting Teacher.

The Referred Teacher

A teacher referred by the Principal to receive assistance as a result of an unsatisfactory final evaluation is the
Referred Teacher. The specific criteria, which determine an unsatisfactory evaluation, shall be mutually
agreed upon in the collective bargaining agreement. The principal shall state in writing the areas of deficiency
and a history of interventions attempted with the Referred Teacher.

1.    If teachers demonstrate areas of need other than those listed below, these should be addressed
outside of the PAR process.

•    The progress of pupils toward the established Board standards of expected pupil achievement
at each grade level in each area of study.

•    The instructional techniques and strategies used by the employee.

•    The employee's adherence to curricular objectives.

•    The establishment and maintenance of a suitable learning environment, within the scope of the
employee's responsibilities.

2.    The term of assistance shall normally be for one school year.

3.    It is essential that the Due Process Rights of the Referred Teacher are protected at all times and
that a checklist be developed that reflects each step in the process. The Referred Teacher shall:

a.    Have the right to receive, in writing, a progress report after any observation by the
Consulting Teacher,

b.    Have the right to receive copies and attach a written response to any communications the
Consulting Teacher has with the Joint Committee,

c.    Have the right to receive the final report in writing, consult with the Consulting Teacher,
and attach a written response to the final report,

d.    Shall have the right to address the Joint Committee,

e.    Sign the final report indicating only that the report has been seen by the Referred
Teacher.

4.    The results of the Referred Teacher's participation in the PAR Program shall be made available
for placement in the individual's personnel file.
