The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding agreement
(“Agreement”) by and between the COALINGA-HURON UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT (“District"
or “Employer”) and the COALINGA-HURON UNIFIED TEACHERS ASSOCIATION/CTA/NEA
(“CHUTA”, “Association” or “Exclusive Representative”) and employee organization (jointly
referred to as “the parties").

This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Government Code sections 3540 through 3549,
commonly referred to as Education Employment Relations Act (“EERA” or “the Act”).

This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2014 until June 30, 2016.

The terms of this Agreement shall not be applied or implemented in an arbitrary, capricious,
or discriminatory manner.

During the 2014/2015 school year, CHUTA and the DISTRICT agree to reopen the contract
for articles pertaining to Salaries and Teacher Evaluations. The remainder of the contract will
be closed until the agreement terminates.

ARTICLE - 2
DEFINITIONS

“Employee” refers to any certificated employee who is included in the bargaining unit and is
therefore covered by the terms and provisions of this Agreement. When a provision of this
Agreement specifies a classification of employees (e.g., counselor, school psychologist) or
category of employees (e.g., non-lnstructional), the provisions relates solely to the identified
classification or category. Employee classifications included in the bargaining unit are:

2.1.1    "Counselor"    refers    to    a    unit member who is assigned to provide guidance services to

students pursuant to a “Pupil Personnel Services' credential.

2.1.2    “Nurse"    refers    to a unit member who is assigned to provide health services to students

pursuant to a “School Nurse” credential.

2.1.3    “Psychologist”    refers    to    a unit member who is assigned to provide assessment and

program-related services to students pursuant to a “School Psychologist" credential.

2.1.4    “Speech    Pathologist”    refers    to a unit member who is assigned to provide specialized

speech-related services to students pursuant to a “Speech Pathologist” credential.

2.1.5    “Teacher"    refers    to    a    unit    member who possesses a teaching credential and is assigned

to provide classroom-based instructional services.

2.1.6    “Library    Media    Teacher"    refers to a unit member who is assigned to provide instruction on

use of library materials pursuant to a “Teacher Librarian Services” credential.

“Days” means days during which the employee is regularly scheduled to work.

“Business Day” means any day in which the Administration Office is open for business.

“Daily Rate of Pay” means the employee’s annual salary divided by the number of days he or she
is required by the District to be on duty.

been a violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the terms of this Agreement.

Representative, who files a grievance.

“Party in Interest" is any person who might be required to take action or against whom action
might be taken in order to resolve the claim.

“Class Size” means the number of students for whom a teacher is responsible in the classroom
including - Special Needs students.

“Increment” means the step-by-step, vertical advancement on the salary schedule.

"Transfer" refers to a change of assignment from one school site to another school site, e.g., from
Bishop to Sunset. A transfer may be teacher-initiated (“voluntary") or District initiated
(“involuntary”).

“Reassignment” refers to a change in assigned grade level (elementary schools), or subject area
or department assignment (secondary level). At the secondary level, a change in assigned
classes or class periods is not a reassignment. A reassignment may be employee-initiated
(“voluntary”) or District-initiated (“involuntary").

Kindergarten through third grade teachers can be reassigned between K-3 grade levels. Fourth
and Fifth grade teachers can be reassigned between 4-5 grade levels.

Secondary reassignments are limited by credential and within a site. Alternative Education is
considered a separate site. Teachers cannot be reassigned in consecutive years. Teachers
cannot be reassigned between communities of Huron and Coalinga. Teachers will be notified of
reassignment by May 15.

ARTICLE - 6
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Purpose: The purpose of this procedure is to secure, at the lowest possible administrative level,
equitable solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise affecting the welfare or
working conditions of employees. Both parties agree that these proceedings will be kept as
informal and confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the procedure.

6.1.1    Nothing contained herein will be construed as limiting the right of any employee having a
grievance to discuss the matter informally with any appropriate member of the
administration to have the grievance adjusted without intervention by the Association,
provided that the adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement and
that the Association has been given an opportunity to be present as such adjustment and
to state its views, at levels after the informal stages.

6.1.2    Time    limits    may be extended by mutual agreement.

6.1.3    In the event a grievance Is filed at such a time that it cannot be processed through all the
steps in the grievance procedure by the end of the school year, and, if left unresolved until
the beginning of the following school year, could result in harm to the grievant, the time
limits set forth herein will be reduced so that the procedure may be exhausted prior to the
end of the school year or as soon as is practicable.

Informal Resolution: Before filing a formal grievance, the employee shall attempt to resolve the
grievance by means of an informal conference with the immediate supervisor or appropriate
administrator.

Formal Resolution: Level One - Within ten (10) days of the act or omission giving rise to a
grievance or within ten (10) days of when the grievant might reasonably have known of the
grievance, the grievant must file a formal grievance with the grievant's immediate supervisor.

6.3.1    The written statement of the grievant shall include the general and specific grounds of
the grievance, the date or dates the alleged act or omission occurred, the specific
provisions or contract violated, and any other pertinent information which might assist in
determining the proper resolution of the grievance.

6.3.2    The written statement shall also include the remedy sought by the grievant.

6.3.3    The immediate supervisor shall communicate the decision to the grievant in writing
within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance. If the administrator does not respond

ARTICLE - 7

PROFESSIONAL DUES OR FEES AND PAYROLL DEDUCTIONS

7.1    An employee who is a member of the Association, or who has applied for membership, may sign
and deliver to the District an assignment authorizing deduction of unified membership dues,
initiation fees and general assessments in the Association. Such authorization shall continue in
effect from year to year unless revoked in writing between June 1 and September 1 of any year.
Pursuant to such authorization, the District shall deduct one-eighth (1/8) of such dues from the
employee's regular salary check each month for eight (8) months. Deductions for employees
who sign such authorization after the commencement of the school year shall be appropriately
prorated to complete payments by the end of the school year.

7.2    The Association, as the Exclusive Representative of all the employees in the bargaining unit, will
represent all such employees, Association and non-Association members, fairly and equally. No
employee shall be required to join the Association, but membership In the Association shall be
made available to all employees who apply consistent with the Association constitution and by¬
laws. No employee shall be denied Association membership because of race, creed, color or sex.
The District agrees to deduct from the monthly earning of all member employees in the
Association an amount of money equivalent to the monthly dues certified by the Association as
the current dues uniformly required of all members and pay the amount to the Association on or
before the end of the month following the month in which such deduction was made. Changes in
the amount of dues to be deducted shall be certified by the Association twenty (20) days before
the effective date of the change. The District will provide the Association with a list of employees
from whom such deductions are made with each monthly remittance to the Association.

7.3    An employee who is not a member of the Association, or who does not make application for

membership within thirty (30) days from the date of commencement of employment, shall pay to
the Association a fee in an amount equal to unified membership dues, initiation fees and general
assessment, payable to the Association; provided, however, that the employee may authorize
payroll deduction for such fee in the same manner as provided in paragraph 7.1 of the Article. In
the event that an employee does not pay such a fee directly to the Association or authorize
payment through payroll deductions, as provided In paragraph 7.1, the District shall deduct such
amount and remit to the Association as required below. In no instance will an employee be
dismissed because of failure to pay the dues or remain In good standing with the Association.
However, current members must maintain their membership for the duration of the Agreement.
Any employee who was permitted to do so and chose not to join the association as provided for
in the negotiated 1993-1996 District/Association Agreement shall retain that option without
being subject to dues, fees or assessments.

Shared Teaching Assignments

19.8

Preamble: The parties realize that for personal reasons, a teacher sometimes may prefer to share
the responsibility, salary and benefits of a full-time teaching position with a fellow teacher. When
those sharing a position have compatible work patterns and similar goals, this situation can
benefit teachers and students alike. By accommodating requests for shared teaching
assignments, the District is more likely to retain teachers of outstanding capabilities. The
Superintendent or designee may authorize two teachers to share one full teaching assignment
under terms and conditions specified herein.

19.9

Eligibility: Shared teaching assignments shall be available only to teachers who possess a clear
credential, who are already employed by the District and who have mutually agreed to work
together.

19.9.1

designee by March 1 of the preceding school year.

19.9.2

by first working day of June.

19.9.3

assignment and determine whether or not it may continue the following year. He or she
shall notify the teachers of this decision on or before March 15 of each year.

19.9.4

she shall be designated as on a partial leave of absence in order that the other teacher

attended or not. The job share relationship does not absolve the teacher from acquiring
such information.

19.10.2

nights. Both teachers also may be expected to attend specific in-service meetings and to
work a full day on at least the first two days for the first week of school.

19.10.3

Both teachers shall assume full responsibility for the class instructional program. They will
regularly meet to jointly develop lesson plans and ensure clear lines of communication
with parents/guardians.

The principal shall approve the teachers’ working calendar before school opens.

19.13

An employee who may be subject to disciplinary action shall be notified of the right to Association

level before the Education Code dismissal:

warning letter.

Step 2: Upon the second occurrence, the immediate supervisor shall place a written reprimand in
the file.

Step 3: Upon the third occurrence, the employee shall be placed on a paid leave of three to five
working days.

Step 4: Upon the fourth occurrence, the employee shall be placed on unpaid leave of not more
than ten working days.

date of the warning or reprimand.

based on a public complaint shall be provided a written statement of the allegation or
allegations and given an opportunity to respond prior to issuance of the warning or
reprimand.

differentiated activities

levels of challenge

•    Samples of materials used to challenge students

thinking

•    Copy of teacher made test and/or other assessment
measures

•    Copy of rubric used for student project
