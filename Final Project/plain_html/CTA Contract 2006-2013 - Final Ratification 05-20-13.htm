PREAMBLE

A.    Parties: The Articles and provisions contained herein constitute a bilateral and binding
agreement (“Agreement”) by and between the Board of Education of the Chowchilla
Union High School District (“Board” or “District”) and the Chowchilla High Teachers’
Association, Charter No. 126, CTA/NEA (“Association”), an employee organization.

B.    This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, sections 3540-3549 of the
Government Code.

C. Term: This Agreement shall remain in full force and effect from July 1, 2006 until June
30, 2013.

ARTICLE I

RECOGNITION

1. Exclusive Representative: The District confirms its recognition of the Association as the
exclusive representative for those unit members employed in the following
classifications: Classroom Teachers and Counselors; and excluding all other positions
not designated, including, but not limited to: Superintendent, Principal, Vice Principal,
Learning Coordinator, Coordinator of Student Services, Dean of Students, Director of
Guidance, Principal or Head Teacher - Continuation School, Media Director, Athletic
Director, Consultants, Cadet Teachers, Part-time Extra-Curricular Personnel, and Student
Teachers.

ARTICLE II
DISTRICT RIGHTS

2.1 The Board, on its own behalf and on behalf of the electors of the District, hereby retains
and reserves unto itself, without limitation, all powers, rights, authority, duties and
responsibilities conferred upon and vested in it by the laws and the Constitution of the
State of California, and of the United States, but without limiting or foregoing the right
to:

2.1.1    Direct the executive management, organization, and administrative control of the
District and its properties and facilities.

2.1.2    Direct the work of its employees, determine the time and hours of operation, and
determine the kinds and levels of services to be provided and the methods and
means of providing those services, including entering into contracts with private
vendors for services;

2.1.3    Hire all employees, and, subject to the provisions of law, to determine their
qualifications and the conditions for their continued employment, discipline,
dismissal, or demotion; and to promote, assign, suspend, and transfer all such
employees (Education Code sections 44932 through 44944);

2

2.1.4    Establish educational opportunities of students; to determine mission goals and
objectives; to evaluate programs; to determine the curriculum; to determine
staffing patterns; to determine the number and kinds of personnel required in
order to maintain the efficiency of District operations; and

2.1.5    Build or modify facilities; establish budget procedures and determine budgetary
allocations; determine the methods of raising revenue; and take action on any
matter in the event of an emergency.

2.2    The exercise of the foregoing powers, rights, authority, duties and responsibilities by the
Board, the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance thereof,
and the use of judgment and discretion in connection therewith, shall be limited only by
the specific express terms of this Agreement and then only to the extent such specific and
express terms hereof are in conformance with the Constitution and laws of the State of
California and the Constitution and laws of the United States.

2.3    The District retains its right to amend, modify or rescind policies and practices referred to
in this Agreement in cases of emergency. The determination of whether or not an
emergency exists is solely within the discretion of the Superintendent in consultation with
the Board, and is expressly excluded from the provisions of Article IV, “Grievance
Procedure.” The Board shall, by ratification, make its determination by adoption of a
resolution, which shall include a statement of the reasons for the declaration of an
emergency.

2.4    The District agrees to inform the Association of the proposed school calendar for the
following school year.

ARTICLE IV
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES

4.1    Definitions:

4.1.1    A “grievance” is an allegation by a grievant that he or she has been adversely
affected by an interpretation, violation, or misapplication of the specific
provisions of this Agreement. An action to challenge or change the terms of this
Agreement shall not be considered a grievance.

4.1.2    A “grievant” is a member of the bargaining unit covered by this Agreement who
files a grievance or the Association.

4.1.3    A “day” is any working day in which the District administrative office is open for
business.

4.2    General Information:

4.2.1 In the event the District fails to adhere to any decision deadline set forth in this
Article, the grievant shall have the right to appeal automatically to the next step
(higher level). Failure of the unit member to adhere to any submission deadline
shall mean that the unit member accepts the Districts decision and waives any
right to further appeal. However, deadlines in this Article may be extended by
mutual written agreement of the grievant and the District.

5

4.2.2    Until final disposition of a grievance takes place, the grievant is required to
conform to the original direction of his/her supervisor.

4.2.3    All documents dealing with the processing of a grievance shall be filed separately
from the official personnel files of the participants except a final decision
rendered pursuant to this article.

4.2.4    A bargaining unit member whose presence (whether as a grievant, representative,
or witness) is necessary during his/her working hours at any mutually scheduled
meeting, conference or hearing required by this Article shall, upon notice to
his/her immediate management supervisor, be released without loss of pay.

4.2.5    A grievant may terminate his/her grievance at any time by giving written notice to
the District. Failure to comply with time limits, to attend scheduled meetings
relating to the grievance, or to provide requested information at the grievant’s
disposal relating to the subject matter of the grievance shall be deemed a
termination of the grievance by the unit member. The District shall be required to
give written notice of such termination to the unit member.

4.2.6    When possible, the District shall schedule meetings regarding the grievance at
times that do not interfere with instructional time.

4.2.7    Any grievant shall have his or her identity and grievance kept confidential.

4.3    Informal Resolution of Grievances:

4.3.1    Any unit member who believes he or she has a grievance shall present the concern
orally to the immediate management supervisor within fifteen (15) days after the
grievant knew, or reasonably should have known, of the circumstances which
form the basis for the grievance. Failure to do so within the time period specified
above renders the grievance null and void. The supervisor shall hold a discussion
with the unit member in order to attempt to resolve the concern.

4.4    Formal Resolution of Grievances:

4.4.1    Step 1. If the unit member’s concern is not resolved during the informal
discussion and the unit member wishes to pursue the matter, the unit member
shall present the grievance to the immediate management supervisor in writing,
on forms provided by the District, within ten (10) days after the oral decision by
the supervisor based on the informal discussion. The written grievance shall
include:

a. A description of the specific grounds of the grievance, including

names, dates, and places necessary for a complete understanding of the
grievance;

6

b.    A listing of the specific provisions of this Agreement which are
alleged to have been violated or misapplied;

c.    A listing of the reasons why the supervisor’s proposed resolution of
the problem is unacceptable; and

d.    A listing of specific actions requested of the District, which will
remedy the grievance.

Within ten (10) days after the receipt of the written grievance, the supervisor shall
investigate the grievance and give his/her decision in writing to the grievant.

4.4.2    Step 2. If the grievance issue is denied at Step 1, he or she may appeal the
decision within five (5) days to the Superintendent or his/her designee.
The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance, the decision
rendered at Step 1, and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for the
appeal. Within fifteen (15) days after the Step 2 appeal is filed, the
Superintendent or his/her designee shall investigate the grievance and give
issue a decision in writing to the grievant.

4.4.3    Step 3. If the grievance is denied at Step 2 the grievant, the grievant shall
have the right to appeal the matter to the Board within five (5) days of the
decision rendered by the Superintendent or his/her designee.

The Board may set the grievance for hearing within a reasonable time but not less
than ten (10) days following the grievant’s appeal to the Board. The decision of
the Board shall be final.

4.5 Representation: A unit member may request an Association representative to be present
at any step of the grievance procedure. The grievant shall be present at each step of the
grievance procedure.

4.5.1 If the District schedules a meeting regarding the grievance during the
instructional time of the grievant, a Designated Association representative
shall receive time off without loss of compensation for the purpose of
processing grievances beyond the informal level, subject to the following
conditions:

4.5.1.1 No later than fourteen (14) days following the ratification of this
Agreement, the Association shall designate in writing to the
Superintendent no more than two (2) unit members who shall be
entitled to time off as Association grievance representatives.

7

4.5.1.2    The representative shall notify the District at least twenty-four (24)
hours prior to release from duties in order that a substitute may be
obtained, unless a conference is scheduled between the grievant
and the administration with less than twenty-four (24) hours notice
to the grievant.

4.5.1.3    Such time shall be limited solely to accompany the grievant for a
conference with a management person and in no way shall include
the use of time for matters such as gathering information,
interviewing witnesses or preparing presentations.

4.5.1.4    The grievant shall be represented by no more than one Association
representative per grievance.

ARTICLE V
LEAVES

5.1    General Provision: Any statutory or regulatory leave benefits not addressed in this
Agreement are not subject to Article IV, “Grievance Procedure.”

5.2    Personal Illness and Injury Leave:

5.2.1    Unit members who work full time shall be entitled to ten (10) days leave
of absence for illness or injury with full pay for each year of service. A
unit member who receives remuneration for less than a full regular year of
service shall be entitled to that proportion of ten (10) days of sick leave as
his/her actual remuneration for a year of service bears to the amount
he/she would have received for a full regular year of service.

5.2.2    If a unit member has utilized all of his or her current and accumulated sick
leave and is absent from his or her duties on account of illness or injury,
the unit member shall be entitled to differential pay for a period not to
exceed five (5) school months, provided that the provisions of paragraph

5.2.4 below are met. The five-month period shall begin on the first day
following the exhaustion of the unit member’s current and accumulated
sick leave. The amount deducted from the unit member’s salary under this
provision shall not exceed the amount actually paid a substitute employee
employed to fill the position during the leave, or, if no substitute is
employed, the amount which would have been paid to a substitute. The
differential pay leave provided under this provision does not accrue from
year to year.

5.2.3    If a unit member does not utilize the full amount of leave as authorized in
paragraph 5.2.1 above in any school year, the amount not utilized shall be
accumulated from year to year.

8

5.2.4    Upon request by District Administration, a unit member shall be required
to present a medical doctor’s certificate verifying the personal illness or
injury and/or a medical authorization to return to work. If the illness or
injury exceeds three (3) consecutive days, the District Administration may
require a certified medical specialist to visit or otherwise contact the unit
member and make all necessary inquiries in order to be fully informed as
to the nature and severity of the illness or injury, and to report such
findings to the Superintendent or his/her designee. If the report concludes
that the absence is not due to personal illness or injury, or that the illness is
not sufficiently severe to warrant continued absence, then the
Superintendent or his/her designee, after notice to the unit member, may
refuse to grant such leave. If requested by the District Administration, a
unit member shall not return to work until he or she submits a medical
doctor’s authorization to return to work.

5.2.5    Whenever possible, a unit member must contact his or her immediate
supervisor as soon as the need to be absent is known, but in no event less
than two (2) hours prior to the start of the work day to permit the employer
time to secure a substitute. Failure to provide adequate notice shall be
grounds for denial of leave with pay or for disciplinary action.

5.2.6    A unit member who is absent for 1 to 2 hours shall have deducted one-
third day from his or her accumulated leave; if the absence exceeds two
hours and is less than three-and-three-quarter hours, one-half day shall be
deducted; if the absence is three-and-three-quarter hours or more, a full
day shall be deducted.

5.2.7    A unit member shall not be allowed to return to work and shall be placed
on leave without pay if the unit member fails to notify the District of the
unit member’s intent to return to work at least two (2) hours prior to the
close of the preceding work day if such failure results in a substitute being
secured.

5.2.8    Each unit member shall be notified of the amount of his or her
accumulated leave by no later than September 10 of each school year.

5.2.9    Unpaid “family care leave” may be requested for up to twelve (12) weeks
in a one-year period for (1) the birth or adoption of an employee’s child;

(2) the placement of a foster child with the employee; or (3) the serious
health condition of an employee’s family member. Family members shall
include spouse, child, legal ward, adult dependent child, parent, or any
person with whom the employee has an “in loco parentis” relationship.

9

5.2.10 Unpaid “medical leave” may be requested for up to twelve (12) weeks in a
one-year period for an employee’s own serious health condition.

5.3 Personal Necessity Leave:

5.3.1    Unit members may use up to seven (7) days of accumulated sick leave
during each school year for purposes of personal necessity , one of them
being a “no tell” day (see 5.3.4). Personal necessity is defined as any of
the circumstances listed below:

5.3.1.1    Death or serious illness of a member of the unit member’s
immediate family as defined in paragraph 5.4.1 below;

5.3.1.2    Accident involving the unit member’s person or property, or the
person or property of a unit member’s immediate family as defined
in paragraph 5.4.1 below; or

5.3.1.3    Other personal necessity allowed at the discretion of the
Superintendent or his/her designee, provided that under no
circumstances shall leave be available for purposes of personal
convenience, for the extension of a holiday or vacation period, for
matters which can be taken care of outside work hours, or for
recreational or political activities.

5.3.2    Prior to the utilization of personal necessity leave a unit member must
obtain prior written approval from the Superintendent or his/her designee,
except for leave taken due to the circumstances described in paragraphs

5.3.1.1 or 5.3.1.2 above. Requests for such leave shall be submitted to the
office of the Superintendent not less than two (2) work days prior to the
date of the requested leave. Should the circumstances described in
paragraphs 5.3.1.1 or 5.3.1.2 arise, the unit member shall make every
effort to comply with District procedures to enable the District to secure a
substitute.

5.3.3    Under all circumstances a unit member shall verify in writing that the
personal necessity leave was used only for purposes set forth in paragraph
above. If it was not, the leave will be denied and the unit member may be
subject to appropriate discipline if the leave was used for purposes other
than stipulated.

5.3.4    A unit member may use at his/her election, one personal necessity leave
day without the restrictions listed above, except that requests for such
leave be submitted to the office of the superintendent no less than two (2)

10

days prior to the date of the requested leave. Under no circumstances shall
such leave be available for the extension of a holiday or vacation period.

5.4    Bereavement Leave:

5.4.1    A unit member shall be entitled to a maximum of three (3) days paid leave
of absence, or a maximum of five (5) days of paid leave if the unit
member travels a minimum of 325 miles one-way, on account of the death
of any member of his or her immediate family. The term “immediate
family” as used in this Agreement means mother, father, grandmother,
grandfather, or grandchild of the unit member or of the spouse of the unit
member, and the spouse, son, son-in-law, daughter, daughter-in-law,
brother or sister of the unit member, or any person living in the immediate
household of the employee.

5.4.2    Unit members may request permission of their immediate supervisor to be
absent without pay on account of the death of any relative not designated
as immediate family.

5.4.3    A unit member taking bereavement leave shall notify the District of the
expected duration of the leave.

5.4.4    In the event the District suspects that a unit member has abused the leave
privileges provided herein, the District may request verification, including,
but not limited to, death certificate, notice of death, or receipts evidencing
travel to a destination at least 325 miles away.

5.5    Pregnancy Disability Leave:

5.5.1    Unit members are entitled to use sick leave as set forth in paragraphs 5.2.2
and 5.2.3 for disabilities caused or contributed to by pregnancy,
miscarriage, childbirth, and recovery. The beginning and ending date of
the leave shall be determined by the employees attending physician or
practitioner and shall be provided to the District in writing. This leave
shall not be used for childcare, child rearing, or preparation for child
bearing, but shall be limited to those disabilities as set forth above.

5.5.2    Unit members are entitled to leave without pay or other benefits for
disabilities caused or contributed to by pregnancy, miscarriage, childbirth,
and recovery there from when sick leave as set forth in paragraphs 5.2.1
and 5.2.2 has been exhausted.

5.5.3    A unit member who takes pregnancy disability leave shall be entitled to
return to a position comparable to that held at the time the leave
commenced.

11

5.5.4 A unit member who has exhausted all available paid time off may request
a leave without pay for child rearing purposes.

5.6 Industrial Accident and Illness Leave:

5.6.1    Unit members are entitled to industrial accident and illness leave
according to the provisions of Education Code section 44984 for any
injury or illness that is determined to have arisen out of the unit member’s
employment with the District. Such leave shall not exceed sixty (60) days
during which the schools of the District are required to be in session or
when the unit member would otherwise have been performing work for
the District in any one fiscal year for the same industrial illness or injury.

5.6.2    Allowable leave shall not be accumulated from year to year.

5.6.3    Industrial accident or illness leave shall commence on the first day of
absence.

5.6.4    The total of a unit member’s disability indemnity and the portion of salary
due the unit member during industrial accident or illness leave shall not
exceed his/her full salary. If the unit member fails to endorse to the
District any temporary disability indemnity check received on account of
the industrial illness or injury, the District shall deduct from the unit
member’s salary warrant, the amount of such disability indemnity actually
paid to and retained by the unit member.

5.6.5    Industrial accident or illness leave shall be reduced by one day for each
day of authorized absence regardless of a temporary disability indemnity
award.

5.6.6 An industrial accident or illness leave may overlap into the next
fiscal year by no more than the amount of leave remaining at the end of
the fiscal year in which the illness or injury occurred.

5.6.7    Any unit member receiving benefits as a result of this paragraph 5.6 shall,
during periods of injury or illness, remain within the State of California
unless the Board authorizes travel outside the State.

5.6.8    In order to be eligible for Industrial Illness and Accident Leave, a unit
member who is absent from his/her assigned duties as a result of an
employment related illness or accident shall report said illness or accident
to the District within five (5) days after he or she becomes aware or can
reasonably be expected to be aware that the accident or illness is
employment related. The report shall be submitted on a form, which shall
be made available at each school site.

12

5.7 Judicial Leave:

5.7.1    Unit members shall be entitled to as many days of paid leave as are
necessary for appearances as a juror before any duly convened court of
law. A copy of the jury summons shall be attached to the request for
leave. If the unit member receives any juror’s fees while on leave under
this provision, such fees less mileage fees shall be remitted to the District.

5.7.2    Unit members shall be entitled to paid leave for appearance in any court,
under subpoena, as a witness. A copy of the subpoena shall be attached to
the request for leave. If the unit member receives any witness fees, such
fees shall be remitted to the District.

5.7.3    Unit members shall verify the need for appearance as a juror or witness by
calling the evening before the appearance is scheduled. If a unit member
fails to do so and his/her presence is not needed, paid leave will not be
granted.

5.7.4    Any unit member who is granted judicial leave shall only be required to
work that portion of remaining hours which, when added to the number of
jury duty hours served, equals his/her regularly assigned work day.

5.7.5    Unit members who fail to remit juror or witness fees shall have the
amount due the District deducted from their pay warrant.

5.8 Family and Medical Leave:

5.8.1    Unpaid family care/medical leave may be requested for up to twelve (12)
weeks in a one-year period for (1) the birth or adoption of a unit member’s
child; (2) the placement of a foster child with the unit member; or (3) the
serious health condition of the unit member or the unit member’s family
member. Family members shall include spouse, child, legal ward, adult
dependent child, parent, or any person with whom the unit member has an
“in loco parentis” relationship.

5.8.2    To qualify for any leave under section 5.8.1, an employee must have
completed one (1) year of continuous service with the District, consisting
of at least 1250 hours of service.

5.8.3    Unpaid family care leave for the care of a newborn or newly adopted child
must be completed within one (1) year of the qualifying event. If the
District employs both parents, leaves taken by one or both of the parents
for such care may not exceed a combined total of twelve (12) weeks in a
one-year period.

13

5.8.4    A “serious health condition” is an illness, injury, impairment, or other
physical or mental condition, which requires either inpatient care or
continuing treatment or supervision by a licensed physician or osteopath,
or other health care provider designated by the Secretary of Labor.

5.8.5    Any request for medical leave for a unit member’s own serious health
condition or for family care leave to care for a family member with a
serious health condition must be supported by medical certification from a
health care provider. Any request for an extension of the leave also must
be supported by an updated medical certification.

The medical certification for a family member with a serious health condition
shall include (a) the date on which the serious health condition commenced; (b)
the probable duration of the condition; (c) the health care provider’s estimate of
the amount of time needed for family care; (d) the health care provider’s
assurances that the health care condition warrants the participation of the unit
member to provide family care; and (e) in the case of intermittent or reduced
schedule leave where medically necessary, the probable duration of such a
schedule.

The medical certification for leave for the unit member’s own serious health
condition shall include (a) the date on which the serious health condition
commenced; (b) the probable duration of the condition; (c) a statement that, due
to the serious health condition, the unit member is unable to perform the functions
of his/her position; and (d) in the case of intermittent or reduced schedule leave
where medically necessary, the probable duration of such a schedule. In addition,
the certification may, at the unit member’s option, identify the nature of the
serious health condition involved. The District may require the unit member to
obtain a second opinion from a doctor of the District’s choosing at the District’s
expense. If the unit member’s health care provider and the doctor providing the
second opinion do not agree, the District may require a third opinion, also at the
District’s expense, performed by a mutually agreeable doctor who will make a
final determination.

5.8.6    The unit member shall first exhaust his/her sick leave where the leave is
for the unit member’s own serious health condition and may use accrued
sick leave with consent of the District for other conditions warranting
family care leave.

5.8.7    Any paid leave(s) used for the purpose of this section shall run concurrent
with, and shall reduce, the total of twelve (12) weeks of Family Care/
Medical Leave on a day-for-day basis.

5.8.8    District-paid health insurance plan benefits shall be continued during
Family Care/Medical Leave of not more than twelve (12) weeks per year,

including any paid leaves used for this purpose. Unit members who do not
return from Family Care/Medical Leave for reasons other than their own
disability shall be responsible for reimbursing the District for the health
plan premium costs during the period(s) of the leave, unless the unit
member was in paid status during the entire leave.

5.8.9 Except in emergencies, all requests for Family Care Leave must be
submitted at least thirty (30) calendar days in advance. All requests are
subject to verification of need. The specific starting and ending dates of
the leave shall be coordinated with the needs of the District and are subject
to approval by the Superintendent.

5.8.10    Family Care/Medical Leave shall not constitute a break in service
for longevity or seniority purposes, but a unit member shall not
earn credit for sick leave during unpaid Family Care/Medical
Leave.

5.8.11    Unit members shall be restored to the same or an equivalent
position upon return from Family Care/Medical Leave.

5.8.12    The provisions of Family Care Leave shall be in accordance with
Federal and State law, and any changes thereto.

5.8.13    Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article, a unit member
may use in any calendar year the unit member’s accrued and
available sick leave entitlement, in an amount not less than the sick
leave that would be accrued during six months at the current rate of
entitlement, to attend to the illness of the unit member’s child,
parent or spouse. All conditions and restrictions placed by the
District upon a unit member’s use of sick leave shall also apply to
the use by a unit member of sick leave to attend to an illness of
his/her child, parent or spouse. This section does not extend the
maximum period of Family Care/Medical Leave to which a unit
member is entitled, regardless of whether the unit member receives
sick leave compensation during that leave.

5.9 Miscellaneous Leaves:

5.9.1 Association Leave. The Association president shall be entitled, upon
written notification to the Superintendent, to one-half day of leave per year
for the purpose of conducting Association business. Such notification
shall be delivered at least three (3) days in advance.

5.9.2 Other Leaves. Upon recommendation of the Superintendent, the Board
may grant leave for purposes other than those described above, taking into
consideration the following factors: (a) the financial status of the District;

(b) benefit of the proposed leave to the educational process; and (c) benefit
of the proposed leave to the District. Such leave may be paid or unpaid
and shall be determined on a case-by-case basis.

5.9.2.1    Unit members on paid leave shall be granted all benefits accorded other
members of the unit.

5.9.2.2    The terms and conditions of the leave shall be agreed upon in writing, and
shall include, but not be limited to:

5.9.2.2.1    A description of the leave program;

5.9.2.2.2    A stipulation to post-leave service of not less than two (2) full

years;

5.9.2.2.3    An indemnification bond to protect the District against the unit
member’s failure to successfully complete the leave program or
to render the necessary post-leave service; and

5.9.2.2.4    Appropriate reporting procedures as may be designated by the
Superintendent.

5.9.2.3 The application for and granting of such leaves of absence shall be
in writing. A unit member on such leave shall notify the
Superintendent by March 15 of the school year as to an intent to
return to employment in the District. Failure to do so shall be
considered an abandonment of position.

ARTICLE VHI
PERSONNEL FILES

8.1    Materials in personnel files of unit members that may serve as a basis for affecting the
status of their employment are to be made available for the inspection of the person
involved.

8.2    Such material is not to include ratings, reports or records which:

8.2.1    Were obtained prior to employment of the person involved;

8.2.2    Were prepared by identifiable examination committee members; or

8.2.3    Were obtained in connection with a promotional examination.

8.3    Every unit member shall have the right to inspect such materials upon request, provided
that the request is made during non-instructional time.

8.4    Information of a derogatory nature, except material mentioned in section 8.2 above, shall
not be entered or filed unless the unit member is given notice and an opportunity to
review and comment thereon. Such review shall take place during normal business
hours, and the unit member shall be released from duty for this purpose without salary
reduction. A unit member shall have the right to have attached to any such derogatory
statement, his/her own comments thereon.

8.5    A bargaining unit member or his/her authorized representative(s) who wish to review the
unit member’s personnel file shall:

8.5.1    Request access in writing (such request to be directed to the Director of
Personnel);

8.5.2    Provide written authorization from the unit member (in case of unit member’s
representative(s));

8.5.3    Make no alterations or additions to the file, nor remove any material there from;
and

8.5.4    Sign a log attached to the file indicating date and person(s) reviewing.

8.6    The person(s) who draft and/or place written material in a unit member’s personnel file
shall sign the document and signify the date on which such material was drafted.

8.7    Access to personnel files shall be limited to the members of the District administration on
a “need to know” basis. Board members may request the review of a unit member’s file

at a personnel session of a quorum of the Board. The contents of all personnel files shall
be kept in strictest confidence.

8.8 The District shall maintain one primary personnel file per employee at the District’s
office.

reaches the age of sixty five (65), or until the employee becomes covered

ARTICLE XIII
HOURS OF EMPLOYMENT

13.1    The workday shall be from 8:00 a.m. to 3:40 p.m. However, nothing shall prevent the
District and an individual member of the bargaining unit from entering into an agreement
modifying the aforementioned hours. All unit members are responsible for other
instructional duties which include program development, professional growth activities,
parent conferences, committee assignments, faculty and district meetings, and back-to-
school nights. Unit members may indicate their preference for specific student
supervision duties at the beginning of each school term. Duties shall be reasonably and
equitably allocated among the employees of the unit within the constraints of practicality.
Teachers who are class advisors are excluded from night duty schedule. Teachers who
are responsible for clock, announcing, and spotting at all football games are excluded
from day duty schedule. Additional duties may be negotiated with the Association with
the possibility of compensation.

13.2    Unit members shall have one unassigned period per day set aside exclusively for
preparation and lesson planning, except for minimum days of a period or less. The
District shall schedule such preparation time at such times during the working day as the
District determines to be consistent with the objectives of the District’s instructional
program. Preparation time granted pursuant to this Article shall be considered working
time for all purposes.

13.3    Every unit member shall be entitled to one duty-free, uninterrupted relief period (unless
assigned to rotating duty) and one lunch period. The relief period shall be 15 minutes
long, excluding passing. The lunch period shall be equivalent to the student lunch period
or 35 consecutive minutes, exclusive of passing periods, whichever is longer.

ARTICLE XIV
EVALUATIONS

14. The provisions of this Article shall constitute the procedures to be utilized for evaluation
and assessment of the job performance of certificated employees as set forth in California
Education Code 4466, et sec., or successor, commonly known as the “Stull Bill.” The
evaluation shall relate to the criteria set forth in Education Code 44662 (b), 44662 (c),
and 44662 (f) - if applicable, and the California Standards for the teaching Profession.

14.1    Probationary certificated employees shall be evaluated at least twice each school year
during probationary service. A pre-evaluation conference will be held between the
evaluator and unit member prior to the classroom observation.

14.2    Permanent certificated employees may be evaluated each school year, but shall be
evaluated at least once every two (2) school years. A pre-evaluation conference will be
held between the evaluator and unit member prior to the classroom observation.

14.3

Classroom Observation

14.3.1    Each evaluation shall be based upon at least one scheduled classroom observation
lasting no less than thirty (30) minutes. Within ten (10) days of the observation,
the evaluator and the teacher shall have a post-observation meeting.

14.3.2    A teacher who receives a negative observation report may request and receive one
scheduled subsequent classroom observation. At least two classroom
observations shall take place prior to any negative comments being included in
the Stull evaluation.

responsibilities, or other interference with the operations of the District by the
Association or by its officers, agents, or members during the term of this Agreement,
including compliance with the request of other labor organizations to engage in such
activities.

15.2    The Association recognizes the duty and obligation of its representatives to comply with
the provisions of this Agreement and to make every effort toward inducing all unit
members to do so.

15.3    In the event of a strike, work stoppage, slow down, or other interference with the
operations of the District by employees who are represented by the Association, the
Association agrees in good faith to take all necessary steps to cause those employees to
cease such action.

15.4    It is agreed and understood that any unit members violating this Article may be subject to
discipline up to and including termination by the District.

15.5    It is agreed and understood that in the event this Article is violated the District shall be
entitled to withdraw any rights, privileges, or services provided for in this Agreement or
in District Policy from any unit member who violated the article.

ARTICLE XX
TRANSFERS AND REASSIGNMENTS

20.1    Definitions

20.1.1    A transfer is the movement of a unit member from one work location to another
work location at a different work site.

20.1.2    A reassignment is the movement of a unit member from one subject area to
another subject area at the same work location.

20.1.3    A vacancy is any vacated, promotional or newly created position that the district
determines to fill.

20.2    A unit member may submit a written request for transfer or reassignment at any time,
whether or not a vacancy exists. If the unit member requests that his/her application for
transfer or reassignment be kept confidential, the matter will be treated confidentially.

20.3    The Association agrees that the Superintendent is authorized under Education Code
section 35035 to make assignments of all unit members to vacant positions based upon
the needs of the District. A unit member may request a voluntary transfer by submitting
a letter to the Superintendent via the respective Area Coordinator and Principal. The unit
member shall express his/her preference for the desired site, subject(s), and/or other
assignment. The District shall endeavor to honor requests for voluntary transfers when
the training, experience, and abilities of the requesting unit member match the vacant
position’s job specifications.

20.4    Involuntary transfers/reassignments may be made, based upon the needs of the District as
determined by the Superintendent or his/her designee. The District shall consider the
preferences of the involuntary transferee when making such assignment. However, the
needs of the District shall be paramount. The District shall endeavor to give a minimum

24

notice of five (5) calendar days to a unit member prior to the effective date of such an
involuntary transfer/reassignment.

20.5 The Superintendent shall forward to the Association and post in school buildings a list of
all bargaining unit vacancies, promotional positions and new positions that occur during
the school year and for the following school year upon knowledge of the vacancies. The
list shall contain a closing date, a job description and qualifications necessary to meet the
requirements of the position
