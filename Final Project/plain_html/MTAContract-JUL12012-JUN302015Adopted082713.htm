ARTICLE 1: AGREEMENT AND RECOGNITION

1.1    Agreement

This Agreement constitutes a binding bilateral contract by and between the
Moreland School District (District) and the Moreland Teachers Association,
an affiliate of the California Teachers Association/National Education
Association (Association).

This Agreement is entered into pursuant to Chapter 10.7, Sections 3540-

3549.3 of the Government Code. The term “Agreement” means the written
agreement provided under Government Code Section 3540.1(h).

1.2    Recognition

The District recognizes the Moreland Teachers Association (“Association”) as
the exclusive representative for all permanent, probationary, and temporary
certificated employees who are not otherwise designated under the law as
management, supervisory, or confidential. Substitute teachers are not
included in the certificated bargaining unit.

Any unit member who is a member of the Moreland Teachers

ARTICLE 4: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

4.1    Definitions

4.1.1    A “grievance” is an allegation that the grievant has been directly
and adversely affected by a violation, misinterpretation or
misapplication of the specific provision of this Agreement.

4.1.2    A “grievant” may be any unit member, or group of unit members,
covered by the terms of this Agreement, their Association
Representative or the Association.

4.1.3    A “day” is any day in which the District Office is open for business.

4.1.4    “Limitations” - This procedure shall specifically exclude (1) actions
to challenge or change the general policies of the District as set
forth in the rules and regulations or the administrative regulations
except as provided in this Agreement, and (2) other matters for
which specific methods of review are prescribed by law and noted in
the Agreement, including FEPC, EEOC, OSHA, dismissal and
layoff procedures.

4.1.5    “Immediate supervisor” is the management person having
immediate jurisdiction over the grievant.

4.2    Informal Level

4.2.1    The grievant shall meet with the appropriate supervisor in an
attempt to resolve the grievance informally, identify the potential
grievance, parties affected, if any, and attempt to resolve it in good
faith.

4.2.2    Before filing a formal written grievance both parties to this
Agreement encourage the grievant to informally discuss the
problem with his/her immediate supervisor.

4.3    Formal Grievance - Level I

4.3.1 If the informal discussion fails to resolve the grievance to the
satisfaction of the grievant, a formal grievance may be initiated in
writing no later than thirty (30) days after the event or
circumstances occasioning the grievance. This statement shall be a
clear, concise statement of the grievance, parties involved, specific
section of the Agreement allegedly violated, the circumstances
involved, and the specific remedy sought.

4.3.2

The immediate supervisor shall communicate a decision in writing
to the grievant within fifteen (15) days after receiving the
grievance.

4.4 Formal Grievance - Level II

4.4.1

If the grievant is not satisfied with the decision rendered at Level I,
the grievant may appeal the decision within ten (10) days to the
Sup erinten dent.

4.4.2

The appeal shall include a copy of the original grievance, the
decision rendered at Level I, and a concise statement of the
reason(s) for the appeal.

4.4.3

Within fifteen (15) days after the appeal is filed, the
Superintendent or his/her designee shall complete consideration of
the grievance and render a decision in writing to the grievant.

4.5 Formal Grievance - Level III

4.5.1

If the Association is not satisfied with the decision rendered at
Level II, it may submit the grievance to binding arbitration within
thirty (30) days upon receipt of the decision by the grievant. The
submission shall include a copy of the original grievance and the
decision rendered at Level II.

4.5.2

The parties shall select a mutually agreeable arbitrator. In the
event they are unable to agree on an arbitrator within ten (10) days
of the Association’s submittal of the grievance to arbitration, the
arbitrator shall be selected from a fist of arbitrators from the State
Mediation and Conciliation Service.

4.5.3

If the Association and the Superintendent cannot agree on the
arbitrator from the fist, each party shall alternately strike names
until only one name remains.

4.5.4

Prior to hearing argument or evidence on the merits of a grievance,
the arbitrator shall issue a decision on any arbitrability questions
raised by either party.

4.5.5

The arbitrator’s decision shall be in writing and shall set forth
his/her findings of fact, reasoning, and conclusion on the issues
submitted. The arbitrator shall be without power or authority to
make any decision which requires the commission of an act
prohibited by law or that violates the terms of this Agreement, and
the arbitrator shall interpret this Agreement in accordance with
accepted arbitrable standards of contract interpretation. The

decision of the arbitrator shall be submitted to the Superintendent

of this Agreement.

4.5.6

All costs for the arbitrator’s services, including but not limited to,

cost of any hearing room, shall be borne equally by the parties. All
other costs shall be borne by the party incurring them.

4.5.7

If any party requests a transcript of the proceedings, that party
shall bear the full costs for that transcript. If the parties mutually
request one transcript, the total cost of the transcript shall be
divided equally between the District and the Association.

4.5.8

The arbitrator shall conduct the hearing in accordance with the

and the provisions of this procedure.

4.5.9

Upon mutual agreement of the District and the Association, the

It is the objective of the Association and the District to encourage initiative

promote and achieve this objective, the parties recognize that proposals may

event of such a disagreement, a variance may be warranted. The following

decision-making and at the same time protect the integrity of the negotiated
Agreement.

another’s resources and opinions. View differences as helpful
rather than as a hindrance.

•    Everyone can paraphrase the issue.

•    Everyone has a chance to describe his or her feelings about the
issue.

•    Those who continue to disagree indicate publicly that they are
willing to go along for an experimental try for a prescribed
period of time.

•    All share in the final decision.

APPENDIX F: COMMUNITY RELATIONS COMPLAINTS CONCERNING

SCHOOL PERSONNEL

It is believed that direct communication offers the best opportunity to clarify
misunderstandings or communicate concerns. Hence, a parent or citizen with a
complaint regarding a certificated employee should be encouraged to discuss the
complaint directly with the employee involved on an informal basis. If the parent or
citizen is either not satisfied after meeting with the employee or feels strongly about
not meeting directly with the employee involved, the following procedure shall be
used:

1.    The parent or citizen should contact the employee’s immediate supervisor to
outline the concern or complaint. The supervisor shall then inform the
employee as soon as possible, but within five working days, regarding the
allegations or concerns in an effort to resolve the issue at this level, if
possible.

2.    If the matter is not resolved to the satisfaction of the complainant, the
complainant shall be provided the district form to outline the concerns in
writing. The written concerns shall be submitted to the employee’s
immediate supervisor. The employee shall be given the opportunity to review
the written concerns and to prepare a written response. Both the written
concerns and the employee’s response, if one is prepared, shall be forwarded
to the Superintendent (or designee) within five working days. In the absence
of a written complaint at this level, the district will not pursue further action.

3.    The Superintendent should be informed immediately of any charge that
might have implications for the physical welfare of students or other
personnel. If the Superintendent feels the welfare of students or personnel is
in jeopardy, a temporary reassignment may be made until the matter is
resolved. At the time of reassignment the teacher shall be informed of the
reason for reassignment.

4.    The Superintendent (or designee) shall conduct any investigation deemed
necessary and shall meet with the employee and/or the employee’s immediate
supervisor and the complainant within ten working days. The
Superintendent may take steps to resolve the complaint at this level or may
refer the complaint on to the Board of Trustees for a personnel hearing.

5.    The complainant shall have the right of appeal to the Board of Trustees if the
matter has not been resolved to the satisfaction of the complainant, in which
case the Board shall consider the appeal in Executive Session (G.C. 54957).

If a complaint is filed directly with the Board of Trustees, no direct action will
be taken until it has been determined that steps 1, 2, and 3 have been followed.
