AGREEMENT

This is an agreement between the Cascade Union Elementary School
District (the "District") and the Anderson Cascade Teachers Association (the
"Association").

ARTICLE 1:    RECOGNITION

The District confirms its recognition of the Anderson Cascade Teachers
Association as the exclusive representative for that unit of employees certified
by the Public Employment Relations Board in Case Number S-R-60; S-D-139,
October 10, 1991, which includes all certificated employees and excludes all
management, supervisory, and confidential employees.

ARTICLE 2:    EQUAL    OPPORTUNITY

2.1 The District and The Association recognize that they are required by
law not to discriminate against any person with regard to employment or
association because of race, religion, color, sex, national origin or age, and hereby
declare their acceptance and support of such laws.

ARTICLE 3: EMPLOYEE RIGHTS

3.1 The District and the Association recognize the right of employees to
form, join and participate in lawful activities of employee organizations and the
equal alternative right of employees to refuse to form, join and participate in
employee organization activities.

ARTICLE 4:    DISTRICT RIGHTS

4.1    It is understood and agreed that the District retains all of its
powers and authority to direct, manage and control to the full extent of the law.

4.2    The District retains its right to amend, modify or rescind policies and
practices referred to in this Agreement in cases of emergency. Emergency shall
be defined as an act of God or natural disaster or any condition beyond the
control of the District.

4.2.1 The District also retains all of its powers and authority to direct and

control the District and employees to the full extent of the law including, but not
limited to, the right to: direct the work of its employees; determine the methods,
means, and services to be provided; insure the rights and educational

opportunities of students; determine staffing patterns; determine the number and
kinds of personnel required; maintain the efficiency of District operations; build,
move or modify facilities; develop a budget; develop and implement budget
procedures; and determine the methods of raising revenue.

4.2.2. The exercise of the foregoing powers and authorities by the District,
as well as the adoption of policies, rules, regulations and practices in furtherance
thereof, and the use of judgment and discretion in connection therewith, shall be
limited only by the specific and express terms of this Agreement, and then only to
the extent such specific and express terms are in the conformance with the law.
Exercise of discretionary powers reserved to the District by this provision shall not
be subject to the grievance procedure. Review of this provision shall be limited to
the determination of whether the District reserved the right to exercise the
discretionary powers so listed.

ARTICLE 8: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

8.1    Definitions

A "grievance" is a formal written allegation by a grievant that a specific
provision in the contract has been misinterpreted, misapplied, or violated.

A "grievant" may be any certificated non-management employee(s) of the
District, including the Association or representatives thereof, covered by the terms
of this Agreement.

"Immediate Supervisor" is the lowest level administrator having
immediate jurisdiction over the grievant who has been designated to adjust
grievances.

A "day" is any day, which is a workday (consistent with this contract) for the
involved unit member.

8.2 Informal Level

Before filing a formal written grievance, the grievant should attempt to
resolve a problem through an informal conference with immediate supervisor.

8.3 Formal Level

8.3.1    Level One: If the grievance is not resolved at the Informal Level, the
grievant must present the grievance in writing on the appropriate form to the
immediate supervisor within twenty (20) days of when the grievant knew or
reasonably should have known of the occurrence of the act or omission giving
rise to the grievance.

This statement shall be a clear, concise account of the grievance, the
circumstances involved, the decision rendered at the informal conference, and the
specific remedy sought.

The supervisor shall communicate decision to the grievant in writing
within ten (10) days after receiving the grievance. If the supervisor does not
respond within the time limits, the grievant may appeal to the next level.

Within the above time limits either the grievant or the supervisor may
request another personal conference on the grievance.

8.3.2    Level Two: If a grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level
One, the grievant may within ten (10) days appeal the decision in writing to the
Superintendent or their management designee.

This statement shall contain a copy of the original grievance, the decisions
rendered and a clear, concise statement of the reasons for this appeal.

The Superintendent or designee shall communicate decision to the grievant
within ten (10) days. If the Superintendent or designee does not respond within
the time limits provided, a grievant may appeal to the next level.

8.3.3    Level Three: If a grievant is not satisfied with the decision at Level
Two, or if no written response has been received within twenty (20) days after
receipt of the grievance by the Superintendent or designee, the grievant may
within twenty (20) days after receipt of the decision or expiration of the
Superintendent or designee’s time to communicate a decision, request in
writing that the Superintendent or designee submit the grievance to arbitration.
If any question arises as to whether the arbitrator has jurisdiction, such questions
will be ruled upon by the arbitrator only after an opportunity to hear the merits of
the grievance. If the Association chooses to ask for an arbitrator, the Association
and the District shall attempt to agree upon the selection of an arbitrator. If no
agreement can be reached, they shall request the State of California Mediation
and Conciliation Service to supply a panel of five (5) persons experience in
hearing grievances in public schools. Each party shall alternately strike a name
until only one name remains. The remaining panel member shall be the arbitrator.
The order of the striking shall be determined by lot. The arbitrator shall, as
soon as possible, hear evidence and render a decision on the issue or issues.
The arbitrator will have no power to add to, subtract from, or modify the terms
of this Agreement or the written policies, rules, regulations and procedures of the

8

District. Following a hearing where the arbitrator has had the opportunity of
completely hearing the arguments of both parties, the arbitrator shall issue a final
and binding decision. The fees and expenses of the arbitrator shall be borne
equally by the Association and the District.

8.4 Miscellaneous

Any employee may present grievances in accordance with this article prior
to Level Three without the intervention of the Association so long as the
adjustment is not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement. This District shall
not agree to the final resolution of such grievance until the Association has been
provided a copy of the proposed solution and has been given an opportunity to
file a response.

All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of
a grievance will be filed in a separate grievance file and will not be kept in the
personnel file of any of the participants. No reprisals of any kind will be
taken by the Superintendent or by any member or representative of the
administration or the Board of Trustees against any aggrieved person, any
member of the Association or any other participant in the grievance procedure by
reason of such participation.

ARTICLE 9: PUBLIC CHARGES

9.1    Except those involving criminal matters, all citizens or parent complaints
shall first be referred to the unit member involved for a conference. In the
event that a citizen or parent does not wish to confer with the unit member,
the complaint shall be put in writing on the District form (See Appendix
"B"), with written notification being provided to the unit member. Except
those cases involving criminal matters or disciplinary procedures, these
complaints shall be filed in a separate file other than the employee’s
personnel file. Complaints that are determined to form the basis for
necessary personnel management, personnel directives, or disciplinary
matters may be placed in the personnel file at the discretion of the
administrator. It shall be required that the unit member initial and date
complaints giving recognition of knowledge of inclusion in the personnel
file.

9.2    Unit members shall be given an opportunity to make a written response to
each complaint, which shall be attached and filed with the complaint. The
administrator shall be responsible for processing the complaint, subject
to subsequent review by higher level District management. District
administrators shall in no way be restricted from conferring with parents
and citizens concerning a complaint.

Evaluation Procedures - Probationarv/Permanent Basis

10.1    Philosophy on Evaluation of School District Personnel. The Cascade Union
Elementary School District believes that the evaluation of all aspects of an
educational program is necessary to the program’s success. A prime concern of
such a procedure is the evaluation of the District’s certificated personnel which
involves:

1.    A planned program to keep certificated personnel informed of respective
strengths and weaknesses and of options and resources for appropriate
professional growth activities to meet identified needs.

2.    A program to encourage and support certificated employees to continue to
make conscious organized efforts to improve professional competence, thus
guaranteeing that each student will continue to benefit from the service of
professionals who constantly strive to maintain the highest possible standards.

3.    A means for cooperative development of mutually understood standards and
criteria for professional service and student growth.

4.    A method of monitoring and supporting employee success and insuring that
only capable, qualified, certificated employees achieve and retain permanent
status.

The Cascade Union Elementary School District expects the evaluation of
personnel to be positive in nature. Those responsible for personnel evaluations
are expected to follow orderly methods of identifying strengths and/or
weaknesses by using the agreed upon evaluation system. Evaluators are also
expected to extend assistance for remediation of deficiencies, and to maintain
accurate and impartial records as outlined in the evaluation process.

10.2    Statement of Cascade Union Elementary School District

The District recognizes that, in the process of education, the uniqueness and
individuality of the learner must be respected. Therefore, learning experiences
must be matched to readiness and learning styles and directed toward successful
performance, which will enhance the self-image of the learner. With this in mind,
the District believes that the fundamental goal of the educational program is to
provide students with skills and knowledge so they can function effectively, both
in school and society, at a level consistent with their potential and to help students
develop confidence in their own abilities.

10.3    Guidelines and Procedures

The District recognizes the teacher as the primary agent of the educational
program and reaffirms the concept that the role of all other certificated employees
of the district is one of providing support and assistance to the teacher in order to

10.3.1    Evaluation    Procedures    for Certificated Personnel

It is the intent of the Board of Education to establish a useful system of evaluation
of performance that is consistently applied to all certificated personnel. This
system of evaluation should help certificated personnel improve the success of
the instructional program.

The purpose of such an evaluation system shall be to improve the instructional
program. It shall also be the purpose of the system to recognize and support
professional excellence of the certificated staff.

An Evaluation Advisory Committee comprised of teachers and administrators
shall be established to make recommendations to ensure consistency with the
goals and purposes of the evaluation procedure. Teacher members shall be
appointed by the Association. It will be the responsibility of this committee to
monitor the evaluation procedure to determine if it is being appropriately
conducted, where improvements can be made, what problems may arise, what
adjustments may be necessary, and to what extent these procedures support
efforts to improve the instructional program. This Committee will be advisory to
the District and to the Association. It shall not be the responsibility of the Advisory
Committee to review the evaluation of individual employees.

10.3.3 Goals Setting Conference

Inservice orientation in the use of this evaluation procedure will be carried out by
the employee’s immediate supervisor in the orientation meeting at the beginning
of the school year.

The purpose of the Goal Setting Conference is to provide an opportunity for a
conversation about good teaching as reflected in the California Standards of the
Teaching Profession and to establish an evaluation focus. In addressing the six
standards the evaluator and evaluatee will collaboratively select up to three
standards as specific areas of focus. However, in the evaluation process
evaluatee is responsible for basic performance in all six standards.

12

A.    Engaging & Supporting All Students in Learning

B.    Planning Instruction & Designing Learning Experiences for All Students

C.    Creating & Maintaining Effective Environments for Student Learning

D.    Assessing Student Learning

E.    Understanding & Organizing Subject Matter for Student Learning

F.    Developing As A Professional Educator

The evaluatee shall indicate the areas of student outcomes using a standardized
format. Failure to agree upon the objectives and/or the areas of student
outcomes will result in a conflict, which will be submitted to a review panel. A
written report, consisting of the Goals and written attachments, shall be submitted
to the panel within six (6) days.

The Review Panel shall be composed of three (3) certificated employees working
within the school district. One panel member shall be selected by the evaluatee,
one by the evaluator, and one shall be mutually agreed upon by the two persons
selected by the evaluator and the evaluatee. These three people must be
selected, meet and make their recommendations in writing to the parties
concerned within five (5) days of receipt of the Goals attachments.

The purpose of the Review Panel shall be to make recommendations regarding
only those areas requiring mutual agreement between the evaluator and the
evaluatee during the Goals Setting Conference and/or the Goals Update
Conference. The recommendation of the Panel shall be advisory and are not
intended to in any way supplant or circumvent the grievance procedure.

If the recommendations of the Panel are not acceptable to the evaluatee, the
evaluatee or the evaluator may, within five (5) days, appeal the decision to the
Superintendent. The Superintendent will render a decision within five (5) days,
and the decision will be binding.

10.3.4 Final Evaluation Conference

Prior to March 15 for probationary employees or April 30 for permanent
employees, the evaluator will meet with the evaluatee to review the performance
of those duties included in the aforementioned areas of The California Standards
of the Teaching Profession. The evaluatee will receive at least one week notice
prior to the conference, and will bring adequate materials necessary to illustrate
performance in terms of the established goals. The evaluator shall present a
summary of teacher performance, and any recommendations or commendations
the evaluator has made will be entered on the Summary Evaluation Report.

A written Summary Evaluation Report may be presented to the evaluatee at this
conference or within seven (7) days of the conference, but prior to March 15 for
probationary employees or April 30 for permanent employees. The Summary
Evaluation Report shall be based on the procedures specified in this evaluation
procedure.

In addition to the Goals Conference, at least two scheduled observations of
probationary employees, with pre-and post-conferences, will be made prior to the

shall be made prior to the Goals Conference.

At least one scheduled observation of permanent employees will be made before
the evaluation conference. Permanent employees will be evaluated at least every
other year.

Unscheduled observations may occur as the evaluator sees the need, or at the
request of the evaluatee, throughout the school year. If an unscheduled

The evaluatee shall have the right to initiate a written response to the evaluation.
Such response shall become a permanent attachment to the evaluatee’s
personnel file, unless the evaluatee chooses at a later time to remove it.

10.4 Unsatisfactory Evaluation

An unsatisfactory evaluation is defined as a Summary Evaluation Report, which
contains an unsatisfactory rating in any Standard A through F. An unsatisfactory
rating in A through F must be accompanied by evidence as indicated on the
Teaching Standards Rubric. In order for a standards to be rated unsatisfactory,
there must be two or more domains rated unsatisfactory in that standard.

The Summary Evaluation Report containing an unsatisfactory rating for any
Standard A through F will lead to a jointly developed improvement plan with
continuous support to assist the employee in becoming successful. When a
Standard is rated with an unsatisfactory category, multiple conferences will be
held in order to facilitate the improvement plan. The Evaluation Improvement
Plan will include recommendations and suggested options. These options may
include, but not be limited to, district supported staff development, classroom
visitations, subject area conferences, modeling by experiences educators and
appropriate coursework. It will be the evaluator’s responsibility to develop an
improvement plan within twenty (20) days of the final evaluation conference.

Evaluation of a permanent certificated employee on an improvement program will
continue until the employee is successful or is separated. However, the evaluator
shall stipulate the conditions which are to be met in order for the evaluatee’s
performance to be considered proficient.

When the evaluatee’s performance is considered proficient and the improvement
program has been completed, the certificated employee shall be evaluated in the
same manner as other certificated employees whose performance meets or
exceeds district standards.

The evaluatee shall have the right to appeal the content of an unsatisfactory
evaluation directly to the Superintendent whose decision will be binding. (See

ARTICLE 12: LEAVES

12.1 Personal Illness: A unit member’s absence from work due to illness or

injury shall be designated as sick leave and in accordance with policy,

shall be time lost with pay.

12.1.1    Sick leave shall be earned at the rate often (10) days per year (Ed.
Code 44978).

12.1.2    Unit members may accumulate unused sick leave without limitation
(Ed. Code 44978, 45191).

12.1.3    Unit members must contact their supervisor in advance of taking sick
leave whenever possible in order that other arrangements may be
made for the services needed. Failure to give adequate notice
may be grounds for disciplinary action.

12.1.4    Following absences due to illness, a doctor's statement may be
required before returning to work at the discretion of the principal or
the superintendent.

12.1.5    Unit members returning to work from sick leave involving major
surgery or illness shall be required to present a doctor's release
verifying medical permission to return to work.

12.1.6    If a unit member is terminated and has used more sick leave than
was earned, the amount used, but not earned, shall be deducted
from his final warrant.

12.1.7    After a unit member uses all accrued sick leave and is absent from
his duties on account of illness or accident for a period of five
school months or less, the amount deducted from the salary due
him for any month in which the absence occurs shall not exceed

the sum which is actually paid a substitute employee employed to

17

fill his position during his absence or, if no substitute employee was
employed, the amount that would have been paid to the substitute
had he or she been employed. (Ed. Code 44977)

12.1.8    A unit member may transfer accumulated sick leave according to
the provisions of Education Code Section 44979.

12.1.9    For absence due to quarantine, ordered and supervised by city
or county health departments, there will be no deduction of
accrued sick leave or salary if the contact with others having the
contagious disease was made while performing official duties of the
district. If this same contact results in the unit member contracting

15.1 Definitions

15.1.1    A transfer is the movement of a unit member from one work location to
another work location, or from one program to another program, such as
year-round education or restructuring schools. The transfer may also
include a change in grades or subject area.

15.1.2    A reassignment is the movement of a unit member from one subject area
to another subject area, one grade level to another grade level, or from one
configuration to another such as team teacher, restructuring, or other
reconfiguration within the same school site.

The following conditions will apply to voluntary transfer/reassignment requests:

15.2.1    A unit member may request change of grade level or school, or both.

following school year.

position apply for a vacancy, the following factors will be taken into

C.    Credential

D.    Previous experience at that grade level/program/school

E.    Special Training/Skills

day including a minimum of a thirty (30)-minute duty free lunch period.

minutes prior to the start of the instructional day unless other job duties
require his or her presence elsewhere.

ARTICLE 25: PEER ASSISTANCE REVIEW

ACTA agrees to suspend PAR until such times as the parties mutually agree to
reinstate it or until such time as a unit member should receive an “unsatisfactory”
evaluation. The unit members) that receive(s) an “unsatisfactory” evaluation will
receive the full benefit of PAR. Costs will not be limited to the current level of
funding for PAR as funds from other categorical programs can be applied to PAR.

The Anderson Cascade Teachers’ Association and the Cascade Union
Elementary School District are continuously striving to provide the highest
possible quality of education. In order for students to succeed in learning, teachers
must succeed in teaching. Therefore, the parties agree to cooperate in the design
and implementation of programs to improve the quality of instruction through
expanded and improved professional development and peer assistance.
Teachers referred to or who volunteer for the program are viewed as valuable
professionals who deserve to have the best resources available provided to them
in the interest of improving performance to a successful standard. Consulting
Teacher will henceforth be referred to as CT; Referred Participating Teacher will
henceforth be referred to as RPT.

1. Purpose

1.1.    The Peer Assistance and Review Program (Program) allows exemplary
teachers to assist certain permanent teachers in the areas of subject
matter knowledge, teaching strategies and teaching methods.

1.2.    The Program's assistance shall be provided through CT as described in
detail in Section 3.2, 3.3, 3.4 and 3.5 of this document. This assistance
shall not involve the participation in, nor the conducting of the annual
evaluation of certificated unit members as set forth in Article X. of the
Agreement and Education Code 44660, et seq., except for making
available to the evaluator the results of some unit members' participation
in the Program.

36

2. Definition of Terms

2.1.    Classroom Teacher or Teacher - A public school employee within the
meaning of Government Code 3540.1(j) who is currently classified as a
permanent employee pursuant to Education Code 44929.21. This
individual shall hold a clear, valid credential authorizing pupil instruction in
any grade K-8 issued by the California Commission on Teacher
Credentialing.

2.2.    Consulting Teacher (CT) - An exemplary teacher meeting the
requirements of subsection 3.1 who is selected by the Joint Panel to
provide Program assistance to a Referred

Participating Teacher

2.3.    The Referred Participating Teacher (RPT) - A permanent classroom
teacher whose most recent performance evaluation contained an
unsatisfactory rating in two or more domains within one of the three
standards A, B or C: A) Engaging and Supporting all Students in Learning;
B) Planning Instruction and Designing Learning Experiences for all
Students; and/or, C) Creating and Maintaining Effective Environments for
Student Learning.

2.4.    Principal or Evaluating Principal - The certificated administrator appointed
by the District to evaluate a certificated teacher.

3. Consulting Teacher

3.1.    Minimum qualifications for CT

3.1.1.    A full-time clear credentialed teacher with permanent status and at
least five years of recent teaching experience. Three of the five full¬
time years must be in-district experience.

3.1.2.    Demonstrated exemplary teaching ability, as indicated by, among
other things, effective communication skills, subject matter knowledge,
knowledge and commitment to District curricular goals and standards,
and mastery of a range of teaching strategies necessary to meet
students' needs in different contexts.

3.1.3.    Ability to work cooperatively and effectively with other teachers and
administrators, demonstrated effective leadership skills and
experience in working on school or District committees.

3.2.    CT positions shall be filled as follows:

3.2.1. The District will post the position(s) upon the need for a CT should a
permanent unit member need assistance as stated in 3.3.2 and 3.3.4.

37

3.2.2.    Each applicant will be required to submit a completed application to
the Joint Panel, which shall include at least three references noted,
one from each of the following: site principal or immediate supervisor,
Association representative and classroom teacher from current site.

3.2.3.    All applications and references will be treated with confidentiality
and will not be disclosed except as required by law.

3.2.4.    The Joint Panel will select three potential candidates for the RPT to
choose from for the course of the assistance through The Program.

3.2.5.    The Panel's procedures for selecting CT shall include provisions for
classroom observation of CT candidates that shall be scheduled at a
mutually convenient time and shall be a minimum of 30 minutes long,
as well as a formal interview.

3.3. The duties of the CT position(s) shall be a follows:

3.3.1.    A CT term will be for 12 consecutive months on an as needed basis.

3.3.2.    CT shall provide assistance to RPT in the following areas: A)
Engaging and Supporting all Students in Learning; B) Planning
Instruction and Designing Learning Experiences for all Students;
and/or C) Creating and Maintaining Effective Environments for
Student Learning.

3.3.3.    The CT and the RPT shall meet with the Principal to discuss the
PAR Program and to establish mutually agreed upon goals, to
develop the assistance plan and to develop a process for
determination of successful completion of the PAR Program. This
assistance may include, but not be limited to, the following activities:

3.3.3.1.    providing consultative assistance to improve in the specific
areas targeted by the evaluating Principal;

3.3.3.2.    meeting and consulting with the Principal or designee
regarding the nature of the assistance being provided;

3.3.3.3.    observations of the RPT during periods of classroom
instruction;

3.3.3.4.    allowing the RPT to observe the CT or other selected
teachers;

3.3.3.5.    attending trainings and meetings regarding knowledge of
effective peer assistance techniques and/or subject matter
knowledge;

3.3.3.6.    modeling effective practices to the RPT;

38

3.3.3.7. Maintaining appropriate records of each RPT activities and
progress.

3.3.4. CT assistance and review shall focus on the specific areas
recommended for improvement by the RPT evaluator after the
referred teacher received the unsatisfactory rating in two or more
domains within one of the three standards A, B or C: A) engaging and
supporting all students in learning; B) planning instruction and
designing learning experiences for all students and/or; C) creating and
maintaining effective environments for student learning.

3.3.4.1.    The evaluator’s/principal’s recommendation shall be written,
aligned with student learning and will be clearly stated.

3.3.4.2.    The principal, RPT and assigned CT shall meet and discuss
the recommended areas of improvement outlined by the principal.

3.3.4.3.    The CT shall conduct multiple observations of his/her RPT
during classroom instruction, and shall have both pre-observation
and post-observation conferences (within one day) to provide
specific, timely feedback. The CT shall monitor the progress of the
RPT and shall provide periodic written and/or oral reports to the RPT
for discussion and review.

3.3.4.4.    The CT shall be limited to working with one RPT per school
year.

4.    Referred Participating Teacher

4.1 .The Joint Panel will allow a RPT to select from a list of three (3) potential
CT's.

4.2.    The RPT shall meet with the CT, once assigned, to discuss and develop
plan for assistance.

4.3.    The RPT shall meet with the CT and principal or designee regarding the
nature of the assistance to be provided.

4.4.    The RPT in collaboration with the CT will schedule and develop an
assistance plan based on the needs as recognized in the Improvement
Plan. In turn, both the RPT and CT will develop a step-by-step process for
determination of successful completion of the PAR Program.

5.    Joint Panel

The PAR program shall be directed and operated by the Joint Peer Review
Panel, which will perform its duties under the agreement in good faith and

without conflict of interest and solely in the interest of the Referred
Participating Teacher.

5.1.    The Joint Panel will be made up of four (4) members of the ACTA’s
Executive Board (selected by ACTA), two (2) site principals, and the
superintendent (selected by the District).

5.2.    The Joint Panel will make all decisions through consensus. Failing
consensus, decisions will be made by majority vote. Five Panel members
will constitute a quorum for purposes of meeting and conducting business.
A majority of certificated teachers must be part of the quorum.

5.3.    The Joint Panel's primary responsibilities involve establishing the as
needed Program and budget, which includes adequate funding for CT’s to
carry out their duties, and selecting and overseeing the CT’s,

5.3.1.    In addition the Panel is responsible for:

5.3.1.1.    Making available a list of three (3) CT's for selection by the
RPT.

5.3.1.2.    Providing training for CT's and Panel members.

6. Other Provisions

6.1.    Functions performed by unit members under this document shall not
constitute either management or supervisory functions as defined by
Government Code Section 3540.1(g) and (m).

6.2.    Unit members who perform functions as CT's or Panel members under
this document shall have the same protection from liability and access to
appropriate defense as other public school employees.

6.3.    Disputes between the CT and Participating Teacher will be handled in the
following manner:

6.3.1.    Every attempt should be made by the CT and Participating Teacher
to resolve any disputes at the lowest level.

6.3.2.    Within the first six weeks of the assignment, the Participating
Teacher or the CT may petition the panel for an assignment change
for expressed reasons (see Article 3.1.2).

6.3.3.    Either individual may request an opportunity to address the panel
regarding the dispute. At this time the individual may request an
Association representative to be present for purposes of support. The
decision of the panel will be final.

6.3.4.    Following the above attempts, the RPT has the right to be

40

represented throughout these procedures by an Association
representative.

7.    Records

7.1.    All documents and information relating to the participation in this Program
will be regarded as a personnel matter and subject to the personnel
record exemption of the California Public Records Act (Government Code
Section 6250, et seq.). The annual evaluation of the Program impact,
excluding any information on identifiable individuals, shall be subject to
disclosure under the Public Records Act.

7.2.    All parts of the selection process of CT will be treated as confidential and
will not be disclosed except as required by law.

7.3.    All the documents for the PAR Program will be filed by the personnel
office separately from the individual personnel records.

8.    Stipends

8.1. CT's will receive a stipend equivalent the unit member’s per diem for time
