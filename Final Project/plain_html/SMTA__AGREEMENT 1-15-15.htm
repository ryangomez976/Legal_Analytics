ARTICLE I: RECOGNITION

A.    The District acknowledges the Association as the exclusive bargaining representative
for the following unit:

1.    INCLUDED: All probationary and permanent classroom teachers; all

not the new positions are to be included within the unit.

C.    As used in this Agreement, "unit member" is defined as any person in the bargaining
unit represented by the Association.

A. Entire Agreement: Effect Upon Board Rules

stated in this Agreement. Specifically, but not exclusively, the District is not bound by any

both the District or the Association at the time they met and negotiated on and executed this

parties may also meet and negotiate on any other subject if they mutually desire to do so.

Such negotiations may result in agreement to revise or supplement this Agreement;
accordingly, no employee shall be deemed to have a vested right to retain any provision of
the Agreement.

ARTICLE V: DISPUTE RESOLUTION PROCEDURE

A.    A Dispute in the Scope of Dispute Resolution:

A Dispute is an allegation by a unit member that he/she has been adversely affected
by a violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the specific provisions of this
Agreement and that by reason of such violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication, a unit
member's rights (or the Association's rights under Article V, section A.3 below) have been
adversely affected. Excluded from these procedures are all other disputes and those matters
so indicated elsewhere in this Agreement. A dispute, as defined above, may be filed and
processed by any of the following:

1.    One or more affected unit member(s); however, the unit member(s) may not
proceed beyond Level II unless the Association joins in the appeal;

2.    The Association on behalf of one or more specifically identified affected unit
member(s).

3.    The Association on behalf of itself.

B.    The Purpose of Dispute Resolution:

The purpose of these procedures is to solve disputes in an expeditious and effective
manner at the lowest possible administrative level. Both parties agree that these proceedings
will be kept as informal and confidential as may be appropriate at any level of the procedure.

C.    The Desired Outcome of Dispute Resolution:

Swift resolution of disputes and maintenance of a positive labor-management
relationship.

E. Definitions of Dispute Resolution Terms:

F. Detail Outline of Dispute Resolution Steps:

Step 1 - INFORMAL LEVEL:    Disputant/Designated    Administrator

Before filing a completed Dispute Form (Appendix G), the Disputant shall attend an informal
conference with his/her Designated Administrator for the purpose of clarifying the complaint
and seeking resolution.

1.    The Disputant shall have thirty (30) calendar days from when he/she knew of
the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the dispute, to request an informal
conference.

2.    The Designated Administrator shall have three (3) days to schedule the
informal conference. The three days shall commence upon the date following the date of
request from the Disputant.

3.    The Designated Administrator shall have five (5) days from the date of the
conference to render his/her written decision and suggested resolution regarding the dispute.

4.    The informal level shall be concluded within a maximum of thirty-eight (38)
days from the occurrence of the act or omission giving rise to the dispute.

5.    After the informal meeting has concluded, both the Disputant and the
Designated Administrator shall complete and sign this form. A copy shall be retained by each

Step 2 - LEVEL I:    Negotiating    Team

1.    If the Disputant is not satisfied with the decision at the informal level, he/she
may present the dispute in writing, on the appropriate forms (Appendix G), to the Designated
Administrator and the Negotiating Team, within five (5) days of receiving the decision from
the informal level.

2.    The written form shall contain a clear, concise statement of the dispute, the
circumstances involved and a copy of the suggested resolution at the informal conference.

3.    The Negotiating Team shall review and clarify the dispute with the Disputant

4.    If agreement cannot be achieved at Level I within twenty (20) days, the
Disputant may appeal to Level II within five (5) days from the twentieth day at Level I.

Step 3 - LEVEL II:    Superintendent

2.    The Superintendent shall have five (5) days to issue a written recommendation
to resolve the Dispute and to give it to the Disputant.

3.    If the Disputant is not satisfied with the Level II resolution, he/she may appeal
within five (5) days, in writing, to the Association, requesting submission of the Dispute to
Level III.

4.    The Association, by written request to the Superintendent within five (5) days
of the Disputants' request, may submit the Dispute to Level III.

salary will be provided to the affected unit member and his/her authorized Association
representative, if any, and to scheduled witnesses while testifying at arbitration hearings.

This constitutes reasonable periods of release time within the meaning of Government Code
Section 3543.1(c).

A. Work Day and Teaching Hours

1.    As used throughout this Agreement, "work day" shall be defined as a day
when unit members are required to be on duty whether or not such days are partial or

throughout this agreement, “day” refers to a work day, unless specifically specified to be a

established length. The minimum school-based assignment hours shall be subject to the
following provisions:

end of their last class or conference period to take care of student needs, attend scheduled

adjunct duties.

c.    Unit members shall be permitted to leave after the close of the regular
instructional day on Fridays and the day preceding a holiday or vacation.

d.    The District shall establish the length, starting and ending times of the
instructional day within the regular unit member work day specified in Paragraph 2a above.
The District shall make every effort to provide a relief break for each bargaining unit
member every two (2) hours.

ARTICLE VII: EVALUATION PROCEDURES

A.    The District retains the sole responsibility for the evaluation and assessment of
performance of each unit member, subject only to the procedural requirements of this
Article. Accordingly, no dispute arising under this Article shall challenge the substantive
objectives, standards, or assessment techniques or evaluations determined by the evaluator
or District, nor shall it contest the judgment of the evaluator; any disputes shall be limited to
a claim that the procedures of this Article have been violated. For purposes of this clause,
false facts set forth in the final evaluation document are considered procedural but no
content issues shall be disputable.

B.    Purpose:

The District and the Association agree that the principal purpose of evaluation is to
improve and strengthen the quality of the instructional program and to improve performance.

TEvaluation forms for teachers, counselors, speech pathologists and psychologists may be
found in Appendix D1

C.    Frequency of Evaluation:

Probationary and temporary unit members shall be evaluated once each year.
Permanent unit members shall be evaluated at least once every two years. The certificated
evaluation cycle will be held at least once every five years for permanent personnel who have
been employed at least 10 years, are highly qualified, as defined in 20 U.S.C. Sec. 7801, and
whose previous evaluation rated the employee as meeting or exceeding standards, if the
evaluator and certificated employee being evaluated mutually agree. The certificated
employee or the evaluator may withdraw consent at any time.

D.    Evaluator:

In most cases the evaluator shall be a unit member's principal unless another
certificated administrator is designated by the District. If the District designates another
certificated administrator as the evaluator instead of the principal, the unit member shall be
so notified by October 15. In the unusual circumstance where an evaluator becomes

21

incapable of continuing service in that role during the evaluation cycle, the District may
designate another qualified certificated administrator to complete the service as the evaluator
for the unit member’s evaluation. The designated evaluator shall not be the Superintendent.

E.    Information Meeting Regarding Evaluation:

Evaluators shall, by October 15 of each year, hold one or more staff meetings to
review the procedures for setting standards and techniques for assessment, and for reviewing
the evaluation calendar for the year.

F.    Procedures for Setting Standards and Assessment Methods—Professional
Development:

1.    The evaluator and unit member jointly shall determine the unit member's
professional goals and performance objectives for the school year. Development of these
objectives and the criteria for their assessment shall be completed by October 31 except
where extenuating circumstances cause a delay. Professional goals and performance
objectives shall be stated in terms of the students wherever appropriate, and may include
specific instructional methods, activities, and course content.

2.    The evaluator may suggest additional goals and objectives for the unit
member relating to criteria listed in the evaluation form. These suggestions must be
identified as "goals and objectives set by administrator."

3.    At the request of either the unit member or the evaluator, the standards
established shall be subject to review and possible revision during the school year. The
timing of the implementation of such revision shall be reasonably adapted to the
circumstances of each case.

4.    It is acknowledged that there may be constraints which hinder the attainment
of objectives. Either the unit member or the evaluator may propose such constraints as part
of the evaluation process subject to the provisions of Section F of this Article.

1.    Unit members shall not formally or informally evaluate other unit members.

2.    The evaluator may observe a unit member's performance at any time and may
evaluate a unit member's performance at any time. If the evaluator requests that a unit

ARTICLE VIII: LEAVES OF ABSENCE

A. General Provisions

1.    A leave of absence is an authorization for a unit member to be absent from
duty, generally for a specific period of time and for an approved purpose. The District and
the Association recognize that the California Education Code requires that the District grant
some types of leaves of absence. These required leaves are incorporated into this Agreement
by reference as if fully set forth herein, as such leaves now exist or may be changed from
time to time.

2.    A leave protects the unit member by holding a place in the District for such
unit member until the leave expires, usually with the right to return to the District in a
position of the same status and rank at the conclusion of the leave, providing the position
would have otherwise remained. There is, however, no assurance that the return assignment
will be in the school or administrative site where such unit member was assigned when the
leave was authorized.

3.    A condition of each leave of absence is that the credential or permit held at the
time the leave was granted, properly authorizing the service, must be maintained in full force
by the unit member.

4.    Unit members on a paid leave of absence, unless otherwise provided herein,
shall receive wages and health and welfare coverage, and retirement credits, the same as if
they were not on leave. Those who go on an unpaid leave shall continue to receive benefits
through the end of the pay period, and they may be allowed to remain on continued coverage
at their own expense pursuant to the terms of the District’s applicable insurance plan,
provided they make advance payment of the premium in a manner reasonably required by the
District.

5.    Any unit member who regularly works less than five (5) days a week or less
than a full school year of service (September to June) shall be entitled to leaves of absence, if
granted, in the same ratio that his/her employment bears to full-time employment.

6.    Utilization of leave provisions under this Article for whole or partial day(s)
shall be deducted from a unit member's leave entitlement whether or not a paid substitute was
employed to replace the unit member on leave.

25

7.    A unit member returning from any and all absences must contact the school or
site before 2:00 p.m. of the workday preceding the workday of intended return. One who
fails to provide such notice of intention to return to duty before 2:00 p.m. of the work day
prior to the work day of intended return shall be permitted to return to duty that work day and
shall be charged with the cost of the services of a substitute, provided the District is unable in
its discretion to utilize the substitute elsewhere.

8.    It is agreed that a unit member who is absent from work other than for those
days as authorized by State Law, or authorized leave provisions of this Agreement, is taking
an unauthorized absence in breach of contract and in violation of this Agreement. The
District will deduct a salary amount equal to the ratio of days absent to the days of required
annual service.

9.    At the end of each month, the Unit Member shall complete the Monthly
Employee Absence Report for absences incurred during that month.

B. Sick Leave

1.    The purpose of sick leave utilization shall be for physical and emotional
disability absences which make continued employment impracticable, or for legally
established quarantine.

2.    Unit members who are employed full time for five (5) days a week for a
school year of service (September to June) shall be entitled to ten (10) days of leave of
absence annually (September through June) for illness or injury. Unit members shall receive
full pay for sick days thus allowed in any school year, and the number of days not used shall
accumulate from year-to-year. Every unit member who works less than five (5) days a week
or less than a school year of service (September to June) shall be entitled to sick leave in the
same ratio that his/her employment bears to full time employment.

3.    Allowable sick leave credit for any one school year need not be accrued prior
to being taken by the unit member during said year. Such leave may be taken for workdays
during the instructional school year (September through June) and summer school as
provided in the Summer School Article XII. Should a unit member terminate employment
prior to earning sick leave taken in advance of accrual, the District retains the right to recover
any overpayment by any legal means of redress.

Earned or accumulated sick leave shall not be considered as vacation, nor

ARTICLE IX: TRANSFER AND REASSIGNMENT PROCEDURES

A. TRANSFER

1.    Definitions

a.    Transfer. A transfer is defined as the relocation or change of school
site of a unit member. A transfer is either "voluntary" or "involuntary."

b.    Voluntary Transfer. A voluntary transfer is a transfer initiated by the
unit member by the filing of the voluntary transfer form described below.

c.    Involuntary Transfer. An involuntary transfer is a transfer initiated by

the District.

d.    Opening. An opening is an unfilled position which the District has
determined to be filled by a probationary or permanent employee rather than by a substitute
or temporary employee. The District shall have the sole authority to determine when and
where there is an opening.

e.    Applicability. This Article shall apply to probationary and permanent
unit members only, and not to temporary unit members.

2.    Posting

A notice of all openings shall be posted in each building as the openings occur. The
opening notice shall contain a closing date for submitting requests for transfer, which shall be
not less than ten (10) days after posting. The opening shall not be filled until after the
closing date. The notice of opening shall include the position description and location, grade
level, and/or subject matter assignment, credential and/or experience requirement, and other
job-related qualifications, special skills or desired qualities upon which the site administrator
intends to base his/her selection. A copy of each posting notice shall be mailed to the
Association president on the day it is posted. Unit members may request (in writing to the
Superintendent) notification by mail of openings occurring during summer recess or during a
unit member's leave of absence and the District shall mail a copy of the posting to any such
unit member.

3. Voluntary Transfers

a.    Unit members are eligible to apply for any posted opening.

Application shall be made by completing the Voluntary Transfer/Reassignment Application
(see Appendix F) and filing the Application with the District Office prior to the posting
closing date.

b.    The filing of a Voluntary Transfer/Reassignment Application shall not
jeopardize the unit member's present assignment and may be withdrawn at any time prior to
the applicant's notification of new assignment.

c.    The District shall consider each Voluntary Transfer/Reassignment
Application submitted in response to the posting and shall also consider each unit member
who has previously submitted a Voluntary Transfer/Reassignment Application for the school
in question. Such a standing application shall expire at the end of the school year or after six

(6) months, whichever is later. New hires, unit members returning from leave, and unit
members being involuntarily transferred may also be considered.

d Qualified unit members who have been involuntarily transferred from
their previous school shall be given first consideration for openings at that school.

e.    The Superintendent (or his/her designee) shall determine whether to
grant or deny Voluntary Transfer/ Reassignment Applications. The criteria to be considered
in such determinations shall include the credential(s) held by the unit member in relation to
the staffing needs of the District, the unit member's instructional strength(s) in the subject
matter required for the opening, and the quality of performance as determined by the
supervisor responsible for evaluation. In the event two or more unit member applicants are
substantially equal in their qualifications as measured by the above criteria, the
Superintendent shall select for the opening the unit member with the greater (greatest)
District seniority, as determined by seniority provisions in the Education Code.

f.    If a Voluntary Transfer Application is denied, and the unit member
requests in writing reasons for the denial, the unit member shall be provided with specific
reasons for the denial in writing within a reasonable time.

g.    Unit members from within the District who apply for an opening shall
be considered ahead of non-unit members.

40

4. Involuntary Transfers

a.    Involuntary transfers shall be based upon legitimate education
program-related needs of the District and shall not be made for vindictive, capricious, or
arbitrary reasons.

b.    When an involuntary transfer is deemed necessary, the initiating
administrator will submit a written rationale to the Superintendent with a copy to the affected
unit member. An opportunity must be provided for the unit member to meet with the
administrator recommending the transfer prior to effecting the proposed transfer. The unit
member may file a written response to the reasons given.

c.    Upon written request of the unit member, the written rationale and/or
this response shall be placed in the unit member's personnel file.

d.    Unit members to be involuntarily transferred shall have the right to
indicate their preferences in writing from a list of openings.

e.    Unit members involuntarily transferred shall be placed in positions as
comparable as practicable.

f.    No unit member shall be involuntarily transferred more than twice in
any three year period.

1.    Definitions

A reassignment is a change in grade level or subject matter assignment in
which the unit member remains at the same school site.

2.    Implementation

a.    The Superintendent (or his/her designee) shall assign and reassign unit

members.

b.    A unit member desiring reassignment to any posted opening may
submit a Voluntary Transfer/Reassignment Application. This Application will be treated as
provided in Paragraph I, B, above. After implementing such a change, the site administrator

matter assignment, and/or classroom location, the site administrator shall (assuming the unit

interim basis with a new hire, or by a “surplused" unit member from a school with a
declining enrollment or reduced program, without regard to the posting procedures; it may

A.    Eligibility:

All currently employed unit members who have attained age 50 or will turn 50 by the
end of a current school year, and who have served this District for at least ten full years may
be eligible to participate as stated in this Article.

shall be mutually agreed, and may include but are not limited to, group testing, staff

Formal application for participation shall be submitted to the appropriate District
designee on or before June 30 of the school year preceding entry into the program. Such

estimated, and locale of service. Duties that can be performed as well away from a school
site as on shall be allowed.

Notification of acceptance or rejection shall be made in writing to the unit member prior to
July 31 of the year of application. Subsequent to acceptance, the unit member shall furnish
the Board with a formal letter of resignation from current teaching position. Copies of
approved retirement/resignation agreements approved by the Board shall be provided to the
Association.
