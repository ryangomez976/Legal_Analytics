Article III

ORGANIZATIONAL RIGHTS

The rights of the Association and the members of the bargaining unit represented by it are
described in the various articles of this Contract. The Association, as the exclusive
representative of the members of the unit it represents, shall also have the following rights:

A.    Each year, the Association, when authorized by the President, may utilize up to a
maximum of ninety (90) days of time , with the Association paying for the substitute, for
organizational business other than elections. Excepting the local President, national or
state association elected or appointed positions, no one individual shall be excused under
this clause for more than ten (10) days per school year. This time may not be used for
electioneering or exclusive representation disputes. This time would be in addition to the
reasonable time to be utilized for processing of grievances and negotiating under the Act.

B.    By mutual agreement between the District and the Association, the SEA President may be
released from a portion of his/her regular duties in the District and classified as a Teacher-
On-Special-Assignment. The District shall pay the SEA President the same salary and
benefits he/she would have received on regular duty without loss of seniority or other
rights and benefits. The SEA shall reimburse the District for the salary and other related
costs of the lowest paid fully credentialed new teacher hired for the year of release to
accommodate the sharing or replacing of the SEA President’s regular assignment.

As part of the release time assignment, the SEA President will make every reasonable
effort to provide services of mutual interest and benefit to the Parties as jointly determined
by the SEA President and the Superintendent or Designee. Such sendees may include
conducting information meetings concerning professional -growth, developing ways to
assist non-permanent unit members, attempting to resolve reported conflicts of certificated
employees, assisting with the recruitment of new teachers, representing the District and
SEA at appropriate out-of-district functions, gathering data for the use of both parties in the
negotiation/consultation process, developing and presenting in-services to certificated
employees on agreed upon topics, contributing to the District’s employee newsletter,
assisting with the development and implementation of new programs, and providing
representation on District committees.

SEA and the District shall work cooperatively to ensure that continuity of instruction is
maintained. The Parties recognize that it is to the advantage of the District, SEA and the
community for the SEA President to take an active role to make a positive work
environment for employees and a positive learning environment for students.

C.    A District Directoiy shall be provided without cost to the Association and to each member
of the unit no later than December 1 of each school year.

D.    To the extent the District receives reimbursement from the State, a copy of this contract
shall be provided, without cost to the Association, to each member of the unit. Distribution
of the copies of the Contract shall be done by the building representatives.

Article V

HEALTH AND WELFARE BENEFITS

The District will maintain the previously agreed upon health and welfare benefit coverages.

These include a choice of the District Health Plan and a vision care plan, or the Kaiser Health
Plan (including vision care), or the Blue Cross HMO Plan (including vision care). Also included
is a Delta Dental Service Plan. All plans wall include coverages for the unit member and his/her
eligible dependents.

Unit members who work at least one-half time are eligible for these benefits.

Any unit member with ten (10) or more years of service to the District, upon STRS retirement,
shall be entitled to participate in the District Health Plan and Delta Dental programs at the
Active Employee Rate at his/her own expense. Unit members may not participate in the District
Health Plan after age sixty-five (65) or the unit member becomes eligible for medicare,
whichever comes first - Except: When eligible for Medicare Parts A & B (or thru a spouse) may
remain in the plan as long as Medicare becomes primary and the District Health Plan is
secondary.

Any unit member with fifteen (15) or more years of service to the District, upon STRS disability
retirement shall be entitled to participate in the District Health Plan and Delta Dental programs at
the Active Employee Rate at his/her own expense. Unit members may not participate in the
District Health Plan after age sixty-five (65) or the unit member becomes eligible for medicare,
whichever comes first - Except: When eligible for Medicare Parts A & B (or thru a spouse) may
remain in the plan as long as Medicare becomes primary and the District Health Plan is
secondary until age sixty-five (65).

Retirees and dependents shall have the opportunity to take part in the Health Sc Welfare benefit
area as the law allows.

When there is a reduction in Adult School classes, preference will be given to maintaining
those classes which in the judgment of administration serve the needs of the Adult School

health condition;

b.    For the birth of a child, or placement of a child with the family for adoption
or foster care, only within the first 12 months after birth or placement of the
child.

“Child” means a biological, adopted or foster child, a stepchild, a legal ward, or a

must file a Declaration of Domestic Partnership with the California Secretary of
State pursuant to Family Code Section 297.

A “serious health condition” must meet the criteria set forth in federal and/or state

b.    Any period of incapacity requiring continuing treatment by (or under the

attended immediately prior to the leave in accordance with seniority rights. For
leaves exceeding twelve (12) months, the unit member will be guaranteed a position
for which they are credentialed or legally authorized, however it may not be the
same assignment or work site.

A unit member who takes leave has no greater right to reinstatement than if he or she
had been continuously employed. If the District reduces its work force during the

Article XIII

GRIEVANCES

A.    Definition

1.    A grievance is a dispute between the District and an aggrieved person involving
a violation, interpretation, or application of one or more provisions of this
Agreement.

2.    An aggrieved person is a unit member, or group of unit members, or the
Association (or its representative).

3.    A "day" shall be defined as any day with the exception of Saturdays, Sundays,
legal and local holidays, and Winter and Spring recesses. Normally this shall
include days during summer hiatus. However, at the option of the grievant and
without penalty or prejudice, a grievance which has not been settled prior to the
last day of the Spring semester may be deferred for a period of time not to exceed
the end of the first week of the beginning of the regular Fall semester.

B.    Purpose

1.    Prior to the filing of a written grievance at the lowest possible administrative
level, equitable solutions to the problems which may from time to time arise with
respect to this Agreement should be sought.

2.    Nothing contained herein shall be construed as limiting the right of any unit
member having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with the appropriate
member of the administration, and to have the grievance adjusted without
intervention by the Association, provided that the adjustment is not inconsistent
with the terms of the Agreement and the Association has been given an
opportunity to be present at such adjustment and to state its view.

3.    Since it is important that grievances be processed as rapidly as possible, the time
limit specified at each level shall be considered to be a maximum and every
effort shall be made to expedite the process. The time limits may, however, be
extended by mutual agreement. Failure by administration to observe designated
time limits without securing mutual agreement extending such limits shall result
in a waiver of level consideration and immediate processing of the grievance to
the next higher level. Failure by the aggrieved to adhere to submission deadlines
shall mean that the aggrieved accepts the latest decision and waives any right to
further appeal. 51

51

C. Procedure

Level I

1.    The aggrieved shall first discuss the grievance with his/her immediate supervisor.
The aggrieved has the right to have a representative of the Association present
and participating at the discussion(s). In the event that the immediate supervisor
acknowledges his/her lack of authority to provide the remedy requested, the
grievance may be initiated at the lowest level where such authority exists. The
District and the Association agree that every effort will be made by the District
and the aggrieved party to settle grievances at the lowest possible level. In the
event an informal conference is requested and identified by the unit member as
an informal conference related to the grievance procedure, the fifteen (15) days
shall begin the day after such conference takes place except that the conference
shall be requested within fifteen (15) days of the occurrence giving rise to the
alleged grievance.

2.    Whenever the grievant proceeds without Association representation, any
proposed resolution shall be communicated to the Association and the
Association shall have ten (10) days in which to file a response.

3.    In all cases, the grievance must be filed in writing on the approved grievance
form within fifteen (15) days after the aggrieved person knew or reasonably
should have known about the occurrence giving rise to the grievance. The
written grievance shall include: 1) a description of the specified grounds for the
grievance; 2) a listing of the provisions of the Agreement which are alleged to
have been violated; and 3) a statement of the action(s) requested of the District
which will resolve the grievance.

4.    The immediate supervisor or the person at the lowest level where such authority
exists shall respond in writing within ten (10) days to the written grievance.

Level II

1.    If within ten (10) days following the receipt of the written response from the
Level I administrator, the aggrieved is not satisfied with the disposition of the
grievance at Level I, he/she may file the grievance in writing with the
Superintendent or his/her designee.

2.    Within ten (10) days after the receipt of the written grievance by the
Superintendent or his/her designee, he/she shall meet with the aggrieved person
and a representative of the Association (if requested by the aggrieved person) in
an effort to resolve it. A proposed resolution shall be communicated to the
Association immediately after the hearing whenever the grievant proceeds
without Association representation, and the Association shall have ten (10) days
in which to file a response. The written disposition by the Superintendent or
his/her designee shall be rendered within ten (10) days after said meeting or after
the Association's response. 52

52

Level III

1.    If the aggrieved person is not satisfied with the disposition of his/her grievance
at Level II, he/she may, within ten (10) days after a decision by the
Superintendent or his/her designee, request in writing that the Association submit
his/her grievance to arbitration in accordance with the rules and procedures
delineated in this Article, The Association, by written notice to the
Superintendent or his/her designee, within fifteen (15) days after receipt of the
request from the aggrieved person, may submit the grievance to binding
arbitration. If any question arises as to the arbitrability of the grievance, such
question shall be ruled upon by the arbitrator prior to hearing the merits of the
grievance.

2.    The parties shall select a mutually acceptable arbitrator. Should they be unable
to agree oh an arbitrator within ten (10) days of the Association's submission of
the grievance to arbitration, request for a panel of arbitrators from which to select
shall be made to the California Conciliation Service. In any event, the parties
shall then be bound by the rules and procedures of the California Conciliation
Service in the selection of an arbitrator and the arbitrator shall proceed under the
Voluntary Arbitration Rules of said service. By mutual agreement, the
arbitration may be held under the Expedited Rules of the American Arbitration
Association.

3.    The arbitrator's decision and/or award(s) shall be in writing and set forth his/her
findings of fact, reasoning and conclusions on the issues submitted. The
arbitrator shall be without power and authority to make any decision which
requires the commission of an act prohibited by law or which is violative of the
terms of this Agreement. However, it is agreed that the arbitrator's findings will
be submitted to the Superintendent and the Association and will be considered
final and binding upon the parties to this Agreement.

4.    All costs for the services of the arbitrator, including, but not limited to, per diem
expenses, his/her travel and subsistence expenses and the cost of any hearing
room, shall be borne equally by the District and the Association. All other costs
shall be borne by the party incurring them. Either party may request a court
reporter to record the arbitration hearing, the costs to be paid by the requesting
party. If the arbitrator requests a court reporter, the costs shall be borne equally
by the District and the Association.

D. Rights of Employees to Representation

1. The grievant has the right to have a representative present at any or all steps of
the grievance procedure. The grievant, however, must be present at each step of
the procedure. If the grievant is unable to be present due to absence from duty
caused by illness, injury or other emergency, the processing of the grievance
shall be deferred until the unit member returns to duty. The time limits set forth
in the Article shall also be extended by the number of days of absence of the
grievant. 53

53

2. If a unit member is not represented by the Association or its representative, the
Association shall be informed of the grievance and shall have the right to be
present and to state its views through its representative(s) at all stages of the
grievance procedure.

E. Miscellaneous

1.    The Association, either on its own behalf or on . behalf of the affected unit
member, may initiate a grievance which affects more than one unit member in
a single building or unit members in more than one building at Level II.

2.    Until final disposition of the grievance takes place, the aggrieved unit member
is required to conform to the original direction of his/her supervisor.

3.    Every effort will be made to schedule meetings for the processing of grievances

However, when it is necessary for a representative designated by the Association

4.    .    Time limits for appeal at each level shall begin the day following receipt of

written decision.

5.    No reprisals of any kind shall be taken by the District or the Association against

6.    All documents, communications and records dealing with the processing of a

7.    The filing of grievances, the serving of notices, the taking of appeals, the making

INTERMEDIATE DISCIPLINE

The employment status of each unit member is conditional upon reasonable standards of.
personal conduct and job performance. Failure to meet such standards may provide the basis
for appropriate intermediate discipline.

The District retains the right to discipline unit members for just cause. If appropriate,
progressive discipline shall be followed in cases of disciplinary action. The following steps
shall ordinarily be utilized; however, the process may be initiated at any level if, in the
opinion of the supervisor, such action is warranted:

1.    Identification of problem or unacceptable behavior and verbal reprimand, if
appropriate.

2.    Written warning not placed in personnel file.

3.    Written reprimand in personnel file with notice of future possibility of appropriate
intermediate discipline.

4.    Appropriate intermediate discipline which may include, but is not necessarily
limited to, suspension with pay or suspension without pay for a period as
specified in the Education Code.

The following procedures shall apply when, in the judgment of the supervisor, an employee
has committed an act or an omission that justifies intermediate discipline as provided in
number 4 above.

A written recommendation for suspension, a copy of which will be forwarded to the
Superintendent or his/her designee, will be given or sent by Registered Mail to the employee
by the Personnel Office. The written statement shall contain a description of the events
which necessitated the recommendation, a statement of the charges, notification that the
employee may review or make copies of available materials leading to the suspension, and
the right of the employee to meet with the supervisor and to submit, in writing, his/her
response to the proposed action within five (5) days.

The suspension of an employee shall not be implemented for five (5) days pending the
employee's written response.

If, within the five (5) day response period, the employee involved does not file a response,
the action of the supervisor shall be implemented upon the approval of the Superintendent or
his/her designee.

If, within the five (5) day response period, the employees choose to file a response, he/she
shall do so by the option of filing a written response to the Superintendent, or by requesting a
hearing at Level II of the grievance process. The hearing shall be conducted as expeditiously

A.    Preamble

The Association and the District agree that unit members’ knowledge, skills and practices develop
throughout their professional careers. The nature of teaching requires continuous growth in order
to engage and challenge increasingly diverse students in a rapidly changing world. Unit members

education and preparation. If unit members5 expertise, capabilities, and accomplishments are to be
enriched over time, unit members must become reflective practitioners who actively seek to
strengthen and augment their professional skills, knowledge, and perspectives throughout their
careers. This program of professional accountability is designed to assist the unit member in

recognizing that the decisions must conform to State and Federal laws, District policy, and the
Contract unless site specific language has been approved. To be consistent with Board Policies
and Guidelines, District and SEA will consult as needed using an interest-based decision making
process.

Interest-based decision making is a defined model for joint planning and problem-solving that

educational reform.

Memorandum

To:

From:    Cary Dritz, Ed.D.

Asst. Superintendent, Personnel Seiwices
Subject: Maternity Leave Information

Congratulations on the upcoming addition to your family! I hope you are all geared up for some sleepless
nights and the incredible happiness that a new baby brings to a family. Our District wishes you and your
family the best of health throughout this joyous time.

Taking time off from your regular assignment always creates many questions about leave, pay, health
benefits and substitute coverage during your absence. We share your interest in providing your students
with the best possible learning environment in your absence and we want you to be knowledgeable about
the rights and benefits that are available to you at this time.

%

The following will provide you with information regarding your benefits and a timeline to help plan for the
continuity of the school program. Please note that every situation is unique, so please make an
appointment at least 30 days before your projected delivery date to discuss your plans and receive
information and forms for the options that are best for you and your family.

Suggested timelines:

1.    About six months before your due date, you should make an appointment or phone Personnel
Services to discuss your tentative plans. You will need to know the estimated due date and your
number of accumulated sick leave days. Please call Payroll for your number of available sick leave
days. You should also have a general idea about whether you will be returning to work right away
or if you plan to extend your leave beyond the customary six weeks after delivery.

2.    During this timeframe, you should talk to your principal about your tentative plans so that your
principal and I can work with you to find a suitable substitute to cover your class in your absence.
The substitute’s availability, credential and prior requests are all factors, so it is important that you
speak to Personnel and your principal BEFORE you discuss any requests with any possible
substitute.

3.    Sick leave days are used for personal illness or to go to doctor’s appointments. You will probably
be using some of your sick leave days as your due date approaches. Compensation for continued
absence beyond the expiration of accumulated sick leave will be fifty percent (50%) of the regular
salary. If you carry the supplemental Disability Insurance, please check with your carrier to see
how your benefitted amount will coordinate with your illness leave.

4.    When you and your doctor determine that you are physically no longer able to work, we must have
certification from him/her which shows the date of disability, time needed off work, and a medical
notice that you are unable to work This will begin your Maternity Leave of Absence. The duration
of the leave will be six weeks of leave (or eight weeks in the case of a Caesarian delivery) from the
date your baby is born.

5.    When the six weeks (or eight weeks) of time has expired your choices are as follows:

a)    You may return to work.

b)    If you qualify and have requested in advance, you may go on Family Care Leave for up to twelve
(12) weeks. (Family Care is an UNPAID leave, but the District will continue to provide your
current benefit package for up to 12 weeks. You are still responsible for the designated
monthly premium for your health care policy. Please make arrangements with Carla Dickson,
Benefits Coordinator. NOTE: If you decide not to return to the District after this leave, you
will need to reimburse the District for the amount of your benefit payments during your Family
Care Leave.)

c)    You may request Child Care Leave. Child Care Leave is an UNPAID leave and your benefit
package is not funded by the District. (You can choose to continue your benefits at your cost for
the length of your leave.)

d)    If you are medically unable to return to work at the end of your six (6) or eight (8) week leave,
you must obtain a note from your doctor stating so and you must complete paperwork for a
Medical Leave of Absence. The doctor‘s note and Medical Leave of Absence form must be
submitted to Certificated Personnel Services prior to the end of your six (6) or eight (8) week
leave. Continued absence after the conclusion of the Maternity Leave that is not substantiated
by a doctor's note and a Medical Leave of Absence request will be UNPAID.

6.    If at the end of your six (6) or eight (8) week leave you chose to take Family Care Leave, then about
thirty (30) days before your Family Care Leave is over, you again have a decision to make:

a)    You may return to work.

b)    You may request Child Care Leave. Child Care Leave is an UNPAID leave and your benefit
package is not funded by the District. (You can choose to continue your benefits at your cost for
the length of your leave.)

If at any time, you have questions, please call me or schedule an appointment to discuss your situation.
